# Vimanarchana Kalpa of Sage Marīci (Vaikhānasa Āgama)
## Chapter 73: Kautukādi-jīrṇa-bera-parityāga-vidhiḥ (Rules for Discarding Worn-out Movable Deity Icons)

This document contains the Sanskrit text, IAST transliteration, and English translation of the 73rd Chapter (*Trisaptatitamaḥ Paṭalaḥ*) of the **Vimānārcanākalpa** (attributed to Sage Marīci, representing the Vaikhānasa school of Vaishnavism).

This chapter discusses the defects arising from worn-out, decayed, or damaged movable deity icons (*jīrṇa-bera*), the serious consequences of delaying their disposal, and the precise ritual procedures for discarding them in water or fire. It also provides an anatomical classification of the deity's icon into four categories of limbs (*mahāṅga*, *aṅga*, *pratyaṅga*, *upāṅga*) and outlines specific expiatory and repair processes for each category of damage, concluding with rules for renovating painted icons.

---

### Section 1: Consequences of Delaying Disposal of Worn-out Icons

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **अथ त्रिसप्ततितमः पटलः**
> **जीर्णबेरे सति दोषः, तत्त्यागप्रकारश्च**

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **atha trisaptatitamaḥ paṭalaḥ**
> **jīrṇabere sati doṣaḥ, tat-tyāga-prakāraśca**

**Translation (English):**
*   **The Subject:** Now, I shall declare the rules for discarding worn-out deity icons (*jīrṇa-bera-parityāga-vidhiṃ vakṣye*).

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> अथ जीर्णबेर-परित्याग-विधिं वक्ष्ये —
> सद्यस्तद्बेरत्यागं न कुर्यात् चेत्, कर्त्रधिकारिणोः ग्रामस्य च महद्भयं स्यात्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> atha jīrṇabera-parityāga-vidhiṃ vakṣye —
> sadyas tad-bera-tyāgaṃ na kuryāt cet, kartr-adhikāriṇoḥ grāmasya ca mahadbhayaṃ syāt.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Consequences of Delay:** If one does not immediately discard that decayed/worn-out icon (*sadyas tad-bera-tyāgaṃ na kuryāt cet*), great danger or calamity (*mahad-bhayaṃ*) will arise for the patron/sponsor (*kartṛ*), the temple authorities/priests (*adhikārin*), and the village (*grāmasya ca*).

### Section 2: Discarding Protocol for Stone, Metal, and Wooden Icons

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> तस्मात् सर्वप्रयत्नेन जीर्णं सर्वं संशोध्य, नववस्त्रेणाच्छाद्य दर्भैर्बद्ध्वा ब्राह्मणैरेव वाहयित्वा समुद्रगां नदीम् अन्यमशोष्या-जलाशयं वा गत्वा, तीरे प्रपां कृत्वा, अग्निम् औपासनमाधाय आघारं हुत्वा वैष्णवं शतशो हुत्वा, अद्द्भ्यः सवित्रे च अङ्गहोमं व्याहृत्यन्तं हुत्वा, बेर-बन्धं विमोच्य, आचार्यः प्राङ्मुखः उदङ्मुखो वा वारुणं वैष्णवं च जप्त्वा तज्जले प्रक्षिपेत्, तत्तीर्थे स्नानं कुर्यात्।
> *tasmāt sarvaprayatnena jīrṇaṃ sarvaṃ saṃśodhya, navavastreṇācchādyā darbhairbaddhvā brāhmaṇaireva vāhayitvā samudragāṃ nadīm anyamaśoṣyā-jalāśayaṃ vā gatvā, tīre prapāṃ kṛtvā, agnim aupāsanamādhāya āghāraṃ hutvā vaiṣṇavaṃ śataśo hutvā, adbhyaḥ savitre ca aṅgahomaṃ vyāhṛtyantaṃ hutvā, bera-bandhaṃ vimocya, ācāryaḥ prāṅmukhaḥ udaṅmukho vā vāruṇaṃ vaiṣṇavaṃ ca japtvā tajjale prakṣipet, tattīrthe snānaṃ kuryāt.*
> 
> दारवं चेद् एवमेव कृत्वा वह्नौ दाहयित्वा तद्भस्म जले प्रक्षिपेत्। देव्यादि-परिषद्देवानां तन्मन्त्रं वैष्णवं च हुत्वा तथैव भस्मं जले प्रक्षिपेत्।
> पश्चाद् आचार्याय दक्षिणां दत्त्वा आलयं विशेत् ॥
> *dāravaṃ ced evameva kṛtvā vahnau dāhayitvā tadbhasma jale prakṣipet | devyādi-pariṣaddevānāṃ tanmantraṃ vaiṣṇavaṃ ca hutvā tathaiva bhasmaṃ jale prakṣipet |*
> *paścād ācāryāya dakṣiṇāṃ dattva ālayaṃ viśet ||*

#### English Translation
*   **Preparation:** Therefore, having made every effort (*sarva-prayatnena*) to repair or examine everything that is worn out (*jīrṇaṃ sarvaṃ saṃśodhya*), having wrapped/covered it with new cloth (*navavastreṇācchādya*), bound it with Darbha grass (*darbhair baddhvā*), and had it carried only by Brahmins (*brāhmaṇair eva vāhayitvā*),

*   **The Site and Fire Sacrifices:** One should go to a river that flows to the ocean (*samudragāṃ nadīm*) or to another never-drying water body (*anyam aśoṣyā-jalāśayaṃ vā*). Having constructed a temporary pavilion on the bank (*tīre prapāṃ kṛtvā*), established the household fire (*agnim aupāsanam ādhāya*), offered the initial ghee libations (*āghāraṃ hutvā*), offered Vaishnava offerings a hundred times (*vaiṣṇavaṃ śataśo hutvā*), and offered auxiliary fire-offerings to the Waters (*adbhyaḥ*) and Savitr (*savitre*) ending with the Vyahrti libations (*vyāhṛtyantaṃ hutvā*),

*   **Immersion in Water:** Having released the bindings of the icon (*bera-bandhaṃ vimocya*), the chief priest, facing east or north (*ācāryaḥ prāṅmukhaḥ udaṅmukho vā*), should chant the Varuṇa and Vaishnava mantras (*vāruṇaṃ vaiṣṇavaṃ ca japtvā*), submerge the icon in the water (*tajjale prakṣipet*), and take a bath in that sacred water site (*tattīrthe snānaṃ kuryāt*).

*   **Discarding Wooden Icons:** If the icon is made of wood (*dāravaṃ ced*), having performed the same rites, one should burn it in fire (*vahnau dāhayitvā*) and throw its ashes in the water (*tadbhasma jale prakṣipet*).

*   **Subsidiary Deities:** For the Goddesses and retinue deities (*devy-ādi-pariṣad-devānāṃ*), having offered fire oblations using their respective mantras and the Vaishnava mantra (*tanmantraṃ vaiṣṇavaṃ ca hutvā*), one should likewise burn them and throw the ashes in the water.

*   **Conclusion:** Afterward, having given sacrificial fees (*dakṣiṇāṃ*) to the chief priest (*ācāryāya*), one should enter the temple (*ālayaṃ viśet*).

### Section 3: Classification of Deity Limbs

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **कौतुकादीनाम् अङ्गहीने कर्तव्यम्, अङ्गादि-स्वरूपं च**
> कौतुकादीनाम् अङ्गहीने सति (कर्तव्यम्) — उदर-वक्षः-कण्ठ-शिरांसि महाङ्गानि, पाद-हस्त-बाहवोऽङ्गानि, अङ्गुली-कर्ण-नासादयः प्रत्यङ्गानि, अम्बर-आभरण-आयुध-शिरश्चक्र-पीठ-प्रभामण्डलानि उपाङ्गानि, इति आचक्षते ॥

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **kautukādīnām aṅgahīne kartavyam, aṅgādisvarūpaṃ ca**
> kautukādīnām aṅgahīne sati (kartavyam) — udara-vakṣaḥ-kaṇṭha-śirāṃsi mahāṅgāni, pāda-hasta-bāhavo'ṅgāni, aṅgulī-karṇa-nāsādayaḥ pratyaṅgāni, ambara-ābharaṇa-āyudha-śirascatra-pīṭha-prabhāmaṇḍalāni upāṅgāni, iti ācakṣate ||

**Translation (English):**
*   **Anatomical Classification of Deity Icons:** When the limbs of the movable icons (*kautukādīnām*) are damaged: They declare (*iti ācakṣate*) that:
    1.  **Mahāṅgāni (Major Limbs):** The abdomen, chest, neck, and head (*udara-vakṣaḥ-kaṇṭha-śirāṃsi*).
    2.  **Aṅgāni (Primary Limbs):** The feet, hands, and arms (*pāda-hasta-bāhavaḥ*).
    3.  **Pratyaṅgāni (Secondary Limbs):** The fingers, ears, nose, etc. (*aṅgulī-karṇa-nāsādayaḥ*).
    4.  **Upāṅgāni (Subsidiary Parts):** The garments, ornaments, weapons, head-disc, pedestal, and halo (*ambara-ābharaṇa-āyudha-śirascatra-pīṭha-prabhāmaṇḍalāni*).

### Section 4: Ritual Expiation for Damage to Subsidiary Parts

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **उपाङ्गहीने प्रायश्चित्तम्**
> तस्माद् उपाङ्गे हीने पूर्ववत् कुम्भं संसाध्य तच्छक्तिं कुम्भे समावाह्य, बिम्बस्य पुनः सन्धानं कृत्वा, जलाधिवासादीनि कृत्वा, वास्तुहोमं हुत्वा, पञ्चगव्यैः कुम्भोदकैश्च संस्नाप्य, सप्तभिः कलशैः संस्नाप्य, कुम्भस्थां शक्तिं बिम्बे समावाहयेत् ॥
> ***upāṅgahīne prāyaścittam***
> *tasmād upāṅge hīne pūrvavat kumbhaṃ saṃsādhya tacchaktiṃ kumbhe samāvāhya, bimbasya punaḥ sandhānaṃ kṛtvā, jalādhivāsādīni kṛtvā, vāstuhomaṃ hutvā, pañcagavyaiḥ kumbhodakaiśca saṃsnāpya, saptabhiḥ kalaśaiḥ saṃsnāpya, kumbhasthāṃ śaktiṃ bimbe samāvāhayet ||*

#### English Translation
*   **Damage to Subsidiary Parts:** If any of the subsidiary parts (*upāṅge hīne*) like ornaments or weapons are damaged or missing:

*   **Expiation Rites:** One should prepare a water vessel (*kumbhaṃ*) as before, invoke the divine power of the icon into the kumbha (*tacchaktiṃ kumbhe samāvāhya*), repair/join that part of the icon (*bimbasya punaḥ sandhānaṃ kṛtvā*), perform the water immersion and other preparatory rites (*jalādhivāsādīni kṛtvā*), perform the Vāstu fire-offering (*vāstuhomaṃ hutvā*), bathe the deity with the five cow products and the water of the kumbha (*pañcagavyaiḥ kumbhodakaiśca saṃsnāpya*), perform a bath with seven pots (*saptabhiḥ kalaśaiḥ saṃsnāpya*), and then invoke the power from the kumbha back into the icon (*kumbhasthāṃ śaktiṃ bimbe samāvāhayet*).

### Section 5: Ritual Expiation for Damage to Secondary Limbs

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **प्रत्यङ्गहीने प्रायश्चित्तम्**
> प्रत्यङ्गे हीने तच्छक्तिं महाबेरे समारोप्य, बिम्बम् उद्धृत्य पञ्चगव्यैः प्रोक्ष्य, तुल्यलोहेन सन्धानं कृत्वा, जलाधिवासादीनि कृत्वा, पुनः प्रतिष्ठां कारयेत् ॥
> ***pratyaṅgahīne prāyaścittam***
> *pratyaṅge hīne tacchaktiṃ mahābere samāropya, bimbam uddhṛtya pañcagavyaiḥ prokṣya, tulyalohena sandhānaṃ kṛtvā, jalādhivāsādīni kṛtvā, punaḥ pratiṣṭhāṃ kārayet ||*

#### English Translation
*   **Damage to Secondary Limbs:** If a secondary limb (*pratyaṅge hīne*) like fingers or ears is damaged or missing:

*   **Expiation Rites:** One should project its divine power onto the main fixed icon (*tacchaktiṃ mahābere samāropya*), lift/take the movable icon out (*bimbam uddhṛtya*), sprinkle it with the five cow products (*pañcagavyaiḥ prokṣya*), perform the repair/joining using identical metal (*tulyalohena sandhānaṃ kṛtvā*), perform the water immersion rites (*jalādhivāsādīni kṛtvā*), and perform the re-consecration (*punaḥ pratiṣṭhāṃ kārayet*).

### Section 6: Ritual Expiation for Damage to Primary Limbs

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **अङ्गहीने प्रायश्चित्तम्**
> अङ्गहीने तद्वेरं सन्धानयोग्यं चेत् तथैव कुर्यात्। अयुक्तं चेत्, महाङ्गे हीने च, बेरं भूमौ पिधाय, तस्य उपरि अङ्गहोमं हुत्वा, महाशान्तिं च हुत्वा, तद्दिने अतीते बिम्बम् उद्धृत्य पञ्चगव्यैः प्रक्षाल्य, अग्नौ द्रावणं कृत्वा (राजतं चेद् द्रावयित्वा, रौक्मं चेद् द्रावयित्वा), तद्रव्येण पूर्ववत् बिम्बं कृत्वा पुनः प्रतिष्ठां कारयेत् ॥
> ***aṅgahīne prāyaścittam***
> *aṅgahīne tadveraṃ sandhānayogyaṃ cet tathaiva kuryāt | ayuktaṃ cet, mahāṅge hīne ca, beraṃ bhūmau pidhāya, tasya upari aṅgahomaṃ hutvā, mahāśāntiṃ ca hutvā, taddine atīte bimbam uddhṛtya pañcagavyaiḥ praxālya, agnau drāvaṇaṃ kṛtvā (rājataṃ ced drāvayitvā, raokmaṃ ced drāvayitvā), tad-dravyeṇa pūrvavad bimbaṃ kṛtvā punaḥ pratiṣṭhāṃ kārayet ||*

#### English Translation
*   **Damage to Primary Limbs:** If a primary limb (*aṅgahīne*) is damaged or missing, and if that icon is suitable for repair/joining (*sandhānayogyaṃ cet*), one should proceed in the same manner.

*   **Irreparable Major Damage:** If it is irreparable (*ayuktaṃ cet*), or if a major limb (*mahāṅge hīne ca*) is damaged:

*   **Melting and Recasting:** One should bury/cover the icon in the ground (*beraṃ bhūmau pidhāya*), perform the limb fire-offering (*aṅgahomaṃ*) and the great pacification fire-offering (*mahāśāntiṃ ca*) over it. Once that day has passed (*taddine atīte*), retrieve the metal icon (*bimbam uddhṛtya*), wash it with the five cow products (*pañcagavyaiḥ praxālya*), melt it in the fire (*agnau drāvaṇaṃ kṛtvā*)—melting it if it is silver (*rājataṃ ced drāvayitvā*), or melting it if it is gold (*raokmaṃ ced drāvayitvā*)—recreate the icon with that same metal as before (*tad-dravyeṇa pūrvavad bimbaṃ kṛtvā*), and perform the re-consecration (*punaḥ pratiṣṭhāṃ kārayet*).

### Section 7: Renovation and Re-consecration of Paintings

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **पटे वा कुड्यादौ आलिख्य अर्चितस्य जीर्णे सति कर्तव्यम्**
> पटे कुड्ये वा आलेख्य अर्चितं बेरं जीर्णं चेत्, तच्छक्तिम् एककुम्भे समारोप्य, नवीकृत्य, पुनः प्रतिष्ठाम् आचरेत् ॥
> ***paṭe vā kuḍyādou ālikhya arcitasya jīrṇe sati kartavyam***
> *paṭe kuḍye vā ālikhya arcitaṃ beraṃ jīrṇaṃ cet, tacchaktim ekakumbhe samāropya, navīkṛtya, punaḥ pratiṣṭhām āceret ||*

#### English Translation
*   **Damage to Painted Icons:** If a deity icon painted on a cloth canvas (*paṭe*), a wall (*kuḍye vā*), or similar surfaces becomes worn out/decayed (*jīrṇaṃ cet*):

*   **Renovation Protocol:** One should project its divine power into a single water vessel (*tacchaktim ekakumbhe samāropya*), repaint/renew the painting (*navīkṛtya*), and perform the re-consecration (*punaḥ pratiṣṭhām āceret*) as before.

## Glossary of Key Terms

1.  **Jīrṇa-bera (जीर्णबेर):** A worn-out, decayed, or severely damaged deity icon requiring repair, recasting, or ritual disposal.
2.  **Mahāṅgāni (महाङ्गानि):** The four major/primary structural parts of a deity icon: abdomen (*udara*), chest (*vakṣas*), neck (*kaṇṭha*), and head (*śiras*).
3.  **Aṅgāni (अङ्गानि):** The primary limbs of the deity icon: feet (*pāda*), hands (*hasta*), and arms (*bāhu*).
4.  **Pratyaṅgāni (प्रत्यङ्गानि):** The secondary/minor limbs: fingers (*aṅgulī*), ears (*karṇa*), nose (*nāsa*), etc.
5.  **Upāṅgāni (उपाङ्गानि):** The external accessories and attributes of the icon: garments (*ambara*), ornaments (*ābharaṇa*), weapons (*āyudha*), head-disc (*śiras-cakra*), pedestal (*pīṭha*), and halo (*prabhā-maṇḍala*).
6.  **Prapā (प्रपा):** A temporary shed, canopy, or pavilion erected on a riverbank to perform transitional rites.
7.  **Aupāsana (औपासन):** The household fire established for performing domestic fire sacrifices (*homa*).
8.  **Vāstu-homa (वास्तुहोम):** The fire offering performed to pacify and purify the site and surrounding space.
9.  **Aṅga-homa (अङ्गहोम):** A specialized fire offering performed to make amends for damage to specific limbs of a deity icon.
10. **Navīkaraṇa (नवीकरण):** The process of renovating, repainting, or renewing a deteriorated painted icon (*paṭa-bera* or *kuḍya-bera*).
