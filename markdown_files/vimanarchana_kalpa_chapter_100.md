# Vimanarchana Kalpa of Sage Marīci (Vaikhānasa Āgama)
## Chapter 100: Samādhi-vidhiḥ (Rules on Absolute Absorption and the Conclusion of the Text)

This document contains the Sanskrit text, IAST transliteration, and English translation of the 100th and final Chapter (*Śatatamaḥ Paṭalaḥ*) of the **Vimānārcanākalpa** (attributed to Sage Marīci, representing the Vaikhānasa school of Vaishnavism).

This final chapter serves as the grand climax and epilogue (*granthōpasaṃhāra*) of the entire *Vimānārcanākalpa* (also known as the *Ayuta-grantha-saṃhitā*). It expounds *samādhi* (absorption), the eighth and final limb of yoga, defining it as the equalized state of union between the individual soul (*jīvātman*) and the Supreme Soul (*paramātman*). It provides a detailed yogic methodology for consciously leaving the physical body at death (*dēhatyāga-prakāra*), raising the prana through the *suṣumnā* channel to exit via the *brahmarandhra* while chanting *Om* to merge into Vaikuntha. Finally, Sage Marīci concludes by declaring the lineage of this Jnanayoga (bequeathed by Brahmā) and asserting that since constant inner meditation is difficult, one must worship the five manifest forms (*pañca-mūrti*) of Viṣṇu on images (*samūrtārcana*) to attain the supreme state.

### Section 1: The Nature of Samadhi (Absolute Absorption)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **अथ शततमः पटलः**
> **समाधिस्वरूपं तल्लक्षणं च**
> ***atha śatatamaḥ paṭalaḥ***
> ***samādhisvarūpaṃ tallaxanaṃ ca***
> 
> अथातः समाधिं वक्ष्ये --
> जीवात्म-परमात्मनोः समावस्था समाधिः ।
> यथा अनुष्णं जलम् आदित्यमयूखसंस्पर्शवशात् उष्णत्वं भजते, तथा परमात्मसंस्पर्शवशात् प्रत्यगात्मा नित्य-शुद्ध-बुद्ध-मुक्त-स्वभावकः परमानन्दम् एव प्राप्य, परमात्मानं नित्यं सदा पश्यति अनुभवति च । अष्टाङ्गयोगमार्गेण नित्यम् अणिमादिसिद्धिं च प्राप्नोति । जीवन्मुक्तो भवेत् ॥
> *athātaḥ samādhiṃ vaxyē --*
> *jīvātma-paramātmanōḥ samāvasthā samādhiḥ |*
> *yathā anuṣṇaṃ jalam ādityamayūxasaṃsparśavaśāt uṣṇatvaṃ bhajatē, tathā paramātmasaṃsparśavaśāt pratyagātmā nitya-śuddha-buddha-mukta-svabhāvakaḥ paramānandam ēva prāpya, paramātmānaṃ nityaṃ sadā paśyati anubhavati ca | aṣṭāṅgayōgamārgēṇa nityam aṇimādisiddhiṃ ca prāpnōti | jīvanmuktō bhavēt ||*

#### English Translation
*   **Definition of Samadhi:** Now we shall explain absorption (*athātaḥ samādhiṃ vaxyē*). The state of absolute equality and union between the individual soul and the Supreme Soul is absorption (*jīvātma-paramātmanōḥ samāvasthā samādhiḥ*).

*   **The Metaphor of Heated Water:** Just as naturally cool water becomes hot through contact with the rays of the sun (*yathā anuṣṇaṃ jalam ādityamayūxasaṃsparśavaśāt uṣṇatvaṃ bhajatē*), similarly, through contact and union with the Supreme Soul, the individual inner self (*pratyagātmā*), realizing its eternal, pure, wise, and liberated nature (*nityā-śuddha-buddha-mukta-svabhāvakaḥ*), attains supreme bliss itself (*paramānandam ēva prāpya*) and constantly beholds and experiences the Supreme Soul (*paramātmānaṃ nityaṃ sadā paśyati anubhavati ca*).

*   **Liberation in Life:** Through the path of the eightfold yoga, he eternally attains the spiritual powers beginning with *aṇimā* (*aṣṭāṅgayōgamārgēṇa nityam aṇimādisiddhiṃ ca prāpnōti*) and becomes liberated while living, a *jīvanmukta* (*jīvanmuktō bhavēt*).

### Section 2: Leaving the Physical Body and the Final Union

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **देहत्यागप्रकारः**
> अथ च देहत्यागकाले सर्वाणीन्द्रियाणि संयम्य, प्राणम् अपानेन संयोज्य, संशोध्य, सर्वेषु नाडीमार्गेषु निरुध्य, ‘हंसः’ इति सुषुम्नया चोर्ध्वं गत्वा, हृदयाम्भोजे प्राणम् आरोप्य, तदन्तराकाशे परञ्ज्योतिषि मनः सुसंयम्य, परमात्मानं पश्यन्, भ्रुवोर्मध्ये प्राणम् आरोप्य, प्रणवाक्षरेण सविन्दुनादेन समुत्थाप्य, प्राणम् उत्सृजति ।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **dēhatyāgaprakāraḥ**
> atha ca dēhatyāgakālē sarvāṇīndriyāṇi saṃyamya, prāṇam apānēna saṃyōjya, saṃśōdhya, sarvēṣu nāḍīmārgēṣu nirudhya, ‘haṃsaḥ’ iti suṣumnayā cōrdhvaṃ gatvā, hṛdayāmbhōjē prāṇam ārōpya, tadantarākāśē parañjyōtiṣi manaḥ susaṃyamya, paramātmānaṃ paśyan, bhruvōrmadhyē prānam ārōpya, praṇavāxarēṇa savindunādēna samutthāpya, prāṇam utsṛjati |

**Translation (English):**
*   **Method of Leaving the Body:** At the time of discarding the physical body (*atha ca dēhatyāgakālē*), a yogi should restrain all his senses (*sarvāṇīndriyāṇi saṃyamya*), unite prana with apana (*prāṇam apānēna saṃyōjya*), purify them, and lock them in all channel pathways (*saṃśōdhya sarvēṣu nāḍīmārgēṣu nirudhya*).

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> प्राणेन सह सर्वम् आत्मनि प्रलीयते ।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> prāṇēna saha sarvam ātmani pralīyatē |

**Translation (English):**
*   **Ascent through Sushumna:** Chanting *Hamsa* (*‘haṃsaḥ’ iti*), he should ascend upward through the central *suṣumnā* channel (*suṣumnayā cōrdhvaṃ gatvā*), direct the vital breath into the heart-lotus (*hṛdayāmbhōjē prāṇam ārōpya*), and firmly concentrate the mind on the Supreme Light within its space (*tadantarākāśē parañjyōtiṣi manaḥ susaṃyamya*).

#### Verse / Paragraph 3
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> तदा परमात्मना सायुज्यं वैकुण्ठम् अवाप्नोति ॥

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> tadā paramātmanā sāyujyaṃ vaikuṇṭham avāpnōti ||

**Translation (English):**
*   **Merging into Vaikuntha:** Beholding the Supreme Soul (*paramātmānaṃ paśyan*), raising the prana to the space between the eyebrows (*bhruvōrmadhyē prānam ārōpya*), raising it further out of the head by means of the *praṇava* (*Om*) accompanied by the nasal point and resonance (*praṇavāxarēṇa savindunādēna samutthāpya*), he casts off the vital breath (*prāṇam utsृjati*). Along with the breath, everything dissolves into the Self (*prāṇēna saha sarvam ātmani pralīyatē*). Thereupon, he attains absolute union (*sāyujya*) with the Supreme Soul in Vaikuntha (*tadā paramātmanā sāyujyaṃ vaikuṇṭham avāpnōti*).

### Section 3: Epilogue and the Value of Image Worship

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **ग्रन्थोपसंहारः**
> इत्येवं संसार-समुद्र-उत्तरण-उपाय-साधनं ज्ञानयोगं च ब्रह्मणा पुरा मम प्रणीतम् । तदेतद् युष्माकं मया चोक्तम् । ज्ञानोपदेशं गुरुशिष्यमार्गेण ज्ञात्वा, पश्चात् परमात्मानं सदा पश्येत् ।
> एवं सदा ध्यातुम् अशक्यत्वात्, प्रतिमादिषु पञ्चमूर्तिनामभेदैः समावाह्य अभ्यर्चयेत् । एतत् समूर्तार्चनम् । सर्वसिद्धिप्रदत्वाद् गृहे देवायतने वा समूर्तार्चनमेव कारयेत् । अन्यथा न परमं पदम् अवाप्नोति । तस्मात् श्रुतिचोदितं परमगुह्यम् एतद् ज्ञानयोगं च ज्ञात्वा समाचरेद् इत्याह मरीचिः ॥
> ***granthōpasaṃhāraḥ***
> *ityēvaṃ saṃsāra-samudra-uttaraṇa-upāya-sādhanaṃ jñānayōgaṃ ca brahmaṇā purā mama praṇītam | tadētad yuṣmākaṃ mayā cōktam | jñānōpadēśaṃ guruśiṣyamārgēṇa jñātvā, paścāt paramātmānaṃ sadā paśyēt |*
> *ēvaṃ sadā dhyātum aśakyatvāt, pratimādiṣu pañcamūrtināmabhēdaiḥ samāvāhya abhyarcayēt | ētat samūrtārcanam | sarvasiddhipradatvād gṛhē dēvāyatanē vā samūrtārcanamēva kārayēt | anyathā na paramaṃ padam avāpnōti | tasmāt śruticōditaṃ paramaguhyam ētad jñānayōgaṃ ca jñātvā samācarēd ityāha marīciḥ ||*

#### English Translation
*   **Transmission of the Doctrine:** In this way, this Jnanayoga, which is the instrument and means for crossing the ocean of transmigration (*ityēvaṃ saṃsāra-samudra-uttaraṇa-upāya-sādhanaṃ jñānayōgaṃ ca*), was compiled of old by Brahmā for me (*brahmaṇā purā mama praṇītam*). That same has been declared by me to you (*tadētad yuṣmākaṃ mayā cōktam*). Having understood this instruction of knowledge through the lineage of guru and disciple (*jñānōpadēśaṃ guruśiṣyamārgēṇa jñātvā*), one should thereafter always perceive the Supreme Soul (*paścāt paramātmānaṃ sadā paśyēt*).

*   **The Necessity of Samurtarcana (Image Worship):** Since it is impossible to meditate in this way constantly (*ēvaṃ sadā dhyātum aśakyatvāt*), one should invoke and worship Him on images under the distinct names of the five forms (*pratimāṣu pañcamūrtināmabhēdaiḥ samāvāhya abhyarcayēt*). This is worship with form (*ētat samūrtārcanam*).

*   **Worship in Temple and Home:** Because it grants all accomplishments (*sarvasiddhipradatvād*), one should perform worship with form alone, whether in the home or in a temple (*gṛhē dēvāyatanē vā samūrtārcanamēva kārayēt*). Otherwise, one does not attain the supreme state (*anyathā na paramaṃ padam avāpnōti*).

#### Colophon
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> इति श्रीवैखानसे मरीचिप्रोक्ते विमानार्चनाकल्पे अयुतग्रन्थसंहितायां समाधिविधिर्नाम शततमः पटलः ॥१००॥

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> iti śrīvaikhānasē marīciprōktē vimānārcanākalpē ayutagranthasaṃhitāyāṃ samādhividhirnāma śatatamaḥ paṭalaḥ ||100||

**Translation (English):**
*   **The Final Command:** Therefore, having understood this Jnanayoga declared by the Vedas (*tasmāt śruticōditaṃ ... jñānayōgaṃ ca jñātvā*), which is the supreme secret, one should practice it (*paramaguhyam ētad ... samācarēd*) — thus said Sage Marīci (*ityāha marīciḥ*).

### Glossary of Key Terms
*   **Jīvanmukta (जीवन्मुक्त):** One who is liberated from worldly bondage while still inhabiting a physical body.
*   **Hamsaḥ (हंसः):** The sacred breath mantra ("I am He"), representing the movement of life breath and the identity of the individual soul with the Supreme.
*   **Dēhatyāga (देहत्याग):** The conscious abandonment of the physical body by a yogi at the time of death.
*   **Ayuta-grantha-saṃhitā (अयुतग्रन्थसंहिता):** "The Compendium of Ten Thousand Units," the traditional alternate title of the *Vimānārcanākalpa*.
*   **Samūrtārcana (समूर्तार्चन):** Worshipping God with form on consecrated images (*pratimā*), declared in this text to be the primary path of salvation for humanity since continuous meditation (*dhyāna*) is impossible.
*   **Guru-śiṣya-mārga (गुरुशिष्यमार्ग):** The lineage or pathway of transmission from teacher to disciple, through which sacred knowledge is preserved.
