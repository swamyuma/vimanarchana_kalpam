# Vimanarchana Kalpa of Sage Marīci (Vaikhānasa Āgama)
## Chapter 70: Bhaya-Rakṣārtha-Niṣkṛtiḥ (Remedies for Protection in Times of Fear/Distress)

This document contains the Sanskrit text, IAST transliteration, and English translation of the 70th Chapter (*Saptatitamaḥ Paṭalaḥ*) of the **Vimānārcanākalpa** (attributed to Sage Marīci, representing the Vaikhānasa school of Vaishnavism). 

This chapter outlines the specific ritual protocol to be followed when the movable deity icons (*utsava-bera*, *kautuka-bera*, etc.) must be concealed or buried in the earth (*tirodhāna*) to protect them from theft, desecration, or destruction due to threats like thieves (*caura*), enemies (*śatru*), or foreign invasion (*paracakra-bhaya*).

---

### Section 1: The Concealment of Icons (Tirodhāna-vidhi)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **अथ सप्ततितमः पटलः**
> **भयरक्षार्थ-निष्कृतिः**
> ***atha saptatitamaḥ paṭalaḥ***
> ***bhaya-rakṣārtha-niṣkṛtiḥ***
> 
> अथ भयरक्षार्थं निष्कृतिं वक्ष्ये— चौरैः शत्रुभिः परचक्रभयाद्वा ग्रामसङ्कुले सति भयरक्षार्थं कौतुक-स्नपन-उत्सव-बलिबेर-लौहिकप्रतिमानां देवीनां च तिरोधानं कारयेत्।
> *atha bhaya-rakṣārthaṃ niṣkṛtiṃ vakṣye— cauraiḥ śatrubhiḥ paracakra-bhayād-vā grāmasankule sati bhaya-rakṣārthaṃ kautuka-snapana-utsava-balibera-lauhikapratimānāṃ devīnāṃ ca tirodhānaṃ kārayet.*
> 
> **तत्प्रकारः**
> गुप्ते शुचौ देशे अवटं खनित्वा सिकताः प्रक्षिप्य उपरि कुशानास्तीर्य अवटे महीं देवीमभ्यर्च्य "आपो हि ष्ठा" इति प्रोक्ष्य, आचार्यः अर्चको वा यजमानेन भक्तैः सार्धं देवागारं प्रविश्य देवं प्रणम्य "यावत्कालं भयमस्ति तावद् धरण्या सह शयने शयीथाः जनार्दन" इति देवेशमनुमान्य बिम्बस्थां शक्तिं ध्रुवबेरे समारोपयेत्। बेराभावे हृदये समारोपयेत्। "परं रंह" इति अवटेऽप्रमादं संन्यस्य "यद् वैष्णवम्" इति प्राक्छिरसं शाययेत्। अवटं सिकताभिः मृदा वा पूरयित्वा अवटच्छिद्रं सुदृढं कारयेत्।
> ***tat-prakāraḥ***
> *gupte śucau deśe avaṭaṃ khanitvā sikatāḥ prakṣipya upari kuśān-āstīrya avaṭe mahīṃ devīm-abhyarcya "āpo hi ṣṭhā" iti prokṣya, ācāryaḥ arcako vā yajamānena bhaktaiḥ sārdhaṃ devāgāraṃ praviśya devaṃ praṇamya *"yāvatkālaṃ bhayam-asti tāvad dharaṇyā saha śayane śayīthāḥ janārdana"* iti deveśam-anumānya bimbasthāṃ śaktiṃ dhruvabere samāropayet. berābhāve hṛdaye samāropayet. "paraṃ raṃha" iti avaṭe'pramādaṃ saṃnyasya "yad vaiṣṇavam" iti prāk-chirasāṃ śāyayet. avaṭaṃ sikatābhiḥ mṛdā vā pūrayitvā avaṭacchidraṃ sudṛḍhaṃ kārayet.*

#### English Translation
*   **The Expiation/Remedy:** Now, I shall declare the remedy for protection against danger. When there is turmoil or crisis in the village due to thieves (*caura*), enemies (*śatru*), or fear of foreign invasion/hostile armies (*paracakra-bhaya*), one should arrange for the concealment (*tirodhāna*) of the movable icons—the *Kautuka* (worship icon), *Snapana* (bath icon), *Utsava* (festival icon), and *Bali* (offering icon)—as well as the metal images (*lauhika-pratimā*) and those of the Goddesses.

*   **The Method:** 
    *   In a hidden, clean, and pure place, having dug a pit (*avaṭa*), having scattered sand (*sikatāḥ*) inside, having spread *Kuśa* grass on top of it, and having worshipped the Earth Goddess (*Mahī Devī*) in the pit, and having sprinkled it (with water) while reciting the mantra *'āpo hi ṣṭhā'* (the purificatory waters mantra)...
    *   ...the head priest (*ācārya*) or performing priest (*arcaka*), together with the patron (*yajamāna*) and devotees, should enter the temple sanctum, bow to the Lord, and request the Lord of Devas, praying:
        > *"O Janardana! Sleep on the bed of the Earth along with Goddess Dharani for as long as this danger exists."*
    *   Having thus prayed and taken leave of the Lord, the priest should transfer the divine power (*śakti*) present in the movable icons (*bimba*) onto the main fixed stone deity (*dhruva-bera*). If the main fixed icon is absent, he should transfer it to his own heart (*hṛdaya*).
    *   Reciting the mantra *'paraṃ raṃha'*, he should carefully place the icon in the pit, and reciting *'yad vaiṣṇavam'*, he should lay the icon down with its head facing east (*prāk-chiras*).
    *   Finally, having filled the pit with sand or soil, he should seal the opening of the pit (*avaṭacchidra*) very firmly.

### Section 2: Interim Worship via Kūrca (Kūrca-sthāpanam)

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> तत आलये कौतुकादिस्थाने कूर्च-स्थापनादि—
> पश्चाद् अभ्यन्तरं प्रविश्य देवं प्रणम्य जीवस्थाने पञ्चदश-दर्भैः ग्रथितं द्वादशाङ्गुलमात्रं कूर्चं संन्यस्य ध्रुवबेरात् कूर्चे समावाह्य अर्चयेत्।
> बालालये चेद् अर्चापीठे कूर्चं संन्यस्य हृदयात् प्रणिधौ निवेश्य कूर्चं बिम्बवन् मनसाऽक्षराणि तत्स्थाने स्मृत्वा संन्यस्य आवाह्य अर्चयेत्। स्नपनादौ अभ्युक्षणमेव कारयेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> tata ālaye kautukādisthāne kūrca-sthāpanādi—
> paścād abhyantaraṃ praviśya devaṃ praṇamya jīvasthāne pañcadaśa-darbhaiḥ grathitaṃ dvādaśa-aṅgulamātraṃ kūrcaṃ saṃnyasya dhruvaberāt kūrce samāvāhya arcayet.
> bālālaye ced arcāpīṭhe kūrcaṃ saṃnyasya hṛdayāt praṇidhau niveśya kūrcaṃ bimbavan manasā'kṣarāṇi tat-sthāne smṛtā saṃnyasya āvāhya arcayet. snapanādau abhyukṣaṇameva kārayet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Placement of the Kūrca:** Subsequently, the placement of the *Kūrca* (sacred grass bundle representing the deity) should be done in the temple at the spot where the *Kautuka* and other movable icons were situated:
    *   Entering the inner sanctum and bowing to the Lord, the priest should place a *Kūrca* measuring twelve fingers (*dvādaśa-aṅgula*) and woven out of fifteen *Darbha* grass blades at the seat of life (*jīva-sthāna*). He should invoke the deity's presence from the fixed icon (*dhruva-bera*) into this *Kūrca* and perform the daily worship.
    *   If a temporary temple (*bālālaya*) is used, he should place the *Kūrca* on the worship altar (*arcā-pīṭha*). Directing the energy from his heart into the water vessel (*praṇidhi*), he should treat the *Kūrca* as the actual icon (*bimbavat*), mentally project the sacred letters/syllables (*akṣarāṇi*) onto their respective spots, establish them, invoke the deity, and worship.
    *   For consecration baths (*snapana*), etc., during this period, he should perform only the symbolic sprinkling (*abhyukṣaṇa*) of water over the *Kūrca*.

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **मासादूर्ध्वम् अन्यकूर्च-स्थापनम्**
> मासादूर्ध्वं तत्कूर्चं व्यपोह्य अन्यत्कूर्चं संन्यस्य आवाह्य अर्चयेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **māsād-ūrdhvam anya-kūrca-sthāpanam**
> māsād-ūrdhvaṃ tat-kūrcaṃ vyapohya anyat-kūrcaṃ saṃnyasya āvāhya arcayet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Replacing the Kūrca after a Month:** If more than a month passes under these circumstances, he should discard that old *Kūrca*, place a fresh *Kūrca*, invoke the deity's presence into it, and continue the worship.

### Section 3: Recovery and Re-installation of Icons (Punas-sthāpanam)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **सुकाले कर्तव्यम्**
> सुकाले बिम्बमुद्धृत्य, आम्लादिभिः संशोध्य, पुण्याहं कृत्वा, अस्पृश्यैः स्पृष्टं चेत् जलाधिवासादीनि कृत्वा, कालापेक्षाम् अकृत्वा अङ्कुरार्पणं कृत्वा, आलयाभिमुखे दक्षिणे वा मण्डपं कूटं प्रपां वा कृत्वा, तन्मध्ये पौण्डरीकाग्निकुण्डं कृत्वा, तदुत्तरे वास्तुहोमं हुत्वा, बिम्बस्य अतिपार्श्वतः पर्यग्निकरणं कृत्वा संशोध्य, पौण्डरीकाग्नेः पश्चिमतो बिम्बार्धाधिकायाम-विस्तारां भागोन्नतां यथालाभोन्नतां वा धान्यैः वेदिं कृत्वा, अण्डजादीनि पञ्चवस्त्राणि आस्तीर्य, चतुर्दश-कलशैः संस्नाप्य, पुण्याहान्ते प्रतिसरं बध्वा, कौतुक-स्नपन-उत्सव-बलिबेराणि देवीश्च सह एकशयने शाययेत्। अवताराणां शयनं पृथगेव विधीयते।
> ***sukāle kartavyam***
> *sukāle bimbam-uddhṛtya, āmlādibhiḥ saṃśodhya, puṇyāhaṃ kṛtvā, aspṛśyaiḥ spṛṣṭaṃ cet jalādhivāsādīni kṛtvā, kālāpekṣām akṛtvā aṅkurārpaṇaṃ kṛtvā, ālayābhimukhe dakṣiṇe vā maṇḍapaṃ kūṭaṃ prapāṃ vā kṛtvā, tanmadhye pauṇḍarīkāgnikuṇḍaṃ kṛtvā, taduttare vāstuhomaṃ hutvā, bimbasya atipārśvataḥ paryagnikaraṇaṃ kṛtvā saṃśodhya, pauṇḍarīkāgneḥ paścimato bimbārdhādhi-kāyāma-vistārāṃ bhāgonnatāṃ yathālābhonnatāṃ vā dhānyaiḥ vediṃ kṛtvā, aṇḍajādīni pañcavastrāṇi āstīrya, caturdaśa-kalaśaiḥ saṃsnāpya, puṇyāhānte pratisaraṃ badhvā, kautuka-snapana-utsava-baliberāṇi devīśca saha ekaśayane śāyayet. avatārāṇāṃ śayanaṃ pṛthageva vidhīyate.*
> 
> अथाग्निं परिस्तीर्य, परिषिच्य, हौत्रं प्रशंस्य, देवेशं देव्या, अवताराणां च दक्षिणप्रणिधौ तदालयगत-परिषद्देवान् उत्तरप्रणिधौ च समावाह्य, आवाहनक्रमेण निर्वापं कृत्वा आज्याहुतीः यजेत्।
> *athāgniṃ paristīrya, pariṣicya, hautraṃ praśaṃsya, deveśaṃ devyā, avatārāṇāṃ ca dakṣiṇapraṇidhau tadālayagata-pariṣaddevān uttarapraṇidhau ca samāvāhya, āvāhanakrameṇa nirvāpaṃ kṛtvā ājyāhutīḥ yajet.*
> 
> **शान्तिहोम-स्वरूपम्**
> वैष्णवं, विष्णुसूक्तं, पुरुषसूक्तं, विष्णुगायत्रीं, द्वादशाक्षरं, प्राजापत्यं, मिन्दाहुतिं, विच्छिन्नं च जुहुयात्। एतत् शान्तिहोम इत्येके।
> ***śāntihoma-svarūpam***
> *vaiṣṇavaṃ, viṣṇusūktaṃ, puruṣasūktaṃ, viṣṇugāyatrīṃ, dvādaśākṣaraṃ, prājāpatyaṃ, mindāhutiṃ, vicchinnaṃ ca juhuyāt. etat śāntihoma ityeke.*
> 
> **मासषट्केऽतीते महाशान्तिः, तत्स्वरूपं च**
> षण्मासे अतीते महाशान्तिं जुहुयात्। लाज-तिल-शक्तुभिः आज्यमिश्रितैः वैष्णवं, विष्णुसूक्तं प्रत्येकं द्वादश-अष्ट-पर्यायतो जुहुयात्। अष्टोत्तरशतं पद्मं श्वेतं रक्तं वा, अलाभे बिल्वपत्रं वा घृताप्लुतं विष्णुगायत्र्या जुहुयात्। सर्वदेवत्यं पारमात्मिकं च जुहुयात्। एवं महाशान्तिरिति केचिद् वदन्ति।
> ***māsa-ṣaṭke'tīte mahāśāntiḥ, tat-svarūpaṃ ca***
> *ṣaṇmāse atīte mahāśāntiṃ juhuyāt. lāja-tila-śaktubhiḥ ājyamiśritaiḥ vaiṣṇavaṃ, viṣṇusūktaṃ pratyekaṃ dvādaśa-aṣṭa-paryāyato juhuyāt. aṣṭotta-raśataṃ padmaṃ śvetaṃ raktaṃ vā, alābhe bilvapatraṃ vā ghṛtāplutaṃ viṣṇugāyatryā juhuyāt. sarvadevatyaṃ pāramātmikaṃ ca juhuyāt. evaṃ mahāśāntiriti kecid vadanti.*
> 
> पश्चाद् अवताराणां तत्तन्मन्त्रं प्रत्येकम् अष्टोत्तरशतं हुत्वा नृत्त-गेय-वाद्यैः रात्रिशेषं नयेत्। प्रभाते स्नात्वा, देवं प्रणम्य, प्रणवेन बोधयित्वा, अभिवन्द्य, अपराधशतं सहस्रम् अयुतम् असङ्ख्येयं च "करुणाब्धे क्षमस्व" इति याचेत। पूर्ववस्त्र-माल्यादीनि व्यपोह्य अन्यैरलङ्कृत्य सर्ववाद्यघोषयुतं शाकुनसूक्तेन तोयधारापुरस्सरम् आचार्यः पुरतो गच्छेत्।
> *paścād avatārāṇāṃ tattanmantraṃ pratyekam aṣṭottaraśataṃ hutvā nṛtta-geya-vādyaiḥ rātriśeṣaṃ nayet. prabhāte snātvā, devaṃ praṇamya, praṇavena bodhayityā, abhivandya, aparādhaśataṃ sahasram ayutam asaṅkhyeyaṃ ca *"karuṇābdhe kṣamasva"* iti yāceta. pūrvavastra-mālyādīni vyapohya anyairalaṅkṛtya sarvavādyaghoṣayutaṃ śākunasūktena toyadhārāpurassaram ācāryaḥ purato gacchet.*
> 
> आचार्यः देवपादं स्पृशन् वैष्णवं, विष्णुसूक्तं, पुरुषसूक्तं, आत्मसूक्तं च जप्त्वा पूर्ववद् देवं ध्यात्वा ध्रुवबेराच्छक्तिं प्रणिधौ निवेशयेत्। हृदये समारोपिता चेत् हृदयात् प्रणिधिजले समावाह्य पूर्ववद् अक्षरन्यासं कृत्वा आवाह्य, तदालय-परिषद्देवान् पूर्ववद् आवाहयेत्। पादौ प्रक्षाल्य, अभ्यन्तरं प्रविश्य पुण्याहं वाचयेत्। आचार्यादिभ्यो दक्षिणां दद्यात्। अग्निं परिस्तीर्य अन्तर्होमं हुत्वा पश्चाद् उत्सवं स्नपनं च कारयेत्। अशक्तश्चेत् केवलस्नपनं कारयेत्।
> *ācāryaḥ devapādaṃ spṛśan vaiṣṇavaṃ, viṣṇusūktaṃ, puruṣasūktaṃ, ātmasūktaṃ ca japtvā pūrvavat devaṃ dhyātvā dhruvaberācchaktiṃ praṇidhau niveśayet. hṛdaye samāropitā cet hṛdayāt praṇidhijale samāvāhya pūrvavad akṣaranyāsaṃ kṛtvā āvāhya, tadālaya-pariṣaddevān pūrvavad āvāhayet. pādau prakṣālya, abhyantaraṃ praviśya puṇyāhaṃ vācayet. ācāryādibhyo dakṣiṇāṃ dadyāt. agniṃ paristīrya antarhomaṃ hutvā paścād utsavaṃ snapanaṃ ca kārayet. aśaktaścet kevalasnapanaṃ kārayet.*

#### English Translation
*   **Actions in Prosperous Times:** When peace returns and the danger passes (*sukāle*), the priest must unearth the buried icons (*bimbam-uddhṛtya*), thoroughly cleanse and purify them with tamarind and other sour agents (*āmlādibhiḥ saṃśodhya*), and perform a purificatory rite (*puṇyāhaṃ kṛtvā*).

*   **Expiation for Contact/Desecration:** If the icons were touched by unauthorized/impure persons during the crisis (*aspṛśyaiḥ spṛṣṭaṃ cet*), the priest must perform rituals like water-immersion (*jalādhivāsa*). Without delaying for astrological factors (*kālāpekṣām akṛtvā*), he should perform the seed-sowing ritual (*aṅkurārpaṇam*).

*   **Ritual Hall Construction:** Facing the temple or to its south, he should construct a temporary hall (*maṇḍapa*), dome (*kūṭa*), or shed (*prapā*). In the center of this hall, he should prepare the *Pauṇḍarīka* fire pit (*pauṇḍarīkāgnikuṇḍaṃ kṛtvā*), and to its north, perform the *Vāstu-homa* (propitiation of the spirit of the site). He should purify the icons by carrying fire around them (*paryagnikaraṇam*).

*   **Preparation of Altar and Reclining:** To the west of the *Pauṇḍarīka* fire, he should prepare a grain altar (*dhānyaiḥ vedim*) measuring slightly more than half the length of the icon, one-ninth high, or of any available height. He should spread five cloths starting with silk (*aṇḍajādīni pañcavastrāṇi āstīrya*), bathe the deities with fourteen water pots (*caturdaśa-kalaśaiḥ saṃsnāpya*), tie the protective wrist-thread (*pratisaraṃ badhvā*), and lay the *Kautuka*, *Snapana*, *Utsava*, *Bali* icons, and the Goddesses to sleep on a single couch (*saha ekaśayane śāyayet*). A separate couch is ordained for other divine incarnations (*avatāra*).

*   **Consecration Offerings:** Having spread grass around the fire (*agniṃ paristīrya*), sprinkled it with water (*pariṣicya*), and recited the hotṛ hymns (*hautraṃ praśaṃsya*), he should invoke the Lord of Devas with the Goddesses and the incarnations in the right water vessel (*dakṣiṇa-praṇidhau*), and the retinue deities of the temple (*tadālaya-pariṣaddevān*) in the left/northern vessel (*uttara-praṇidhau*). He should offer fire wood (*nirvāpam*) in the order of invocation and perform clarified butter offerings (*ājyāhutīḥ*).

*   **The Pacification Homa (Śānti-homa):** The priest should perform offerings using the *Vaiṣṇava* mantra, *Viṣṇu-sūkta*, *Puruṣa-sūkta*, *Viṣṇu-gāyatrī*, the twelve-syllable mantra (*dvādaśākṣara*), the *Prājāpatya* mantra, the *Minda* offering (*mindāhuti*), and the *Vicchinna* (for omissions). Some refer to this as the *Śānti-homa*.

*   **The Great Pacification after Six Months (Mahā-śānti):** If six months have elapsed while the icons were concealed, he must perform the *Mahā-śānti* homa. Using popped rice (*lāja*), sesame seeds (*tila*), and barley meal (*śaktu*) mixed with ghee, he should offer the *Vaiṣṇava* and *Viṣṇu-sūkta* mantras twelve or eight times each. He should offer 108 white or red lotus flowers, or if unavailable, Bilva leaves soaked in ghee, chanting the *Viṣṇu-gāyatrī*. He should also perform offerings to all deities (*sarva-devatya*) and the Supreme Self (*pāramātmika*).

*   **Awakening and Apology:** For other incarnations, he should offer 108 oblations with their respective mantras and spend the rest of the night with music, dance, and instrumentals (*nṛtta-geya-vādyaiḥ*). In the morning, having bathed, bowed to the Lord, awakened Him with the syllable *Om* (*praṇavena bodhayitvā*), and saluted Him, he should beg forgiveness for hundreds, thousands, tens of thousands, or countless offenses (*aparādhaśataṃ...*), praying: 
    > *"O Ocean of Compassion, forgive me!" (karuṇābdhe kṣamasva).*

*   **Procession and Re-entry:** Discarding the old garments and garlands (*pūrvavastra-mālyādīni vyapohya*) and adorning them with new ones (*anyair-alaṅkṛtya*), the priest should lead the procession with the sounding of all instruments, carrying a stream of water (*toyadhārā-purassaram*), chanting the *Śākuna-sūkta*.

*   **Restoration of Energy:** Touching the feet of the Lord (*devapādaṃ spṛśan*), chanting the *Vaiṣṇava*, *Viṣṇu-sūkta*, *Puruṣa-sūkta*, and *Ātma-sūkta*, and contemplating the Lord as before, the priest should transfer the divine power from the fixed icon into the water vessel (*praṇidhau*). If the power was transferred to his own heart, he should invoke it from his heart into the water vessel, perform the installation of sacred letters (*akṣaranyāsam*) as before, invoke the deity, and similarly invoke the retinue deities of the temple. Having washed the feet of the icons (*pādau prakṣālya*), entered the inner sanctum, and performed the purificatory ceremony (*puṇyāhaṃ vācayet*), he should distribute sacrificial fees (*dakṣiṇā*) to the priest and others. Finally, having spread grass around the fire and performed the internal offerings (*antarhoma*), he should perform the festival and consecration bath. If unable, he should perform the bath (*snapana*) alone.

### Section 4: Neglect and Longer Closures

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> कूर्चादावर्चनाहीने प्रायश्चित्तम्—
> अर्चनं हीनं चेत् अर्चनाहीन-प्रायश्चित्तं कारयेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> kūrcādāv-arcanāhīne prāyaścittam—
> arcanaṃ hīnaṃ cet arcanāhīna-prāyaścittaṃ kārayet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Neglect of the Kūrca:** If the daily worship of the representative grass bundle (*kūrca*) was neglected or omitted during the period of concealment, he must perform the specific expiatory rite for neglected worship (*arcanāhīna-prāyaścitta*).

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **वर्षेऽतीते प्रायश्चित्तम्**
> वर्षातीते च एतमेव संस्कारं कृत्वा पुनःस्थापनम् आचरेत्। अवताराणां पृथगेव कारयेद् इत्येके।
> तेषां पुनः प्रतिष्ठा चेत् तत्तत्प्रधानहोमं कारयेद् इत्येके।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **varṣe'tīte prāyaścittam**
> varṣātīte ca etameva saṃskāraṃ kṛtvā punaḥsthāpanaṃ ācaret. avatārāṇāṃ pṛthageva kārayet ityeke.
> teṣāṃ punaḥ pratiṣṭhā cet tattat-pradhānahomaṃ kārayet ityeke.

**Translation (English):**
*   **If More Than a Year Passes:** If more than a year passes while the icons remain buried, he must perform this same purificatory ceremony, followed by a formal re-consecration and re-installation (*punaḥ-sthāpanam*) of the deities. According to some authorities, this must be performed separately for the incarnations (*avatāra*). If they require complete re-consecration, their respective primary fire offerings (*pradhāna-homa*) should be performed.

#### Colophon
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> इति श्रीवैखानसे मरीचिप्रोक्ते विमानार्चनाकल्ये भृगूक्त-प्रायश्चित्तविधिर्नाम सप्ततितमः पटलः ॥७०॥

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> iti śrī-vaikhānase marīciprokte vimānārcanākalpe bhṛgūkta-prāyaścittavidhir-nāma saptatitamaḥ paṭalaḥ ||70||

**Translation (English):**
*   **Colophon:** Thus ends the Seventieth Chapter, titled *“The Expiatory Rites/Remedies as Declared by Bhrigu”* (Bhṛgūkta-prāyaścitta-vidhi), in the *Vimānārcanākalpa* of the *Vaikhānasa* school, compiled by Sage Marīci.

### Section 5: Glossary of Key Terms

1.  **Dhruva-bera (ध्रुवबेर):** The main, immovable deity icon of the temple, usually carved from stone and fixed permanently in the sanctum sanctorum (*garbhagṛha*). It holds the primary divine energy of the temple.
2.  **Kautuka, Snapana, Utsava, Bali-bera (कौतुक, स्नपन, उत्सव, बलिबेर):** The smaller, movable metal icons used for daily worship, baths, processions/festivals, and daily offerings respectively. These are the ones buried during crises.
3.  **Tirodhāna (तिरोधान):** The act of concealment, disappearance, or temporary removal of the divine presence/idols from public view and worship to safeguard them from harm.
4.  **Avaṭa (अवट):** A specially prepared ritual pit dug in a clean, concealed location where the icons are placed. It is layered with sand and sacred grass to serve as a soft, protective bed.
5.  **Kūrca (कूर्च):** A sacred bundle of grass (usually 15 blades of *Darbha* grass, measuring 12 fingers long) bound together. During the absence of the metal icons, the divine power is invoked into the *Kūrca* to allow the daily rituals to continue in the temple.
6.  **Bālālaya (बालालय):** A temporary shrine or structure built to house the deity's power/icons while the main temple undergoes renovation, or during emergencies.
7.  **Punaḥ-sthāpanam (पुनःस्थापन):** The formal, elaborate ritual of unearthing the hidden icons, purifying them, restoring their divine energy, and placing them back in their respective places within the temple for public worship.
