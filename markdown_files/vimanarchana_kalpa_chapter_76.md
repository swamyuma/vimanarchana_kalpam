# Vimanarchana Kalpa of Sage Marīci (Vaikhānasa Āgama)
## Chapter 76: Śānti-pañcaka-vidhiḥ (Rules for the Five Kinds of Pacification)

This document contains the Sanskrit text, IAST transliteration, and English translation of the 76th Chapter (*Ṣaṭsaptatitamaḥ Paṭalaḥ*) of the **Vimānārcanākalpa** (attributed to Sage Marīci, representing the Vaikhānasa school of Vaishnavism).

This chapter defines and outlines the procedures for the five-fold pacification system (*śānti-pañcakam*): Adbhuta-śānti (Pacification for Portents), Prabhūta-śānti (Abundant Pacification), Mahā-śānti (Great Pacification), Śuddhi (Purification), and Sarva-śānti (All-encompassing Pacification). It details the architectural construction of the sacrificial hall, the positioning of the fire pits (*kunda*), the ingredients for fire offerings, and special procedures for bringing rain during severe droughts (*anāvṛṣṭi*).

---

### Section 1: Classification of the Five Pacifications

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **अथ षट्सप्ततितमः पटलः**
> **शान्तिपञ्चक-विधिः**
> ***atha ṣaṭsaptatitamaḥ paṭalaḥ***
> ***śāntipañcaka-vidhiḥ***
> 
> अथ उत्तमोत्तमोत्तम-मध्यमोत्तम-मध्यम-अधम-अधमाधमानां यथाक्रमेण अद्भुतशान्तिः, प्रभूतशान्तिः, महाशान्तिः, शान्तिः, शुद्धिः इति शान्तिः पञ्चधा भवति ॥
> *atha uttamottamottama-madhyamottama-madhyama-adhama-adhamādhamānāṃ yathākrameṇa adbhutaśāntiḥ, prabhūtaśāntiḥ, mahāśāntiḥ, śāntiḥ, śuddhiḥ iti śāntiḥ pañcadhā bhavati ||*

#### English Translation
*   **The Five Pacifications:** According to the grades of highest of the high (*uttamottamottama*), moderately high (*madhyamottama*), medium (*madhyama*), low (*adhama*), and lowest of the low (*adhamādhama*), the pacification ritual is of five kinds:
    1.  **Adbhuta-śānti** (for celestial/severe portents),
    2.  **Prabhūta-śānti** (abundant/moderate pacification),
    3.  **Mahā-śānti** (great pacification),
    4.  **Śānti / Sarva-śānti** (standard/general pacification),
    5.  **Śuddhi** (purification/minor pacification).

### Section 2: Detailed Rules for Adbhuta-śānti

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> अथ अद्भुतशान्तिं वक्ष्ये — देवालय-अभिमुखे उत्तरे च ऐशान्यां वा, पर्वताग्रे वा, मण्डपं प्रपां कूटं वा, षोडशस्तम्भयुतं, चतुर्हस्त-प्रमाण-स्तम्भान्तरं, तालोन्नत-तलयुतं कृत्वा, मध्ये श्रामणक-अग्निकुण्डं कृत्वा, अधस्तात् द्वादशाङ्गुलं खनित्वा तत्र वेदिं द्विहस्त-आयाम-विस्तारं हस्तमात्र-समुत्सेधं स्थण्डिलं कृत्वा, प्रागादि-चतुर्दिक्षु आहवनीय-अन्वाहार्य-गार्हपत्य-आवसथ्य-कुण्डानि कृत्वा, आग्नेयादिषु कोणेषु औपासनाग्नि-कुण्डानि कृत्वा, द्वारेषु तोरण-पूरणकुम्भ-कदली-पताका-अङ्कुर-दर्भमूल-स्तम्भ-वेष्टनादिभिः अलङ्कृत्य, स्थण्डिले धान्यराशिं विकीर्य, तन्मध्ये तण्डुलेनाऽष्टदलपद्मं कृत्वा, तन्मध्ये स्वर्ण-रजत-ताम्र-मृत्तिकानाम् अन्यतमेन कृतं द्वात्रिंशत्-प्रस्थ-सम्पूरं कुम्भं यवान्तरं तन्तुना वेष्टितं वस्त्रयुग्मेन आवेष्ट्य, सौवर्णानि अङ्गुलादहीनानि अष्टमङ्गल-पात्र-आयुध-वर्णचिह्नानि रत्न-स्वर्ण-रजत-ताम्र-कांस्य-त्रपु-सीस-आयस-तीक्ष्णकान्तानि आदित्यादि-नवग्रहरूपाणि च निक्षिप्य, शुद्धजलेनापूर्य निधाय, कर्पूर-एला-लवङ्ग-चन्दन-उशीरादि-सर्वगन्धद्रव्याणि च अशोक-अश्वत्थ-पल्लवानि च निक्षिप्य, तन्मुखं तन्तुना वेष्टित-पिधानेन पिदध्यात्।
> *atha adbhutaśāntiṃ vakṣye — devālaya-abhimukhe uttare ca aiśānyāṃ vā, parvatāgre vā, maṇḍapaṃ prapāṃ kūṭaṃ vā, ṣodaśastambhayutaṃ, caturhasta-pramāṇa-stambhāntaraṃ, tālonnata-talayutaṃ kṛtvā, madhye śrāmaṇaka-agnikuṇḍaṃ kṛtvā, adhastāt dvādaśāṅgulaṃ khanitvā tatra vediṃ dvihasta-āyāma-vistāraṃ hastamātra-samutsedhaṃ sthaṇḍilaṃ kṛtvā, prāgādi-caturdikṣu āhavanīya-anvāhārya-gārhapatya-āvasathya-kuṇḍāni kṛtvā, āgneyādiṣu koṇeṣu aupāsanāgni-kuṇḍāni kṛtvā, dvāreṣu toraṇa-pūraṇakumbha-kadalī-patākā-aṅkura-darbhamūla-stambhaveṣṭanādibhir alaṅkṛtya, sthaṇḍile dhānyarāśiṃ vikīrya, tanmadhye taṇḍulenā'ṣṭadalapadmaṃ kṛtvā, tanmadhye svarṇa-rajata-tāmra-mṛttikānām anyatamena kṛtaṃ dvātriṃśat-prastha-sampūraṃ kumbhaṃ yavāntaraṃ tantunā veṣṭitaṃ vastrayugmena āveṣṭya, sauvarṇāni aṅgulādahīnāni aṣṭamaṅgala-pātra-āyudha-varṇacihnāni ratna-svarṇa-rajata-tāmra-kāṃsya-trapu-sīsa-āyasa-tīkṣṇakāntāni ādityādi-navagraharūpāṇi ca nixipya, śuddhajalenāpūrya nidhāya, karpūra-elā-lavaṅga-candana-uśīrādi-sarvagandhadravyāni ca aśoka-aśvattha-pallavāni ca nixipya, tanmukhaṃ tantunā veṣṭita-pidhānena pidadhyāt ||*

#### English Translation
*   **The Hall Setup:** Now, I shall declare the rules for Adbhuta-śānti. Facing the temple, to the north, or in the north-east (*aiśānyāṃ vā*), or on a mountain top, having built a pavilion, hall, or shed with sixteen pillars, with a distance of four cubits between pillars, as high as a palm tree with a roof,

*   **The Fire Altar:** In the center, having made the Shramanaka fire pit (*śrāmaṇaka-agnikuṇḍaṃ*), dug twelve fingers deep below it, made a platform there measuring two cubits in length and width and one cubit in height (*sthāṇḍilaṃ*),

*   **Fire Pits Layout:** One should make the Āhavanīya, Anvāhārya, Gārhapatya, and Āvasathya fire pits in the four directions starting with the east, and the domestic/auxiliary fire pits (*aupāsanāgni-kuṇḍāni*) in the corners starting with the south-east.

*   **Decorations and Grains:** Having decorated the doorways with arches, filled water vessels, plantain trees, flags, sprouts, Darbha grass bindings around pillars, scattered a heap of grains (*dhānyarāśiṃ*) on the altar, and drawn an eight-petalled lotus with rice grains (*taṇḍulenā'ṣṭadalapadmaṃ*) in the middle,

*   **Preparing the Kumbha Vessel:** One should place a kumbha vessel made of gold, silver, copper, or clay, of thirty-two prastha capacity, wrapped with thread at intervals of a barley grain (*yavāntaraṃ tantunā veṣṭitaṃ*), and covered with a pair of cloths.

*   **Ritual Contents:** Having placed inside it golden symbols not less than one finger representing the eight auspicious signs, vessels, weapons, and colors; gems, gold, silver, copper, bell-metal, tin, lead, iron, magnet; and forms of the nine planets starting with the Sun (*ādityādi-navagraha-rūpāṇi*); filled it with pure water; placed camphor, cardamom, cloves, sandalwood, vetiver, and all other aromatic substances, along with leaves of Ashoka and Ashvattha; one should cover the mouth of the vessel with a lid wrapped in thread.

### Section 3: Performing the Fire Sacrifice

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> आचार्यम् ऋत्विजश्च वरयित्वा नववस्त्र-उत्तरीयाङ्गुलीयकैः अलङ्कृत्य, अग्निकुण्डेषु आघारं हुत्वा, आदित्य-अङ्गारक-मन्द-बुध-सित-बृहस्पति-चन्द्र-इन्द्र-यमान् प्रागाद्युदगन्तं प्रदक्षिणं ध्यात्वा आवाह्य अभ्यर्च्य, अष्टाक्षरेण अयुतम् अष्टोत्तरसहस्रं वा अभिमन्त्र्य, गृह्य-प्रोक्त-विधानेन तत्तद्देवत्य-चरु-समिद्-आज्यादिभिः नित्यम् अष्टोत्तरसहस्रं हुत्वा, तत्र श्रामणकाग्नौ बिल्वपत्र-घृताप्लुतं नित्यम् अष्टोत्तरसहस्रम् अष्टाक्षरेण हुत्वा, विष्णुगायत्र्या बिल्व-समिद्-आज्य-चरु-स-लाजा-अपूप-व्रीहि-सिद्धार्थ-दूर्वा-पयसैः प्रत्येकम् आचार्यो हुत्वा, आज्येना अष्टोत्तरसहस्रम् अष्टाक्षरेण हुत्वा, आज्येन वैष्णवं विष्णुसूक्तं पुरुषसूक्तं च हुत्वा, नृत्त-गेय-वादैः आघोष्य, चतुर्वेदान् यथादिशम् अध्याप्य, नित्यं ऋत्विग्भ्यः दक्षिणां दद्यात्। एवं सप्ताहम्।
> *ācāryam ṛtvijaśca varayitvā navavastra-uttarīyāṅgulīyakair alaṅkṛtya, agnikuṇḍeṣu āghāraṃ hutvā, āditya-aṅgāraka-manda-budha-sita-bṛhaspati-candra-indra-yamān prāgādyudagantaṃ pradaxiṇaṃ dhyātvā āvāhya abhyarcya, aṣṭākṣareṇa ayutam aṣṭottarasahasraṃ vā abhimantrya, gṛhyaprokta-vidhānena tattaddevatya-caru-samid-ājyādibhiḥ nityam aṣṭottarasahasraṃ hutvā, tatra śrāmaṇakāgnau bilvapatra-ghṛtāplutaṃ nityam aṣṭottarasahasram aṣṭākṣareṇa hutvā, viṣṇugāyatryā bilva-samid-ājya-caru-sa-lājā-apūpa-vrīhi-siddhārtha-dūrvā-payasaiḥ pratyekam ācāryo hutvā, ājyena aṣṭottarasahasram aṣṭākṣareṇa hutvā, ājyena vaiṣṇavaṃ viṣṇusūktaṃ puruṣasūktaṃ ca hutvā, nṛtta-geya-vādair āghoṣya, caturvedān yathādiśam adhyāpya, nityaṃ ṛtvigbhyaḥ dakṣiṇāṃ dadyāt | evaṃ saptāham ||*
> 
> यदेवालये दोषश्चेत् कुमारे (or कुमारे स्थण्डिले?) तं देवं संस्थाप्य, अभ्यज्य, हविर्निविद्य, देवसन्निधौ होमं हुत्वा, अन्ते नवहं सप्ताहं वा उत्सवं कारयेत्। दिव्यान्तरिक्षयोः कुम्भजलेन राजानम् अभिषेचयेत्। भौमेन तं देवं संप्रोक्ष्य, अवशिष्टजलेन राजानं राजपत्नीं च अभिषेचयेत्। सुवर्ण-पशु-भूम्यादिभिः आचार्यं सम्पूज्य, पश्चात् विप्रेभ्यो दक्षिणां दद्यात्। राजगृहे चेत् तत्समीपे मण्डपं कृत्वा, पूर्ववद् गृहशान्तिं श्रामणकाग्निहोमं कुम्भसाधनं च कृत्वा, सप्ताहान्ते राजानं कुम्भजलेन अभिषेचयेत्।
> *yadevālaye doṣaścet kumāre (sthaṇḍile) taṃ devaṃ saṃsthāpya, abhyajya, havir nivedya, devasannidhau homaṃ hutvā, ante navāhaṃ saptāhaṃ vā utsavaṃ kārayet | divyāntarikṣayoḥ kumbhajalena rājānam abhiṣecayet | bhaumena taṃ devaṃ saṃproxya, avaśiṣṭajalena rājānaṃ rājapatnīṃ ca abhiṣecayet | svarṇa-paśu-bhūmyādibhir ācāryaṃ sampūjya, paścād viprebhyo dakṣiṇāṃ dadyāt | rājagṛhe cet tatsamīpe maṇḍapaṃ kṛtvā, pūrvavad gṛhaśāntiṃ śrāmaṇakāgnihomaṃ kumbhasādhanaṃ ca kṛtvā, saptāhante rājānaṃ kumbhajalena abhiṣecayet ||*
> 
> आचार्यादिभ्यः प्रत्येकं यथोक्तदक्षिणां दत्त्वा, अङ्कुरं रोपयित्वा, शुभे अनुकूले नक्षत्रे अभिषेकं कृत्वा, गृहप्रवेशं च कुर्यात्।
> *ācāryādibhyaḥ pratyekaṃ yathoktadakṣiṇāṃ dattva, aṅkuraṃ ropayitvā, śubhe anukūle naxatre abhiṣekaṃ kṛtvā, gṛhapraveśaṃ ca kuryāt ||*
> 
> सर्वग्रासोपरागादि-दिव्याद्भुत-शान्त्यर्थं ब्राह्मणेभ्यः शक्त्यनुकूलं स्वर्ण-पशु-भूम्यादीनि दद्याद् इति विज्ञायते ॥
> *sarvagrāsoparāgādi-divyādbhuta-śāntyarthaṃ brāhmaṇebhyaḥ śaktyanukūlaṃ svarṇa-paśu-bhūmyādīni dadyād iti vijñāyate ||*

#### English Translation
*   **The Ritualists and Invocations:** Having selected the chief priest and assistant priests, decorated them with new garments, upper cloths, and rings, offered the initial ghee libations in the fire pits, meditated upon the Sun, Mars, Saturn, Mercury, Venus, Jupiter, the Moon, Indra, and Yama from east to north clockwise, invoked and worshipped them, and consecrated the setup with ten thousand or one thousand and eight recitations of the eight-syllable mantra (*aṣṭākṣareṇa*),

*   **Daily Fire Offerings:** According to the Grihya rules, one should offer one thousand and eight oblations daily with boiled rice, fuel sticks, and ghee for those respective deities; offer in the Shramanaka fire one thousand and eight Bilva leaves soaked in ghee with the eight-syllable mantra; and the Acharya should offer bilva, fuel sticks, ghee, boiled rice, parched grains, cakes, paddy, mustard seed, Durva grass, and milk-rice individually with the Viṣṇu-gayatri. Having offered ghee libations one thousand and eight times with the eight-syllable mantra, and ghee oblations with the Vaishnava, Viṣṇu-sūkta, and Puruṣa-sūkta hymns, made proclamations with dance, song, and instruments, and had the four Vedas recited in their respective directions, one should give daily dakshina to the priests. This continues for seven days (*evaṃ saptāham*).

*   **Atones in Specific Locations:** If the defect is in a temple, having placed the deity on the platform of the Kumara/subsidiary shrine, anointed him, offered food, performed fire offerings in the presence of the deity, one should conduct a festival for seven or nine days at the end.

*   **Royal Bath:** For celestial and atmospheric portents, one should consecrate the king with the water of the kumbha. For terrestrial portents, having sprinkled that deity, one should consecrate the king and the queen with the remaining water. Having worshipped the Acharya with gold, cows, land, etc., one should subsequently give dakshina to the Brahmins.

*   **Palace Portents:** If the portent occurs in the royal palace, having constructed a pavilion near it, performed the domestic pacification, Shramanaka fire sacrifice, and kumbha preparation as before, one should consecrate the king with the kumbha water at the end of seven days.

*   **Re-entry:** Having given the prescribed dakshina individually to the Acharya and others, sown sprouts, performed the anointing ceremony under an auspicious and favorable constellation, one should conduct the palace re-entry (*gṛhapraveśaṃ*).

*   **Alms for Eclipses:** To pacify celestial portents like total eclipses, one should give gold, cows, land, etc. to the Brahmins according to one's capacity.

### Section 4: Expiation for Droughts

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **अनावृष्टौ कर्तव्यम्**
> अनावृष्टि-दोषश्चेत् शीतकुम्भं निदध्यात्। द्वारात् परतः सम्पूरण-कर्म सङ्गा पूर्ववत् साधयित्वा, सर्वतीर्थम् आवाह्य, अभ्यर्च्य, देवाभिमुखे धान्यपीठे सौवर्णम् अष्टदलयुतं पद्मं, हेमपात्रे न्यस्य तस्मिन् देवेशम् आवाह्य अभ्यर्च्य, तस्यैव चतुष्पार्श्वेषु कुम्भान् संन्यस्य, कुम्भमुखे सूचीमात्र-छिद्रेण अविच्छिन्नधारया देवेशं सेचयेत्।
> ***anāvṛṣṭou kartavyam***
> *anāvṛṣṭi-doṣaścet śītakumbhaṃ nidadhyāt | dvārāt parataḥ sampūraṇa-karma saṅgā pūrvavat sādhayitvā, sarvatīrtham āvāhya, abhyarcya, devābhimukhe dhānayapīṭhe sauvarṇam aṣṭadalayutaṃ padmaṃ, hemapātre nyasya tasmin deveśam āvāhya abhyarcya, tasyaiva catuṣpārśveṣu kumbhān saṃnyasya, kumbhamukhe sūcīmātra-chidreṇa avicchinnadhārayā deveśaṃ secayet ||*
> 
> वेतस-समिद्भिः पलाशैः वा, पञ्चवारुणम् अष्टोत्तर-सहस्रं श्रामणकाग्नौ हुत्वा, दिशा-होमेषु (अष्टसु प्रत्येकं) अष्टोत्तरसहस्रं पञ्चवारुणं बेतस-समिद्भिः जुहुयात्। आज्येना बेतस-समिद्भिश्च प्रत्येकम् "इन्द्रं प्रणवन्तम्" (or "इन्द्रं प्रणववन्तम्") इत्यष्टाक्षरेण (or इत्यृचा?) सहस्रं जुहुयात्। श्रामणकाग्नाव् एकमेव वा कुर्यात्, तद् दक्षिणे वारुणरूपाणि आवाह्य अभ्यर्च्य "शची सहाया" इति होतव्यम्। देवं च विशेषतोऽभ्यर्च्य, स्नपन-उत्सवादीनि कारयेत्।
> *vetasa-samidbhiḥ palāśair vā, pañcavāruṇam aṣṭottara-sahasraṃ śrāmaṇakāgnau hutvā, diśā-homeṣu (aṣṭasu pratyekaṃ) aṣṭottarasahasraṃ pañcavāruṇaṃ vetasa-samidbhiḥ juhuyāt | ājyena vetasa-samidbhiśca pratyekam "indraṃ praṇavantam" ity aṣṭākṣareṇa sahasraṃ juhuyāt | śrāmaṇakāgnāv ekameva vā kuryāt, tad dakṣiṇe vāruṇarūpāṇi āvāhya abhyarcya "śacī sahāyā" iti hotavyam | devaṃ ca viśeṣato'bhyarcya, snapana-utsavādīni kārayet ||*
> 
> एवम् एव तत्तत् कर्मगौरवं ज्ञात्वा त्र्यहादि विंशतिदिनान्तं श्रामणकाग्निं सङ्कल्प्य दिने दिने कुम्भं पूरयित्वा सेचयेत्। तद् दिनान्ते देवस्य स्नपनं कृत्वा अभ्यज्य हविर्निवेदयेत्। आचार्यादिभ्यः दक्षिणां दद्यात्। तत्तत् कर्म ततः समग्रः स्यात्। तस्माद् यत्नतः कुर्यात् ॥
> *evam eva tattat karmagauravaṃ jñātvā tryahādi viṃśatidināntaṃ śrāmaṇakāgniṃ saṅkalpya dine dine kumbhaṃ pūrayitvā secayet | tad dinānte devasya snapanaṃ kṛtvā abhyajya havir nivedayet | ācāryādibhyaḥ dakṣiṇāṃ dadyāt | tattat karma tataḥ samagraḥ syāt | tasmād yatnataḥ kuryāt ||*

#### English Translation
*   **The Cooling Vessel (Śīta-kumbha):** If there is a defect of drought (*anāvṛṣṭi-doṣaścet*), one should set up a cooling vessel (*śīta-kumbhaṃ*). Having performed the filling ceremony outside the doorway as before, invoked all sacred waters (*sarva-tīrtham āvāhya*), and worshipped them,

*   **Continuous Bathing (Dhrārā-abhiṣeka):** One should place a golden eight-petalled lotus in a gold vessel on a heap of grains facing the deity, invoke and worship the Lord of gods (*deveśam*) therein, place four water vessels in the four directions around it, and bathe the Lord of gods with a continuous stream of water (*avicchinna-dhārayā*) through a pinhole (*sūcīmātra-chidreṇa*) in the lid of the vessels.

*   **Fire Offerings for Rain:** One should offer one thousand and eight oblations in the Shramanaka fire with Vetasa (willow) or Palash fuel sticks chanting the five Varuṇa hymns (*pañca-vāruṇam*), and offer one thousand and eight oblations with Vetasa sticks in the fire offerings of the eight directions. He should offer one thousand ghee and Vetasa fuel stick oblations chanting the hymn *"Indraṃ praṇavantam"*. Alternatively, one can perform this only in the Shramanaka fire; to its south, having invoked and worshipped the forms of Varuṇa, he should make offerings chanting *"Śacī sahāyā"*.

*   **Duration:** Having worshipped the deity specially, one should conduct bathing ceremonies and festivals. Knowing the severity of the situation, one should resolve to maintain the Shramanaka fire from three days to twenty days, fill the kumbha, and bathe the deity day by day. At the end of that period, having bathed the deity, anointed him, offered food, and given dakshina to the priests, the ritual is completed.

### Section 5: Alternative Method with Milk Bath

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **कल्पान्तरम् — क्षीराभिषेक-विधिः**

> ***kalpāntaram — kṣīrābhiṣekavidhiḥ***
> *athavā varṣārthī, darbha-kṛśādi-sarvopadrava-śāntyarthaṃ go-tara-dadhi-kāmī ca, snapanālayaṃ pūrvavad alaṅkṛtya, tattat karmagauravaṃ jñātvā, yatnato bahuxīraṃ saṅgṛhya, ācāryaṃ vastra-uttarīyādyaiḥ pūjayitvā, so'pi śvabhrārcāyāṃ taṇḍulair dakṣiṇa-uttara-āyatāṃ paṅktiṃ kṛtvā, śvabhra-dakṣiṇasyāṃ śrāmaṇaka-agnikuṇḍaṃ kṛtvā, agnim ādhāya, navakumbhaṃ tantunā pariveṣṭya, praxālya, kuśodakena utpūya, utpūtena xīreṇa āpūrya, tad paṅktyāṃ nidhāya, pidhānaiḥ pidhāya, adhidevam ārādhya, upari navavastreṇa āchādayet ||*
>

> *śvabhrapūjānte devaṃ praṇamya, svastisūktena ānamya, ānīya śvabhramadhye pratiṣṭhāpya arcayet ||*
> 
> तत-नवेक्षित-कलश-क्षीरं गृत्वा, विष्णुसूक्त-पुरुषसूक्त-वारुणकादिभिः मन्त्रात् क्षीरधारया देवं संस्नापयेत्। अग्निं परिषिच्य, विष्णोरुक्थैः "अतो देवा" चैः (अतो देवा अवन्तु नः) मन्त्रेण अष्टाक्षरेण विष्णुगायत्र्या च अष्टोत्तर-सहस्रं जुहुयात्।
> *tata-navexita-kalaśa-xīraṃ gṛtvā, viṣṇusūkta-puruṣasūkta-vāruṇakādibhir mantrāt xīradhārayā devaṃ saṃsnāpayet | agniṃ pariṣicya, viṣṇorukthaiḥ "ato devā" caiḥ (ato devā avantu naḥ) mantreṇa aṣṭākṣareṇa viṣṇugāyatryā ca aṣṭottarasahasraṃ juhuyāt ||*
> 
> **कल्पान्तरम्**
> अथवा यावत् स्नपनं तावद् द्रोण-चूर्णेन उद्वर्त्य, अद्भ्यः संशोध्य, गन्धोदकैः संस्नप्य, प्लोतेन प्रोञ्छ्य, वस्त्र-उत्तरीयाद्यैः अलङ्कृत्य, अभ्यर्च्य, पायसादि-हविर्निवेदयेत्। आचार्यादिभ्यो दक्षिणां दद्यात्। तत्तत् कर्म सम्पूरणम् असंशयं लभेत।
> ***kalpāntaram***
> *athavā yāvat snapanaṃ tāvad droṇa-cūrṇena udvartya, adbhyaḥ saṃśodhya, gandhodakaiḥ saṃsnapya, plotena proñchya, vastra-uttarīyādyair alaṅkṛtya, abhyarcya, pāyasādi-havirnivedayet | ācāryādibhyo dakṣiṇāṃ dadyāt | tattat karma sampūraṇam asaṃśayaṃ labheta ||*
>

> ध्रुवबेर-अर्चनं चेत्, मुखमण्डपे च एवम् एव संसाध्य आनीय, तत्रैव स्नपनं कुर्यात्। तत्तत् कर्मानुरूपम्, एतेन विधिना द्व्यहं, त्र्यहं वा, क्षीरस्नानं कारयेत्। एवं घृताभिषेकं वा कारयेद् इति विज्ञायते ॥
> ***dhruvaberārcanaṃ cet kartavyam***
> *dhruvabera-arcanaṃ cet, mukhamaṇḍape ca evam eva saṃsādhya ānīya, tatraiva snapanaṃ kuryāt | tattat karmānurūpam, etena vidhinā dvyahaṃ, tryahaṃ vā, xīrasnānaṃ kārayet | evaṃ ghṛtābhiṣekaṃ vā kārayed iti vijñāyate ||*

#### English Translation
*   **Milk Offering for Rain:** Alternatively, one who desires rain (*varṣārthī*), or who wishes to pacify all obstacles like disease, or who desires cattle, curd, etc.:

*   **Setup of Milk Vessels:** Having decorated the bathing hall as before, collected plenty of milk (*bahuxīraṃ*), worshipped the Acharya with garments and upper cloths, made a north-south row of rice grains on the altar, established the Shramanaka fire pit to the south of the altar, invoked the fire, wrapped new pots with threads, washed them, purified them with Durva water, filled them with the purified milk, placed them in the row, covered them with lids, worshipped the presiding deity, and covered them with a new cloth.

*   **The Bath:** Bowing to the deity, chanting the Svasti-sūkta, bringing the deity onto the altar, one should bathe the deity with streams of milk (*xīradhārayā*) from the new vessels, chanting the Viṣṇu-sūkta, Puruṣa-sūkta, and Vāruṇa-sūktas.

*   **Fire Offerings:** Having sprinkled water around the fire, he should offer one thousand and eight oblations chanting the Viṣṇor-uktha, *"Ato devā avantu naḥ"*, the eight-syllable mantra, and the Viṣṇu-gāyatrī.

*   **Alternate Powder Bath:** Alternatively, throughout the bathing, having rubbed the deity with Drona powder (*droṇa-cūrṇena*), washed it with water, bathed it with scented water, dried it with a clean cloth, decorated it with garments and upper cloths, worshipped it, one should offer milk-rice (*pāyasa*) and other food. The sacrificial fees should be given to the priests, and the ritual will bear full fruit without doubt (*asaṃśayaṃ*).

*   **Fixed Deity Bath:** If it is performed for the main fixed icon (*dhruva-bera*), having prepared the setup in the porch (*mukha-maṇḍapa*), one should conduct the bath there. According to the gravity of the ritual, one should conduct the milk bath (*kṣīra-snānaṃ*) for two or three days. In the same manner, one can also perform the ghee bath (*ghṛtābhiṣekaṃ*).

### Section 6: Rules for Prabhūta-śānti

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **प्रभूतशान्ति-विधिः**
> अथ प्रभूतशान्तिं वक्ष्ये —
> पूर्ववन् मण्डपादीनि कृत्वा, दिक्-होमं बिना श्रामणकाग्नावेव यथोक्तं हुत्वा, पूर्ववत् कुम्भसाधनं कृत्वा, सप्ताहान्ते अष्टोत्तरशतम् अष्टचत्वारिंशद् वा कलशान् अभ्यर्च्य, अभिषिच्य, देवे विशेषतोऽभ्यर्च्य निवेदयेद् इति विज्ञायते ॥
> ***prabhūtaśāntividhiḥ***
> *atha prabhūtaśāntiṃ vakṣye —*
> *pūrvavan maṇḍapādīni kṛtvā, dik-homaṃ binā śrāmaṇakāgnāveva yathoktaṃ hutvā, pūrvavat kumbhasādhanaṃ kṛtvā, saptāhante aṣṭottaraśatam aṣṭacatvāriṃśad vā kalaśān abhyarcya, abhiṣicya, deve viśeṣato'byarcya nivedayed iti vijñāyate ||*

#### English Translation
*   **Abundant Pacification (Prabhūta-śānti):** Now, I shall declare Prabhūta-śānti. Having constructed the pavilions etc. as before, offered the prescribed oblations only in the Shramanaka fire without the directional fire-offerings (*dik-homaṃ binā*), and prepared the kumbha vessels as before,

*   **Conclucion:** At the end of seven days, having worshipped and bathed the deity with one hundred and eight or forty-eight water pots, and worshipped the deity specially, one should offer the food.

*   **Feast:** Having given dakshina to the Acharya, one should feed the Brahmins. One should perform the pot consecration (*ghaṭa-saṃskāraṃ*) in all cases.

## Glossary of Key Terms

1.  **Śānti-pañcakam (शान्तिपञ्चकम्):** The five classes of pacification fire sacrifices: Adbhuta, Prabhūta, Mahā, Śānti/Sarva-śānti, and Śuddhi.
2.  **Śrāmaṇaka-agni (श्रामणकाग्नि):** A primary, consecrated fire of the Vaikhānasa system, particularly used in major purificatory sacrifices.
3.  **Sthaṇḍila (स्थण्डिल):** A raised platform or sacrificial altar prepared with sand, grains, and diagrams.
4.  **Dvādaśākṣara-mantra (द्वादशाक्षरमन्त्र):** The sacred twelve-syllable mantra of Viṣṇu (*Oṃ Namo Bhagavate Vāsudevāya*).
5.  **Aṣṭākṣara-mantra (अष्टाक्षरमन्त्र):** The sacred eight-syllable mantra of Viṣṇu (*Oṃ Namo Nārāyaṇāya*).
6.  **Śīta-kumbha (शीतकुम्भ):** A specialized "cooling" water vessel setup used in rites to bring rain during droughts.
7.  **Dhārā-abhiṣeka (धाराभिषेक):** The ritual of continuously pouring water over the deity through a pinhole in the vessel lid.
8.  **Droṇa-cūrṇa (द्रोणचूर्ण):** A medicinal/purificatory powder of standard measure used to cleanse and scrub the deity icon.
9.  **Kṣīrābhiṣeka (क्षीराभिषेक):** The bathing of the deity icon with milk, performed for bringing rain or prosperity.
10. **Apamṛtyu (अपमृत्यु):** Untimely or accidental death, pacified by the Sarva-śānti ritual.
