# Vimanarchana Kalpa of Sage Marīci (Vaikhānasa Āgama)
## Chapter 6: Prathama-Śilā-Iṣṭakā-Vinyāsa-Vidhiḥ (Procedure for Laying the First Foundation Stones or Bricks)

This document contains the Sanskrit text, IAST transliteration, and English translation of the 6th Chapter (*Ṣaṣṭhaḥ Paṭalaḥ*) of the **Vimānārcanākalpa** (attributed to Sage Marīci, representing the Vaikhānasa school of Vaishnavism).

This chapter outlines the astrological and technical guidelines for laying the first foundation stones (*prathama-śilā*) or bricks (*prathama-iṣṭakā*). It includes determining auspicious months and Nakshatras, erecting the gnomon (*śaṅku*) to determine directions using solar shadows (including declination corrections for each zodiac sign), digging and compacting the foundation, the dimensions of the foundation stones, their consecration in a temporary pavilion (*prapā*), fire sacrifices (*homa*) aligned with the four Vedas, and finally, the layout and placement of the four primary stones in the cardinal directions of the sanctum.

---

### Section 1: Astrological Timing and Gnomon Setup

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **अथ षष्ठः पटलः**
> **प्रशस्तकालनिरूपणम्**
> ***atha ṣaṣṭhaḥ paṭalaḥ***
> ***praśastakālanirūpaṇam***
> 
> अतः ऊर्ध्वं प्रथम-शिला-इष्टका-आदि-विन्यासविधिं वक्ष्ये।
> मासेषु फाल्गुन-चैत्र-वैशाख-तैष्य-ज्येष्ठेषु उत्तमं; श्रावण-आषाढ-कार्तिक-आश्वयुजेषु मध्यमं; शेषेषु अधमम्।
> पूर्वपक्षे, कृष्णपक्षे अन्त्यत्रिभागं हित्वा वा, श्रवण-रोहिणी-उत्तर-हस्त-स्वाति-पुनर्वसु-वारुण-मैत्रेषु एकस्मिन् यजमान-अनुकूल-ऋक्षे, स्थिरराशौ अनिन्दिते च आरभेत।
> *ataḥ ūrdhvaṃ prathama-śilā-iṣṭakā-ādi-vinyāsavidhiṃ vakṣye.*
> *māseṣu phālguna-caitra-vaiśākha-taiṣya-jyeṣṭheṣu uttamaṃ; śrāvaṇa-āṣāḍha-kārtika-āśvayujeṣu madhyamaṃ; śeṣeṣu adhamam.*
> *pūrvapakṣe, kṛṣṇapakṣe antyatribhāgaṃ hitvā vā, śravaṇa-rohiṇī-uttara-hasta-svāti-punarvasu-vāruṇa-maitreṣu ekasmin yajamāna-anukūla-ṛkṣe, sthirarāśau anindite ca ārabhet.*
> 
> **शङ्कुस्थापनम्**
> कृष्टभूमिमध्ये चतुर्दिशं चतुर्हस्तं जलेन समं कृत्वा, तन्मध्ये शङ्कुं संस्थापयेत्। शङ्कुवृक्षान् सारवत्तरान् याज्ञिकान् वा आहरेत्; मानाङ्गुलेन अष्टाङ्गुलायतं द्वादशाङ्गुलायतं वा, सुवृत्तं निर्व्रणम् आयामसदृश-मूलनाहं छत्राकारं शङ्कुं कृत्वा, उदयात् पूर्वं तन्मध्ये शङ्कु-द्विगुण-मानेन मण्डलम् आलिख्य, तन्मध्ये शङ्कुं संस्थापयेत्।
> ***śaṅkusthāpanam***
> *kṛṣṭabhūmimadhye caturdiśaṃ caturhastaṃ jalena samaṃ kṛtvā, tanmadhye śaṅkuṃ saṃsthāpayet. śaṅkuvṛkṣān sāravattarān yājñikān vā āharet; mānāṅguleṇa aṣṭāṅgulāyataṃ dvādaśāṅgulāyataṃ vā, suvṛttaṃ nirvraṇam āyāmasadṛśa-mūlanāhaṃ chatrākāraṃ śaṅkuṃ kṛtvā, udayāt pūrvaṃ tanmadhye śaṅku-dviguṇa-mānena maṇḍalam ālikhya, tanmadhye śaṅkuṃ saṃsthāpayet.*

#### English Translation
*   **Auspicious Time Determination (*Praśasta-kāla-nirūpaṇam*):**
    *   Hereafter, I shall describe the procedure for laying the first stones or bricks (*prathama-śilā-iṣṭakā-ādi-vinyāsa-vidhiḥ*).
    *   Among the months, commencing in Phālguna, Caitra, Vaiśākha, Taiṣya (Pauṣa), or Jyeṣṭha is best (*uttama*). Commencing in Śrāvaṇa, Āṣāḍha, Kārtika, or Āśvayuja (Āśvina) is middling (*madhyama*). Commencing in the remaining months is inferior (*adhama*).
    *   It should be commenced during the bright fortnight (*pūrva-pakṣe*), or during the dark fortnight (*kṛṣṇa-pakṣe*) omitting the last third part (the last five days).
    *   It should be done under one of the following asterisms: Śravaṇa, Rohiṇī, the three Uttarās (Uttara-phālguni, Uttarāṣāḍhā, Uttarabhādrapadā), Hasta, Svātī, Punarvasu, Vāruṇa (Śatabhiṣaj), or Maitra (Anurādhā), choosing one that is favorable to the patron (*yajamāna-anukūla-ṛkṣa*).
    *   It should begin when a fixed zodiac sign (*sthira-rāśi*) is rising, during an auspicious and blameless hour (*anindita*).

*   **Erection of the Gnomon (*Śaṅku-sthāpanam*):**
    *   In the center of the ploughed ground, level a square plot of four cubits in all directions using water. In the center thereof, erect the gnomon (*śaṅku*).
    *   One should procure wood for the gnomon from trees that are strong/resilient (*sāravat*) or sacrificial (*yājñika*).
    *   Using standard angula units (*mānāṅgula*), make the gnomon eight or twelve angulas long (*āyata*). It should be perfectly round (*suvṛtta*), free from blemishes (*nirvraṇa*), with a base circumference equal to its height (*āyāma-sadṛśa-mūla-nāha*), and shaped like an umbrella (*chatrākāra*) at the top.
    *   Before sunrise, having drawn a circle (*maṇḍala*) in that center with a radius equal to twice the height of the gnomon, one should establish the gnomon exactly in the center.

### Section 2: Determining Directions and Foundation Digging

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **दिङ्निर्णयः**
> पूर्वाह्णाऽपराह्नयोः छाया यदि तन्मण्डलान्तगा, तद्विन्दुद्वयगतं सूत्रं प्रसारयेत्; तत् पूर्वापरदिक्। उक्तच्छायां दक्षिणतो वामे च निदध्यात्। स शङ्कुः [निखातः / द्वादशाङ्गुलो] वा, तस्मात् षण्णवतिभागं कृत्वा एकांशम् अङ्गुलं, तदङ्गुलेनाऽवछायाः प्रतिमासं विशोधयेत् - मेषे मिथुने सिंहे तुलायां च रवौ स्थिते द्व्यङ्गुलं, कुलीरे वृश्चिके मीने चतुरङ्गुलं, धनुषि कुम्भे षडङ्गुलं, मकरे अष्टाङ्गुलं नयेत्; वृषभे कन्यायां न अवछाया भवेत्। मासि मासि उक्तम् अङ्गुलं त्रिंशद्भागं कृत्वा दिने दिने च एकैकं भागं युक्त्या शोधयेत्। तस्मिन् पुनर्बिन्दुद्वयं कृत्वा तयोर्मध्यं पूर्वापरसूत्रं प्रसार्य, दक्षिणोत्तरं तद्विन्दुद्वयेन मत्स्यम् उत्पाद्य तन्मुखपुच्छविनिर्गतं सूत्रं प्रसारयेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **diṅnirṇayaḥ**
> pūrvāhṇāparāhnayoḥ chāyā yadi tanmaṇḍalāntagā, tadvindudvayagataṃ sūtraṃ prasārayet; tat pūrvāparadik. uktacchāyāṃ dakṣiṇato vāme ca nidadhyāt. sa śaṅkuḥ [nikhātaḥ / dvādaśāṅgulo] vā, tasmāt ṣaṇṇavatibhāgaṃ kṛtvā ekāṃśam aṅgulaṃ, tadaṅgulena avachāyāḥ pratimāsaṃ viśodhayet - meṣe mithune siṃhe tulāyāṃ ca ravau sthite dvy-aṅgulaṃ, kulīre vṛścike mīne catur-aṅgulaṃ, dhanuṣi kumbhe ṣaḍ-aṅgulaṃ, makare aṣṭāṅgulaṃ nayet; vṛṣabhe kanyāyāṃ na avachāyā bhavet. māsi māsi uktam aṅgulaṃ triṃśadbhāgaṃ kṛtvā dine dine ca ekaikaṃ bhāgaṃ yuktyā śodhayet. tasmin punarbindudvayaṃ kṛtvā tayormadhyaṃ pūrvāparasūtraṃ prasārya, dakṣiṇottaraṃ tadvindudvayena matsyam utpādya tanmukhapucchavinirgataṃ sūtraṃ prasārayet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Determination of Directions (*Diṅ-nirṇayaḥ*):**
    *   If the shadow of the gnomon in the forenoon and afternoon touches the circumference of the circle, stretch a thread passing through those two marked points; that constitutes the true East-West axis.
    *   One should adjust the shadow to the South and North (left/right) by the specified declination correction (*apachāyā*).
    *   The height of the gnomon is conceptually divided into ninety-six parts (*ṣaṇṇavati-bhāga*), where one part represents one angula. Using that unit of angula, one should correct the monthly declination shadow:
        *   When the Sun is in Aries (*meṣa*), Gemini (*mithuna*), Leo (*siṃha*), or Libra (*tulā*): shift by two angulas.
        *   When the Sun is in Cancer (*kulīra*), Scorpio (*vṛścika*), or Pisces (*mīna*): shift by four angulas.
        *   When the Sun is in Sagittarius (*dhanuṣi*) or Aquarius (*kumbhe*): shift by six angulas.
        *   When the Sun is in Capricorn (*makare*): shift by eight angulas.
        *   When the Sun is in Taurus (*vṛṣabhe*) or Virgo (*kanyāyām*): no shadow correction is required.
    *   Dividing the specified monthly correction into thirty parts, one should apply the correction day by day, adjusting by one part daily using reasoning (*yuktyā*).
    *   Having marked these two corrected points, stretch the East-West thread between them.
    *   To find the North-South axis, draw intersecting arcs (resembling a fish-shape or *matsya*) using those two points, and stretch a thread passing through the mouth and tail of the fish-shape.

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **शिलान्तजलान्तभूशोधनपूर्वं शिलादिस्थापनम्**
> ततः पश्चात्, 'मेदिनी' इति मन्त्रेण जलान्तं शिलान्तं वा खनित्वा, शल्यान् अपोह्य, वालुकाभिः आपूर्य, हस्तिपादेन दृढं कृत्वा; सप्ताहे अतीते, तत् खातं पञ्चधा कृत्वा, त्रिभागावसाने च उत्तमं, द्विभागावसाने मध्यमं, एकभागावसाने च अधमं, तस्मिन् शिलां वा इष्टकां वा विन्यसेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **śilāntajalāntabhūśodhanapūrvaṃ śilādisthāpanam**
> tataḥ paścāt, 'medinī' iti mantreṇa jalāntaṃ śilāntaṃ vā khanitvā, śalyān apohya, vālukābhiḥ āpūrya, hastipādena dṛḍhaṃ kṛtvā; saptāhe atīte, tat khātaṃ pañcadhā kṛtvā, tribhāgāvasāne ca uttamaṃ, dvibhāgāvasāne madhyamaṃ, ekābhāgāvasāne ca adhamaṃ, tasmin śilāṃ vā iṣṭakāṃ vā vinyaset.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Digging the Foundation and Soil Purification (*Śilānta-jalānta-bhū-śodhana-pūrvam*):**
    *   Thereafter, reciting the hymn *"medinī..."*, dig the ground down to the level of water (*jalānta*) or rock (*śilānta*).
    *   Remove all foreign impurities (*śalya*, such as bones, husks, or charcoal).
    *   Fill the pit with sand (*vālukā*) and compact it firmly using an elephant-foot rammer (*hasti-pāda*).
    *   After seven days have passed (*saptāhe atīte*), divide the depth of the dug pit into five parts:
        *   Laying the foundation at three parts deep is best (*uttama*).
        *   At two parts deep is middling (*madhyama*).
        *   At one part deep is inferior (*adhama*).
    *   In that designated level, one should lay the foundation stone (*śilā*) or brick (*iṣṭakā*).

### Section 3: Characteristics of Stones/Bricks and Consecration Preparation

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **शिलेष्टकादिलक्षणम्**
> तयोः लक्षणं युग्म-अयुग्म-हस्त-विमानानां तद्धस्तं प्रति त्र्यङ्गुलं द्व्यङ्गुलं वा वर्धयेत्; अङ्गुलिसङ्ख्या-समायामं, तस्य अर्धविस्तारम्, अर्धबाहुल्यम्; अथवा त्र्यङ्गुलादि-एकादशाङ्गुलान्तम् एकाङ्गुलवृद्ध्या विस्तारं नवधा भवति, तदर्धबाहुल्यं विस्तार-द्विगुण-आयातं च। एवं चतस्रः शिलाः वा इष्टकाः वा आग्रपादं मुखं पृष्ठं च संलक्ष्यैव आहरेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **śilāiṣṭakādilakṣaṇam**
> tayoḥ lakṣaṇaṃ yugma-ayugma-hasta-vimānānāṃ taddhastaṃ prati tryaṅgulaṃ dvyaṅgulaṃ vā vardhayet; aṅgulasankhyā-samāyāmaṃ, tasya ardhavistāram, ardhabāhulyam; athavā tryaṅgulādi-ekādaśāṅgulāntam ekāṅgulavṛddhyā vistāraṃ navadhā bhavati, tadardhabāhulyaṃ vistāra-dviguṇa-āyātaṃ ca. evaṃ catasraḥ śilāḥ vā iṣṭakāḥ vā āgrapādaṃ mukhaṃ pṛṣṭhaṃ ca saṃlakṣyaiva āharet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Characteristics of Foundation Stones and Bricks (*Śilā-iṣṭakā-lakṣaṇam*):**
    *   Their dimensions are determined based on whether the temple measurements in cubits are even or odd (*yugma-ayugma*). Increase the size by three or two angulas for every cubit of the temple.
    *   The length (*āyāma*) of the stone/brick equals this number of angulas. Its width (*vistāra*) should be half of the length, and its thickness (*bāhulya*) should be half of the width.
    *   Alternatively, the width can have nine options: from three angulas up to eleven angulas, increasing by one angula at a time. The thickness is half of the width, and the length is twice the width (*vistāra-dviguṇa-āyatam*).
    *   In this manner, procure four stones or four bricks, carefully identifying their top (*agra*), base (*pāda*), front face (*mukha*), and back (*pṛṣṭha*).

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **शिलेष्टकाप्रतिष्ठापूर्वकं तद्विन्यासः तन्मध्ये रत्नविन्यासश्च**
> अङ्कुरान् अर्पयित्वा, जलाधिवासं त्र्यहम् एकाहं वा कृत्वा, प्रत्येकं वस्त्रेण आवेष्ट्य, वारुणैः मन्त्रैः अभिमन्त्र्य; तत्स्थानात् प्रमुखे षोडश-द्वादश-अष्ट-चतुर्भिः पादैः युक्तां प्रपां कृत्वा, तोरणाद्यैः अलङ्कृत्य; पूर्वस्यां रात्रौ तत्स्थानात् उत्तरे वास्तुहोमं हुत्वा, वालुकोपरि पर्यग्नि-पञ्चगव्याभ्यां संशोध्य, पुण्याहं कृत्वा; यागस्थानस्य मध्ये चतुर्दिशं द्विहस्तायतविस्तारं तालोत्सेधां वेदिं कृत्वा, प्रागादि चत्वारि अग्निकुण्डानि, तत्र प्राच्याम् ऐशान्यां वा स्नानश्वभ्रं च कृत्वा; पश्चात् आघारं हुत्वा, शिलां वा इष्टकां वा स्नानश्वभ्रे प्रतिष्ठाप्य, तत्र प्राच्यां व्रीहिभिः दण्डवत्-पङ्क्तिं कृत्वा, चतुर्दशकलशान् विन्यस्य, विधिना संस्नाप्य; वेद्यां धान्योपरि पञ्च वस्त्राणि आस्तीर्य, शिलाद्यं च प्रत्येकं वस्त्रेण आवेष्ट्य, चतुर्वेद-रूपं ध्यात्वा; प्रागादि विन्यस्य, पुरुषादि-मूर्तीनाम् आभिः अभ्यर्च्य, तन्मध्ये नवरत्नानि विन्यस्य, तस्मिन् रत्नेशं विष्णुम् अभ्यर्च्य, पुण्याहं कृत्वा, प्रत्येकं 'स्वस्तिदा विशस्पतिः' (ऋ० सं० १०-६६-१०) इति प्रतिसरं बद्ध्वा; चतुर्वेदादि-मन्त्रैः शिलाद्यं प्राच्याम् उत्तराग्रं, दक्षिणे प्रागग्रं, पश्चिमे च उत्तराग्रम्, उत्तरे प्रागग्रं च शाययेत्; उत्तराच्छादनं कृत्वा, हविः निवेद्य...

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **śilāiṣṭakāpratiṣṭhāpūrvakaṃ tadvinyāsaḥ tanmadhye ratnavinyāsaśca**
> aṅkurān arpayitvā, jalādhivāsaṃ tryaham ekāhaṃ vā kṛtvā, pratyekaṃ vastreṇa āveṣṭya, vāruṇaiḥ mantraiḥ abhimantrya; tatsthānāt pramukhe ṣoḍaśa-dvādaśa-aṣṭa-caturbhiḥ pādaiḥ yuktāṃ prapāṃ kṛtvā, toraṇādyaiḥ alaṅkṛtya; pūrvasyāṃ rātau tatsthānāt uttare vāstuhomaṃ hutvā, vālukopari paryagni-pañcagavyābhyāṃ saṃśodhya, puṇyāhaṃ kṛtvā; yāgasthānasya madhye caturdiśaṃ dvihastāyatavistāraṃ tālotsedhāṃ vedīṃ kṛtvā, prāgādi catvārī agnikuṇḍāni, tatra prācyām aiśānyāṃ vā snānaśvabhraṃ ca kṛtvā; paścāt āghāraṃ hutvā, śilāṃ vā iṣṭakāṃ vā snānaśvabhre pratiṣṭhāpya, tatra prācyāṃ vrīhibhiḥ daṇḍavat-paṅktiṃ kṛtvā, caturdaśakalaśān vinyasya, vidhinā saṃsnāpya; vedyāṃ dhānyopari pañca vastrāṇi āstīrya, śilādyaṃ ca pratyekaṃ vastreṇa āveṣṭya, caturveda-rūpaṃ dhyātvā; prāgādi vinyasya, puruṣādi-mūrtīnām ābhiḥ abhyarcya, tanmadhye navaratnāni vinyasya, tasmin ratneśaṃ viṣṇum abhyarcya, puṇyāhaṃ kṛtvā, pratyekaṃ 'svastidā viśaspatiḥ' (Ṛgveda Saṃhitā 10.66.10) iti pratisaraṃ baddhvā; caturvedādi-mantraiḥ śilādyaṃ prācyām uttarāgraṃ, dakṣiṇe prāgagraṃ, paścime ca uttarāgraṃ, uttare prāgagraṃ ca śāyayet; uttarācchādanaṃ kṛtvā, haviḥ nivedya...

**Translation (English):**
*   **Consecration of Foundation Stones/Bricks:**
    *   Having sown the sprouts (*aṅkura*), performed the water-immersion (*jala-adhivāsa*) for three days or one day, wrap each stone/brick in a garment and consecrate them reciting the Varuṇa mantras.
    *   Construct a temporary canopy (*prapā*) in front of that location, supported by sixteen, twelve, eight, or four pillars, and decorated with archways.
    *   On the preceding night, perform the *Vāstu-homa* sacrifice to the North of that site. Cleanse the area with sand, fire, and *Pañcagavya*, and perform the *Puṇyāha* purification.
    *   In the center of the sacrificial hall, construct a bed-altar (*vedī*) measuring two cubits in length and width, and one *tāla* in height. Set up the four fire pits in the four directions starting with East, and a bathing pit (*snāna-śvabhra*) in the East or North-East.
    *   Having offered the initial ghee oblations (*āghāra*), place the stones or bricks in the bathing pit.
    *   To their East, arrange a straight line of paddy grains like a staff (*daṇḍavat-paṅkti*), place fourteen pots (*kalaśa*), and bathe the stones/bricks in accordance with the rules.
    *   Spread five garments on the altar over grains, wrap each stone/brick in a garment, and meditate upon them as the embodiments of the four Vedas.
    *   Place them in order starting from the East, worship them with the mantras of Puruṣa and other deities, place the nine gems in their center, and worship Lord Viṣṇu as the Lord of Gems (*Ratneśa*).
    *   Perform the *Puṇyāha* ritual, and tie a protective thread (*pratisara*) around each while reciting *"svastidā viśaspatiḥ..."* (Rigveda 10.66.10).
    *   Reciting the mantras of the four Vedas, lay the stones/bricks down to sleep:
        *   In the East: with its tip pointing North (*uttarāgram*).
        *   In the South: with its tip pointing East (*prāgagram*).
        *   In the West: with its tip pointing North (*uttarāgram*).
        *   In the North: with its tip pointing East (*prāgagram*).
    *   Cover them with an upper cloth and offer the food oblation (*havis*).

**Translation (English):**
*   **The Laying of the Foundation Stones:**
    *   In the morning, having bathed, when the auspicious hour arrives, the patron and Acharya should decorate the temple builders (*sthāpakas*) and others with garments and upper cloths, and distribute sacrificial gifts (*dakṣiṇā*).
    *   Process around the decorated village or the temporary temple, chanting the *Śakuna* hymn.
    *   Enter the site, extinguish/relocate the sacrificial fire, and the Acharya, along with the patron and the master builder (*sthapati*), stretch the main thread (*nitya-sūtra*) of the temple to determine the doorway.

    *   Worship the master builder and have him secure the stones firmly in that order.
