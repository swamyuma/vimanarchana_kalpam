# Chapter 34: Consecration — Individual Consecration Rules for Entourage Deities (Parivārāṇāṃ Pṛthak-pratiṣṭhā-vidhiḥ)
### Section 1: Consecration of the Gateways, Gatekeepers, and Entourage Deities

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **परिवाराणां पृथक्-प्रतिष्ठा-विधिः -**
> परिवाराणां पृथक्-प्रतिष्ठा-विधिं वक्ष्ये — द्वारं विधिवत् कृत्वा षडङ्गानि विधिना शिल्पिना योजयेत्। प्रथमं पट्टिकां मूर्ध्वाननां दक्षिणशिरस्कां न्यस्य; तदूर्ध्वे दक्षिणस्तम्भम् उत्तराभिमुखं, उत्तरस्तम्भं दक्षिणाभिमुखं, तयोः उपरिष्ठाद् उत्तरपट्टिकाम् अधोमुखीम् उत्तरशिरस्कां न्यस्य; अन्तरे दक्षिणकवाटम् उत्तराभिमुखं, उत्तरकवाटं दक्षिणाभिमुखं संस्थाप्य शिल्पिनां विसृज्य;
> ***parivārāṇāṃ pṛthak-pratiṣṭhā-vidhiḥ -***
> *parivārāṇāṃ pṛthak-pratiṣṭhā-vidhiṃ vakṣye — dvāraṃ vidhivat kṛtvā ṣaḍaṅgāni vidhinā śilpinā yojayet. prathamaṃ paṭṭikāṃ mūrdhvānanāṃ dakṣiṇaśiraskāṃ nyasya; tadūrdhve dakṣiṇastambhambh uttarābhimukhaṃ, uttarastambhaṃ dakṣiṇābhimukhaṃ, tayoḥ upariṣṭād uttarapaṭṭikām adhomukhīm uttaraśiraskāṃ nyasya; antare dakṣiṇakavātam uttarābhimukhaṃ, uttarakavāṭaṃ dakṣiṇābhimukhaṃ saṃsthāpya śilpināṃ visṛjya;*
> 
> तद्-द्वाराभिमुखे च उत्तरे वास्तु-होमं हुत्वा, पर्यग्निकृत्वा, पञ्चगव्याद्भिः संशोध्य, षडङ्गानि प्रत्येकं वस्त्रेण आवेष्ट्य; अभिमुखे धान्यपीठे कुम्भं संन्यस्य; आचार्यः उत्तराभिमुखम् आसीत्वा ध्यायन् — धातारं, विधातारं, भुवङ्गं, पतङ्गं, पतिरं, वरुणं च — तस्मिन् कुम्भ-अम्भसि (कुम्भ-जले) समावाह्य; पश्चाद् धौत्रं प्रशंस्य, धात्रादीनाम् आवाहनं, जुष्टाकार-स्वाहाकारान् कृत्वा, धात्रादि-षड्देव-मन्त्रान् प्रत्येकं दश-पर्यायं सवैष्णवं च हुत्वा; प्रभाते तत्-कुम्भ-उदकेन धात्रादीन् समावाह्य, अभ्यर्च्य, हविः-निवेदनं च कारयेत्।
> *tad-dvārābhimukhe ca uttare vāstu-homaṃ hutvā, paryagnikṛtvā, pañcagavyādbhiḥ saṃśodhya, ṣaḍaṅgāni pratyekaṃ vastreṇa āveṣṭya; abhimukhe dhānyapīṭhe kumbhaṃ saṃnyasya; ācāryaḥ uttarābhimukham āsītvā dhyāyan — dhātāraṃ, vidhātāraṃ, bhuvaṅgaṃ, pataṅgaṃ, patiraṃ, varuṇaṃ ca — tasmin kumbha-ambhasi samāvāhya; paścād dhoutraṃ praśaṃsya, dhātrādīnām āvāhanaṃ, juṣṭākāra-svāhākārān kṛtvā, dhātrādi-ṣaḍdeva-mantrān pratyekaṃ daśa-paryāyaṃ savaiṣṇavaṃ ca hutvā; prabhāte tat-kumbha-udakena dhātrādīn samāvāhya, abhyarcya, haviḥ-nivedanaṃ ca kārayet.*
> 
> ब्रह्म-ईशयोः, भृगु-मार्कण्डेयोः च मुखमण्टपे सोपाने वा दक्षिण-उत्तरयोः पृथग् एव होमं हुत्वा कुम्भ-पूजनं च कारयेत्।
> *brahma-īśayoḥ, bhṛgu-mārkaṇḍeyoḥ ca mukhamantape sopāne vā dakṣiṇa-uttarayoḥ pṛthag eva homaṃ hutvā kumbha-pūjanaṃ ca kārayet.*
> 
> मणिकादीनां द्वारपालानां तत्-तत्-पार्श्वे अभिमुखे वा होमं कुम्भ-पूजनं च। अन्येषां परिवाराणां तत्-तत्-आलयाभिमुखे सर्वेषां पृथग् एव वास्तु-होमं हुत्वा, अभिमुखे शय्या-वेदिं द्वि-हस्त-आयत-विस्तारां तत्-तत्-बिम्ब-अर्ध-अधिक-मानां वा तद्-तुरीयांश-उत्सेधं सङ्कल्प्य, तत्-तत्-मन्त्रेण पूर्ववद् अक्ष्युन्मेषण-अधिवास-आदीनि कृत्वा;
> *maṇikādīnāṃ dvārapālānāṃ tat-tat-pārśve abhimukhe vā homaṃ kumbha-pūjanaṃ ca. anyeṣāṃ parivārāṇāṃ tat-tat-ālayābhimukhe sarveṣāṃ pṛthag eva vāstu-homaṃ hutvā, abhimukhe śayyā-vediṃ dvi-hast-āyata-vistārāṃ tat-tat-bimba-ardha-adhika-mānāṃ vā tad-turīyāṃśa-utsedhaṃ saṅkalpya, tat-tat-mantreṇa pūrvavad akṣyunmeṣaṇa-adhivāsa-ādīni kṛtvā;*
> 
> पश्चाद् औपासन-अग्नौ आघारं हुत्वा, कुम्भांश्च चतुराढक-सम्पूर्णान् खण्ड-स्फुटित-काल-रहितान् सङ्गृह्य, तन्तुना परिवेष्ट्य, अद्भिः आपूर्य, वस्त्र-युग्मेन आवेष्ट्य, रत्नं सुवर्णं वा तत्-तत्-आयुध-सहितं तत्-तत्-मन्त्रेण तत्-तत्-कुम्भे प्रक्षिप्य;
> *paścād oupāsana-agnou āghāraṃ hutvā, kumbhāṃśca caturāḍhaka-sampūrṇān khaṇḍa-sphuṭita-kāla-rahitān saṅgṛhya, tantunā pariveṣṭya, adbhiḥ āpūrya, vastra-yugmena āveṣṭya, ratnaṃ suvarṇaṃ vā tat-tat-āyudha-sahitaṃ tat-tat-mantreṇa tat-tat-kumbhe prakṣipya;*
> 
> सप्तभिः कलशैः संस्नाप्य, शय्यावेद्यां धान्योपरि पञ्च-वस्त्राणि आस्तीर्य, प्रतिसरं बद्ध्वा, तत्-तत्-मन्त्रेण शाययित्वा, उत्तराच्छादनं कृत्वा, हौत्रं प्रशंस्य, तत्-तत्-मूर्ति-मन्त्रेण आवाहन-जुष्टाकार-स्वाहाकारं कृत्वा; सर्वेषां परिवाराणां तत्-तत्-मन्त्रान् सवैष्णवान् षोडश-वारं द्वादश-वारं वा जुहुयात्।
> *saptabhiḥ kalaśaiḥ saṃsnāpya, śayyāvedyāṃ dhānyopari pañca-vastrāṇi āstīrya, pratisaraṃ baddhvā, tat-tat-mantreṇa śāyayitvā, uttarācchādanaṃ kṛtvā, houtraṃ praśaṃsya, tat-tat-mūrti-mantreṇa āvāhana-juṣṭākāra-svāhākāraṃ kṛtvā; sarveṣāṃ parivārāṇāṃ tat-tat-mantrān savaiṣṇavān ṣoḍaśa-vāraṃ dvādaśa-vāraṃ vā juhuyāt.*
> 
> रात्रि-शेषं व्यपोह्य, प्रभाते स्नात्वा, आलयम् आविश्य, कुम्भं बिम्बं च समानीय, तत्-तत्-मन्त्रेण रत्न-न्यासं कृत्वा, तत्-तत्-मन्त्रेण तत्-तत्-बेर-हृदये तत्-तत्-बीजाक्षरं न्यस्य, तत्-तत्-मन्त्रेण आवाह्य, अभ्यर्च्य, हविः निवेदयेद् इत्याह मरीचिः ॥
> *rātri-śeṣaṃ vyapohya, prabhāte snātvā, ālayam āviśya, kumbhaṃ bimbaṃ ca samānīya, tat-tat-mantreṇa ratna-nyāsaṃ kṛtvā, tat-tat-mantreṇa tat-tat-ber-hṛdaye tat-tat-bījākṣaraṃ nyasya, tat-tat-mantreṇa āvāhya, abhyarcya, haviḥ nivedayed ityāha marīciḥ.*

#### English Translation
*   **Assembly of the Door-Frame:** I shall describe the individual consecration rules for the entourage deities (*parivārāṇām*). Having constructed the gateway according to the rules, the architect (*śilpin*) should assemble the six parts of the frame:
    1. First, he places the lower sill (*paṭṭikā*), facing upward, with its head to the south.
    2. Above that, he raises the southern pillar, facing north.
    3. He raises the northern pillar, facing south.
    4. Over them, he places the upper lintel (*uttara-paṭṭikā*), facing downward, with its head to the north.
    5. In the opening, he installs the southern door-leaf, facing north.
    6. He installs the northern door-leaf, facing south. He then dismisses the artisans.

*   **Gateway Purification and Worship:**
    *   Facing that doorway, and to the north, he offers the *vāstu-homa* (site fire offering), carries the fire around the gateway, purifies the wood with five products of the cow (*pañca-gavya*) and water, and wraps each of the six frame parts in cloth.
    *   In front of the door, on a heap of grain (*dhānya-pīṭha*), he places the sacred pot (*kumbha*). The Ācārya, sitting facing north and meditating on the door-deities — Dhātṛ, Vidhātṛ, Bhuvaṅga, Pataṅga, Patira, and Varuṇa — invokes them into the water of that pot.
    *   Afterward, having praised the *hotṛ* hymns, he performs the invocation (*āvāhana*) of Dhātṛ etc., makes the *juṣṭākāra* and *svāhākāra* offerings, and offers fire oblations with the mantras of the six deities of the gateway ten times each, along with the *Vaiṣṇava* homas. In the morning, with the water from that pot, he invokes the deities on the gateway, worships them, and offers food (*havis*).

*   **Consecration of Attendants and Enclosure Deities:**
    *   For Brahmā and Śiva (*Brahmeśayoḥ*), and Bhṛgu and Mārkaṇḍeya, he offers separate homas and performs pot-worship (*kumbha-pūjā*) on the south and north sides in the entrance hall (*mukha-maṇṭapa*) or on the steps.
    *   For Maṇika and the other door-guardians, he performs the homa and pot-worship beside them or directly facing them.
    *   For all other entourage deities, facing their respective shrines, he offers separate *vāstu-homas*. In front, he prepares a couch-altar (*śayyā-vedi*), two cubits in length and width, or dimensioned to be larger than half the height of the image, and sets its height to one-quarter of the image's height.
    *   Using their respective mantras, he performs the eye-opening (*akṣi-unmeṣaṇa*), couch-placement (*adhivāsa*), etc., as before.

*   **The Ritual Pot Setup:**
    *   Afterward, having offered the primary ghee oblations (*āghāra*) in the domestic fire (*aupāsana-agni*), he takes pots that hold four *āḍhakas* of water and are free from chips, cracks, or black spots. He wraps them with thread, fills them with water, covers them with a pair of cloths, and drops gems, gold, and the respective weapons of the deities into the pots while chanting their respective mantras.
    *   He bathes the images with seven pots (*kalaśas*), spreads five cloths on the grains upon the couch-altar, ties the protective thread (*pratisara*), lays them down on the couch while chanting their respective mantras, and covers them with an upper cloth.
    *   Praising the *hotṛ* hymns, he performs the invocation and fire offerings (*juṣṭākāra-svāhākāra*) with their respective deity-mantras. He offers the specific deity-mantras along with the *Vaiṣṇava* homas sixteen or twelve times for each entourage deity.

*   **Final Installation:**
    *   At the end of the night, having bathed in the morning, he enters the temple, brings both the pot and the image, performs the gem-placement (*ratna-nyāsa*) with their respective mantras, and places the respective seed-syllables (*bījākṣaras*) on the hearts of the images.
    *   He invokes the deities into the images with their respective mantras, worships them, and offers food (*havis*). Thus says Sage Marīci.
