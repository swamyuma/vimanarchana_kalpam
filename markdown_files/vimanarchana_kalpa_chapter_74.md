# Vimanarchana Kalpa of Sage Marīci (Vaikhānasa Āgama)
## Chapter 74: Ācārya-saṅkara-yogabhogādi-saṅkara-vidhiḥ (Rules for the Mixing of Priests, Postures, and Icon Configurations)

This document contains the Sanskrit text, IAST transliteration, and English translation of the 74th Chapter (*Catuḥsaptatitamaḥ Paṭalaḥ*) of the **Vimānārcanākalpa** (attributed to Sage Marīci, representing the Vaikhānasa school of Vaishnavism).

This chapter outlines the rules and expiations concerning the substitution or changing of priests (*ācārya-saṅkara*), the proxy rituals when a chosen priest becomes unavailable, the prohibition of altering the theological path of installation (e.g. from Yoga to Bhoga), the regulations for changing the posture (standing, seated, reclining) of deity icons, and the consequences of mixing retinue/incarnation icons (*saṅkara*).

---

### Section 1: Selection of the Priest and the Sin of Substituting the Priest

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **अथ चतुःसप्ततितमः पटलः**
> **आचार्यसङ्करे दोषः प्रायश्चित्तं च**
> ***atha catuḥsaptatitamaḥ paṭalaḥ***
> ***ācāryasaṅkare doṣaḥ prāyaścittaṃ ca***
> 
> अथाचार्यं कर्षणाकाले वरयित्वा, कर्षणादीनि प्रतिष्ठान्तानि, नित्यार्चन-स्नपन-उत्सव-प्रायश्चित्त-विशेषाच्चनादीनि नित्यनैमित्तिककर्माणि सर्वाणि च तेनैव कारयेत्। आचार्याभावे तत्पुत्रं, पौत्रं, भ्रातरं, तद्वंशजं वा कुर्यात्। ते अयुक्ताश्चेत्, तान् अननुज्ञाप्य पूर्ववद् अन्यम् आचार्यं वरयेत्; तदभावे तच्छिष्यं प्रशिष्यं वा वरयेत्।
> *athācāryaṃ karṣaṇākāle varayitvā, karṣaṇādīni pratiṣṭhāntāni, nityārcana-snapana-utsava-prāyaścitta-viśeṣārcanādīni nityanaimittikakarmāṇi sarvāṇi ca tenaiva kārayet | ācāryābhāve tatputraṃ, pautraṃ, bhrātaraṃ, tadvaṃśajaṃ vā kuryāt | te ayuktāścet, tān ananujñāpya pūrvavad anyam ācāryaṃ varayet; tadabhāve tacchicyaṃ praśicyaṃ vā varayet ||*
> 
> आचार्यसन्निधौ न अन्यम् आचार्यं वरयेत्। यदि वरयेत् तत्स्थानस्य, ग्रामयजमानयोश्च, आशु विनाशो भवति। राज्ञः, राष्ट्रस्य च, परचक्रभयं भवति। तदर्चनं निष्फलं भवति। तस्माद् आचार्यसङ्करं न कुर्यात्। अज्ञानाद् अर्थलोभाद् वा आचार्यसङ्करे सति, पौण्डरीकाग्नौ महाशान्तिं हुत्वा, आचार्यं नमस्कृत्य "क्षमस्व" इति याचित्वा पूजयित्वा तेनैव तत्तत्कर्म पुनरपि कारयेत्।
> *ācāryasannidhau na anyam ācāryaṃ varayet | yadi varayet tatsthānasya, grāmayajamānayośca, āśu vināśo bhavati | rājñaḥ, rāṣṭrasya ca, paracakrabhayaṃ bhavati | tadarcanaṃ niṣphalaṃ bhavati | tasmād ācāryasaṅkaraṃ na kuryāt | ajñānād arthalobhād vā ācāryasaṅkare sati, pauṇḍarīkāgnau mahāśāntiṃ hutvā, ācāryaṃ namaskṛtya "kṣamasva" iti yācitvā pūjayitvā tenaiva tattatkarma punarapi kārayet ||*

#### English Translation
*   **The Chosen Priest:** Having selected the chief priest (*ācārya*) at the time of ploughing (*karṣaṇākāle*), one should have all the daily and occasional rituals—from the initial ploughing to the final installation, including daily worship, bathing ceremonies, festivals, expiations, and special worships—performed by him alone (*tenaiva kārayet*).

*   **Succession Rules:** If the priest is unavailable, one should employ his son, grandson, brother, or someone from his lineage (*tadvaṃśajaṃ vā*). If they are incompetent/unqualified (*te ayuktāścet*), one may select another priest as before without needing their permission (*tān ananujñāpya*). In the absence of a lineage successor, one should select his disciple (*śiṣyaṃ*) or grand-disciple (*praśiṣyaṃ*).

*   **The Sin of priest Substitution:** One must not select another priest while the original priest is available (*ācāryasannidhau na anyam ācāryaṃ varayet*). If one does so, the temple, the village, and the patron/host suffer immediate ruin (*āśu vināśo bhavati*); for the king and the state, fear of enemy invasion (*paracakrabhayaṃ*) arises, and the worship becomes fruitless (*niṣphalaṃ*).

*   **Expiation for priest Substitution:** Therefore, one must not mix or substitute priests. If priest substitution occurs due to ignorance or greed for money (*ajñānād arthalobhād vā*), one should perform the great pacification offering (*mahā-śāntiṃ*) in the Paundarika fire (*pauṇḍarīkāgnau*), bow down to the original priest, request his forgiveness (*"kṣamasva" iti yācitvā*), worship him, and have those respective rituals performed by him again.

### Section 2: Procedure in the Absence of the Selected Priest

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **आचार्यादीनाम् अलाभे कर्तव्यम्**
> आचार्यादीनाम् अलाभे तन्नाम पत्रे लिखित्वा तं नमस्य तत्सन्निधौ अन्यं युक्तम् आचार्यं वरयेत्। एकस्मिन् काले बहुकर्मसु परधामसु सत्सु, आचार्यं प्रणम्य, तदनुज्ञया तत्पुत्रादिभिः कारयेत्। तदलाभे आचार्य एव तन्त्रयित्वा करोतीति विज्ञायते ।
> ***ācāryādīnām alābhe kartavyam***
> *ācāryādīnām alābhe tannāma patre likhitvā taṃ namasya tatsannidhau anyaṃ yuktam ācāryaṃ varayet | ekasmin kāle bahukarmasu paradhāmasu satsu, ācāryaṃ praṇamya, tadanujñayā tatputrādibhiḥ kārayet | tadalābhe ācārya eva tantrayitvā karotīti vijñāyate ||*

#### English Translation
*   **Absence of the Priest:** In the absolute absence or death of the original priest (*ācāryādīnām alābhe*), one should write his name on a leaf/paper (*tannāma patre likhitvā*), pay obeisance to it, and in its presence (*tatsannidhau*), select another qualified priest.

*   **Simultaneous Ceremonies:** If multiple rituals occur at the same time in different temples (*ekasmin kāle bahukarmasu paradhāmasu satsu*), one should bow to the chief priest, and with his permission (*tadanujñayā*), have them conducted by his sons and others. If they are unavailable, it is understood that the chief priest himself performs them by combining the systems (*tantrayitvā*).

### Section 3: Changing Consecration Paths

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **योगमार्गेण प्रतिष्ठितस्य मार्गान्तरेण प्रतिष्ठाकरणे दोषः**
> पूर्वं योगमार्गेण प्रतिष्ठाप्य, अर्चनायां प्रवर्तमानायाम्, पश्चात् तस्य देवीभ्यां सह प्रतिष्ठां कारयेत् चेद् आभिचारिकं भवति।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **yogamārgeṇa pratiṣṭhitasya mārgāntareṇa pratiṣṭhākāraṇe doṣaḥ**
> pūrvaṃ yogamārgeṇa pratiṣṭhāpya, arcanāyāṃ pravartamānāyām, paścāt tasya devībhyāṃ saha pratiṣṭhāṃ kārayet ced ābhicārikaṃ bhavati |

**Translation (English):**
*   **Changing Consecration Path:** If an icon was previously consecrated according to the path of Yoga (*yoga-mārgeṇa pratiṣṭhāpya*), and while the worship is going on, one subsequently performs a consecration along with the two Goddesses (which represents the active/Bhoga path), it becomes malevolent/harmful (*ābhicārikaṃ bhavati*).

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> (पूर्वं ध्रुवकौतुकसंयुते अर्च्यमाने, यदि तयोः ध्रुवयोः विनाशश्चेत्, ध्रुवार्चनम् एव ब्रह्मस्थाने प्रतिष्ठाप्य विधिवद् अर्चयेत्। तत्तत् कौतुकबिम्बम् उत्सवार्थं प्रतिष्ठापयेद् इति केचित्) ॥

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> (pūrvaṃ dhruvakautukasaṃyute arcyamāne, yadi tayoḥ dhruvayoḥ vināśascet, dhruvārcanam eva brahmasthāne pratiṣṭhāpya vidhivad arcayet | tattat kautukabimbam utsavārthaṃ pratiṣṭhāpayed iti kecit) ||

**Translation (English):**
*   **Loss of Fixed Deity:** (If worship was previously going on with both fixed and movable icons in place, and if the fixed icon is destroyed, some say one should install only a single fixed worship icon in the Brahmā zone (*Brahma-sthāne*) and worship it according to the rules, and consecrate those respective movable icons for the purpose of festivals (*utsavārthaṃ*).)

### Section 4: Arrangement of Standing, Seated, and Reclining Postures

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **स्थानकासनशयनेषु व्यत्यासे व्यवस्था**
> स्थानके च आसनं शयनं वा कर्तुः इच्छया कुर्यात्। आसने स्थानकं विना शयनम् एव कुर्यात्। शयने स्थानकासने न कारयेत्। कौतुकं स्थितम् उत्सवम् आसीनं, शयनम् उत्सवं स्थितं वा कारयेत्। स्नपन-बलिबेरं च सर्वत्र कारयेत्।
> ***sthānakāśanaśayaneṣu vyatyāse vyavasthā***
> *sthānake ca āsanaṃ śayanaṃ vā kartuḥ icchayā kuryāt | āsane sthānakaṃ vinā śayanam eva kuryāt | śayane sthānakāsane na kārayet | kautukaṃ sthitam utsavamaśīnaṃ, śayanam utsavaṃ sthitaṃ vā kārayet | snapana-baliberaṃ ca sarvatra kārayet ||*

#### English Translation
*   **Postures of Fixed Icons:** 
    1.  For a temple where the deity is standing (*sthānake*), one can make it seated (*āsanaṃ*) or reclining (*śayanaṃ*) according to the patron's desire (*kartuḥ icchayā*).
    2.  If the deity is seated (*āsane*), one can make it reclining (*śayanam*), but not standing (*sthānakaṃ vinā*).
    3.  If the deity is reclining (*śayane*), one must not make it standing or seated (*sthānakāsane na kārayet*).

*   **Postures of Movable Icons:** One should make a standing movable icon (*kautuka*) for a seated festival icon (*utsava*), or a reclining festival icon for a standing movable icon. The bath (*snapana*) and offering (*bali*) icons should be made in all cases.

### Section 5: Mixing of Incarnation and Retinue Icons

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **अवतारदीनां कौतुकबेराणां सङ्करे सति प्रायश्चित्तम्**
> अवताराणां पञ्चमूर्तीनां च कौतुकबेराणि अन्योन्यसङ्करेण स्थापयेत् चेत् महद् दोषो भवति।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **avatārādīnāṃ kautukaberāṇāṃ saṅkare sati prāyaścittam**
> avatārāṇāṃ pañcamūrtīnāṃ ca kautukaberāṇi anyonyasaṅkareṇa sthāpayet cet mahad doṣo bhavati |

**Translation (English):**
*   **Mixing of Deities:** If the movable icons (*kautuka-berāṇi*) of the various incarnations (*avatārāṇāṃ*) and the five primary forms (*pañca-mūrtīnāṃ ca*) are installed in a mixed manner (*anyonya-saṅkareṇa*), a very great defect/sin arises (*mahad doṣo bhavati*).

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> तद्दोष-शमनार्थं महाशान्तिं हुत्वा यथास्थाने स्थापयेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> taddoṣa-śamanārthaṃ mahāśāntiṃ hutvā yathāsthāne sthāpayet ||

**Translation (English):**
*   **Expiation:** To pacify that defect (*taddoṣa-śamanārthaṃ*), one should perform the great pacification fire sacrifice (*mahā-śāntiṃ hutvā*) and install each icon in its proper/original place (*yathā-sthāne sthāpayet*).

### Section 6: Omission of Defect during Re-consecration

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **कौतुकादीनां पुनःप्रतिष्ठासमये साङ्कर्येऽपि दोषाभावः**
> पुनःप्रतिष्ठाकाले कौतुक-स्नपन-उत्सव-बलिबेराणाम् अन्योन्यसङ्कर-स्थापनं निर्दोषम् इति आचष्टे मरीचिः ॥

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **kautukādīnāṃ punaḥpratiṣṭhāsamaye saṅkarye'pi doṣābhāvaḥ**
> punaḥpratiṣṭhākāle kautuka-snapana-utsava-baliberāṇām anyonyasaṅkara-sthāpanaṃ nirdoṣam iti ācaṣṭe marīciḥ ||

**Translation (English):**
*   **Exception during Re-consecration:** At the time of re-consecration (*punaḥ-pratiṣṭhā-kāle*), the mutual mixing/exchange in the placement of the movable worship, bath, festival, and offering icons (*kautuka-snapana-utsava-baliberāṇām anyonya-saṅkara-sthāpanaṃ*) is faultless/defect-free (*nirdoṣam*), so declares Sage Marīci (*iti ācaṣṭe marīciḥ*).

## Glossary of Key Terms

1.  **Ācārya-saṅkara (आचार्यसङ्कर):** The defect of substituting, changing, or mixing priests during the course of ongoing temple rituals.
2.  **Pauṇḍarīka-agni (पौण्डरीकाग्नि):** A sacred fire used in Vaikhānasa rituals, associated with elaborate temple purifications.
3.  **Yogāmārga (योगमार्ग):** The meditative or ascetic style of deity installation, typically without consorts (Goddesses) and focusing on silent contemplation.
4.  **Bhoga-mārga (भोगमार्ग):** The active or ritualistic style of deity installation, featuring the deity accompanied by the Goddesses (Śrī and Bhū).
5.  **Ābhicārika (आभिचारिक):** Malevolent or harmful energy/rituals (black magic) that bring destruction to the sponsor and the temple.
6.  **Sthānaka, Āsana, Śayana (स्थानक, आसन, शयन):** The three primary postures of the fixed deity icon—standing, seated, and reclining.
7.  **Saṅkara (सङ्कर):** The mixing, swapping, or improper combination of deity icons, postures, or retinue members.
8.  **Mahā-śānti (महाशान्ति):** The grand pacification fire sacrifice performed to rectify severe ritual errors or negative omens.
9.  **Pañca-mūrti (पञ्चमूर्ति):** The five primary theological forms of Viṣṇu in Vaikhānasa doctrine (Viṣṇu, Puruṣa, Satya, Acyuta, Aniruddha).
10. **Tantrayitvā (तन्त्रयित्वा):** Combining or simplifying multiple rituals by performing them under a single unified system or controller.
