# Chapter 33: Consecration — The Rules of Gem-Placement and Consecration (Ratnanyāsa-pratiṣṭhā-vidhiḥ)
### Section 1: Gem-Placement in the Pedestal Pit and Fee-Giving (Pīṭhāvaṭe Ratnādinyāsaḥ Dakṣiṇādānaṃ ca)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **पीठ-अवटे रत्नादि-न्यासः दक्षिणा-दानं च**
> ततः प्रभाते स्नात्वा आचम्य सन्ध्याम् उपास्य देवादीन् त्रिः तर्पयित्वा ब्रह्मयज्ञं कृत्वा देवालयं प्रविश्य 'प्रतद्विष्णुः' (ऋ॰ १-२२-२०) इति समप्रदक्षिणं कृत्वा यज्ञालयं प्रविश्य अर्चापीठे कृतेऽवटे पञ्चविंशतिभागं कृत्वा अधिवासितरत्नान्यादाय तस्मिन् ब्रह्माणं इन्द्रादि दिग्देवांश्चाभ्यर्च्य पुष्पादीन् संशोध्य 'तमेकनेमि' (वै० म० प्र० ८) इत्युक्त्वाऽऽमृश्य तेनैव ब्रह्मस्थाने कूर्चं प्रतिष्ठाप्य 'शतधारं' (वै० प्र० ८) इति गरुडं तस्मिन् 'ब्रह्मा देवानाम्' (तै० आ० ८) इति ब्रह्मस्थाने ब्रह्ममणिं, 'इन्द्रं प्रवणन्तं' (वै० प्र०) इति ऐन्द्रे वज्रमणिम्, 'अग्नावग्निः' (वै० प्र० ८) इति आग्नेय्यां मौक्तिकं, 'यमो दाधार' (वै० म० ६, तै० आ० ८) इति याम्ये वैडूर्यम्, 'वसवः प्रथमः' (वै० म० प्र० ८) इति नैर्ऋत्यां शङ्खजं, 'एते शतं वरुण' (वै० प्र० ८) इति वारुण्यां स्फटिकम्, 'मरुतः परमात्मा' (वै० प्र० ६) इति वायव्यां पुष्यकं, 'सोमं राजानम्' (वै० प्र० १) इति सौम्ये चन्द्रकान्तं, 'ईशानः' (वै० म० ८) इति ऐशान्यां नीलं च;
> ***pīṭha-avaṭe ratnādi-nyāsaḥ dakṣiṇā-dānaṃ ca***
> *tataḥ prabhāte snātvā ācamya sandhyām upāsya devādīn triḥ tarpayitvā brahmayajñaṃ kṛtvā devālayaṃ praviśya 'pratadviṣṇuḥ' (ṛg. 1.22.20) iti samapradakṣiṇaṃ kṛtvā yajñaśālāṃ praviśya arcāpīṭhe kṛte'vaṭe pañcavaiṃśatibhāgaṃ kṛtvā adhivāsitaratnānyādāya tasmin brahmāṇaṃ indrādi digdevāṃścābhyarcya puṣpādīn saṃśodhya 'tamekanemi' (vai. ma. pra. 8) ityuktva''mṛśya tenaiva brahmasthāne kūrcaṃ pratiṣṭhāpya 'śatadhāraṃ' (vai. pra. 8) iti garuḍaṃ tasmin 'brahmā devānām' (tai. ā. 8) iti brahmasthāne brahmamaṇiṃ, 'indraṃ pravaṇantaṃ' (vai. pra.) iti aindre vajramaṇim, 'agnāvagniḥ' (vai. pra. 8) iti āgneyyāṃ mauktikaṃ, 'yamo dādhāra' (vai. ma. 6, tai. ā. 8) iti yāmye vaiḍūryam, 'vasavaḥ prathamaḥ' (vai. ma. pra. 8) iti nairṛtyāṃ śaṅkhajaṃ, 'ete śataṃ varuṇa' (vai. pra. 8) iti vāruṇyāṃ sphāṭikam, 'marutaḥ paramātmā' (vai. pra. 6) iti vāyavyāṃ puṣyakaṃ, 'somaṃ rājānam' (vai. pra. 1) iti saumye candrakāntaṃ, 'īśānaḥ' (vai. ma. 8) iti aiśānyāṃ nīlaṃ ca;*
> 
> इन्द्राग्न्योरन्तरे 'शन्नो निधत्ताम्' (वै० प्र० ८) इति अमृताश्मकं, यमाग्न्योरन्तरे 'धाता धातृणाम्' (वै० म० ८) इति लोहिताक्षकं, यम-निरृत्योः (यमनीयोः) मध्ये 'विश्वेदेवस्य' (वै० म० ३) इति उत्पलमणिं, निरृति-वरुणयोः (नीलवरुणयोः) मध्ये 'अधि ब्रह्मा यतताम्' (वै० प्र० ८) इति प्रवालं, वरुण-वाय्वोरन्तरे 'ममाग्ने अर्धधान्यं' (वै० म० प्र० ८) इति मनःशिलां, वायु-सोमयोः मध्ये 'अप्सरः' (वै० प्र० ८) इति शुक्तिजं, सोम-ईशानयोः अन्तरे 'काद्रवेयः' (वै० प्र० ८) इति विमलमणिं, ईशान-इन्द्रयोः मध्ये 'अर्यम्णः कुम्भी' (वै० प्र० ८) इति मरुदं सन्यस्य;
> *indrāgnyorantare 'śanno nidhattām' (vai. pra. 8) iti amṛtāśmakaṃ, yamāgnyorantare 'dhātā dhātṝṇām' (vai. ma. 8) iti lohitākṣakaṃ, yama-nirṛtyoḥ (yamanīyoḥ) madhye 'viśvedevasya' (vai. ma. 3) iti utpalamaṇiṃ, nirṛti-varuṇayoḥ (nīlavaruṇayoḥ) madhye 'adhi brahmā yatatām' (vai. pra. 8) iti pravālaṃ, varuṇa-vāyvorantare 'mamāgne ardhadhānyaṃ' (vai. ma. pra. 8) iti manaḥśilāṃ, vāyu-somayoḥ madhye 'apsaraḥ' (vai. pra. 8) iti śuktijaṃ, soma-īśānayoḥ antare 'kādraveyaḥ' (vai. pra. 8) iti vimalamaṇiṃ, īśāna-indrayoḥ madhye 'aryamṇaḥ kumbhī' (vai. pra. 8) iti marudaṃ sanyasya;*
> 
> अभ्यन्तरे पदे च ऐन्द्रादीन् विदिक्षु गैरिका, हरिताळ, मनःशिलां, जनश्यामका (अञ्जनश्यामकौ), सीस, पारत, सौराष्ट्र (सौराष्ट्रमृत्तिका), गोरोचनाऽन्तं तत्-तत्-दिगीशानां मन्त्रेण असङ्करं सन्यस्य;
> *abhyantare pade ca aindrādīn vidikṣu gairikā, haritāla, manaḥśilāṃ, janaśyāmakā (añjanaśyāmakou), sīsa, pārata, saurāṣṭra (saurāṣṭramṛttikā), gorocanā'ntaṃ tat-tat-digīśānāṃ mantreṇa asaṅkaraṃ sanyasya;*
> 
> तथा शालि, व्रीहि, यव, निष्पाव, नीवार, षाष्टिका, माष, मुद्ग, तिल, तिल्वादीनां रूपं सुवर्णं कृत्वा, श्रीवत्सादीनि मङ्गलादीनि च सन्यस्य, पञ्चायुधानि मध्ये चतुर्दिक्षु सन्यस्य, मध्ये पदे सौवर्णचिह्नानि कूर्मरूप-मेघ-विद्युल्लताश्च विन्यसेत्। एवं कर्तुम् अशक्तश्चेत् नवरत्नम् अष्टमङ्गल-पञ्चायुध-वर्णचिह्नानि सन्यस्य, तच्छ्वभ्रं दुकूलेन आच्छाद्य सुधया आपूरयित्वा सुदृढं कृत्वा 'विष्णुस्त्वारक्षतु' इति उक्त्वा (वै० प्र० ३) अभ्युक्ष्य पौण्डरीकाग्निं विसृज्य गर्भालायं संशोध्य,
> *tathā śāli, vrīhi, yava, niṣpāva, nīvāra, ṣāṣṭikā, māṣa, mudga, tila, tilvādīnāṃ rūpaṃ suvarṇaṃ kṛtvā, śrīvatsādīni maṅgalādīni ca sanyasya, pañcāyudhāni madhye caturdikṣu sanyasya, madhye pade souvarṇacihnāni kūrmarūpa-megha-vidyullatāśca vinyaset. evaṃ kartum aśaktaścet navaratnam aṣṭamaṅgala-pañcāyudhā-varṇacihnāni sanyasya, tacchvabhraṃ dukūlena ācchādya sudhayā āpūrayitvā sudṛḍhaṃ kṛtvā 'viṣṇustvārakṣatu' iti uktvā (vai. pra. 3) abhyukṣya pouṇḍarīkāgniṃ visṛjya garbhālāyaṃ saṃśodhya,*
> 
> उपालिप्य पैष्ट्या भूमिम् अलङ्कृत्य उपरिष्टात् विमान-फलक-मुक्तादाम-पुष्पमालाद्यैः अलङ्कृत्य अधस्तात् कुसुमाक्षतान् आकीर्य, अगरु-धूपैः कर्पूर-दीपैः द्वार-पार्श्वे दीपाङ्कुर-उपकुम्भाद्यैः अलङ्कृत्य यज्ञशालाम् आगत्य देवेशं प्रणम्य यजमानो गुरुम् अभिवन्द्य अनुज्ञाप्य वस्त्र-उत्तरीयोष्णीष-आभरणैः गन्ध-माल्यैः च अभिपूज्य देव-सन्निधौ सोदकं सुवर्णं दद्यात् ॥
> *upālipya paiṣṭyā bhūmim alaṅkṛtya upariṣṭāt vimāna-phalaka-muktādāma-puṣpamālādyaiḥ alaṅkṛtya adhastāt kusumākatān ākīrya, agaru-dhūpaiḥ karpūra-dīpaiḥ dvāra-pārśve dīpāṅkura-upakumbhādyaiḥ alaṅkṛtya yajñaśālām āgatya deveśaṃ praṇamya yajamāno gurum abhivandya anujñāpya vastra-uttarīyoṣṇīṣa-ābharaṇaiḥ gandha-mālyaiḥ ca abhipūjya deva-sannidhou sodakaṃ suvarṇaṃ dadyāt.*
> 
> **दक्षिणा-दानम् -**
> राजा चेत् — चतुष्षष्टि-निष्कं गुरवे, ग्रामश्चेत् अष्ट-चत्वारिंशत्, इतरे तु आढ्याः एकविंशति-निष्कं दद्युः। अन्येषाम् अपि अशक्तानां दक्षिणादानं नवधा उच्यते —
> ***dakṣiṇā-dānam -***
> *rājā cet — catuṣṣaṣṭi-niṣkaṃ gurave, grāmaścet aṣṭa-catvāriṃśat, itare tu āḍhyāḥ ekaviṃśati-niṣkaṃ dadyuḥ. anyeṣām api aśaktānāṃ dakṣiṇādānaṃ navadhā ucyate —*
> 
> पादाधिक-नव-निष्कम् उत्तमोत्तमं, तथा अष्ट-निष्कम् उत्तम-मध्यमं, तथा सप्त-निष्कम् उत्तम-अधमं, पादाधिक-षण्-निष्कं मध्यमोत्तमं, तथा पञ्च-निष्कं मध्यम-मध्यमं, निष्क-चतुष्टयं मध्यम-अधमं, पादाधिक-त्रय-निष्कम् अधमोत्तमं, तथा निष्क-द्वयम् अधम-मध्यमं, तथा एक-निष्कम् अधमाधमम् इति; तस्मात् शक्तिं न वञ्चयेत्। अज्ञानाद् अर्थलोभाद् वा शक्ति-वञ्चने कृते ते पदार्थिन एव तत्-फलम् आप्नुयुः यजमानो रौरवम् आयाति।
> *pādādhika-nava-niṣkam uttamottamaṃ, tathā aṣṭ-niṣkam uttama-madhyamaṃ, tathā sapta-niṣkam uttamādhamaṃ, pādādhika-ṣaṇ-niṣkaṃ madhyamottamaṃ, tathā pañca-niṣkaṃ madhyama-madhyamaṃ, niṣka-catuṣṭayaṃ madhyamādhamaṃ, pādādhika-traya-niṣkam adhamottamaṃ, tathā niṣka-dvayam adhama-madhyamaṃ, tathā eka-niṣkam adhamādhamam iti; tasmāt śaktiṃ na vañcayet. ajñānād arthalobhād vā śakti-vañcane kṛte te padārthina eva tat-phalam āpnuyuḥ yajamāno rauravam āyāti.*
> 
> स्थापकानां सभ्याध्वर्योः होतुः पौण्डरीकाध्वर्योः च आचार्य-दक्षिणा-चतुर्थांशं, तदर्धम् आहवनीयाध्वर्यूणां, तदर्धम् अन्य-पदार्थिनां च दद्यात्। यज्ञस्य दक्षिणा जीवो भवेत्; अल्प-दक्षिणा-यागो यजमानं भ्रूणहत्यां प्रापयति इति एके। तस्माद् अवश्यं दक्षिणादानं यथोक्तं कुर्यात्। द्रव्यहीने तद्-ग्राम-यजमानस्य अपि द्रव्य-हानिः, क्रियाहीने पुण्य-क्रिया-हानिः, मन्त्रहीने स्वाध्याय-आयुष्य-हानिः, श्रद्धा-भक्ति-हीने सर्व-हानिर्भवति; तस्मात् शास्त्रं संलक्ष्य यथाविधि समाचरेत् ॥
> *sthāpakānāṃ sabhyādhvaryoḥ hotuḥ pouṇḍarīkādhvaryoḥ ca ācārya-dakṣiṇā-caturthāṃśaṃ, tadardham āhavanīyādhvaryūṇāṃ, tadardham anya-padārthināṃ ca dadyāt. yajñasya dakṣiṇā jīvo bhavet; alpa-dakṣiṇā-yāgo yajamānaṃ bhrūṇahatyāṃ prāpayati iti eke. tasmād avaśyaṃ dakṣiṇādānaṃ yathoktaṃ kuryāt. dravyahīne tad-grāma-yajamānasya api dravyā-hāniḥ, kriyāhīne puṇya-kriyā-hāniḥ, mantrahīne svādhyāya-āyuṣya-hāniḥ, śraddhā-bhakti-hīne sarva-hānirbhavati; tasmāt śāstraṃ saṃlakṣya yathāvidhi samāceret.*

#### English Translation
*   **Preparatory Morning Purifications:** In the morning, after bathing, sipping water (*ācamana*), performing the dawn-worship (*sandhyā*), offering libations (*tarpaṇa*) to the deities three times, and performing the *brahma-yajña*, the priest enters the temple. Reciting *"pra tad viṣṇuḥ"* (Ṛg Veda 1.22.20), he circumambulates the temple clockwise, enters the sacrificial hall (*yajña-śālā*), and goes to the pit made in the pedestal (*arcā-pīṭha-avaṭa*).

*   **The Pedestal Grid and Centering:** He divides this pit into twenty-five compartments (*pañca-viṃśati-bhāga*). Taking the gems that have undergone preliminary consecration (*adhivāsita-ratnāni*), he worships Brahmā and the directional deities like Indra there. Having cleaned the flowers, etc., and chanting *"tam ekanemi"*, he touches the pit and places a *kūrca* (bunch of grass) in the central space (*brahma-sthāna*).

*   **Deposition of the Nine Primary Gems:**
    *   **Center (Brahma-sthāna):** Places the *brahma-maṇi* (emerald) with the mantra *"brahmā devānām"*.
    *   **East (Aindra):** Places the diamond (*vajra*) with *"indraṃ pravaṇantam"*.
    *   **Southeast (Āgneya):** Places the pearl (*mauktika*) with *"agnāv agniḥ"*.
    *   **South (Yāmya):** Places the cat's eye (*vaiḍūrya*) with *"yamo dādhāra"*.
    *   **Southwest (Nairṛta):** Places the conch-gem (*śaṅkhaja*) with *"vasavaḥ prathamaḥ"*.
    *   **West (Vāruṇa):** Places the crystal (*sphāṭika*) with *"ete śataṃ varuṇa"*.
    *   **Northwest (Vāyavya):** Places the topaz (*puṣyaka*) with *"marutaḥ paramātmā"*.
    *   **North (Saumya):** Places the moonstone (*candrakānta*) with *"somaṃ rājānam"*.
    *   **Northeast (Aiśānya):** Places the sapphire (*nīla*) with *"īśānaḥ"*.

*   **Deposition of intermediate Gems:**
    *   **East-Southeast:** Places *amṛtāśma* (amber) with *"śanno nidhattām"*.
    *   **South-Southeast:** Places *lohitākṣa* (carnelian) with *"dhātā dhātṝṇām"*.
    *   **South-Southwest:** Places *utpala-maṇi* (lapis/blue lotus-gem) with *"viśvedevasya"*.
    *   **Southwest-West:** Places coral (*pravāla*) with *"adhi brahmā yatatām"*.
    *   **West-Northwest:** Places realgar (*manaḥ-śilā*) with *"mamāgne ardhadhānyam"*.
    *   **Northwest-North:** Places mother-of-pearl (*śuktija*) with *"apsaraḥ"*.
    *   **North-Northeast:** Places *vimala-maṇi* (clear quartz) with *"kādraveyaḥ"*.
    *   **Northeast-East:** Places *maruda-gem* with *"aryamṇaḥ kumbhī"*.

*   **Deposition of Minerals and Grains:**
    *   In the inner compartments, starting from the east, he places red ochre (*gairika*), orpiment (*haritāla*), realgar (*manaḥ-śilā*), antimony (*añjana-śyāmaka*), lead (*sīsa*), mercury (*pārata*), *saurāṣṭra* clay, and *go-rocana* (bovine gallstone) in the intermediate directions without mixing (*asaṅkara*), using the mantras of their respective directional guardians.
    *   Likewise, he places golden replicas representing the grains: *śāli* rice, *vrīhi* rice, barley (*yava*), kidney beans (*niṣpāva*), wild rice (*nīvāra*), sixty-day rice (*ṣāṣṭika*), black gram (*māṣa*), green gram (*mudga*), sesame (*tila*), and *tilvādi*.
    *   He places golden auspicious signs like the *śrīvatsa*, the five weapons (*pañcāyudha*) in the center and the four directions, and golden replicas of a tortoise (*kūrma*), cloud (*megha*), and lightning streak (*vidyul-latā*) in the central compartment.
    *   If unable to perform all these, he may place the nine gems (*nava-ratna*), eight auspicious signs (*aṣṭa-maṅgala*), five weapons, and color signs.

*   **Sealing the Pedestal Pit:** He covers the pit with a silk cloth (*dukūla*), fills it with mortar/lime plaster (*sudhā*), making it very firm. Saying *"viṣṇustvā rakṣatu"*, he sprinkles it with water, concludes the *Pauṇḍarīka* fire, and purifies the inner sanctum.

*   **Decorating the Gateway:** He smears the ground with cow dung, decorates it with flour paste (*paiṣṭyā*), hangs strings of pearls (*muktādāma*), flower garlands, etc., on the ceiling frame (*vimāna-phalaka*). He scatters flowers and parched grain (*akṣata*) on the floor. He perfumes the area with aloeswood (*agaru*) incense, lights camphor lamps, and decorates the sides of the doorway (*dvāra-pārśve*) with lamps, sprouts, and secondary pots (*upakumbha*).

*   **Guru Honorarium (Guru-Pūjā):** The patron (*yajamāna*) returns to the sacrificial hall, bows to the Lord of Gods, bows to the guru, and asks for permission. He worships the guru with garments, upper-cloths, turbans (*uṣṇīṣa*), ornaments, sandal paste, and garlands, and offers gold accompanied by water in front of the deity.

*   **Scale of Fees (Dakṣiṇā-dānam):**
    *   **Sovereign scale:** If the patron is a king, he gives sixty-four *niṣkas* of gold to the guru; if a village community, forty-eight *niṣkas*; other wealthy patrons give twenty-one *niṣkas*.
    *   **The Nine Grades of Fees:**
        1. *Uttamotama* (Highest-high): 9.25 *niṣkas*.
        2. *Uttamamadhyama* (Highest-middle): 8 *niṣkas*.
        3. *Uttamādhama* (Highest-low): 7 *niṣkas*.
        4. *Madhyamotama* (Middle-high): 6.25 *niṣkas*.
        5. *Madhyamamadhyama* (Middle-middle): 5 *niṣkas*.
        6. *Madhyamādhama* (Middle-low): 4 *niṣkas*.
        7. *Adhamotama* (Lowest-high): 3.25 *niṣkas*.
        8. *Adhamamadhyama* (Lowest-middle): 2 *niṣkas*.
        9. *Adhamādhama* (Lowest-low): 1 *niṣka*.

*   **Warning Against Deceit:** Therefore, one must not cheat according to one's capacity (*śaktiṃ na vañcayet*). If one cheats on the fees due to ignorance or greed, the priests (*padārthinaḥ*) alone obtain the spiritual fruit of the sacrifice, while the patron goes to the Raurava hell.

*   **Distribution to Officiants:** He should distribute fees to the other priests: the *Sthāpakas*, the *Sabhya-adhvaryu*, the *Hotṛ*, and the *Pauṇḍarīka-adhvaryu* receive a quarter of the guru's fee; the *Āhavanīya-adhvaryus* receive half of that; and the other assistants (*padārthins*) receive half of that.

*   **The Spiritual Value of Fees:** Fees are the life of a sacrifice; some say that a sacrifice with insufficient fees brings the sin of abortion (*bhrūṇa-hatyā*) to the patron. Therefore, one must definitely give the fees as prescribed. If there is a deficiency of materials, the patron and the village suffer loss of wealth; if there is a deficiency of ritual action, merit is lost; if there is a deficiency of mantras, scriptural study and lifespan are lost; if there is a lack of faith and devotion, everything is lost. Hence, observing the scriptures, one must conduct the ritual according to the rules.

### Section 3: The Nyāsa Mantras (Nyāsa-mantra-svarūpam)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)

#### English Translation
*   **Characteristics of the Nyāsa Mantras:** I shall now describe the nature of the *nyāsa* (placement) mantras of these three. The priest should perform the placement while meditating on them as representing the neuter, masculine, and feminine energies; consisting of the Rig, Yajur, and Sama Vedas; shining like white, gold, and dark complexions; clad in red, dark, and white garments; holding a sword, a staff, and a lotus; and possessing cheerful, cool, and gentle eyes.

*   **Mantra Attributes:** He should remember that for these three, the seer is Āstika, the meter is Triṣṭubh, and the deity is Viṣṇu. He should place the letter **Ya (य)** on the pedestal between the feet, and the letter **A (अ)** on the heart, and encircle both with *praṇavas*.

*   **The Letter Ya:** I shall describe their forms. He should place the letter **Ya (य)**, meditating on it as masculine, having a golden splendor, wearing white garments, two-armed, and holding a staff of flowers. He should remember that its seer is Antaryāmī (the Inner Ruler), its meter is Devī-Gāyatrī, and its deity is Vāyu (Wind).

*   **The Letter A:** He should place the letter **A (अ)**, meditating on it as masculine, shining like a Kadamba flower, wearing yellow garments, mounted on the power of majesty (*vaibhavī-śakti*), four-armed, showing the gesture of protection (*abhaya*) and resting a hand on his hip, and holding the discus and conch. He should remember that its seer is Brahmā, its meter is Devī-Gāyatrī, and its deity is the Paramapuruṣa (Supreme Spirit).

*   **Writing the Syllables:** Having meditated on these thus, he should write the letters in their refined Sanskrit script forms. Each letter should be individually encircled with *praṇavas* (Om). We shall explain the nature of the *praṇava* later.

### Section 4: The Order of Invocation (Āvāhana-kramaḥ)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **आवाहनक्रमः**
> 'इदं विष्णुः' (ऋ॰ १-२२-१७) इति मन्त्रेण कुम्भस्थं माधवं (माधवम्) शक्तियुतं कूर्चेन आदाय 'आयातु भगवान्' इति बिम्बस्य मूर्ध्नि 'विष्णुम् आवाहयामि' इति संश्राव्य आवाहयेत्।
> ***āvāhanakramaḥ***
> *'idaṃ viṣṇuḥ' (ṛg. 1.22.17) iti maqtreṇa kumbhasthaṃ mādhavaṃ śaktiyutaṃ kūrcena ādāya 'āyātu bhagavān' iti bimbasya mūrdhni 'viṣṇum āvāhayāmi' iti saṃśrāvya āvāhayet.*
> 
> तत्-पीठ-परितः पुरुषादि-आवरण-त्रय-देवानां तत्-तद्-नाम्ना समावाह्य; श्रीदेव्याः दक्षिणे 'श्रीकारं' (श्रीं), वामे भूमेः 'लकारं' (लं) च हृदये संन्यस्य, तत्-तत्-मन्त्रेण आवाह्य;
> *tat-pīṭha-paritaḥ puruṣādi-āvaraṇa-traya-devānāṃ tat-tad-nāmnā samāvāhya; śrīdevyāḥ dakṣiṇe 'śrīkāraṃ' (śrīṃ), vāme bhūmeḥ 'lakāraṃ' (laṃ) ca hṛdaye saṃnyasya, tat-tat-mantreṇa āvāhya;*
> 
> ततो ब्रह्मणः 'मकारं' (मं), शङ्करस्य 'शकारं' (शं), मार्कण्डेयस्य (पुण्यस्य) 'मकारं' (मं), भृगोः च 'भकारं' (भं) बीजाक्षरं संन्यस्य, तत्-तत्-मन्त्रैः तत्-तत्-नाम-मन्त्रं संयोज्य आवाहयेत् (वै० म० ८)।
> *tato brahmaṇaḥ 'makāraṃ' (maṃ), śaṅkarasya 'śakāraṃ' (śaṃ), mārkaṇḍeyasya 'makāraṃ' (maṃ), bhṛgoḥ ca 'bhakāraṃ' (bhaṃ) bījākṣaraṃ saṃnyasya, tat-tat-mantraiḥ tat-tad-nāma-mantraṃ saṃyojya āvāhayet (vai. ma. 8).*
> 
> 'संयक्तम् एतत्' (वै० प्र० ८) इति ध्रुव-कौतुकयोः सम्बन्ध-कूर्चं प्रक्षिप्य, कौतुकादीनां पूर्वोक्त-न्यासं च कृत्वा; सौवर्णादि-पात्रे शङ्खे वा पूतवारिणा आपूर्य, पुष्प-अक्षत-कुश-कूर्चान् विन्यस्य, दीपात् दीपम् इव ध्रुवबेरात् शक्तिं प्रणिधि-जले तथा ध्यायन्, कौतुके बिम्बे समावाह्य, तद्-दक्षिणे देवीं श्रियं, वामे हरिणीं (भूमिं) च आवाहयेत्। तथैव स्नपन-उत्सव-बलि-बेरेषु क्रमेण आवाहयेत्।
> *'saṃyaktam etat' (vai. pra. 8) iti dhruva-kautukayoḥ sambandha-kūrcaṃ prakṣipya, kautukādīnāṃ pūrvokta-nyāsaṃ ca kṛtvā; souvarṇādi-pātre śaṅkhe vā pūtavāriṇā āpūrya, puṣpa-akṣata-kuśa-kūrcān vinyasya, dīpāt dīpam iva dhruvaserāt śaktiṃ praṇidhi-jale tathā dhyāyan, kautuke bimbe samāvāhya, tad-dakṣiṇe devīṃ śriyaṃ, vāme hariṇīṃ (bhūmiṃ) ca āvāhayet. tathāiva snapana-utsava-bali-bereṣu krameṇa āvāhayet.*
> 
> कुम्भस्थ-उदकेन कौतुकं परितः सुभद्रादि-आवरण-त्रय-देवान् आवाह्य, अन्येषु परिवार-बिम्बेषु तत्-तत्-बीजं विन्यस्य, तत्-तत्-मन्त्रैः तत्-तत्-नाम-मन्त्रैः च संयोज्य आवाहयेत्।
> *kumbhastha-udakena kautukaṃ paritaḥ subhadrādi-āvaraṇa-traya-devān āvāhya, anyeṣu parivāra-bimbeṣu tat-tat-bījaṃ vinyasya, tat-tat-mantraiḥ tat-tat-nāma-mantraiḥ ca saṃyojya āvāhayet.*
> 
> आचार्यः स्थापकाद्यैः सहितः पुण्याहं वाचयित्वा ब्रह्मघोषं च कारयेत्। तत्-कुम्भेनाऽभ्युक्षणं यः कुर्यात्, तस्य मनोवाक्कायकृतं पापं तत्क्षणाद् एव नश्यति ॥
> *ācāryaḥ sthāpakādyaiḥ sahitaḥ puṇyāhaṃ vācayitvā brahmaghoṣaṃ ca kārayet. tat-kumbhenā'bhyukṣaṇaṃ yaḥ kuryāt, tasya manovākkāyakṛtaṃ pāpaṃ tatkṣaṇād eva naśyati.*
> 
> देवं नित्यार्चना-विधानेनाऽभ्यर्च्य पायसादि हवींषि निवेद्य, होमं हुत्वा, बलिं निर्वाप्य, विधिना बलिबेरम् आराध्य, बलिं बलिबेरं च आलय-प्रदक्षिणं कारयित्वा, पश्चात् ब्राह्मणान् अन्नेन तर्पयित्वा, वैष्णवान् सम्पूज्य, तद्रात्रौ ध्वजारोहणं कृत्वा, उत्सवोक्त-क्रमेण उत्सवं कारयेत्। अशक्तश्चेत् तद्रात्रौ ग्रामम् आलयं वा प्रदक्षिणं कारयित्वा, स्नपनोक्त-क्रमेण स्नपनं कुर्यात् ॥
> *devaṃ nityārcanā-vidhānenā'bhyarcya pāyasādi havīṃṣi nivedya, homaṃ hutvā, baliṃ nirvāpya, vidhinā baliberam ārādhya, baliṃ baliberaṃ ca ālaya-pradakṣiṇaṃ kārayitvā, paścāt brāhmaṇān annena tarpayitvā, vaiṣṇavān saṃpūjya, tadrātrou dhvajārohaṇaṃ kṛtvā, utsavokta-krameṇa utsavaṃ kārayet. aśaktaścet tadrātrou grāmamālayaṃ vā pradakṣiṇaṃ kārayitvā, snapanokta-krameṇa snapanaṃ kuryāt.*

#### English Translation
*   **Invoking Lord Mādhava from the Pot:** Reciting the mantra *"idaṃ viṣṇuḥ"*, the priest takes the Lord (*Mādhava*) residing in the pot along with the power (*śakti-yuta*), using the *kūrca*. Saying *"May the Lord come!"* and announcing aloud *"I invoke Viṣṇu"* upon the head of the image, he invokes Him.

*   **Invoking Enclosure and Attendant Deities:**
    *   Around the pedestal, he invokes the deities of the three enclosures starting with Puruṣa, using their respective names.
    *   On the right side of Goddess Śrī, he places the seed-syllable **Śrī (श्रीं)**, and on the left of Goddess Bhūmi, he places the seed-syllable **La (लं)** on their hearts, and invokes them with their respective mantras.
    *   Then, he places the seed-syllable **Ma (मं)** for Brahmā, **Śa (शं)** for Śaṅkara, **Ma (मं)** for Mārkaṇḍeya, and **Bha (भं)** for Bhṛgu on their hearts, and combining them with their respective mantras and name-mantras, he invokes them.

*   **Connecting the Fixed and Movable Images:**
    *   Chanting *"saṃyaktam etat"*, he places the connecting *kūrca* (cord of grass) between the fixed image (*dhruva-bera*) and the movable image (*kautuka-bera*). He performs the aforementioned placement (*nyāsa*) for the movable image, etc.
    *   Filling a golden or silver vessel or a conch shell with purified water, placing flowers, parched grains, kuśa grass, and a *kūrca* therein, and meditating on the transference of power like lighting one lamp from another (*dīpād dīpam iva*) from the fixed image into the water of the vessel, he invokes the energy into the movable image.
    *   He invokes Goddess Śrī on His right, and Goddess Harinī (Bhūmi) on His left. In the same manner, he invokes the energies in the bathing, festival, and offering images in order.

*   **Enclosure Invocations and Astrological Rites:**
    *   With the water from the main pot (*kumbha*), he invokes the deities of the three enclosures starting with Subhadra around the movable image. In the other family/subsidiary shrines (*parivāra-bera*), he places their respective seed-syllables, and combining them with their respective mantras and name-mantras, he invokes them.
    *   The Ācārya, along with the assistant priests (*sthāpakas*), performs the recitation of the auspicious day (*puṇyāha-vācana*) and causes the chanting of the Vedas (*brahma-ghoṣa*). Anyone who receives the sprinkling (*abhyukṣaṇa*) from that main pot is instantly freed from sins committed by mind, speech, and body.

*   **Daily Worship and Procession:**
    *   Having worshipped the Lord according to the rules of daily worship, offered sweet rice (*pāyasa*) and other food oblations, performed the fire offering (*homa*), distributed the offerings (*bali*), and worshipped the offering image (*bali-bera*), he leads the offering image in a clockwise procession around the temple.
    *   Afterward, having satisfied the Brahmins with food and worshipped the Vaishnavas, he performs the flag-hoisting (*dhvajārohaṇa*) that night and conducts the festival according to the rules. If unable, he should lead the images in a clockwise procession around the village or the temple that night, and perform the bathing ceremony (*snapana*) according to the bathing rules.

    3. *Akṣi-mocana:* Opening the eyes of the image.

    23. *Brāhmaṇa-bhojana & Vaiṣṇava-pūjana:* Feeding the Brahmins and honoring the Vaishnavas.

    25. *Snapana:* Perform the final bathing ceremony.

### Section 6: Phala-śruti (Spiritual Fruits of Consecration)

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **फलश्रुतिः -**
> एवं यः कुरुते भक्त्या विष्णोः स्थापनं ब्राह्मणः श्रद्दधानः, तस्य मनोवाक्कायकृतं पापं क्षिप्रं नश्यति। सर्वयज्ञफलं लब्ध्वा सर्वान् कामानवाप्य, दश पूर्वान् दश अपरान् आत्मानं च एकविंशतिकं विष्णुलोकं गमयित्वा, तद्विष्णोः परमं सायुज्यपदं प्राप्नोति।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **phalaśrutiḥ -**
> evaṃ yaḥ kurute bhaktyā viṣṇoḥ sthāpanaṃ brāhmaṇaḥ śraddadhānaḥ, tasya manovākkāyakṛtaṃ pāpaṃ kṣipraṃ naśyati. sarvayajñaphalaṃ labdhvā sarvān kāmānavāpya, daśa pūrvān daśa aparān ātmānaṃ ca ekaviṃśatikaṃ viṣṇulokaṃ gamayitvā, tadviṣṇoḥ paramaṃ sāyujyapadaṃ prāpnoti.

**Translation (English):**
*   **For Brahmins (Sāyujya):** If a devout and faithful Brahmin performs the consecration of Viṣṇu in this manner, his sins committed by mind, speech, and body are destroyed quickly. Obtaining the fruit of all sacrifices and achieving all desires, he leads ten generations before him, ten generations after him, and himself (twenty-one in total) to the world of Viṣṇu, and obtains the supreme state of union (*sāyujya*) with Viṣṇu.

**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**

**Translation (English):**
*   **For Kṣatriyas (Sārūpya):** A Kṣatriya, obtaining victory, wealth, and fame, possessing undiminished treasuries and imperishable strength, revered by all kings, rules the earth bounded by the oceans as a sovereign ruler (*cakravartin*), and obtains the state of similarity (*sārūpya*) to Viṣṇu.

**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
