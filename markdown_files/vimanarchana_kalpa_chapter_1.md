# Vimanarchana Kalpa of Sage Marīci (Vaikhānasa Āgama)
## Chapter 1: Ṛṣipraśnottara-Paṭalaḥ (The Question of Sages and the Answer of Marīci)

This document contains the Sanskrit text, IAST transliteration, and English translation of the 1st Chapter (*Prathamaḥ Paṭalaḥ*) of the **Vimānārcanākalpa** (attributed to Sage Marīci, representing the Vaikhānasa school of Vaishnavism). 

This chapter acts as the introductory section of the text, presenting the setting (sages questioning Sage Marīci), establishing the absolute supremacy of Lord Viṣṇu based on Vedic authority, defining the term *Vimānārcana*, declaring the spiritual benefits of this worship, outlining the selection of priests, and explaining the manifest (*sakala*) and unmanifest (*niṣkala*) forms of meditation.

> अविघ्नमस्तु ।
> *śrīnivāsaparabrahmaṇe namaḥ.*
> *śrīmadvikhanasamahāgurave namaḥ.*
> *avighnamastu.*
> 
> शुक्लाम्बरधरं विष्णुं शशिवर्णं चतुर्भुजम् ।

#### English Translation
*   Salutations to Śrīnivāsa, the Supreme Brahman!

*   Salutations to Śrī Vikhanas, the great primordial Guru!

*   May there be no obstacles!

*   **Verse 1:** One should meditate on Lord Viṣṇu, who is clad in white garments, who is of the color of the moon, who has four arms, and who possesses a serene/smiling countenance, for the pacification of all obstacles.

*   **Verse 2:** I bow to Śrī Venkatesa, the supreme Guru and Lord, who is like the moon to the blue lotus eyes of Goddess Śrī (Lakṣmī), who is the Lord of Sheshachala (Tirumala), whose pair of lotus feet are worshipped by Brahmā and other deities, who is a festive celebration for the eyes of the entire world, who is ever praised by the assembly of sages beginning with the descendants of Vasistha and Sanaka, and who bestows all desires upon His devotees.

*   **Verse 3:** May the face of Lord Venkatesa be present in my heart—the face adorned with a golden vertical mark (*ūrdhvapuṇḍra*), a crown that is never removed (*ajahan-mukuṭa*), a beautiful nose, a gentle smile, beautiful cheeks adorned with alligator-shaped earrings (*makara-kuṇḍala*), lips red like the bimba fruit, and glances of abundant and long-lasting compassion.

*   **Verse 4:** May all auspiciousness be unto Lord Śrīnivāsa, who resides on the Venkata hill, who is the beloved consort of Goddess Lakṣmī, who is the reservoir of all auspiciousness, and who is a treasure house for those who seek His grace.

*   **Verse 1 (Kṛṣṇa):** I bow to Lord Kṛṣṇa, the beloved Lord of Rukmini, who possesses the beauty of crores of Cupids, and who is like all the Gandharvas in elegance.

*   **Verse 2 (Vikhanas):** I bow to Sage Vikhanas, who is like an ocean filled with the rivers of Śruti (Vedas) and Smṛti (scriptures), agitated by the waves of Sastras (treatises), and purified by the water of devotion to Lord Viṣṇu.

### Section 2: The Sages' Inquiry (Ṛṣi-praśnaḥ)

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **मरीचिं प्रति ऋषिप्रश्नः**
> श्रीमरीचिं महामुनिं स्वशिष्यार्पितकुशासने समासीनं सर्वज्ञं जितेन्द्रियं शुद्धं नानालोकनिवासिनो धर्मसंसक्ता ऋषयः समागम्य, आनम्य, "भगवन्! केन मार्गेण कैः मन्त्रैः कं देवम् अर्चयन्तः कान् लोकान् गमिष्यन्ति?" इत्यूचुः।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **marīciṃ prati ṛṣipraśnaḥ**
> śrīmarīciṃ mahāmuniṃ svaśiṣyārapitakuśāsane samāsīnaṃ sarvajñaṃ jitendriyaṃ śuddhaṃ nānālokanivāsino dharmasaṃsaktā ṛṣayaḥ samāgamya, ānamya, "bhagavan! kena mārgeṇa kaiḥ mantraiḥ kaṃ devam arcayantaḥ kān lokān gamiṣyanti?" ityūcuḥ.

**Translation (English):**
*   **The Sages' Question to Sage Marīci:** 
    *   The sages residing in various worlds, who were deeply devoted to Dharma, approached the great sage, Śrī Marīci—who was sitting on a seat of Kusa grass offered by his disciples, who was all-knowing, who had conquered his senses, and who was pure. 
    *   Having bowed to him, they asked:
        > *"O Lord! By what path, with what mantras, and worshipping which deity, will people attain which worlds?"*

### Section 3: The Supremacy of Lord Viṣṇu (Viṣṇoḥ Sarvottamatve Śrutiḥ)

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **मरीच्युक्तमुत्तरम्**
> मरीचिरुवाच — सुप्रसन्नं परमात्मानं नारायणं ध्यात्वा अभिवन्द्य श्रुत्यनुकूलेन मार्गेण चतुर्वेदोद्भवैर्मन्त्रैः तमर्चयन्तः श्रुतिभिरभिहितं शाश्वतम् अतीन्द्रियं परात्परतरं देवैरनभिलक्ष्यं तद्विष्णोः परमं पदं गच्छेयुः (प्राप्नुवन्ति) इति।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **marīciyuktām-uttaram**
> marīciruvāca — suprasannaṃ paramātmānaṃ nārāyaṇaṃ dhyātvā abhivandya śrutyanukūlena mārgeṇa caturvedodbhavair-mantraiḥ tam-arcayantaḥ śrutibhir-abhihitaṃ śāśvatam atīndriyaṃ parātparataraṃ devair-anabhilakṣyaṃ tad-viṣṇoḥ paramaṃ padaṃ gaccheyuḥ (prāpnuvanti) iti.

**Translation (English):**
*   **The Answer of Marīci:** 
    *   Sage Marīci said:
        > *"Having meditated upon and bowed to Nārāyaṇa, the supreme Self who is exceedingly pleased, and worshipping Him through the path in accordance with the Vedas using the mantras originating from the four Vedas, they shall go to (attain) the supreme abode of Viṣṇu (*tad viṣṇoḥ paramaṁ padaṁ*), which is declared by the Vedas, eternal, beyond the senses, higher than the highest, and imperceptible even to the demigods."*

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **विष्णोः सर्वोत्तमत्वे श्रुतिः**
> श्रुतिस्तावत् — "विष्णुर्वै सर्वा देवताः", "पुरुष एवेदं सर्वम्" (ऋ० स० १०-९०-२), "स ब्रह्मा स शिवः सेन्द्रः" (तै० आ० १०-२४), "एष ब्रह्मा एष इन्द्रः एष प्रजापतिः एष सर्वा देवताः", "अग्निर्वै देवानामवमः विष्णुः परमः तदन्तरेण सर्वा अन्या देवताः" (ऐत० ब्रा० १-१-१), "यस्मात् परं नापरमस्ति किञ्चित् यस्मान्नाणीयो न ज्यायोऽस्ति कश्चित्। वृक्ष इव स्तब्धो दिवि तिष्ठत्येकस्तेनेदं पूर्णं पुरुषेण सर्वम्" (महानारायण० उ० / श्वेताश्वतर० उ० ३-९), "परं ब्रह्म परं ज्योतिः आपो ज्योतिः" इति।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **viṣṇoḥ sarvottamatve śrutiḥ**
> śrutistāvat — "viṣṇur-vai sarvā devatāḥ", "puruṣa evedaṃ sarvam" (Ṛgveda Samhitā 10.90.2), "sa brahmā sa śivaḥ sendraḥ" (Taittirīya Āraṇyaka 10.24), "eṣa brahmā eṣa indraḥ eṣa prajāpatiḥ eṣa sarvā devatāḥ", "agnir-vai devānām-avamaḥ viṣṇuḥ paramaḥ tadantareṇa sarvā anyā devatāḥ" (Aitareya Brāhmaṇa 1.1.1), "yasmāt paraṃ nāparam-asti kiñcit yasmān-nāṇīyo na jyāyo'sti kaścit | vṛkṣa iva stabdho divi tiṣṭhaty-ekas-tenedaṃ pūrṇaṃ puruṣeṇa sarvam" (Mahānārāyaṇa Up. / Śvetāśvatara Up. 3.9), "paraṃ brahma paraṃ jyotiḥ āpo jyotiḥ" iti.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Scriptural Proofs of Viṣṇu's Supremacy:** 
    *   The Śruti (Veda) indeed declares:
        *   *"Viṣṇu indeed is all the deities"* (*viṣṇur-vai sarvā devatāḥ*).
        *   *"Puruṣa indeed is all this (what has been and what will be)"* [Rigveda Samhita 10.90.2].
        *   *"He is Brahmā, He is Śiva, He is Indra"* [Taittiriya Aranyaka 10.24].
        *   *"He is Brahmā, He is Indra, He is Prajāpati, He is all the deities."*
        *   *"Agni is the lowest of the deities, and Viṣṇu is the highest; between them reside all other deities"* [Aitareya Brahman 1.1.1].
        *   *"There is nothing higher or lower than Him; nothing smaller or larger than Him. He stands firm like a tree in the heavens, and by that Puruṣa is all this filled"* [Mahanarayana Upanisad / Svetasvatara Upanisad 3.9].
        *   *"He is the Supreme Brahman, the Supreme Light, the Light of Waters"* (*paraṃ brahma paraṃ jyotiḥ āpo jyotiḥ*).

### Section 4: Definition & Benefits of Vimānārcana

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **विमानार्चनपदनिरुक्तिः**
> तस्मात् — परं ब्रह्म परं ज्योतिरक्षरं सर्वभूतात्मकं सर्वाधारं सनातनं परमपुरुषमर्चयेत् — तत् — विमानार्चनम्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **vimānārcanapadaniruktiḥ**
> tasmāt — paraṃ brahma paraṃ jyotir-akṣaraṃ sarvabhūtātmakeṃ sarvādhāraṃ sanātanaṃ paramapuruṣam-arcayet — tat — vimānārcanam.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Etymology of Vimānārcana:** 
    *   Therefore, worshipping the Supreme Brahman, the Supreme Light, the Imperishable, who is the soul of all beings, the support of all, and eternal—that Supreme Puruṣa—is *Vimānārcanam* (temple worship).

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **अर्चनफलम्**
> ग्रामाग्रहारयोः निवासिनाम् अनाहिताग्नीनां सामान्यम् अग्निहोत्रफलदम्, आहिताग्नीनां सर्वनिष्कृतिहोमार्थं, सर्वसमृद्ध्यर्थं च भवेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **arcanaphalam**
> grāmāgrahārayoḥ nivāsinām anāhitāgnīnāṃ sāmānyam agnihotraphaladam, āhitāgnīnāṃ sarvaniṣkṛtihomārthaṃ, sarvasamṛddhyarthaṃ ca bhavet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **The Fruits of Worship:** 
    *   For the residents of villages and Agrahara settlements, this worship brings:
        *   For those who do not maintain the sacred fires (*anāhitāgni*), the general benefits of Agnihotra (daily fire offering).
        *   For those who maintain the sacred fires (*āhitāgni*), it serves as a complete expiation (*sarva-niṣkṛti*) of all omissions in their fire offerings, and brings all-round prosperity.

#### Verse / Paragraph 3
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **तद्विधिपूर्वकं विखनसोक्तस्य विस्तरेण कथनप्रतिज्ञा**
> तस्मात् — "अग्नौ नित्यहोमान्ते विष्णोर्नित्यार्चा गृहे देवायतने वा भक्त्या भगवन्तं नारायणमर्चयेत्" इति विखनसा सूत्रे समासेनोक्तं, तदहम् अशेषविशेषेण समन्त्रक-सप्रयोगं क्रियामार्गक्रमेण विस्तरतो वक्ष्यामि।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **tadvidhipūrvakeṃ vikhanasoktasya vistareṇa kathanapratijñā**
> tasmāt — "agnau nityahomānte viṣṇor-nityārcā gṛhe devāyatane vā bhaktyā bhagavantaṃ nārāyaṇam-arcayet" iti vikhanasā sūtre samāsenoktaṃ, tad-aham aśeṣaviśeṣeṇa samantraka-saprayogaṃ kriyāmārgakrameṇa vistarato vakṣyāmi.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Vow to Detail the Method:** 
    *   Therefore, that which was briefly declared by Sage Vikhanas in the Smarta/Grihya Sutra: 
        > *"At the end of the daily fire sacrifice, one should worship the eternal form of Viṣṇu in the home or temple with devotion,"*
    *   I (Marīci) shall declare that method in detail in the order of the path of action (*kriyā-mārga*), with all its specific details, along with the corresponding mantras and practical applications.

#### Verse / Paragraph 4
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **आधारादीनां गृह्ये द्रष्टव्यता**
> तस्मात् यत्र यत्र होमविधानं तत्र तत्र अग्न्याधार-स्विष्टकृत्प्रभृत्यन्तहोमाश्च अस्य मन्त्राः वैखानससूत्रे (गृह्यसूत्रे) द्रष्टव्याः।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **ādhārādīnāṃ gṛhye draṣṭavyatā**
> tasmāt yatra yatra homavidhānaṃ tatra tatra agnyādhāra-sviṣṭakṛt-prabhṛty-antahomāś-ca asya mantrāḥ vaikhānasasūtre draṣṭavyāḥ.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Consulting the Grihya Sutra for Fire Offerings:** 
    *   Therefore, wherever a fire sacrifice (*homa*) is prescribed, the rules for establishing the fire (*agnyādhāra*), the *āghāra* offerings, the offerings to *Sviṣṭakṛt*, and the final offerings, along with their mantras, should be consulted in the Vaikhānasa Sutra (Gṛhya Sūtra).

### Section 5: The Authority of Worship and Manifestation through Meditation

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **भगवदर्चनेश्रुतिः**
> तस्यार्चने श्रुतिः — "प्र वः पान्थमन्धसो धियायते महे शूराय विष्णवे चार्चत" (ऋ० स० १-१५४-२), "धृतोर्ध्वपुण्ड्रः परमीशितारं नारायणं पूजयति स्म भक्त्या, अर्घ्यादिभिः पुरुषसूक्तमन्त्रैः संप्राप्नुयाद् विष्णुपदं महात्मा" इति।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **bhagavadarcaneśrutiḥ**
> tasyārcane śrutiḥ — "pra vaḥ pāntham-andhaso dhiyāyate mahe śūrāya viṣṇave cārcata" (Ṛgveda Samhitā 1.154.2), "dhṛtordhvapuṇḍraḥ paramīśitāraṃ nārāyaṇaṃ pūjayati sma bhaktyā, arghyādibhiḥ puruṣasūktamantraiḥ saṃprāpnuyād viṣṇupadaṃ mahātmā" iti.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Vedic Authority on Worshipping the Lord:** 
    *   The Śruti declares regarding His worship:
        *   *"Worship Him, O ye wayfarers, with food and meditation; worship the great hero Viṣṇu"* [Rigveda Samhita 1.154.2].
        *   *"He who wears the vertical sectarian mark (*ūrdhvapuṇḍra*) and worships the Supreme Ruler Nārāyaṇa with devotion, using the mantras of the Puruṣa Sūkta along with offerings of water (*arghya*), etc., that great-souled one shall attain the abode of Viṣṇu."*

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **ध्यानाद्भगवतः सकलत्वाऽभिव्यक्तिकथनम्, तदाराधनस्य फलकथनम्**
> पद्मकोशप्रतीकाशे विश्वस्यायतने पृथौ, हृदयेऽग्निशिखा परमात्मानं व्यवस्थितं ध्यानेन पश्येत्। यथा आकाशगतो वायुः विहरणेन सन्निहितो भवति, तथा तत्र अचलं मनः कृत्वा तद्भक्त्या तेजोभासुरं रुक्मवर्णं रक्तास्यं शुचिस्मितं ज्योत्स्नावभासिताधरपल्लवं रक्तनेत्रं सुस्निग्धतनुं शङ्खचक्रधरं मकुट-हार-केयूरादि-भूषणैर्भूषितं सकलं शाश्वतं सर्वभूतेषु अशरीरम् अवस्थितम् अतिसूक्ष्मम्, अनिर्देश्यम्, अतिमात्रम्, अतीन्द्रियम्, अव्यक्तं प्रकृतेर्मूलम्, अनादिनिधनम् अखिलजगत्सृष्टिस्थितिलयकारणम् अचिन्त्यं निर्गुणं अप्रमेयम् अप्रमत्तं सत्तामात्रम् इति ज्ञात्वा ध्यायेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **dhyānādbhagavataḥ sakalatvābhiyaktikathanam, tadārādhanasya phalakathanam**
> padmakośapratīkāśe viśvasyāyatane pṛthau, hṛdaye'gniśikhā paramātmānaṃ vyavasthitaṃ dhyānena paśyet. yathā ākāśagato vāyuḥ viharaṇena sannihito bhavati, tathā tatra acalaṃ manaḥ kṛtvā tadbhaktyā tejobhāsuraṃ rukmavarṇaṃ raktāsyaṃ śucismitaṃ jyotsnāvabhāsitādharapallaveṃ raktanetraṃ susnigdhatanuṃ śankhacakradharaṃ makuṭa-hāra-keyūrādi-bhūṣaṇairbhūṣitaṃ sakalaṃ śāśvataṃ sarvabhūteṣu aśarīram avasthitam atisūkṣmam, anirdeśyam, atimātram, atīndriyam, avyaktaṃ prakṛtermūlam, anādinidhanam akhilajagatsṛṣṭisthitilayakāraṇam acintyaṃ nirguṇam aprameyam apramattaṃ sattāmātram iti jñātvā dhyāyet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **The Nature of Meditation on the Lord:** 
    *   One should visualize through meditation the Supreme Self established in the heart, which is like a lotus bud (*padma-kośa*), wide and spacious, in the form of a flame of fire.
    *   Just as the wind blowing in the sky is present everywhere through its movement, similarly, having made the mind steady and unwavering there, one should meditate with devotion on Him as:
        *   **Manifest (*Sakala*):** Radiant with light, of golden color (*rukma-varṇa*), with a red mouth (*raktāsya*), a pure smile (*śuci-smita*), a lower lip illuminated like a tender leaf by moonlight, red eyes (*rakta-netra*), a very smooth and elegant body (*susnigdha-tanu*), holding the Conch and Disc (*śaṅkha-cakra-dhara*), and adorned with ornaments like the crown (*makuṭa*), necklaces (*hāra*), and armlets (*keyūra*).
        *   **Unmanifest (*Niṣkala*):** Eternal (*śāśvata*), abiding in all beings, formless (*aśarīra*), extremely subtle (*atisūkṣma*), indescribable (*anirdeśya*), immeasurable (*atimātra*), beyond the senses (*atīndriya*), unmanifest (*avyakta*), the root of primordial nature (*prakṛter mūlam*), without beginning or end (*anādinidhana*), the cause of the creation, sustenance, and dissolution of the entire universe, unthinkable (*acintya*), devoid of attributes (*nirguṇa*), immeasurable (*aprameya*), ever-vigilant (*apramatta*), and pure existence (*sattāmātra*).

#### Verse / Paragraph 3
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> तद्ध्यानकथनसंकल्पनात् सकलो भवति, भगवदाराधनेन ऊर्ध्वगतिर्भवति।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> taddhyānakathanasankalpanāt sakalo bhavati, bhagavadārādhanena ūrdhvagatirbhavati.

**Translation (English):**
*   **The Fruits of Meditation and Worship:** 
    *   By resolving to declare this meditation, one becomes complete/blessed (*sakala*), and by worshipping the Lord, one attains the upward path (liberation).

### Section 6: Selection of Priests & Classification of Worship

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **आचार्यादिवरणम्**
> तत्र सर्वकार्योपदेशकं वैखानससूत्रोक्त-निषेकादि संस्कारैः संस्कृतम् आचार्यम्, अर्चक-परिचारक-स्थापकादीन् ऋत्विजश्च वरयेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **ācāryādivaraṇam**
> tatra sarvakāryopadeśakaṃ vaikhānasasūtrokta-niṣekādi-saṃskāraiḥ saṃskṛtam ācāryam, arcaka-paricārakasthāpakādīn ṛtvijaś-ca varayet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **The Selection of Priests:** 
    *   For this, one should select:
        *   The Acharya (head priest) who instructs in all activities and who is consecrated/purified by the sacraments beginning with Nisheka (conception) as declared in the Vaikhānasa Sutra.
        *   The Archaka (performing priest), the Paricharaka (assistant), the Sthapaka (temple architect/builder), and other officiating priests (*ṛtvij*).

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **आराधनस्य भेदः प्रतिमार्चनस्य सहेतुकं श्रैष्ठ्यम्**
> तद् आराधनं द्विविधम् — अमूर्तं समूर्तम् इति। अग्नौ हुतम् अमूर्तम्, प्रतिमाराधनं समूर्तम्। तत् श्रेष्ठं यजमानाभावेऽपि अविच्छिन्नं भवति।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **ārādhanasyabhedaḥ pratimārcanasya sahetukaṃ śraiṣṭhyam**
> tad-ārādhanaṃ dvividham — amūrtaṃ samūrtam iti. agnau hutam amūrtam, pratimārādhanaṃ samūrtam. tat śreṣṭhaṃ yajamānābhāve'pi avicchinnāṃ bhavati.

**Translation (English):**
*   **The Two Modes of Worship:** 
    *   That worship is of two kinds: *Amūrta* (without form) and *Samūrta* (with form).
        *   Offering oblations in the fire is *Amūrta*.
        *   Worshipping the deity in the image/icon (*pratimā*) is *Samūrta*.
    *   The worship with form (*Samūrta*) is superior (*śreṣṭha*) because it remains uninterrupted (*avicchinna*) even in the absence of the patron (*yajamāna*).

#### Verse / Paragraph 3
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **विमानार्चनस्य फलजनकत्वम्**
> तद् विमानार्चनं सर्वक्रतुफलप्रदं सर्वकामप्रदं च इति ब्रह्माऽऽशंसत्। तस्मात् क्रमेण क्रियामार्गं विस्तरतो वक्ष्यामि, तद् अवहितमनसः शृणुध्वम् इति आह मरीचिः ॥

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **vimānārcanasya phalajanakatvam**
> tad-vimānārcanaṃ sarvakratuphalapradaṃ sarvakāmapradaṃ ca iti brahmā'śamsat. tasmāt krameṇa kriyāmārgaṃ vistarato vakṣyāmi, tad avahitamanasaḥ śṛṇudhvam iti āha marīciḥ.

**Translation (English):**
*   **The Efficacy of Vimanarchana:** 
    *   This *Vimānārcanam* bestows the fruits of all sacrifices and fulfills all desires; thus did Lord Brahmā declare. 
    *   *"Therefore, I shall speak of the path of action (*kriyā-mārga*) in detail. Listen to it with attentive minds!"* Thus spoke Sage Marīci.
