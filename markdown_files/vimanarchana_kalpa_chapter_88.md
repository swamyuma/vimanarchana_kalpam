# Vimanarchana Kalpa of Sage Marīci (Vaikhānasa Āgama)
## Chapter 88: Dehotpatti-vidhiḥ (Rules for the Manifestation and Origin of the Physical Body)

This document contains the Sanskrit text, IAST transliteration, and English translation of the 88th Chapter (*Aṣṭāśītitamaḥ Paṭalaḥ*) of the **Vimānārcanākalpa** (attributed to Sage Marīci, representing the Vaikhānasa school of Vaishnavism).

This chapter explains the physiological and biological process of the formation of the physical body (*deha*), from the digestion of food to embryological development. It details the three-fold classification of digested food (urine, feces, and semen/ovum), the conception process driven by the vital wind (*vāyu*) and divine destiny (*daiva-yogena*), the day-by-day and month-by-month development of the fetus in the womb, the seven bodily tissues (*saptadhātu*), and the biological factors determining the gender and features of the newborn.

---

### Section 1: The Digestion of Food and Conception

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **अथ देहोत्पत्तिप्रकारः**
> ओषधीभ्योऽन्नमुपजायते, तदन्नं त्रिविधं भवति — एकं मूत्रम्, एकं पुरीषम्, एकं पुरुषाणां शुक्लम् (or शुक्रम्), स्त्रीणां शोणितं च ।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **atha dēhōtpattiprakāraḥ**
> ōṣadhībhyō'nnamupajāyatē, tadannaṃ trividhaṃ bhavati — ēkaṃ mūtram, ēkaṃ purīṣam, ēkaṃ puruṣāṇāṃ śuklam (or śukram), strīṇāṃ śōṇitaṃ ca |

**Translation (English):**
*   **The Origin of Food:** From vegetation, food is produced (*ōṣadhībhyō'nnamupajāyatē*). That digested food is processed into three parts (*tadannaṃ trividhaṃ bhavati*): one part becomes urine (*ēkaṃ mūtram*), one part becomes feces (*ēkaṃ purīṣam*), and one part becomes the semen (*śuklam*) of men and the ovum/blood (*śōṇitam*) of women.

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> शुक्रशोणितयोः क्षीरे सर्पिरिव सर्वव्यापी मायाश्रयो भवति ।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> śukraśōṇitayōḥ kṣīrē sarpiriva sarvavyāpī māyāśrayō bhavati |

**Translation (English):**
*   **The Latent Power:** Within the semen and ovum (*śukraśōṇitayōḥ*), there exists the all-pervading substratum of Maya (*sarvavyāpī māyāśrayō bhavati*), just like butter latent in milk (*kṣīrē sarpiriva*).

#### Verse / Paragraph 3
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> पुरुषस्य रेत उपचितं (or रेतसः बीजं) मूलसञ्चितं शुक्लम्, स्त्रियाः कुचमूलसञ्चितं शोणितं च, संयोगकाले दैवयोगेन वायुना गर्भाशयं प्रविशति ॥

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> puruṣasya rēta upacitaṃ (or rētasāṃ bījaṃ) mūlasañcitaṃ śuklam, striyāḥ kucamūlasañcitaṃ śōṇitaṃ ca, saṃyōgakālē daivayōgēna vāyunā garbhāśayaṃ praviśati ||

**Translation (English):**
*   **Conception:** The semen accumulated in the male reproductive organ (*puruṣasya rētas bījaṃ mūlasañcitaṃ śuklam*) and the ovum/blood accumulated in the breasts/womb of the female (*striyāḥ kucamūlasañcitaṃ śōṇitaṃ ca*), enter the womb at the time of union driven by the vital wind and divine destiny (*saṃyōgakālē daivayōgēna vāyunā garbhāśayaṃ praviśati*).

### Section 2: Embryological Development (Garbha-vṛddhi)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **गर्भस्य वृद्धिक्रमः**
> एकरात्रोषितं कलिलं, द्विरात्रोषितं बुद्बुदं, त्रिरात्रोषितं मांसलं, चतुरात्रोषितं बन्धं, पञ्चरात्रोषितं (or अष्टरात्रोषितं) सुकुमारं, नवरात्रोषितं यानने (or यापनम्), दशरात्रोषितं वयसं (or वपुषं), अर्धमासे पिण्डाकृतिः, मासे शरीरकृतिः, माषद्वये शिरोबाहुप्रदेशाः, त्रिमासे जठरकटिदेशाः, चतुर्थे मासे पाणिपादं, पञ्चमे मासे रोमरूपाणि, षष्ठे मासे अस्थिसङ्घातं, सप्तमे मासे जीवप्रकाशः, अष्टमे मासे देहं देहं करोति (or देहाकारः), नवमे मासे प्रचलनं करोति, तत ऊर्ध्वं जायते ।
> ***garbhasya vṛddhikramaḥ***
> *ēkarātrōṣitaṃ kalilaṃ, dvirātrōṣitaṃ budbudaṃ, trirātrōṣitaṃ māṃsalaṃ, caturātrōṣitaṃ bandhaṃ, pañcarātrōṣitaṃ (or aṣṭarātrōṣitaṃ) sukumāraṃ, navarātrōṣitaṃ yānanē (or yāpanam), daśarātrōṣitaṃ vayasaṃ (or vapuṣaṃ), ardhamāsē piṇḍākṛtiḥ, māsē śarīrakṛtiḥ, māṣadvayē (or māsadvayē) śirōbāhupradēśāḥ, trimāsē jaṭharakaṭidēśāḥ, caturthē māsē pāṇipādaṃ, pañcamē māsē rōmarūpāṇi, ṣaṣṭhē māsē asthisaṅghātaṃ, saptamē māsē jīvaprakāśaḥ, aṣṭamē māsē dēhaṃ dēhaṃ karōti (or dēhākāraḥ), navamē māsē pracalanaṃ karōti, tata ūrdhvaṃ jāyatē |*
> 
> स्नायु-मज्जा-अस्थीनि रेतोमयानि, त्वग्-रक्त-मांसानि शोणितमयानि, एवं त्वग्-रुधिर-मांस-मेदो-अस्थि-मज्जा-शुक्लम् इति क्रमेण सप्त धातवः । सप्तधातुमयं गात्रं भवति ॥
> *snāyu-majjā-asthīni rētōmayāni, tvag-rakta-māṃsāni śōṇitamayāni | evaṃ tvag-rudhir-māṃsa-mēdō-asthi-majjā-śuklam iti kramēṇa sapta dhātavaḥ | saptadhātumayaṃ gātraṃ bhavati ||*

#### English Translation
*   **Daily Progression:** The embryo after one night is a semi-fluid mass (*ēkarātrōṣitaṃ kalilaṃ*); after two nights, it becomes a bubble (*dvirātrōṣitaṃ budbudaṃ*); after three nights, it becomes a fleshy mass (*trirātrōṣitaṃ māṃsalaṃ*); after four nights, it attains binding or structure (*caturātrōṣitaṃ bandhaṃ*); after five (or eight) nights, it becomes delicate (*pañcarātrōṣitaṃ sukumāraṃ*); after nine nights, it is capable of holding shape or transition (*navarātrōṣitaṃ yāpanam*); and after ten nights, it develops bodily features or youthful potential (*daśarātrōṣitaṃ vapuṣaṃ*).

*   **Monthly Progression:** In half a month (fifteen days), it takes the shape of a lump (*ardhamāsē piṇḍākṛtiḥ*); in one month, it takes a general body shape (*māsē śarīrakṛtiḥ*); in two months, the head and arms appear (*māsadvayē śirōbāhupradēśāḥ*); in three months, the stomach and hip areas appear (*trimāsē jaṭharakaṭidēśāḥ*); in the fourth month, the hands and feet form (*caturthē māsē pāṇipādaṃ*); in the fifth month, the hair forms (*pañcamē māsē rōmarūpāṇi*); in the sixth month, the skeletal system unites (*ṣaṣṭhē māsē asthisaṅghātaṃ*); in the seventh month, consciousness or life manifests (*saptamē māsē jīvaprakāśaḥ*); in the eighth month, it develops a fully complete body (*aṣṭamē māsē dēhākāraḥ*); and in the ninth month, the fetus moves (*navamē māsē pracalanaṃ karōti*), and thereafter is born (*tata ūrdhvaṃ jāyatē*).

*   **The Seven Tissues (Sapta-dhātu):** The tendons, marrow, and bones are derived from the paternal semen (*snāyu-majjā-asthīni rētōmayāni*); while the skin, blood, and flesh are derived from the maternal ovum (*tvag-rakta-māṃsāni śōṇitamayāni*). Thus, skin, blood, flesh, fat, bone, marrow, and semen constitute the seven bodily tissues in sequence (*tvag-rudhir-māṃsa-mēdō-asthi-majjā-śuklam iti kramēṇa sapta dhātavaḥ*). The body is composed of these seven tissues (*saptadhātumayaṃ gātraṃ bhavati*).

### Section 3: Factors Determining Gender and Characteristics

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **पुरुषत्वादि-निमित्तम्**
> शुक्राधिके पुरुषः, शोणिताधिके स्त्री, द्वयोः समत्वे नपुंसकं भवति । पुंसो वा स्त्रियो वा परस्परभावेन तयोरेको गर्भवर्णः (or गर्भविकारः) इति विज्ञायते ।
> ***puruṣatvādi-nimittam***
> *śukrādhikē puruṣaḥ, śōṇitādhikē strī, dvayōḥ samatvē napuṃsakaṃ bhavati | puṃsō vā striyō vā parasparabhāvēna tayōrēkō garbhāvarṇaḥ (or garbhagarbhavarṇaḥ) iti vijñāyatē |*

#### English Translation
*   **Determination of Gender:** If the paternal semen is dominant, a male is born (*śukrādhikē puruṣaḥ*); if the maternal ovum/blood is dominant, a female is born (*śōṇitādhikē strī*); if both are equal, a hermaphrodite is born (*dvayōḥ samatvē napuṃsakaṃ bhavati*).

*   **Determination of Complexion:** The complexion of the child (whether resembling the father or the mother) is determined by the mutual proportion and combination of the two parents' factors (*puṃsō vā striyō vā parasparabhāvēna tayōrēkō garbhāvarṇaḥ iti vijñāyatē*).

#### Colophon
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> इति श्रीवैखानसे मरीचिप्रोक्ते विमानार्चनाकल्पे देहोत्पत्तिविधिर्नाम अष्टाशीतितमः पटलः ॥८८॥ (मातृकान्तरे ८२)

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> iti śrīvaikhānasē marīciprōktē vimānārcanākalpē dēhōtpattividhirnāma aṣṭāśītitamaḥ paṭalaḥ ||88|| (mātṛkāntarē 82)

**Translation (English):**
*   **Colophon:** Thus ends the Eighty-Eighth Chapter, named *Dēhōtpatti-vidhiḥ* (Rules for the Origin of the Physical Body), in the *Vimānārcanākalpa* declared by Sage Marīci in the Śrī Vaikhānasa tradition.

### Glossary of Key Terms

1.  **Dehōtpatti (देहोत्पत्ति):** The origin, generation, or manifestation of the physical body.
2.  **Śukram / Rētas (शुक्रम् / रेतस्):** Semen, representing the paternal contribution to the physical body.
3.  **Śōṇitam (शोणितम्):** Ovum or blood, representing the maternal contribution to the physical body.
4.  **Kalilam (कलिलम्):** The initial semi-fluid or jelly-like state of the embryo.
5.  **Budbudam (बुद्बुदम्):** The bubble-like stage of early embryonic development.
6.  **Sapta-dhātu (सप्तधातु):** The seven essential bodily tissues: skin (*tvak*), blood (*rudhira*), flesh (*māṃsa*), fat (*mēdas*), bone (*asthi*), marrow (*majjā*), and reproductive essence (*śukra*).
7.  **Samatvam (समत्वम्):** Balance or equality, referring to the equal proportion of paternal and maternal factors leading to a hermaphrodite.
8.  **Garbhāśayam (गर्भाशयम्):** The womb or uterus where the embryo develops.
