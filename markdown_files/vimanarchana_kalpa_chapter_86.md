# Vimanarchana Kalpa of Sage Marīci (Vaikhānasa Āgama)
## Chapter 86: Sakala-vidhiḥ / Bhagavato Jagatsṛṣṭi-vidhiḥ (Rules for the Manifest Form and the Lord's Creation of the Universe)

This document contains the Sanskrit text, IAST transliteration, and English translation of the 86th Chapter (*Ṣaḍaśītitamaḥ Paṭalaḥ*) of the **Vimānārcanākalpa** (attributed to Sage Marīci, representing the Vaikhānasa school of Vaishnavism).

This chapter outlines the transition of the Supreme Lord Nārāyaṇa from the unmanifest (*niṣkala*) to the manifest (*sakala*) state, using classical analogies such as fire emerging from wood through churning, and clay taking forms on a potter's wheel. It describes the relationship between Viṣṇu (as the supreme agent), Śrī (as His eternal, all-pervading power of resolve, or *Mūlaprakṛti*), and the individual souls (*jīvātman* or *kṣetrajña*) who are bound by Prakriti and undergo cycles of rebirth due to beginningless ignorance (*avidyā*).

---

### Section 1: The Manifestation of the Lord (Sakalatvam)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **अथ षडशीतितमः पटलः**
> **भगवतो जगत्सृष्टिः तयोर्भेदश्च**
> ***atha ṣaḍaśītitamaḥ paṭalaḥ***
> ***bhagavato jagatsṛṣṭiḥ tayorbhedaśca***
> 
> अथ सकलः — काष्ठे अग्निः मथनात् ज्वलन् इव, निष्कलात्मको विष्णुः ध्यान-मथनेन भक्त्या सङ्कल्पनात् सकलो भवति । तस्माद् विस्फुलिङ्ग इव ब्रह्मरुद्रादिदेवतारूपैः नानात्वात्, कुलालचक्रस्थमृदः घटशरावविभेदा इव यद्रूपं मनसा भावितं तत्तद्रूपो भूत्वा विष्णुः प्रकाशते । ‘विश्वतश्चक्षुरुत विश्वतोमुखो विश्वतोबाहुरुत विश्वतस्पात् । सं बाहुभ्यां धमति सं पतत्रैर्द्यावाभूमी जनयन् देव एकः’ (Rigveda 10.81.3 / Svetasvatara Upanishad 3.3) इति श्रुतिः । तत्र सर्वव्यापी शरीरी परमात्मैव सर्वभूतानां प्रभव-रक्षण-संहृति-निमित्त-शरीरी भवति । ‘अग्निर्वै देवानामवमो विष्णुः परमस्तदन्तरेण सर्वा अन्या देवताः’ (Aitareya Brahmana 1.1.1) इति —
> *atha sakalaḥ — kāṣṭhe agniḥ mathanāt jvalan iva, niṣkalātmakō viṣṇuḥ dhyāna-mathanēna bhaktyā saṅkalpanāt sakalō bhavati | tasmād visphuliṅga iva brahmarudrādidevatārūpaiḥ nānātvāt, kulālacakrasthamṛdaḥ ghaṭaśarāvavibhedā iva yadrūpaṃ manasā bhāvitaṃ tattadrūpō bhūtvā viṣṇuḥ prakāśate | ‘viśvataścakṣuruta viśvatōmukho viśvatōbāhuruta viśvataspāt | saṃ bāhubhyāṃ dhamati saṃ patatrairdyāvābhūmī janayan dēva ēkaḥ’ iti śrutiḥ | tatra sarvavyāpī śarīrī paramātmaiva sarvabhūtānāṃ prabhava-rakṣaṇa-saṃhṛti-nimitta-śarīrī bhavati | ‘agnirvai devānāmavamō viṣṇuḥ paramastadantarēṇa sarvā anyā dēvatāḥ’ iti —*

#### English Translation
*   **The Analogy of Churning Fire:** Now is described the manifest form (*sakalaḥ*). Just as fire latent in wood blazes forth upon churning (*kāṣṭhe agniḥ mathanāt jvalan iva*), similarly the Supreme Lord Viṣṇu, who is essentially unmanifest (*niṣkalātmakō viṣṇuḥ*), becomes manifest (*sakalō bhavati*) through the churning of meditation, fueled by devotion and His own divine resolve (*dhyāna-mathanēna bhaktyā saṅkalpanāt*).

*   **Analogy of Sparks and Clay:** Therefore, just like sparks emerging from a fire appearing in the various distinct forms of deities such as Brahmā, Rudra, and others (*tasmād visphuliṅga iva brahmarudrādidevatārūpaiḥ nānātvāt*), and just like the clay on a potter's wheel is differentiated into various products like pots, saucers, and so forth (*kulālacakrasthamṛdaḥ ghaṭaśarāvavibhedā iva*), whatever form is contemplated in the mind [by the devotee] (*yadrūpaṃ manasā bhāvitaṃ*), Lord Viṣṇu manifests, assuming that very form (*tattadrūpō bhūtvā viṣṇuḥ prakāśate*).

*   **Vedic Assertions:** The Śruti declares: *"Having eyes everywhere, a face everywhere, arms everywhere, and feet everywhere, the one sole Lord, welding with His arms and wings, creates the heaven and the earth."*

*   **The Instrumental Body:** In that manifest state, the all-pervading embodied Supreme Soul Himself (*tatra sarvavyāpī śarīrī paramātmaiva*) assumes a body to serve as the instrumental cause for the creation, protection, and dissolution of all beings (*sarvabhūtānāṃ prabhava-rakṣaṇa-saṃhṛti-nimitta-śarīrī bhavati*). As the Brahmana text states: *"Agni is the lowest among the gods, Viṣṇu is the highest; between them are all the other deities."*

### Section 2: The Cosmic Power (Śri) and Material Nature (Prakṛti)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **— ब्राह्मणम् । तस्मात् प्रधानः परमोऽव्ययो विष्णुः स एव पुरुषः स एव स्रष्टा, पाता, हर्ताऽखिलस्य । तदधीनाः पुरुषाः ।**
> **श्रियो विष्णुविभूतित्वम्**
> ***— brāhmaṇam | tasmāt pradhānaḥ paramō'vyayō viṣṇuḥ sa ēva puruṣaḥ sa ēva sraṣṭā, pātā, hartā'khilasya | tadadhīnāḥ puruṣāḥ |***
> ***śriyō viṣṇuvibhūtitvam***
> 
> **तद्विष्णोर्विभूतिः श्रीः, सा च नित्या आद्यन्तरहिता अव्यक्तरूपिणी, प्रमाणाप्रमाणसाधारणभूता, विष्णोः सङ्कल्पानुरूपा, नित्यानन्दमयी, मूलप्रकृतिरूपा शक्तिः । तद्भिन्ना प्रकृत्यंशभूता पौष्णी, तद्विभूताः स्त्रियः । तदात्मिका माया प्रकृतिः, मायी विष्णुः, प्रकृतिपुरुषावेतौ अनादी, ताभ्यां लोकविकाराः सर्वे प्रवर्तन्ते; कार्यकरणकर्तृत्वे प्रकृतिः, पुरुषः सुखदुःखानां भोक्तृत्वे हेतुः, प्रकृतिस्थः पुरुषः प्रकृतिजान् गुणान् भुङ्क्ते ।**
> ***tadviṣṇōrvibhūtiḥ śrīḥ, sā ca nityā ādyantarahitā avyaktarūpiṇī, pramāṇāpramāṇasādhāraṇabhūtā, viṣṇōḥ saṅkalpanurūpā, nityānandamayī, mūlaprakṛtirūpā śaktiḥ | tadbhinnā prakṛtyaṃśabhūtā pauṣṇī, tadvibhūtāḥ striyaḥ | tadātmikā māyā prakṛtiḥ, māyī viṣṇuḥ, prakṛtipuruṣāvētau anādī, tābhyāṃ lokavikārāḥ sarvē pravartantē; kāryakaraṇakartṛtvē prakṛtiḥ, puruṣaḥ sukhaduḥkhānāṃ bhōktṛtvē hētuḥ, prakṛtisthaḥ puruṣaḥ prakṛtijān guṇān bhuṅktē |***

#### English Translation
*   **The Supreme Ruler:** Thus says the Brahmana text. Therefore, Viṣṇu is the principal (*pradhānaḥ*), supreme (*paramaḥ*), and immutable Lord (*avyayō viṣṇuḥ*); He alone is the Supreme Spirit (*sa ēva puruṣaḥ*), and He alone is the creator, protector, and destroyer of the entire universe (*sa ēva sraṣṭā, pātā, hartā'khilasya*). All other souls are subordinate to Him (*tadadhīnāḥ puruṣāḥ*).

*   **The Nature of Śrī:** Śrī is the glory and divine manifestation (*vibhūtiḥ*) of that Lord Viṣṇu. She is eternal (*nityā*), without beginning or end (*ādyantarahitā*), having an unmanifest form (*avyaktarūpiṇī*), common to both valid and invalid means of knowledge (*pramāṇāpramāṇasādhāraṇabhūtā*), acting in accordance with the resolve of Viṣṇu (*viṣṇōḥ saṅkalpanurūpā*), composed of eternal bliss (*nityānandamayī*), and is His primary power in the form of root-nature (*mūlaprakṛtirūpā śaktiḥ*).

*   **Manifestations of Śrī:** Differentiated from her is the portion of nature called Paushni (*tadbhinnā prakṛtyaṃśabhūtā pauṣṇī*), and all women are manifestations of her (*tadvibhūtāḥ striyaḥ*).

*   **Maya, Prakriti, and Puruṣa:** The Maya that is identical to Him is Prakriti (*tadātmikā māyā prakṛtiḥ*), and Viṣṇu is the Lord of Maya (*māyī viṣṇuḥ*). Both Prakriti and Puruṣa are beginningless (*prakṛtipuruṣāvētau anādī*), and from these two, all the transformations of the world proceed (*tābhyāṃ lokavikārāḥ sarvē pravartantē*). Prakriti is the cause in the production of effects and instruments (*kāryakaraṇakartṛtvē prakṛtiḥ*), while Puruṣa is the cause in the experiencing of pleasure and pain (*puruṣaḥ sukhaduḥkhānāṃ bhōktṛtvē hētuḥ*). The Puruṣa, seated in Prakriti, experiences the qualities born of Prakriti (*prakṛtisthaḥ puruṣaḥ prakṛtijān guṇān bhuṅktē*).

### Section 3: The Division of Nature (Prakṛti) and the Cycle of Rebirth

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **प्रकृतेर्द्वैविध्यादिकम्**
> **सा प्रकृतिः द्विधा भवति — चेतना अचेतना इति । अचेतना पञ्चभूतैः मनो-बुद्ध्य-अहङ्कार-रूपैः अष्टधा भिन्ना । अन्या जीवभूता चेतनेति । तथा प्रकृत्या सदासक्ताः पुरुषाः प्रकृतिस्थाः जीवात्मानः क्षेत्रज्ञा बहवः, तेऽपि नित्याः, अनाद्यविद्यासञ्चित-पुण्यपाप-फलम् भोक्तुं बहुविधं देहं प्रविश्य तत्तदात्माभिमानिनः, तत्र शुभाशुभ-कर्माणि कृत्वा तत्तत्फलानुरूपं देहं पुनः पुनः प्राप्य निवर्तन्ते (or प्राप्य वर्तन्ते) इति विज्ञायते ।**
> ***prakṛterdvaividhyādikam***
> ***sā prakṛtiḥ dvidhā bhavati — cetanā acetanā iti | acetanā pañcabhūtaiḥ manō-buddhy-ahaṅkāra-rūpaiḥ aṣṭadhā bhinnā | anyā jīvabhūtā cetanēti | tathā prakṛtyā sadāsaktāḥ puruṣāḥ prakṛtisthāḥ jīvātmānaḥ kṣētrajñā bahavaḥ, tē'pi nityāḥ, anādyavidyā-sañcita-puṇyapāpa-phalam bhōktuṃ bahuvidhaṃ dēhaṃ praviśya tattadātmābhimāninaḥ, tatra śubhāśubha-karmāṇi kṛtvā tattat-phalānurūpaṃ dēhaṃ punaḥ punaḥ prāpya nivartantē (or prāpya vartantē) iti vijñāyatē |***

#### English Translation
*   **The Twofold Prakriti:** That Prakriti is of two kinds: conscious and unconscious (*sā prakṛtiḥ dvidhā bhavati — cetanā acetanā iti*). The unconscious nature is divided eightfold, consisting of the five elements, mind, intellect, and ego (*acetanā pañcabhūtaiḥ manō-buddhy-ahaṅkāra-rūpaiḥ aṣṭadhā bhinnā*). The other, which constitutes the individual souls, is the conscious Prakriti (*anyā jīvabhūtā cetanēti*).

*   **The Many Souls:** Likewise, the souls who are always bound by Prakriti (*tathā prakṛtyā sadāsaktāḥ puruṣāḥ*), residing in Prakriti, are the individual souls, the knowers of the body (*prakṛtisthāḥ jīvātmānaḥ kṣētrajñā bahavaḥ*); they too are eternal (*tē'pi nityāḥ*).

*   **The Cycle of Karma:** To experience the fruits of merit and demerit accumulated through beginningless ignorance (*anādyavidyā-sañcita-puṇyapāpa-phalam bhōktuṃ*), they enter various kinds of bodies and identify with them (*bahuvidhaṃ dēhaṃ praviśya tattadātmābhimāninaḥ*). Performing good and bad deeds there (*tatra śubhāśubha-karmāṇi kṛtvā*), they repeatedly obtain bodies corresponding to the fruits of those actions and cycle in samsara (*tattat-phalānurūpaṃ dēhaṃ punaḥ punaḥ prāpya nivartantē*). Thus it is known.

#### Colophon
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **इति श्रीवैखानसे मरीचिप्रोक्ते विमानार्चनाकल्पे सकलविधिर्नाम षडशीतितमः पटलः ॥८६॥ (मातृकान्तरे ८०)**

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **iti śrīvaikhānasē marīciprōktē vimānārcanākalpē sakalavidhirnāma ṣaḍaśītitamaḥ paṭalaḥ ||86|| (mātṛkāntarē 80)**

**Translation (English):**
*   **Colophon:** Thus ends the Eighty-Sixth Chapter, named *Sakala-vidhiḥ* (Rules for the Manifest Form), in the *Vimānārcanākalpa* declared by Sage Marīci in the Śrī Vaikhānasa tradition.

### Glossary of Key Terms

1.  **Sakala (सकल):** The manifest, qualified, and embodied form of the Supreme Lord Nārāyaṇa, which is suitable for visualization, meditation, and temple worship (*arcanā*).
2.  **Niṣkala (निष्कल):** The unmanifest, formless, and attributeless aspect of the Supreme Brahman.
3.  **Vibhūti (विभूति):** Divine manifestation, glory, or power of the Supreme Lord Viṣṇu.
4.  **Mūlaprakṛti (मूलप्रकृति):** The root material nature or primordial energy from which the universe is created.
5.  **Pauṣṇī (पौष्णी):** A specific manifestation of the Goddess (Śrī) associated with nourishment, preservation, or the earth element.
6.  **Māyā (माया):** The creative energy or cosmic illusion of the Lord that facilitates creation.
7.  **Māyī (मायी):** The master or possessor of Maya, i.e., Lord Viṣṇu Himself.
8.  **Puruṣa (पुरुष):** The conscious spirit or individual soul; in the singular, it refers to the Supreme Spirit (Paramatman).
9.  **Kṣetrajña (क्षेत्रज्ञ):** The knower of the field, i.e., the individual soul (*jīvātman*) residing in the body (*kṣetra*).
10. **Avidyā (अविद्या):** Ignorance, specifically the beginningless spiritual ignorance that binds souls to the cycle of rebirth (*saṃsāra*).
11. **Prārabdha / Karma (कर्म):** Actions performed by the individual soul that accumulate fruits (*phala*) of merit (*puṇya*) and demerit (*pāpa*).
