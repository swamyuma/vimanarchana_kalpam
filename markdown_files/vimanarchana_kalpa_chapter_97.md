# Vimanarchana Kalpa of Sage Marīci (Vaikhānasa Āgama)
## Chapter 97: Pratyāhāra-vidhiḥ (Rules on Sensory Withdrawal and the Eighteen Vital Points)

This document contains the Sanskrit text, IAST transliteration, and English translation of the 97th Chapter (*Saptanavatitamaḥ Paṭalaḥ*) of the **Vimānārcanākalpa** (attributed to Sage Marīci, representing the Vaikhānasa school of Vaishnavism).

This chapter expounds *pratyāhāra* (sensory withdrawal), the fifth limb of the eightfold yoga. It defines *pratyāhāra* under five distinct types, focusing on the withdrawal of the senses from external objects, viewing all things as the Self, mentally relinquishing prescribed actions, raising and retaining the vital breath through the eighteen vital points (*aṣṭādaśa-marmasthānāni*), and locking the breath within the subtle channels. It lists the eighteen vital points of the human body, details their exact relative distances in *angulas*, and describes the method of drawing, retaining, and directing the vital wind through these points up to the eyebrow center to perceive the inner light.

---

### Section 1: Definition and Five Types of Pratyahara

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **अथ सप्तनवतितमः पटलः**
> **प्रत्याहारविधिः**
> ***atha saptanavatitamaḥ paṭalaḥ***
> ***pratyāhāravidhiḥ***
> 
> अथातः प्रत्याहारः पञ्चधा भवति --
> १. इन्द्रियाणां सर्वेभ्यो विषयेभ्यो बलाद् आहरणम् ।
> २. आत्मनि सर्वम् आत्मवद् ईक्षणम् ।
> ३. विहितकर्मणां विरक्तमनसा (or मनसा) त्यागः (विसर्जनम्) ।
> ४. पादाङ्गुष्ठाद् ऊर्ध्वान्तं (मूर्धान्तम्) अष्टादश-मर्मस्थानेषु वायुम् आरोप्य, धारयित्वा, स्थानात् स्थानाद् ऊर्ध्वतः अधस्तच्च समाकर्षणम् ।
> ५. नाडीमार्गेषु वायुम् आरोप्य निरोधनम् इति च ॥
> *athātaḥ pratyāhāraḥ pañcadhā bhavati --*
> *1. indriyāṇāṃ sarvēbhyō viṣayēbhyō balād āharaṇam |*
> *2. ātmani sarvam ātmavad īxaṇam |*
> *3. vihitakarmaṇāṃ viraktamanasā (or manasā) tyāgaḥ (visarjanam) |*
> *4. pādāṅguṣṭhād ūrdhvāntaṃ (mūrdhāntam) aṣṭādaśa-marmasthānēṣu vāyum ārōpya, dhārayitvā, sthānāt sthānād ūrdhvataḥ adhastacca samākarṣaṇam |*
> *5. nāḍīmārgēṣu vāyum ārōpya nirōdhanam iti ca ||*

#### English Translation
*   **The Fivefold Withdrawal:** Now, sensory withdrawal (*pratyāhāra*) is fivefold (*pañcadhā bhavati*):
    1.  **Somatic Control:** The forcible pulling back of the senses from all their respective objects (*indriyāṇāṃ sarvēbhyō viṣayēbhyō balād āharaṇam*).
    2.  **Self-Realization:** Beholding all things in the Self as the Self (*ātmani sarvam ātmavad īxaṇam*).
    3.  **Renunciation of Actions:** The mental relinquishment (or dedication) of prescribed ritual actions with a detached mind (*vihitakarmaṇāṃ viraktamanasā tyāgaḥ*).
    4.  **Vital Point Retention:** Directing the vital wind from the big toes up to the crown of the head, retaining it in the eighteen vital points, and drawing it from point to point upwards and downwards (*pādāṅguṣṭhād ūrdhvāntaṃ aṣṭādaśa-marmasthānēṣu vāyum ārōpya, dhārayitvā, sthānāt sthānād ūrdhvataḥ adhastacca samākarṣaṇam*).
    5.  **Channel Locking:** Directing the vital wind into the subtle channel pathways and locking it there (*nāḍīmārgēṣu vāyum ārōpya nirōdhanam*).

### Section 2: The Eighteen Vital Points (Marmas) and their Distances

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **अष्टादश-मर्मस्थानानि तल्लक्षणं च**
> पादाङ्गुष्ठ-गुल्फ-जङ्घामध्य-चितिमूल-जानु-जानुमध्य-पायुमूल-देहमध्य-मेढ्र-नाभि-हृदय-कण्ठ-तालुमूल-नासिकामूल-अक्षिमण्डल-भ्रूमध्य-ललाट-मूर्धानः इति अष्टादश स्थानानि ॥

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **aṣṭādaśa-marmasthānāni tallaxanaṃ ca**
> pādāṅguṣṭha-gulpha-jaṅghāmadhya-citimūla-jānu-jānumadhya-pāyumūla-dēhamadhya-mēḍhra-nābhi-hṛdaya-kaṇṭha-tālumūla-nāsikāmūla-aximaṇḍala-bhrūmadhya-lalāṭa-mūrdhānaḥ iti aṣṭādaśa sthānāni ||

**Translation (English):**
*   **The Eighteen Vital Centers:** The eighteen vital points (*marmasthānāni*) are: the big toe, the ankle, the middle of the shin, the calf root (*citimūla*), the knee, the middle of the thigh/knee, the root of the anus, the body center, the genitals, the navel, the heart, the throat, the root of the palate, the root of the nose, the eye region, the eyebrow center, the forehead, and the crown of the head (*pādāṅguṣṭha-gulpha-jaṅghāmadhya-citimūla-jānu-jānumadhya-pāyumūla-dēhamadhya-mēḍhra-nābhi-hṛdaya-kaṇṭha-tālumūla-nāsikāmūla-aximaṇḍala-bhrūmadhya-lalāṭa-mūrdhānaḥ*).

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> एतेषां प्रमाणं -- पादमानं सार्ध-चतुरङ्गुुलं, तदवसानं (गुल्फः) द्व्यङ्गुलं, ततो दशाङ्गुलं जङ्घामध्यं, ततो दशाङ्गुलं चितिमूलं, ततो नवाङ्गुलं जानु, ततो नवाङ्गुलं जानुमध्यं (pāyumūlaṃ vā), ततो द्व्यङ्गुलं देहमध्यं, ततो द्व्यङ्गुलं मेढ्रमूलं, नाभिः द्वादशाङ्गुुलं, तस्मात् दशाङ्गुुलं (or द्वादशाङ्गुुलं) हृन्मध्यं, ततो द्वादशाङ्गुुलं कण्ठकूबरं, षडङ्गुलं तालुमूलं, ततश्चतुरङ्गुुलं नासिकामूलं, अक्षिमण्डलं द्व्यङ्गुलं, द्व्यङ्गुलं भ्रूमध्यं, ततो द्व्यङ्गुलं ललाटान्तं, त्र्यङ्गुलं मूर्धेति ।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> ētēṣāṃ pramāṇaṃ -- pādamānaṃ sārdha-caturaṅgulaṃ, tadavasānaṃ (gulphaḥ) dvyangulaṃ, tatō daśāṅgulaṃ jaṅghāmadhyaṃ, tatō daśāṅgulaṃ citimūlaṃ, tatō navāṅgulaṃ jānu, tatō navāṅgulaṃ jānumadhyaṃ (or pāyumūlaṃ), tatō dvyangulaṃ dēhamadhyaṃ, tatō dvyangulaṃ mēḍhramūlaṃ, nābhiḥ dvādaśāṅgulaṃ, tasmāt daśāṅgulaṃ (or dvādaśāṅgulaṃ) hṛdmadhyaṃ, tatō dvādaśāṅgulaṃ kaṇṭhakūbaraṃ, ṣaḍaṅgulaṃ tālumūlaṃ, tataścaturaṅgulaṃ nāsikāmūlaṃ, aximaṇḍalaṃ dvyangulaṃ, dvyangulaṃ bhrūmadhyaṃ, tatō dvyangulaṃ lalāṭāntaṃ, tryangulaṃ mūrdhēti |

**Translation (English):**
*   **Distances of Vital Centers:** The measurement of their relative distances is as follows:
    *   The foot spans four and a half angulas (*sārdha-caturaṅgulaṃ*).
    *   The ankle is located at its end, spanning two angulas (*dvyangulaṃ*).
    *   Then, ten angulas to the middle of the shin (*daśāṅgulaṃ jaṅghāmadhyaṃ*).
    *   Then, ten angulas to the calf root (*daśāṅgulaṃ citimūlaṃ*).
    *   Then, nine angulas to the knee (*navāṅgulaṃ jānu*).
    *   Then, nine angulas to the middle of the knee/thigh (or root of anus) (*navāṅgulaṃ jānumadhyaṃ*).
    *   Then, two angulas to the body center (*dvyangulaṃ dēhamadhyaṃ*).
    *   Then, two angulas to the root of the genitals (*dvyangulaṃ mēḍhramūlaṃ*).
    *   The navel is located twelve angulas from the genitals (*nābhiḥ dvādaśāṅgulaṃ*).
    *   From the navel, the heart center is ten (or fourteen) angulas (*tasmāt daśāṅgulaṃ hṛdmadhyaṃ*).
    *   From the heart, the throat cavity is twelve angulas (*dvādaśāṅgulaṃ kaṇṭhakūbaraṃ*).
    *   The root of the palate is six angulas from the throat (*ṣaḍaṅgulaṃ tālumūlaṃ*).
    *   The root of the nose is four angulas from the palate (*caturaṅgulaṃ nāsikāmūlaṃ*).
    *   The eye region is two angulas from the nose (*aximaṇḍalaṃ dvyangulaṃ*).
    *   The eyebrow center is two angulas from the eyes (*dvyangulaṃ bhrūmadhyaṃ*).
    *   The end of the forehead is two angulas from the eyebrows (*daxiṇapādaṃ lalāṭāntaṃ*).
    *   The crown of the head is three (or six) angulas from the forehead (*tryangulaṃ mūrdhēti*).

### Section 3: The Practice of Sensory and Breath Withdrawal

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **तत्र वायुशमन-प्रकारः**
> तेषु स्थानेषु मनसा वायुम् आरोप्य, स्थानात् समाकृष्य, निरोधं चोर्ध्वतः अधस्तच्च यथाक्रमेण करोति । इडा-पिङ्गलाभ्यां वायुम् आपूर्य (आकृष्य), कुण्डलिनीपार्श्वयोः आपूर्य, नासाभ्याम् उदरस्थं वायुम् आकर्ष्य, पूरयित्वा, भ्रूमध्ये कूर्चे (आज्ञाचक्रे) समारोप्य, धारयति । एवं नित्यम् आचरेत् । आत्मनः प्राणवायुं सुषुम्नया भ्रूमध्यान्तं समारोप्य, यावन्मण्डलोदयं (मण्डलदर्शनं) तावत् संयमनं करोतीति विज्ञायते ॥
> ***tatra vāyuśamana-prakāraḥ***
> *tēṣu sthānēṣu manasā vāyum ārōpya, sthānāt samākṛṣya, nirōdhaṃ cōrdhvataḥ adhastacca yathākramēṇa karōti | iḍā-piṅgalābhyāṃ vāyum āpūrya, kuṇḍalinīpārśvayōḥ āpūrya, nāsābhyām udarasthaṃ vāyum ākarṣya, pūrayitvā, bhrūmadhyē kūrcē samārōpya, dhārayati | ēvaṃ nityam ācarēt | ātmanaḥ prāṇavāyuṃ suṣumnayā bhrūmadhyāntaṃ samārōpya, yāvanmaṇḍalōdayaṃ tāvat saṃyamanaṃ karōtīti vijñāyatē ||*

#### English Translation
*   **Controlling the Breath at the Centers:** At those eighteen points, one should direct the vital wind mentally (*tēṣu sthānēषु manasā vāyum ārōpya*), draw it from each point (*sthānāt samākṛṣya*), and perform retention (*nirōdhaṃ*) upwards and downwards in proper sequence (*ūrdhvataḥ adhastacca yathākramēṇa karōti*).

*   **Directing to the Brow Center:** Drawing the breath through the Ida and Pingala nostrils, filling the two sides of the Kundalini energy (*iḍā-piṅgalābhyāṃ vāyum āpūrya, kuṇḍalinīpārśvayōḥ āpūrya*), drawing the air residing in the abdomen, fully inhaling, one should raise and focus it at the *kūrca* (space between the eyebrows) and retain it (*nāsābhyām udarasthaṃ vāyum ākarṣya, pūrayitvā, bhrūmadhyē kūrcē samārōpya, dhārayati*). One should practice this daily (*ēvaṃ nityam ācarēt*).

#### Colophon
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> इति श्रीवैखानसे मरीचिप्रोक्ते विमानार्चनाकल्पे प्रत्याहारविधिर्नाम सप्तनवतितमः पटलः ॥९७॥

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> iti śrīvaikhānasē marīciprōktē vimānārcanākalpē pratyāhāravidhirnāma saptanavatitamaḥ paṭalaḥ ||97||

**Translation (English):**
*   **Perceiving the Inner Light:** Raising one's own vital breath through the central *suṣumnā* channel up to the eyebrow center (*ātmanaḥ prāṇavāyuṃ suṣumnayā bhrūmadhyāntaṃ samārōpya*), one should restrain the breath as long as the inner circle of light manifests (*yāvanmaṇḍalōdayaṃ tāvat saṃyamanaṃ karōtīti vijñāyatē*).

### Glossary of Key Terms
*   **Pratyāhāra (प्रत्याहार):** Sensory withdrawal; the inward drawing of energy and attention, moving from the gross outer world to the subtle inner regions.
*   **Marma-sthāna (मर्मस्थान):** Vital junctions or points in the physical and subtle body where energy is concentrated. Yoga identifies eighteen such principal points.
*   **Kūrca (कूर्च):** The subtle center located between the eyebrows, corresponding to the *ājñā* chakra, used as a focal point for concentration.
*   **Maṇḍalōdaya (मण्डलोदय):** The arising of the inner luminous orb or circle of light during deep concentration at the brow center.
*   **Sīvanī (सीवनी):** The perineal suture, a vital physical and energetic point located between the anus and the genitals.
