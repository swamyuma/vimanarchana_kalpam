# Vimanarchana Kalpa of Sage Marīci (Vaikhānasa Āgama)
## Chapter 87: Vaikuṇṭha-lakṣaṇam (Description of the Supreme Abode Vaikuntha)

This document contains the Sanskrit text, IAST transliteration, and English translation of the 87th Chapter (*Saptāśītitamaḥ Paṭalaḥ*) of the **Vimānārcanākalpa** (attributed to Sage Marīci, representing the Vaikhānasa school of Vaishnavism).

This chapter describes the supreme spiritual realm of the Vaishnavas (*Vaiṣṇavāṇḍam*), located above the golden cosmic egg (*hiraṇmayamaṇḍam*). It details the four distinct worlds of Viṣṇu (*Āmōda*, *Pramōda*, *Sammōda*, and *Vaikuṇṭha*) and their corresponding manifestations (Viṣṇu, Mahāviṣṇu, Sadāviṣṇu, and Nārāyaṇa). It further details the supremacy of Lord Nārāyaṇa, the creation of the cosmos by His free will (*svalīlayā*), and the birth of the four-faced creator Brahmā (*Caturmukha*) from the navel-lotus of Nārāyaṇa reclining upon Adisesha during the dissolution (*pralaya*).

### Section 1: The Abode of Viṣṇu (Vaikuṇṭha-lōka)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **अथ सप्ताशीतितमः पटलः**
> **वैकुण्ठस्वरूपं तत्र प्रमोदादिभेदश्च**
> **तेषु विमाने भगवद्रूपाणि**
> ***atha saptāśītitamaḥ paṭalaḥ***
> ***vaikuṇṭhasvarūpaṃ tatra pramōdādibhedaśca***
> ***tēṣu vimānē bhagavadrūpāṇi***
> 
> अथातः परमात्मनः सकाशाद् हिरण्मयमण्डमभवत् । तदन्तर्गतानां सर्वेषामण्डानाम्युपरि सनातनं नित्यम् अचिन्त्यं देवैरप्यनभिलक्ष्यं नित्यशुद्धबुद्धमुक्तस्वभावं पुरुषैरनुभूयमानं वैष्णवाण्डम् । तत्र विष्णुलोकाश्चत्वारः — आमोदः, प्रमोदः, सम्मोदः, वैकुण्ठम् इति । परस्परस्योपरि यथाक्रमेण भवन्ति । तस्मिन् हेममयप्राकार-चतुर्द्वार-गोपुर-तोरण-शतसहस्रैः कनककमलकुसुमादिभिरपि अलङ्कृते, अमृतजलतरङ्ग-शतसहस्रैः सरितां प्रभासमाने दिव्ये लोके सहस्रादित्यसङ्काशं हेममयं द्वादशतलैर्युतं विमानं, नित्यज्ञान-क्रिया-ऐश्वर्य-ब्रह्मादि-देव-ऋषिभिः नित्य- (or दिव्य-) परिजनैः युतं मन्दिरम् । तस्मिन् व्योमनिलये परमात्मा स्वसङ्कल्पेन देवी-भूषण-आयुधैः सह, आमोदे विष्णुः, प्रमोदे महाविष्णुः, सम्मोदे सदाविष्णुः, वैकुण्ठे सर्वव्यापी नारायणः आसीनो भवेत् ।
> *athātaḥ paramātmanaḥ sakāśād hiraṇmayamaṇḍamabhavat | tadantargatānāṃ sarvēṣāmaṇḍānāmupari sanātanaṃ nityaṃ acintyaṃ dēvairapyanabhilaxyaṃ nityaśuddhabuddhamuktasvabhāvaṃ puruṣairanubhūyamānaṃ vaiṣṇavāṇḍam | tatra viṣṇulōkāścatvāraḥ — āmōdaḥ, pramōdaḥ, sammōdaḥ, vaikuṇṭham iti | parasparasyōpari yathākramēṇa bhavanti | tasmin hēmamayaprākāra-caturdvāra-gōpura-tōraṇa-śatasahasraiḥ kanakakamalakusumādibhirapi alaṅkṛtē, amṛtajalātaraṅga-śatasahasraiḥ saritāṃ prabhāsamānē divyē lōkē sahasrādityasaṅkāśaṃ hēmamayaṃ dvādaśatalairyutaṃ vimānaṃ, nityajñāna-kriyā-aiśvarya-brahmādideva-ṛṣibhiḥ nitya- (or divya-) parijanairyutaṃ mandiram | tasmin vyōmanilayē paramātmā svasaṅkalpēna dēvī-bhūṣaṇa-āyudhaiḥ saha, āmōdē viṣṇuḥ, pramōdē mahāviṣṇuḥ, sammōdē sadāviṣṇuḥ, vaikuṇṭhē sarvavyāpī nārāyaṇaḥ āsīnō bhavēt |*

#### English Translation
*   **The Golden Egg and Vaishnavandam:** Thereafter, from the Supreme Soul, the golden cosmic egg (*hiraṇmayamaṇḍam*) came into existence (*sakāśād hiraṇmayamaṇḍamabhavat*). Above all the cosmic eggs contained within it (*tadantargatānāṃ sarvēṣāmaṇḍānāmupari*), there exists the eternal, constant, unthinkable, invisible even to the gods, eternally pure, wise, and liberated by nature, experienced by liberated souls (*sanātanaṃ nityaṃ acintyaṃ dēvairapyanabhilaxyaṃ nityaśuddhabuddhamuktasvabhāvaṃ puruṣairanubhūyamānaṃ*), the Vaishnava realm (*vaiṣṇavāṇḍam*).

*   **The Four Viṣṇu Worlds:** Within that realm, there are four worlds of Viṣṇu (*tatra viṣṇulōkāścatvāraḥ*): *Āmōda*, *Pramōda*, *Sammōda*, and *Vaikuṇṭha*. They exist one above the other in successive order (*parasparasyōpari yathākramēṇa bhavanti*).

*   **The Divine Abode:** In that resplendent divine world, decorated with hundreds of thousands of golden ramparts, four-fold gateways, towers, arches, and golden lotus flowers (*tasmin hēmamayaprākāra-caturdvāra-gōpura-tōraṇa-śatasahasraiḥ kanakakamalakusumādibhirapi alaṅkṛtē*), and beautified by hundreds of thousands of rivers with waves of nectar water (*amṛtajalātaraṅga-śatasahasraiḥ saritāṃ prabhāsamānē divyē lōkē*), stands the golden temple (*hēmamayaṃ vimānaṃ*), resplendent like a thousand suns (*sahasrādityasaṅkāśaṃ*) and consisting of twelve stories (*dvādaśatalairyutaṃ*). It is a temple filled with eternal knowledge, action, sovereignty, sages, gods like Brahmā, and eternal (or divine) attendants (*nityajñāna-kriyā-aiśvarya-brahmādideva-ṛṣibhiḥ nitya- parijanairyutaṃ mandiram*).

*   **The Four Manifestations:** In that celestial sky-abode, the Supreme Soul resides with His consorts (*devī*), ornaments (*bhūṣaṇa*), and weapons (*āyudha*) by His own will (*paramātmā svasaṅkalpēna dēvī-bhūṣaṇa-āyudhaiḥ saha*). He is seated as:
    1.  **Viṣṇu** in *Āmōda*
    2.  **Mahāviṣṇu** in *Pramōda*
    3.  **Sadāviṣṇu** in *Sammōda*
    4.  **Nārāyaṇa** (the all-pervading) in *Vaikuṇṭha*

### Section 2: The Supremacy of the Lord and Creation by Will

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **भगवतः सर्वोत्तमत्वम्, इच्छया जगत्सृष्टिः**
> ‘तमीश्वराणां परमं महेश्वरं तं देवानां परमं च दैवतम्’ (Svetasvatara Upanishad 6.7) इति श्रुतिः । “यदा यदा प्रजाः सृजेय इति सोऽकामयत, तदा तदा स्वाभिमताऽनुरूप-स्वरूप-गुण-स्वशक्त्या स्वलीलयैव इमं प्रपञ्चं कार्य-कारण-भावेन यथापूर्वं ससर्ज” इति श्रुतिः । सर्वेषां जीवानां प्रभोस्सिसृक्षोर्ब्रह्मणः स्वेच्छया भूतान्यसृज्यन्त । ‘आत्मन आकाशः सम्भूतः । आकाशाद्वायुः । वायोरग्निः । अग्नेरापः । अद्भ्यः पृथिवी । पृथिव्या ओषधयः । ओषधीभ्योऽन्नम् । अन्नाद्भूतानि जायन्ते’ (Taittiriya Upanishad 2.1) इति श्रुतिः ॥
> ***bhagavataḥ sarvōttamatvam, icchayā jagatsṛṣṭiḥ***
> *‘tamīśvarāṇāṃ paramaṃ mahēśvaraṃ taṃ dēvānāṃ paramaṃ ca daivatam’ iti śrutiḥ | “yadā yadā prajāḥ sṛjēya iti sō'kāmayata, tadā tadā svābhimatā'nurūpa-svarūpa-guṇa-svaśaktyā svalīlayēva imaṃ prapañcaṃ kārya-kāraṇa-bhāvēna yathāpūrvaṃ sasarja” iti śrutiḥ | sarvēṣāṃ jīvānāṃ prabhōssisṛxōrbrahmaṇaḥ svēcchayā bhūtānyasṛjyanta | ‘ātmana ākāśaḥ sambhūtaḥ | ākāśādvāyuḥ | vāyōragniḥ | agnērāpaḥ | adbhyaḥ pṛthivī | pṛthivyā ōṣadhayaḥ | ōṣadhībhya'nnam | annādbhūtāni jāyantē’ iti śrutiḥ ||*

#### English Translation
*   **The Supreme Deity:** The Śruti states: *"He is the Supreme Lord of lords, the Supreme Deity of deities."*

*   **Creation by Resolve:** The Śruti further states: *"Whenever He desired, 'Let Me create offspring,' at that very moment, by His own power, matching His desired forms and attributes, and through His own play, He created this universe in the form of cause and effect as it was in the past."*

*   **Cosmic Manifestation:** By the free will of Brahman (the Lord), who wished to create all living beings, the elements were produced (*sarvēṣāṃ jīvānāṃ prabhōssisṛxōrbrahmaṇaḥ svēcchayā bhūtānyasṛjyanta*). The Śruti declares: *"From the Self arose space; from space, air; from air, fire; from fire, water; from water, earth; from earth, plants; from plants, food; and from food, living beings are born."*

### Section 3: Origin of the Four-Faced Brahmā

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **चतुर्मुखस्योत्पत्तिः**
> एकदा प्रलयकाले भुजङ्गपतिभोग-पर्यङ्क-शयने शयितस्य नारायणस्य नाभिकमले ब्रह्मोद्भूतो भगवदंशश्चतुर्मुखो जगत्सर्वं सृजतीति विज्ञायते ॥

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **caturmukhasyōtpattiḥ**
> ēkadā pralayakālē bhujaṅgapatibhōga-paryaṅka-śayanē śayitasya nārāyaṇasya nābhikamalē brahmōdbhūtō bhagavadaṃśaścaturmukhō jagatsarvaṃ sṛjatīti vijñāyatē ||

**Translation (English):**
*   **The Birth of Brahmā:** It is known that once during the time of cosmic dissolution (*ēkadā pralayakālē*), when Lord Nārāyaṇa was reclining on the couch of the serpent-king Adisesha (*bhujaṅgapatibhōga-paryaṅka-śayanē śayitasya nārāyaṇasya*), from His navel-lotus arose Brahmā (*nābhikamalē brahmōdbhūtō*). Being a portion of the Lord, the four-faced one created the entire universe (*bhagavadaṃśaścaturmukhō jagatsarvaṃ sṛjatīti vijñāyatē*).

### Glossary of Key Terms

1.  **Vaiṣṇavāṇḍam (वैष्णवाण्डम्):** The "Egg of Viṣṇu" or the supreme cosmic sphere containing the highest spiritual abodes.
2.  **Āmōda (आमोद):** The first of the four spiritual realms of Viṣṇu, representing the joy of divine proximity.
3.  **Pramōda (प्रमोद):** The second of the four spiritual realms of Viṣṇu, representing delight.
4.  **Sammōda (सम्मोद):** The third of the four spiritual realms of Viṣṇu, representing intense joy/communion.
5.  **Vaikuṇṭha (वैकुण्ठ):** The fourth and highest spiritual realm, representing the state of liberation without limitation.
6.  **Sahasrādityasaṅkāśam (सहस्रादित्यसङ्काशम्):** Resplendent like a thousand suns, signifying supreme divine radiance.
7.  **Paryaṅka-śayanam (पर्यङ्क-शयनम्):** Reclining on a couch, specifically Nārāyaṇa reclining on the serpent Ananta in the yogic sleep.
8.  **Bhagavadaṃśa (भगवदंश):** A portion or expansion of the Lord's divine essence (applied here to Brahmā).
