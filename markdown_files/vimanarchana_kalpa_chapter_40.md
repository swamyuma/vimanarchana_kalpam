# Chapter 40: Consecration and Marriage of the Goddesses, and Consecration of Bathing, Festival, and Offering Icons (Devyoḥ Pṛthak Pratiṣṭhā-Vivāha-Snapanotsava-Balibera-Pratiṣṭhā-vidhiḥ)
### Section 1: Consecration, Marriage, and Consecration of Bathing, Festival, and Offering Icons

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **देव्योः श्रीभूम्योः पृथक् प्रतिष्ठा-विधिः -**
> अथ देव्योः श्रीभूम्योः पृथक् प्रतिष्ठाविधिं वक्ष्ये — ध्रुवे देवीसमायुक्ते वियुक्ते वा कौतुकस्य देव्योः स्थापनं कारयेत्। कौतुके देवीसमायुक्ते वियुक्ते वा अपी औत्सवं देवीभ्यां सहितं स्थापितं चेत् तद्-ग्रामस्य समृद्धिः भवति; यद्-द्रव्येण कृतं बिम्बं तद्-द्रव्येण देव्यौ च कारयेत्। हीनाधिकद्रव्यैः न कारयेत्। स्थानके स्थानकं, आसने च आसनं, देवस्य बाहुमानं स्तनान्तरं वा स्थानकस्य, आसनस्य कर्णान्तम् आस्यान्तं वा यथोक्त-तत्-क्रमेण देव्यौ सम्यक् प्रकल्पयेत्।
> ***devyoḥ śrībhūmyoḥ pṛthak pratiṣṭhā-vidhiḥ -***
> *atha devyoḥ śrībhūmyoḥ pṛthak pratiṣṭhāvidhiṃ vakṣye — dhruve devīsamāyukte viyukte vā kautukasya devyoḥ sthāpanaṃ kārayet. kautuke devīsamāyukte viyukte vā apī outsavaṃ devībhyāṃ sahitaṃ sthāpitaṃ cet tad-grāmasya samṛddhiḥ bhavati; yad-dravyeṇa kṛtaṃ bimbaṃ tad-dravyeṇa devyou ca kārayet. hīnādhikadravyaiḥ na kārayet. sthānake sthānakaṃ, āsane ca āsanaṃ, devasya bāhumānaṃ stanāntaraṃ vā sthānakasya, āsanasya karṇāntam āsyāntam vā yathokta-tat-krameṇa devyou samyak prakalpayet.*
> 
> **यागशाला-संस्कारः -**
> स्थापन-दिवसात् पूर्वम्, अङ्कुरान् अर्पयित्वा, पूर्ववद् अक्षिमोचन-अधिवासौ च कृत्वा; आलय-अभिमुखे यागशालां, तन्मध्ये दक्षिणे: श्रीदेव्याः शय्यावेदिं द्विहस्तायतविस्तारां तत्-तुरीयांश-उत्सेधां, तत्प्राच्याम् औपासनाग्निकुण्डं, तत्प्राच्यां स्नानश्वभ्रं च कारयेत्। तन्मध्ये च उत्तरे: शय्यावेदिं, तथैव महीदेव्याः तत्प्राच्याम् औपासनाग्निकुण्डं, तत्प्राच्यां स्नानश्वभ्रं च कारयेत्।
> ***yāgāśālā-saṃskāraḥ -***
> *sthāpana-divasāt pūrvam, aṅkurān arpayitvā, pūrvavad akṣimocana-adhivāsou ca kṛtvā; ālaya-abhimukhe yāgāśālāṃ, tanmadhye dakṣiṇe: śrīdevyāḥ śayyāvediṃ dvihastāyatavistārāṃ tat-turīyāṃśa-utsedhāṃ, tatprācyām aupāsanāgnikuṇḍaṃ, tatprācyāṃ snānaśvabhraṃ ca kārayet. tanmadhye ca uttare: śayyāvediṃ, tathaiva mahīdevyāḥ tatprācyām aupāsanāgnikuṇḍaṃ, tatprācyāṃ snānaśvabhraṃ ca kārayet.*
> 
> अधिवासगते देव्यौ समादाय, शुद्धोदकैः संस्नाप्य, वस्त्राद्यैः अलङ्कृत्य; यज्ञालये पश्चिमे विष्टरे देव्यौ संस्थाप्य, वास्तुहोमं हुत्वा, पर्यग्नि-पञ्चगव्याभ्यां शोधयित्वा; द्वयोः औपासनाग्निकुण्डयोः आघारं हुत्वा, द्वौ कुम्भौ संगृह्य;
> *adhivāsagate devyou samādāya, śuddhodakaiḥ saṃsnāpy, vastrādyaiḥ alaṅkṛtya; yajñālaye paścime viṣṭare devyou saṃsthāpya, vāstuhomaṃ hutvā, paryagni-pañcagavyābhyāṃ śodhya; dvayoḥ aupāsanāgnikuṇḍayoḥ āghāraṃ hutvā, dvou kumbhou saṃgṛhya;*
> 
> पूर्ववत् तन्तुना परिवेष्ट्य, अद्भिः प्रक्षाल्य, उत्पूतैः वारिणा आपूर्य, पञ्चरत्नानि अष्टमङ्गल-पञ्चायुध-वर्णचिह्नानि पूर्ववत् प्रक्षिप्य, वस्त्रयुग्मेन आवेष्ट्य; अभ्यन्तरं प्रविश्य, पूर्वं श्रीदेव्याः अभिमुखे धान्यराशौ कुम्भं संन्यस्य, पश्चात् महीदेव्याः अभिमुखे धान्यराशौ कुम्भं संन्यस्य; देव्योः ध्रुवबेरहीने देवस्य ध्रुवाद् अर्कमण्डलाद् वा, तत्-तत्-रूपं तथैव ध्यात्वा, कुम्भेऽम्भसि समावाह्य;
> *pūrvavat tantunā pariveṣṭya, adbhiḥ prakṣālya, utpūtaiḥ vāriṇā āpūry, pañcaratnāni aṣṭamaṅgala-pañcāyudha-varṇacihnāni pūrvavat prakṣipya, vastrayugmena āveṣṭya; abhyantaraṃ praviśya, pūrvaṃ śrīdevyāḥ abhimukhe dhānyarāśou kumbhaṃ saṃnyasya, paścāt mahīdevyāḥ abhimukhe dhānyarāśou kumbhaṃ saṃnyasya; devyoḥ dhruvaberahīne devasya dhruvād arkamanḍalād vā, tat-tat-rūpaṃ tathaiva dhyātvā, kumbhe'mbhasi samāvāhya;*
> 
> कुम्भौ समादाय, शकनुसूक्तं जप्त्वा, देवीभ्यां सह स्नानश्वभ्रे प्रतिष्ठाप्य, पूर्ववत् सप्तभिः कलशैः संस्नाप्य, वस्त्राद्यैः विभूष्य अभ्यर्च्य; शय्यावेद्यां धान्योपरि पूर्ववत् प्रत्येकम् अण्डजादीनि पञ्चशयनानि वासांसि वा आस्तीर्य, शयने समारोप्य, पुण्याहं कृत्वा, पूर्ववत् प्रतिसरं बद्ध्वा, तत्-तत्-मन्त्रेण शाययित्वा, पश्चात् होता समाहूय अभिपूजयेत्। स होता तयोः प्राच्यां तिष्ठन् हौत्रं प्रशंसयेत्।
> *kumbhou samādāya, śakanusūktaṃ japtvā, devībhyāṃ saha snānaśvabhre pratiṣṭhāpy, pūrvavat saptabhiḥ kalaśaiḥ saṃsnāpy, vastrādyaiḥ vibhūṣya abhyarcya; śayyāvedyāṃ dhānyopari pūrvavat pratyekam aṇḍajādīni pañcaśayanāni vāsāṃsi vā āstīrya, śayane samāropya, puṇyāhaṃ kṛtvā, pūrvavat pratisaraṃ baddhvā, tat-tat-mantreṇa śāyayitvā, paścāt hotā samāhūya abhipūjayet. sa hotā tayoḥ prācyāṃ tiṣṭhan hautraṃ praśaṃsayet.*
> 
> प्रत्येकं होता अध्वर्युः देव्यौ जयादि-अप्सरसश्च आवाह्य, आवाहनक्रमेण निरूप्य, आज्याहुतीः हुत्वा; दक्षिणे अग्निकुण्डे श्रीसूक्तं श्रीदेवत्यं जयादीनां मूर्तिमन्त्रान्, उत्तरे अग्निकुण्डे महीसूक्तं महीदेवत्यं तथा जयादीनां मूर्तिमन्त्रांश्च जुहुयात्।
> *pratyekaṃ hotā adhvaryuḥ devyou jayādi-apsarasaśca āvāhya, āvāhanakrameṇa nirūpya, ājyāhūtīḥ hutvā; dakṣiṇe agnikuṇḍe śrīsūktaṃ śrīdevatyaṃ jayādīnāṃ mūrtimantrān, uttare agnikuṇḍe mahīsūktaṃ mahīdevatyaṃ tathā jayādīnāṃ mūrtimantrāṃśca juhuyāt.*
> 
> **प्रातः-काले देव्याः प्रतिष्ठा -**
> पश्चात् नृत्तगेयवाद्यैः च रात्रिशेषं व्यपोह्य; प्रभाते तु आचार्याद्याः स्नात्वा आलयं प्रविशेयुः। आचार्याद्याः देव्यौ समुत्थाप्य अभ्यर्च्य, अभ्यन्तरं तत्-पीठे पञ्चरत्नानि सुवर्णं वा निक्षिपेयुः; आचार्यादिभ्यो दक्षिणां दद्यात्। अग्निं विसृज्य, सुमुहूर्ते प्राप्ते तु, आचार्यः श्रीदेव्याः कुम्भं, पश्चात् महीदेव्याः कुम्भं च शिरसा धारयन् अग्रतो गच्छेत्; स्थापकाः देव्यौ समादाय अनुगच्छेयुः।
> ***prātaḥ-kāle devyāḥ pratiṣṭhā -***
> *paścāt nṛttageyavādyaiḥ ca rātriśeṣaṃ vyapohya; prabhāte tu ācāryādyāḥ snātvā ālayaṃ praviśeyuḥ. ācāryādyāḥ devyou samutthāpya abhyarcya, abhyantare tat-pīṭhe pañcaratnāni suvarṇaṃ vā nikṣipeyuḥ; ācāryadibhyo dakṣiṇāṃ dadyāt. agniṃ visṛjya, sumuhūrte prāpte tu, ācāryaḥ śrīdevyāḥ kumbhaṃ, paścāt mahīdevyāḥ kumbhaṃ ca śirasā dhārayan agrato gacchet; sthāpakāḥ devyou samādāya anugaccheyuḥ.*
> 
> सर्ववाद्यसमायुक्तं देवागारं प्रदक्षिणीकृत्य, अभ्यन्तरं प्रविश्य, ध्रुवस्य अभिमुखे धान्यराशौ द्वौ कुम्भौ न्यसेत्। कौतुकस्य दक्षिण-वामयोः कृते पीठे देव्यौ तत्-तत्-मन्त्रेण स्थापयेयुः। आचार्यः च उत्तराभिमुखः तिष्ठन् ध्रुवबेर-अक्षरन्यासं कृत्वा, पूर्वम् आवाह्य, पश्चात् कौतुके अक्षरन्यासं कृत्वा, तद्वद् देव्यौ ध्यायन् दीपाद्वीपम् इव आवाहयेत्। ध्रुवे देवीयुक्ते देवीबेरे अक्षरन्यासं कृत्वा समावाह्य, पश्चात् देव्योः अक्षरन्यासं कृत्वा समावाहयेत्।
> *sarvavādyasamāyuktaṃ devāgāraṃ pradakṣiṇīkṛtya, abhyantaraṃ praviśya, dhruvasya abhimukhe dhānyarāśou dvou kumbhou nyaset. kautukasya dakṣiṇa-vāmayoḥ kṛte pīṭhe devyou tat-tat-mantreṇa sthāpayeyuḥ. ācāryaḥ ca uttarābhimukhaḥ tiṣṭhan dhruvabera-akṣaranyāsaṃ kṛtvā, pūrvam āvāhya, paścāt koutuke akṣaranyāsaṃ kṛtvā, tadvad devyou dhyāyan dīpādvīpam iva āvāhayet. dhruve devīyukte devībere akṣaranyāsaṃ kṛtvā samāvāhya, paścāt devyoḥ akṣaranyāsaṃ kṛtvā samāvāhayet.*
> 
> 'अस्त्व् आसनम्' इति आसनं दत्त्वा, पुण्याहं वाचयित्वा, नित्यार्चनविधानेन देवेशेन सह देव्यौ समभ्यर्च्य, हविः निवेद्य, पञ्चप्रणामान् कुर्यात्। ते देव्यौ पृथक् पृथक् चेत् पृथग् एवं कारयेत् ॥
> *'astv āsanam' iti āsanaṃ dattva, puṇyāhaṃ vācayitvā, nityārcanavidhānena deveśena saha devyou samabhyarcya, havis nivedya, pañcapraṇāmān kuryāt. te devyou pṛthak pṛthak cet pṛthag eva kārayet ॥*
> 
> **देव्योः विवाह-विधिः -**
> अथ देव्योः विवाहक्रियाविधिं वक्ष्ये — देव्योः नवबिम्ब-प्रतिष्ठानन्तरं विवाहक्रियां कारयेत्। यजमानः द्विजश्चेत् गृहे देवालये वा यागशालां कृत्वा, यथाविभवम् अलङ्कुर्यात्। तन्मध्ये दक्षिणे श्रीदेव्याः, वामे महीदेव्याश्च औपासनाग्निकुण्डं स्थण्डिलं वा कुर्यात्।
> ***devyoḥ vivāha-vidhiḥ -***
> *atha devyoḥ vivāhakriyāvidhiṃ vakṣye — devyoḥ navabimba-pratiṣṭhānantaraṃ vivāhakriyāṃ kārayet. yajamānaḥ dvijaścet gṛhe devālaye vā yāgāśālāṃ kṛtvā, yathāvibhavam alaṅkuryāt. tanmadhye dakṣiṇe śrīdevyāḥ, vāme mahīdevyāśca aupāsanāgnikuṇḍaṃ sthaṇḍilaṃ vā kuryāt.*
> 
> शुभे मुहूर्ते सम्प्राप्ते पूर्वं देव्यौ आनीय, शुभासने संस्थाप्य, अर्चयित्वा, फलोपहार-ताम्बूलादीनि निवेद्य; आचार्यः परिचारकैः सह देवम् अलङ्कृत्य, यानम् आरोप्य, शङ्ख-भेर्यादि-घोषयुतं नृत्त-गेय-स्तोत्र-ध्वनि-समायुक्तं यागशालां प्रविश्य; अग्निकुण्डात् पश्चिमे महार्ह-संस्तरण-पर्यङ्कोपरि देवं प्राङ्मुखं संस्थाप्य, राजवद् उपचारं कुर्यात्। यजमानः बान्धवैः सह देवेशं प्रणम्य, आचार्यं पूजयेत्।
> *śubhe muhūrte samprāpte pūrvaṃ devyou ānīya, śubhāsane saṃsthāpya, arcayitvā, phalopahāra-tāmbūlādīni nivedya; ācāryaḥ paricārakaiḥ saha devam alaṅkṛtya, yānam āropya, śaṅkha-bheryādi-ghoṣayutaṃ nṛtta-geya-stotra-dhvani-samāyuktaṃ yāgāśālāṃ praviśya; agnikuṇḍāt paścime mahārha-saṃstaraṇa-paryaṅkopari devaṃ prāṅmukhaṃ saṃsthāpya, rājavad upacāraṃ kuryāt. yajamānaḥ bāndhavaiḥ saha deveśaṃ praṇamya, ācāryaṃ pūjayet.*
> 
> आचार्यो देवं पाद्यादि-अर्घ्यान्तम् अभ्यर्च्य, मधुपर्कं निवेद्य, वैखानस-सूत्रोक्त-विधिना विवाहं कारयेत्। आत्मसूक्तं जप्त्वा, देवं ध्यायन् एकाक्षरादि-सूक्तं, विष्णुसूक्तं, पुरुषसूक्तं, वैष्णवं च जपेत्। देवेशं प्रणम्य, प्रतिसरं बध्नीयात्।
> *ācāryo devaṃ pādi-arghyāntam abhyarcya, madhuparkaṃ nivedya, vaikhānasa-sūtrokta-vidhinā vivāhaṃ kārayet. ātmasūktaṃ japtvā, devaṃ dhyāyan ekākṣarādi-sūktaṃ, viṣṇusūktaṃ, puruṣasūktaṃ, vaiṣṇavaṃ ca japet. deveśaṃ praṇamya, pratisaraṃ badhnīyāt.*
> 
> 'प्रसुग्मन्ते' इति देवः देवीम् ईक्षेत, 'अभ्रातृघ्नीम्' इति देवं देवी प्रेक्षेत, पश्चात् आज्याघारं जुहुयात्।
> *'prasugmante' iti devaḥ devīm īkṣeta, 'abhrātṛghnīm' iti devaṃ devī prekṣeta, paścāt ājyāghāraṃ juhuyāt.*
> 
> **कन्याप्रदान-विधिः -**
> श्रीदेव्याः प्रदाने देवसन्निधौ देवीं विनीय, यजमानः स्वात्मानं भृगुं स्मृत्वा, 'मूलप्रकृतिरूपाम् अयोनिजां सङ्कल्पतनयां भृगुपुत्रीं श्रियं विष्णवे तुभ्यं ददामि' इति 'प्रजापतिस्त्रियाम्' इति च उक्त्वा, सुवर्ण-गो-भूमि-सहितं जलं दद्यात्।
> ***kanyāpradāna-vidhiḥ -***
> *śrīdevyāḥ pradāne devasannidhou devīṃ vinīya, yajamānaḥ svātmānaṃ bhṛguṃ smṛtvā, 'mūlaprakṛtirūpām ayonijāṃ saṅkalpatanayāṃ bhṛguputrīṃ śriyaṃ viṣṇave tubhyaṃ dadāmi' iti 'prajāpatistriyām' iti ca uktvā, suvarṇa-go-bhūmi-sahitaṃ jalaṃ dadyāt.*
> 
> हरिण्याः प्रदाने यजमानः स्वात्मानं प्रजापतिं स्मृत्वा 'विष्णोः पुराणमहिषीं प्रकृत्यंशभूतां सङ्कल्पतनयां काश्यपीं हरिणीं विष्णवे तुभ्यं ददामि' इति पूर्ववद् दद्यात्।
> *hariṇyāḥ pradāne yajamānaḥ svātmānaṃ prajāpatiṃ smṛtvā 'viṣṇoḥ purāṇamahiṣīṃ prakṛtyaṃśabhūtāṃ saṅkalpatanayāṃ kāśyapīṃ hariṇīṃ viṣṇave tubhyaṃ dadāmi' iti pūrvavad dadyāt.*
> 
> प्रादुर्भावेषु सर्वेषु तत्-तत्-देवी-पितरं सञ्चिन्त्य, तत्-तत्-नाम्ना दद्यात्। यजमानः शूद्रादिश्चेत् तन्नियोगेन द्विजः एवं कुर्यात्।
> *prādurbhāveṣu sarveṣu tat-tat-devī-pitaraṃ sañcintya, tat-tat-nāmnā dadyāt. yajamānaḥ śūdrādiścet tanniyogena dvijaḥ evaṃ kuryāt.*

> **पाणिग्रहण-लाजहोमादि -**
> एवं जलप्रदाने कृते देवस्य दक्षिण-वामयोः देव्यौ संस्थाप्य, नवैः वस्त्राभरण-गन्धाद्यैः अलङ्कृत्य, विवाहविषयमन्त्रात् लाजहोम-आदि-प्रदक्षिणादि-कर्म देव्योः सन्निधौ आचार्यः एव आचरेत्। पाणिग्रहणे मन्त्रम् उच्चरन् दर्भेण देव-देव्योः पाणी स्पर्शयेत्।
> ***pāṇigrahaṇa-lājahoma-ādi -***
> *evaṃ jalapradāne kṛte devasya dakṣiṇa-vāmayoḥ devyou saṃsthāpy, navaiḥ vastrābharaṇa-gandhādyaiḥ alaṅkṛty, vivāhaviṣayamantrāt lājahoma-ādi-pradakṣiṇādi-karma devyoḥ sannidhou ācāryaḥ eva ācaret. pāṇigrahaṇe mantram uccaran darbheṇa deva-devyoḥ pāṇī sparśayet.*
> 
> अश्मसंस्पर्शने देव्योः दक्षिणपादाङ्गुष्ठेन अश्म संस्पर्शयेत्। सप्तपदाक्रान्तौ देवस्य अभिमुखे नववस्त्रे च उत्तराग्रान् दर्भान् आस्तीर्य, यथाक्रमेण संस्पृशेत्। स्तोमारोपणे वैष्णवैः देवस्य देव्योश्च तत्-तत्-मन्त्रेण पादयोः प्रक्षिपेत्। ध्रुवदर्शनादीन् न आचरेत्।
> *aśmasaṃsparśane devyoḥ dakṣiṇapādāṅguṣṭhena aśma saṃsparśayet. saptapadākrāntou devasya abhimukhe navavastre ca uttarāgrān darbhān āstīrya, yathākrameṇā saṃspṛśet. stomāropaṇe vaiṣṇavaiḥ devasya devyośca tat-tat-mantreṇa pādayoḥ prakṣipet. dhruvadarśanādīn na ācaret.*
> 
> एककाले तयोः देव्योः विवाहक्रिया चेत् एकाग्नौ पृथग् एव कुर्याद् इति केचित्। आचार्यादिभ्यो दक्षिणां दद्यात्।
> *ekakāle tayoḥ devyoḥ vivāhakriyā cet ekāgnou pṛthag eva kuryād iti kecit. ācāryādibhyo dakṣiṇāṃ dadyāt.*
> 
> **अन्तहोमः -**
> वैष्णवान् सम्पूज्य, सर्वेषाम् अपि गन्ध-पुष्प-मुखवासादीनि दद्यात्। पुण्याहं वाचयित्वा, अन्तहोमं हुत्वा, अग्निं नित्याग्नौ समारोप्य; प्रभूतं हविः निवेद्य अपूपादीनि निवेद्य, आचमन-मुखवासं च दद्यात्। देवं देवीभ्यां सह यानम् आरोप्य, ग्रामम् आलयं वा सर्वालङ्कारसंयुक्तं प्रदक्षिणीकृत्य, अभ्यन्तरं प्रविश्य, शुद्ध-स्नपन-विधिना संस्नाप्य अभ्यर्च्य, हविः निवेद्य, प्रणामं कुर्यात्।
> ***antahomaḥ -***
> *vaiṣṇavān sampūjya, sarveṣām api gandha-puṣpa-mukhavāsādīni dadyāt. puṇyāhaṃ vācayitvā, antahomaṃ hutvā, agniṃ nityāgnou samāropya; prabhūtaṃ haviḥ nivedya apūpādīni nivedya, ācamana-mukhavāsaṃ ca dadyāt. devaṃ devībhyāṃ saha yānam āropya, grāmam ālayaṃ vā sarvālaṅkārasaṃyuktaṃ pradakṣiṇīkṛtya, abhyantaraṃ praviśya, śuddha-snapana-vidhinā saṃsnāpy abhyarcya, havis nivedya, praṇāmaṃ kuryāt.*
> 
> एवं हरेः वैवाहिक-उत्सवे कृते सर्वसम्पत्-समृद्धिः भवति, तद्-ग्रामवासिन्यः स्त्रियः पुत्र-मङ्गल-संयुक्ताः भवन्ति। यजमानः इष्टान् कामान् अवाप्य विष्णोः सायुज्यम् आप्नुयात्। स्नपनबिम्बं बलिबेरं च देवीभ्यां सहितं रहितं वा कारयेत्।
> *evaṃ hareḥ vaivāhika-utsave kṛte sarvasampat-samṛddhiḥ bhavati, tad-grāmavāsinyaḥ striyaḥ putra-maṅgala-saṃyuktāḥ bhavanti. yajamānaḥ iṣṭān kāmān avāpya viṣṇoḥ sāyujyam āpnuyāt. snapanabimbaṃ baliberaṃ ca devībhyāṃ sahitaṃ rahitaṃ vā kārayet.*
> 
> **जीर्णोद्धार-विवाह-प्रायश्चित्त-नियमाः -**
> कौतुकादि-बेरे अक्षिरेखा-भ्रूरेखा-आदि अतिजीर्णे, घृष्टे स्पृष्टे अङ्गहीने चोर-आदि-अपहृते; तद्वद् पुनः बिम्बं कृतं चेत्, बिम्बं प्रतिष्ठाप्य, विवाहक्रियां न आचरेत्। अर्चाहीने प्रायश्चित्तं कृत्वा सह अर्चयेत्। देव्योः पुनः बिम्बं कृतं चेत्, प्रतिष्ठाप्य, विवाहक्रियां कारयेत्। योगमार्गेण विरहमार्गेण वा ध्रुवबेरं यत्रैव प्रतिष्ठाप्य अर्चितं, तत्र पश्चाद् देवीयुक्तं न कारयेत्।
> ***jīrṇoddhāra-vivāha-prāyaścitta-niyamāḥ -***
> *kautukādi-bere akṣirekhā-bhrūrekhā-ādi atijīrṇe, ghṛṣṭe spṛṣṭe aṅgahīne cora-adi-apahṛte; tadvad punaḥ bimbaṃ kṛtaṃ cet, bimbaṃ pratiṣṭhāpy, vivāhakriyāṃ na ācaret. arcāhīne prāyaścittaṃ kṛtvā saha arcayet. devyoḥ punaḥ bimbaṃ kṛtaṃ cet, pratiṣṭhāpy, vivāhakriyāṃ kārayet. yogamārgeṇa virahamārgeṇa vā dhruvaberaṃ yatraiva pratiṣṭhāpy arcitaṃ, tatra paścād devīyuktaṃ na kārayet.*
> 
> **स्नपन-उत्सव-बलि-बेराणां पृथक् प्रतिष्ठा-विधिः -**
> अथ स्नपन-उत्सव-बलि-बेराणां पृथक् प्रतिष्ठाविधिं वक्ष्ये — पूर्वोक्तेन विधानेन बिम्बं सलक्षणं कृत्वा, अङ्कुरान् अर्पयित्वा, सम्भारान् सम्भृत्य, यागशालां कृत्वा; त्रयाणां बिम्बानां सह प्रतिष्ठा चेत् सह एव अक्षिमोचनं कृत्वा, प्रत्येकं गवादि-दर्शन-द्रव्याणि दर्शयित्वा; पृथक् पृथक् यथोक्तविधिना अधिवासनं कृत्वा;
> ***snapana-utsava-bali-berāṇāṃ pṛthak pratiṣṭhā-vidhiḥ -***
> *atha snapana-utsava-bali-berāṇāṃ pṛthak pratiṣṭhāvidhiṃ vakṣye — pūrvoktena vidhānena bimbaṃ salakṣaṇaṃ kṛtvā, aṅkurān arpayitvā, sambhārān sambhṛtya, yāgāśālāṃ kṛtvā; trayāṇāṃ bimbānāṃ saha pratiṣṭhā cet saha eva akṣimocanaṃ kṛtvā, pratyekaṃ gavādi-darśana-dravyāṇi darśayitvā; pṛthak pṛthak yathoktavidhinā adhivāsanaṃ kṛtvā;*
> 
> पञ्चाग्निं पौण्डरीकाग्निं च कृत्वा, कुम्भं संसाध्य; सप्तभिः कलशौः संस्नाप्य, शय्यावेद्याम् एकस्यां धान्योपरि प्रत्येकम् अण्डजादीनि पञ्चशयनानि वासांसि वा आस्तीर्य, प्रतिसरं बद्ध्वा, तद्-देवं शाययित्वा; सभ्याग्नौ हौत्रं प्रशंस्य, सर्वेषु अग्निषु पूर्ववद् होमं हुत्वा, रात्रि-शेषं व्यपोह्य; प्रभाते स्नात्वा, आलयम् आविश्य, देवान् उत्थाप्य अभ्यर्च्य, अभ्यन्तरं प्रविश्य कृते पीठे सुवर्णं निक्षिप्य, अग्निं विसृज्य, यजमानः आचार्यादिभ्यो दक्षिणां दद्यात्।
> *pañcāgniṃ pouṇḍarīkāgniṃ ca kṛtvā, kumbhaṃ saṃsādhya; saptabhiḥ kalaśouḥ saṃsnāpy, śayyāvedyām ekasyāṃ dhānyopari pratyekam aṇḍajādīni pañcaśayanāni vāsāṃsi vā āstīrya, pratisaraṃ baddhvā, tad-devaṃ śāyayitvā; sabhyāgnou hautraṃ praśaṃsya, sarveṣu agniṣu pūrvavad homaṃ hutvā, rātri-śeṣaṃ vyapohya; prabhāte snātvā, ālayam āviśya, devān utthāpya abhyarcya, abhyantaraṃ praviśya kṛte pīṭhe suvarṇaṃ nikṣipya, agniṃ visṛjya, yajamānaḥ ācāryādibhyo dakṣiṇāṃ dadyāt.*
> 
> आचार्यः शिरसा कुम्भं धारयन् अग्रतो गच्छेत्; स्थापकाः देवान् आदाय अनुगच्छेयुः; आलयं प्रदक्षिणीकृत्य, अभ्यन्तरं प्रविश्य, ध्रुवबेरे देवं समावाह्य, तस्मात् स्नपन-उत्सव-बलि-बेरेषु समावाहयेत्, एष विशेषः अन्यत् सर्वं समानम्। तेषां पृथक् चेत् पृथग् एव समाचरेत्। स्नपने बेरे तद्-रात्रौ स्नपनोक्तक्रमेण स्नपनं कारयेत्। औत्सवे च उत्सवोक्तक्रमेण उत्सवं, ऐकाहिकोत्सवं वा कारयेत्; बलिबेरे तत्काल बल्युत्सवं कारयेत्। एवं यः कुरुते भक्त्या स महाप्रतिष्ठाफलं लभेद् इति विज्ञायते ॥
> *ācāryaḥ śirasā kumbhaṃ dhārayan agrato gacchet; sthāpakāḥ devān ādāya anugaccheyuḥ; ālayaṃ pradakṣiṇīkṛtya, abhyantaraṃ praviśya, dhruvabere devaṃ samāvāhya, tasmāt snapana-utsava-bali-bereṣu samāvāhayet, eṣa viśeṣaḥ anyat sarvaṃ samānam. teṣāṃ pṛthak cet pṛthag eva samācaret. snapane bere tad-rātrou snapanoktakrameṇa snapanaṃ kārayet. outsave ca utsavoktakrameṇa utsavaṃ, aikāhikotsavaṃ vā kārayet; balibere tatkāle balyutsavaṃ kārayet. evaṃ yaḥ kurute bhaktyā sa mahāpratiṣṭhāphalaṃ labhed iti vijñāyate ॥*

#### English Translation
*   **Consecration of the Two Goddesses (Śrī and Bhūmi):** I shall now declare the individual consecration of the two Goddesses, Śrī and Bhūmi. Whether the main fixed deity (*dhruva-bera*) is accompanied by the Goddesses or is alone, one should perform the consecration of the Goddesses for the *Kautuka* (worship icon). If the movable *Kautuka* is accompanied or unaccompanied by the Goddesses, installing the festival icon (*utsava-bera*) with the two Goddesses brings prosperity to the village. The two Goddesses should be fashioned of the same material as the main deity; one should not make them of inferior or superior materials.

*   **Iconographic Alignment and Placement:**
    *   For a standing main deity, the Goddesses should be standing; for a seated deity, they should be seated.
    *   The height of the Goddesses should reach the arm-level (*bāhu-māna*) or the chest-level (*stana-anta*) of the main deity.
    *   In a seated posture, their height should reach the ear-level (*karṇa-anta*) or the mouth-level (*āsya-anta*) of the main deity. One should project the two Goddesses properly according to the prescribed order.

*   **Sacrificial Hall (Yāgaśālā) Setup:**
    *   Prior to the day of installation, having sown the seeds (*aṅkurārpaṇa*), and having performed the eye-opening (*akṣi-mocana*) and couch-placement (*adhivāsa*) as before, the priest sets up a sacrificial pavilion facing the temple.
    *   *Śrī Devi Altars (South):* In the middle on the south, he makes a couch-altar (*śayyā-vedi*) for Śrī Devi measuring two cubits in length and width, and a quarter of that in height. To the east of it (*tat-prācyāṃ*), he makes an *aupāsana* fire-pit, and to the east of that, a bathing pit (*snāna-śvabhra*).
    *   *Mahi Devi Altars (North):* In the middle on the north, he makes a couch-altar for Mahi Devi (Bhumi Devi), to the east of it an *aupāsana* fire-pit, and to the east of that a bathing pit.

*   **Preparation of the Vessels and Invocation:**
    *   Having brought the two Goddesses who have entered the couch-placement (*adhivāsa*), having bathed them with pure water and decorated them with cloths, etc., and having placed them on seats in the west of the sacrificial hall:
    *   Having offered the site-homa, purified the hall with fire circumambulation and *pañcagavya*, and offered the introductory libations (*āghāra*) in both *aupāsana* fire-pits:
    *   He takes two pots (*kumbha*), wraps them with thread as before, washes them with water, fills them with purified water, places five gems, the eight auspicious symbols, and the marks of the five weapons and castes as before, wraps them in a pair of cloths, and enters the sanctum.
    *   He places the pot of Śrī Devi on a heap of grains in front, and then the pot of Mahi Devi on a heap of grains in front.
    *   If the fixed main deity lacks the Goddesses, he invokes their forms from the fixed icon of the Lord or from the solar orb (*arka-maṇḍala*), meditating on their respective forms. He invokes them into the water of the pots.

*   **The Couch-Placement of the Goddesses:**
    *   Carrying the pots, chanting the *Śakuna-sūkta*, and establishing the two Goddesses along with the pots in the bathing pit:
    *   Having bathed them with seven pots as before, decorated them with cloths and ornaments, and worshipped them:
    *   On the couch-altar over grains, having spread five layers of bedding starting with feathers (*aṇḍaja*) or cloths as before, he places them on the couch, performs the *puṇyāha*, ties the protective thread as before, and lays them to rest with their respective mantras.
    *   Subsequently, summoning the performing priest (*hotā*), he should worship him. Standing to the east of them, the *hotā* should praise the *hautra* duties.

*   **Fire Sacrifices for the Goddesses:**
    *   Each *hotā* and *adhvaryu* should invoke the two Goddesses along with Jaya and other Apsarasas, determine them in order of invocation, and offer ghee-oblations.
    *   In the southern fire-pit, he should offer the *Śrī-sūkta* for Śrī Devi, and the icon-mantras of Jaya, etc.
    *   In the northern fire-pit, he should offer the *Mahī-sūkta* for Mahi Devi, and the icon-mantras of Jaya, etc.

*   **Morning Rituals and Procession:**
    *   He passes the rest of the night with dance, song, and music. In the morning, having bathed, the teachers and others enter the temple.
    *   The teachers awaken the two Goddesses, worship them, and place five gems or gold in the pedestals in the inner sanctum.
    *   The patron gives sacrificial fees (*dakṣiṇā*) to the teachers. Dismissing the fire, at the auspicious hour, the teacher carries the pot of Śrī Devi and the pot of Mahi Devi on his head, walking in front, and the assistants follow carrying the two Goddesses.
    *   Accompanied by all musical instruments, they circumambulate the temple, enter the sanctum, and place the two pots on a heap of grains facing the fixed deity.
    *   They place the two Goddesses on the pedestals to the right and left of the *Kautuka* icon with their respective mantras.

*   **Invoking the Goddesses:**
    *   The teacher, facing north, performs the letter-placement (*akṣara-nyāsa*) on the fixed icon, invokes the energy first, and then performs the letter-placement on the *Kautuka* icon. Meditating on the two Goddesses, he invokes their presence from the fixed icon to the movable ones, like transferring light from one lamp to another (*dīpād dīpam iva āvāhayet*).
    *   If the fixed main deity is accompanied by the Goddesses, he performs the letter-placement on the main deity's Goddesses, invokes them, and then does the same for the two movable Goddesses.
    *   Offering them a seat with *"astv āsanam"*, declaring the day auspicious, and worshipping the Lord along with the two Goddesses according to the daily worship rules, he offers food oblations and performs five prostrations. If the Goddesses are consecrated in separate shrines, he should perform the same ritual for them separately.

*   **Marriage Ritual (Devyor-Vivāha-vidhiḥ):**
    *   I shall now declare the marriage ritual of the two Goddesses. After the consecration of the new icons of the two Goddesses, one should perform their marriage.
    *   If the patron is a twice-born (*dvija*), he sets up a sacrificial pavilion in his house or in the temple, and decorates it according to his wealth. In the middle, he makes an *aupāsana* fire-pit or an altar (*sthaṇḍila*) on the south for Śrī Devi, and on the west for Mahi Devi.
    *   At the auspicious hour, he brings the two Goddesses, places them on auspicious seats, worships them, and offers fruits, oblation, and betel leaves.
    *   The teacher, accompanied by assistants, decorates the Lord, places Him on a vehicle, and accompanied by the sound of conches and drums, and dances, songs, and praises, enters the pavilion. He places the Lord facing east on a couch with valuable bedding to the west of the fire-pit. The patron, along with his relatives, bows to the Lord and worships the teacher.

*   **The Marriage Ceremony:**
    *   The teacher worships the Lord with offerings from water for washing the feet (*pādya*) up to water for sipping (*arghya*), offers the honey-mixture (*madhuparka*), and performs the marriage according to the Vaikhānasa scriptures.
    *   Chanting the *Ātma-sūkta* and meditating on the Lord, he should repeat the *Ekākṣarādi-sūkta*, *Viṣṇu-sūkta*, *Puruṣa-sūkta*, and *Vaiṣṇava* mantras. Bowing to the Lord, he ties the protective thread (*pratisara*).
    *   Reciting *"prasugmante"*, the Lord gazes at the Goddess, and reciting *"abhrātṛghnīm"*, the Goddess gazes at the Lord. Subsequently, he offers the introductory libations (*āghāra*).

*   **Giving Away the Bride (Kanyākādānam):**
    *   **Śrī Devi's Giving Away:** In the giving away of Śrī Devi, in front of the main deity, the patron, meditating on himself as Sage Bhṛgu (the father of Lakṣmī), and designating Śrī Devi as the primordial nature (*mūla-prakṛti*), not born of a womb (*ayonijā*), and his mentally-conceived daughter, gives her away, saying: *"O Viṣṇu! I give this Śrī, the daughter of Bhṛgu, to You."* He says *"prajāpatis-triyām"* and pours water along with gold, cows, and land.
    *   **Bhumi Devi's Giving Away:** For the giving away of Harini (Bhumi Devi), the patron, meditating on himself as Sage Prajāpati (or Kaśyapa), designates her as the ancient queen of Viṣṇu, a portion of nature (*prakṛti-aṃśa-bhūtā*), his mentally-conceived daughter, the daughter of Kaśyapa, and gives her away, saying: *"O Viṣṇu! I give this Harini to You."* He pours water as before.
    *   For all incarnations (*prādurbhāva*), he should contemplate the respective father of the Goddess and give her away in his name. If the patron is a Shudra, etc., a twice-born (*dvija*) should perform this on his behalf.

*   **Joining Hands (Pāṇigrahaṇam):**
    *   Having poured the water, he places the two Goddesses on the right and left of the Lord, decorates them with new cloths, ornaments, and scents, and the teacher performs the marriage rites, including the fried-grain offering (*lāja-homa*) and circumambulation, in front of the two Goddesses.
    *   For the joining of hands (*pāṇigrahaṇa*), reciting the mantra, he touches the hands of the Lord and the Goddesses with *Kuśa* grass.
    *   For touching the stone (*aśma-saṃsparśana*), he makes the right big toes of the Goddesses touch the stone.
    *   For the taking of seven steps (*saptapadākrānti*), facing the Lord, he spreads new cloths and *Kuśa* grass pointing north, and makes them step on them in order.
    *   For placing the garland (*stoma-āropaṇa*), he throws the garlands on the feet of the Lord and the two Goddesses with their respective *Vaiṣṇava* mantras. He should not perform the sight of the pole star (*dhruva-darśana*), etc.
    *   If the marriage of both Goddesses is performed at the same time, some say it should be done in a single fire, and others say it should be done in separate fires. The patron gives sacrificial fees to the teachers.

*   **The Final Oblations (Antahoma) and Procession:**
    *   Having worshipped the Vaishnavas, he should distribute scents, flowers, and mouth-perfumes to all. Having declared the day auspicious and offered the final oblation (*antahoma*), he attributes the fire to the daily fire (*nityāgnau*), offers abundant oblation, cakes, etc., and gives water for rinsing and mouth-perfumes.
    *   Placing the Lord along with the two Goddesses on a vehicle, and circumambulating the village or the temple with all decorations, they enter the temple, bathe them according to the pure bathing rules, worship them, offer food, and bow.
    *   Conducting the marriage festival of Lord Viṣṇu in this manner brings all wealth and prosperity, and the women of that village will be endowed with sons and auspiciousness. The patron achieves his desires and attains union (*sāyujya*) with Viṣṇu. One should perform this for the bathing icon, festival icon, and offering icon, either with or without the Goddesses.

*   **Prohibitions and Damaged Icons:**
    *   If the lines of the eyes or eyebrows (*akṣi-rekha*, *bhrū-rekha*) of the *Kautuka* and other icons are extremely worn out, old, split, or broken, or if they are stolen by thieves: if a new icon is made to replace it, after consecrating it, one should not repeat the marriage ritual.
    *   For minor damages, having performed the expiation (*prāyaścitta*), he should continue the worship. If the icons of the two Goddesses are made anew, after consecrating them, one should perform the marriage ritual.
    *   In temples consecrated according to the *yoga-mārga* (ascetic layout) or *viraha-mārga* (solitary layout) where the fixed main deity has been established and worshipped alone, one should not subsequently add the Goddesses.

*   **Consecration of Bathing, Festival, and Offering Icons:**
    *   I shall now declare the separate consecration of the bathing, festival, and offering icons. Having made the icons with proper characteristics according to the previously declared rules, having sown seeds, gathered materials, and built the sacrificial hall:
    *   If the three icons are consecrated together, having performed their eye-opening together, and having shown them auspicious sights like cows, he performs their couch-placement (*adhivāsana*) separately according to the rules.
    *   Having set up the five fires and the *Pauṇḍarīka* fire, prepared the pot, bathed them with seven pots, spread five layers of bedding starting with feathers on the couch-altar over grains, tied the protective thread, and laid the deity to rest:
    *   Having praised the *hautra* duties in the *Sabhya* fire and offered oblations in all fires as before, he passes the rest of the night.
    *   In the morning, having bathed, entered the temple, awakened the deities, worshipped them, entered the sanctum, and placed gold in the pedestal, he dismisses the fire and the patron gives fees.
    *   The teacher carries the pot on his head, walking in front, and the assistants carry the icons. Circumambulating the temple and entering the sanctum, he invokes the energy from the fixed icon, and then invokes it into the bathing, festival, and offering icons. This is the special rule; the rest is same.
    *   If they are consecrated separately, he should perform it separately. For the bathing icon, he performs the bath on that night in the prescribed order; for the festival icon, the festival or a one-day festival; for the offering icon, the offering festival. He who does this with devotion obtains the fruit of grand consecration (*mahā-pratiṣṭhā*).
