# Chapter 51: The Great Temple Festival and Flag Hoisting (Brahmotsava-vidhiḥ / Dhvajārohaṇa-vidhiḥ)
### Section 1: Liturgical Rules for the Great Temple Festival and the Flag-Hoisting Ritual

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **अथ एकपञ्चाशत्तमः पटलः**
> **ब्रह्मोत्सव-ध्वजारोहण-विधिः -**
> ***atha ekapañcāśattamaḥ paṭalaḥ***
> ***brahmotsava-dhvajārohaṇa-vidhiḥ -***
> 
> **(ब्रह्मोत्सवविधिः)**
> अथ देवेशस्य उत्सवविधिं व्याख्यास्यामः - त्रिविध उत्सवः, नित्योत्सवः, शान्त्युत्सवः, श्रद्धोत्सव इति ।
> ***(brahmotsavavidhiḥ)***
> *atha deveśasyotsavavidhiṃ vyākhyāsyāmaḥ - trivida utsavaḥ, nityotsavaḥ, śāntyutsavaḥ, śraddhotsava iti.*
> 
> द्वादशमासेषु एकस्मिन् मासेऽब्दान् प्रति तत्काले नित्यं संकल्प्य यः क्रियते स नित्योत्सवः, भयप्रद-दुर्भिक्ष-व्याधि-ग्रहपीडाद्यशुभशान्त्यर्थं संकल्प्य यः क्रियते स शान्त्युत्सवः, श्रद्धा-वित्ते यदा युक्ते तदा यः सम्यक् क्रियते स श्रद्धोत्सवो भवेत् ।
> *dvādaśamāseṣu ekasmin māse’bdān prati tatkāle nityaṃ saṅkalpya yaḥ kriyate sa nityotsavaḥ, bhayaprada-durbhixa-vyādhi-grahapīḍādyāśubhaśāntyarthaṃ saṅkalpya yaḥ kriyate sa śāntyotsavaḥ, śraddhā-vitte yadā yukte tadā yaḥ samyak kriyate sa śraddhotsavo bhavet.*
> 
> **(उत्सवदिनसंख्या तत्कालश्च)**
> नवाहं, सप्ताहं, पञ्चाहं वाऽप्युत्सवः । राज्ञो राष्ट्रस्य ग्रामस्य यजमानस्य वा जन्मर्क्षे, प्रतिष्ठादिने, विष्णुपञ्चदिनेषु, मासनक्षत्रे, विषुवे, अयने वा अवभृथं संकल्प्य, तत्पूर्वे च उत्सवदिनं निश्चित्य; तद्दिनं त्रिगुणं द्विगुणं वा कृत्वा; तदादौ उत्सवादौ वा सायं ध्वजारोहणं कारयेत् ।
> ***(utsavadinasarkhyā tatkālaśca)***
> *navāhaṃ, saptāhaṃ, pañcāhaṃ vā’pyutsavaḥ. rājño rāṣṭrasya grāmasya yajamānasya vā janmarṣe, pratiṣṭhādine, viṣṇupañcadineṣu, māsanaxatre, viṣuve, ayane vā avabhṛthaṃ saṅkalpya, tatpūrve ca utsavadinaṃ niścitya; taddinṃ triguṇaṃ dviguṇaṃ vā kṛtvā; tadādou utsavādou vā sāyaṃ dhvajārohaṇaṃ kārayet.*
> 
> **(ध्वजदण्डलक्षणम्)**
> ध्वजदण्डमृजुं कृमिकोटररहितम् अनुद्वेजिनं विमानसमं पादाधिकम् अर्धाधिकं पादहीनं अर्धहीनं वा चतुर्विंशत्यङ्गुलपरिणाहं, यथालाभपरिणाहं वा, वेणुजातिं चम्पकं क्रमुकं वा अन्यं शुभवृक्षसारं वा गृह्णीयात् । आगस्त्यं वेति केचित् ॥
> ***(dhvajadaṇḍalaxanam)***
> *dhvajadaṇḍamṛjuṃ kṛmikotararahitam anudvejinaṃ vimānasamaṃ pādādhikam ardhādhikaṃ pādahīnaṃ ardhahīnaṃ vā caturviṃśatyaṅgulapariṇāhaṃ, yathālābhapariṇāhaṃ vā, veṇujātiṃ campakaṃ kramukaṃ vā anyaṃ śubhavṛxasāraṃ vā gṛhṇīyāt. āgastyaṃ veti kecit.*
> 
> **(ध्वजपटलक्षणम्)**
> ध्वजपटं महत् शुद्धं दृढं शुक्लं कार्पासतन्तुना कृतं, चतुर्दश-त्रयोदश-द्वादश-एकादश-नव-अष्ट-सप्तहस्तम् अन्यतमायामं समषट्-पञ्च-चतुस्तालविस्तृतं वस्त्रद्वयं संयोज्य, एकं वेति केचित् । केशादीन् परिहृत्य, जलेन पेषयित्वा, अग्रेणाऽग्रं पुच्छेन पुच्छं कृत्वा, ऊर्ध्वभागे तत्पटविस्तारार्द्धायतं शूर्पाकारमग्रं कृत्वा, पटस्याधोभागे तद्विस्तारार्द्धायतविस्तारौ पादौ मध्यादाप्रान्तं क्रमात् कृशौ युक्तिं कृत्वा, अग्रादधः पादोर्ध्वे च तिर्यग्यष्टिं वैणवीम् अन्यैः शुभैर्वृक्षैर्वा, तत्पटविस्तारायामाम् अङ्गुलाग्रपरिणाहां योजयेत् ।
> ***(dhvajapaṭalaxanam)***
> *dhvajapaṭaṃ mahat śuddhaṃ dṛḍhaṃ śuklaṃ kārpāsatantunā kṛtaṃ, caturdaśa-trayodaśa-dvādaśa-ekādaśa-nav-aṣṭa-saptahastam anyatamāyāmaṃ samaṣaṭ-pañca-catustālaviṣtṛtaṃ vastradvayaṃ saṃyojya, ekaṃ veti kecit. keśādīn parihṛtya, jalena peṣayitvā, agreṇā’graṃ pucchena puccaṃ kṛtvā, ūrdhvabhāge tatpaṭavistārārddhāyataṃ śūrpākāramagraṃ kṛtvā, paṭasyādhobhāge tatpaṭavistārārddhāyatavistārou pādou madhyādāprāntaṃ kramāt kṛśou yuktiṃ kṛtvā, agrādadhaḥ pādordhve ca tiryagyaṣṭiṃ vaiṇavīm anyaiḥ śubhairvṛxairvā, tatpaṭavistārāyāmām aṅgulāgrapariṇāhāṃ yojayet.*
> 
> **(ध्वजपटे लेख्यगरुडलक्षणम्)**
> एवं कृतध्वजपटमध्ये गरुडं मूलबेरस्य बाहुसमं कर्णान्तं स्तनान्तं नाभ्यन्तं शुद्धद्वारसमं पादाधिकम् अर्धाधिकं पादहीनं अर्धहीनं वा गर्भगृहस्याऽर्धं त्रिपादं समं वा एतेष्विष्टमानेन नवतालविभागेन वा - वामपादमाकुञ्च्य; अपक्षीणं दक्षिणं किंचित्प्रसार्य स्थितं; द्विभुजं प्राञ्जलिं पक्षद्वययुतं सुवर्णवर्णं ललाटे ताम्रचूडं नासाग्रं श्यामावर्णं करपादतलं रक्ताभं नखदशनं शङ्खाभम् एवं पञ्चवर्णयुतं सर्वालङ्कारभूषितं सुमुखाख्य-उरगारूढ-दक्षिणभुजं कौबेरमुकुटोज्ज्वलं शुकश्यामवाससं समेखल-किङ्किणीजालयुतं हाराङ्गद-नूपुर-किरीटसहितं जवेन ब्रह्मलोकाद् देवान् उत्सवार्थम् आह्वयन्तं च लेखयेत् ।
> ***(dhvajapaṭe lekhyagaruḍalaxanam)***
> *evaṃ kṛtadhvajapaṭamadhye garuḍaṃ mūlaberasya bāhusamāṃ karṇāntaṃ stanāntaṃ nābhyantaṃ śuddhadvārasamaṃ pādādhikam ardhādhikaṃ pādahīnaṃ ardhahīnaṃ vā garbhagṛhasyā’rdhaṃ tripādaṃ samaṃ vā eteṣviṣṭamānena navatālāvibhāgena vā - vāmapādamākuñcya; apxīṇaṃ daxiṇaṃ kiñcitprasārya sthitaṃ; dvibhujaṃ prāñjaliṃ paxadvayayutaṃ suvarṇavarṇaṃ lalāṭe tāmracūḍaṃ nāsāgraṃ śyāmāvarṇaṃ karapādatalaṃ raktābhaṃ naṣadaśanaṃ śaṅkhābham evaṃ pañcavarnyutaṃ sarvālaṅkārabhūṣitaṃ sumuṣākhya-uragārūḍha-daxiṇabhujaṃ kouberamukuṭojjvalaṃ śukāśyāmavāsasaṃ samekhala-kiṅkiṇījālayutaṃ hārāṅgada-nūpura-kirīṭasahitaṃ javena brahmalokād devān utsavārtham āhvayantaṃ ca lekhayet.*
> 
> तस्योर्ध्वे चोभयोः पार्श्वयोः चक्र-शङ्खौ दीपधरौ चामरे उपरि छत्रम् अष्टमङ्गलानि धूपदीपपात्रे घण्टां चन्द्र-सूर्यौ सनक-सनत्कुमारौ च अन्यानि वर्णचिह्नानि अधस्तात् पूर्णकुम्भाङ्कुराँश्च लेखयेत् । अन्यत्र पटं सर्वं कृष्णेन परिपूरयेत् ।
> *tasyordhve cobhayoḥ pārśvayoḥ cakra-śaṅkhou dīpadharou cāmare upari chatram aṣṭamaṅgalāni dhūpadīpapātre ghaṇṭāṃ candra-sūryou sanaka-sanatkumārou ca anyāni varṇacihnāni adhastāt pūrṇakumbhāṅkurāṃśca lekhayet. anyatra paṭaṃ sarvaṃ kṛṣṇena paripūrayet.*
> 
> ध्वजदण्डायामं षड्भागं पञ्चभागं चतुर्भागं वा कृत्वा एकांशां वैणवीं यष्टिं संगृह्य; दण्डायज्ञोक्तवृक्षैः अन्यैः शुभैर्वा द्वितालायामानि तद्दण्डार्हविस्ताराणि मध्ये छिद्रयुतानि यष्ट्याधाराणि त्रीणि समाहृत्य; अचलाणि योजयेत् ।
> *dhvajadaṇḍāyāmaṃ ṣadbhāgaṃ pañcabhāgaṃ caturbhāgaṃ vā kṛtvā ekāṃśāṃ vaiṇavīṃ yaṣṭiṃ saṃgṛhya; daṇḍāyajñoktavṛxaiḥ anyaiḥ śubhairvā dvitālāyāmāni taddaṇḍārhavistārāṇi madhye chidrayutāni yaṣṭyādhārāṇi trīṇi samāhṛtya; acalāṇi yojayet.*
> 
> तेषामग्रे यष्टियोजनार्थं सुषिरं कृत्वा दण्डाग्रे यमं यमद्वयं वा वैणवमधिकं यन्त्रवलयं कृत्वा तत्रैकमाधारं तदर्धं श्वभ्रं तयोर्मध्ये चैकं क्रमेण संयोज्य तद्यद्यग्रे द्विभागं त्रिभागं वा हित्वा यन्त्रवलयं ताम्रेणायसेन वा कृत्वा संयोज्य ध्वजदण्डद्विगुणायामाम् अङ्गुलाग्रपरिणाहां त्रिवृतां तन्तुना रज्जुं कृत्वा योजयेत् । दण्डं प्रक्षाल्य दर्भमालया वेष्टयेत् ।
> *teṣāmagre yaṣṭiyojanārthaṃ suṣiraṃ kṛtvā daṇḍāgre yamaṃ yamadvayaṃ vā vaiṇavamadhikaṃ yantravalayaṃ kṛtvā tatraikamādhāraṃ tadardhaṃ śvabhraṃ tayormadhye caikaṃ krameṇa saṃyojya tadyadyagre dvibhāgaṃ tribhāgaṃ vā hitvā yantravalayaṃ tāmreṇāyasena vā kṛtvā saṃyojya dhvajadaṇḍadviguṇāyāmām aṅgulāgrapariṇāhāṃ trivṛtāṃ tantunā rajjuṃ kṛtvā yojayet. daṇḍaṃ praṣālya darbhamālayā veṣṭayet.*
> 
> **(ध्वजारोहणविधिः)**
> यूथाधिप-अक्षहन्त्रोः पृष्ठे चक्रस्य पृष्ठे वा षट्तालं पञ्चतालं चतुस्तालं वा व्यापोह्य; पञ्चतालं चतुस्तालं त्रितालं वा अवटं खनित्वा, मध्ये प्राग्-दक्षिण-पश्चिम-उत्तरेषु पञ्चरत्नानि विक्षिप्य; शाल्यादि धान्यं निधाय; तदवटे ध्वजारोहणकालात्पूर्वं शकुनसूक्तं जपन् ध्वजदण्डं संस्थाप्य; वाद्यघोषयुतं ध्वजाग्रयष्टिः यत्र विमानं तद्दिग्गता यथा भवति तथा संस्थापयेत् ।
> ***(dhvajārohaṇavidhiḥ)***
> *yūthādhipa-axahantrōḥ pṛṣṭhe cakrasya pṛṣṭhe vā ṣaṭtālaṃ pañcatālaṃ catustālaṃ vā vyāpohya; pañcatālaṃ catustālaṃ tritālaṃ vā avaṭṃ khanitvā, madhye prāg-daxiṇa-paścim-uttareṣu pañcaratnāni vixipya; śālyādi dhānyaṃ nidhāy; tadavaṭe dhvajārohaṇakālātpūrvaṃ śakunasūktaṃ japan dhvajadaṇḍaṃ saṃsthāpya; vādyaghoṣayutaṃ dhvajāgrayaṣṭiḥ yatra vimānaṃ taddiggatā yathā bhavati tathā saṃsthāpayet.*
> 
> तन्मूले प्रतिदिक् द्वादशाङ्गुलविस्तारोत्सेधं त्रिवेदिसहितं पीठं कारयेत् ।
> *tanmūle pratidik dvādaśāṅgulavistārotsedhaṃ trivedisahitaṃ pīṭhaṃ kārayet.*
>

#### English Translation
*   **The Threefold Class of Festivals:** Now we shall explain the rules for the temple festivals (*utsava*). The festival is of three kinds: the daily festival (*nityotsava*), the expiatory/peace-seeking festival (*śāntyutsava*), and the devotional festival (*śraddhotsava*).

*   **Definitions:** 
    *   *Nityotsava*: That which is performed regularly in one of the twelve months, or annually at a designated time.
    *   *Śāntyutsava*: That which is performed to pacify inauspicious events, fear, famine, diseases, and planetary afflictions.
    *   *Śraddhotsava*: That which is performed beautifully when the patron possesses faith (*śraddhā*) and resources (*vitta*).

*   **Festival Duration and Auspicious Days:** The festival lasts for nine, seven, or five days. It should conclude with the sacred bath (*avabhṛtha*) on the birth star of the king, the country, the village, or the patron, or on the temple's consecration day, on the five days of Viṣṇu, on the monthly star, on the equinox, or on the solstice. Having determined the festival days, doubling or tripling the days, one should perform the flag hoisting (*dhvajārohaṇa*) in the evening at the beginning of the festival.

*   **Specifications of the Flagstaff (*Dhvaja-daṇḍa*):** The flagstaff should be straight, free from insects and hollows, non-irritating to touch, and equal in height to the temple *vimāna*, or one-quarter higher, one-half higher, one-quarter lower, or one-half lower. Its circumference at the base should be twenty-four *aṅgulas* or as available. It should be made from bamboo, champaka, areca-nut wood, or other strong wood of auspicious trees. Some say it can be made of *Agastya* wood.

*   **Specifications of the Flag (*Dhvaja-paṭa*):** The flag should be large, clean, strong, white, and woven from cotton threads. Its length should be fourteen, thirteen, twelve, eleven, nine, eight, or seven cubits (*hastas*), and its width should be six, five, or four spans (*talas*). It can be made by joining two cloths, or some say a single cloth is sufficient. Hair and other impurities must be avoided. The fabric should be ground-painted with water. The top of the flag should be shaped like a winnowing basket (*śūrpa*), measuring half the flag's width. The bottom should have two legs, measuring half the width, tapering gradually from the middle to the ends. Bamboo or other auspicious wood rods (*yaṣṭi*), matching the width of the flag and one finger-tip in circumference, should be attached horizontally at the top, bottom, and above the legs.

*   **Iconography of Garuḍa on the Flag:** In the middle of the prepared flag, a figure of Garuḍa should be drawn. His size should correspond to the main deity's arms, chest, breasts, navel, or equal to the temple door, or one-quarter more, one-half more, one-quarter less, one-half less, or half of the sanctum, or three-fourths, or equal to it. Using the nine-span (*navatāla*) system, he should be drawn with his left leg bent (*ākuñcya*), his right leg slightly extended, standing with folded hands, two-armed, with two wings. His body should be golden, his crest on the forehead copper-red, his nose-tip dark/green, his palms and soles reddish, and his nails and teeth white like a conch shell. Adorned with all ornaments, he should have a serpent named Sumukha wrapped around his right arm, a crown like Kubera's, and wear bright green garments with a girdle and bells. He should be depicted as calling the gods from the Brahma-world for the festival.

*   **Other Auspicious Insignia on the Flag:** Above Garuḍa, draw the Cakra and conch shell on both sides, lamp-bearers, fly-whisks (*cāmara*), an umbrella on top, the eight auspicious symbols, incense and lamp vessels, a bell, the sun and moon, and the sages Sanaka and Sanatkumāra, along with other colored symbols. Below him, draw full water pots (*pūrṇa-kumbha*) and sprouts. Fill the rest of the flag with black color.

*   **The Pulley System and Rigging:** Divide the flagstaff's height into six, five, or four parts. Secure one part of bamboo rod. Prepare three rings made of bamboo or other strong woods, two spans long, with holes in the middle to slide onto the staff. At the top of the staff, make a hole to fit the rod, install a double-pulley or single-pulley system, and run a three-stranded rope, twice the length of the flagstaff and one finger-tip thick, through the pulley. Wash the flagstaff and wrap it with Darbha grass garlands.

*   **The Foundation Pit:** Behind the vehicle of the Commander (*Yūthādhipa* / *Senāpati*) or behind the Cakra, digging a pit five, four, or three spans deep, after clearing six, five, or four spans of space. Throw five gems in the center, East, South, West, and North of the pit, place shali and other grains, and before the flag hoisting, reciting the *Śakuna-sūkta*, raise the flagstaff. Accompanied by music, ensure the rod at the top of the staff points towards the temple *vimāna*.

*   **The Altar and Consecration at the Base:** Build a pedestal at the base of the flagstaff, twelve *aṅgulas* in width and height, with three tiers. Or make a single altar two or three cubits wide and one cubit high, decorated with an eight-petaled lotus. Build a pavilion or shed in front of the temple, decorated with canopies and flags. On the east, prepare a grain pedestal with three tiers, wider than the flag, for the flag. On its west, prepare a pedestal twenty-four *aṅgulas* long for the Cakra, and on its north, a similar one for Viṣvaksena. In the Northwest or Northeast, prepare an Aupāsana fire-pit.

*   **Flag Consecration Ritual:** Establish the flag facing east on the grain pedestal to the west of the fire-pit. Perform site fire-oblation (*vāstu-homa*), purify with fire and *pañcagavya*. Offer oblations to Garuḍa, the flag, and Viṣṇu. Perform the opening of the eyes (*akṣi-mocana*), show auspicious objects like cows, and sprinkle with water reciting "Āpo hi ṣṭhā".

*   **Consecrating the Pot for Garuḍa:** Wrap a vessel holding four *āḍhakas* of water with threads, wash it, fill with water, wrap with two cloths, touch it reciting "Idamāpaḥ śivāḥ", throw in unhusked rice, gold, and a *kūrcha*. Place it on the grain pedestal facing Garuḍa. Meditating on Garuḍa, invoke his power into the water of the vessel. Sprinkling the vessel and the flag with water, tying the wrist-band (*pratisara*), spreading five cloths on the grains, arranging the bedding, lighting the fire, and praising the chanter, offer oblations to the flag, Garuḍa, and Viṣṇu. Engrave the seed-letter on his heart. Transfer the power from the vessel with a *kūrcha*. Meditating on Garuḍa, invoke him with form-mantras.

*   **Mounting the flag:** Place the flag facing west on the grain pedestal in the pavilion, the Cakra facing east on the west, and the peaceful icon facing south on the north. Worship Garuḍa with the Garuḍa-gāyatrī, and the others with their respective mantras, using eleven or eight services. Offer sweet rice (*mudgānna*) to Garuḍa, and pure rice (*śuddhānna*) to the other two. Give *ācamana* and mouth-fresheners, and perform the *puṇyāha*.

*   **Drum Consecration and Proclaiming the Festival:** In the Southeast, on a drum placed over grains, invoke Bhūteśa (Śiva) reciting "Bhūtīśaṃ mahābhujam...", worship Him, and offer *havis*. Decorate the drummer with cloths. The priest, facing east or north, reciting the drum sūkta "Upaśvāsaya pṛthivīm", should strike the drum three times with the drumstick with his right hand, reciting "Bhūr bhuvaḥ svaḥ". Afterwards, the drummer should beat the drum accompanied by all instruments.

*   **Preparing the Offerings (*Bali*):** The priest should prepare cooked rice with one *droṇa*, half a *droṇa*, or two *āḍhakas* of grains, mix it with cooked mung beans, flat beans, sesame, sesame cakes, flour, and parched grains. Gathering this abundant *bali*, having it carried by a disciple, placing the flag on a chariot or palanquin, he should lead the procession followed by the Cakra, Viṣvaksena, and the peaceful icon. Or he may carry the flag on his head.

*   **The Bali Procession:** Placing the food offering, water offering, and flower offering on the processional icon, the priest should walk in front. Offering *bali* to the gatekeepers, directional guardians, and temple guardians, he should enter the village and offer *bali* in order starting with Indra. If it is a West-facing temple, offer starting with Varuṇa. Proclaim the festival. In the village, chant "Yajñasya ghoṣad asi" and the mantras of the directional deities. Elsewhere, proclaim with prose, poetry, the local language, and other songs: "May all the gods, sages, ancestors, and the eighteen hosts of Siddhas, Vidyādharas, Garudas, Gandharvas, Yakṣas, Kinnaras, Nāgas, Bhūtas, Asuras, and Rākṣasas come!"

*   **Hoisting the Flag:** Circumambulating the village, reciting the *Śakuna-sūkta*, circumambulating the temple, establishing Viṣvaksena and the guardian in their respective spots, entering the flag area, unloading the flag, worshipping it with *pādya* etc., taking the rope, he should tie the flag along with a bell reciting "Svastidā viśaspatiḥ". The priest, facing east or north, should connect the flag to the top of the flagstaff with the pulley system reciting "Suparṇo'si". Holding the base of the flagstaff with both hands reciting "Bhūrasi", he should establish it reciting "Dhvajaṃ pratiṣṭhāpayāmi". Worshipping it, performing the *puṇyāha*, chanting the Vedas, offering sweet rice, *ācamana*, and mouth-fresheners.

*   **Benefits of the Ritual:** The food offered to Garuḍa on the flag should be made into a ball and eaten by a woman desiring a son; she will give birth to a strong, healthy, and long-lived son. As long as the flag remains hoisted, one should offer food two or three times, or once a day. Meditating on the victorious and auspicious flag, one should worship daily at twilight with twenty-seven forms using the four name-mantras — so says Marīci.
