# Chapter 53: Rules for the Festival (Utsava-vidhiḥ)
### Section 1: Liturgical Rules for the Festival Procession and Sacrificial Offerings

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **अथ त्रयः पञ्चाशत्तमः पटलः**
> **उत्सव-विधिः -**
> ***atha trayaḥpañcāśattamaḥ paṭalaḥ***
> ***utsava-vidhiḥ -***
> 
> पश्चाद् आलये अभिमुखे, दक्षिणे, आग्नेय्यां वा यागशालां षोडश-स्तम्भ-संयुक्तां पञ्च-हस्ताद् अहीनां चतुर्द्वार-युतां एकद्वार-युतां वा मण्डपं कूटं वा कारयेत्। तां तोरण-पूर्णकुम्भ-वितान-ध्वज-दर्भमालाद्यैः अलङ्कृत्य; तन्मध्ये अष्टहस्त-आयत-विस्तारां (मुद्रितपाठे अष्टाङ्गालायतविस्तारां) चतुरस्रां द्विताल-उत्सेधां कुम्भवेदिं च कृत्वा; तत्प्राच्यां पूर्ववत् सभ्याग्निकुण्डे हुत्वा आघारं (मुद्रितपाठे सभ्याऽग्निकुंडेत्त्वाधारं हुत्वा), आचार्यः तद्वेद्यां दक्षिणे च उत्तराभिमुखः तिष्ठन्, प्राणायामं कृत्वा, समाहितः देवं ध्यायन्, तन्मध्ये प्रधानकुम्भे देवेशं विष्णुं देव्यौ च;
> *paścād ālaye abhimukhe, dakṣiṇe, āgneyyāṃ vā yāgāśālāṃ ṣoḍaśa-stambha-saṃyuktāṃ pañca-hastād ahīnāṃ caturdvāra-yutāṃ ekadvāra-yutāṃ vā maṇḍapaṃ kūṭaṃ vā kārayet. tāṃ toraṇa-pūrṇakumbha-vitāna-dhvaja-darbhamālādyaiḥ alaṅkṛtya; tanmadhye aṣṭahasta-āyata-vistārāṃ caturasrāṃ dvitāla-utsedhāṃ kumbhavediṃ ca kṛtvā; tatprācyāṃ pūrvavat sabhyāgnikuṇḍe hutvā āghāraṃ, ācāryaḥ tadvedyāṃ dakṣiṇe ca uttarābhimukhaḥ tiṣṭhan, prāṇāyāmaṃ kṛtva, samāhitaḥ devaṃ dhyāyan, tanmadhye pradhānakumbhe deveśaṃ viṣṇuṃ devyou ca;*
> 
> प्राच्यां पुरुषं, दक्षिणस्यां सत्यं, प्रतीच्याम् अच्युतं, उदीच्याम् अनिरुद्धं, आग्नेय्यां कपिलं, नैर्ऋत्यां यज्ञं, वायव्यां नारायणं, ऐशान्यां पुण्यं च समावाह्य; पाद्याद्यैः अभ्यर्च्य; अग्निं प्रज्वाल्य;
> *prācyāṃ puruṣaṃ, dakṣiṇasyāṃ satyaṃ, pratīcyām acyutaṃ, udīcyām aniruddhaṃ, āgneyyāṃ kapilaṃ, nairṛtyāṃ yajñaṃ, vāyavyāṃ nārāyaṇaṃ, aiśānyāṃ puṇyaṃ ca samāvāhya; pādyādyaiḥ abhyarcya; agniṃ prajvālya;*
> 
> पश्चाद् उत्सव-बिम्बम् आसाद्य; अष्टोपचारैः अभ्यर्च्य; पुण्याहं वाचयित्वा; हेमादि-पात्रे तण्डुल-उपरि न्यस्तं सौवर्णं तान्तवं (मुद्रितपाठे तां तवं) प्रतिसरं पूर्ववद् बद्ध्वा; यस्य देवस्य यजनार्थं प्रतिसरबन्धः कृतः, तस्य हविः प्रतिषिद्धं, तस्माद् रात्रौ उत्सवान्ते स्नानकाले प्रतिसरं विमोच्य स्नापयित्वा; अभ्यर्च्य; हविः निवेदयेत्।
> *paścād utsava-bimbam āsādya; aṣṭopacāraiḥ abhyarcya; puṇyāhaṃ vācayitvā; hemādi-pātre taṇḍula-upari nyastaṃ souvarṇaṃ tāntavaṃ pratisaraṃ pūrvavad baddhvā; yasya devasya yajanārthaṃ pratisarabandhaḥ kṛtaḥ, tasya haviḥ pratiṣiddhaṃ, tasmād rātrou utsavānte snānakāle pratisaraṃ vimocya snāpayitvā; abhyarcya; haviḥ nivedayet.*
> 
> पश्चात् यज्ञशालाम् आगत्य; अग्निं प्रज्वाल्य; परिषिच्य; हौत्रं प्रशंस्य; 'अतो देवा' (ऋ॰ सं॰ १-२२-१६) इत्यादिभिः, 'विष्णोर्नुकम्' (ऋ॰ सं॰ १-१५४-१) इत्यादिभिः, 'सहस्रशीर्षा' (ऋ॰ सं॰ १०-९०-१) इत्यादिभिः, देवादि-तदालयगत-परिषद्-देवानां मूर्तिमन्त्रैः च हुत्वा; उत्सवाधिदैवतं तिथिवारनक्षत्रदैवतं च क्रमाज्जुहुयात्।
> *paścāt yajñaśālām āgatya; agniṃ prajvālya; pariṣicya; houtraṃ praśaṃsya; 'ato devā' ityādibhiḥ, 'viṣṇornukam' ityādibhiḥ, 'sahasraśīrṣā' ityādibhiḥ, devādi-tadālayagata-pariṣad-devānāṃ mūrtimantraiḥ ca hutvā; utsavādhidevataṃ tithivāranaxatradevataṃ ca kramājjuhuyāt.*
> 
> उत्सवाधिदैवतं स्रुवेण आज्येन, तिथिदैवतम् उपभृता साज्येन पक्वेन, वारदैवतम् आज्येन अग्निहोत्रहवण्या, नक्षत्रदैवतम् आज्येन जुहुयात्। सर्वालाभे तत्-तत् स्मृत्वा स्रुवेण होतव्यम्। यावत्संख्या आहुतिः तावत् समिद्धोमं चरुहोमं च कुर्यात्।
> *utsavādidevataṃ sruveṇa ājyena, tithidevatam upabhṛtā sājyena pakvena, vāradevatam ājyena agnihotrahavaṇyā, naxatradevatam ājyena juhuyāt. sarvālabhe tat-tat smṛtvā sruveṇa hotavyam. yāvatsaṃkhyā āhutiḥ tāvat samiddhomaṃ caruhomaṃ ca kuryāt.*
> 
> **होतव्य-समिदादि -**
> प्रथमदिने पालाशं, द्वितीये बैल्वं, तृतीये शमीं, चतुर्थे नैयग्रोधं, पञ्चमे औदुम्बरं, षष्ठे चाश्वत्थं, सप्तमे खादिरं, अष्टमे प्लाक्षं, नवमे पालाशम् इध्मं जुहुयात्। एतेषाम् अलाभे पालाशम् अश्वत्थं वा जुहुयात्। आज्यालाभे आज्यमिश्र-चरुणा होतव्यम्। दधि-क्षीर-आज्य-मिश्रं लाजाम् उपजुह्वा आदाय वैष्णवेन जुहुयात् ॥
> ***hotavya-samidādi -***
> *prathamadine pālāśaṃ, dvitīye bailvaṃ, tṛtīye śamīṃ, caturthe naiyagrodhaṃ, pañcame oudumbaraṃ, ṣaṣṭhe cāśvatthaṃ, saptame khādiraṃ, aṣṭame plākṣaṃ, navame pālāśam idhmaṃ juhuyāt. eteṣām alābhe pālāśam aśvatthaṃ vā juhuyāt. ājyālābe ājyamiśra-caruṇā hotavyam. dadhi-kṣīra-ājya-miśraṃ lājām upajhuvā ādāya vaiṣṇavena juhuyāt.*
> 
> **द्विगुणार्चनाद्यर्थं पद्मादि-सङ्ग्रहः -**
> देवस्य द्विगुणार्चनार्थं कुम्भदेवाराधनार्थं, होमार्थं, आस्थानार्चनार्थं, बलिदानार्थं च, प्रथमदिने पद्मं; द्वितीये बिल्वपत्रं; तृतीये करवीरं; चतुर्थे कुमुदं; पञ्चमे नन्द्यावर्त्तं; षष्ठे तुलसीं; सप्तमे स्थलारविन्दम्, अष्टमे विष्णुकान्तं, नवमे पङ्कजम्, एतेषाम् अलाभे करवीरं, तुलसीं वा आहरेत् ॥
> ***dviguṇārcanādyarthaṃ padmādi-saṅgrahaḥ -***
> *devasya dviguṇārcanārthaṃ kumbhadevarādanārthaṃ, homārthaṃ, āsthānārcanārthaṃ, balidānārthaṃ ca, prathamadine padmaṃ; dvitīye bilvapatraṃ; tṛtīye karavīraṃ; caturthe kumudaṃ; pañcame nandyāvartaṃ; ṣaṣṭhe tulasīṃ; saptame sthalāravindam, aṣṭame viṣṇukāntaṃ, navame paṅkajam, eteṣām alābhe karavīraṃ, tulasīṃ vā āharet.*
> 
> त्रिगुणीकृत-नवाहिक-त्रिंशाद्-दिनाद्यधिक-दिने नवाहिक-उत्सव-समिद्-पुष्प-बलिविधानादीनि (मुद्रितपाठे नवाहिकोत्ससस्रित्युष्प-बलिविहनादीनि) पूर्ववत्।
> *triguṇīkyta-nahāvika-triṃśād-dinādyadhika-dine nāhāvika-utsava-samid-puṣpa-balividhānādīni pūrvavat.*
> 
> **कुम्भस्थ-देवपूजनम् बलिहरणक्रमश्च -**
> कुम्भस्थान् देवान् एकादशोपचारैः वा अभ्यर्च्य; द्विकालं हविः निवेदयेत्। तद्वेद्यां पश्चिमे विष्टरे देवेशं, दक्षिणे चक्रं, उत्तरे विष्वक्सेनं च संस्थाप्य, अभ्यर्च्य; पश्चिमे देवाभिमुखे धान्यपीठे बलिरक्षकं च आराध्य; मुखवासं प्रदाय; देवेशम् अभ्यर्च्य; हविः निवेद्य; विधिना अन्नबलिं अर्घ्यबलिं पुष्पबलिं वा आराध्य; चक्रमग्रे, विष्वक्सेनं (वीशं) शान्तं बलिबेरं च क्रमेण नयेत्।
> ***kumbhastha-devapūjanam baliharaṇakramaśca -***
> *kumbhasthān devān ekādaśopacāraiḥ vā abhyarcya; dvikālaṃ haviḥ nivedayet. tadvedyāṃ paścime viṣṭare deveśaṃ, daxiṇe cakraṃ, uttare viṣvakṣenaṃ ca saṃsthāpya, abhyarcya; paścime devābhimukhe dhānypīṭhe baliraxakaṃ ca ārādhya; muṣavāsaṃ pradāya; deveśam abhyarcya; haviḥ nivedya; vidhinā annabaliṃ arghyabaliṃ puṣpabaliṃ vā ārādhya; cakramagre, viṣvakṣenaṃ śāntaṃ baliberaṃ ca krameṇa nayet.*
> 
> पूर्ववत् द्वाराभावे चक्रादीन् पात्रेषु समावाह्य; अभ्यर्च्य; नयेत्। अथवा चक्रं बलिबेरं च नयेत्। पूर्ववत् प्रभूतं बलिम् आहत्य; शिष्येण वाहयित्वा; पुष्प-गन्ध-धूप-दीप-अक्षत-युतं बलिदाता अग्रतो गच्छेत्। पूर्ववत् द्वारपालादिभ्यः बलिं दत्त्वा; ग्रामस्य बाह्यवीथ्यां, महत्त्वे अभ्यन्तरवीथ्यां वा, प्रदक्षिणवशेन दिक्पालानां मध्ये ब्रह्मणश्च बलिनिर्वापं कारयेत्। ग्राममध्ये विमानं चेत् मध्यादि-नव-दिक्षु बलिं दद्यात्। पश्चिमादि विमानेषु ब्रह्मबलिं विना दद्यात् इत्येके।
> *pūrvavat dvārābhāve cakrādīn pātreṣu samāvāhya; abhyarcya; nayet. athavā cakraṃ baliberaṃ ca nayet. pūrvavat prabhūtaṃ balim āhṛtya; śiṣyeṇa vāhayitvā; puṣpa-gandha-dhūpa-dīpa-axata-yutaṃ balidātā agrato gacchet. pūrvavat dvārapālādibhyaḥ baliṃ dattvā; grāmasya bāhyavīthyāṃ, mahattve abhyantaravīthyāṃ vā, pradaxiṇavaśena dikpālānāṃ madhye brahmaṇaśca balinirvāpaṃ kārayet. grāmamadhye vimānaṃ cet madhyādi-nav-dikṣu baliṃ dadyāt. paścimādi vimāneṣu brahmabaliṃ vinā dadyāt ityeke.*
> 
> यत्र वास्तुविन्यासविशेषो नास्ति, तत्राऽष्टदिक्षु, नवदिक्षु वा, बलिं क्षिपेत्। पौराणिकी श्रेणी चेत् चतुष्कोणेषु सन्धिषु सर्वत्र क्षिपेत्। दण्डकश्चेत् पश्चिमे वरुण-निर्ऋति-वायुभ्यः, मध्ये ब्रह्म-यम-कुबेरेभ्यः, पूर्वे इन्द्र-अनल-ईशेभ्यो दद्यात्। दक्षिणोत्तरं चेत् दक्षिणे यम-निर्ऋति-पावकेभ्यः, मध्ये ब्रह्म-वरुण-इन्द्रेभ्यः, उत्तरे कुबेर-पवन-ईशेभ्यो बलिं दद्यात्। नदीतीर-अद्रि-स्थानेषु आवरणाद् बहिः अष्टदिक्षु क्षिपेत्। अथवा ग्रामः योजनाद् अर्वाक् चेत् तत्र गत्वा बलिम् आचरेत्।
> *yatra vāstuvinyāsaviśeṣo nāsti, tatrā’ṣṭadikṣu, navadikṣu vā, baliṃ xiṣet. pourāṇikī śreṇī cet catuṣkoṇeṣu sandhiṣu sarvatra xiṣet. daṇḍakaścet paścime varuṇa-nirṛti-vāyubhyaḥ, madhye brahma-yama-kuberebhyaḥ, pūrve indra-anala-īśebhyo dadyāt. daxiṇottaraṃ cet daxiṇe yama-nirṛti-pāvakebhyaḥ, madhye brahma-varuṇa-indrebhyaḥ, uttare kubera-pavana-īśebhyo baliṃ dadyāt. nadītīra-adri-sthāneṣu āvaraṇād bahiḥ aṣṭadikṣu xiṣet. athavā grāmaḥ yojanād arvāk cet tatra gatvā balim ācaret.*
> 
> तस्मिन् स्थाने दर्भान् विकीर्य; तत्तद्दिगीशाय बलिं दत्त्वा; प्रागाद्यष्टदिक्षु सर्वेभ्यो देवेभ्यो, भूतेभ्यो, यक्षेभ्यो (मुद्रितपाठे न्पक्षेभ्यो), राक्षसेभ्यः, पिशाचेभ्यः, नागेभ्यो, गन्धर्वेभ्यो, अष्टादश-गणेभ्यश्च बलिं दद्यात् उत्तमम् ॥
> *tasmin sthāne darbhān vikīrya; tattaddigīśāya baliṃ dattvā; prāgādyaṣṭadikṣu sarvebyo devebyo, bhūtebyo, yaṣebyo, rāṣasebyaḥ, piśācebyaḥ, nāgebyo, gandharvebyo, aṣṭādaśa-gaṇebhyaśca baliṃ dadyāt uttamam.*
> 
> प्रागादि चतसृषु दिक्षु सर्वेभ्यो भूतेभ्यो यक्षेभ्यो राक्षसेभ्यो बलिं दद्यात् मध्यमम्।
> *prāgādi catasṛṣu dikṣu sarvebyo bhūtebyo yaṣebyo rāṣasebyo baliṃ dadyāt madhyamam.*
> 
> तन्मध्ये तद्दिगीशाय बलिं दद्यात् अधमम्।
> *tanmadhye tattaddigīśāya baliṃ dadyāt adhamam.*
> 
> तत्तन्नाम्ना पूर्वम् उदक-पुष्पं बलिं उदकं च दद्यात्।
> *tattannāmnā pūrvam udaka-puṣpaṃ baliṃ udakaṃ ca dadyāt.*
> 
> एवं इन्द्रादीशान-अन्तं सर्वेषु स्थानेषु एकं विष्टरं निधाय; तस्मिन् बलि-निर्वापं कुर्याद् इति केचित्। एवं क्रमात् प्रदक्षिणवशेन बलिं दत्त्वा; आलयम् आविश्य, भूतपीठे बलिशेषं निर्वपेत्। यदि राजधानी चेत् राजवेश्माभिमुखम् अङ्गणं त्रिधा कृत्वा; पश्चिमभागे प्रासादसमीपे महेन्द्राय (मुद्रितपाठे वेंद्राय), तत्पार्श्वयोः जयश्रियै शचीदेव्यै च, द्वारपार्श्वयोः शङ्ख-पद्म-निधिभ्यां च बलिं दत्त्वा; सर्वत्र निर्वाप-अन्ते 'ये भूताः' (वै॰ म॰ प्र॰ ३) इति आकाशे विसृजेत्। अथवा देवेशेन सह अग्रतो बलिम् एवं क्षिपेत् ॥
> *evaṃ indrādīśāna-antaṃ sarveṣu sthāneṣu ekaṃ viṣṭaraṃ nidhāya; tasmin bali-nirvāpaṃ kuryād iti kecit. evaṃ kramāt pradaxiṇavaśena baliṃ dattvā; ālayam āviśya, bhūtapīṭhe baliśeṣaṃ nirvapet. yadi rājadhānī cet rājaveśmābhimuxam aṅgaṇaṃ tridhā kṛtvā; paścimabhāge prāsādasamīpe mahendrāya, tatpārśvayoḥ jayaśriyai śacīdevyai ca, dvārapārśvayoḥ śaṅkha-padma-nidhibhyāṃ ca baliṃ dattvā; sarvatra nirvāpa-ante 'ye bhūtāḥ' iti ākāśe visṛjet. athavā deveśena saha agrato balim evaṃ xiṣet.*
> 
> **शिबिकादि-यानोत्सवः -**
> शिबिकादि-यानं वस्त्र-गन्ध-माल्याद्यैः अलङ्कृत्य; देवेशं नानावर्णाम्बरैः नवैः कौशेयाद्यैः सूक्ष्म-कार्पास-तन्तु-कृतैश्च, रत्नयुत-मुकुट-अङ्गद-मकरकुण्डल-केयूर-हार-कटिसूत्र-उदरबन्धन-अङ्गुलीयक-मुक्तादाम-प्रलम्ब-यज्ञोपवीताद्यैः आभरणैः, सुगन्ध-माल्यैः नानाविध-गन्धैश्च अलङ्कुर्यात्। अज्ञानाद् अर्थलोभाद् वा देवस्य अलङ्कारे हीने सर्वहानिः भवेत्, तस्मात् यत्नेन अलङ्कुर्यात्।
> ***śibikādi-yānotsavaḥ -***
> *śibikādi-yānaṃ vastra-gandha-mālyādyaiḥ alaṅkṛtya; deveśaṃ nānavarṇāmbaraiḥ navaiḥ kouśeyādyaiḥ sūxma-kārpāsa-tantu-kytaiśca, ratnayuta-mukuṭa-aṅgada-makarakuṇḍala-keyūra-hāra-kaṭisūtra-udarabandhana-aṅgulīyaka-muktādāma-pralamba-yajñopavītādyaiḥ ābharaṇaiḥ, sugandha-mālyaiḥ nānāvidha-gandhaiśca alaṅkuryāt. ajñānād arthalobhād vā devasya alaṅkāre hīne sarvahāniḥ bhavet, tasmāt yatnena alaṅkuryāt.*
> 
> वस्त्रं जीर्णं स्फुटितम् उपयुक्तं वा यो दद्यात्, तस्य महत्तरो दोषो भवति। नवं मृदु सूक्ष्मं वस्त्रं यो दद्यात्, स कुलैः सप्तभिः युक्तः विष्णुलोकम् अवाप्नुयात्।
> *vastraṃ jīrṇaṃ sphuṭitam upayuktaṃ vā yo dadyāt, tasya mahattaro doṣo bhavati. navaṃ mṛdu sūxmaṃ vastraṃ yo dadyāt, sa kulaiḥ saptabhiḥ yuktaḥ viṣṇulokam avāpnuyaat.*
> 
> भक्तियुक्तैः शूद्रैः अनुलोमैः वा वाहयित्वा न दोषो भवति; तत्-प्रतिलोमैः भक्तियुक्तैः वा वाहनं दोषाय भवति। अकिञ्चन-ब्राह्मणैः वाहनं तत् मुख्यं भवति ॥
> *bhaktiyuktāiḥ śūdraiḥ anulomaiḥ vā vāhayitvā na doṣo bhavati; tat-pratilomaiḥ bhaktiyuktāiḥ vā vāhanaṃ doṣāya bhavati. akiñcana-brāhmaṇaiḥ vāhanaṃ tat muṣyaṃ bhavati.*
> 
> देवेशं प्रणम्य, 'वेदाहम्' (तै॰ आ॰ ३-१२-७) इति यानम् आरोप्य; सौवर्णैः पुष्पैः अन्यैः वा प्रभामण्डपम् अलङ्कृत्य; योजयेत्। नर्तक-गायक-वादकाः चामर-व्यजन-मयूरताल-वितान-वृन्तादि-धराः, हेम-रजत-ताम्र-परिच्छद-ढक्का-वितान-ध्वज-पताकादि-धराः, तोय-विक्षेपकाः च जनाः पुरतो गच्छेयुः। छत्रधराः धूपदीपधराः परितो गच्छेयुः। वेदान् जयशब्दान् स्तोत्रादीनि च पठन्तः अनुगच्छेयुः।
> *deveśaṃ praṇamya, 'vedāham' iti yānam āropya; souvarṇaiḥ puṣpaiḥ anyaiḥ vā prabhāmaṇḍapam alaṅkṛtya; yojeyet. nartaka-gāyaka-vādakāḥ cāmara-vyajana-mayūratāla-vitāna-vṛntādi-dharāḥ, hema-rajata-tāmra-paricchada-ḍhakkā-vitāna-dhvaja-patākādi-dharāḥ, toya-vixepakāḥ ca janāḥ purato gaccheyuḥ. chatradharāḥ dhūpadīpadharāḥ parito gaccheyuḥ. vedān jayaśabdān stotrādīni ca paṭhantaḥ anugaccheyuḥ.*
> 
> उत्सवे देवेशं ये सेवन्ते ते सर्वे पदे पदे यज्ञफलं लभेरन् इति भृग्वादयो वदन्ति। तस्माद् अस्पृश्य-स्पर्शनं न दोषाय भवेत्। तद्ग्रामवासिनो जना यानस्थं देवं स्वेषु गृहेषु दीपाङ्कुराद्यैः प्रत्युत्थानं कृत्वा नमस्कुर्युः। कदली-चूत-पनस-नालिकेर-अपूप-लाज-पृथुकादीनि ब्राह्मण-आनीतानि सङ्गृह्य अमन्त्रकं निवेदयेत्। शूद्र-अनुलोम-आनीतानि अपक्वानि निवेदयेत्। प्रतिलोम-आनीतानि तानि तानि सर्वाणि वर्जयेत्।
> *utsave deveśaṃ ye sevante te sarve pade pade yajñaphalaṃ labheran iti bhṛgvādayo vadanti. tasmād aspṛśya-sparśanaṃ na doṣāya bhavet. tadgrāmavāsino janā yānasthaṃ devaṃ sveṣu gṛheṣu dīpāṅkurādyaiḥ pratyutthānaṃ kṛtva namaskuryuḥ. kadalī-cūta-panasa-nālikera-apūpa-lāja-pṛthukādīni brāhmaṇa-ānītāni saṅgṛhya amantrakaṃ nivedayet. śūdra-anuloma-ānītāni apakvāni nivedayet. pratiloma-ānītāni tāni tāni sarvāṇi varjayet.*
> 
> एवं ग्रामं प्रदक्षिणीकृत्य, शकुनसूक्तेन आलयम् आवेश्य, आस्थानमण्डपे सिंहासने विष्टरे वा देवं संस्थाप्य, पाद्याद्यैः अभ्यर्च्य, मुखवासं दत्त्वा, राजवद् उपचारं कृत्वा, शुद्धस्नपनविधिना संस्नाप्य, अभ्यर्च्य, महाहविः प्रभूतं वा निवेद्य, पानीय-आचमन-मुखवासं दत्त्वा, द्वितीयाहः प्रभृति नित्य-सायं-प्रातः नित्यार्चन-अन्ते द्विगुणार्चनं हविर्निवेदनं कुम्भदेवाराधनं होमं बलिदानं च कृत्वा सायं प्रातः एवम् एव उत्सवं कारयेत् ॥
> *evaṃ grāmaṃ pradacṣiṇīkytya, śakunasūktena ālayam āveśya, āsthānamaṇḍape siṃhāsane viṣṭare vā devaṃ saṃsthāpya, pādyādyaiḥ abhyarcya, muṣavāsaṃ dattvā, rājavad upacāraṃ kṛtvā, śuddhasnapanavidhinā saṃsnāpya, abhyarcya, mahāhaviḥ prabhūtaṃ vā nivedya, pānīya-ācamana-muṣavāsaṃ dattvā, dvitīyāhaḥ prabhṛti nitya-sāyaṃ-prātaḥ nityārcana-ante dviguṇārcanaṃ havisnivedanaṃ kumbhadevarādhanaṃ homaṃ balidānaṃ ca kṛtva sāyaṃ prātaḥ evam eva utsavaṃ kārayet.*

#### English Translation
*   **The Sacrificial Hall (*yāgāśālā*):** Now, the rules for the festival procession (*utsava-vidhiḥ*): In front of the temple, towards the South or Southeast, one should construct a sacrificial hall supported by sixteen pillars, measuring not less than five hands (*hastas*), having either four doors or a single door, or build a pavilion (*maṇḍapa*) or a vaulted shrine (*kūṭa*).

*   **The Altar and Pot Arrangements:** Decorating the hall with archways (*toraṇa*), full water pots (*pūrṇa-kumbha*), canopies, banners, and Darbha grass garlands; make a square bedding altar for the pots (*kumbha-vedi*) in the center, measuring eight hands in length and width, and two spans high. On the East of it, prepare a Sabhya fire-pit as before, smear with cow dung, decorate the floor with flour drawings, and spread grains to a thickness of two finger-units or one finger-unit on the altar. Divide it into nine compartments, scatter flowers, Darbha grass, and unhusked rice, and place lamps, sprouts, the eight auspicious symbols (*aṣṭa-maṅgala*), and the five weapons around it.

*   **Preparation of the Sacred Vessels:** Having gathered nine new pots free from cracks and black spots, take one principal vessel (*pradhāna-kumbha*) capable of holding two *droṇas* of water, and other secondary vessels holding one *droṇa*. Wrap them with cotton threads at intervals of a barley-grain or a finger-unit reciting "Indraṃ naro na", wash them reciting "Śucī vo havyā", collect river water during the day, filter it through a cloth reciting "Dhārāsu", scent it with vetiver (*uśīra*) and other fragrances, and fill the pots reciting "Idam āpaḥ śivāḥ". Drop in unhusked rice and a Darbha brush (*kūrca*), wrap each pot with a pair of garments, deposit nine gems inside the pots starting from the center with the Viṣṇu-gāyatrī (or gold if gems are unavailable), and place the five golden weapons of Viṣṇu in the principal pot. Place the pots in order on the altar reciting the *Viṣṇu-sūkta*, and cover them with lids.

*   **Invocations and Wrist-band Ritual:** Having offered the introductory libation (*āghāra*) in the Sabhya fire-pit, the priest, standing on the south side of the altar and facing north, performing breath control, with a concentrated mind meditating on the Lord, should invoke: the Lord of Gods, Viṣṇu, along with the two Goddesses (Śrī and Bhū) in the principal vessel in the center; Puruṣa in the East; Satya in the South; Acyuta in the West; Aniruddha in the North; Kapila in the Southeast; Yajña in the Southwest; Nārāyaṇa in the Northwest; and Puṇya in the Northeast. Worshipping them with *pādya* and other services, lighting the fire, bringing the processional icon (*utsava-bera*), worshipping Him with eight services, performing the purification ceremony (*puṇyāha*), he should tie the golden or cotton-thread wrist-band (*pratisara*) placed on top of raw rice in a golden vessel as before. The food offering is prohibited for the deity on whose behalf the wrist-band has been tied; therefore, on the final night of the festival, at the time of the concluding bath, he should untie the wrist-band, bathe the deity, worship Him, and offer food.

*   **Sacrificial Oblations (*Homa*):** Returning to the sacrificial hall, lighting the fire, sprinkling water around it, praising the chanter's role, he should make fire-offerings with the *Ato devā*, *Viṣṇor nu kam*, and *Sahasra-śīrṣā* hymns, and with the form-mantras of the presiding deities of the temple enclosures. He should then offer oblations to the presiding deities of the festival days, dates, weekdays, and constellations in sequence.

*   **Instruments and Specific Offerings:** The oblation for the festival deity should be offered with clarified ghee using the wooden ladle (*sruva*); for the date deity, with cooked food (*caru*) and ghee using the flat spoon (*upabhṛt*); for the weekday deity, with ghee using the sacrificial ladle (*agnihotra-havaṇī*); and for the constellation deity, with ghee. If any of these are unavailable, one should offer ghee using the *sruva* while meditating on them. He should perform fire-offerings of fuel sticks (*samid-homa*) and cooked rice (*caru-homa*) matching the number of oblations.

*   **Fuel Wood Specifications:** The sacrificial fuel wood (*idhma*) should be: Palāśa on the first day; Bilva on the second; Śamī on the third; Nyagrodha on the fourth; Udumbara on the fifth; Aśvattha on the sixth; Khādira on the seventh; Plakṣa on the eighth; and Palāśa on the ninth day. If these are unavailable, one should burn Palāśa or Aśvattha wood. If ghee is unavailable, cooked rice mixed with ghee should be offered. Taking parched grains (*lāja*) mixed with yogurt, milk, and ghee using the secondary spoon (*upajuhū*), he should offer them with the Vaishnava mantra.

*   **Flower Varieties for Double Worship:** For the double worship (*dviguṇārcana*), pot worship, fire-offerings, assembly hall worship, and daily food-offerings: gather lotus (*padma*) on the first day; Bilva leaves on the second; Karavīra on the third; Kumuda on the fourth; Nandyāvarta on the fifth; Tulasī on the sixth; Sthalāravinda on the seventh; Viṣṇukānta on the eighth; and Paṅkaja (red lotus) on the ninth day. If these are unavailable, Karavīra or Tulasī should be gathered. For festivals extended to twenty-seven, thirty, or more days, the rules for festival duration, fuel sticks, flowers, and offerings are as before.

*   **The Offering Procession (*Bali-haraṇa*):** Having worshipped the deities in the vessels with eleven services, he should offer food twice daily. Placing the Lord on a seat to the West of the altar, with the Cakra on His right and Viṣvakṣena on His left, and worshipping them, he should worship the guardian of the offerings (*bali-rakṣaka*) on a grain pedestal facing the deity in the West. Offering mouth-fresheners, worshipping the Lord, presenting food, and offering cooked rice, libation water, and flowers in accordance with the rules, he should carry the Cakra first, followed by Viṣvakṣena (the peaceful form), and the processional icon (*bali-bera*) in sequence.

*   **Offering Layout in the Village Streets:** If the temple doors are not aligned, he should invoke the Cakra and others into metal plates, worship them, and carry them. Alternatively, he should carry the Cakra and the *bali-bera*. Gathering a large quantity of offering food as before, having it carried by a disciple, the giver of offerings should go in front accompanied by flowers, fragrances, incense, lamps, and unhusked rice. Having offered portion-offerings to the door guardians and other deities, he should offer the *bali* in the outer street of the village, or if the village is large, in the inner street, in a clockwise direction. He should place offerings in the middle of the directional guardians (*dikpālas*) and for Brahmā. If the temple is situated in the center of the village, he should offer it in nine directions beginning from the center. Some say that in temples facing West and other directions, the offering to Brahmā should be omitted.

*   **Detailed Enclosure Offerings:** Where there is no specific site layout (*vāstu-vinyāsa*), one should scatter offerings in the eight or nine directions. If the village is arranged in a traditional layout (*paurāṇikī-śreṇī*), he should scatter it at all four corners and junctions. If it is a linear layout (*daṇḍaka*), he should offer it: in the West to Varuṇa, Nirṛti, and Vāyu; in the center to Brahmā, Yama, and Kubera; and in the East to Indra, Agni, and Iśāna. If it is a North-South street layout, he should offer it: in the South to Yama, Nirṛti, and Agni; in the center to Brahmā, Varuṇa, and Indra; and in the North to Kubera, Vāyu, and Iśāna. In areas near riverbanks or mountains, he should scatter it in the eight directions outside the enclosure. If the village is located within one *yojana*, he should go there and perform the offering.

*   **Invocations to Earthly Spirits:** Scattering Darbha grass in that spot, offering *bali* to the lord of that direction, he should give the highest class of offering (*uttama*) to all the gods, elemental spirits (*bhūtas*), demigods (*yakṣas*), demons (*rākṣasas*), ghosts (*piśācas*), serpents (*nāgas*), celestial musicians (*gandharvas*), and the eighteen classes of spirits in the eight directions beginning with the East. Giving the middle class of offering (*madhyama*) to all elemental spirits, *yakṣas*, and *rākṣasas* in the four principal directions starting with the East. Giving the lowest class of offering (*adhama*) to the lord of that direction in the center. He should offer the water-flower offering first, mentioning each by name, followed by water.

*   **Royal Palace and Concluding Offerings:** Some say that one should place a seat at all locations from East to Northeast and make the offering on it. Having offered the *bali* in order in a clockwise direction, entering the temple, he should deposit the remainder of the offering (*bali-śeṣa*) on the platform of the spirits (*bhūta-pīṭha*). If it is a capital city, dividing the courtyard facing the royal palace into three parts, he should offer *bali* to Mahendra in the western part near the palace, to Jaya-śrī and Śacī-devī on both sides, and to Śaṅkha-nidhi and Padma-nidhi at the sides of the gate. Having completed the offerings everywhere, he should dismiss the spirits into the sky reciting "Ye bhūtāḥ". Alternatively, he should scatter the offering in front while walking along with the Lord.

*   **Vehicles and Dressing Etiquette:** Decorating the palanquin or other vehicles with cloths, fragrances, and garlands; the Lord should be dressed in new, multi-colored silk garments or fine cotton garments. He should be adorned with ornaments such as a gem-studded crown, armlets, alligator-earrings, keyūra, necklaces, waist-bands, belly-bands, rings, pearl strands, and long sacred threads, and decorated with fragrant garlands and various perfumes. If the Lord's decoration is deficient due to ignorance or greed, everything will be ruined; therefore, he should decorate Him with great effort.

*   **Dangers of Damaged Garments:** He who offers a worn-out, torn, or already used garment commits a grave sin. He who offers a new, soft, and fine garment ascends to the world of Viṣṇu along with seven generations of his family.

*   **Rules for Bearers:** There is no defect if the vehicle is carried by devoted Shudras of mixed-caste (*anuloma*) origin. Carrying it by reverse-caste (*pratiloma*) people, even if devoted, causes defect. Carrying it by poor Brahmins is the most superior method.

*   **The Festival Procession:** Bowing to the Lord of Gods, mounting Him on the vehicle reciting "Vedāham", decorating the halo-arch (*prabhā-maṇḍapa*) with golden flowers or other ornaments, he should start the procession. Dancers, singers, and musicians; bearers of fans, chowries, peacock-feather umbrellas, canopies, and round fans; individuals carrying gold, silver, and copper vessels, kettledrums, canopies, banners, and flags; and people sprinkling sacred water should walk in front. Bearers of umbrellas, incense, and lamps should walk around Him. Devotees should follow chanting the Vedas, cries of victory, and hymns.

*   **Ritual Merit and Food Offerings:** Sages like Bhrigu declare that those who serve the Lord during the festival acquire the merit of a horse-sacrifice at every step. Therefore, the contact of untouchables during this time does not cause any pollution. The residents of the village should welcome the Lord seated on the vehicle at their homes with lamps and sprouts, and bow to Him. One should accept bananas, mangoes, jackfruit, coconuts, cakes, parched grains, and flattened rice brought by Brahmins and offer them without mantras. He should offer uncooked items brought by Shudras and *anulomas*. He must reject all items brought by *pratiloma* castes. Having circumambulated the village in this manner, entering the temple reciting the *Śakuna-sūkta*, placing the Lord on a throne or seat in the assembly hall, worshipping Him with *pādya* and other services, offering mouth-fresheners, presenting royal-like services, bathing Him according to pure bathing rules, worshipping, offering the great food-offering (*mahā-havis*) or abundant food, and giving water, rinsing water, and mouth-fresheners, he should perform the festival every evening and morning from the second day onwards—concluding the daily morning and evening worships with double worship, food offerings, pot-deity worship, fire-offerings, and bali-offerings.
