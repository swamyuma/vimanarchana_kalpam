# Vimanarchana Kalpa of Sage Marīci (Vaikhānasa Āgama)
## Chapter 77: Tantra-saṅkarādi-sarvadoṣa-prāyaścitta-vidhiḥ (Rules of Expiation for Mixing Worship Systems and Unspecified Defects)

This document contains the Sanskrit text, IAST transliteration, and English translation of the 77th Chapter (*Saptasaptatitamaḥ Paṭalaḥ*) of the **Vimānārcanākalpa** (attributed to Sage Marīci, representing the Vaikhānasa school of Vaishnavism).

This chapter outlines the expiations and purifications required when different systems of Vaishnava worship (*tantra-saṅkara*)—specifically the Vedic *Vaikhānasa* and the non-Vedic *Pancharatra*—are mixed, detailing the severe consequences of such combinations on the king and kingdom. It also defines general expiation protocols (*sāmānya-prāyaścitta*) for all unspecified ritual errors (*anukta-doṣa*) of minor or major severity.

---

### Section 1: Expiation for Mixing Worship Systems

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **अथ सप्तसप्ततितमः पटलः**
> **तन्त्रसङ्करे प्रायश्चित्तम्**
> ***atha saptasaptatitamaḥ paṭalaḥ***
> ***tantrasaṅkare prāyaścittam***
> 
> अथ तन्त्रसङ्कर-प्रायश्चित्तं वक्ष्ये —
> वैष्णवस्य अन्यतन्त्रेण सङ्करे सति, तद् ग्राम-यजमान-नृपाणाम् (or राष्ट्राणाम्) आशु विनाशाय भवति। तस्मात् शीघ्रतरम् अब्जाग्नौ वैष्णवं, विष्णुसूक्तं, पुरुषसूक्तं, पारमात्मिकं, ओङ्कारम् अष्टाशीतिं च अष्टोत्तरशतं हुत्वा, पूर्ववत् कारयेत् ॥
> *atha tantrasaṅkara-prāyaścittaṃ vakṣye —*
> *vaiṣṇavasya anyatantreṇa saṅkare sati, tad grāma-yajamāna-nṛpāṇām āśu vināśāya bhavati | tasmāt śīghrataram abjāgnou vaiṣṇavaṃ, viṣṇusūktaṃ, puruṣasūktaṃ, pāramātmikaṃ, oṅkāram aṣṭāśītiṃ ca aṣṭottaraśataṃ hutvā, pūrvavat kārayet ||*

#### English Translation
*   **The Subject:** Now, I shall declare the expiation for mixing systems of worship (*tantra-saṅkara-prāyaścittaṃ vakṣye*).

*   **Consequences of System Mixing:** If the Vaishnava worship is mixed with another system (*vaiṣṇavasya anya-tantreṇa saṅkare sati*), it leads to the immediate destruction (*āśu vināśāya bhavati*) of the village, the patron/host, and the king/state.

*   **Expiation Rites:** Therefore, one should immediately perform fire offerings in the lotus-fire (*abjāgnou*), offering one hundred and eight and eighty-eight oblations of the Vaiṣṇava, Viṣṇu-sūkta, Puruṣa-sūkta, Pāramātmika, and Oṅkāra mantras, and then conduct the consecration as before.

### Section 2: Classification of Vaishnava Tantras

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **तन्त्रद्वैविध्यादिकम्**
> वैष्णवं द्विविधं — वैखानसं पाञ्चरात्रम् इति; वैखानसं वैदिकं, वैदिकैर् अर्चितम्, ऐहिक-आमुष्मिक-फल-प्रदम्। पाञ्चरात्रम् अपेयम्, अवैदिकं दीक्षितैर् अर्चितम्, आमुष्मिक-फल-प्रदम्, सौम्यं सर्वत्र सम्पूज्यम्।
> ***tantradvaividhyādikam***
> *vaiṣṇavaṃ dvividhaṃ — vaikhānasaṃ pāñcarātram iti | vaikhānasaṃ vaidikaṃ, vaidikair arcitam, aihika-āmuṣmika-phala-pradam | pāñcarātram apeyam, avaidikaṃ dīxitair arcitam, āmuṣmika-phala-pradam, saumyaṃ sarvatra sampūjyam ||*
> 
> वैदिकं ग्रामादिषु वास्त्वङ्ग-आलये मध्ये पश्चिमे ब्राह्मणानां गृहेषु वा अर्चयेत्।
> *vaidikaṃ grāmādiṣu vāstvaṅga-ālaye madhye paścime brāhmaṇānāṃ gṛheṣu vā arcayet ||*
> 
> आत्रेयम् अवास्त्वङ्ग-आलये पर्वत-अरण्य-आदिषु च अर्चयेत् ॥
> *ātreyam avāstvaṅga-ālaye parvata-araṇya-ādiṣu ca arcayet ||*

#### English Translation
*   **Two Vaishnava Schools:** Vaishnava worship is of two kinds: *Vaikhānasa* and *Pancharatra*.
    1.  **Vaikhānasa:** It is Vedic (*vaidikaṃ*). When performed by Vedic practitioners (*vaidikair arcitam*), it yields both worldly prosperity and other-worldly liberation (*aihika-āmuṣmika-phala-pradam*).
    2.  **Pancharatra:** It is non-Vedic (*avaidikaṃ*). When performed by initiated practitioners (*dīxitair arcitam*), it yields other-worldly fruits, and its gentle/peaceful form (*saumyaṃ*) is worshipped everywhere.

*   **Geographical Layout:** One should perform the Vedic worship in villages, in temples situated within the residential layout, in the center, in the west, or in the houses of Brahmins. The *Atreya* worship should be performed in temples located outside the residential layout, on mountains, in forests, etc.

### Section 3: Mixing of Vaishnava Sub-systems

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> सौम्येऽस्मिन् विधौ पाञ्चरात्रेण आत्रेयेण सङ्करे सति, राज-राष्ट्र-विनाशाय भवति। तद्दोष-शमनार्थम् अब्जाग्नौ महाशान्तिं हुत्वा, अष्टोत्तरशतकलशैः देवं संस्नाप्य, वैष्णवान् सम्पूज्य, ब्राह्मणान् भोजयित्वा, पूर्वोक्तेन विधिना कारयेत्।
> *saumye'smin vidhou pāñcarātreṇa ātreyeṇa saṅkare sati, rāja-rāṣṭra-vināśāya bhavati | taddoṣa-śamanārtham abjāgnou mahāśāntiṃ hutvā, aṣṭottaraśatakaśaśair devaṃ saṃsnāpya, vaiṣṇavān sampūjya, brāhmaṇān bhojayitvā, pūrvoktena vidhinā kārayet ||*
> 
> पाञ्चरात्रविधानेन प्रतिष्ठाप्य, वर्तमाने अर्चने हीने सति, सौम्यस्य (वैखानसस्य) प्रवेशादि-कर्म कर्तव्यं, तत् राज-राष्ट्र-समृद्धिकरं भवति। कर्षणादिभिः संस्कारैः संस्कृत्य प्रतिष्ठां कारयेत्। ततःप्रभृति तेनैव सर्वं समाचरेद् इति विज्ञायते ॥
> *pāñcarātravidhānena pratiṣṭhāpya, vartamāne arcane hīne sati, saumyasya (vaikhānasasya) praveśādi-karma kartavyaṃ, tat rāja-rāṣṭra-samṛddhikaraṃ bhavati | karṣaṇādibhiḥ saṃskāraiḥ saṃskṛtya pratiṣṭhāṃ kārayet | tataḥprabhṛti tenaiva sarvaṃ samācared iti vijñāyate ||*

#### English Translation
*   **Mixing Sub-systems:** In this peaceful/Vaikhānasa system (*saumye'smin vidhou*), if a mixture with the Pancharatra or Atreya systems occurs (*pāñcarātreṇa ātreyeṇa saṅkare sati*), it leads to the destruction of the king and the state.

*   **Expiation:** To pacify that defect (*taddoṣa-schamanārtham*), one should perform the grand pacification sacrifice (*mahā-śāntiṃ*) in the lotus-fire (*abjāgnou*), bathe the deity with one hundred and eight water pots (*aṣṭottara-śata-kalaśaiḥ*), worship the Vaishnavas, feed the Brahmins, and perform the rites according to the previous rules.

*   **Conversion to Vaikhānasa:** If a temple was originally consecrated according to Pancharatra rules (*pāñcarātra-vidhānena pratiṣṭhāpya*), and the daily worship has ceased or been neglected (*vartamāne arcane hīne sati*), one should perform the purificatory re-entry rites according to the peaceful/Vaikhānasa system. That brings prosperity to the king and the state. Having refined it with rituals starting with ploughing (*karṣaṇādibhiḥ saṃskāraiḥ*), one should perform the consecration. From then onwards, one should conduct everything according to that system alone (*tataḥ-prabhṛti tenaiva sarvaṃ samācaret*).

### Section 4: Expiation for Unspecified Defects

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **अनुक्तदोष-प्रायश्चित्तम्**
> अथ अनुक्तदोष-प्रायश्चित्तं वक्ष्ये —
> अत्र अनुक्तेषु दोषेषु सर्वेषु पौण्डरीकाग्नौ वैष्णवं शतकृत्वो, व्याहृतीः चतुष्कृत्वः, अष्टाक्षरम् अष्टकृत्वो, द्वादशाक्षरं द्वादशकृत्वो हुत्वा, विष्णुसूक्तं, पुरुषसूक्तम्, एकाक्षरादिसूक्तं, श्रीसूक्तं, महीसूक्तं च हुत्वा, देवं संस्नाप्य अभ्यर्च्य हविर्निवेदयेत्।
> ***anuktadoṣa-prāyaścittam***
> *atha anuktadoṣa-prāyaścittaṃ vakṣye —*
> *atra anukteṣu doṣeṣu sarveṣu pauṇḍarīkāgnou vaiṣṇavaṃ śatakṛtvo, vyāhṛtīḥ catuṣkṛtvaḥ, aṣṭākṣaram aṣṭakṛtvo, dvādaśākṣaraṃ dvādaśakṛtvo hutvā, viṣṇusūktaṃ, puruṣasūktam, ekāxarādisūktaṃ, śrīsūktaṃ, mahīsūktaṃ ca hutvā, devaṃ saṃsnāpya abhyarcya havir nivedayet ||*
> 
> तद् तद्दोष-शमनं सर्व-शान्तिकरं भवति ॥
> *tad taddoṣa-śamanaṃ sarva-śāntikaraṃ bhavati ||*

#### English Translation
*   **Unspecified Minor Defects:** Now, I shall declare the expiations for unspecified defects (*anukta-doṣa-prāyaścittaṃ vakṣye*). For all minor errors not explicitly mentioned here:

*   **Fire Offerings:** One should offer in the Paundarika fire (*pauṇḍarīkāgnou*) the Vaiṣṇava mantra one hundred times (*śatakṛtvaḥ*), the Vyahrti mantras four times (*catuṣkṛtvaḥ*), the eight-syllable mantra eight times, the twelve-syllable mantra twelve times; and offer oblations chanting the Viṣṇu-sūkta, Puruṣa-sūkta, Ekākṣara-sūkta, Śrī-sūkta, and Mahī-sūkta.

*   **Purification:** Having bathed the deity, worshipped him, and offered food, that pacifies all defects and brings peace (*sarva-śāntikaraṃ bhavati*).

### Section 5: Expiation for Major Defects

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> महादोषेषु तैः सह पारमात्मिकम् ओङ्कारम् अष्टाशीतिं च हुत्वा, अष्टोत्तरशतकलशैः देवं संस्नाप्य अभ्यर्च्य महाहविर्निवेदयेत्। एतत् सामान्य-प्रायश्चित्तं भवति। शान्ति-होम-विधानेन वैष्णवं जुहुयात्। महाशान्ति-विधाने च तैः सह पारमात्मिक-एकाक्षरादिसूक्तं, श्रीसूक्तं, महीसूक्तं च जुहुयाद् इति केचिद् इति आह मरीचिः ॥
> *mahādoṣeṣu taiḥ saha pāramātmikam oṅkāram aṣṭāśītiṃ ca hutvā, aṣṭottaraśatakalaśair devaṃ saṃsnāpya abhyarcya mahāhavir nivedayet | etat sāmānya-prāyaścittaṃ bhavati | śānti-homa-vidhānena vaiṣṇavaṃ juhuyāt | mahāśānti-vidhāne ca taiḥ saha pāramātmika-ekāxarādisūktaṃ, śrīsūktaṃ, mahīsūktaṃ ca juhuyād iti kecid iti āha marīciḥ ||*

#### English Translation
*   **Major Defects Expiation:** For severe or major defects (*mahā-doṣeṣu*), along with those offerings, one should offer the Pāramātmika and Oṅkāra mantras eighty-eight times, bathe the deity with one hundred and eight water pots (*aṣṭottara-śata-kalaśaiḥ*), worship him, and offer a large food offering (*mahā-havir nivedayet*). This constitutes the general expiation (*sāmānya-prāyaścittaṃ*).

*   **Homa Methods:** One should offer Vaishnava oblations according to the pacification fire offering rules. In the grand pacification ritual (*mahā-śānti*), some say one should offer along with those the Pāramātmika, Ekākṣara, Śrī-sūkta, and Mahī-sūkta hymns, so declares Sage Marīci.

## Glossary of Key Terms

1.  **Tantra-saṅkara (तन्त्रसङ्कर):** The mixing, exchange, or contamination of different Agamic systems of worship (e.g. Vaikhānasa and Pancharatra).
2.  **Vaikhānasa (वैखानस):** The Vedic school of Vaishnavism, representing the Saumya (gentle) and orthodox path of worship.
3.  **Pāñcarātra (पाञ्चरात्र):** The non-Vedic/Tantric school of Vaishnavism, requiring special initiations (*dīkṣā*).
4.  **Ātreya (आत्रेय):** A specific sub-system of installation and worship associated with Sage Atri, typically performed in isolated settings.
5.  **Anukta-doṣa (अनुक्तदोष):** Lapses or defects during temple rituals that are not explicitly detailed in the scriptures.
6.  **Sāmānya-prāyaścitta (सामान्यप्रायश्चित्त):** The general or standard expiatory ritual performed to clear miscellaneous errors.
7.  **Pāramātmika-sūkta (पारमात्मिकसूक्त):** The Vedic hymn dedicated to the Supreme Soul (Paramatman).
8.  **Mahā-havis (महाहविः):** A grand food offering made to the deity at the conclusion of major expiatory rituals.
9.  **Abjāgni / Abja-agni (अब्जाग्नि):** The sacred fire pit named after the lotus, used for offering pacification oblations.
10. **Praveśa-karma (प्रवेशकर्म):** The ritual of reclaiming and purifying a temple site that was previously under another system.
