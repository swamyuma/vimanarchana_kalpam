# Vimanarchana Kalpa of Sage Marīci (Vaikhānasa Āgama)
## Chapter 17: Dāru-Saṅgrahaṇa-Vidhiḥ (Rules for Collecting Wood for Deity Icons)

This document contains the Sanskrit text, IAST transliteration, and English translation of the 17th Chapter (*Saptadaśaḥ Paṭalaḥ*) of the **Vimānārcanākalpa** (attributed to Sage Marīci, representing the Vaikhānasa school of Vaishnavism).

This chapter outlines the rules for selecting appropriate trees, wood classifications based on gender, the felling rituals including forest omens, the measurements and carving of the wooden armature elements (*śūla* and *daṇḍa*) for Viṣṇu and the consort Goddesses, and the symbolic correspondence of the seven layers of the deity icon to the seven physiological elements of the human body.

---

### Section 1: Selection of Trees and Gender Classification (Vṛkṣa-bheda-grahaṇa-vidhiḥ)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> अथ बेरार्थं दारु-संग्रहणं वक्ष्ये -
> खदिर-असन-तिल-शमी-चन्दन-चम्पक-मधूक-जाति-वृक्षाः च अन्ये स्निग्धाः साराः शुभवृक्षाः च; सस्यक्षेत्र-समीप-उत्पन्नाः, पद्म-उत्पल-कुमुद्वत्-तटाक-समीप-जाताः, याज्ञिकाः द्रुमाः, तुलसी-वृक्ष-कुश-पुष्प-समीप-जाताः च वृक्षाः संग्राह्याः।
> ***atha saptadaśaḥ paṭalaḥ***
> ***vṛkṣabhedagrahaṇavidhiḥ***
> 
> पुष्प-फल-विस्तीर्णाः (अत्यन्त-पुष्पिताः अपि), कृमि-दष्टाः, जन्तु-युताः, अग्नि-दग्धाः, वात-आहताः, जीर्णाः, कोटर-युक्ताः, त्वचा-हीनाः, भिन्नाः, अनेक-पक्षि-निवासाः, वल्मीक-सङ्कटाः, सर्प-वासाः, देवालयस्थाः, श्मशान-चण्डालवास-समीपस्थाः, अनुक्त-स्थानस्थाः च वृक्षाः अग्राह्याः।
> *atha berārthaṃ dārusangrahaṇaṃ vakṣye -*
> *khadira-asana-tila-śamī-candana-campaka-madhūka-jāti-vṛkṣāḥ ca anye snigdhāḥ sārāḥ śubhavṛkṣāḥ ca; sasyakṣetra-samīpa-utpannāḥ, padma-utpala-kumudvat-taṭāka-samīpa-jātāḥ, yājñikāḥ drumāḥ, tulasī-vṛkṣa-kuśa-puṣpa-samīpa-jātāḥ ca vṛkṣāḥ saṅgrāhyāḥ.*
>

> *   आमूलाग्र-मार्जव-युतं, सुवृत्तं, शाखा-अनेक-युक्तं - **पुंवृक्षम्**।
> *   स्थूल-मूलं, कृश-अग्रं - **स्त्रीवृक्षम्**।
> *   स्थूल-अग्रं, कृश-मूलं - **नपुंसक-वृक्षम्**। (तत्) न गृह्णीयात्।
> *puṣpa-fal-vistīrṇāḥ, kṛmi-daṣṭāḥ, jantu-yutāḥ, agni-dagdhāḥ, vāta-āhatāḥ, jīrṇāḥ, koṭara-yuktāḥ, tvacā-hīnāḥ, bhinnāḥ, aneka-pakṣi-nivāsāḥ, valmīka-saṅkaṭāḥ, sarpa-vāsāḥ, devālayasthāḥ, śmaśāna-caṇḍālavāsa-samīpasthāḥ, anukta-sthānasthāḥ ca vṛkṣāḥ agrāhyāḥ.*
> 
> पुंवृक्षेण पुंबेरं, स्त्रीवृक्षेण स्त्रीबेरं कारयेत्।
> *vṛkṣāḥ trividhāḥ - puṃ-strī-napuṃsakāḥ iti.*
> **   āmūlāgra-mārjava-yutaṃ, suvṛttaṃ, śākhā-aneka-yutaṃ - **puṃvṛkṣam**.*

#### English Translation
*   **Rules for Selecting Wood (*Vṛkṣa-bheda-grahaṇa-vidhiḥ*):**
    *   Now, I shall describe the collection of wood (*dāru-saṅgrahaṇa*) for carving deity icons.
    *   **Approved Trees (*Saṅgrāhya-vṛkṣāḥ*):** Collect wood from trees such as Khadira (Acacia catechu), Asana (Bridelia retusa), Tila (Sesamum-like tree), Śamī (Prosopis cineraria), Candana (Sandalwood), Campaka (Michelia campaca), Madhūka (Madhuca longifolia), Jāti (Jasmine tree), or other glossy, strong-hearted, benefic trees. Trees growing near agricultural fields, near ponds filled with lotuses and lilies, sacrificial trees (*yājñikāḥ drumāḥ*), and those growing near sacred Basil (*tulasī*) plants or holy Kuśa grass are highly acceptable.
    *   **Prohibited Trees (*Agrāhya-vṛkṣāḥ*):** Avoid trees that are overly overgrown with flowers and fruits, worm-eaten, infested with insects, burnt by fire, damaged by wind, decayed with age, hollowed (*kotara*), stripped of bark, split, serving as nests for numerous birds, surrounded by anthills, nesting snakes, located inside temple compounds, or growing near cremation grounds or outcastes' settlements, as well as those growing in other unprescribed locations.
    *   **Gender of Trees:** Trees are of three types:
        1.  **Masculine Tree (*Puṃvṛkṣam*):** Straight from bottom to top, perfectly round, and having many branches. Use masculine wood to carve male deity icons.
        2.  **Feminine Tree (*Strīvṛkṣam*):** Thick at the base and tapering at the top. Use feminine wood to carve female deity icons.
        3.  **Neuter Tree (*Napuṃsakavṛkṣam*):** Thick at the top and thin at the base. Do not collect neuter wood for making deity icons.

### Section 2: Forest Journey, Auspicious Omens, and Felling Rites (Darucchedana-prakāraḥ)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> पूर्वपक्षे कर्तुः अनुकूल-नक्षत्रे तदर्थम् आचार्यः यजमानश्च साधनैः होम-सम्भ्रारैः शिल्पिभिः सार्धं प्राङ्मुखः उदङ्मुखः वा वनं गत्वा निमित्तानि संलक्षयेत्।
> *pūrvapakṣe kartuḥ anukūla-nakṣatre tadarthaṃ ācāryaḥ yajamānaś ca sādhanaiḥ homasambhāraiḥ śilpibhiḥ sārdhaṃ prāṅmukhaḥ udaṅmukho vā vanaṃ gatvā nimittāni saṃlakṣayet.*
> 
> **शकुन-निर्णयः -**
> क्रौञ्चः, कृकवायसः (क्रिवायसः), पिङ्गलाक्षः (पिंगळाऽक्षः), चकोरः, कपोतः, हंसः, कृकलास-आदयो दक्षिणगाः शुभाः अभ्युदयकराश्च। चाषः, श्येनः, टिट्टिभः च वामगाः सिद्धिदाः। गृहगौळी, कौशिकः, क्रोष्टुः, पिङ्गलाक्षः, वनकुक्कुट-आद्याः वामतः सर्वे सिद्धिप्रदाः।
> ***śakunaniṇayaḥ -***
> *krauñcaḥ, kṛkavāyasaḥ, piṅgalākṣaḥ, cakoraḥ, kapotaḥ, haṃsaḥ, kṛkalāsa-ādayo dakṣiṇagāḥ śubhāḥ abhyudayakarāś ca. cāṣaḥ, śyenaḥ, tiṭṭibhaḥ ca vāmagāḥ siddhidāḥ. gṛhagauḷī, kauśikaḥ, kroṣṭuḥ, piṅgalākṣaḥ, vanakukkuta-ādyāḥ vāmataḥ sarve siddhipradāḥ.*
> 
> प्रभा, प्रिया वधूः (प्रभामिनी वधूः), ध्वजः, छत्रं, पूर्णकुम्भः, शुक्लवसनं, पुष्पम्, गजः, वृषः, गोमिथुनं, दधि, पयः, रुधिरं, मांसं, मधुभाण्डं, गर्भिणी, सुरा, वेश्या च यदि दृश्यन्ते, तत् शुभकरं भवति। इत्येवं शिलाग्रहण-काले अपि ज्ञेयम्।
> *prabhā, priyā vadhūḥ, dhvajaḥ, chatraṃ, pūrṇakumbhaḥ, śuklavasanaṃ, puṣpam, gajaḥ, vṛṣaḥ, gomithunaṃ, dadhi, payaḥ, rudhiraṃ, māṃsaṃ, madhubhāṇḍaṃ, garbhiṇī, surā, veśyā ca yadi dṛśyante, tat śubhakaraṃ bhavati. ityevaṃ śilāgrahaṇa-kāle api jñeyam.*
> 
> **वृक्ष-च्छेदन-विधिः -**
> शुभे मुहूर्ते तं वृक्षम् आसाद्य, परितः तृण-गुल्म-लतादीन् संशोध्य, अस्मिन् देशे "वसन्ते न व्यथते" (वसंत उद्धसते) इति उक्त्वा, शान्तिं वाचयित्वा; वृक्षस्य उत्तर-पार्श्वे वास्तुहोमं हुत्वा, वृक्षमूले पर्यग्निकरणं कृत्वा, पञ्चगव्यैः समभ्युक्ष्य, पुण्याहं वाचयित्वा;
> ***vṛkṣacchedanavidhiḥ -***
> *śubhe muhūrte taṃ vṛkṣam āsādya, paritaḥ tṛṇa-gulma-latādīn saṃśodhya, asmin deśe "vasante na vyathate" iti uktvā, śāntiṃ vācayitvā; vṛkṣasya uttara-pārśve vāstuhomaṃ hutvā, vṛkṣamūle paryagnikaraṇaṃ kṛtvā, pañcagavyaiḥ samabhyukṣya, puṇyāhaṃ vācayitvā;*
> 
> चरुं पक्त्वा तिल-चूर्ण-सक्तु-मुद्ग-निष्पाव-युतं - वृक्षमूले "वनराजाय" इति, परितः प्रागादि-ईशानान्तं (प्रागादीशनान्तं) "वनस्पतिभ्यो वनदेवताभ्यो भूतेभ्यो यक्षेभ्यः पिशाचेभ्यो नागेभ्यो विद्याधरेभ्यो ह्यष्टादश-गणेभ्यः" च इति पूर्वम् उदकं पुष्पबलिमुदकं च दद्यात्। "अस्तु तृप्तिः" इति उक्त्वा आचार्यं संपूजयेत्।
> *caruṃ paktvā tila-cūrṇa-saktu-mudga-niṣpāva-yutaṃ - vṛkṣamūle "vanarājāya" iti, paritaḥ prāgādi-īśānāntaṃ "vanaspatibhyo vanadevatābhyo bhūtebhyo yakṣebhyo piśācebyo nāgebhyo vidyādharebyo hyaṣṭādaśagaṇebhyaś ca" iti pūrvam udakaṃ puṣpabalim udakaṃ ca dadyāt. "astu tṛptiḥ" iti uktvā ācāryaṃ saṃpūjayet.*
> 
> आचार्यः प्राङ्मुखः उदङ्मुखो वा "इदं विष्णुः" इति हस्ताभ्यां द्रुमं सङ्गृह्य, श्री-भूमि-आदीनां तत्तन्-मन्त्रेण दारुं सङ्गृह्य; परशुम् आदाय विष्णुगायत्रीं, "सोमं राजानम्" इति च जपन्, प्रागादि-चतुर्दिशं छित्त्वा; पश्चात् तक्षाणम् (वर्धकिम्) आहूय अभिपूज्य, सर्वं छेदयेत्।
> *ācāryaḥ prāṅmukhaḥ udaṅmukho vā "idaṃ viṣṇuḥ" iti hastābhyāṃ drumaṃ saṅgṛhya, śrī-bhūmi-ādīnāṃ tattan-mantreṇa dāruṃ saṅgṛhya; paraśum ādāya viṣṇugāyatrīṃ, "somaṃ rājānam" iti ca japan, prāgādi-caturdiśaṃ chittvā; paścāt takṣāṇam āhūya abhipūjya, sarvaṃ chedayet.*
> 
> महादिक्षु पतनं श्रेष्ठं, विदिक्षु पतनं मध्यमं, अन्यासु कुदिक्षु पतनम् अधमम्। यथोचितं छित्त्वा, ऊर्ध्व-भाग-मुखं, पार्श्वादीन् च अङ्कयेत्।
> *mahādikṣu patanaṃ śreṣṭhaṃ, vidikṣu patanaṃ madhyamaṃ, anyāsu kudikṣu patanam adhamam. yathocitaṃ chittvā, ūrdhvabhāga-mukhaṃ, pārśvādīn ca aṅkayet.*

#### English Translation
*   **The Forest Journey and Felling Rites (*Darucchedana-prakāraḥ*):**
    *   **Ritual Entry into the Forest:** During the bright fortnight (*pūrvapakṣe*), under a star favorable to the patron, the preceptor, the patron, and the sculptors go to the forest carrying ritual equipment and fire offerings. Facing East or North, they must observe the surrounding omens (*nimittāni*).
    *   **Interpretation of Omens (*Śakuna-nirṇayaḥ*):**
        *   **Auspicious Right-side Animals:** Heron (*krauñca*), crow (*kṛkavāyasa*), yellow-eyed bird (*piṅgalākṣa*), Greek partridge (*cakora*), pigeon (*kapota*), swan (*haṃsa*), and chameleon (*kṛkalāsa*) crossing to the right are benefic and bring prosperity.
        *   **Auspicious Left-side Animals:** Blue jay (*cāṣa*), hawk (*śyena*), and lapwing (*tiṭṭibha*) crossing to the left bring success. Wall lizard (*gṛhagauḷī*), owl (*kauśika*), jackal (*kroṣṭu*), yellow-eyed bird, and wild fowl crossing to the left are all heralds of success.
        *   **Auspicious Visual Sights:** Beholding a brilliant light, a beautiful bride (*prabhāminī vadhūḥ*), a flag, an umbrella, a filled water-jar (*pūrṇakumbha*), white garments, flowers, an elephant, a bull, a pair of mating cattle, curd, milk, blood, meat, a pot of honey, a pregnant woman, liquor, or a courtesan brings great fortune. Note that these same rules of omens apply when gathering stone (*śilāgrahaṇa*) as well.
    *   **Tree-Felling Ritual (*Vṛkṣacchedana-vidhiḥ*):**
        *   At an auspicious moment, approach the chosen tree, clear away grass, shrubs, and creepers, recite the protective Vedic phrase "Vasantena..." or "Vasante na...", and perform peace invocations (*śānti-vācanam*).
        *   To the North of the tree, perform the *vāstu-homa*. Carry out a fire circumambulation (*paryagni*) around the base of the tree, sprinkle it with *pañcagavya*, and perform the purificatory declaration (*puṇyāham*).
        *   Cook sacrificial rice (*caru*) mixed with sesame powder, barley meal, green gram, and flat beans. At the base of the tree, present an offering saying "To the King of the Forest" (*vanarājāya*). Around it, from East to North-East, offer water, flowers, and food-balis to the Lord of Trees (*vanaspati*), forest deities (*vanadevatā*), bhūtas, yakṣas, piśācas, nāgas, vidyādharas, and the eighteen hosts, saying "Let there be satisfaction" (*astu tṛptiḥ*).
        *   The preceptor, facing East or North, touches the tree trunk with both hands reciting the mantra "Idaṃ viṣṇur...", and gathers the wood invoking the protective energies of Śrī, Bhūmi, and others using their specific mantras.
        *   Holding the axe (*paraśu*), recite the *Viṣṇu-Gāyatrī* and the hymn "Somaṃ rājānam..." (Soma, the King). Make cuts in the four cardinal directions beginning with the East. Then, summon the woodcutter/carpenter (*takṣan*), worship him, and let him fell the rest of the tree.
        *   Felling the tree so it falls toward the cardinal directions is excellent (*śreṣṭham*); falling toward the intermediate directions is middling (*madhyamam*); falling toward other inauspicious directions is inferior (*adhamam*). Trim the wood appropriately, and mark the upper side, the face, and the lateral sides.

### Section 3: Transportation and Layout of the Armature (Śūla-dandocchraya-vinyāsa-vidhiḥ)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> यजमानः आचार्यादिभ्यो दक्षिणां दत्त्वा, तं वृक्षं शकटे समारोप्य, नरैः वाहयित्वा, आलयम् आविश्य, कर्ममण्डपे संस्थाप्य, शोषयित्वा, प्रतिमा-विधानेन अत्वरेण कारयेत्।
> *yajamānaḥ ācāryādibhyo dakṣiṇāṃ dattvā, taṃ vṛkṣaṃ śakate samāropya, naraiḥ vāhayitvā, ālayam āviśya, karmamaṇḍape saṃsthāpya, śoṣayitvā, pratimā-vidhānena atvareṇa kārayet.*
> 
> ध्रुवबेर-उक्त-मानेषु इष्ट-मानं विनिश्चित्य, तन्-मानं चतुर्विंशति-शत-भागं (१२४ भागाः) विभज्य; एकांशं देहलब्ध-अङ्गुलं (प्रतिभा-अङ्गुलं) भवति। तद् शूलानाम् अग्रं पादं च विदित्वा, तल्लक्षणोपेतं तनुकरणं कृत्वा, शूलान् आहृत्य, ऊर्ध्वकायं च अधःकायं च समं कुर्यात्।
> *dhruvabera-ukta-māneṣu iṣṭa-mānaṃ viniścitya, tan-mānaṃ caturviṃśati-śata-bhāgaṃ vibhajya; ekāṃśaṃ dehalabdha-aṅgulaṃ bhavati. tad śūlānām agraṃ pādaṃ ca viditvā, tallakṣaṇopetaṃ tanukaraṇaṃ kṛtvā, śūlān āhṛtya, ūrdhvakāyaṃ ca adhaḥkāyaṃ ca samaṃ kuryāt.*
> 
> **वंशदण्ड-मानम् (Armature Spinal-Staff):**
> वंशदण्डस्य आयामं द्विषष्ट्यङ्गुलं (६२ अङ्गुलाः) - स्थानक-आसन-शयनानां;
> *   मेढ्रान्तं (मेढ्राऽन्तं) चतुरश्रं,
> *   नाभ्यन्तं चतुरश्रं,
> *   नाभेः हिक्कान्तम् (नाभेर्हिकान्तम्) अष्टाश्रं,
> *   हिक्कायाः (हिकाया) उष्णीषान्तं (उष्णी षांतं) वृत्तं भवति।
> अन्येषां देवानां चतुरश्रं नियुञ्जीत।
> ***vaṃśadaṇḍa-mānam -***
> *vaṃśadaṇḍasya āyāmaṃ dviṣaṣṭyaṅgulaṃ - sthānaka-āsana-śayanānāṃ;*
> **   meḍhrāntaṃ caturaśraṃ,*
> **   nābhyantaṃ caturaśraṃ,*
> **   nābheḥ hikkāntam aṣṭāśraṃ,*
> **   hikkāyāḥ uṣṇīṣāntaṃ vṛttam.*
> *anyeṣāṃ devānāṃ caturaśraṃ niyuñjīt.*
> 
> वंशदण्ड-मूल-विस्तारं - भाग-अर्ध-अङ्गुलं (सार्ध-अङ्गुलम्), अग्रभागं मूलाद् अष्टांश-ऊनं वा।

> *vaṃśadaṇḍa-mūla-vistāraṃ - bhāga-ardha-aṅgulaṃ, agrabhāgaṃ mūlād aṣṭāṃśa-ūnaṃ vā.*
> *āsanasya - aṣṭāṅgulam adhikaṃ nītvā, caturaṅgulam avagāḍha-praveśaṃ, caturaṅgulam āsanodayam bhavati.*

#### English Translation
*   **Transportation and Armature Layout (*Śūla-dandocchraya-vinyāsa-vidhiḥ*):**
    *   **Seasoning and Sculpting:** The patron distributes fees (*dakṣiṇā*) to the preceptor and others. Load the timber block onto a cart, have men transport it carefully to the temple workshops, deposit it in the sculpting pavilion (*karma-maṇḍapam*), season the wood properly, and carve the icon without haste according to traditional iconographic rules.
    *   **Determining Proportional Digits (*Dehalabdha-aṅgulam*):** Select the desired absolute height among the options for the main icon. Divide this height into 124 equal parts; one part represents a single proportional digit (*dehalabdha-aṅgulam*). Settle the dimensions of the armature frame (*śūla*) based on this unit. Make the wooden frame slender, assemble the staffs, and ensure the upper torso (*ūrdhva-kāyam*) and lower body (*adhaḥ-kāyam*) are balanced.
    *   **The Spinal Backbone Staff (*Vaṃśadaṇḍaḥ*):**
        *   The length of the main spinal shaft (*vaṃśadaṇḍa*) should be 62 digits for standing, sitting, or reclining icons.
        *   Its profile shape should be:
            *   Square (*caturaśram*) from the base up to the genitals (*meḍhrāntam*).
            *   Square up to the navel (*nābhyantam*).
            *   Octagonal (*aṣṭāśram*) from the navel up to the collarbone/throat-level (*hikkāntam*).
            *   Circular (*vṛttam*) from the collarbone up to the crown of the head (*uṣṇīṣāntam*).
        *   For other (non-Viṣṇu) deities, the spinal staff may be square throughout.
    *   **Dimensions of the Spinal Staff and Altar:**
        *   The base width of the spinal staff should be 1.5 digits, and its top width should be 1/8 less than the base.
        *   For the pedestal armature (*āsana-śūla*), extend it 8 digits beyond, insert it 4 digits deep into the base, and let the height of the seat armature be 4 digits.

#### English Translation
*   **Proportions of Armature Elements (*Śūla-pramāṇa-bhedaḥ*):**
    *   **Chest Staff (*Vakṣodaṇḍaḥ*):** The length of the chest-armature staff should be 34 digits, its width 1 digit, and its thickness half of the width.
    *   **Waist/Hip Staff (*Kaṭidaṇḍaḥ*):** The length of the waist staff should be 16 digits, its width 8 digits, and its thickness half of that. Shape a depression in the middle of its two sides. At this depression, connect the two lateral hip staffs (*pārśvadaṇḍau*) on the right and left, and fit them by creating a corresponding depression in the sides of the chest staff.
    *   **Tenons (*Śikhā*):**
        *   At the base and top of the chest staff, fashion four tenons (*śikhāḥ*), each measuring 1 digit (*kala-aṅgulam*) in length, of equal width, and half a digit thick. Use four tenons for a four-armed icon, and two tenons for a two-armed icon.
        *   For the waist staff joints, carve tenons 3 digits long, 2 digits wide, and of matching thickness, and join them to the spinal backbone staff (*vaṃśadaṇḍa*).
    *   **Thigh and Leg Staffs (*Ūru-jaṅghā-daṇḍau*):**
        *   The length of the thigh staff (*ūru-daṇḍa*) should be 27 digits (corrupted in print to 20 digits due to a font error), configured with joints at the base for tenon insertion.
        *   The knee portion (*jānu*) and calf staff (*jaṅghā*) should match the length of the thigh staff (27 digits).
        *   The foot portion (*pāda-bhāga*) should be 4 or 3 digits wide at the base. The calf staff should be 3 digits wide at the base, and 1 digit wide at the top. The sole length (*talāyāma*) should be 17 digits, with its heel measuring 3 digits and its front width measuring 4 digits.
        *   Alternatively, for a standing icon, one need not carve separate knee joints when joining the leg staffs; some authorities advise carving the entire leg armature out of a single block of wood.
    *   **Arm Staffs (*Bāhu-prakoṣṭha-daṇḍau*):**
        *   The length of the upper arm staff (*bāhudaṇḍa*) should be 27 digits. Its base should match the thickness of the top of the spinal staff, and its end should be 1/8 less. Joint it to the top of the spinal staff and secure it tightly with copper bands/plates (*tāmrapaṭṭena*). Some advise carving the arm staffs and the top of the spine from a single block of wood.
        *   The length of the forearm staff (*prakoṣṭhadaṇḍa*) should be 21 digits. Its joint at the upper arm base should measure 3 digits, tapering to 2 digits at the end. The forearm base should be 2.5 digits wide, tapering to 1.5 digits at the wrist. Join all other secondary limbs in their respective locations according to anatomical proportions.

#### English Translation
*   **Assembly Rules, Consecration, and Metaphorical Layers (*Darubera-saṃskāra-ṣaḍādhāra-vidhiḥ*):**
    *   **Joint Assembly:** If the arms or legs of the deity are depicted in an outstretched position (*prasārita*), do not create separate joints at the elbows or knees. The architect who knows the scriptures (*śāstrajñaiḥ śilpibhiḥ*) should design this armature frame with precision.
    *   **Armature of the Consort Goddesses (*Devyoḥ Śūla-pramāṇam*):**
        *   The armature frame of the Goddesses (Śrīdevī and Bhūdevī) should be square in cross-section and slightly curved.
        *   The dimensions of the Goddess's spinal staff (*vaṃśadaṇḍa*) should be 60 digits in length, with a base width of 1.25 digits, and its top width reduced by 1/8.
        *   Its chest staff should be 25 digits in length, 6 digits wide, and half that in thickness.
        *   Its waist staff should be 19.5 digits (20 digits less half a barley-corn) in length, 1 digit wide, and half that in thickness.
        *   For the thigh staff, its base width should be reduced by 1/4, and the calf staff reduced by 1/3, with their end widths reduced by 1/8.
        *   All other proportions are equal to the main deity's armature, except that the Goddesses should not be fitted with lateral side-staffs (*pārśvadaṇḍam*).
    *   **Visual Alignment:** Carve a straight, unbent armature frame (*avakra*) for a deity with a straight posture (*ṛjukāyasya* - samabhaṅga). For a deity with a bent posture (*vakratanoḥ* - abhaṅga, dvibhaṅga, tribhaṅga), carve a correspondingly bent armature. Any detail not specified here should be executed according to standard architectural treatises (*śilpaśāstra*).
    *   **Sanctum Placement:** As described previously, divide the inner sanctum floor into 256 grid squares, and install the standing, sitting, or reclining main icon in the designated zones.
    *   **The Seven Metaphorical Metamorphic Layers (*Sapta-saṃskāraḥ*):** The wooden icon must be constructed with seven distinct structural layers, representing the components of a human body:
        1.  **Asthi (Bone):** Represented by the wooden armature frame (*śūlam*).
        2.  **Medas (Fat/Marrow):** Represented by the *Aṣṭabandha* cement sealant (*aṣṭabandhaḥ*).
        3.  **Sirā (Veins):** Represented by the coconut-fiber binding ropes (*rajjuḥ*).
        4.  **Māṃsa (Flesh):** Represented by the consecrated clay layers (*mṛt*).
        5.  **Śoṇita (Blood):** Represented by the lime-gravel plaster (*ghaṭīśarkarā*).
        6.  **Tvac (Skin):** Represented by the cotton cloth wrap (*paṭaḥ*).
        7.  **Jīva (Soul/Life):** Represented by the colors and paint layers (*varṇaḥ*).
    *   Knowing this, constructing the icon with all these seven layers brings all forms of prosperity (*sarva-sampat-karam*). If the icon is constructed omitting even one of these layers, it becomes an inauspicious, black-magic act (*ābhicārikam*).
    *   Thus concludes the Seventeenth Chapter (*Saptadaśaḥ Paṭalaḥ*) of the *Vimānārcanākalpa*, titled "Rules for Collecting Wood".
