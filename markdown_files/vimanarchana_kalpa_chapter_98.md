# Vimanarchana Kalpa of Sage Marīci (Vaikhānasa Āgama)
## Chapter 98: Dhāraṇā-vidhiḥ (Rules on Concentration)

This document contains the Sanskrit text, IAST transliteration, and English translation of the 98th Chapter (*Aṣṭanavatitamaḥ Paṭalaḥ*) of the **Vimānārcanākalpa** (attributed to Sage Marīci, representing the Vaikhānasa school of Vaishnavism).

This chapter expounds *dhāraṇā* (concentration), the sixth limb of the eightfold yoga. It defines *dhāraṇā* under eight specific forms: 1) stabilizing the mind in the Self, 2) concentration on the inner space of the heart, 3) concentration on the outer space, 4) concentration on Earth (*pṛthivī*) at the feet, 5) concentration on Water (*ap*) at the knees, 6) concentration on Fire (*tējas*) at the anus, 7) concentration on Wind (*vāyu*) at the heart, and 8) concentration on Space (*ākāśa*) at the eyebrows. It details the seed-syllables (*bīja-mantra*), boundaries, and corresponding forms of Viṣṇu (Aniruddha, Acyuta, Satya, Puruṣa, and Viṣṇu) for each elemental concentration, culminating in deep concentration on the supreme bliss-form (*paramānanda-vigraha*) of Nārāyaṇa.

### Section 1: The Eight Types of Concentration and the Five Elements

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **अथ अष्टनवतितमः पटलः**
> **धारणाविधिः**
> ***atha aṣṭanavatitamaḥ paṭalaḥ***
> ***dhāraṇāvidhiḥ***
> 
> अथातो धारणाः अष्टधा भवन्ति । तेषु --
> १. आत्मनि यम-नियमादि-गुणयुक्त-मनसः स्थितिः,
> २. हृदन्तर-आकाशे धारणा,
> ३. बाह्याकाशे च धारणा,
> ४-८. पृथिव्यादि-महाभूतेषु पञ्चसु देवानां पञ्च धारणाः,
> ९. हृत्पद्ममध्ये परमात्मानन्द-विग्रह-धारणा इति ।
> *athātō dhāraṇāḥ aṣṭadhā bhavanti | tēṣu --*
> *1. ātmani yama-niyamādi-guṇayukta-manasaḥ sthitiḥ,*
> *2. hṛdantara-ākāśē dhāraṇā,*
> *3. bāhyākāśē ca dhāraṇā,*
> *4-8. pṛthivyādi-mahābhūtēṣu pañcasu dēvānāṃ pañca dhāraṇāḥ,*
> *9. hṛtpadmamadhyē paramātmananda-vigraha-dhāraṇā iti ||*
> 
> **पञ्चभूतधारणाः**
> *   **पृथिवीधारणा:** पादादि-जानुपर्यन्तं पृथिवीस्थानं, तत्र लकार-संयुक्तं वायुम् आरोप्य, अनिरुद्धमूर्तिं ध्यात्वा धारयेत् ।
> *   **अब्धारणा:** जानोः पायुपर्यन्तम् अप्-स्थानं (अब्-स्थानं), तत्र वकार-संयुक्तं वायुम् आरोप्य, अच्युतमूर्तिं ध्यात्वा धारयेत् ।
> *   **अग्निधारणा:** पायोः हृदवान्तम् अग्निस्थानं, तत्र रकार-संयुक्तं (रेफबीज-युक्तं) वायुम् आरोप्य, सत्यमूर्तिं ध्यात्वा धारयेत् ।
> ***pañcabhūtadhāraṇāḥ***
> **   **pṛthivīdhāraṇā:** pādādi-jānuparyantaṃ pṛthivīsthānaṃ, tatra lakāra-saṃyuktaṃ vāyum ārōpya, aniruddhamūrtiṃ dhyātvā dhārayēt |*
> **   **abdhāraṇā:** jānōḥ pāyuparyantam ap-sthānaṃ, tatra vakāra-saṃyuktaṃ vāyum ārōpya, acyutamūrtiṃ dhyātvā dhārayēt |*
> **   **agnidhāraṇā:** pāyōḥ hṛdayāntaṃ agnisthānaṃ, tatra rakāra-saṃyuktaṃ vāyum ārōpya, satyamūrtiṃ dhyātvā dhārayēt |*

#### English Translation
*   **The Eight Forms of Concentration:** Concentration (*dhāraṇā*) is of eight kinds (*aṣṭadhā bhavanti*). They are:
    1.  **Mental Stability:** Stabilizing the mind, which is endowed with virtues like yama and niyama, in the Self (*ātmani yama-niyamādi-guṇayukta-manasaḥ sthitiḥ*).
    2.  **Inner Space:** Concentration on the inner space of the heart (*hṛdantara-ākāśē dhāraṇā*).
    3.  **Outer Space:** Concentration on the outer space (*bāhyākāśē ca dhāraṇā*).
    4-8. **Elemental Concentrations:** The five concentrations on the deities in the five gross elements beginning with Earth (*pṛthivyādi-mahābhūtēṣu pañcasu dēvānāṃ pañca dhāraṇāḥ*).
    9.  **The Form of Bliss:** Concentration on the supreme bliss-form of the Lord in the heart-lotus (*hṛtpadmamadhyē paramātmananda-vigraha-dhāraṇā iti*).

*   **Elemental Concentrations (Earth, Water, Fire):**
    *   **Earth:** From the feet up to the knees is the region of Earth (*pādādi-jānuparyantaṃ pṛthivīsthānaṃ*). Directing the vital wind associated with the seed-syllable *Lam* there (*tatra lakāra-saṃyuktaṃ vāyum ārōpya*), and meditating on the form of Aniruddha, one should concentrate (*aniruddhamūrtiṃ dhyātvā dhārayēt*).
    *   **Water:** From the knees to the anus is the region of Water (*jānōḥ pāyuparyantam ap-sthānaṃ*). Directing the vital wind associated with the seed-syllable *Vam* there (*tatra vakāra-saṃyuktaṃ vāyum ārōpya*), and meditating on the form of Acyuta, one should concentrate (*acyutamūrtiṃ dhyātvā dhārayēt*).
    *   **Fire:** From the anus to the heart is the region of Fire (*pāyōḥ hṛdayāntaṃ agnisthānaṃ*). Directing the vital wind associated with the seed-syllable *Ram* there (*tatra rakāra-saṃyuktaṃ vāyum ārōpya*), and meditating on the form of Satya, one should concentrate (*satyamūrtiṃ dhyātvā dhārayēt*).

### Section 2: Wind and Space Concentrations and the Supreme Union

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> हृदयात् भ्रूमध्यान्तं वायुस्थानं, तत्र यकार-संयुक्तं वायुम् आरोप्य, पुरुषमूर्तिं ध्यात्वा धारयेत् ।
> आभ्रूमध्यात् मूर्धान्तं व्योमस्थानं, तत्र हकार-संयुक्तं वायुम् आरोप्य, विष्णुमूर्तिं ध्यात्वा धारयेत् ।
> एतेषां देवानां ध्यानस्वरूपं पूर्वं नाडी-ध्यान-कथने विधिना चोक्तम् ।
> स्वे स्वे स्थाने समाकर्षणे, प्रणवस्य नादान्ते परमानन्द-विग्रहं परमात्मानं नारायणं शुद्धस्फटिकसङ्काशं ध्यात्वा धारयेत् ।
> एतां धारणां यम-नियमादि-संयुक्तः नित्यम् आचरेद् इत्याह मरीचिः ॥
> *hṛdayāt bhrūmadhyāntaṃ vāyusthānaṃ, tatra yakāra-saṃyuktaṃ vāyum ārōpya, puruṣamūrtiṃ dhyātvā dhārayēt |*
> *ābhrūmadhyāt mūrdhāntaṃ vyōmasthānaṃ, tatra hakāra-saṃyuktaṃ vāyum ārōpya, viṣṇumūrtiṃ dhyātvā dhārayēt |*
> *ētēṣāṃ dēvānāṃ dhyānasvarūpaṃ pūrvaṃ nāḍī-dhyāna-kathanē vidhinā cōktam |*
> *svē svē sthānē samākarṣaṇē, praṇavasya nādāntē paramānanda-vigrahaṃ paramātmānaṃ nārāyaṇaṃ śuddhasphaṭikasaṅkāśaṃ dhyātvā dhārayēt |*
> *ētāṃ dhāraṇāṃ yama-niyamādi-saṃyuktaḥ nityam ācarēd ityāha marīciḥ ||*

#### English Translation
*   **Concentrations (Wind and Space):**
    *   **Wind:** From the heart to the eyebrow center is the region of Wind (*hṛdayāt bhrūmadhyāntaṃ vāyusthānaṃ*). Directing the vital wind associated with the seed-syllable *Yam* there (*tatra yakāra-saṃyuktaṃ vāyum ārōpya*), and meditating on the form of Puruṣa, one should concentrate (*puruṣamūrtiṃ dhyātvā dhārayēt*).
    *   **Space:** From the eyebrow center to the crown of the head is the region of Space (*ābhrūmadhyāt mūrdhāntaṃ vyōmasthānaṃ*). Directing the vital wind associated with the seed-syllable *Ham* there (*tatra hakāra-saṃyuktaṃ vāyum ārōpya*), and meditating on the form of Viṣṇu, one should concentrate (*viṣṇumūrtiṃ dhyātvā dhārayēt*).

*   **Iconography and Final Concentration:** The iconography of these deities has been declared previously in the rules for meditating on the channels (*ētēṣāṃ dēvānāṃ dhyānasvarūpaṃ pūrvaṃ nāḍī-dhyāna-kathanē vidhinā cōktam*). Drawing the breath to each respective center (*svē svē sthānē samākarṣaṇē*), at the resonance-end of the *praṇava* (*praṇavasya nādāntē*), one should meditate on the supreme bliss-form of the Supreme Soul Nārāyaṇa, who resembles pure crystal, and hold concentration (*paramānanda-vigrahaṃ paramātmānaṃ nārāyaṇaṃ śuddhasphaṭikasaṅkāśaṃ dhyātvā dhārayēt*).

#### Colophon
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> इति श्रीवैखानसे मरीचिप्रोक्ते विमानार्चनाकल्पे धारणाविधिर्नाम अष्टनवतितमः पटलः ॥९८॥

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> iti śrīvaikhānasē marīciprōktē vimānārcanākalpē dhāraṇāvidhirnāma aṣṭanavatitamaḥ paṭalaḥ ||98||

**Translation (English):**
*   **Daily Practice:** A yogi endowed with yama, niyama, etc., should practice this concentration daily — thus declared Sage Marīci (*ētāṃ dhāraṇāṃ yama-niyamādi-saṃyuktaḥ nityam ācarēd ityāha marīciḥ*).

### Glossary of Key Terms
*   **Dhāraṇā (धारणा):** Concentration; the binding of the mind's attention to a single region, element, or deity, serving as the sixth limb of yoga.
*   **Bīja-mantra (बीजमन्त्र):** Seed-syllables representing the elemental energies: *Lam* (Earth), *Vam* (Water), *Ram* (Fire), *Yam* (Wind), and *Ham* (Space).
*   **Aniruddha-Acyuta-Satya-Puruṣa-Viṣṇu (अनिरुद्ध-अच्युत-सत्य-पुरुष-विष्णु):** The five aspects of Godhead corresponding to the five elemental centers in the body.
*   **Nādānta (नादान्त):** The subtle resonance at the end of the sound of *Om* (Pranava), where the mind merges into silent consciousness.
*   **Śuddhasphaṭika-saṅkāśa (शुद्धस्फटिकसङ्काश):** Resembling pure crystal; a description of the radiant, unblemished light of Nārāyaṇa in deep concentration.
