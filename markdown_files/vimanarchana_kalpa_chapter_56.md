# Chapter 56: Consecration and Iconography of Varāha (Varāha-vidhi-pratiṣṭhā)
### Section 1: Liturgical Rules for the Dimensions, Iconography, and Consecration of the Varāha Incarnations

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **अथ षट्पञ्चाशत्तमः पटलः**
> **वराह-विधि-प्रतिष्ठा -**
> ***atha ṣaṭpañcāśattamaḥ paṭalaḥ***
> ***varāha-vidhi-pratiṣṭhā -***
> 
> अथ वराहः त्रिविधो भवति — आदिवराहः, प्रलयवराहः, यज्ञवराहः इति।
> *atha varāhaḥ trivido bhavati — ādivarāhaḥ, pralayavarāhaḥ, yajñavarāhaḥ iti.*
> 
> एतेषां मुखं वराहस्य इव; मूर्धादि-पाद-पर्यन्तं मध्यम-दश-तालेन नयेत्।
> *eteṣāṃ muṣaṃ varāhasya iva; mūrdhādi-pāda-paryantaṃ madhyama-daśa-tālena nayet.*
> 
> मूर्ध्नः केशान्तं चतुर्मात्रं, तस्माद् दृगन्तं भागम् — उष्णीषं (मुद्रितपाठे उष्णातं) रुद्रमात्रं, कण्ठं कलायुतं (द्विमात्रं), कण्ठाद् हृदयान्तं (मुद्रितपाठे कण्ठाद्वु-द्वयान्तं), हृदयात् नाभ्यन्तं, नाभेः मेढ्रमूलान्तं च त्र्योदशाङ्गुलम्; ऊरू त्र्योदश-द्वयं (षड्विंशत्यङ्गुलं), जानु वेदाङ्गुलं (चतुरङ्गुलं), जङ्घे ऊरु-समे (षड्विंशत्यङ्गुले), तलं भागं, तदायामं समदशाङ्गुलम्; नेत्रं त्र्यङ्गुलं, भ्रूनेत्रतत्सृक्क-भूताङ्गुलं (पञ्चाङ्गुलं), सृक्काद् वेदाङ्गुलं (चतुरङ्गुलं), हनु-नेत्रान्तरं त्रिमात्रं, नेत्रायामं द्व्यङ्गुलं, नेत्रात् श्रोत्रं समाङ्गुलं, कर्णतारं द्वियवं त्र्यङ्गुलं, द्विगुणं तदायामम्; एकाङ्गुलविस्तारं मुखम्, एकाङ्गुलं नेत्रव्यासं, नासामूलविस्तारं समाङ्गुलं, तन्मध्ये शरङ्गुलं (मुद्रितपाठे शरुङ्गुलं), नासाग्रं तालं भागाधं मात्रं, श्रोत्रं तथा एकाङ्गुलं, पुटव्यासम् (मुद्रितपाठे पुरव्यासम्) अधरं त्रिकलाङ्गुलं निर्गमं, मूलतारं भागं, तदर्धं तस्याग्रविस्तारं, दंष्ट्रायामं द्विमात्रं, कण्ठतारं नवाङ्गुलं, तथैव बाहुमूलविस्तारं मध्यम-दशतालोक्तमार्गेण कारयेत् ॥
> *mūrdhnaḥ keśāntaṃ caturmātraṃ, tasmād drgantaṃ bhāgam — uṣṇīṣaṃ rudramātraṃ, kaṇṭhaṃ kalāyutaṃ (dvimātraṃ), kaṇṭhād hrdayāntaṃ, hrdayāt nābhyantaṃ, nābheḥ meḍhramūlāntaṃ ca trayodaśāṅgulam; ūrū trayodaśa-dvyaṃ (ṣaḍviṃśatyāṅgulaṃ), jānu vedāṅgulaṃ (caturāṅgulaṃ), jaṅghe ūru-same (ṣaḍviṃśatyāṅgule), talaṃ bhāgaṃ, tadāyāmaṃ samadaśāṅgulam; netraṃ tryāṅgulaṃ, bhrūnetratatsṛkka-bhūtāṅgulaṃ (pañcāṅgulaṃ), sṛkkād vedāṅgulaṃ (caturāṅgulaṃ), hanu-netrāntaraṃ trimātraṃ, netrāyāmaṃ dvyāṅgulaṃ, netrāt śrotraṃ samāṅgula, karṇatāraṃ dviyavaṃ tryāṅgulaṃ, dviguṇaṃ tadāyāmam; ekāṅgulavistāraṃ muṣam, ekāṅgulaṃ netravyāsaṃ, nāsāmūlavistāraṃ samāṅgulaṃ, tanmedhye śarāṅgulaṃ, nāsāgraṃ tālaṃ bhāgādhhaṃ mātraṃ, śrotraṃ tathā ekāṅgulaṃ, puṭavyāsam adharaṃ trikalāṅgulaṃ nirgamaṃ, mūlatāraṃ bhāgaṃ, tadardhaṃ tasyāgravistāraṃ, daṃṣṭrāyāmaṃ dvimātraṃ, kaṇṭhatāraṃ navāṅgulaṃ, tathāiva bāhumūlavistāraṃ madhyama-daśatāloktamārgeṇa kārayet.*
> 
> **(आदिवराहस्य लक्षणम्)**
> आदिवराहं चतुर्भुजं शङ्खचक्रधरं सस्यश्यामनिभं नागेन्द्र-फणामणि-स्थापित-दक्षिणपदं रसातलाद् उत्क्रमणाया आकुञ्चित-वामपादं, तदूर्वोः (तदूरौ) महीं दधानं, दक्षिणहस्तेन देव्याः पादौ गृह्णन्तं, वामहस्तेन ताम् उपगूहन्तं, मुखेन देवीं जिघ्रन्तं कृत्वा; तां महीं प्राञ्जलीकृतहस्तां प्रसारितपदां पुष्प-अम्बरधरां श्यामाभां किञ्चित् देवं समीक्ष्य व्रीडा-हर्ष-संयुक्तां सर्वाभरणसंयुतां देवस्य स्तमान्तां नाभ्यन्तां वा पञ्चतालेन मानेन कारयेत् ॥
> ***(ādivarāhasya laxanam)***
> *ādivarāhaṃ caturbhujaṃ śaṅkhacakradharaṃ sasyāśyāmanibhaṃ nāgendra-phaṇāmaṇi-sthāpita-daxiṇapadaṃ rasātalād utkramaṇāyā ākuñcita-vāmapādaṃ, tadūrvoḥ mahīṃ dadhānaṃ, daxiṇahastena devyāḥ pādou gṛhṇantaṃ, vāmahastena tām upagūhantaṃ, muṣena devīṃ jighrantaṃ kṛtvā; tāṃ mahīṃ prāñjalīkytahastāṃ prasāritapadāṃ puṣpa-ambaradharāṃ śyāmbhāṃ kiñcit devaṃ samīṣya vrīḍā-harṣa-saṃyuktāṃ sarvābharaṇasaṃyutāṃ devasya stanāntāṃ nābhyantāṃ vā pañcatālena mānena kārayet.*
> 
> **(प्रलयवराहस्य लक्षणम्)**
> अथ वक्ष्ये प्रलयवराहं — वामपादं समाकुञ्च्य दक्षिणं प्रसार्य सिंहासने समासीनं नीलाभं चतुर्भुजं शङ्खचक्रधरम् अभय-दक्षिण-हस्तं ऊरुप्रतिष्ठित-वामहस्तं पीताम्बरधरं सर्वाभरणभूषितं कारयित्वा; तस्य दक्षिणे देवीं महीं वामपादम् आकुञ्च्य (मुद्रितपाठे पादवासमाकुञ्च्य) दक्षिणं प्रसार्य आसीनां श्यामाभां सर्वाभरणभूषितां उत्पलधर-वाम-करां (मुद्रितपाठे उमलधर-) आसने निहित-दक्षिण-करां किञ्चित् देवं समीक्ष्य विस्मय-उत्फुल्ल-लोचनां कारयेत्।
> ***(pralayavarāhasya laxanam)***
> *atha vaxye pralayavarāhaṃ — vāmapādaṃ samākuñcya daxiṇaṃ prasārya siṃhāsane samāsīnaṃ nīlābhaṃ caturbhujaṃ śaṅkhacakradharavam abhaya-daxiṇa-hastaṃ ūrupratiṣṭhita-vāmahastaṃ pītāmbaradharaṃ sarvābharaṇabhūṣitaṃ kārayitvā; tasya daxiṇe devīṃ mahīṃ vāmapādam ākuñcya daxiṇaṃ prasārya āsīnaṃ śyāmābhāṃ sarvābharaṇabhūṣitāṃ utpaladhara-vāma-karāṃ āsane nihita-daxiṇa-karāṃ kiñcit devaṃ samīṣya vismaya-utphulla-locanāṃ kārayet.*
> 
> **(यज्ञवराहस्य लक्षणम्)**
> अथ यज्ञवराहं श्वेताभं चतुर्भुजं शङ्खचक्रधरं वामपादं समाकुञ्च्य दक्षिणं प्रसार्य सिंहासने समासीनं पीताम्बरधरं सर्वाभरणभूषितं कारयेत्।
> ***(yajñavarāhasya laxanam)***
> *atha yajñavarāhaṃ śvetābhaṃ caturbhujaṃ śaṅkhacakradharaṃ vāmapādaṃ samākuñcya daxiṇaṃ prasārya siṃhāsane samāsīnaṃ pītāmbaradharaṃ sarvābharaṇabhūṣitaṃ kārayet.*
> 
> तस्य दक्षिणे देवीं श्रियं हेमाभां वामपादं समाकुञ्च्य दक्षिणं प्रसार्य आसीनां पद्मधर-वामहस्तां आसने निहित-दक्षिणहस्तां; वामपार्श्वे महीं देवीं सस्यश्यामनिभां दक्षिणपादम् आकुञ्च्य वामं प्रसार्य आसीनां उत्पलधर-दक्षिणहस्तां आसने निहित-वामहस्तां देवं किञ्चित् समीक्ष्य विस्मय-उत्फुल्ल-लोचनां कारयेत् ॥
> *tasya daxiṇe devīṃ śriyaṃ hemābhāṃ vāmapādaṃ samākuñcya daxiṇaṃ prasārya āsīnaṃ padmadhara-vāmahastāṃ āsane nihita-daxiṇahastāṃ; vāmapārśve devīṃ mahīṃ sasyāśyāmanibhāṃ daxiṇapādam ākuñcya vāmaṃ prasārya āsīnaṃ utpaladhara-daxiṇahastāṃ āsane nihita-vāmahastāṃ devaṃ kiñcit samīṣya vismaya-utphulla-locanāṃ kārayet.*
> 
> त्रयाणां वराहाणां तद्रूपं (वराहरूपं) कौतुकं च, बिम्बं चतुर्भुजं वा कारयेत् ॥
> *trayāṇāṃ varāhāṇāṃ tadrūpaṃ kautukaṃ ca, bimbaṃ caturbhujaṃ vā kārayet.*
> 
> **(एषां प्रतिष्ठा)**
> एषां प्रतिष्ठाविशेषं वक्ष्ये — पूर्ववद् यागशालां सर्वालङ्कारसंयुक्तां तन्मध्ये शय्यावेदिं चतुर्दिशं तद्-भित्ति-सम-विस्तारां (मुद्रितपाठे तद्वित्ति-), पादहीन-विस्तारां अर्धविस्तारां वा तत्तुरीयांशोत्सेधां तत्परितः पञ्चाग्नींश्च परिकल्प्य; सभ्ये प्रधानाग्नौ हौत्रशंसनं कृत्वा, 'वाराहं वरदं महीधरं वज्रदंष्ट्रिणम्' इत्यादिवराहस्य; 'गां पृथुगाम् उर्वीम्' इति मह्याः (मुद्रितपाठे मद्याः); 'प्रलयवराहं प्रलयहरं भूरिशब्दं जगत्त्रायकम्' (मुद्रितपाठे जगत्रायकम्) इति प्रलयवराहस्य; देव्याः पूर्ववत्; 'यज्ञवराहं देवमयं यज्ञेशं यज्ञवर्धनम्' इति यज्ञवराहस्य; श्रीभूम्योः पूर्ववत्।
> ***(eṣāṃ pratiṣṭhā)***
> *eṣāṃ pratiṣṭhāviśeṣaṃ vaxye — pūrvavad yāgāśālāṃ sarvālaṅkārasaṃyuktāṃ tanmadhye śayyāvediṃ caturdiśaṃ tad-bhitti-sam-vistārāṃ, pādahīn-vistārāṃ ardhavistārāṃ vā tatturīyāṃśotsedhāṃ tatparitaḥ pañcāgnīṃśca parikalpya; sabhye pradhānāgnou houtraśaṃsanaṃ kṛtvā, 'vārāhaṃ varadaṃ mahīdaraṃ vajradaṃṣṭriṇam' ityādivarāhasya; 'gāṃ pṛthugām urvīm' iti mahyāḥ; 'pralayavarāhaṃ pralayaharaṃ bhūriśabdaṃ jagattrāyakam' iti pralayavarāhasya; devyāḥ pūrvavat; 'yajñavarāhaṃ devamayaṃ yajñeśaṃ yajñavardhanam' iti yajñavarāhasya; śrībhūmyoḥ pūrvavat.*
> 
> एवम् आवाहन-जुष्टाकार-स्वाहाकारान् च कृत्वा; आदिवराहस्य प्रधानाग्नौ 'क्षमो धरः' (मुद्रितपाठे क्षमोमक, वै॰ म॰ प्र॰ ८) इति मन्त्रेण अष्टोत्तरशतं हुत्वा; प्रलयवराहस्य 'स्वयम् आदिः' (वै॰ म॰ प्र॰ ८) इति अष्टोत्तरशतं हुत्वा; यज्ञवराहस्य 'यं यज्ञैः' (वै॰ म॰ प्र॰ ८) इति मन्त्रेण अष्टोत्तरशतं जुहुयात्। एष विशेषः, अन्यत् सर्वं समानम्।
> *evam āvāhana-juṣṭākāra-svāhākārān ca kṛtvā; ādivarāhasya pradhānāgnou 'kṣamo dharaḥ' (vai. ma. pra. 8) iti mantreṇa aṣṭottaraśataṃ hutvā; pralayavarāhasya 'svayam ādiḥ' (vai. ma. pra. 8) iti aṣṭottaraśataṃ hutvā; yajñavarāhasya 'yaṃ yajñāiḥ' (vai. ma. pra. 8) iti mantreṇa aṣṭottaraśataṃ juhuyāt. eṣa viśeṣaḥ, anyat sarvaṃ samānam.*
> 
> त्रयाणां ध्रुवबेराणि तत्-तत्-मूर्तिमन्त्रैः अष्टोपचारैः अभ्यर्च्य; पश्चात् कौतुकानि तत्-तत्-नाम संयोज्य षोडशोपचारैः अभ्यर्चयेत्। स्नपन-उत्सवादीनि सर्वाणि पूर्वोक्तवत् कारयेत् ॥
> *trayāṇāṃ dhruvaberāṇi tat-tat-mūrtimantraiḥ aṣṭopacāraiḥ abhyarcya; paścāt kautukāni tat-tat-nāma saṃyojya ṣoḍaśopacāraiḥ abhyarcayet. snapanotsavādīni sarvāṇi pūrvoktavat kārayet.*

#### English Translation
*   **Three Classes of Varāha:** Now, the rules for Varāha: the Boar incarnation is of three types—Ādi-Varāha (the Primordial Boar), Pralaya-Varāha (the Dissolution Boar), and Yajña-Varāha (the Sacrificial Boar). Their face is like that of a boar, and their overall body from head to foot should be made in the *madhyama-daśatāla* (medium ten-span) system.

*   **Torso Proportions:** The distance from the crown to the hairline is four units (*mātras*); from the hairline to the eye line is nine units; the crown-ornament (*uṣṇīṣa*) is eleven units; the neck height is two units; from the neck to the heart is thirteen units; from the heart to the navel is thirteen units; and from the navel to the genitals is thirteen units. The thighs measure twenty-six units; the knees, four units; the shanks are equal to the thighs (twenty-six units); and the feet height is four units, making the total height one hundred and twenty finger-units (*aṅgulas*).

*   **Facial and Snout Proportions:** The eye length is three units, the distance from eyebrow to mouth corner is five units, and the mouth corners measure four units. The snout length is four units, and the mouth opening is ten units. The snout tip is five units. The lower lip is three units. The tusks are two units, and the neck width is nine units. The other limb dimensions should be executed in accordance with the *madhyama-daśatāla* system.

*   **Iconography of Ādi-Varāha:** Ādi-Varāha should have four arms, hold the conch and disc, and be of a dark green hue like green crops. His right foot should be firmly placed on the hood-gem of the serpent king (Śeṣa), and His left leg should be bent/lifted to rise from the netherworld (*rasātala*). He supports Goddess Earth (*Mahī*) on His chest/thigh, holding Her feet with His right hand and embracing Her with His left hand, while sniffing/kissing Her face with His snout. The Goddess Earth should have Her hands folded in supplication (*prāñjali*), Her legs extended, be dressed in flower-patterned garments, be of a dark complexion, and look slightly at the Lord with a mixture of bashfulness (*vrīḍā*) and joy (*harṣa*), adorned with all ornaments. Her height should reach the Lord's breast or navel, carved in the *pañcatāla* (five-span) measurement.

*   **Iconography of Pralaya-Varāha:** Pralaya-Varāha is depicted seated on a throne, with His left leg folded and His right leg extended. He is blue-hued, has four arms, holds the conch and disc, has His right hand offering protection (*abhaya*), and His left hand resting on His thigh. He wears yellow garments and is adorned with all ornaments. On His right, Goddess Earth (*Mahī*) is seated with Her left leg folded and Her right leg extended, dark-hued, holding a blue lotus (*utpala*) in Her left hand, with Her right hand resting on the seat, looking at the Lord with eyes wide with wonder.

*   **Iconography of Yajña-Varāha:** Yajña-Varāha is white-hued, has four arms, holds the conch and disc, is seated on a throne with His left leg folded and His right leg extended, wears yellow garments, and is adorned with all ornaments. On His right, Goddess Śrī is seated, golden-hued, holding a lotus in Her left hand, with Her right hand resting on the seat. On His left, Goddess Earth is seated, green-hued, with Her right leg folded and Her left leg extended, holding a blue lotus in Her right hand, with Her left hand resting on the seat, looking at the Lord with eyes wide with wonder. The processional icons (*kautuka-bera*) of all three Varāhas must be made in the four-armed Viṣṇu form; they must not be carved in the boar form.

*   **Consecration of the Boar Deities (*Varāha-pratiṣṭhā*):** Preparing the sacrificial hall as before with all decorations, building the bedding altar in the center, and setting up the five fires around it. Having offered the chanter's praise at the primary Sabhya fire: invoke the Lord reciting the four Boar-mantras starting with "Vārāhaṃ varadaṃ mahīdharaṃ vajradaṃṣṭriṇam" for Ādi-Varāha; "Gāṃ pṛthugām urvīm" for Goddess Earth; "Pralayavarāhaṃ pralayaharaṃ bhūriśabdaṃ jagattrāyakam" for Pralaya-Varāha; and "Yajñavarāhaṃ devamayaṃ yajñeśaṃ yajñavardhanam" for Yajña-Varāha.

*   **Concluding Offerings:** Having completed the invocation, the *juṣṭa* and *svāhā* offerings, he should offer one hundred and eight oblations with the "Kṣamo dharaḥ" mantra for Ādi-Varāha; the "Svayam ādiḥ" mantra for Pralaya-Varāha; and the "Yaṃ yajñaiḥ" mantra for Yajña-Varāha in the primary fire. All other rites are identical to Viṣṇu's consecration. Worship the stationary deities with their form-mantras and eight services, and the processional deities combining their names with the sixteen services. Perform the bathing and festivals as described before.
