# Vimanarchana Kalpa of Sage Marīci (Vaikhānasa Āgama)
## Chapter 72: Dhruva-bera-prāyaścitta-vidhiḥ (Rules of Expiation/Repairs for the Fixed Deity Icon)

This document contains the Sanskrit text, IAST transliteration, and English translation of the 72nd Chapter (*Dvisaptatitamaḥ Paṭalaḥ*) of the **Vimānārcanākalpa** (attributed to Sage Marīci, representing the Vaikhānasa school of Vaishnavism).

This chapter discusses the expiations, repair protocols (*sandhāna*), and replacement procedures for the main fixed deity icon (*dhruva-bera*) and temple structure (*vimāna*). It covers minor and major damages to stone and clay icons, the protective wooden cage (*kāṣṭha-pañjara*) used during renovations, the temporary projection of divine power (*śakti*) into a water vessel (*kumbha*) for short-term painting/repairs, and the procedure for rectifying damages in multi-deity installations (*nava-mūrty-ādi*).

---

### Section 1: General Rule on Materials and Damage to Stone Icons

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **अथ द्विसप्ततितमः पटलः**
> **ध्रुवबेर-प्रायश्चित्त-विधिः**

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **atha dvisaptatitamaḥ paṭalaḥ**
> **dhruvabera-prāyaścitta-vidhiḥ**

**Translation (English):**
*   **General Alternative:** Or, in all cases, one should make a stone icon (*śailam*), even if it is devoid of color (unpainted/uncolored, *varṇa-hīnam*), according to some teachers (*ity eke*).

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **सर्वत्र पक्षान्तरम्**
> अथवा सर्वत्र वर्णहीनं शैलमेव कारयेदित्येके ॥

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **sarvatra pakṣāntaram**
> athavā sarvatra varṇahīnaṃ śailam eva kārayet ity eke ||

**Translation (English):**
*   **Minor Damage to Stone Icons:** For a stone icon (*śaila*), if it is half-broken (*ardha-chinnasya*), or if its minor limbs and auxiliary parts (*aṅgopāṅga-pratyaṅgeṣu*) are damaged or missing (*hīneṣu*), there is no loss of its divine power (*tacchakti-hānir na*).

#### Verse / Paragraph 3
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **शैलबेरस्य अङ्गोपाङ्गहीने**
> शैलस्य अर्ध-छिन्नस्य बेरस्याङ्गोपाङ्गप्रत्यङ्गेषु हीनेषु तच्छक्तिहानिर्न। तस्माच्छक्तिं बालागारं नीत्वा तद्बेरं भूमौ पिधाय उपरि महाशान्तिम् अब्जाग्नौ हुत्वा शिलाग्रहणवद् हुत्वा तदङ्गसमुत्पत्तिं युक्त्या तत्क्षणात् कारयेत् ॥

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **śailaberasya aṅgopāṅgahīne**
> śailasya ardha-chinnasya berasyāṅgopāṅgapratyaṅgeṣu hīneṣu tacchaktihānir na | tasmācchaktiṃ bālāgāraṃ nītvā tadberaṃ bhūmau pidhāya upari mahāśāntim abjāgnau hutvā śilāgrahaṇavad hutvā tadaṅgasamutpattiṃ yuktyā tatkṣaṇāt kārayet ||

**Translation (English):**
*   **Repair Protocol:** Therefore, having transferred the divine energy (*śakti*) to the temporary shrine (*bālāgāra*), buried/covered that old icon in the ground (*bhumau pidhāya*), performed the great pacification fire-offering (*mahā-śāntim*) in the lotus-fire (*abjāgnau*), offered oblations following the procedure for selecting a stone (*śilā-grahaṇavad*), one should immediately arrange for the fabrication of those missing limbs (*tadaṅga-samutpattiṃ*) using appropriate techniques (*yuktyā*) without delay (*tatkṣaṇāt*).

### Section 2: Damage to Major Limbs of the Icon

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **बेरस्य महाङ्गे हीने कर्तव्यम्**
> महाङ्गे हीने अङ्गे तस्मिन् विनाशिते चेत्, तद् विधिना विसृज्य शिलाग्रहणं कृत्वा यथापूर्वं तथा आचरेत् ॥

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **berasya mahāṅge hīne kartavyam**
> mahāṅge hīne aṅge tasmin vināśite cet, tad vidhinā visṛjya śilāgrahaṇaṃ kṛtvā yathāpūrvaṃ tathā āceret ||

**Translation (English):**
*   **Damage to Major Limbs:** When a major/primary limb (*mahāṅga*) is missing or damaged (*hīne*), and if that limb is completely destroyed (or cannot be repaired, *tasmin vināśite cet*), one should discard it (the old icon, *tad*) according to the rules (*vidhinā visṛjya*), select a new stone (*śilā-grahaṇaṃ kṛtvā*), and perform all rituals as before (*yathāpūrvaṃ tathā āceret*).

### Section 3: Sin of Abandoning Repaired Icons

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **सन्धान-योग्य-बेर-त्यागे**
> सन्धानयोग्यबेरं यस्त्यजेत् स पापीयान् भवति, सर्वविनाशश्च भवति।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **sandhāna-yogya-bera-tyāge**
> sandhānayogyaberaṃ yas tyajet sa pāpīyān bhavati, sarvavināśaśca bhavati |

**Translation (English):**
*   **Abandonment of Repairable Icons:** He who discards/abandons an icon that is suitable for repair (*sandhāna-yogya-beraṃ yas tyajet*) becomes a great sinner (*sa pāpīyān bhavati*), and all-round ruin/destruction occurs (*sarva-vināśaśca bhavati*).

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> तस्मात् सर्वप्रयत्नेन सन्धानं कारयेत्॥

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> tasmāt sarvaprayatnena sandhānaṃ kārayet ||

**Translation (English):**
*   **Mandatory Repair:** Therefore, one should make every effort (*sarva-prayatnena*) to perform the repair/joining (*sandhānaṃ kārayet*) of the icon.

### Section 4: Provision for a Single Fixed Worship Icon

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **ध्रुवार्चयोः पूर्ववत् करणायोगे**
> अथवा ध्रुवबेरम् अर्चबेरं च पूर्ववत् कर्तुम् अयुक्तं चेत्, ध्रुवार्चाबेरम् एकमेव कृत्वा प्रतिष्ठाप्य अर्चयेद् इति केचित् ॥

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **dhruvārcayoḥ pūrvavat karaṇāyoge**
> athavā dhruvaberaṃ arcaberaṃ ca pūrvavat kartum ayuktaṃ cet, dhruvārcāberam ekam eva kṛtvā pratiṣṭhāpya arcayed iti kecit ||

**Translation (English):**
*   **Inability to Re-create Both Icons:** Alternatively, if it is impossible or improper (*ayuktaṃ cet*) to make both the fixed icon (*dhruva-bera*) and the worship icon (*arca-bera*) as before, some teachers say (*iti kecit*) one should make only a single fixed worship icon (*dhruvarca-beram ekam eva*), consecrate it, and worship it.

---




### Section 5: Damage in Single Icon Setup

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **एकबेरपक्षे तस्याङ्गहीने**
> एकबेरं प्रतिष्ठाप्य अर्चनायां प्रवर्तमानायाम्, तस्य अङ्गादिषु हीनेषु सन्धानं कृत्वा पूर्ववत् स्थापयेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **ekaberapakṣe tasyāṅgahīne**
> ekaberaṃ pratiṣṭhāpya arcanāyāṃ pravartamānāyām, tasya aṅgādiṣu hīneṣu sandhānaṃ kṛtvā pūrvavat sthāpayet |

**Translation (English):**
*   **Damage in Single Icon Setup:** In the case of a single icon (*eka-bera*) setup, when the daily worship is going on (*arcanāyāṃ pravartamānāyām*), if its limbs etc. become damaged or missing (*aṅgādiṣu hīneṣu*), one should perform repair/joining (*sandhānaṃ kṛtvā*) and install it as before (*pūrvavat sthāpayet*).

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> तद् यदि त्याज्यं चेत् विधिना विसृज्य यथापूर्वं तथा आचरेत् ॥

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> tad yadi tyājyaṃ cet vidhinā visṛjya yathāpūrvaṃ tathā āceret ||

**Translation (English):**
*   **Discarding Protocol:** If that icon must indeed be discarded (*tad yadi tyājyaṃ cet*), one should discard it according to the rules (*vidhinā visṛjya*) and proceed as before (*yathāpūrvaṃ tathā āceret*).

---




### Section 6: Location of Fixed Worship Icon

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **ध्रुवार्चाबेरस्य स्थानम्**
> अशक्तश्चेत् ध्रुवबेरम् अर्चबेरं च देवीभ्यां सहितं रहितं वा कृत्वा संस्थापयेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **dhruvārcāberasya sthānam**
> aśaktaścet dhruvaberaṃ arcaberaṃ ca devībhyāṃ sahitaṃ rahitaṃ vā kṛtvā saṃsthāpayet |

**Translation (English):**
*   **Inability to Install All Icons:** If one is unable/incapable of performing the full installation (*aśaktaścet*), one can install the fixed icon (*dhruva-bera*) and the worship icon (*arca-bera*) either with or without the two Goddesses (Śrī and Bhū/Hariṇī).

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> महालये ब्रह्मस्थाने ध्रुवार्चाबेरम् आहरेत् ॥

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> mahālaye brahmasthāne dhruvārcāberam āharet ||

**Translation (English):**
*   **Placement in Temple:** In the main temple (*mahālaye*), one should bring/install the fixed worship icon (*dhruvarca-beram*) in the Brahmā zone (*Brahma-sthāne*).

### Section 7: Temple Building with Goddesses

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **तस्य देवीभ्यां सह विमान-करणेच्छायाम्**
> तस्य देवीभ्यां युक्तं शिलादिभिः विमानं कर्तुम् इच्छेत् चेत्, देवं दैविकभागस्थं यथा तथा विमानं परिकल्प्य ब्रह्मस्थाने कौतुकं लोहं जङ्गमम् एव कुर्यात्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **tasya devībhyāṃ saha vimāna-karaṇecchāyām**
> tasya devībhyāṃ yuktaṃ śilādibhir vimānaṃ kartum icchet cet, devaṃ daivikabhāgasthaṃ yathā tathā vimānaṃ parikalpya brahmasthāne kautukaṃ lohaṃ jaṅgamam eva kuryāt |

**Translation (English):**
*   **Temple with Goddesses:** If one desires to construct a temple of stone etc. (*śilādibhir vimānaṃ kartum icchet*) along with the two Goddesses, one should design the temple such that the main deity is situated in the Daivika zone (*daivika-bhāgasthaṃ*).

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> कौतुकं शैलं चेत् तस्मिन् स्थावरम् एव ॥

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> kautukaṃ śailaṃ cet tasmin sthāvaram eva ||

**Translation (English):**
*   **Movable vs. Immovable Worship Icons:** In the Brahmā zone (*Brahma-sthāne*), one should make the worship icon (*kautukaṃ*) of metal (*lohaṃ*) and keep it movable (*jaṅgamam*). If the worship icon is made of stone (*śailaṃ*), it should only be installed as fixed/immovable (*sthāvaram*).

### Section 8: Transition from Clay Temple

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **मृण्मय-विमाने ध्रुवार्चने सति शैल-विमान-करणेच्छायाम्**
> मृण्मये विमाने ब्रह्मस्थाने लघुबेरं प्रतिष्ठाप्य, अर्चने वर्तमाने, पश्चात् ध्रुवबेरं शिलादिभिः विमानं च कर्तुम् इच्छेत् चेत्, तद् ध्रुववद् एव विमानं परिकल्प्य, दैविकभागे देवीभ्यां सह देवं प्रतिष्ठाप्य, पूर्व-अर्चित-बेरं कौतुकार्थं तस्य उत्तरे च उत्सवं बलिबेरं च प्रतिष्ठापयेत् ॥
> ***mṛṇmaya-vimāne dhruvārcane sati śaila-vimāna-karaṇecchāyām***
> *mṛṇmaye vimāne brahmasthāne laghuberaṃ pratiṣṭhāpya, arcanāyāṃ vartamānāyām, paścāt dhruvaberaṃ śilādibhir vimānaṃ ca kartum icchet cet, tad dhruvavad eva vimānaṃ parikalpya, daivikabhāge devībhyāṃ saha devaṃ pratiṣṭhāpya, pūrva-arcita-beraṃ kautukārthaṃ tasya uttare ca utsavaṃ baliberaṃ ca pratiṣṭhāpayet ||*

#### English Translation
*   **Replacing a Clay Temple/Icon:** In a clay temple (*mṛṇmaye vimāne*), when a small/light icon (*laghu-beraṃ*) has been installed in the Brahmā zone (*Brahma-sthāne*) and worship is going on, if one later desires to make a fixed deity (*dhruva-bera*) and temple (*vimānaṃ ca*) of stone etc.,

*   **Re-installation Procedure:** One should design the temple just like the fixed deity temple (*tad dhruvavad eva vimānaṃ parikalpya*), install the Lord along with the two Goddesses in the Daivika zone (*daivikabhāge devībhyāṃ saha*), and install the previously worshipped icon for the purpose of worship (*kautukārthaṃ*). To its north, one should install the festival icon (*utsavaṃ*) and the offering icon (*baliberaṃ ca*).

### Section 9: Reconstruction over Fixed Icon

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **पुनर्विमानकरणे विशेषः**
> पूर्वं महालये ध्रुवार्चायाम् अर्चयमानायाम्, पश्चात् शिलाभिः इष्टकाभिः वा विमानं कर्तुम् इच्छेत्, तस्य बेरस्य रक्षार्थं शिल्पिस्पर्श-विघात-अर्थं च बालधामवत् काष्ठ-पञ्जरं कृत्वा, तस्य अधस्तात् सशल्यं चेत् येन केनचित् संशोध्य, युग्महस्तेन तदन्तर-द्वारं वा सौधं कृत्वा तदधः समविस्तारम् अधमम्, अध्यर्धं मध्यमं, द्विगुणम् उत्तमं, समचतुरश्रमं कृत्वा तस्य चतुरभागं त्रिभागम् अर्धं वा गर्भागारं तच्छेषं भित्ति-विष्कम्भं समण्डपं विमानं पूर्ववत् कारयेत्। शिला-इष्टका-मिश्र-विमाने तस्य भागान्ते वज्रलेपेन वा मिश्रं कारयेत्। एवं कर्तुम् अशक्तश्चेत् तस्य अधिष्ठानाद् ऊर्ध्वं यथेष्ट-स्थाने मिश्र-द्रव्येण कारयेत् ॥
> ***punarvimānakaraṇe viśeṣaḥ***
> *pūrvaṃ mahālaye dhruvārcāyām arcayamānāyām, paścāt śilābhir iṣṭakābhir vā vimānaṃ kartum icchet, tasya berasya rakṣārthaṃ śilpisparśa-vighāta-arthaṃ ca bāladhāmavat kāṣṭha-pañjaraṃ kṛtvā, tasya adhastāt saśalyaṃ cet yena kenacit saṃśodhya, yugmahastena tadantara-dvāraṃ vā saudhaṃ kṛtvā tadadhaḥ samavistāram adhamam, adhyardhaṃ madhyamaṃ, dviguṇam uttamam, samacaturaśramaṃ kṛtvā tasya caturabhāgaṃ tribhāgam ardhaṃ vā garbhāgāraṃ taccheṣaṃ bhitti-viṣkambhaṃ samaṇḍapaṃ vimānaṃ pūrvavat kārayet | śilā-iṣṭakā-miśra-vimāne tasya bhāgānte vajralepena vā miśraṃ kārayet | evaṃ kartum aśaktaścet tasya adhiṣṭhānād ūrdhvaṃ yatheṣṭa-sthāne miśra-dravyeṇa kārayet ||*

#### English Translation
*   **Renovation over the Fixed Icon:** Previously, while the fixed icon (*dhruvarcāyām*) in the main temple (*mahālaye*) is being worshipped, if one later desires to build a temple of stone or brick (*śilābhir iṣṭakābhir vā*),

*   **Protection Cage:** For the protection of that icon (*berasya rakṣārthaṃ*) and to prevent injury or contact by the sculptors (*silpi-sparśa-vighāta-arthaṃ ca*), one should construct a wooden cage (*kāṣṭha-pañjaraṃ*) over it like a temporary shrine (*bāladhāmavat*).

*   **Cleansing and Pedestal Dimensions:** If there are any impurities or defects (*saśalyaṃ cet*) underneath it, one should purify it by some means, and having made a brick/plaster structure (*saodhaṃ kṛtvā*) with two cubits or its inner door (*tadantara-dvāraṃ vā*), one should make it square, measuring below it equal to the width (lowest/adhama), one and a half times (medium/madhyama), or twice (best/uttama).

*   **Sanctum Proportion:** Having made the sanctum sanctorum (*garbhāgāraṃ*) one-fourth, one-third, or half of that, and the remainder as the wall thickness (*bhitti-viṣkambhaṃ*), one should build the temple along with the pavilion (*samaṇḍapaṃ vimānaṃ*) as before.

*   **Materials:** In a temple built of a mixture of stone and brick (*śilā-iṣṭakā-miśra-vimāne*), at the boundary/joints of those parts, one should join them with plaster (*vajra-lepa*) or mixed materials. If one is unable to do so, one should make it with mixed materials in any desired place above the base (*adhiṣṭhānād ūrdhvam*).

### Section 10: Fading of Color and Minor Defects

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **ध्रुवबेरे वर्णक्षयादौ कर्तव्यम्**
> ध्रुवबेरे वर्ण-क्षये, स्फुटितादि-दोष-सम्भवे च, अल्प-दोषे, ध्रुवबेरस्थां शक्तिं कुम्भेऽम्भसि समावाह्य, तत्-कुम्भं कौतुकादींश्च समादाय मालिकायाम् मण्डपे वा संन्यस्य, कुम्भस्थां शक्तिं कौतुकादिषु समावाह्य, पूजा-वेलायां कुम्भे समारोपयेत्।
> ***dhruvabere varṇakṣayādou kartavyam***
> *dhruvabere varṇa-kṣaye, sphuṭitādi-doṣa-sambhave ca, alpa-doṣe, dhruvaberasthāṃ śaktiṃ kumbhe'mbhasi samāvāhya, tat-kumbhaṃ kautukādīṃśca samādāya mālikāyām maṇḍape vā saṃnyasya, kumbhasthāṃ śaktiṃ kautukādiṣu samāvāhya, pūjā-velāyāṃ kumbhe samāropayet |*
> 
> दिनद्वये अतीते अन्य-कुम्भं समादाय साधयित्वा कुम्भात् शक्तिं कुम्भे समावाह्य अर्चयेत् ॥
> *dinadvaye atīte anya-kumbhaṃ samādāya sādhayitvā kumbhāt śaktiṃ kumbhe samāvāhya arcayet ||*

#### English Translation
*   **Fading of Color and Minor Cracks:** If the color of the fixed icon fades (*varṇa-kṣaye*), or if minor defects like cracks appear (*sphuṭitādi-doṣa-sambhave ca*), and the damage is minor (*alpa-doṣe*),

*   **Temporary Transfer to Kumbha:** One should invoke the divine power (*śakti*) of the fixed icon into water in a water vessel (*kumbhe'mbhasi samāvāhya*), take that kumbha and the movable icons (*kautukādīn ca*), place them in the cloister (*mālikā*) or a pavilion (*mण्डपे वा*), invoke the power from the kumbha into the movable icons, and during worship time (*pūjā-velāyāṃ*), project it back into the kumbha.

*   **Vessel Replacement:** If two days pass under these conditions, one should take another kumbha, consecrate it, invoke the power from the old kumbha to the new kumbha, and perform the worship.

### Section 11: Re-consecration after Painting

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **वर्णानुलेपनादि-संस्कार-विधिः**
> दिनत्रये च एवं कुम्भ-शोधनं कृत्वा समाचरेत्।
> ***varṇānulepanādi-saṃskāra-vidhiḥ***
> *dinatraye ca evaṃ kumbha-śodhanaṃ kṛtvā samācaret |*
> 
> पश्चात् वर्ण-अनुलेपनादीनि कृत्वा संशोध्य वास्तु-होमं हुत्वा पुण्याहं वाचयित्वा ध्रुवबेर-शुद्धिं कुम्भं बिम्बं च समादाय, अभ्यन्तरं प्रविश्य कौतुकादीन् संस्थाप्य पूर्ववद् अक्षरन्यासादीनि कृत्वा कुम्भस्थां शक्तिं ध्रुवबेरे समारोप्य ध्रुवबेरात् कौतुकादिषु समावाह्य अर्चयेत् ॥
> *paścāt varṇa-anulepanādīni kṛtvā saṃśodhya vāstuhomaṃ hutvā puṇyāhaṃ vācayitvā dhruvabera-śuddhiṃ kumbhaṃ bimbaṃ ca samādāya, abhyantaraṃ praviśya kautukādin saṃsthāpya pūrvavad akṣaranyāsādīni kṛtvā kumbhasthāṃ śaktiṃ dhruvabere samāropya dhruvaberāt kautukādiṣu samāvāhya arcayet ||*

#### English Translation
*   **Cleansing and Third Day Rites:** And on the third day, having cleansed/replaced the vessel (*kumbha-śodhanaṃ kṛtvā*) in this manner, one should perform the rites.

*   **Painting and Purification:** Afterwards, having applied paint and ointments (*varṇa-anulepanādīni kṛtvā*), purified the area, performed the Vāstu fire-offering (*vāstu-homaṃ hutvā*), chanted the purificatory mantras (*puṇyāhaṃ vācayitvā*), taken the purified fixed icon, the kumbha, and the movable icons (*dhruvabera-śuddhiṃ kumbhaṃ bimbaṃ ca samādāya*),

*   **Re-consecration:** One should enter the inner sanctum, place the movable icons in their positions, perform the placement of letters (*akṣara-nyāsa*) as before, project the divine power from the kumbha onto the fixed icon (*kumbhasthāṃ śaktiṃ dhruvabere samāropya*), and then invoke it from the fixed icon into the movable icons (*dhruvaberāt kautukādiṣu samāvāhya*), and perform the worship.

### Section 12: Long-term Repairs and Multi-Deity Layouts

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **दीर्घकाल-संशोधनम्, नवमूर्त्यादितले दुष्टे च**
> दोष-गौरवाद् मासाद् ऊर्ध्वं कालम् अपेक्षितं चेत्, देवं बालालये प्रतिष्ठाप्य, मृण्मयं चेत् दारु-शूलं शोधयित्वा, शैलं चेत् शिलान्तं शोधयेत्।
> ***dīrghakāla-saṃśodhanam, navamūrtyāditale duṣṭe ca***
> *doṣa-gauravād māsād ūrdhvaṃ kālam apekṣitaṃ cet, devaṃ bālālaye pratiṣṭhāpya, mṛṇmayaṃ cet dāru-śūlaṃ śodhayitvā, śailaṃ cet śilāntaṃ śodhayet |*
> 
> **नवमूर्त्यादि-तले दुष्टे कर्तव्यम्**
> नव-षट्-पञ्च-मूर्ति-विमाने यद्यत् दोष-दुष्टं तत्तत् समुद्धृत्य, बालस्थानं प्रकल्प्य, कौतुकादीन् संस्थाप्य अर्चयेत्। पश्चात् निर्दोषे तले कौतुकादीन् समभ्यर्च्य पुनःस्थापनं कृत्वा अर्चयेत् ॥
> ***navamūrtyāditale duṣṭe kartavyam***
> *nava-ṣaṭ-pañca-mūrti-vimāne yadyat doṣa-duṣṭaṃ tattat samuddhṛtya, bālasthānaṃ prakalpya, kautukādīn saṃsthāpya arcayet | paścāt nirdoṣe tale kautukādīn samabhyarcya punaḥsthāpanaṃ kṛtvā arcayet ||*

#### English Translation
*   **Long-term Repairs:** If the repair takes a long time, more than a month (*māsād ūrdhvaṃ kālam*), due to the severity of the damage (*doṣa-gauravāt*), one should temporarily install the deity in the temporary shrine (*bālālaye pratiṣṭhāpya*).

*   **Clay vs. Stone Core Repairs:** If the icon is of clay (*mṛṇmayaṃ*), one should repair/purify the wooden core (*dāru-śūlaṃ śodhayitvā*); if it is of stone (*śailaṃ*), one should polish/refurbish the stone surface itself (*śilāntaṃ śodhayet*).

*   **Damage to Multi-Deity Layouts:** In a temple layout of nine, six, or five deities (*nava-ṣaṭ-pañca-mūrti-vimāne*), if any part of the floor/pedestal is damaged or polluted (*yadyat doṣa-duṣṭaṃ*), one should remove that damaged part (*tattat samuddhṛtya*), establish a temporary shrine (*bālasthānaṃ prakalpya*), install the movable icons (*kautukādīn saṃsthāpya*) there, and worship them.

*   **Re-installation:** Once the floor is repaired and made free of defects (*nirdoṣe tale*), one should worship the movable icons there, perform the re-installation (*punaḥ-sthāpanaṃ kṛtvā*), and conduct the regular worship.

## Glossary of Key Terms

1.  **Dhruva-bera / Mūla-bera (ध्रुवबेर / मूलबेर):** The primary, immovable deity icon fixed in the main sanctum (*garbhagṛha*).
2.  **Arcā-bera / Kautuka-bera (अर्चाबेर / कौतुकबेर):** The movable deity icon used for daily worship rituals in the inner sanctum.
3.  **Śaila-bera (शैलबेर):** A deity icon made of stone (*śilā*).
4.  **Mṛṇmaya-bera (मृण्मयबेर):** A deity icon made of clay (*mṛttikā*).
5.  **Dāru-śūla (दारुशूल):** The inner wooden core or framework of a clay deity icon.
6.  **Vajra-lepa / Vajra-bandha (वज्रलेप / वज्रबन्ध):** A durable architectural mortar/plaster used in temple construction and renovation.
7.  **Abja-agni / Abjāgni (अब्जाग्नि):** A sacred fire used in Vaikhānasa rituals, associated with fire-offerings.
8.  **Sandhāna (सन्धान):** The act of repairing, joining, or restoring damaged parts of a deity icon.
9.  **Kāṣṭha-pañjara (काष्ठपञ्जर):** A protective wooden cage or scaffolding built over a deity icon to safeguard it during temple renovations.
10. **Saodha / Saudha (सौध):** A brick, mortar, or plastered temple structure.
