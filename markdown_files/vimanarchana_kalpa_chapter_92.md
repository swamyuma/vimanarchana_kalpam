# Vimanarchana Kalpa of Sage Marīci (Vaikhānasa Āgama)
## Chapter 92: Nāḍī-cakra-vidhiḥ (Rules on the Network of Subtle Channels)

This document contains the Sanskrit text, IAST transliteration, and English translation of the 92nd Chapter (*Dvinavatitamaḥ Paṭalaḥ*) of the **Vimānārcanākalpa** (attributed to Sage Marīci, representing the Vaikhānasa school of Vaishnavism).

This chapter provides a detailed map of the subtle energy channels (*nāḍī*) in the human body. It begins by stating the traditional count of 72,000 channels originating from the root bulb (*kanda*) at the navel, and identifies the fourteen principal ones. It describes the path, characteristics, and elemental/cosmic associations of the three primary channels (*iḍā*, *piṅgalā*, and *suṣumnā*) and maps the remaining eleven channels (*sarasvatī*, *varuṇī*, *pūṣā*, *hasti-jihvā*, *yaśasvinī*, *viśvōdarā*, *kuhū*, *śaṅkhinī*, *payasvinī*, *alambuṣā*, and *gāndhārī*) to their specific terminations in the extremities (eyes, ears, tongue, hands, feet, and excretory organs). It concludes with a vivid metaphor comparing the network of minor channels to the veins of a pipal (*aśvattha*) leaf.

### Section 1: The Fourteen Principal Channels and the Sushumna

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **अथ द्विनवतितमः पटलः**
> **नाडीसंख्याः, तत्र मुख्याः, तत्स्वरूपं च**
> ***atha dvinavatitamaḥ paṭalaḥ***
> ***nāḍīsaṅkhyāḥ, tatra mukhyāḥ, tatsvarūpaṃ ca***
> 
> अथातो नाडीचक्रं वक्ष्ये --
> *athātō nāḍīcakraṃ vaxyē --*
> 
> कन्दोद्भूतानि द्विसप्तति-सहस्राणि नाड्यः । तासु मुख्याश्चतुर्दश -- इडा, पिङ्गला, सुषुम्ना, सरस्वती, वारुणी, पूषा, हस्तिजिह्वा, यशस्विनी, विश्वोदरा, कुहूः, शङ्खिनी, पयस्विनी, अलम्बुषा, गान्धारीति । आसाम्रादौ तिस्रो मुख्याः । तिसृषु सुषुम्नैव प्रधाना, व्यक्ता, वैष्णवी, सात्त्विकी, मुक्तिमार्ग इति विद्वद्भिः कथिता । सा कन्दमध्ये पद्मसूत्रप्रतीकाशा वीणादण्डान्तर्गुता अलाबूसदृशा ब्रह्मरन्ध्रान्तं स्थिता ।
> *kandōdbhūtāni dvisaptati-sahasrāṇi nāḍyaḥ | tāsu mukhyāścaturdaśa -- iḍā, piṅgalā, suṣumnā, sarasvatī, vāruṇī, pūṣā, hastijihvā, yaśasvinī, viśvōdarā, kuhūḥ, śaṅkhinī, payasvinī, alambuṣā, gāndhārīti | āsāmādau tisrō mukhyāḥ | tisṛṣu suṣumnāiva pradhānā, vyaktā, vaiṣṇavī, sāttvikī, muktimārga iti vidvadbhiḥ kathitā | sā kandamadhyē padmasūtrapratīkāśā vīṇādaṇḍāntargatā alābusadṛśā brahmarandhrāntaṃ sthitā |*

#### English Translation
*   **The Network of Channels:** Now we shall explain the network of subtle channels (*athātō nāḍīcakraṃ vaxyē*). There are seventy-two thousand channels originating from the root bulb (*kandōdbhūtāni dvisaptati-sahasrāṇi nāḍyaḥ*).

*   **The Fourteen Chief Channels:** Among them, fourteen are the principal ones (*tāsu mukhyāścaturdaśa*), namely: Iḍā, Piṅgalā, Suṣumnā, Sarasvatī, Vāruṇī, Pūṣā, Hasti-jihvā, Yaśasvinī, Viśvōdarā, Kuhū, Śaṅkhinī, Payasvini, Alambuṣā, and Gāndhārī (*iḍā, piṅgalā, suṣumnā, sarasvatī, vāruṇī, pūṣā, hastijihvā, yaśasvinī, viśvōdarā, kuhūḥ, śaṅkhinī, payasvinī, alambuṣā, gāndhārīti*).

*   **The Prime Channel:** Of these, the first three are primary (*āsāmādau tisrō mukhyāḥ*). Among the three, Suṣumnā alone is the chief (*tisṛṣu suṣumnāiva pradhānā*). It is manifest, related to Viṣṇu, sattvika in nature, and is declared by the wise to be the path of liberation (*vyaktā, vaiṣṇavī, sāttvikī, muktimārga iti vidvadbhiḥ kathitā*).

*   **Path of Sushumna:** Located in the middle of the *kanda* (*sā kandamadhyē*), it resembles a lotus fiber (*padmasūtrapratīkāśā*), runs inside the spinal column (*vīṇādaṇḍāntargatā*), resembles a bottle gourd stem (*alābusadṛśā*), and extends all the way up to the *brahmarandhra* (aperture at the crown of the head) (*brahmarandhrāntaṃ sthitā*).

### Section 2: Pingala, Ida, and the Remaining Eleven Channels

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> तस्याः दक्षिणभागे दक्षिणनासान्तं स्थिता पिङ्गला; तस्यां भास्करश्चरति । इडा वामभागे (or परभागे) वामनासान्तं स्थिता; तस्यां चन्द्रश्चरति । तौ राजस-तामसौ विष-अमृत-भागौ दिवा-रात्रौ स्याताम् ।
> *tasyāḥ daxiṇabhāgē daxiṇanāsāntaṃ sthitā piṅgalā; tasyāṃ bhāskaraścarati | iḍā vāmabhāgē (or parabhāgē) vāmanāsāntaṃ sthitā; tasyāṃ candraścarati | tau rājasa-tāmasau viṣa-amṛta-bhāgau divā-rātrau syātām |*
> 
> सुषुम्नायाः पूर्वभागे च ऊर्ध्वाधोगता मेढ्रान्तं स्थिता कुहूः । पश्चिमे (or अपरे) च ऊर्ध्वाधोगता जिह्वान्तं स्थिता सरस्वती । पिङ्गलायाः पूर्वभागे च ऊर्ध्वाधोगता दक्षिण-पादाङ्गुष्ठान्तं स्थिता यशस्विनी । पिङ्गलायाः पश्चिमे (or अपरे) दक्षिणनेत्रान्तं स्थिता पूषा । पूषा-गान्धार्योः मध्ये वामनेत्रान्तं स्थिता हस्तिजिह्वा । यशस्विन्याः कुहोश्च मध्ये ऊर्ध्वगता सर्वगामिनी दक्षिणपाण्याङ्गुष्ठान्तं स्थिता वारुणी । पूषा-सरस्वत्योः मध्ये दक्षिणकर्णान्तं स्थिता पयस्विनी । इडायाः पूर्वभागे च ऊर्ध्वाधोगता वाम-पादाङ्गुष्ठान्तं स्थिता हस्तिजिह्वा (गान्धारी) । अपरे वामनेत्रान्तं स्थिता गान्धारी । हस्तिजिह्वा-कुहूर्-मध्ये अधोर्ध्वगता सर्वगामिनी वामपाण्याङ्गुष्ठान्तं स्थिता विश्वोदरा । गान्धारी-सरस्वत्योः मध्ये वामकर्णान्तं स्थिता शङ्खिनी । अलम्बुषा कन्दमध्ये ऊर्ध्वाधोगता सर्वगामिनी पायुमूलाग्रगता । अन्याः सिराश्च अश्वत्थपत्र-सिरावत् समुत्पन्ना इति विज्ञायते ॥
> *suṣumnāyāḥ pūrvabhāgē ca ūrdhvādhōgatā mēḍhrāntaṃ sthitā kuhūḥ | paścimē (or aparē) ca ūrdhvādhōgatā jihvāntaṃ sthitā sarasvatī | piṅgalāyāḥ pūrvabhāgē ca ūrdhvādhōgatā daxiṇa-pādāṅguṣṭhāntaṃ sthitā yaśasvinī | piṅgalāyāḥ paścimē (or aparē) daxiṇanētrāntaṃ sthitā pūṣā | pūṣā-gāndhāryōḥ madhyē vāmanētrāntaṃ sthitā hastijihvā | yaśasvinyāḥ kuhōśca madhyē ūrdhvagatā sarvagāminī daxiṇapāṇyāṅguṣṭhāntaṃ sthitā vāruṇī | pūṣā-sarasvatyōḥ madhyē daxiṇakarṇāntaṃ sthitā payasvinī | iḍāyāḥ pūrvabhāgē ca ūrdhvādhōgatā vāma-pādāṅguṣṭhāntaṃ sthitā hastijihvā (gāndhārī) | aparē vāmanētrāntaṃ sthitā gāndhārī | hastijihvā-kuhūrmadhyē adhōrdhvagatā sarvagāminī vāmapāṇyāṅguṣṭhāntaṃ sthitā viśvōdarā | gāndhārī-sarasvatyōḥ madhyē vāmakarṇāntaṃ sthitā śaṅkhinī | alambuṣā kandamadhyē ūrdhvādhōgatā sarvagāminī pāyumūlāgragatā | anyāḥ sirāśca aśvatthapatra-sirāvat samutpannā iti vijñāyatē ||*

#### English Translation
*   **Pingala (Solar Channel):** On the right side of Sushumna, extending up to the right nostril, is Pingala (*tasyāḥ daxiṇabhāgē daxiṇanāsāntaṃ sthitā piṅgalā*); the Sun moves/functions within it (*tasyāṃ bhāskaraścarati*).

*   **Ida (Lunar Channel):** On the left (or opposite) side, extending up to the left nostril, is Ida (*iḍā vāmabhāgē vāmanāsāntaṃ sthitā*); the Moon moves within it (*tasyāṃ candraścarati*).

*   **Cosmic Duality:** Those two channels represent the Rajasic and Tamasic qualities, the portions of poison and nectar, and correspond to day and night (*tau rājasa-tāmasau viṣa-amṛta-bhāgau divā-rātrau syātām*).

*   **Paths of the Eleven Channels:**
    *   **Kuhū:** Situated on the front side of Sushumna, running upwards and downwards, and extending to the genitals (*suṣumnāyāḥ pūrvabhāgē ca ūrdhvādhōgatā mēḍhrāntaṃ sthitā kuhūḥ*).
    *   **Sarasvatī:** Situated on the back (opposite) side, running upwards and downwards, and extending to the tongue (*paścimē ca ūrdhvādhōgatā jihvāntaṃ sthitā sarasvatī*).
    *   **Yaśasvinī:** On the front side of Pingala, running upwards and downwards, extending to the right big toe (*piṅgalāyāḥ pūrvabhāgē ca ūrdhvādhōgatā daxiṇa-pādāṅguṣṭhāntaṃ sthitā yaśasvinī*).
    *   **Pūṣā:** Situated behind (to the west of) Pingala, extending to the right eye (*piṅgalāyāḥ paścimē daxiṇanētrāntaṃ sthitā pūṣā*).
    *   **Hasti-jihvā:** Situated between Pūṣā and Gāndhārī, extending to the left eye (*pūṣā-gāndhāryōḥ madhyē vāmanētrāntaṃ sthitā hastijihvā*).
    *   **Vāruṇī:** Situated between Yaśasvinī and Kuhū, running upwards, all-pervading, and extending to the right thumb (*yaśasvinyāḥ kuhōśca madhyē ūrdhvagatā sarvagāminī daxiṇapāṇyāṅguṣṭhāntaṃ sthitā vāruṇī*).
    *   **Payasvinī:** Situated between Pūṣā and Sarasvatī, extending to the right ear (*pūṣā-sarasvatyōḥ madhyē daxiṇakarṇāntaṃ sthitā payasvinī*).
    *   **Hasti-jihvā (Variant: Gāndhārī):** On the front side of Ida, running upwards and downwards, extending to the left big toe (*iḍāyāḥ pūrvabhāgē ca ūrdhvādhōgatā vāma-pādāṅguṣṭhāntaṃ sthitā hastijihvā*).
    *   **Gāndhārī:** On the other side, extending to the left eye (*aparē vāmanētrāntaṃ sthitā gāndhārī*).
    *   **Viśvōdarā:** Situated between Hasti-jihvā and Kuhū, running downwards and upwards, all-pervading, and extending to the left thumb (*hastijihvā-kuhūrmadhyē adhōrdhvagatā sarvagāminī vāmapāṇyāṅguṣṭhāntaṃ sthitā viśvōdarā*).
    *   **Śaṅkhinī:** Situated between Gāndhārī and Sarasvatī, extending to the left ear (*gāndhārī-sarasvatyōḥ madhyē vāmakarṇāntaṃ sthitā śaṅkhinī*).
    *   **Alambuṣā:** Located in the center of the root bulb (*kanda*), running upwards and downwards, all-pervading, and extending to the root and tip of the anus (*alambuṣā kandamadhyē ūrdhvādhōgatā sarvagāminī pāyumūlāgragatā*).

*   **Metaphor of Leaf Veins:** The other minor channels and nerves branch out like the veins of a pipal leaf (*anyāḥ sirāśca aśvatthapatra-sirāvat samutpannā iti vijñāyatē*).

### Glossary of Key Terms
*   **Nāḍī-cakra (नाडीचक्र):** The wheel or network of subtle energy channels in the human body, through which prana flows.
*   **Kanda (कन्द):** The root bulb of the channels, situated at the navel center.
*   **Iḍā (इडा):** The left/lunar channel, associated with cooling energy, nectar (*amṛta*), Tamas, and night.
*   **Piṅgalā (पिङ्गला):** The right/solar channel, associated with heating energy, fire (*bhāskara*), Rajas, and day.
*   **Suṣumnā (सुषुम्ना):** The central channel, the path of liberation (*mukti-mārga*) and seat of Vaishnavi energy.
*   **Vīṇā-daṇḍa (वीणादण्ड):** The spinal column, compared to the stem of a lute.
*   **Brahmarandhra (ब्रह्मरन्ध्र):** The aperture of Brahman at the crown of the head, where Sushumna terminates.
*   **Sarasvatī (सरस्वती):** The channel extending to the tongue (*jihvā*), associated with speech and sound.
*   **Pūṣā (पूषा):** The channel extending to the right eye (*daxiṇa-nētra*).
*   **Gāndhārī (गान्धारी):** The channel extending to the left eye (*vāma-nētra*).
*   **Śaṅkhinī (शङ्खिनी):** The channel extending to the left ear (*vāma-karṇa*).
*   **Payasvinī (पयस्विनी):** The channel extending to the right ear (*daxiṇa-karṇa*).
*   **Aśvatthapatra-sirā (अश्वत्थपत्रसिरा):** The veins of a pipal leaf, serving as a classical metaphor for the complex branching of the nervous/subtle energy system.
