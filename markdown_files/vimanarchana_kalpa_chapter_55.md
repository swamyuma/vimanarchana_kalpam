# Chapter 55: Rules for the Fish and Tortoise Incarnations (Matsya-kūrma-vidhiḥ)
### Section 1: Liturgical Rules for the Installation and Iconography of the Matsya and Kūrma Incarnations

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **अथ पञ्चपञ्चाशत्तमः पटलः**
> **मत्स्य-कूर्म-विधिः -**
> ***atha pañcapañcāśattamaḥ paṭalaḥ***
> ***matsya-kūrma-vidhiḥ -***
> 
> **(मत्स्याद्यवतारविधिः)**
> अथ मत्स्य-आदि-अवतार-विधिं वक्ष्ये — ग्रामादौ यथोक्ते देशे पूर्वोक्तेषु विमानेषु मत्स्य-कूर्म-वाराह-नारसिंह-वामन-जामदग्न्य-राघव-बलभद्र-कृष्ण-कल्किन इति।
> ***(matsyādyavatāravidhiḥ)***
> *atha matsya-ādi-avatāra-vidhiṃ vakṣye — grāmādou yathokte deśe pūrvokteṣu vimāneṣu matsya-kūrma-vārāha-nārasiṃha-vāmana-jāmadagnya-rāghava-balabhadra-kṛṣṇa-kalkina iti.*
> 
> एतेषां दशावताराणां प्रत्येकं पूर्वोक्त-देशमानेन ध्रुवबेरं निश्चित्य; पश्चाद् अभिदेशमानं (मुद्रितपाठे पश्चाद्भिर्देर्शामानं) कुर्यात्।
> *eteṣāṃ daśāvatārāṇāṃ pratyekaṃ pūrvokta-deśamānena dhruvaberaṃ niścitya; paścād abhideśamānaṃ kuryāt.*
> 
> **(मत्स्यस्य लक्षणम्)**
> पूर्वं मत्स्यस्य देहलब्धाङ्गुलेन भागाधिकं द्वितालं, नासाग्रात् पुच्छपर्यन्तं; नासाग्रात् नेत्रद्वयान्तं चतुरङ्गुलं, नेत्रात् कर्णं द्विमात्रं, तस्मात् पुच्छमूलं षट्त्रिंशदङ्गुलं (मुद्रितपाठे त्रिषदंगुलं), तस्मात् पुच्छाग्रं चतुरङ्गुलं, कुक्षि-पुच्छ-विस्तार-कर्म ग्रह-वेद-अङ्गुलं, तत्-तत् त्रिगुण-परिणाहं पुच्छमध्य-विष्कम्भम्, एकाङ्गुुलम् अग्रविस्तारं, षडङ्गुलं, मात्राद्यर्धमात्रं (मुद्रितपाठे मात्राधर्मात्रं), पक्षायाम-विस्तारं; तस्यायामं समांशं कृत्वा, द्व्यंशं त्र्यंशं वा पीठोत्तुङ्गं सप्ताङ्गुलविस्तारं (मुद्रितपाठे ससाङ्गुलविस्तारं) समवृत्तं पद्माकारम्। अत्र अनुक्तं शिल्पशास्त्रोक्तवत्। एवं ध्रुवबेरं कारयेत्।
> ***(matsyasya laxanam)***
> *pūrvaṃ matsyasya dehalabdhāṅgulena bhāgādhikaṃ dvitālaṃ, nāsāgrāt pucchaparyantaṃ; nāsāgrāt netradvayāntaṃ caturaṅgulaṃ, netrāt karṇaṃ dvitram, tasmāt pucchamūlaṃ ṣaṭtriṃśadāṅgulaṃ, tasmāt pucchāgraṃ caturaṅgulaṃ, kukṣi-puccha-vistāra-karma graha-veda-aṅgulaṃ, tat-tat triguṇa-pariṇāhaṃ pucchamadhya-viṣkambham, ekāṅgulam agravistāraṃ, ṣaḍaṅgulaṃ, mātrādyardhamātraṃ, paxāyāma-vistāraṃ; tasyāyāmaṃ samāṃśaṃ kṛtvā, dvyāṃśaṃ tryāṃśaṃ vā pīṭhottuṅgaṃ saptāṅgulavistāraṃ samavṛttaṃ padmākāram. atra anuktaṃ śilpaśāstrokta-vat. evaṃ dhruvaberaṃ kārayet.*
> 
> कौतुकं विष्णुं चतुर्भुजम् एव कारयेत्; तद्रूपं (मत्स्यरूपं) न कारयेत्।
> *kautukaṃ viṣṇuṃ caturbhujam eva kārayet; tadrūpaṃ na kārayet.*
> 
> **(मत्स्यप्रतिष्ठा)**
> अथ प्रतिष्ठाविशेषं वक्ष्ये — तद्-आलयाभिमुखे पूर्ववद् यागशालां कृत्वा; तोरणाद्यैः अलङ्कृत्य; पञ्चाग्नीन् पौण्डरीकाग्निं च कारयित्वा; तस्य प्रधानं पौण्डरीकाग्निः तस्मिन् हौत्रशंसनं कृत्वा; 'मत्स्यं विष्णुं भद्रं क्रीडात्मकं' इति चतुर्भिः मन्त्रैः आवाह्य; निरूप्य; आज्याहुतीः हुत्वा; 'यद्वै ससृष्टः' (मुद्रितपाठे यद्वैयसृष्ट) इति मन्त्रेण अष्टोत्तरशतं हुत्वा; अन्यत् सर्वं प्रतिष्ठा-उक्त-विधिना कारयेत्।
> ***(matsyapratiṣṭhā)***
> *atha pratiṣṭhāviśeṣaṃ vakṣye — tad-ālayābhimuxe pūrvavat yāgāśālāṃ kṛtvā; toraṇādyaiḥ alaṅkrtya; pañcāgnīn pouṇḍarīkāgniṃ ca kārayitvā; tasya pradhānaṃ pouṇḍarīkāgnistaṣmin houtraśaṃsanaṃ kṛtvā; 'matsyaṃ viṣṇuṃ bhadraṃ krīḍātmakam' iti caturbhiḥ mantraiḥ āvāhya; nirūpya; ājyāhutīḥ hutvā; 'yad vai sasṛṣṭaḥ' iti mantreṇa aṣṭottaraśataṃ hutvā; anyat sarvaṃ pratiṣṭhā-ukta-vidhinā kārayet.*
> 
> नित्यार्चनायां ध्रुवबेरं मूर्तिमन्त्रैः अष्टोपचारैः अभ्यर्च्य, कौतुकं पूर्वोक्त-षोडशोपचार-मन्त्रैः मूर्तिमन्त्रान् संयोज्य अर्चयेत्। स्नपन-उत्सवादीनि सर्वाणि पूर्वोक्त-विधिना कारयेत् ॥
> *nityārcanāyāṃ dhruvaberaṃ mūrtimantraiḥ aṣṭopacāraiḥ abhyarcya, kautukaṃ pūrvokta-ṣoḍaśopacāra-mantraiḥ mūrtimantrān saṃyojya arcayet. snapanotsavādīni sarvāṇi pūrvokta-vidhinā kārayet.*
> 
> **(कूर्मस्य लक्षणम्)**
> अथ कूर्मस्य लक्षणं वक्ष्ये — देहलब्धाङ्गुलेन एकाङ्गुलं शिरोमानं, द्विमात्रं कण्ठं, एकाङ्गुलं कर्णं, त्र्यङ्गुलं हृदयान्तं, तत्-समं नाभ्यन्तं, त्रिमात्रं पृष्ठान्तं, प्रकोष्ठं पाणितलं प्रत्येकम् एकाङ्गुलं, तथैव ऊरु-जङ्घा-पादतलं; तस्य आयाम-समं वैपुल्यं, पञ्चवर्ण-समायुक्तं, अन्तःश्वेतनिभम्। तस्य आयामं षड्भागं कृत्वा, द्विभागं द्विभागं वा पीठोत्तुङ्गं वस्वङ्गुुलविस्तारं पद्माकारं, शेषं युक्त्यैव कारयेत् ॥
> ***(kūrmasya laxanam)***
> *atha kūrmasya laxaṇaṃ vakṣye — dehalabdhāṅgulena ekāṅgulaṃ śiromānaṃ, dvimātraṃ kaṇṭhaṃ, ekāṅgulaṃ karṇaṃ, tryaṅgulaṃ hṛdayāntaṃ, tat-samaṃ nābhyantaṃ, trimātraṃ pṛṣṭhāntaṃ, prakoṣṭhaṃ pāṇitalaṃ pratyekam ekāṅgulaṃ, tathaiva ūru-jaṅghā-pādatalaṃ; tasya āyāma-samāṃśaṃ vaipulyaṃ, pañcavarṇa-samāyuktaṃ, antaḥśvetanibham. tasya āyāmaṃ ṣadbhāgaṃ kṛtvā, dvibhāgaṃ dvibhāgaṃ vā pīṭhottuṅgaṃ vasvaṅgulavistāraṃ padmākāraṃ, śeṣaṃ yuktyāiva kārayet.*
> 
> **(कूर्मप्रतिष्ठा)**
> तस्य प्रतिष्ठाविशेषं वक्ष्ये — पूर्वोक्त-यागशाला-मध्ये शय्यावेदिं परितः पञ्चाग्नीन् परिकल्प्य; गार्हपत्ये प्रधानाग्नौ हौत्रशंसनं कृत्वा; 'अकूपारं कूर्मरूपं विष्णुं वसुधाधरम्' इति चतुर्भिः मन्त्रैः आवाह्य; तथैव जुष्टाकार-स्वाहाकारौ कृत्वा; 'रायामीशः' (वै॰ म॰ प्र॰ ७) इति मन्त्रेण अष्टाधिकशतम् आवर्त्य जुहुयात्। अन्यत् सर्वं विष्णोः इव समाचरेत्। कूर्मस्य कौतुकं विष्णुं चतुर्भुजम् एव कुर्यात्; तद्रूपं (कूर्मरूपं) नैव कारयेत्।
> ***(kūrmapratiṣṭhā)***
> *tasya pratiṣṭhāviśeṣaṃ vakṣye — pūrvokta-yāgāśālā-madhyye śayyāvediṃ paritaḥ pañcāgnīn parikalpya; gārhapatye pradhānāgnou houtraśaṃsanaṃ kṛtvā; 'akūpāraṃ kūrmarūpaṃ viṣṇuṃ vasudhādharam' iti caturbhiḥ mantraiḥ āvāhya; tathaiva juṣṭākāra-svāhākārou kṛtvā; 'rāyāmīśaḥ' iti mantreṇa aṣṭādhikaśatam āvartya juhuyāt. anyat sarvaṃ viṣṇoriva samācaret. kūrmasya kautukaṃ viṣṇuṃ caturbhujam eva kuryāt; tadrūpaṃ naiva kārayet.*
> 
> नित्यार्चनायां तन्मूर्तिमन्त्रैः ध्रुवबेरम् अष्टोपचारैः अभ्यर्च्य, कौतुकबिम्बं यथोक्त-षोडशोपचार-मन्त्रैः तन्मूर्तिमन्त्रान् संयोज्य अर्चयेत्। अन्यत् सर्वं समानम् इति विज्ञायते ॥
> *nityārcanāyāṃ tanmūrtimantraiḥ dhruvaberaṃ aṣṭopacāraiḥ abhyarcya, kautukabimbaṃ yathokta-ṣoḍaśopacāra-mantraiḥ tanmūrtimantrān saṃyojya arcayet. anyat sarvaṃ samānam iti vijñāyate.*

#### English Translation
*   **The Ten Incarnations (*daśāvatāra*):** I shall now describe the rules for the installation of the incarnations beginning with Matsya: in the village or other designated areas, within the previously specified temples, one should install Matsya, Kūrma, Varāha, Narasiṃha, Vāmana, Jamadagnya (Paraśurāma), Rāghava (Rāma), Balabhadra (Balarāma), Kṛṣṇa, and Kalkin.

*   **Deciding the Icon Dimensions:** Deciding the height of the stationary deity (*dhruva-bera*) for each of these ten incarnations based on the previously defined rules of the temple size, one should afterwards calculate the dimensions of the icons (*abhideśa-māna*) in finger-units (*aṅgulas*).

*   **Measurements of the Fish Incarnation (*Matsya*):**
    *   **Length and Divisions:** First, for the Fish form, using the patron's finger units (*dehalabdha-aṅgula*), it should be made in the *dvitāla* (two-span) system, measuring slightly more than twenty-four parts from the tip of the nose to the tail.
    *   **Proportions:** The distance from the tip of the nose to the eyes should be four *aṅgulas*; from the eyes to the ears, two units; from the ears to the base of the tail, thirty-six *aṅgulas*; and from the base of the tail to the tail-tip, four *aṅgulas*.
    *   **Fins and Thickness:** The width of the belly and tail should be nine or four *aṅgulas*. The thickness of the middle of the tail should be three times the circumference of those parts. The front thickness should be one *aṅgula*, the fin length six *aṅgulas*, and the fin width one and a half units.
    *   **Pedestal and Processional Icon:** Dividing its total length into equal parts, the pedestal height should be two or three parts, and its width seven *aṅgulas*, perfectly circular and shaped like a lotus. Anything not specified here should be done according to the rules of the *Śilpa-śāstra*. Thus, one should carve the stationary deity. The processional icon (*kautuka-bera*) must be made as the four-armed Viṣṇu only; it should not be made in the form of a fish.

*   **Consecration of Matsya (*Matsya-pratiṣṭhā*):** Now I shall describe the specific consecration of Matsya: having built a sacrificial hall in front of the temple as before, decorated it with arches, set up the five fires and the Pauṇḍarīka fire, and performed the chanter's praise at the primary Pauṇḍarīka fire; invoke the deity reciting the four mantras beginning with "Matsyaṃ viṣṇuṃ bhadraṃ krīḍātmakam". Offering ghee oblations, he should make one hundred and eight fire-offerings reciting "Yad vai sasṛṣṭaḥ", and perform all other rites according to the rules of consecration (*pratiṣṭhā-vidhi*).

*   **Daily and Periodic Worship:** For the daily worship (*nityārcanā*), worship the stationary deity with His specific form-mantras and eight services, and worship the processional icon combining the form-mantras with the previously defined sixteen services. He should perform the bathing (*snapana*) and festival (*utsava*) ceremonies according to the previously described rules.

*   **Measurements of the Tortoise Incarnation (*Kūrma*):**
    *   **Head and Neck:** Now I shall describe the specifications for the Tortoise form: in the units derived from the deity's body, the head should measure one *aṅgula*; the neck, two units; the ears, one *aṅgula*.
    *   **Body Proportions:** The distance from the neck to the heart should be three *aṅgulas*; from the heart to the navel, equal to that (three *aṅgulas*); and from the navel to the tail-tip, three units.
    *   **Limbs and Shell:** The forearms and claws should measure one *aṅgula* each. The thighs, shanks, and hind claws should be carved in the same way. The width of the shell should be equal to its length. It should be colored with five colors and be white underneath.
    *   **Pedestal:** Dividing its total length into six parts, the pedestal height should be two parts, and its width eight *aṅgulas*, shaped like a lotus. The remaining features should be carved using his own judgment.

*   **Consecration of Kūrma (*Kūrma-pratiṣṭhā*):** I shall describe the specific consecration of Kūrma: in the middle of the previously described sacrificial hall, setting up the five fires around the bedding altar, and performing the chanter's praise at the primary Gārhapatya fire; invoke the deity reciting the four mantras beginning with "Akūpāraṃ kūrmarūpaṃ viṣṇuṃ vasudhādharam". Offering the *juṣṭa* and *svāhā* libations, he should offer one hundred and eight oblations reciting "Rāyāmīśaḥ". All other rites should be performed as for Viṣṇu. The processional icon of Kūrma must be made as the four-armed Viṣṇu; it should not be made in the form of a tortoise.

*   **Concluding Rites:** In the daily worship, worship the stationary deity with His form-mantras and eight services, and worship the processional icon combining the form-mantras with the sixteen services. All other rituals are identical—so it is understood.
