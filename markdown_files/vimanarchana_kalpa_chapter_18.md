# Vimanarchana Kalpa of Sage Marīci (Vaikhānasa Āgama)
## Chapter 18: Śūla-Sthāpana-Varṇa-Vidhiḥ (Rules for Installing the Armature and Applying Colors)

This document contains the Sanskrit text, IAST transliteration, and English translation of the 18th Chapter (*Aṣṭādaśaḥ Paṭalaḥ*) of the **Vimānārcanākalpa** (attributed to Sage Marīci, representing the Vaikhānasa school of Vaishnavism).

This chapter discusses the rules for selecting auspicious times for installing the wooden armature (*śūla*), the ritual counts of staffs for different icon postures, the felling omens, felling and felling directions, joint wrapping, the detailed preparation and application of the *Aṣṭabandha* (eight-ingredient) cement, the *Rajju-bandha* (coconut-fiber rope wrapping), the *Mṛt-saṃskāra* (sacred clay layers), the *Ratna-nyāsa* (gem deposits), the *Śarkarā-lepa* (lime-gravel plaster sealant), the *Paṭācchādana* (cloth wrapping), and the final *Varṇa-saṃskāra* (application of colors).

---

### Section 1: Auspicious Times and Ritual Hall Setup (Śūlasthāpana-kāla-yāgaśālā-vidhiḥ)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> अथ शूल-स्थापन-विधिं वक्ष्ये -
> अयनं च उत्तरं (उत्तरायणं) श्रेष्ठं, दक्षिणे (दक्षिणायने) मार्गशीर्ष-माघौ विवर्ज्य; शुक्लपक्षे, कृष्णे (कृष्णपक्षे) अन्त्य-त्रिभागं मुक्त्वा; कर्तुः अनुकूल-नक्षत्रे, स्थिरे राशौ स्थापनं कारयेत्।
> ***athāṣṭādaśaḥ paṭalaḥ***
> *atha śūla-sthāpana-vidhiṃ vakṣye -*
> *ayanaṃ ca uttaraṃ śreṣṭhaṃ, dakṣiṇe mārgaśīrṣa-māghau vivarjya; śuklapakṣe, kṛṣṇe antya-tribhāgaṃ muktvā; kartuḥ anukūla-nakṣatre, sthire rāśau sthāpanaṃ kārayet.*
> 
> तद्-दिनात् पूर्वं नवमे, सप्तमे, पञ्चमे, तृतीये वा अहनि (अह्नि) अङ्कुर-अर्पणं कुर्यात्।
> *tad-dināt pūrvaṃ navame, saptame, pañcame, tṛtīye vā ahani aṅkura-arpaṇaṃ kuryāt.*
> 
> आलयात् प्रमुखे (पूर्वभागे) दक्षिणे वा यागशालां (यागशालं) तोरण-आद्यैः अलङ्कृत्य; तन्मध्ये शय्यावेदिं तद्-भित्ति-सम-आयाम-विस्तारां तुरीयांश-उत्सेधां (पाद-उच्छ्राययुक्तां) कृत्वा;
> *ālayāt pramukhe dakṣiṇe vā yāgaśālāṃ toraṇa-ādyaiḥ alaṅkṛtya; tanmadhye śayyāvediṃ tad-bhitti-sama-āyāma-vistārāṃ turīyāṃśa-utsedhāṃ kṛtvā;*

#### English Translation
*   **Auspicious Times and Pavilion Setup (*Śūlasthāpana-kāla-yāgaśālā-vidhiḥ*):**
    *   **Auspicious Timing:** Now, I shall describe the procedure for installing the armature frame (*śūla-sthāpana*). The Northern solstice (*uttarāyaṇam*) is considered best. During the Southern solstice (*dakṣiṇāyanam*), avoid the months of Mārgaśīrṣa and Māgha. Perform the installation during the bright fortnight, or during the dark fortnight excluding its last five days. The ceremony must be conducted under a star favorable to the patron, when a stable sign of the zodiac (*sthira-rāśi*) is rising.
    *   **Sprout Offering:** On the ninth, seventh, fifth, or third day prior to the installation day, perform the sprout offering (*aṅkura-arpaṇam*).
    *   **Sacrificial Pavilion:** In front of the temple or to its South, construct a sacrificial pavilion (*yāgaśālā*) decorated with arches and banners. In its center, build a square couch-platform (*śayyā-vedī*) whose length and width are equal to the thickness of the pavilion walls, and whose height is one-fourth of that width.

### Section 2: Consecration of Armature Staffs and Couches (Dandapramāṇa-śayanādhivāsa-vidhiḥ)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> तां परितः सभ्यादि-पञ्च-अग्निकुण्डानि कृत्वा; पूर्ववद् आलयाद् उत्तरे वास्तुहोमं हुत्वा, पर्यग्नि-पञ्चगव्याभ्यां (पर्य- म्निपञ्चगव्याभ्यां) संशोध्य; अग्निकुण्डेषु आधारं हुत्वा, शय्यावेद्याम् ऐशान्यां द्विहस्तायत-विस्तारां भागोन्नतां धान्यवेदिं कृत्वा;
> *tāṃ paritaḥ sabhyādi-pañca-agnikuṇḍāni kṛtvā; pūrvavad ālayād uttare vāstuhomaṃ hutvā, paryagni-pañcagavyābhyāṃ saṃśodhya; agnikuṇḍeṣu ādhāraṃ hutvā, śayyāvedyām aiśānyāṃ dvihastāyata-vistārāṃ bhāgonnatāṃ dhānyavediṃ kṛtvā;*
> 
> उत्तराग्रान् दर्भान् आस्तीर्य, वस्त्रोपरि बेर-शूलान् (बेरशूलान्) संन्यस्य।
> **अष्टादश-दण्डाः (The Eighteen Armature Staffs of Viṣṇu):**
> *   वंशदण्डः (Spinal staff) - १
> *   वक्षोदण्डः (Chest staff) - १
> *   कटिदण्डः (Waist staff) - १
> *   पार्श्वदण्डौ (Side staffs) - २
> *   बाहुदण्डाः (Arm staffs) - ४
> *   प्रकोष्ठदण्डाः (Forearm staffs) - ४
> *   ऊरुदण्डौ (Thigh staffs) - २
> *   जङ्घादण्डौ (Calf staffs) - २
> *   मौलिदण्डः (Crown staff) - १
> एते अष्टादश-दण्डाः **स्थानके** (standing posture)।
> *uttarāgrān darbhān āstīrya, vastropari bera-śūlān sanyasya.*
> *vaṃśadaṇḍaḥ, vakṣodaṇḍaḥ, kaṭidaṇḍaḥ, pārśvadaṇḍau, bāhudaṇḍāḥ, prakoṣṭhadaṇḍāḥ, ūrudaṇḍau, jaṅghādaṇḍau, maulidaṇḍaḥ - ete aṣṭādaśa-daṇḍāḥ sthānake.*
> 
> *   **आसने** (sitting posture) - विंशति-दण्डाः (२०)।
> *   **शयने** (reclining posture) - चतुर्भुजे एकोनविंशति-दण्डाः (१९); द्विभुजे सप्तदश-दण्डाः (१७)। मौलिदण्डः सहजः (वंशदण्डेन सह एकीभूतः) चेत्, एकोनान् (एकविहीनान्) एतान् च एव संस्कृत्य (समास्स्कृत्य);
> **   āsane - viṃśatidaṇḍāḥ.*
> **   śayane - caturbhuje ekonaviṃśatidaṇḍāḥ; dvibhuje saptadaśadaṇḍāḥ. maulidaṇḍaḥ sahajaś cet, ekonān etāṃś caiva saṃskṛtya;*
> 
> असङ्करार्थं (असंकरार्थं) चिह्नं कृत्वा, शुद्धोदकैः पवमानाद्यैः (पवमानैः नाद्यैः) प्रक्षाल्य; धान्यवेद्यां विष्णुगायत्र्या शूलान् विन्यस्य, "वसोः पवित्रम्" इति पञ्चगव्यैः समभ्युक्ष्य, "शन्नो देवीः" (शन्नोदेवी) इति गव्येन पयसा प्रक्षाल्य, तैलाभ्यञ्जनं कृत्वा; देव्यादीनाम् (देव्यादीना मेवमेव) एवम् एव कृत्वा;
> *asaṅkarārthaṃ cihnaṃ kṛtvā, śuddhōdakaiḥ pavamānādyaiḥ prakṣālya; dhānyavedyāṃ viṣṇugāyatryā śūlān vinyasya, "vasoḥ pavitram" iti pañcagavyaiḥ samabhyukṣya, "śanno devīḥ" iti gavyena payasā prakṣālya, tailābhyañjanaṃ kṛtvā; devyādīnām evam eva kṛtvā;*
> 
> तत्-प्राच्यां दण्डवेद्यां (दण्ड वेद्यां) यथाविधि चतुर्दश कलशान् विन्यस्य, तत्तद्-द्रव्यैः सोपस्नानैः (सोपस्नानैश्ट) शूलान् समभ्युक्ष्य, देव्यादीनां पृथक् पृथग् एवं कारयित्वा;
> *tat-prācyāṃ daṇḍavedyāṃ yathāvidhi caturdaśa kalaśān vinyasya, tattad-dravyaiḥ sopasnānaiḥ śūlān samabhyukṣya, devyādīnāṃ pṛthak pṛthag evaṃ kārayet.*
> 
> शय्यावेद्यां धान्योपरि पञ्चशयनानि पञ्चवस्त्राणि वा आस्तीर्य (पञ्चवस्त्राणिवाऽऽस्तीर्य), शूलान् स्वस्तिसूक्तेन असङ्कुलान् (परस्परं विविक्तान्) आरोप्य; तन्मध्ये वंशदण्डं, तत्-पार्श्वयोः पार्श्वदण्डौ, तद्-ऊर्ध्वॆ वक्षोदण्डं, तद्-अधस्तात् कटिदण्डं च संन्यस्य (कटिदण्डञ्च); अथवा वंशदण्डेन एतान् संयोज्य संन्यसेत् इति केचित् (संन्यसे दिति)।
> *śayyāvedyāṃ dhānyopari pañcaśayanāni pañcavastrāṇi vā āstīrya, śūlān svastisūktena asaṅkulān āropya; tanmadhye vaṃśadaṇḍaṃ, tat-pārśvayoḥ pārśvadaṇḍau, tad-ūrdhve vakṣodaṇḍaṃ, tad-adhastāt kaṭidaṇḍaṃ ca sanyasya; athavā vaṃśadaṇḍena etān saṃyojya saṃnyaset iti kecit.*

#### English Translation
*   **Armature Counts and Couch Consecration (*Dandapramāṇa-śayanādhivāsa-vidhiḥ*):**
    *   **Ritual Altar:** Around the couch-platform, construct five fire-pits starting with the *sabhyāgni*. Perform the *vāstu-homa* to the North of the temple as before, purify with fire and *pañcagavya*, and make initial offerings into the fire-pits. In the North-East (*aiśānyām*) of the couch-platform, build a grain altar (*dhānya-vedī*) measuring 2 cubits in length and width, and 1 part high.
    *   **The 18 Armature Staffs (*Aṣṭādaśa-daṇḍāḥ*):**
        *   Spread sacred Kuśa grass facing North over the grain altar, lay a cloth, and place the armature staffs (*bera-śūlān*) upon it.
        *   For a standing icon (*sthānaka*), there are **18 staffs**: spinal staff (1), chest staff (1), waist staff (1), side staffs (2), upper arm staffs (4), forearm staffs (4), thigh staffs (2), calf staffs (2), and crown staff (1).
        *   For a sitting icon (*āsana*), there are **20 staffs**.
        *   For a reclining icon (*śayana*), there are **19 staffs** for a four-armed form, and **17 staffs** for a two-armed form.
        *   If the crown staff is integrated into the spinal staff, the count is reduced by one. Consecrate these staffs.
    *   **Purification of the Staffs:** Mark each staff to prevent confusion, wash them with pure water and *pavamāna* hymns. Lay them on the grain altar chanting the *Viṣṇu-Gāyatrī*. Sprinkle them with *pañcagavya* chanting "Vasoḥ pavitram...", wash them with cow's milk chanting "Śanno devīḥ...", and apply oil (*tailābhyañjanam*). Perform the same purification for the consort Goddesses' armatures.
    *   **Laying to Rest:**
        *   To the East of the altar, set up a staff-altar (*daṇḍa-vedī*), place 14 water jars in accordance with rules, and sprinkle the armature staffs with their consecrated liquids while wrapped in cloths. Perform this separately for the Goddesses' armatures.
        *   On the couch-platform, spread five mattresses or five cloths over a bed of grains. Lay the armature staffs on the couch without crowd/overlap (*asaṅkulān*), chanting the *Svasti-sūkta*. Place the spinal staff in the middle, the side staffs on its sides, the chest staff above it, and the waist staff below it. Alternatively, some say one may assemble these staffs to the spinal staff first and then place the assembled frame on the couch.

### Section 3: Binding the Protective Band and Fire Rituals (Pratisarabandha-homa-vidhiḥ)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> तद्-दक्षिण-पार्श्वे (तद्काष्णपार्श्वे) दक्षिणाङ्गानि (दक्षिणांगांनि), वाम-पार्श्वे वामाङ्गानि (वामांगानि) च संन्यस्य; यदि द्वारं तद्-दिशि वंशदण्ड-अग्रं (वंश दण्डाऽग्रं) कृत्वा;
> *tad-dakṣiṇapārśve dakṣiṇāṅgāni, vāmapārśve vāmāṅgāni ca sanyasya; yadi dvāraṃ taddiśi vaṃśadaṇḍa-agraṃ kṛtvā;*
> 
> पुण्याह-अन्ते प्रकोष्ठदण्डे प्रतिसरं बद्ध्वा (प्रतिसरंबध्वा), "अस्थिभ्यो नमः" (अस्थिभ्योनम) इति अभ्यर्च्य, "यद्वैष्णवम्" (यद्वैष्णव' मिति) इति मन्त्रेण शूलान् शाययेत्। देव्यादीनां तत्तन्-मन्त्रेण शाययित्वा, उत्तराच्छादनं कृत्वा, चतुर्दिक्षु चतुर्वेद-अध्ययनं कारयेत्।
> *puṇyāha-ante prakoṣṭhadaṇḍe pratisaraṃ baddhvā, "asthibhyo namaḥ" iti abhyarcya, "yadvaiṣṇavam" iti mantreṇa śūlān śāyayet. devyādīnāṃ tattan-mantreṇa śāyayitvā, uttarācchādanaṃ kṛtvā, caturdikṣu caturvedān adhyāpayet.*
> 
> सभ्याग्निं परिस्तीर्य, हौत्रं प्रशंस्य, आहवनीय-आदीनि (अग्नीन्) कृत्वा; वैष्णवं, विष्णुसूक्तं, पुरुषसूक्तं, "आ त्वा हार्षम्" इति सूक्तं च, श्रीदैवत्यं भूमिदैवत्यं च हुत्वा (चहुत्वा);
> *sabhyāgniṃ paristīrya, hautraṃ praśaṃsya, āhavanīyādīni kṛtvā; vaiṣṇavaṃ, viṣṇusūktaṃ, puruṣasūktam, "ā tvā hārṣam" iti ca, śrīdaivatyaṃ bhūmidaivatyaṃ ca hutvā;*
> 
> "आस्थिभ्यः स्वाहा" इति मन्त्रेण अष्टोत्तर-शतम् आवर्त्य हुत्वा; सर्वदैवत्यं च हुत्वा, आहवनीय-आदिषु पूर्ववत् होमं हुत्वा (पूर्ववद्धोमंहुत्वा), सभ्यं विना अन्तर्होमं (सभ्यंविनाऽन्तहोमं) जुहुयात्। अथवा सभ्याग्नम् एकम् एव कारयेत् इति एके (इत्येके)।
> *"āsthibhyaḥ svāhā" iti mantreṇa aṣṭottara-śatam āvartya hutvā; sarvadaivatyaṃ ca hutvā, āhavanīyādiṣu pūrvavad homaṃ hutvā, sabhyaṃ vinā antarhomaṃ juhuyāt. athavā sabhyāgnim ekam eva kārayet ityeke.*

#### English Translation
*   **Vigil, Offerings, and Night Rites (*Pratisarabandha-homa-vidhiḥ*):**
    *   **Positioning the Limbs:** Arrange the right-side armature staffs on the right side of the couch, and the left-side staffs on the left. Position the top head portion (*agram*) of the spinal staff in the direction of the temple doorway.
    *   **Wrist-Band and Sleeping Rites:** At the conclusion of the purification rite, tie the protective wrist-thread (*pratisara*) on the forearm staff. Worship them chanting "Salutation to the bones" (*asthibhyo namaḥ*), and lay the staffs to rest chanting the hymn "Yad vaiṣṇavam...". Lay the Goddesses' armatures to rest chanting their respective mantras, cover them with upper cloths, and arrange for the recitation of the four Vedas in the four directions.
    *   **Sacrificial Fire Offerings:**
        *   Spread the *sabhyāgni* fire, praise the Hotṛ priest's duties, and prepare the auxiliary fires such as *āhavanīya*. Offer oblations while chanting the *Vaiṣṇava-sūkta*, *Viṣṇu-sūkta*, *Puruṣa-sūkta*, and the hymn "Ā tvā hārṣam...", as well as oblations dedicated to Śrī and Bhūmi.
        *   Offer 108 oblations chanting: "To the bones, Svāhā!" (*āsthibhyaḥ svāhā*). Offer the all-deities oblation, and perform the final internal sacrifice (*antarhoma*) in all fires except the *sabhyāgni*. Alternatively, some say one may perform all offerings in the *sabhyāgni* fire alone.

### Section 4: Pedestal Gem Deposit and Armature Assembly (Ratnanyāsa-śūlasaṃyojana-vidhiḥ)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> रात्रिशेषं व्यपोह्य; प्रभाते स्नात्वा, आलयं प्रविश्य, गर्भालाय-पदविभागं (गर्भालाये पदविभागं) कृत्वा, तद्योग्यपदे पीठे रत्नानि विन्यसेत्। यजमानः आचार्यादिभ्यो दक्षिणां दद्यात्।
> *rātriśeṣaṃ vyapohya; prabhāte snātvā, ālayaṃ praviśya, garbhālaya-padavibhāgaṃ kṛtvā, tadyogyapade pīṭhe ratnāni vinyaset. yajamānaḥ ācāryādibhyo dakṣiṇāṃ dadyāt.*
> 
> अग्निं विसृज्य (अग्निंविसृज्य), (शुभ)मुहूर्ते (मुहूर्त्ते) समनुप्राप्ते, शय्यावेदिम् आसाद्य; आचार्यः स्थापकैः सह (स्थापकैस्सह) शूलान् असङ्करम् (शूलानसंकरम्) आहृत्य, शकुनसूक्तं समुच्चार्य, वाद्यघोष-पुरस्सरं सर्वालङ्कार-संयुक्तम् आलयं प्रदक्षिणीकृत्य (प्रदक्षिणीकृत्याऽभ्यन्तरं) प्रविश्य;
> *agniṃ visṛjya, muhūrte samanuprāpte, śayyāvedim āsādya; ācāryaḥ sthāpakaiḥ saha śūlān asaṅkaram āhṛtya, śakunasūktaṃ samuccārya, vādyaghoṣa-purassaraṃ sarvālaṅkāra-saṃyuktam ālayaṃ pradakṣiṇīkṛtya, abhyantaraṃ praviśya;*
> 
> विष्णुसूक्तं विष्णुगायत्रीं च जप्त्वा, ब्रह्मदण्डं स्थाप्य (ब्रह्मदण्डंस्थाप्य); देव्यादीनां तत्तन्-मन्त्रेण तत्तत्-स्थाने स्थापयेत्।
> *viṣṇusūktaṃ viṣṇugāyatrīṃ ca japtvā, brahmadaṇḍaṃ sthāpya; devyādīnāṃ tattan-mantreṇa tattat-sthāne sthāpayet.*
> 
> **शूल-संयोजन-प्रकारः (Assembly of the Armature):**
> वंश-वक्षः-कटी-पार्श्व-ऊरु-दण्डान् (वंशवक्षःकटीपार्श्वोरुदण्डान्) संस्थाप्य, कटिदण्डात् वक्षोदण्डाद् (कटीदण्डाद्वक्षोदण्डा-) अधस्तात् च (दधस्ताच्च) द्वयोः आधार-भित्तिम् आश्रित्य पृष्ठतः तथा (पृष्ठतस्तथा) योजयेत्। आसने वक्षोदण्डाद् अधस्ताद् एकम् आधारं योजयेत्। अथवा भित्तिशूलं मौलिदण्डे संयोज्य आसने च आसनोपरि (संयोज्याऽऽसनेचाऽऽसनोपरि) वंशदण्डं संस्थापयेत्।
> ***śūlasaṃyojanaprakāraḥ -***
> *vaṃśa-vakṣaḥ-kaṭī-pārśva-ūrudaṇḍān saṃsthāpya, kaṭidaṇḍāt vakṣodaṇḍād adhastāc ca dvayoḥ ādhārabhittim āśritya pṛṣṭhatas tathā yojayet. āsane vakṣodaṇḍād adhastād ekam ādhāraṃ yojayet. athavā bhittiśūlaṃ maulidaṇḍe saṃyojya, āsane ca āsanopari vaṃśadaṇḍaṃ saṃsthāpayet.*
> 
> *   **स्थानके (Standing Icon):** पाददण्डौ शिलोपरि दृढं संस्थाप्य (दृढसंस्थाप्य), तयोः कटिदण्डं वंशदण्डं च संस्थाप्य, यावद् दृढतरं तावद् योग्यं शूलं कृत्वा युक्त्या समूहं (समूह) कारयेत्।
> *   **शयने (Reclining Icon):** स्थानकवत् द्वयोः आधारं (द्व्योराधारं) शयनोर्ध्वे संस्थाप्य तस्मिन् स्थापयेत्।
> **   **sthānake** - pādadaṇḍau śilōpari dṛḍhaṃ saṃsthāpya, tayoḥ kaṭidaṇḍaṃ vaṃśadaṇḍaṃ ca saṃsthāpya, yāvad dṛḍhataraṃ tāvad yogyaṃ śūlaṃ kṛtvā yuktyā samūhaṃ kārayet.*
> **   **śayane** - sthānakavat dvayoḥ ādhāraṃ śayanordhve saṃsthāpya tasmin saṃsthāpayet.*

> *paścāc śilpinam āhūya pūjayitvā śiṣṭaṃ sarvaṃ kārayet. paścāt dṛḍhabandhanārthaṃ tāmrapaṭṭaiḥ - pañca-ṣaḍ-yava-vistāraiḥ, vistārāt ṣadbhāga-ghanānvitaiḥ, tāvadāyataiḥ ca - atidṛḍhaṃ baddhvā, aṣṭabandhaṃ nidadhyāt.*

#### English Translation
*   **Grid Installation and Armature Assembly (*Ratnanyāsa-śūlasaṃyojana-vidhiḥ*):**
    *   **Gem Deposit:** In the morning, after the vigil, bathe, enter the temple, lay out the spatial grid of the inner sanctum, and deposit the gems (*ratnāni*) in the designated squares of the pedestal. The patron must distribute fees to the preceptor and priests.
    *   **Entering the Sanctum:** Dismiss the sacrificial fires. At the arrival of the auspicious hour, approach the couch-platform. The preceptor, accompanied by the assisting priests, lifts the armature staffs carefully without mixing them, chants the *Śakuna-sūkta*, circumambulates the decorated temple to the accompaniment of music, and enters the inner sanctum.
    *   **Positioning the Spinal Staff:** Chanting the *Viṣṇu-sūkta* and the *Viṣṇu-Gāyatrī*, erect the main spinal staff (*brahmadaṇḍaṃ/vaṃśadaṇḍaṃ*). Install the consort Goddesses and other retinue deities in their respective places chanting their specific mantras.
    *   **Assembly of the Armature:**
        *   Align and assemble the spinal staff, chest staff, waist staff, side staffs, and thigh staffs. Connect them from behind, anchoring them to the supporting back-wall below the waist and chest staffs.
        *   For a sitting icon, attach a single support anchor below the chest staff. Alternatively, one may connect the wall-armature (*bhitti-śūla*) to the crown staff, and establish the spinal staff upon the pedestal.
        *   For a standing icon (*sthānaka*), fix the feet staffs firmly on the stone pedestal, attach the waist and spinal staffs above them, and ensure the entire frame is as sturdy as possible, assembling them together with reasoning.
        *   For a reclining icon (*śayana*), like the standing form, fix two support anchors above the couch and establish the frame upon them.
    *   **Securing the Joints:** Summon the architect, honor him, and let him complete the remaining carpentry work. To secure the joints firmly, wrap them tightly with copper bands (*tāmrapaṭṭaiḥ*) measuring 5 or 6 barleycorns wide, with a thickness equal to 1/6 of their width, and of suitable length. Then, apply the *Aṣṭabandha* cement.

#### English Translation
*   **Preparation and Consecration of Aṣṭabandha Paste (*Aṣṭabandha-saṃskāra-vidhiḥ*):**
    *   **Proportions:** (Continuing from page 87) Combine 4 parts of pine-resin (*śrīveṣṭaka*), 5 parts of bdellium resin (*guggulu*), 3 parts of bronze/metal filings (*kāṃśyam*), 1/8 part of jaggery (*guḍam*), 3 parts of sal-tree resin (*sajjarasa*), and 3 parts of mineral/lime powder (*dhātucūrṇam*).     *   **Consecration:**
        *   Erect the *āhavanīya* fire-pit in front of the temple, make the initial offerings, and place the *Aṣṭabandha* paste on a cloth over a grain-pedestal to the West of the fire.

(*ato devā avantu naḥ*). Then, let the architect apply it smoothly. The patron should give fees to the preceptor and worship the architect.

#### English Translation
*   **Application of Ropes, Sacred Clay, and Gems (*Rajju-mṛt-saṃskāra-vidhiḥ*):**
    *   **The Rope-Binding Layer (*Rajju-bandhaḥ*):**
        *   Prepare two long ropes made from coconut-husk fibers (*nālikera-tantu*), with a thickness equal to two barleycorns.
        *   Erect the *dakṣiṇāgni* fire-pit, offer the initial oblations, lay the rope on the grain-pedestal, and worship it chanting: "Salutation to the veins" (*sirābhyo namaḥ*). Consecrate it and offer 108 oblations with the mantra: "To the veins, Svāhā!" (*sirābhyaḥ svāhā*).
        *   Dismiss the fire, circumambulate the temple, enter the sanctum, and wrap the rope around the frame starting from the navel, tracing the paths of the fourteen principal yogic channels (*nāḍis*) such as the *suṣumnā*.
        *   Wrap both sides of the frame from head to feet like veins. Cover the palms and soles of the icon with copper plates, wrap the entire frame with cotton threads, and wrap it again with the coconut fiber rope. Pay fees to the preceptor, honor the architect, and perform subsequent rites.
    *   **The Sacred Clay Layer (*Mṛt-saṃskāraḥ*):**
        *   **Clay Selection:** Collect sacred clay from rivers (*nādeyam*), fields, lakes, temple boundaries, sacred rivers, or perennial reservoirs. On an auspicious day, spread it over a new cloth or mat, dry it, sift it, and store it in a new earthen pot in a clean, windless place.
        *   **Clay Decoction:** Mix in fragrant sand, and knead it with decoctions of Khadira and Kakubha bark. Let it sit in water for one month.
        *   **Aqueous Mixtures:** Wet it with decoctions of Aśvattha, Udumbara, Plakṣa, and Nyagrodha bark. Let it sit for half a month, then knead it with Triphalā water. Add stone powder equal to one-fourth of the clay volume, and let it sit for another half-month.
        *   **Binding Agents:** Combine it with flour of barley, wheat, and black gram, along with 1/16 part of pine-resin, bdellium, kuṣṭha herb, and sal resin. Knead it with curd, honey, ghee, linseed oil, sesame oil, and wood-apple pulp. Let it sit for half a month.
        *   **Fine Spices and Powders:** Add turmeric (*rajanī*) and three-spices powder (*katutraya* - ginger, black pepper, and long pepper) equal to 1/10 of the volume, and grind it finely. Add wood-apple resin, haritāla, kuṣṭha, rocanā, and black aloeswood powder, along with gold and silver leaf. Mix in banana pulp, coconut water, and cow's milk. Knead the clay thoroughly. Add small amounts of earth dug up by elephant tusks, bull horns, or crab holes, refine it, powder it, and mix it. Let the compound sit for three nights.
        *   **Consecration:** Under an auspicious star, erect the *gārhapatya* fire-pit, offer the initial oblations, and place the clay on a cloth over a grain-pedestal. Worship it chanting: "Salutation to the flesh" (*māṃsebhyo namaḥ*). Perform the adhivāsa, offer oblations with the *Mahī-sūkta*, *Viṣṇu-sūkta*, *Puruṣa-sūkta*, *Vaiṣṇava-sūkta*, and the sages' mantras, and offer 108 oblations chanting: "To the flesh, Svāhā!" (*māṃsebhyaḥ svāhā*). Honor the preceptor.
        *   **Application:** Meditating on the Lord, touch the clay chanting the single-syllable mantra (*ekākṣara*), and recite the earth hymns. Smear the clay over the head and main limbs of the icon. Let the architect apply it smoothly over the whole body using a spatula (*karaṇḍikā*) rather than bare hands. Bind it with ropes again, dry it, and apply another layer of clay. Do not wrap ropes over this final clay layer.
    *   **The Gem Deposit Layer (*Ratna-nyāsaḥ*):**
        *   Upon the clay layer, deposit the sacred gems while chanting the earth and supreme soul mantras:
            *   At the heart: Ruby (*padmarāgam*).
            *   At the throat: Pearl (*mauktikam*).
            *   At the arms: Cat's eye (*vaiḍūryam*).
            *   At the navel: Diamond/emerald (*brahmamaṇim*).
            *   At the genitals: Gold (*sauvarṇam*).
            *   At the feet: Topaz (*puṣpakam*).
            *   At the right eye: Sunstone (*sūryakāntam*).
            *   At the left eye: Moonstone (*candrakāntam*).
            *   At the right ear: Diamond (*vajram*).
            *   At the left ear: Conch gem/mother of pearl (*śaṅkhajam*).
            *   At the nostrils: Jade (*amṛtāśmakam*).
            *   At the mouth: Coral (*pravālam*).
        *   After a month has passed (*māse atīte*), apply the lime-gravel plaster sealant (*śarkarā-kalkalam*).

> अन्यथा तद्-बेरे देवो न वसेत् (देवो नव सेत्)। चक्षुः-छिद्रे यजमानस्य अन्धत्वं (चक्षुश्छिद्रे यजमानस्यान्धत्वं), कर्ण-छिद्रे बधिरत्वं (कर्णच्छिद्रेबधिरत्वम्), आस्य-छिद्रे मूकत्वं (आस्यच्छिद्रे- मूकत्वं), घ्राणादि-मार्ग-छिद्रे (घ्राणादिमार्गच्छिद्रे) प्रमेह-कुष्ठ-आदि-व्याधयो धान्यादि-नाशश्च अचिराद् एव भवेयुः (धान्यादिनाशश्चाचिरा-देवभवेयुः)। महाङ्ग-उपाङ्ग-प्रत्यङ्ग-भेदैः (महाङ्गोपाङ्गप्रत्यङ्गभेदैः) स्थानभ्रंशो भवेत्। तस्मात् सर्वं परिहृत्य एव (सर्वपरिहृत्यैव) कारयेत्॥
> *anyathā tad-bere devo na vaset. cakṣuḥ-chidre yajamānasya andhatvaṃ, karṇa-chidre badhiratvaṃ, āsya-chidre mūkatvaṃ, ghrāṇādi-mārga-chidre prameha-kuṣṭha-ādi-vyādhayo dhānyādināśaś ca acirād eva bhaveyuḥ. mahāṅga-upāṅga-pratyaṅga-bhedaiḥ sthānabhraṃśo bhavet. tasmāt sarvaṃ parihṛtya eva kārayet.*
> 
> **वर्ण-संस्कार-विधिः (Application of Colors):**
> अथ वर्ण-संस्कारं वक्ष्ये -
> देवं युगानां क्रमेण - (कृतयुगे) श्वेतवर्णं, (त्रेतायुगे) सुवर्णवर्णं, (द्वापरयुगे) श्यामवर्णं, (कलियुगे) अञ्जनवर्णं; (अथवा) सर्वेषु श्याम-वर्णम् एव कारयेत् (सर्वेषुश्यामवर्णमेवकारयेत्)।
> ***varṇasaṃskāravidhiḥ***
> *atha varṇasaṃskāraṃ vakṣye -*
> *devaṃ yugānāṃ krameṇa - śvetavarṇaṃ, suvarṇavarṇaṃ, śyāmavarṇaṃ, añjanavarṇaṃ; sarveṣu śyāmavarṇam eva kārayet.*
> 
> सर्ववर्णान् सङ्ग्रह्य पृथक् पृथक् पात्रे कपित्थ-निर्यास-अम्बुना (कपित्थ-निर्यासाम्बुना) सम्पेष्य गृहीत्वा; पौण्डरीक-अग्निकुण्डं (पौण्डरीकाग्निकुण्डं) कृत्वा, आधारं हुत्वा (आधारंहुत्वा), पूर्ववद् धान्य-पीठे नववस्त्रोपरि वर्णान् संन्यस्य; "जीवाय नमः" (जीवायनमः) इति अभ्यर्च्य, आधिवास्य;
> *sarvavarṇān saṅgṛhya pṛthak pṛthak pātre kapittha-niryāsa-ambunā sampeṣya gṛhītvā; pauṇdarīka-agnikuṇḍaṃ kṛtvā, ādhāraṃ hutvā, pūrvavad dhānyapīṭhe navavastre varṇān sanyasya; "jīvāya namaḥ" iti abhyarcya, ādhivāsya;*
> 
> अग्निं परिस्तीर्य (अग्निंपरि स्तीर्य), वैष्णवं, विष्णुसूक्तं, पुरुषसूक्तं, पारमात्मिकं, पार्षदं (पार्षंदं) च हुत्वा; "जीवाय स्वाहा" (जीवायस्वाहे) इति अष्टोत्तरशतं हुत्वा जुहोति।
> *agniṃ paristīrya, vaiṣṇavaṃ, viṣṇusūktaṃ, puruṣasūktaṃ, pāramātmikaṃ, pārṣadaṃ ca hutvā; "jīvāya svāhā" iti aṣṭottaraśataṃ hutvā juhoti.*
> 
> "हिरण्यगर्भः..." इति मन्त्रेण ("हिरण्यगर्भ" इति) वर्णम् अभिमृश्य (वर्णमभिमृश्य), आत्मसूक्तं जप्त्वा; "अतो देवा अवन्तु नः" इति मन्त्रेण (अतोदेवादिना) हेमपात्रे वर्णं गृहीत्वा, तूर्यघोषं (तूर्यघोषणं) कारयित्वा; विष्णुसूक्तं पुरुषसूक्तं च जप्त्वा, तूलिका-अग्रे वर्णम् आदाय, "सहस्रशीर्षा..." इति मन्त्रेण देवस्य ललाटे संलेपयेत् (ललाटेसंलेपयेत्)।
> *"hiraṇyagarbhaḥ..." iti mantreṇa varṇam abhimṛśya, ātmasūktaṃ japtvā; "ato devā avantu naḥ" iti mantreṇa hemapātre varṇaṃ gṛhītvā, tūryaghoṣaṃ kārayitvā; viṣṇusūktaṃ puruṣasūktaṃ ca japtvā, tūlikā-agre varṇam ādāya, "sahasraśīrṣā..." iti mantreṇa devasya lalāṭe saṃlepayet.*
> 
> आचार्यं सम्पूजयेत् (आचार्या संपूजयेत्)। पुण्याहं वाचयित्वा, अग्निं परिस्तीर्य (अग्निपरिस्तीर्य) शान्तिहोमं हुत्वा, अग्निं विसृज्य (अग्निंविसृज्य);
> *ācāryaṃ sampūjayet. puṇyāhaṃ vācayitvā, agniṃ paristīrya śāntihomaṃ hutvā, agniṃ visṛjya; paścāc śilpinaṃ sampūjya, tena tattad-aṅga-anurūpaṃ varṇaṃ saṃlepayet. atyantaṃ darśanīyaṃ yathokta-vidhinā saṃlekṣya, paścād evam eva saṃskāraṃ kṛtvā, suvarṇa-sahitaṃ varṇaṃ saṃlepayet. tasmād śakti-yutaṃ bhavati iti vijñāyate.*

#### English Translation
*   **Lime-Gravel Plaster, Cloth Wrapping, and Painting (*Śarkarā-paṭācchādana-varṇa-saṃskāra-vidhiḥ*):**
    *   **The Lime-Gravel Layer (*Śarkarā-lepanam*):**
        *   **Preparation:** Under an auspicious star, dig up earth near a riverbank or in some other clean, delightful spot. Wash, dry, and powder it. Sprinkle it with Triphalā water, dry it gradually, and knead it with wood-apple resin water. Combine it with fine cotton fibers to form an extremely fine, smooth paste.
        *   **Consecration:** Erect the *āvasathya* fire-pit, offer initial oblations, and place the lime-gravel plaster on a new cloth over the grain altar. Worship it chanting: "Salutation to the blood" (*rudhirāya namaḥ*). Establish its adhivāsa. Perform fire sacrifices with the *Vaiṣṇava-sūkta*, *Viṣṇu-sūkta*, *Puruṣa-sūkta*, *Śrī-sūkta*, and peace offerings. Offer 108 oblations chanting: "To the blood, Svāhā!" (*rudhirāya svāhā*). Offer final oblations to Viṣṇu, Śrī, Hariṇī, for long life, and to the retinue. Dismiss the fire.
        *   **Application:** Pay fees to the preceptor. Touch the plaster chanting the mantra "Agnirindraḥ..." (Agni and Indra) and apply it chanting the *Puruṣa-sūkta*. Let the architect smear the paste over the entire body of the icon. Let it dry for one month. Apply further coats of the paste mixed with resin water to ensure all primary and secondary limbs are perfectly complete, without cracks or breaks.
    *   **The Cloth-Wrapping Layer (*Paṭācchādanam*):**
        *   Take a fine sheet of pure hand-woven cotton cloth. Erect the *sabhyāgni* fire-pit, place the cloth on the grain-pedestal, and worship it chanting: "Salutation to the skin" (*tvace namaḥ*). Consecrate it and offer 1000 oblations, and 108 oblations chanting: "To the skin, Svāhā!" (*tvace svāhā*). Dismiss the fire and pay fees to the preceptor.
        *   Sprinkle the cloth with water chanting the *Viṣṇu-Gāyatrī*, and wrap it tightly around the icon without any gaps chanting the mantra: "Thou art the body of Soma" (*somasya tanurasi*). Let the architect seal it with a coat of plaster paste, and then adorn it with ornaments.
        *   For stone and wooden icons, apply the lime-gravel plaster in this same manner. Cover the outer parts with pearl and clay pastes, ensuring that the eyes, ears, mouth, nostrils, and all main and minor limbs are completely unbroken, not deficient, and not excessive, modeled precisely as before.
        *   **Consequences of Defective Work:** If these details are neglected, the deity will not reside in the icon. A defect in the eyes causes blindness in the patron; a defect in the ears causes deafness; a defect in the mouth causes muteness; a defect in the nostrils causes diseases like diabetes or leprosy, and rapid loss of wealth. Disproportions in the limbs cause displacement from one's home/status. Therefore, execute the work avoiding all such errors.
    *   **The Painting Layer (*Varṇa-saṃskāraḥ*):**
        *   **Colors of the Deity:** According to the cosmic ages (*yugas*), the Lord's color should be: white in the Kṛta age, golden in the Tretā age, dark/green in the Dvāpara age, and black/collyrium in the Kali age. Alternatively, in all ages, one may paint the Lord in a beautiful dark complexion (*śyāma-varṇam*).
        *   **Consecration of Colors:** Collect all colors, grind them separately in vessels using wood-apple resin water. Erect the *pauṇdarīka* fire-pit, place the colors on a cloth over the grain-pedestal, and worship them chanting: "Salutation to the soul/life" (*jīvāya namaḥ*). Consecrate them, perform fire sacrifices, and offer 108 oblations chanting: "To the soul/life, Svāhā!" (*jīvāya svāhā*).
        *   **Painting the Eyes/Face:** Touch the colors chanting "Hiraṇyagarbhaḥ...", recite the self-hymn (*ātma-sūkta*), and take up the colors in a golden vessel. To the accompaniment of music, recite the *Viṣṇu-sūkta* and *Puruṣa-sūkta*. Taking up paint on the tip of the brush (*tūlikā-agre*), apply it first to the deity's forehead chanting: "The thousand-headed..." (*sahasraśīrṣā*).
        *   **Completion:** Honor the preceptor. Recite purification prayers, perform the peace fire sacrifice, and dismiss the fire. Honor the architect, and let him paint the entire icon in accordance with the colors of the limbs. Once it is beautifully illustrated, apply a final coating mixed with gold leaf (*suvarṇa-sahita-varṇam*). Thus, the icon becomes fully infused with divine power (*śakty-yutam*).
    *   Thus concludes the Eighteenth Chapter (*Aṣṭādaśaḥ Paṭalaḥ*) of the *Vimānārcanākalpa*, titled "Rules for Consecrating the Armature and Colors".
