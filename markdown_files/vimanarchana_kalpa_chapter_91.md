# Vimanarchana Kalpa of Sage Marīci (Vaikhānasa Āgama)
## Chapter 91: Hṛd-padma-madhya-bhagavad-dhyāna-vidhiḥ (Rules for Meditating on the Lord in the Center of the Heart-Lotus)

This document contains the Sanskrit text, IAST transliteration, and English translation of the 91st Chapter (*Ekanavatitamaḥ Paṭalaḥ*) of the **Vimānārcanākalpa** (attributed to Sage Marīci, representing the Vaikhānasa school of Vaishnavism).

This chapter explains the practice of meditating on the Supreme Soul (*paramātmā*), Viṣṇu, in the heart-lotus. It describes the physical-spiritual structure of the heart-lotus with its eight petals representing the *aṣṭa-siddhis* (eight spiritual powers) and its pericarp of Prakriti. It details how the lotus blossoms and turns upward through pranayama, revealing the inner fire, the golden flame (quoting the *Mahānārāyaṇa Upaniṣad*), and the four-armed deity Viṣṇu. It concludes by linking this inner meditation to the Vedic authority of Rigvedic hymns and the absolute necessity of daily worship (*viṣṇv-arcana*) for the twice-born (*dvija*).

### Section 1: The Heart Lotus, Inner Flame, and the Form of Viṣṇu

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **अथ एकनवतितमः पटलः**
> **हृत्पद्ममध्य-भगवद्ध्यान-विधिः**
> ***atha ēkanavatitamaḥ paṭalaḥ***
> ***hṛtpadmamadhya-bhagavaddhyāna-vidhiḥ***
> 
> अथ कन्दादुत्थितं द्वादशाङ्गुलं सुषुम्नानाड्यन्तं अणिमा-महिमा-लघिमा-गरिमा-प्राप्ति-प्राकाम्य-ईशित्व-वशित्वम् इत्यष्टदलोपेतं प्रकृत्यात्मक-कर्णिकं विद्याकेसर-संयुक्तं अधोमुखं हृदयकमलं प्राणायामेन विकसितं ऊर्ध्वमुखं भवति । तस्यान्तःकर्णिकामध्ये महानग्निर्ज्वलन् विश्वार्चिर्विश्वतोमुखः आपादतलमस्तकं सन्तापयति । तन्मध्ये पीताभा नीवारशूकवत्तन्वी वह्निशिखा, तस्याः शिखाया मध्ये प्रज्वलित-ज्योतिःस्वरूपमेव स्वसङ्कल्पेन तप्तजाम्बूनदप्रभः पीताम्बरधरः पद्माक्षः रक्तास्य-पाणि-पादः चतुर्भुजः चक्र-शङ्ख-धरोऽभय-कट्यवलम्बित-हस्तः श्रीवत्साङ्कः सुप्रसन्नः शुचिस्मितः सर्वाभरणभूषितः हृदि स्थितः परमात्मा ॥
> *atha kandādutthitaṃ dvādaśāṅgulaṃ suṣumnānāḍyantaṃ aṇimā-mahimā-laghimā-garimā-prāpti-prākāmya-īśitva-vaśitvam ityaṣṭadalōpētaṃ prakṛtyātmakakarṇikaṃ vidyākēsarasaṃyuktaṃ adhōmuxaṃ hṛdayakamalaṃ prāṇāyāmēna vikasitaṃ ūrdhvamuxaṃ bhavati | tasyāntaḥkarṇikāmadhyē mahānagnirjvalan viśvārcirviśvatōmuxaḥ āpādatalamastakaṃ santāpayati | tanmadhyē pītābhā nīvāraśūkavattanvī vahnīśikhā, tasyāḥ śikhāyā madhyē prajvalitajyōtiḥsvarūpamēva svasaṅkalpēna taptajāmbūnadaprabhaḥ pītāmbaradharaḥ padmāxaḥ raktāsyapāṇipādaḥ caturbhujaḥ cakra-śaṅkha-dharō'bhaya-kaṭyavalambita-hastaḥ śrīvatsāṅkaḥ suprasannaḥ śucismitaḥ sarvābharaṇabhūṣitaḥ hṛdi sthitaḥ paramātmā ||*

#### English Translation
*   **The Structure of the Heart-Lotus:** Now, rising from the *kanda* (bulb of energy) at a distance of twelve angulas and extending inside the *suṣumnā* channel (*atha kandādutthitaṃ dvādaśāṅgulaṃ suṣumnānāḍyantaṃ*), is the heart-lotus. It is endowed with eight petals representing the eight spiritual powers beginning with *aṇimā* (namely: *aṇimā*, *mahimā*, *laghimā*, *garimā*, *prāpti*, *prākāmya*, *īśitva*, and *vaśitva*) (*aṇimā-mahimā-laghimā-garimā-prāpti-prākāmya-īśitva-vaśitvam ityaṣṭadalōpētaṃ*). It has a pericarp representing Prakriti (*prakṛtyātmakakarṇikaṃ*) and is associated with the filaments of Vidya/knowledge (*vidyākēsarasaṃyuktaṃ*).

*   **Blossoming of the Lotus:** Normally facing downwards (*adhōmuxaṃ*), this heart-lotus blossomed and turned upwards through the practice of pranayama (*hṛdayakamalaṃ prāṇāyāmēna vikasitaṃ ūrdhvamuxaṃ bhavati*).

*   **The Inner Blazing Fire:** In the middle of its pericarp, a great fire burns (*tasyāntaḥkarṇikāmadhyē mahānagnirjvalan*), whose rays are all-pervasive and face all directions (*viśvārcirviśvatōmuxaḥ*), heating/illuminating the entire body from the soles of the feet to the head (*āpādatalamastakaṃ santāpayati*).

*   **The Slender Flame:** In the center of that fire is a golden/yellow flame, as slender as the awn of a wild rice grain (*tanmadhyē pītābhā nīvāraśūkavattanvī vahnīśikhā*).

*   **The Supreme Lord in the Flame:** In the middle of that flame resides the Supreme Soul (*tasyāḥ śikhāyā madhyē ... paramātmā*), who is the self-effulgent light itself (*prajvalitajyōtiḥsvarūpamēva*), manifested by his own will (*svasaṅkalpēna*). He shines like molten gold (*taptajāmbūnadaprabhaḥ*), wears yellow garments (*pītāmbaradharaḥ*), is lotus-eyed (*padmāxaḥ*), and has reddish face, hands, and feet (*raktāsyapāṇipādaḥ*).

*   **Divine Iconography:** He is four-armed, holding the discus and conch, with one hand showing the gesture of protection and another resting on his hip (*caturbhujaḥ cakra-śaṅkha-dharō'bhaya-kaṭyavalambita-hastaḥ*). He is marked with the Shrivatsa (*śrīvatsāṅkaḥ*), is highly gracious (*suprasannaḥ*), has a pure smile (*śucismitaḥ*), is adorned with all ornaments (*sarvābharaṇabhūṣitaḥ*), and remains seated in the heart (*hṛdi sthitaḥ*).

### Section 2: Consorts, Scriptural Authorities, and Worship

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> दक्षिणवामयोः (श्रीभूमिभ्यां) विभूति-मायाभ्यां परिषत्कैः सह संस्थितः । “नीवारशूकवत्तन्वी पीता भास्वत्यणूपमा । तस्याः शिखाया मध्ये परमात्मा व्यवस्थितः” इति श्रुतिः । तं विष्णुं जिज्ञासुः ध्यानयोगेन ज्ञानचक्षुषा पश्येत् ।
> *daxiṇavāmayōḥ (śrībhūmibhyāṃ) vibhūti-māyābhyāṃ pariṣatkaiḥ saha saṃsthitaḥ | “nīvāraśūkavattanvī pītā bhāsvatyaṇūpamā | tasyāḥ śikhāyā madhyē paramātmā vyavasthitaḥ” iti śrutiḥ | taṃ viṣṇuṃ jijñāsuḥ dhyānayōgēna jñānacaxuṣā paśyēt |*
> 
> “तद्विष्णोः परमं पदं सदा पश्यन्ति सूरयः” इति श्रुतिः । विश्वव्यापिनं विष्णुं ध्यानमथनेनाऽविर्भूत-तेजोभासुरं भक्त्या सकलं सङ्कल्प्य, आवाह्य अर्चयेदिति । “प्र वः पान्तमन्धसो धियायते महे विष्णवे चार्चत” इति श्रुतिः । तस्मात् विष्ण्वर्चनमेव द्विजैः अहरहः कर्तव्यमिति विज्ञायते ॥
> *“tadviṣṇōḥ paramaṃ padaṃ sadā paśyanti sūrayaḥ” iti śrutiḥ | viśvavyāpinaṃ viṣṇuṃ dhyānamathanēnā'virbhūta-tējōbhāsuraṃ bhaktyā sakalaṃ saṅkalpya, āvāhya arcayēditi | “pra vaḥ pāntamandhasō dhiyāyatē mahē viṣṇavē cārcata” iti śrutiḥ | tasmāt viṣṇvarcanamēva dvijaiḥ aharahaḥ kartavyamiti vijñāyatē ||*

#### English Translation
*   **Consorts and Attendants:** On his right and left, he is well-seated along with Śrī and Bhū (or Vibhūti and Māyā) and his retinue (*daxiṇavāmayōḥ śrībhūmibhyāṃ vibhūti-māyābhyāṃ pariṣatkaiḥ saha saṃsthitaḥ*).

*   **The Upanishadic Witness:** The Vedic scripture declares: *"Slender as the awn of a wild rice grain, golden, brilliant and extremely subtle; in the middle of that flame, the Supreme Soul is established"* (*“nīvāraśūkavattanvī pītā bhāsvatyaṇūpamā | tasyāḥ śikhāyā madhyē paramātmā vyavasthitaḥ”*). One who desires to know (*jijñāsuḥ*) should perceive that Viṣṇu with the eye of knowledge through the yoga of meditation (*taṃ viṣṇuṃ dhyānayōgēna jñānacaxuṣā paśyēt*).

*   **Worship of the All-Pervading:** The scripture also says: *"The wise always behold that supreme state of Viṣṇu"* (*“tadviṣणōḥ paramaṃ padaṃ sadā paśyanti sūrayaḥ”*). Having envisioned with devotion the all-pervading Viṣṇu, who is brilliant with the light manifested through the churning of meditation, in a manifest form (*viśvavyāpinaṃ viṣṇuṃ dhyānamathanēnā'virbhūta-tējōbhāsuraṃ bhaktyā sakalaṃ saṅkalpya*), one should invoke and worship him (*āvāhya arcayēditi*).

*   **Daily Devotion:** The scripture declares: *"Pour out for Him the offering, think of Him with meditation, and worship the great Viṣṇu"* (*“pra vaḥ pāntamandhasō dhiyāyatē mahē viṣṇavē cārcata”*). Therefore, it is understood that twice-born householders must perform the worship of Viṣṇu daily (*tasmāt viṣṇvarcanamēva dvijaiḥ aharahaḥ kartavyamiti vijñāyatē*).

### Glossary of Key Terms
*   **Hṛd-padma (हृत्पद्म):** The heart-lotus, located at the heart center (*anāhata*), containing the seat of the Supreme Lord.
*   **Aṣṭa-siddhi (अष्टसिद्धि):** The eight classical occult powers: *aṇimā* (minuteness), *mahimā* (greatness), *laghimā* (lightness), *garimā* (heaviness), *prāpti* (attainment), *prākāmya* (willpower), *īśitva* (lordship), and *vaśitva* (control).
*   **Vidyā-kēsara (विद्याकेसर):** The filaments of the heart-lotus, symbolizing spiritual knowledge or Vidya.
*   **Prāṇāyāma (प्राणायाम):** Breath control; the yogic practice through which the downward-facing heart lotus is made to blossom and turn upward.
*   **Nīvāra-śūka (नीवारशूक):** The awn of a grain of wild rice, used in the Upanishads as a metaphor for extreme slenderness and subtlety.
*   **Taptajāmbūnada-prabha (तप्तजाम्बूनदप्रभ):** Possessing the bright, pure lustre of molten gold.
*   **Dhyāna-mathana (ध्यानमथन):** The "churning of meditation," a metaphor comparing concentration of the mind to the churning of milk/wood to manifest divinity.
*   **Viṣṇv-arcana (विष्ण्वर्चन):** The ritual worship of Viṣṇu, emphasized here as the primary daily duty of twice-born householders (*dvija*).
