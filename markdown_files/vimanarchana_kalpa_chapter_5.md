# Vimanarchana Kalpa of Sage Marīci (Vaikhānasa Āgama)
## Chapter 5: Nava-Ṣaṭ-Pañca-Mūrti-Taruṇālaya-Pratiṣṭhā-Vidhiḥ (Consecration of Temporary Temples for Nine, Six, or Five Icons)

This document contains the Sanskrit text, IAST transliteration, and English translation of the 5th Chapter (*Pañcamaḥ Paṭalaḥ*) of the **Vimānārcanākalpa** (attributed to Sage Marīci, representing the Vaikhānasa school of Vaishnavism).

This chapter details the rules for establishing a temporary temple (*taruṇālaya* or *bālālaya*) housing multiple deities—specifically nine, six, or five icons. It describes the geometric grid layout of the temple, the precise positioning and orientation of each of the nine deities (Viṣṇu, Puruṣa, Satya, Acyuta, Aniruddha, Varāha, Nārasiṃha, Nara, and Nārāyaṇa), the setup of their individual sacrificial chambers and fire altars, the bathing rites, and the final installation and daily worship guidelines. It also provides alternative instructions for simpler six-icon or five-icon layouts.

### Section 1: Nine-Deity Temporary Temple Layout

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **अथ पञ्चमः पटलः**
> **नव-षट्-पञ्च-मूर्ति-तरुणालय-विशेषः**

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **atha pañcamaḥ paṭalaḥ**
> **nava-ṣaṭ-pañca-mūrti-taruṇālaya-viśeṣaḥ**

**Translation (English):**
*   **Site and Layout of the Nine-Deity Temporary Temple:**
    *   In the previously mentioned site, without exceeding the temple enclosure walls (*prākāra*), construct a temporary temple (*taruṇālaya*) measuring nine cubits in length and width.
    *   Divide the site into eighty-one grid squares (*ekāśīti-vibhāga*).
    *   In the center of this grid, the nine central squares constitute the inner sanctum (*garbhagāra*).
    *   The surrounding sixteen squares constitute the inner wall (*abhyantara-bhitti*).
    *   Outside that, the twenty-four squares constitute the verandah/gallery (*alinda*).
    *   Outside that, the outer thirty-two squares constitute the outer wall (*bāhya-bhitti*).
    *   In that outer wall, there should be four doors (one in each of the four directions) or a single door, with lattice windows (*jālaka*) on the other sides.
    *   The front porch (*mukha-maṇṭapa*) should measure equal to, three-quarters, or half the width of the sanctum.

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **नवमूर्तिबालालयविधिः**
> पूर्वोक्ते देशे तस्य प्राकारान् नातिक्रम्य नवहस्तायतविस्तारं तरुणालयं कृत्वा, तस्मिन् एकाशीतिविभागं कृत्वा, तन्मध्ये नवभागं गर्भगारं, तद्बहिः षोडशभागम् अभ्यन्तरभित्तिः, तद्बहिः चतुर्विंशतिभागम् अलिन्दं, तद्बहिः द्वात्रिंशद्भागं बाह्यभित्तिः; तद्भित्तौ (चतुर्दिक्षु) चतुर्द्वारयुतम् एकद्वारयुतं वा, अन्यासु जालकं वा, समं त्रिपादम् अर्धं वा मुखमण्टपम्। गर्भगारं सम-सम-विभागं कृत्वा, ब्राह्मे विष्णुमूर्तिं, तत्पश्चिमे मानुषभागे तृतीयतले कौतुकस्य द्वितालोच्छ्रायं पीठं कृत्वा; अलिन्दे द्वारदक्षिणे पुरुषमूर्तिं प्राङ्मुखम्, अलिन्दे दक्षिणाभिमुखं सत्यं, तथा पश्चिमे पश्चिमाभिमुखम् अच्युतम्, उत्तरे च उत्तराभिमुखम् अनिरुद्धं, तदलिन्दे अनिरुद्धमूर्तेः वामपार्श्वे द्वितीयतले वराहम् उत्तराभिमुखम्, अच्युतमूर्तेः उत्तरपार्श्वे पश्चिमाभिमुखं नारसिंहं, सत्यमूर्तेः पश्चिमतो दक्षिणाभिमुखौ नरनारायणौ च कृत्वा, एवं नवमूर्तिप्रतिष्ठाक्रमेण प्रतिष्ठापयेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **navamūrtibālālayavidhiḥ**
> pūrvolkte deśe tasya prākārān nātikramya navahāstayatavistāraṃ taruṇālayaṃ kṛtvā, tasminn ekāśītivibhāgaṃ kṛtvā, tanmadhye navabhāgaṃ garbhagāraṃ, tadbahiḥ ṣoḍaśabhāgam abhyantarabhittiḥ, tadbahiḥ caturviṃśatibhāgam alindaṃ, tadbahiḥ dvātriṃśadbhāgaṃ bāhyabhittiḥ; tadbhittau (caturdikṣu) caturdvārayutam ekadvārayutaṃ vā, anyāsu jālakaṃ vā, samaṃ tripādam ardhaṃ vā mukhamanṭapam. garbhagāraṃ sama-sama-vibhāgaṃ kṛtvā, brāhme viṣṇumūrtiṃ, tatpaścime mānuṣabhāge tṛtīyatale kautukasya dvitālocchrāyaṃ pīṭhaṃ kṛtvā; alinde dvāradakṣiṇe puruṣamūrtiṃ prāṅmukham, alinde dakṣiṇābhimukhaṃ satyaṃ, tathā paścime paścimābhimukham acyutaṃ, uttare ca uttarābhimukham aniruddhaṃ, tadalinde aniruddhamūrter vāmapārśve dvitīyatale varāham uttarābhimukham, acyutamūrter uttarapārśve paścimābhimukhaṃ nārasiṃhaṃ, satyamūrteḥ paścimato dakṣiṇābhimukhau nara-nārāyaṇau ca kṛtvā, evaṃ navamūrtipratiṣṭhākrameṇa pratiṣṭhāpayet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Postures and Orientations of the Nine Deities:**
    *   Having divided the inner sanctum into equal parts, place the primary icon **Viṣṇu-mūrti** (*Ādimūrti*) in the central *Brāhma* division.
    *   To the West of it, in the *Mānuṣa* zone, on the third level/tier (*tṛtīya-tale*), make a pedestal of two spans high for the worship icon (*kautuka*).
    *   In the verandah, to the right of the doorway, place **Puruṣa-mūrti** facing East.
    *   In the verandah, place **Satya-mūrti** facing South.
    *   Similarly, in the West, place **Acyuta-mūrti** facing West.
    *   In the North, place **Aniruddha-mūrti** facing North.
    *   In the verandah, on the left side of Aniruddha-mūrti, on the second level/tier (*dvitīya-tale*), place **Varāha-mūrti** facing North.
    *   On the North side of Acyuta-mūrti, place **Nārasiṃha-mūrti** facing West.
    *   To the West of Satya-mūrti, place **Nara and Nārāyaṇa** facing South.
    *   Having positioned them thus, one should consecrate them according to the rules of the nine-deity consecration (*nava-mūrti-pratiṣṭhā-krama*).

### Section 2: Six-Deity and Five-Deity Layouts

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **षण्मूर्तिबालालयविधिः**
> षण्मूर्तिप्रतिष्ठा चेत् - एवम् एव बालागारं कृत्वा, वाराहं नारसिंहं नर-नारायणौ च हित्वा, विष्ण्वादि-पञ्चमूर्तिं तृतीयतल-कौतुकं च तत्क्रमेण प्रतिष्ठापयेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **ṣaṇmūrtibālālayavidhiḥ**
> ṣaṇmūrtipratiṣṭhā cet - evam eva bālāgāraṃ kṛtvā, varāhaṃ nārasiṃhaṃ nara-nārāyaṇau ca hitvā, viṣṇvādi-pañcamūrtiṃ tṛtīyatala-kautukaṃ ca tatkrameṇa pratiṣṭhāpayet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Six-Deity Temporary Temple Protocol:**
    *   If consecrating six deities, construct the temporary temple in the same manner, but omit Varāha, Nārasiṃha, and Nara-Nārāyaṇa.
    *   One should install the five deities starting with Viṣṇu, along with the Kautuka icon on the third level, in that sequence.

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **पञ्चमूर्तिबालालयविधिः**
> पञ्चमूर्तिप्रतिष्ठा चेत् - तथैव बालागारं कृत्वा, तृतीयतले पञ्चमूर्तिकौतुकं वाराहादींश्च हित्वा, विष्ण्वादि-पञ्चमूर्तीः तत्क्रमेण प्रतिष्ठापयेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **pañcamūrtibālālayavidhiḥ**
> pañcamūrtipratiṣṭhā cet - tathaiva bālāgāraṃ kṛtvā, tṛtīyatale pañcamūrtikautukaṃ varāhādīṃś ca hitvā, viṣṇvādi-pañcamūrtīḥ tatkrameṇa pratiṣṭhāpayet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Five-Deity Temporary Temple Protocol:**
    *   If consecrating five deities, construct the temporary temple in the same way, but omit the five Kautuka icons on the third level, as well as Varāha and the others.
    *   One should install the five deities starting with Viṣṇu in that sequence.

### Section 3: Sacrificial Setup, Water Pots, and Ritual Bathing

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **नवमूर्त्यादिबालालयप्रतिष्ठाविधिः**
> एतेषां बालबेराणि पूर्ववत् कृत्वा, प्रत्येकम् अक्ष्युन्मेष-अधिवासनादि कृत्वा; आदिमूर्तेः यागशालाभिमुखे, तत्प्राच्यां पुरुषमूर्तेः, तत्प्राच्यां तृतीयतले कौतुकस्य; आलयात् दक्षिणे सत्यमूर्तेः यागशालां, दक्षिणे नरनारायणयोः; आलयात् पश्चिमे अच्युतमूर्तेः यागशालाम्, तत्पश्चिमे नृसिंहस्य; आलयात् उत्तरे अनिरुद्धमूर्तेः यागशालाम्, तदुत्तरे वाराहस्य च; एवं षोडश-द्वादश-अष्ट-चतुः-स्तम्भयुक्तां कृत्वा, प्रत्येकं तोरणपूर्ण-कुम्भवितान-ध्वज-दर्भ-मालाद्यैः अलङ्कृत्य; मध्ये शय्यावेदिं चतुः-त्रि-द्वि-हस्तायतविस्तारां तत्-तुरीयांशोत्सेधां चतुरश्रां च कारयेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **navamūrtyādibālālayapratiṣṭhāvidhiḥ**
> eteṣāṃ bālaberāṇi pūrvavat kṛtvā, pratyekam akṣyunmeṣa-adhivāsanādi kṛtvā; ādimūrteḥ yāgaśālābhimukhe, tatprācyāṃ puruṣamūrteḥ, tatprācyāṃ tṛtīyatale kautukasya; ālayāt dakṣiṇe satyamūrteḥ yāgaśālāṃ, dakṣiṇe nara-nārāyaṇayoḥ; ālayāt paścime acyutamūrteḥ yāgaśālām, tatpaścime nṛsiṃhasya; ālayāt uttare aniruddhamūrteḥ yāgaśālām, taduttare vārāhasya ca; evaṃ ṣoḍaśa-dvādaśa-aṣṭa-catuḥ-stambhayuktāṃ kṛtvā, pratyekaṃ toraṇapūrṇa-kumbhavitāna-dhvaja-darbha-mālādyaiḥ alaṅkṛtya; madhye śayyāvedīṃ catuḥ-tri-dvi-hastāyatavistārāṃ tatturīyāṃśotsedhāṃ caturaśrāṃ ca kārayet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Preparation of Icons and Setup of Sacrificial Chambers:**
    *   Having prepared the temporary icons (*bālabera*) of these deities as detailed before, and performed eye-opening (*akṣy-unmeṣa*), preliminary consecration (*adhivāsa*), etc., for each of them;
    *   Erect the individual sacrificial chambers (*yāga-śālā*):
        *   Facing the **Ādimūrti** (Viṣṇu), in the East: for **Puruṣa-mūrti**.
        *   East of that, on the third level: for the **Kautuka** icon.
        *   To the South of the temple: for **Satya-mūrti**.
        *   South of that: for **Nara-Nārāyaṇa**.
        *   To the West of the temple: for **Acyuta-mūrti**.
        *   West of that: for **Nṛsiṃha**.
        *   To the North of the temple: for **Aniruddha-mūrti**.
        *   North of that: for **Varāha**.
    *   Construct these chambers with sixteen, twelve, eight, or four pillars respectively, and decorate each with archways, pot canopies, flags, Kusa grass, and garlands.
    *   In the center of each chamber, construct a square bed-altar (*śayyā-vedī*) measuring four, three, or two cubits in length and width, and one-fourth of that in height.

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> आदिमूर्तेः पञ्चाग्नीन् पौण्डरीकाग्निं च कृत्वा, अनन्तशयनस्य नृसिंहस्य च गार्हपत्यं, नरनारायणयोः पौण्डरीकं कारयेत्; आदिमूर्तेः शयानस्य च प्रत्येकं सभ्याग्निम् एकमेव कारयेत् इति वदन्ति केचित्। तत्तद्यागशालायां प्रत्येकं स्नानश्वभ्रं च कृत्वा, आलयस्य उत्तरे वास्तुहोमम् एकमेव कृत्वा, गर्भगाराणि पर्यग्नि-पञ्चगव्याभ्यां संशोध्य, पुण्याहं वाचयित्वा, सर्वेषु अग्निकुण्डेषु आघारं हुत्वा, आचार्यः तद्बेराभिमुखे प्रतिष्ठोक्तविधिना कुम्भान् सम्पूज्य, आवाह्य, सप्तभिः कलशैः बेराणि संस्नाप्य, तत्तद्वेद्यां धान्योपरि 'वेदाहमेतम्' इति अण्डजादीनि पञ्चशयनानि वासांसि वा आस्तीर्य, सर्वान् देवान् तत्तत्कुम्भं च तत्तद्वेद्यां समारोप्य, पुण्याहं वाचयित्वा प्रतिसरं बद्ध्वा, 'यद्वैष्णवम्' इति यद्दिग्द्वारं तद्दिङ्मौलिशाययेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> ādimūrteḥ pañcāgnīn pauṇḍarīkāgniṃ ca kṛtvā, anantaśayanasya nṛsiṃhasya ca gārhapatyaṃ, nara-nārāyaṇayoḥ pauṇḍarīkaṃ kārayet; ādimūrteḥ śayanasya ca pratyekaṃ sabhyāgnim ekameva kārayet iti vadanti kecit. tattadyāgaśālāyāṃ pratyekaṃ snānaśvabhraṃ ca kṛtvā, ālayasya uttare vāstuhomam ekameva kṛtvā, garbhagārāṇi paryagni-pañcagavyābhyāṃ saṃśodhya, puṇyāhaṃ vācayitvā, sarveṣu agnikuṇḍeṣu āghāraṃ hutvā, ācāryaḥ tadberābhimukhe pratiṣṭhoktavidhinā kumbhān saṃpūjya, āvāhya, saptabhiḥ kalaśaiḥ berāṇi saṃsnāpya, tattadvedyāṃ dhānyopari 'vedāham etam' iti aṇḍajādīni pañcaśayanāni vāsāṃsi vā āstīrya, sarvān devān tattatkumbhaṃ ca tattadvedyāṃ samāropya, puṇyāhānte pratisaraṃ baddhvā, 'yad vaiṣṇavam' iti yaddigdvāraṃ taddiṅmauliśāyayet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Sacrificial Fires and Purification Rites:**
    *   For the **Ādimūrti**, establish five fires (*pañcāgni*) and the *Pauṇḍarīka* fire.
    *   For **Ananta-śayana** (reclining Viṣṇu) and **Nṛsiṃha**, set up the *Gārhapatya* fire.
    *   For **Nara-Nārāyaṇa**, set up the *Pauṇḍarīka* fire.
    *   Some say that for the reclining form of the **Ādimūrti**, one should establish only a single *Sabhya* fire for each.
    *   In each sacrificial chamber, construct a bathing pit (*snāna-śvabhra*).
    *   Perform a single Vāstu-homa to the North of the temple.
    *   Purify the inner sanctums (*garbhagāra*) with fire and *Pañcagavya*, and perform the *Puṇyāha-vācana* purification rite.
    *   Having offered the initial ghee oblations (*āghāra*) in all the fire pits, the Acharya, in front of the respective icons, should worship the water pots (*kumbha*) according to the consecration rules, and invoke the deities into them.
    *   Bathe the icons with seven pots of water (*saptabhiḥ kalaśaiḥ*).
    *   On the respective altars, on top of grains, spread five beds/sheets (*pañca-śayana*) made of bird down (*aṇḍaja-ādīni*) or garments, while reciting the mantra starting with *"vedāham etam..."*.
    *   Place all the deities along with their respective water pots on their respective bed-altars. At the end of the *Puṇyāha* ritual, tie the protective threads (*pratisara*).
    *   Reciting the hymn *"yad vaiṣṇavam..."*, lay them down to sleep on the beds with their heads pointing towards whichever direction the door of that shrine faces.

### Section 4: Fire Sacrifices, Morning Procession, and Final Installation

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> तत्तत्प्रधानाग्नौ हौत्रं प्रशंस्य, तत्तन्मूर्त्तिमन्त्रैः तत्तत्परिषद्देवानाम् आवाहनादीनि कारयेत्। आदिमूर्तेः अनन्तशयनस्य च सभ्याग्नौ वैष्णवं, विष्णुसूक्तं, पुरुषसूक्तं, श्रीभूमिदैवत्यं, सर्वदैवत्यं च हुत्वा; पुरुषमूर्तेः आहवनीये पुरुषसूक्तं; सत्यमूर्तेः अन्वाहार्ये विष्णुसूक्तं; अच्युतमूर्तेः गार्हपत्ये वैष्णवं; अनिरुद्धमूर्तेः आवसथ्ये रुद्रसूक्तम् एकाक्षरं च चतुष्कृत्वो हुत्वा; वाराहस्य सभ्याग्नौ 'क्षमामेकाम्' इति मन्त्रेण अष्टाधिकशतं हुत्वा; नृसिंहस्य गार्हपत्याग्नौ 'यो वा नृसिंहः' इति एकं; नरनारायणयोः पौण्डरीकाग्नौ 'सत्यः सत्यस्थः' इति मन्त्रं विष्णुगायत्रीं च अष्टोत्तरशतं हुत्वा, अत्रानुक्तं सर्वं महाप्रतिष्ठोक्तक्रमेण कृत्वा, रात्रिशेषं व्यपोह्य...

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> tattatpradhānāgnau hautraṃ praśaṃsya, tattanmūrtimantraiḥ tattatpariṣaddevānām āvāhanādīni kārayet. ādimūrteḥ anantaśayanasya ca sabhyāgnau vaiṣṇavaṃ, viṣṇusūktaṃ, puruṣasūktaṃ, śrībhūmidaivatyaṃ, sarvadaivatyaṃ ca hutvā; puruṣamūrteḥ āhavanīye puruṣasūktaṃ; satyamūrteḥ anvāhārye viṣṇusūktaṃ; acyutamūrteḥ gārhapatye vaiṣṇavaṃ; aniruddhamūrteḥ āvasathye rudrasūktaṃ ekākṣaraṃ ca catuṣkṛtvo hutvā; vārāhasya sabhyāgnau 'kṣamām ekām' iti mantreṇa aṣṭādhikaśataṃ hutvā; nṛsiṃhasya gārhapatyāgnau 'yo vā nṛsiṃhaḥ' iti ekaṃ; naranārāyaṇayoḥ pauṇḍarīkāgnau 'satyaḥ satyasthaḥ' iti mantraṃ viṣṇugāyatrīṃ ca aṣṭottaśataṃ hutvā, atrānuktaṃ sarvaṃ mahāpratiṣṭhoktakrameṇa kṛtvā, rātriṣeṣaṃ vyapohya...

**Translation (English):**
*   **Specific Sacrificial Fire Oblations:**
    *   Having praised the *Hotri* recitations in each of the primary fires, perform the invocation and other rituals for the surrounding council-deities (*pariṣad-devān*) using their respective deity-mantras.
    *   For the **Ādimūrti** and **Ananta-śayana**, in the *Sabhya* fire, offer oblations with the Vaishnava mantras, Viṣṇu Sūkta, Puruṣa Sūkta, Śrī-Bhumi-daivatya hymn, and Sarva-daivatya hymn.
    *   For **Puruṣa-mūrti**, in the *Āhavanīya* fire: offer oblations with the Puruṣa Sūkta.
    *   For **Satya-mūrti**, in the *Anvāhārya* (Dakṣiṇa) fire: offer oblations with the Viṣṇu Sūkta.
    *   For **Acyuta-mūrti**, in the *Gārhapatya* fire: offer oblations with the Vaishnava mantras.
    *   For **Aniruddha-mūrti**, in the *Āvasathya* fire: offer the Rudra Sūkta and the single-syllable (*ekākṣara*) mantra four times.
    *   For **Varāha**, in the *Sabhya* fire: offer oblations 108 times with the mantra starting with *"kṣamām ekām..."*.
    *   For **Nṛsiṃha**, in the *Gārhapatya* fire: offer an oblation once with the mantra starting with *"yo vā nṛsiṃhaḥ..."*.
    *   For **Nara-Nārāyaṇa**, in the *Pauṇḍarīka* fire: offer oblations 108 times with the *"satyaḥ satyasthaḥ..."* mantra and the Viṣṇu Gayatri.
    *   Perform all other rites not specified here according to the guidelines of the great consecration (*mahā-pratiṣṭhā-vidhi*). Spend the remaining portion of the night.

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> ...प्रभाते स्नात्वा, आचार्यादिभ्यो दक्षिणां दद्यात्; सर्वान् देवान् उत्थाप्य, रत्नन्यासं विना चरराशौ तत्तत्स्थाने प्रतिष्ठाप्य, पुण्याहं वाचयित्वा, नित्यार्चनविधानेन अभ्यर्च्य, हविः निवेदयेत्॥

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> ...prabhāte snātvā, ācāryādibhyo dakṣiṇāṃ dadyāt; sarvān devān utthāpya, ratnanyāsaṃ vinā cararāśau tattatsthāne pratiṣṭhāpya, puṇyāhaṃ vācayitvā, nityārcanavidhānena abhyarcya, haviḥ nivedayet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Morning Rituals and Installation:**
    *   In the morning, having bathed, the patron (*yajamāna*) should distribute sacrificial gifts (*dakṣiṇā*) to the Acharya and other officiating priests.
    *   Awaken all the deities, and without performing the deposition of gems (*ratna-nyāsa*), install them in their respective places under a moveable zodiac sign (*cara-rāśi*).
    *   Recite the *Puṇyāha*, worship them according to the rules of daily worship (*nityārcana-vidhāna*), and offer the food oblation (*havis*).

#### Verse / Paragraph 3
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> षण्मूर्तिप्रतिष्ठा चेत् - वाराहं नारसिंहं नरनारायणौ च हित्वा, षण्मूर्तीः संस्थाप्य अर्चयेत्। पञ्चमूर्तिप्रतिष्ठा चेत् - अनन्तशयन-वाराहादीन् विना आदिमूर्त्यादि-पञ्चमूर्तीः संस्थाप्य अर्चयेत्; एष विशेषः, अन्यत् सर्वं समानम्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> ṣaṇmūrtipratiṣṭhā cet - varāhaṃ nārasiṃhaṃ naranārāyaṇau ca hitvā, ṣaṇmūrtīḥ saṃsthāpya arcayet. pañcamūrtipratiṣṭhā cet - anantaśayana-varāhādīn vinā ādimūrtyādi-pañcamūrtīḥ saṃsthāpya arcayet; eṣa viśeṣaḥ, anyat sarvaṃ samānam.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Alternative Configurations (Six-Deity & Five-Deity):**
    *   If consecrating six deities: install and worship the six deities, omitting Varāha, Nārasiṃha, and Nara-Nārāyaṇa.
    *   If consecrating five deities: install and worship the five deities starting with the primary deity, omitting Ananta-śayana, Varāha, and the others. This is the distinction; everything else is identical.

**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> yasmin ekatalavimāne dhruvaberasya ekasyaiva kautukāni pañcaiva santi, ataḥ teṣāṃ kautukānāṃ bālasthāpane sthāpanaṃ yadīcchet, pūrvaṃ taruṇālayaṃ sālindram abhyantarabhittivihīnaṃ vā kṛtvā, tattatpade viṣṇvādi-pañcānāṃ kautukānāṃ pañcāgnīn parikalpya, homaṃ hutvā saṃsthāpya arcayet iti āha marīciḥ.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Special Case for Single-Tier Temples:**
    *   In a single-tier temple (*eka-tala-vimāna*) where there are five Kautuka icons belonging to a single immovable main icon (*dhruva-bera*), if one desires to install those Kautuka icons in the temporary temple:
        *   One should first construct a temporary temple with a verandah (*sālindra*) but without the inner walls (*abhyantara-bhitti-vihīna*).
        *   Establish five fires in the respective grid positions of the five Kautuka icons starting with Viṣṇu.
        *   Offer the homa oblations, install the icons, and worship them.
        *   Thus declares Sage Marīci.
