# Vimanarchana Kalpa of Sage Marīci (Vaikhānasa Āgama)
## Chapter 7: Vimāna-Vidhiḥ (Rules for Temple Structure and Foundations)

This document contains the Sanskrit text, IAST transliteration, and English translation of the 7th Chapter (*Saptamaḥ Paṭalaḥ*) of the **Vimānārcanākalpa** (attributed to Sage Marīci, representing the Vaikhānasa school of Vaishnavism).

This chapter outlines the classifications, styles, proportions, and foundation rules for temple structures (*vimāna*). It defines the height limits and width ranges for single-tiered up to twelve-tiered temples, classifications of Composite (*saṅkīrṇa*) structures, the five sacred height proportions (Śāntika, Pauṣṭika, Jayada, Adbhuta, and Sārvakāmika), the three classical architectural styles (Nāgara, Vesara, and Drāviḍa), the layout of the primary measurement axis (*māna-sūtra*), and the detailed preparation and ramming of the foundation pit (*khāta-vidhiḥ*).

---

### Section 1: Classification and Tiers of Temple Structures

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **अथ सप्तमः पटलः**
> **विमानविधिः**
> ***atha saptamaḥ paṭalaḥ***
> ***vimānavidhiḥ***
> 
> अथ विमानविधिं वक्ष्ये - नलिनकादि-षण्णवति-भेदैः त्रि-चतुः-हस्तादि-रुद्र-अर्क-कर-अन्तम् एकभूमिं, पञ्च-षड्-हस्तादि-त्रयोदश-चतुर्दश-कर-अन्तं द्विभूमिं, सम-अष्ट-हस्तादि-पक्ष-षोडश-कर-अन्तं त्रितलं, सप्तदश-हस्तादि-षट्-शत-कर-अन्तं चतुर्भूभ्यादि-द्वादशतलान्तं युग्म-अयुग्म-हस्तैः युतं संयोजयेत्; एवं तलप्रतिमानं दशधा भवति।
> *atha vimānavidhiṃ vakṣye - nalinakādi-ṣaṇṇavati-bhedaiḥ tri-catuḥ-hastādi-rudra-arka-kara-antam ekabhūmiṃ, pañca-ṣaḍ-hastādi-trayodaśa-caturdaśa-kara-antaṃ dvibhūmiṃ, sama-aṣṭa-hastādi-pakṣa-ṣoḍaśa-kara-antaṃ tritalaṃ, saptadaśa-hastādi-ṣaṭ-śata-kara-antaṃ caturbhūmyādi-dvādaśatalāntaṃ yugma-ayugma-hastaiḥ yutaṃ saṃyojayet; evaṃ talapratimānaṃ daśadhā bhavati.*
> 
> **सङ्कीर्णविमानलक्षणम्**
> एतेषु इष्टहस्तैः वा सायाम-समचतुरश्र-वृत्तं विस्तारात् पाद-अधिकम् अर्ध-अधिकं पादोन-द्विगुणं द्विगुणं वा, आयतम् आयत-चतुरश्र-वृत्तं च, तेषु ग्रीवोपरि भिन्नाङ्गं सङ्कीर्णं स्यात्।
> ***saṅkīrṇavimānalakṣaṇam***
> *eteṣu iṣṭahastaiḥ vā sāyāma-samacaturaśra-vṛttaṃ vistārāt pāda-adhikam ardha-adhikaṃ pādona-dviguṇaṃ dviguṇaṃ vā, āyatam āyata-caturaśra-vṛttaṃ ca, teṣu grīvopari bhinnāṅgaṃ saṅkīrṇaṃ syāt.*

#### English Translation
*   **Tiers and Dimensions of the Temple Structure (*Vimāna-vidhiḥ*):**
    *   Now, I shall describe the guidelines for temple structures (*vimāna*).
    *   There are ninety-six classifications starting with *Nalinaka*. The width and heights are measured in even or odd cubits (*yugma-ayugma-hasta*):
        *   **Single-tiered (*eka-bhūmi*):** ranges from 3 or 4 cubits up to 11 or 12 cubits (*rudra-arka-kara-antam*).
        *   **Two-tiered (*dvi-bhūmi*):** ranges from 5 or 6 cubits up to 13 or 14 cubits.
        *   **Three-tiered (*tritala*):** ranges from 8 cubits up to 15 or 16 cubits (*pakṣa-ṣoḍaśa-kara-antam*).
        *   **Four-tiered up to twelve-tiered (*catur-bhūmy-ādi dvādaśa-tala-antam*):** ranges from 17 cubits up to 600 cubits (*ṣaṭ-śata-kara-antam*).
    *   In this manner, the height ratio for each tier has ten varieties (*tala-prati mānaṃ daśadhā bhavati*).

*   **Composite Temple Structures (*Saṅkīrṇa-vimāna-lakṣaṇam*):**
    *   Among these, based on the desired measurements in cubits, the plan can be a regular square (*sama-caturaśra*), circular (*vṛtta*), rectangular (*āyata*), or apsidal (*āyata-caturaśra-vṛtta*).
    *   The width proportions can be 1.25 times (*pāda-adhika*), 1.5 times (*ardha-adhika*), 1.75 times (*pādona-dviguṇa*), or 2 times (*dviguṇa*) the basic unit.
    *   If the upper components above the neck (*grīva-upari*) have a different shape/design compared to the lower structure, it is classified as a Composite (*saṅkīrṇa*) Vimāna.

### Section 2: Proportions, Styles, and Layout Guidelines

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **शान्तिकादिविमानलक्षणम्**
> विमानविपुलस्य सम-उत्सेधं शान्तिकं, पादाधिक-उत्सेधं पौष्टिकं, अर्ध-अधिक-उत्सेधं जयदं, पादोन-द्विगुण-उत्सेधम् अद्भुतं, द्विगुणात् सार्वकामिकं स्यात्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **śāntikādivimānalakṣaṇam**
> vimānavipulasya sama-utsedhaṃ śāntikaṃ, pādādhika-utsedhaṃ pauṣṭikaṃ, ardha-adhika-utsedhaṃ jayadaṃ, pādona-dviguṇa-utsedham adbhutaṃ, dviguṇāt sārvakāmikaṃ syāt.

**Translation (English):**
*   **The Five Height Proportions (*Śāntikādi-vimāna-lakṣaṇam*):**
    *   The height (*utsedha*) of the temple relative to its width (*vipula*) has five proportions:
        *   Height equal to width (1.0 ratio) is Peaceful (*śāntikam*).
        *   Height equal to 1.25 times the width (*pādādhika*) is Nourishing (*pauṣṭikam*).
        *   Height equal to 1.5 times the width (*ardhādhika*) is Victory-giving (*jayadam*).
        *   Height equal to 1.75 times the width (*pādona-dviguṇa*) is Miraculous (*adbhutam*).
        *   Height equal to 2.0 times the width (*dviguṇa*) fulfills all desires (*sārvakāmikam*).

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **हर्म्यभेदः तत्स्वरूपं च**
> नागरं, द्राविडं, वेसरम् इति त्रिविधं हर्म्यरूपम्। खुरादि-स्तूपिकान्तं चतुरश्रं नागरं, तथैव वृत्तं वेसरम्; अथवा प्रस्तरान्तं समचतुरश्रं तदुपरि वृत्त-ग्रीवा-शिखरं च वेसरं, तथैव च षडस्र-शिखर-ग्रीवं द्राविडं स्यात्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **harmyabhedaḥ tatsvarūpaṃ ca**
> nāgaraṃ, drāviḍaṃ, vesaram iti trividhaṃ harmyarūpam. khurādi-stūpikāntaṃ caturaśraṃ nāgaraṃ, tathaiva vṛttaṃ vesaram; athavā prastarāntaṃ samacaturaśraṃ tadupari vṛtta-grīvā-śikharaṃ ca vesaraṃ, tathaiva ca ṣaḍasra-śikhara-grīvaṃ drāviḍaṃ syāt.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Three Classical Architectural Styles (*Harmya-bhedaḥ*):**
    *   There are three styles of temple structures: Nāgara, Drāviḍa, and Vesara.
        *   **Nāgara:** Square from the plinth/base (*khura*) up to the finial (*stūpikā*).
        *   **Vesara:** Circular throughout; or square up to the entablature (*prastara*) and circular in the neck (*grīva*) and dome (*śikhara*) above it.
        *   **Drāviḍa:** Hexagonal or octagonal (*ṣaḍasra* / *aṣṭāsra*) in the neck and dome.

#### Verse / Paragraph 3
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> पूर्वापरायामं दक्षिणापवर्गं भवति; आयत-चतुरश्र-वृत्ते चायामेन शान्तिकोत्सेधग्रहणं कारयेत्। अर्पितम् अनर्पितम् इति द्विविधं भवति; अर्पितम् अलिन्दविहीनम्, अनर्पितम् अलिन्दयुक्तम् एवं कारयेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> pūrvāparāyāmaṃ dakṣiṇāpavargaṃ bhavati; āyata-caturaśra-vṛtte cāyāmena śāntikotsedhagrahaṇaṃ kārayet. arpitatam anarpitam iti dvividhaṃ bhavati; arpitam alindavihīnam, anarpitam alindayuktam evaṃ kārayet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Orientations and Layout Forms:**
    *   The length runs East-West (*pūrva-apara*), and the width is oriented North-South (*dakṣiṇa-apavarga*).
    *   For rectangular or apsidal plans, the height proportion (such as Śāntika) should be calculated based on the length.
    *   Vimānas are of two types: *Arpita* (without a surrounding verandah/aisle) and *Anarpita* (with a surrounding verandah/aisle).

### Section 3: Laying the Measuring Thread and Foundation Digging

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> विमानस्य विस्तारं पादबाह्यम्, उत्सेधम् उपानदादि-स्तूपिपर्यन्तं स्यात्; मानसूत्रं विन्यस्य, ऐशान्य्यां किञ्चिद् अधिकम् आग्नेय्यां किञ्चिन् न्यूनम् एवं कारयेत्; अन्यथा चेत् सर्वं नश्यति।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> vimānasya vistāraṃ pādabāhyam, utsedham upānadādi-stūpiparyantaṃ syāt; mānasūtraṃ vinyasya, aiśānyyāṃ kiñcid adhikaṃ āgneyyāṃ kiñcin nūnam evaṃ kārayet; anyathā cet sarvaṃ naśyati.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Measurement Reference Lines (*Māna-sūtra*):**
    *   The width of the temple (*vistāra*) is measured from the outer side of the pillars/walls (*pāda-bāhyam*). The height (*utsedha*) is measured from the base plinth (*upāna*) up to the finial (*stūpi*).
    *   In laying the primary measuring thread (*māna-sūtra*), it should be slightly larger in the North-East (*aiśānyām*) and slightly smaller in the South-East (*āgneyyām*). If not done in this way, the entire structure will collapse or be ruined (*anyathā cet sarvaṃ naśyati*).

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> मानसूत्राद् बहिः हस्तम् अध्यर्ध-हस्तं द्विहस्तं वा अधिक-विस्तारायामं, जलान्तं शिलान्तं वा, दृढप्रकृतिः चेत् द्विहस्तं सार्धहस्तम् एकहस्तं वा खनित्वा; तत् खातं चतुर्धा कृत्वा, द्व्यंशं त्र्यंशं वा वालुका-उपल-शकलाद्यैः जलेन सम्पूर्य, मुसलैः हस्तिपादैः दुरुण्डैः बृहत्तरैः वा दृढं कृत्वा, शेषांशं पादम् अर्धं त्रिपादं हस्तं वा; महद् विमानं चेत् अधिष्ठान-द्विगुणम् अध्यर्धं समं वा खातकुड्यं प्रकल्पयेत्; तदूर्ध्वे दण्डप्रमाणेन समं कृत्वा, तदूर्ध्वे अधिष्ठानम् उपपीठं वा कुर्यात्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> mānasūtrād bahiḥ hastam adhyardha-hastaṃ dvihastaṃ vā adhika-vistārāyāmaṃ, jalāntaṃ śilāntaṃ vā, dṛḍhaprakṛtiḥ cet dvihastaṃ sārdhahastam ekahastaṃ vā khanitvā; tat khātaṃ caturdhā kṛtvā, dvyaṃśaṃ tryaṃśaṃ vā vālukā-upala-śakalādyaiḥ jalena saṃpūryyā, musalaiḥ hastipādaiḥ duruṇḍaiḥ bṛhattaraiḥ vā dṛḍhaṃ kṛtvā, śeṣāṃśaṃ pādam ardhaṃ tripādaṃ hastaṃ vā; mahad vimānaṃ cet adhiṣṭhāna-dviguṇam adhyardhaṃ samaṃ vā khātakuḍyaṃ prakalpayet; tadūrdhve daṇḍapramāṇena samaṃ kṛtvā, tadūrdhve adhiṣṭhānam upapīṭhaṃ vā kuryāt.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Foundation Digging and Compacting (*Khāta-vidhiḥ*):**
    *   Extend the excavation beyond the measuring line by one, one-and-a-half, or two cubits in width and length. Dig down until water (*jalānta*) or rock (*śilānta*) is reached.
    *   If the ground is naturally firm (*dṛḍha-prakṛtiḥ*), dig to a depth of two, one-and-a-half, or one cubit.
    *   Divide the depth of the excavated pit into four parts. Fill two or three parts of it with sand (*vālukā*), gravel (*upala*), stone chips (*śakala*), etc., along with water.
    *   Compact it firmly using pestles (*musala*), elephant-foot rammers (*hasti-pāda*), and other larger rammers.
    *   The remaining part of the pit should be a quarter, half, three-quarters, or one cubit deep.
    *   If it is a large temple structure, construct a masonry retaining wall (*khāta-kuḍya*) inside the pit, measuring twice, one-and-a-half times, or equal to the height of the basement plinth.
    *   Level the ground above it using a leveling rod (*daṇḍa-pramāṇa*), and above that, construct the basement plinth (*adhiṣṭhāna*) or the pedestal (*upapīṭha*).
