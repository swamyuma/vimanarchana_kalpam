# Vimanarchana Kalpa of Sage Marīci (Vaikhānasa Āgama)
## Chapter 90: Deha-lakṣaṇa-vidhiḥ (Rules for Body Measurements and Inner Regions)

This document contains the Sanskrit text, IAST transliteration, and English translation of the 90th Chapter (*Navatitamaḥ Paṭalaḥ*) of the **Vimānārcanākalpa** (attributed to Sage Marīci, representing the Vaikhānasa school of Vaishnavism).

This chapter outlines the subtle anatomy and inner spiritual geography of the human body (*deha-lakṣaṇa*). It details the dimensions of the body, the location and description of the internal fire-mandala (*vahni-maṇḍala*) with the deity Vaishvanara, the bulb of life energy (*kanda*) at the navel with the revolving soul (*jīva*), the dormant serpentine energy (*kundalinī śakti*), the solar-disk (*arka-bimba*) at the heart with Viṣṇu, the lunar-disk (*candra-bimba*) at the nose-tip with Nārāyaṇa, and the sixteen-petaled head-lotus (*śiraḥ-padma*) representing Vāsudeva.

---

### Section 1: Body Dimensions and the Fire Mandala

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **अथ नवतितमः पटलः**
> **अथातो देहलक्षणं वक्ष्ये --**
> ***atha navatitamaḥ paṭalaḥ***
> ***athātō dēhalaxaṇaṃ vaxyē --***
> 
> स्वाङ्गुलीभिः षण्णवत्यङ्गुलो देहः । तद्द्वादशाङ्गुलाधिकः प्राणः प्राणायामेन समो भवति । गुदाद् द्व्यङ्गुलं देहमध्यम् । तस्मिन् हेमं त्रिकोणं वह्निमण्डलम् । तन्मध्ये बिन्दुनादेन रेफबीजं ज्वलति । तन्मध्ये मण्डलपुरुषो वैश्वानरमूर्तिः पिङ्गलाभः द्विशीर्षः (द्विशीर्षकः) चतुःशृङ्गः षण्नेत्रः सप्तहस्तः दक्षिणे अभय-स्रुक्-शक्ति-खड्ग-युतः, वामे वरद-स्रुव-खेट-युतः, त्रिपादः, पीताम्बरधरः श्रीवत्साङ्कः किरीट-केयूर-हार-आदि-सर्वाभरणयुक्तः । दक्षिणवामयोः स्वाहा-स्वधाभ्यां सहितः, सर्वदेवैः परिवृतः स्थितः ॥
> *svāṅgulībhiḥ ṣaṇṇavatyangulō dēhaḥ | taddvādaśāṅgulādhikaḥ prāṇaḥ prāṇāyāmēna samō bhavati | gudād dvyangulaṃ dēhamadhyam | tasmin hēmaṃ trikōṇaṃ vahnimaṇḍalam | tanmadhyē bindunādēna rēphabījaṃ jvalati | tanmadhyē maṇḍalapuruṣō vaiśvānaramūrtiḥ piṅgalābhaḥ dviśīrṣaḥ catuḥśṛṅgaḥ ṣaṇnētraḥ saptahastaḥ daxiṇē abhaya-sruk-śakti-khaḍga-yutaḥ, vāmē varada-sruva-khēṭa-yutaḥ, tripādaḥ, pītāmbaradharaḥ śrīvatsāṅkaḥ kirīṭa-kēyūra-hāra-ādi-sarvābharaṇayutaḥ | daxiṇavāmayōḥ svāhā-svadhābhyāṃ sahitaḥ, sarvadēvaiḥ parivṛtaḥ sthitaḥ ||*

#### English Translation
*   **Body Dimension:** Measured by one's own finger-breadths, the height of the human body is ninety-six angulas (*svāṅgulībhiḥ ṣaṇṇavatyangulō dēhaḥ*).

*   **Life Breath:** The vital breath (*prāṇaḥ*) extends twelve angulas beyond the body (*taddvādaśāṅgulādhikaḥ*). Through pranayama, it becomes equalized and balanced (*prāṇāyāmēna samō bhavati*).

*   **The Region of Fire:** Two angulas above the anus is the middle of the body (*gudād dvyangulaṃ dēhamadhyam*). Within that space lies the golden, triangular sphere of fire (*tasmin hēmaṃ trikōṇaṃ vahnimaṇḍalam*).

*   **The Fire Seed-Syllable:** In the center of it, the seed-syllable *Ram* (*rēphabījaṃ*), accompanied by the nasal point (*bindu*) and resonance (*nāda*), shines brightly (*tanmadhyē bindunādēna rēphabījaṃ jvalati*).

*   **The Form of Vaishvanara:** In the center of that seed-syllable stands the Person of the sphere, the deity Vaishvanara (*tanmadhyē maṇḍalapuruṣō vaiśvānaramūrtiḥ*). He is of a reddish-brown lustre (*piṅgalābhaḥ*), has two heads (*dviśīrṣaḥ*), four horns (*catuḥśṛṅgaḥ*), six eyes (*ṣaṇnētraḥ*), and seven hands (*saptahastaḥ*).

*   **Attributes of Hands:** In his right hands, he holds the gesture of protection, the large sacrificial ladle (*sruc*), the spear/javelin (*śakti*), and the sword (*khaḍga*); in his left hands, he holds the boon-giving gesture, the small sacrificial spoon (*sruva*), and the shield (*khēṭa*) (*daxiṇē abhaya-sruk-śakti-khaḍga-yutaḥ, vāmē varada-sruva-khēṭa-yutaḥ*).

*   **Ornaments and Consorts:** He has three feet (*tripādaḥ*), wears yellow garments (*pītāmbaradharaḥ*), is marked with the Shrivatsa (*śrīvatsāṅkaḥ*), and is adorned with all ornaments such as a crown, armlets, and necklaces (*kirīṭa-kēyūra-hāra-ādi-sarvābharaṇayutaḥ*). He is accompanied by Svāhā on his right and Svadhā on his left (*daxiṇavāmayōḥ svāhā-svadhābhyāṃ sahitaḥ*), standing surrounded by all the gods (*sarvadēvaiḥ parivṛtaḥ sthitaḥ*).

### Section 2: The Bulb of Life and the Revolving Soul

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **नाभिः तत्र द्वादशारचक्रं, तत्र जीवः**
> तदूर्ध्वं नवाङ्गुलं कन्दस्थानम् ।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **nābhiḥ tatra dvādaśāracakraṃ, tatra jīvaḥ**
> tadūrdhvaṃ navāṅgulaṃ kandasthānam |

**Translation (English):**
*   **The Region of Kanda:** Nine angulas above the body center is the location of the *kanda* (bulb of life energy) (*tadūrdhvaṃ navāṅgulaṃ kandasthānam*). It is four angulas in height and width (*caturaṅgulōtsēdhāyāmaṃ*), covered with the seven bodily tissues beginning with the skin (*tvagādi-saptadhātubhirbhūṣitaṃ*), and shaped like an egg (*aṇḍākṛtikaṃ*).

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> चतुरङ्गुलोत्सेधायामं, त्वगादि-सप्तधातुभिर्भूषितं, अण्डाकृतिकं, कन्दमध्ये नाभिः ।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> caturaṅgulōtsēdhāyāmaṃ, tvagādi-saptadhātubhirbhūṣitaṃ, aṇḍākṛtikaṃ, kandamadhyē nābhiḥ |

**Translation (English):**
*   **The Navel and Spoked Wheel:** In the center of this *kanda* is the navel (*kandamadhyē nābhiḥ*). At the navel is a wheel with twelve spokes (*nābhau dvādaśārayutaṃ cakraṃ*).

#### Verse / Paragraph 3
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> नाभौ (नाभ्याः) द्वादशारयुतं चक्रं, तत्र पुण्य-पाप-प्रचोदितः तन्तुपञ्जरमध्यस्थो लूतिक इव प्राणारूढः परिवर्तते (भ्रमति) जीवः ॥

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> nābhau (nābhyāḥ) dvādaśārayutaṃ cakraṃ, tatra puṇya-pāpa-pracōditaḥ tantupañjaramadhyasthō lūtika iva prāṇārūḍhaḥ parivartatē (bhramati) jīvaḥ ||

**Translation (English):**
*   **The Revolving Soul:** There, driven by merit and demerit (*tatra puṇya-pāpa-pracōditaḥ*), the individual soul (*jīvaḥ*), mounted on the life-force (*prāṇārūḍhaḥ*), revolves (wanders) like a spider in the middle of its web (*tantupañjaramadhyasthō lūtika iva parivartatē*).

### Section 3: The Dormant Kundalini Shakti

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **नाभेरूर्ध्वं कुण्डलिनी शक्तिः**
> तस्य नाभेः तिर्यग्गता अधरूर्ध्वगता अष्टप्रकृतिरूपा नागरूपा विद्युन्मुखेन मूत्रद्वारं (ऊर्ध्वद्वारं) संरुध्य, स्थिता कुण्डलीभूता सर्पफणामणिमण्डलश्रियमुपेयुषी कुण्डलिनी शक्तिः, आत्मनः फणेन ब्रह्मरन्ध्रं सुषुम्नानाडीरन्ध्रमुपगूह्य तिष्ठति ॥
> ***nābhērūrdhvaṃ kuṇḍalinī śaktiḥ***
> *tasya nābhēḥ tiryaggatā adharūrdhvagatā aṣṭaprakṛtirūpā nāgarūpā vidyunmukhēna mūtradvāraṃ (ūrdhvadvāraṃ) saṃrudhya, sthitā kuṇḍalībhūtā sarpaphaṇāmaṇimaṇḍalaśriyamupēyuṣī kuṇḍalinī śaktiḥ, ātmanaḥ phaṇēna brahmarandhraṃ suṣumnānāḍīrandhramupagūhya tiṣṭhati ||*

#### English Translation
*   **The Serpent Energy:** Located above the navel, moving transversely, downwards, and upwards (*tasya nābhēḥ tiryaggatā adharūrdhvagatā*), is the Kundalini Shakti (*kuṇḍalinī śaktiḥ*). She is of the form of the eightfold cosmic nature (*aṣṭaprakṛtirūpā*) and has the shape of a serpent (*nāgarūpā*).

*   **Blocking the Passages:** With a mouth resembling lightning (*vidyunmukhēna*), obstructing the urinary passage (or the upward gateway of prana) (*mūtradvāraṃ saṃrudhya*), she remains coiled up (*sthitā kuṇḍalībhūtā*).

*   **Sealing the Central Channel:** Possessing the beauty of a serpent's hood adorned with a circle of gems (*sarpaphaṇāmaṇimaṇḍalaśriyamupēyuṣī*), she remains covering and sealing the aperture of Brahman (*brahmarandhraṃ*), which is the opening of the central energy channel (*suṣumnānāḍīrandhram upagūhya tiṣṭhati*), with her own hood (*ātmanaḥ phaṇēna*).

### Section 4: The Heart Center and the Form of Viṣṇu

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **हृदयं - तत्र सूर्यमण्डलम् - तत्र मूर्तिः**
> नाभ्या उपरि वितस्त्यन्तरे हृदयं तत्र सर्वं प्रतिष्ठितम् । हृदये अर्कबिम्बं तस्मिन् सकारबीजान्वितं सहस्रज्वालायुतं ज्योतिर्ज्वलति । तन्मध्ये मण्डलपुरुषो विष्णुमूर्तिः तरुणादित्यसङ्काशो हेममयो हिरण्यश्मश्रुकेशनखयुतः पीताम्बरधरः श्रीवत्साङ्कः चतुर्भुजः शङ्ख-चक्र-धरः अभय-कट्यवलम्बित-हस्तः रक्तास्यनेत्रपाणिपादः सौम्यः सुप्रसन्नमुखः किरीट-केयूर-हार-कटिप्रलम्ब-यज्ञोपवीतादि-सर्वाभरणभूषितः सृष्टि-स्थिति-अन्त-कारणः देवीभ्यां परिषत्कैश्च सहितः स्थितः ॥
> ***hṛdayaṃ - tatra sūryamaṇḍalam - tatra mūrtiḥ***
> *nābhyā upari vitastyantarē hṛdayaṃ tatra sarvaṃ pratiṣṭhitam | hṛdayē arkabimbaṃ tasmin sakārabījānvitaṃ sahasrajvālāyutaṃ jyōtirjvalati | tanmadhyē maṇḍalapuruṣō viṣṇumūrtiḥ taruṇādityasaṅkāśō hēmamayō hiraṇyaśmaśrukēśanaxayutaḥ pītāmbaradharaḥ śrīvatsāṅkaḥ caturbhujaḥ śaṅkha-cakra-dharaḥ abhaya-kaṭyavalambita-hastaḥ raktāsyanētrapāṇipādaḥ saumyaḥ suprasannamuxaḥ kirīṭa-kēyūra-hāra-kaṭipralamba-yajñōpavītādi-sarvābharaṇabhūṣitaḥ sṛṣṭi-sthiti-anta-kāraṇaḥ dēvībhyāṃ pariṣatkaiśca sahitaḥ sthitaḥ ||*

#### English Translation
*   **The Heart Space:** A span (twelve angulas) above the navel is the heart (*nābhyā upari vitastyantarē hṛdayaṃ*). In that center, everything is established (*tatra sarvaṃ pratiṣṭhitam*).

*   **The Solar Disk:** In the heart is the solar disk (*hṛdayē arkabimbaṃ*). Within it, associated with the seed-syllable *Sa* (*tasmin sakārabījānvitaṃ*), a light consisting of a thousand flames shines (*sahasrajvālāyutaṃ jyōtirjvalati*).

*   **The Golden Deity Viṣṇu:** In the center of that light is the Person of the sphere, the form of Viṣṇu (*tanmadhyē maṇḍalapuruṣō viṣṇumūrtiḥ*). He is brilliant like the morning sun (*taruṇādityasaṅkāśō*), completely golden (*hēmamayō*), and possesses golden beard, hair, and nails (*hiraṇyaśmaśrukēśanaxayutaḥ*).

*   **Iconographic Details:** He wears yellow garments, bears the Shrivatsa mark, has four arms, and holds the conch and discus (*pītāmbaradharaḥ śrīvatsāṅkaḥ caturbhujaḥ śaṅkha-cakra-dharaḥ*). One hand is in the gesture of protection, and another rests on his hip (*abhaya-kaṭyavalambita-hastaḥ*).

*   **Aura and Retinue:** His mouth, eyes, hands, and feet are reddish (*raktāsyanētrapāṇipādaḥ*). He is gentle, with a highly pleasing countenance (*saumyaḥ suprasannamuxaḥ*), and is adorned with all ornaments, including a crown, armlets, necklaces, waistband, and a hanging sacred thread (*kirīṭa-kēyūra-hāra-kaṭipralamba-yajñōpavītādi-sarvābharaṇabhūṣitaḥ*). He is the cause of creation, preservation, and dissolution (*sṛṣṭi-sthiti-anta-kāraṇaḥ*), and stands accompanied by the two Goddesses (Śrī and Bhu) and his retinue (*dēvībhyāṃ pariṣatkaiśca sahitaḥ sthitaḥ*).

### Section 5: The Nose-Tip and the Form of Nārāyaṇa

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **नासाग्रे चन्द्रमण्डलम् - तत्र मूर्तिः**
> नासाग्रे शुद्धस्फटिकसङ्काशं चन्द्रबिम्बम् । तस्मिन् ऋकारबीजान्वितं, अमृतं स्रवन्तं श्वेतोर्चिषं ध्यात्वा; तन्मध्ये मण्डलपुरुषो नारायणमूर्तिः शुद्धस्फटिकसङ्काशः पीताम्बरधरः श्रीवत्साङ्कः चतुर्भुजः शङ्ख-चक्र-धरः अभय-कट्यवलम्बित-हस्तः पद्मोदरदलाभनेत्रः रक्तास्यपाणिपादः किरीट-केयूर-हार-आदि-सर्वाभरणभूषितः ॥
> ***nāsāgrē candramaṇḍalam - tatra mūrtiḥ***
> *nāsāgrē śuddhasphaṭikasaṅkāśaṃ candrabimbam | tasmin ṛkārabījānvitaṃ, amṛtaṃ sravantaṃ śvētōrciṣaṃ dhyātvā; tanmadhyē maṇḍalapuruṣō nārāyaṇamūrtiḥ śuddhasphaṭikasaṅkāśaḥ pītāmbaradharaḥ śrīvatsāṅkaḥ caturbhujaḥ śaṅkha-cakra-dharaḥ abhaya-kaṭyavalambita-hastaḥ padmōdaradalābhānētraḥ raktāsyapāṇipādaḥ kirīṭa-kēyūra-hāra-ādi-sarvābharaṇabhūṣitaḥ ||*

#### English Translation
*   **The Lunar Disk:** At the tip of the nose is the lunar disk, which resembles pure crystal (*nāsāgrē śuddhasphaṭikasaṅkāśaṃ candrabimbam*).

*   **Meditation on Nārāyaṇa:** Having meditated on it as associated with the seed-syllable *Ṛ* (*tasmin ṛkārabījānvitaṃ*), dripping nectar, and possessing white radiance (*amṛtaṃ sravantaṃ śvētōrciṣaṃ dhyātvā*); in the center of it is the Person of the sphere, the form of Nārāyaṇa (*tanmadhyē maṇḍalapuruṣō nārāyaṇamūrtiḥ*).

*   **Divine Attributes:** He resembles pure crystal (*śuddhasphaṭikasaṅkāśaḥ*), wears yellow garments (*pītāmbaradharaḥ*), bears the Shrivatsa mark (*śrīvatsāṅkaḥ*), is four-armed (*caturbhujaḥ*), and holds the conch and discus (*śaṅkha-cakra-dharaḥ*). One hand is in the protection gesture, and another rests on the hip (*abhaya-kaṭyavalambita-hastaḥ*).

*   **Lotus Eyes and Ornaments:** His eyes resemble the inner petals of a lotus (*padmōdaradalābhānētraḥ*), his mouth, hands, and feet are reddish (*raktāsyapāṇipādaḥ*), and he is adorned with all ornaments such as a crown, armlets, and necklaces (*kirīṭa-kēyūra-hāra-ādi-sarvābharaṇabhūṣitaḥ*).

### Section 6: The Crown of the Head and the Form of Vāsudeva

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **मूर्धनि मुक्तिद्वारम्**
> स्मेरमुखः देवीभ्यां परिषत्कैः सहितः स्थितः । तस्योपरि मूर्ध्नि सुषुम्नाया अग्रे मुक्तिद्वारम् । तस्मिन् शिरःपद्मम् अधोमुखमूर्ध्वमूलं षोडशदलयुतं तस्मिन् स्थितं अमृतधाराभिः सहस्राभिरप्लाव्यमानं मण्डलपुरुषं वासुदेवं ध्यायेदिति विज्ञायते ॥
> ***mūrdhani muktidvāram***
> *smēramuxaḥ dēvībhyāṃ pariṣatkaiḥ sahitaḥ sthitaḥ | tasyōpari mūrdhni suṣumnāyā agrē muktidvāram | tasmin śiraḥpadmam adhōmuxamūrdhvamūlaṃ ṣōḍaśadalayutaṃ tasmin sthitaṃ amṛtadhārābhiḥ sahasrābhiraplāvyamānaṃ maṇḍalapuruṣaṃ vāsudēvaṃ dhyāyēditi vijñāyatē ||*

#### English Translation
*   **The Smiling Deity:** With a smiling face (*smēramuxaḥ*), he (Nārāyaṇa) stands accompanied by the two Goddesses and his retinue (*dēvībhyāṃ pariṣatkaiḥ sahitaḥ sthitaḥ*).

*   **The Head Lotus (Sahasrara):** Above that, in the crown of the head, at the tip of the Sushumna channel, is the gate of liberation (*tasyōpari mūrdhni suṣumnāyā agrē muktidvāram*). Therein is the head-lotus, facing downwards and rooted above, possessing sixteen petals (*tasmin śiraḥpadmam adhōmuxamūrdhvamūlaṃ ṣōḍaśadalayutaṃ*).

#### Colophon
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> इति श्रीवैखानसे मरीचिप्रोक्ते विमानार्चनाकल्ये देहलक्षणं नाम नवतितमः पटलः ॥९०॥

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> iti śrīvaikhānasē marīciprōktē vimānārcanākalpē dēhalaxaṇaṃ nāma navatitamaḥ paṭalaḥ ||90||

**Translation (English):**
*   **Meditation on Vāsudeva:** One should meditate on the Person of the sphere, Vāsudeva, established therein and being flooded by thousands of streams of nectar (*tasmin sthitaṃ amṛtadhārābhiḥ sahasrābhiraplāvyamānaṃ maṇḍalapuruṣaṃ vāsudēvaṃ dhyāyēditi vijñāyatē*).

### Glossary of Key Terms
*   **Deha-lakṣaṇa (देहलक्षण):** The physical and subtle characteristics, proportions, and energetic architecture of the human body.
*   **Angula (अङ्गुल):** A traditional Indian unit of measurement equal to the width of a finger. The human body is measured as 96 angulas in height.
*   **Deha-madhya (देहमध्य):** The physical center of the body, located two angulas above the anus, containing the internal fire-mandala.
*   **Vahni-maṇḍala (वह्निमण्डल):** The region of internal fire located in the lower torso, corresponding to the digestive and energetic fire (*jaṭharāgni*).
*   **Vaiśvānara (वैश्वानर):** The universal fire deity representing Agni, envisioned here with Vedic iconography (two heads, four horns, seven hands, three feet).
*   **Kanda (कन्द):** The "bulb" or root-source of all subtle energy channels (*nāḍī*), located above the body center and shaped like an egg.
*   **Kundalinī Śakti (कुण्डलिनी शक्तिः):** The coiled, dormant serpentine energy located at the base of the spine/navel, which seals the opening of the *suṣumnā*.
*   **Suṣumnā (सुषुम्ना):** The central energy channel of the subtle body, extending from the base of the torso to the crown of the head.
*   **Arka-bimba (अर्कबिम्ब):** The solar disk, representing the source of fire, light, and wisdom located at the heart center (*anāhata*).
*   **Candra-bimba (चन्द्रबिम्ब):** The lunar disk, representing nectar, coolness, and purity, located at the nasal center (*nasāgra*).
*   **Śiraḥ-padma (शिरःपद्म):** The head-lotus, corresponding to the *sahasrāra* chakra at the crown of the head, representing the state of liberation.
