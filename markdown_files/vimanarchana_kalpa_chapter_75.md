# Vimanarchana Kalpa of Sage Marīci (Vaikhānasa Āgama)
## Chapter 75: Utpāta-śānti-vidhiḥ (Rules of Pacification for Portents and Bad Omens)

This document contains the Sanskrit text, IAST transliteration, and English translation of the 75th Chapter (*Pañcasaptatitamaḥ Paṭalaḥ*) of the **Vimānārcanākalpa** (attributed to Sage Marīci, representing the Vaikhānasa school of Vaishnavism).

This chapter discusses the classification, effects, and pacification rituals (*śānti*) for various portents, natural anomalies, and bad omens (*utpāta* or *adbhuta*) that occur due to human sins or transgressions. It divides portents into three classes—celestial (*divya*), atmospheric (*āntarikṣa*), and terrestrial (*bhauma*)—and details specific rituals to rectify abnormalities in animate beings, temple structures, and deity icons, as well as portents affecting the royal household.

---

### Section 1: General Definition and Need for Expiation of Portents

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **अथ पञ्चसप्ततितमः पटलः**
> **अद्भुतोदयनिमित्तम्, तत्र शान्त्यकरणे दोषश्च**

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **atha pañcasaptatitamaḥ paṭalaḥ**
> **adbhutodayanimittam, tatra śāntyakaraṇe doṣaśca**

**Translation (English):**
*   **Need for Pacification:** Now, we shall explain the pacification of portents (*adbhuta-śāntiṃ vyākhyāsyāmaḥ*). Due to the abundance of human sins and transgressions (*narāṇāṃ pāpa-bāhulyād aparādha-bāhulyācca*), the gods manifest portents/anomalies (*devāḥ sṛjanty adbhutāni*).

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> अथाद्भुतशान्तिं व्याख्यास्यामः — नराणां पापबाहुल्याद् अपराधबाहुल्याच्च देवाः सृजन्त्यद्भुतानि, तेषु जातेषु सद्यः शान्तिं कुर्यात्। अज्ञानाद् अर्थलोभाद् वा न कुर्यात् चेत्, कर्तृ-अधिकारिणोः ग्रामस्य च महद् भयं, राज्ञः राष्ट्रस्य च क्षोभश्च जायते।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> athādbhutaśāntiṃ vyākhyāsyāmaḥ — narāṇāṃ pāpabāhulyād aparādhabāhulyācca devāḥ sṛjantyadbhutāni, teṣu jāteṣu sadyaḥ śāntiṃ kuryāt | ajñānād arthalobhād vā na kuryāt cet, kartr-adhikāriṇoḥ grāmasya ca mahad bhayaṃ, rājñaḥ rāṣṭrasya ca kṣobhaśca jāyate ||

**Translation (English):**
*   **Consequences of Neglect:** When these occur, one should perform pacification immediately (*sadyḥ śāntiṃ kuryāt*). If one does not perform it out of ignorance or greed for wealth (*ajñānād arthalobhād vā na kuryāt cet*), great fear/calamity arises for the patron, the temple officials, and the village, and turmoil/unrest (*kṣobhaśca*) is generated for the king and the state.

### Section 2: Timing for Expiation

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **शान्तिकरणे कालः**
> तस्माद् द्वादशाह-अभ्यन्तरे शान्तिं कारयेत्। द्वादशाहादतीते महाशान्तिं हुत्वा वैष्णवान् सम्पूज्य शक्तितो दक्षिणां दत्त्वा आरभेत्। मासे अतीते महाशान्ति-शतं हुत्वा विप्रशतं भोजयित्वा देवम् अनुमन्त्य कुर्यात्। षण्मासातीते भगवान् न रमेत। तस्मात् सर्वप्रयत्नेन शान्तिम् आरभेत ॥

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **śāntikaraṇe kālaḥ**
> tasmād dvādaśāha-abhyantare śāntiṃ kārayet | dvādaśāhādatīte mahāśāntiṃ hutvā vaiṣṇavān sampūjya śaktito dakṣiṇāṃ dattva ārabhet | māse atīte mahāśānti-śataṃ hutvā vipraśataṃ bhojayitvā devam anumantya kuryāt | ṣaṇmāsātīte bhagavān na rameta | tasmāt sarvaprayatnena śāntim ārabheta ||

**Translation (English):**
*   **Expiation Timeline:** 
    1.  Therefore, one should perform the pacification within twelve days (*dvādaśāha-abhyantare*).
    2.  If twelve days have passed (*dvādaśāhādatīte*), one should perform the grand pacification fire sacrifice (*mahā-śāntiṃ hutvā*), worship the Vaishnavas, give dakshina according to one's capacity (*śaktito dakṣiṇāṃ dattva*), and begin the rites.
    3.  If a month has passed (*māse atīte*), one should perform one hundred grand pacification fire offerings (*mahā-śānti-śataṃ hutvā*), feed one hundred Brahmins (*vipra-śataṃ bhojayitvā*), obtain the Lord's approval (*devam anumantya*), and then perform it.
    4.  If six months pass (*ṣaṇ-māsātīte*), the Lord will not delight/stay there (*bhagavān na rameta*). Therefore, with all effort, one should begin the pacification.

### Section 3: Three Classes of Portents

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **अद्भुतानां त्रैविध्यम्**
> अद्भुतदानि त्रिविधानि — दिव्यानि, आन्तरिक्षाणि, भौमानि चेति।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **adbhutānāṃ traividhyam**
> adbhutāni trividhāni — divyāni, āntarikṣāṇi, bhaumāni ceti ||

**Translation (English):**
*   **Threefold Portents:** Portents are of three kinds: celestial (*divyāni*), atmospheric (*āntarikṣāṇi*), and terrestrial (*bhaumāni*).

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **तत्र दिव्यानि**
> दिव्यानि ग्रहविकार-ग्रहयुद्ध-महाअनावृष्टि-सर्वग्रासोपराग-नक्षत्रभेदादित्येवमादीनि।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **tatra divyāni**
> divyāni grahavikāra-grahayuddha-mahāanāvṛṣṭi-sarvagrāsoparāga-nakṣatrabhedādityevamādīni ||

**Translation (English):**
*   **Celestial Portents:** Celestial portents include planetary aberrations, planetary conflicts (*graha-yuddha*), major droughts (*mahā-anāvṛṣṭi*), total eclipses (*sarva-grāsoparāga*), and splitting of constellations (*nakṣatrabheda*).

#### Verse / Paragraph 3
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **आन्तरिक्षाणि**
> आन्तरिक्षाणि निरन्तर-परिवेषः वर्णान्तर-बहुल-परिवेषः रात्राविन्द्रचापम्, आकाशे देवसद्म-विमान-उल्कापात-राहुपुच्छदर्शनं, स्तनित-धूमकेतु-प्रतिसूर्य-नक्षत्रपुच्छ-प्रतिचन्द्रादीनि।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **āntarikṣāṇi**
> āntarikṣāṇi nirantara-pariveṣaḥ varṇāntara-bahula-pariveṣaḥ rātrāvindracāpam, ākāśe devasadma-vimāna-ulkāpāta-rāhupucchadarśanaṃ, stanita-dhūmaketu-pratisūrya-nakṣatrapuccha-praticandrādīni ||

**Translation (English):**
*   **Atmospheric Portents:** Atmospheric portents include continuous halos around the sun/moon, halos of multiple colors, rainbows appearing at night (*rātrāv indracāpam*), meteors falling on temples or structures in the sky (*devasadma-vimāna-ulkāpāta*), the appearance of Rahu's tail/comet (*rāhu-puccha-darśanaṃ*), thunder (*stanita*), comets (*dhūmaketu*), mock suns (*prati-sūrya*), tails of stars, and mock moons (*prati-candra*).

#### Verse / Paragraph 4
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **भौमानाम् अनेकत्वम्**
> भौमानि चरस्थिरभावाद् अनेकधा।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **bhaumānām anekatvam**
> bhaumāni carasthirabhāvād anekadhā ||

**Translation (English):**
*   **Terrestrial Portents:** Terrestrial portents are of many kinds, divided into those occurring in animate/moving entities (*cara*) and inanimate/stationary entities (*sthira*).

### Section 4: Terrestrial Portents in Animate Beings

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **तत्र चरेषु भौमानि**
> चरेषु — देश-काल-स्वभाव-विपरीत-विकल-सूतिः, वानर-सर्प-अश्व-मार्जार-आदि-योनिजानां, नाग-गो-हय-महिषादि-विपरीत-योनि-जननं, पक्षि-सरीसृप-कृमि-कीटादीनां विपरीत-वर्णं विपरीत-आकारं विपरीत-आलय-वासं विपरीताश्रयाश्च, आलये कृकलास-सर्प-दर्शनम् इत्येवमादीनि।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **tatra careṣu bhaumāni**
> careṣu — deśa-kāla-svabhāva-viparīta-vikala-sūtiḥ, vānara-sarpa-aśva-mārjāra-ādi-yonijānāṃ, nāga-go-haya-mahiṣādi-viparīta-yoni-jananaṃ, pakṣi-sarīsṛpa-kṛmi-kīṭādīnāṃ viparīta-varṇaṃ viparīta-ākāraṃ viparīta-ālaya-vāsaṃ viparītāśrayāśca, ālaye kṛkalāsa-sarpa-darśanam ityevamādīni |

**Translation (English):**
*   **Anomalies in Animate Beings:** Terrestrial portents in moving beings include:
    1.  Contrary or deformed births (*viparīta-vikala-sūtiḥ*) out of season, place, or nature among those born from monkeys, snakes, horses, cats, etc.
    2.  Cross-species birth (*viparīta-yoni-jananaṃ*) among elephants, cows, horses, and buffaloes (e.g. a cow giving birth to a horse).
    3.  Strange colors, shapes, nests, or shelters (*viparīta-varṇaṃ viparīta-ākāraṃ viparīta-ālaya-vāsaṃ*) of birds, reptiles, worms, and insects.
    4.  The appearance of chameleons/lizards and snakes (*kṛkalāsa-sarpa-darśanam*) inside the temple sanctum.

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> तेषां दिव्यान्तरिक्षभवानि उत्तमोत्तमानि; नरभवानि उत्तमानि; नाग-गो-हय-महिषादिभवानि मध्यमानि; पक्षि-सरीसृपादिभवानि अधमानि।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> teṣāṃ divyāntarikṣabhavāni uttamottamāni; narabhavāni uttamāni; nāgagohayamahiṣādibhavāni madhyamāni; pakṣisarīsṛpādibhavāni adhamāni ||

**Translation (English):**
*   **Severity Levels:** Among these, portents originating from celestial/atmospheric sources are the most severe (*uttamottamāni*); those involving humans are high (*uttamāni*); those involving elephants, cows, horses, and buffaloes are medium (*madhyamāni*); and those involving birds, reptiles, etc. are low (*adhamāni*).

### Section 5: Terrestrial Portents in Inanimate Things

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **स्थिरेषु भौमानि**
> स्थिरेषु — प्रतिमा-रोदन-हसन-जल्पन-परिवर्तन-स्वेद-रुधिरस्राव-ज्वलना-धूमायित-कृमि-कीट-पतङ्गाद्युद्भव-अकालस्फोटन-अङ्गकम्प-स्वापन-तृण-वल्मीकोद्भव-भित्तिदरण-प्रतिलोम-पतित-पाषण्ड-अन्त्यजाति-शृगालादि-अस्पृश्य-स्पर्श-प्रवेश-स्थूणा-स्तम्भविरोहण-चलन-परिवर्तन-अपसर्पण-भित्ति-कवाट-आसन-शयन-आयुधानाम् अग्निहोत्र-अपस्कर-विकार-मक्षिका-वृक्षपतन-परिवर्तन-उपसर्पण-फल-पुष्प-पत्र-शाखादि-विपरीतदर्शन-जलोत्पीडादीनि।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **sthireṣu bhaumāni**
> sthireṣu — pratimā-rodana-hasana-jalpana-parivartana-sveda-rudhirasrāva-jvalanā-dhūmāyita-kṛmi-kīṭa-pataṅgādyudbhava-akālasphoṭana-aṅgakampa-svāpana-tṛṇa-valmīkodbhava-bhittidaraṇa-pratiloma-patita-pāṣaṇḍa-antyajāti-śṛgālādi-aspṛśya-sparśa-praveśa-sthūṇā-stambhavirohaṇa-calana-parivartana-apasarpaṇa-bhitti-kavāṭa-āsana-śayana-āyudhānām agnihotra-apaskara-vikāra-makṣikā-vṛkṣapatana-parivartana-upasarpaṇa-phala-puṣpa-patra-śākhādi-viparītadarśana-jalotpīḍādīni ||

**Translation (English):**
*   **Anomalies in Inanimate Entities:** Terrestrial portents in stationary things include:
    1.  The weeping, laughing, talking, shifting, sweating, or bleeding (*rodana-hasana-jalpana-parivartana-sveda-rudhirasrāva*) of deity icons.
    2.  Icons catching fire, smoking (*jvalanā-dhūmāyita*), trembling in limbs (*aṅgakampa*), or going to sleep (*svāpana*).
    3.  The sudden growth of worms, insects, moths, grass, or anthills (*kṛmi-kīṭa-pataṅgādyudbhava-tṛṇa-valmīkodbhava*) inside the temple.
    4.  The cracking of walls (*bhitti-daraṇa*), backward movement, falling, or entry and touch by heretics, outcasts, jackals, or other untouchables (*pāṣaṇḍa-antyajāti-śṛgālādi-aspṛśya-sparśa-praveśa*).
    5.  The sprouting of pillars or posts (*sthūṇā-stambhavirohaṇa*); the shifting, rotating, or sliding away of walls, doors, seats, beds, and weapons.
    6.  Deformation in sacrificial fires or utensils (*agnihotra-apaskara-vikāra*); swarms of flies; the untimely falling, shifting, or sliding of trees; and unnatural appearances of fruits, flowers, leaves, and branches, or the boiling/spouting of water (*jalotpīḍādīni*).

### Section 6: Effects of Portents according to Temple Zones

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **तेषां स्थानभेदात् फलानि**
> तेषु बेर-पीठ-गर्भगृहोद्भवानि ब्राह्मण-राज-राष्ट्र-विनाश-कारणानि। विशेषेण कर्तृ-आराधकयोश्च प्रासादोत्पन्नानि क्षत्रियस्य। प्रासादे अधिष्ठान-पाद-प्रस्तर-ग्रीवा-शिखरोद्भवानि क्रमेण क्षत्रिय-वैश्य-शूद्र-इतर-मुख्याभिषेकयोनि-युवराज-अभिषिक्तमहाराजानाम् आतङ्क-भय-अनावृष्टिकराणि। प्रथमावरणे द्विजातीनां, मन्त्रि-पुरोहितानां, माण्डलिकानां च भयावहम्, प्राणपीडादि-भयप्रदं च। द्वितीयावरणादिषु शूद्रजातीनां सर्वदुःखकरम्। सस्य-नाशनं, गज-अश्व-अज-महिष-पशूनां विनाशकरम्। अग्निकुण्ड-पुरोहितस्य, चतुर्मुख-स्थाने द्विजातीनां, रुद्रालये सर्वजातीनां, गुहालये तपस्विनां माण्डलिकानां च, वक्रतुण्डालये सेनानीनां, श्रियः आलये राजपत्नीनां, दुर्गालये अन्त्यानां, मातृस्थाने निकृष्टजातीनां, सोमस्थाने चौषधीनां, भास्करालये नृप-वाहन-शस्त्रजीविनां, अन्यदेवालये तत्तद्भक्तानाम् अपि लक्षयेत्। आस्थानमण्डप-सभामण्डप-राजवेश्म-प्राकार-क्रीडास्थान-महानस-आचार्य-यजमानादिषु सर्वत्र वरण-कदम्ब-चैत्यवृक्षादिषु सञ्जातानां वल्मीक-रक्तपिपीलिका-मक्षिकादीनां फलानि दिग्भागेन विज्ञेयानि।
> ***teṣāṃ sthānabhedāt phalāni***
> *teṣu bera-pīṭha-garbhagṛhodbhavāni brāhmaṇa-rājā-rāṣṭra-vināśa-kāraṇāni | viśeṣeṇa kartr-ārādhakayośca prāsādotpannāni kṣatriyasya | prāsāde adhiṣṭhāna-pāda-prastara-grīvā-śikharodbhavāni krameṇa kṣatriya-vaiśya-śūdra-itara-mukhyābhiṣekayoni-yuvarāja-abhiṣiktamahārājānām ātaṅka-bhaya-anāvṛṣṭikarāṇi | prathamāvaraṇe dvijātīnāṃ, mantri-purohitānāṃ, māṇḍalikānāṃ ca bhayāvaham, prāṇapīḍādi-bhayapradaṃ ca | dvitīyāvaraṇādiṣu śūdrajātīnāṃ sarvaduḥkhakaram | sasya-nāśanaṃ, gaja-aśva-aja-mahiṣa-paśūnāṃ vināśakaram | agnikuṇḍa-purohitasya, caturmukha-sthāne dvijātīnāṃ, rudrālaye sarvajātīnāṃ, guhālaye tapasvināṃ māṇḍalikānāṃ ca, vakratuṇḍālaye senānīnāṃ, śriyaḥ ālaye rājapatnīnāṃ, durgālaye antyānāṃ, mātṛsthāne nikṛṣṭajātīnāṃ, somasthāne caoṣadhīnāṃ, bhāskarālaye nṛpa-vāhana-śastrajīvināṃ, anyadevalaye tattadbhaktānām api lakṣayet | āsthānamaṇḍapa-sabhāmaṇḍapa-rāja-veśma-prākāra-krīḍāsthāna-mahānasa-ācārya-yajamānādiṣu sarvatra varaṇa-kadamba-caityavṛkṣādiṣu sañjātānāṃ valmīka-raktapipīlikā-makṣikādīnāṃ phalāni digbhāgena vijñeyāni ||*

#### English Translation
*   **Locational Consequences of Portents:** 
    1.  Portents occurring on the deity icon (*bera*), pedestal (*pīṭha*), or inside the sanctum sanctorum (*garbhagṛha*) cause the destruction of the Brahmins, the king, and the state.
    2.  Anomalies in the temple structure (*prāsāda*) specifically affect the patron/sponsor and the priest, and are harmful to the Kshatriya.
    3.  Anomalies occurring in the base (*adhiṣṭhāna*), pillars (*pāda*), entablature (*prastara*), neck/drum (*grīvā*), or roof/tower (*śikhara*) of the temple bring panic, fear, and drought to the Kshatriyas, Vaishyas, Shudras, other communities, chiefs, crown princes, and anointed kings respectively.
    4.  Portents in the first enclosure (*prathamāvaraṇe*) bring fear and threat to life to the twice-born (Brahmins), ministers, family priests, and provincial governors.
    5.  Portents in the second enclosure (*dvitīyāvaraṇa*) bring all kinds of misery to Shudras, destroy crops, and kill elephants, horses, goats, buffaloes, and cattle.

*   **Affections by Deities:** 
    1.  Portents in the fire-altar (*agnikuṇḍa*) affect the priest; in the Brahmā shrine (*caturmukha-sthāne*), they affect the twice-born; in Śiva's temple (*rudrālaye*), they affect all castes; in Kumara's shrine (*guhālaye*), they affect ascetics and governors; in Gaṇeśa's shrine (*vakratuṇḍālaye*), they affect army commanders; in Lakṣmī's shrine (*śriyaḥ ālaye*), they affect queens; in Durgā's shrine (*durgālaye*), they affect outcasts; in the shrine of the Seven Mothers, they affect the lowest castes; in Soma's shrine, they affect medicinal herbs; and in the Sun temple (*bhāskarālaye*), they affect the king's drivers and soldiers.
    2.  For other temples, one should observe the same for their respective devotees.

*   **General Sites:** The results of the sudden appearance of anthills, red ants, and bees (*valmīka-raktapipīlikā-makṣikā*) on the Varana, Kadamba, or sacred fig trees (*caitya-vṛkṣa*) in assembly halls, palaces, ramparts, kitchens, or the quarters of priests and patrons should be determined according to the compass directions (*digbhāgena*).

### Section 7: Expiation Procedures for Terrestrial Portents

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **भौमेषु उत्तमत्वादि**
> अथ भौमेषु देवबेर-अर्चापीठ-गर्भगृहाभ्यन्तरेषु उत्पन्ना दोषा उत्तमोत्तमाः। प्रथमावरणे मध्यमाः। इतरेषु अधमाः। राजवेश्मनि उत्तमोत्तमाः। परितोऽङ्गणे उत्तमाः। अन्तर्वस्तुके मध्यमाः। बहिर्वस्तुकेषु अधमाः ॥
> ***bhaumeṣu uttamatvādi***
> *atha bhaumeṣu devabera-arcāpīṭha-garbhagṛhābhyantareṣu utpannā doṣā uttamottamāḥ | prathamāvaraṇe madhyamāḥ | itareṣu adhamāḥ | rājaveśmani uttamottamāḥ | parito'ṅgaṇe uttamāḥ | antarvastuke madhyamāḥ | bahirvastukeṣu adhamāḥ ||*
> 
> **दिव्यादिषु कर्तव्यम्**
> दिव्यान्तरिक्ष-भौमेषु चिकित्सा नास्ति, शान्तिर्भवति। भौमेषु चरेषु निमित्त-नैमित्तिकौ देशात् निर्वास्य शान्तिम् आरभेत। स्थिरेषु — प्रतिमा-रोदन-हसन-ज्वलनेषु, तरुणालये बेरं संस्थाप्य, तद्वेरस्थां शक्तिं कुम्भे समारोप्य, शान्तिं कृत्वा पुनःस्थापनम् आचरेत्।
> ***divyādiṣu kartavyam***
> *divyāntarikṣa-bhaumeṣu cikitsā nāsti, śāntirbhavati | bhaumeṣu careṣu nimitta-naimittikou deśāt nirvāsya śāntim ārabheta | sthireṣu — pratimā-rodana-hasana-jvalaneṣu, taruṇālaye beraṃ saṃsthāpya, tadverasthāṃ śaktiṃ kumbhe samāropya, śāntiṃ kṛtvā punaḥsthāpanam ācaret ||*
> 
> परिवर्तन-समारोपणाऽनन्तरं भित्त्या, अन्येन वा, पुनःस्थापनं कृत्वा पूर्ववत् शक्त्यादिकम् आचरेत्।
> स्वेदरुधिरस्रावयोश्च रोदनवद् आचरेत्। कृमिकीट-पतङ्ग-तृण-कवकाद्युद्भवे पूर्ववत् शक्तिं समारोप्य, पुनः सन्धानं युक्तितः कृत्वा, शान्त्यादि आचरेत् ॥
> *parivartana-samāropaṇā'nantaraṃ bhittyā, anyena vā, punaḥsthāpanaṃ kṛtvā pūrvavat śaktyādikam ācaret |*
> *svedarudhirasrāvayośca rodanavad ācaret | kṛmikīṭa-pataṅga-tṛṇa-kavakādyudbhave pūrvavat śaktiṃ samāropya, punaḥsandhānaṃ yuktitaḥ kṛtvā, śāntyādi ācaret ||*

#### English Translation
*   **Severity of Terrestrial Portents:** Terrestrial portents occurring on the deity, pedestal, or inside the sanctum are the most severe (*uttamottamāḥ*); in the first enclosure, medium (*madhyamāḥ*); elsewhere, low (*adhamaḥ*). In the royal palace, they are the most severe; around the courtyard, high; in internal matters, medium; and in external matters, low.

*   **Treatment of Moving Portents:** For celestial and atmospheric portents, there is no direct remedy (*cikitsā nāsti*), but pacification can be done (*śāntir bhavati*). For terrestrial portents in animate beings, one should remove/exile the abnormal creature (*nimitta-naimittikou deśāt nirvāsya*) and begin the pacification.

*   **Treatment of Animate Icons:** If the icon cries, laughs, or burns (*pratimā-rodana-hasana-jvalaneṣu*), one should install the deity in a temporary shrine (*taruṇālaye beraṃ saṃsthāpya*), project the divine power into a kumbha vessel (*kumbhe samāropya*), perform the pacification rites, and perform the re-installation (*punaḥ-sthāpanam ācaret*).

*   **Sweating and Bleeding:** The same should be done if the icon sweats or bleeds (*sveda-rudhirasrāvayośca*).

*   **Fungus or Insects:** If worms, insects, moths, grass, or fungi/mushrooms sprout inside the temple, having projected the power as before, repaired/renovated the structure appropriately (*punaḥ-sandhānaṃ yuktitaḥ kṛtvā*), one should perform the pacification and other rituals.

### Section 8: Rectifying Cracks and Holes in Structures

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> अकाल-स्फोटनादिकेऽपि, तद्वद् वल्मीकोद्भवे, देव-ध्रुवार्चा-पीठ-आलाभ्यन्तरेषु, तरुणालये देवं प्रतिष्ठाप्य, यावद् दोष-दर्शनं तावत् खनित्वा, सर्वत्र संशोध्य, पञ्चगव्यादिनाऽभ्युक्ष्य, पुनः सन्धानं कृत्वा, पुनःस्थापनम् आचरेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> akālasphoṭanādike'pi, tadvad valmīkodbhave, deva-dhruvārcā-pīṭha-ālābhyantareṣu, taruṇālaye devaṃ pratiṣṭhāpya, yāvad doṣa-darśanaṃ tāvat khanitvā, sarvatra saṃśodhya, pañcagavyādinā'bhyukṣya, punaḥsandhānaṃ kṛtvā, punaḥsthāpanam ācaret ||

**Translation (English):**
*   **Anthills or Cracks inside Sanctum:** If there is sudden/untimely splitting/cracking, or if anthills grow inside the sanctum, pedestal, or temple, one should install the deity in the temporary temple, excavate the soil as deep as the anomaly is seen (*yāvad doṣa-darśanaṃ tāvat khanitvā*), purify the entire area, sprinkle it with the five cow products (*pañcagavyādinā'bhyukṣya*), perform the repair/renovation, and perform the re-installation.

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> प्रासादादौ इष्टका-बिम्बेषु विवरेषु उत्पन्नेषु, मृत्-सुधा-लेपेन शिल्पिना अन्येन वा, व्यपरोधिते रन्ध्रे वा, दधि-मरीचादीनि जले पेषयित्वा आपूर्य, अश्मना कर्करैः आलिप्य, दिव्यशान्तिं कुर्यात्। पुनरुत्थितं चेद् एवम् एव समाचरेत् ॥

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> prāsādādou iṣṭakā-bimbeṣu vivareṣu utpanneṣu, mṛt-sudhā-lepena śilpinā anyena vā, vyaparodhite randhre vā, dadhi-marīcādīni jale peṣayitvā āpūrya, aśmanā karkaraiḥ ālipya, divyaśāntiṃ kuryāt | punarutthitaṃ ced evam eva samācaret ||

**Translation (English):**
*   **Holes and Cracks in Masonry:** If holes or cracks (*vivareṣu*) appear in the brickwork or icons of the temple structure, a sculptor or another person should seal the holes (*vyaparodhite randhre vā*) using clay and plaster (*mṛt-sudhā-lepena*), grind curd, pepper, etc. in water and fill/smear the area (*dadhi-marīcādīni jale peṣayitvā āpūrya*), plaster it with stone powder and gravel (*aśmanā karkaraiḥ ālipya*), and perform the celestial pacification ritual (*divya-śāntiṃ kuryāt*). If the cracks reappear (*punarutthitaṃ ced*), one should do exactly the same.

### Section 9: Expiation for Insects, Desecration, and Royal Portents

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> मक्षिकादीनि तद् व्यपोह्य शुद्धं कृत्वा शान्तिम् आरभेत।
> *makṣikādīni tad vyapohya śuddhaṃ kṛtvā śāntim ārabheta ||*
> 
> अस्पृश्य-स्पर्शने शुद्धं कृत्वा शान्तिं समाचरेत्।
> *aspṛśya-sparśane śuddhaṃ kṛtvā śāntiṃ samācaret ||*
> 
> स्थूणादि-रोहणादिषु पुनः सन्धानं कृत्वा शान्तिम् आरभेत।
> *sthūṇādi-rohaṇādiṣu punaḥ sandhānaṃ kṛtvā śāntim ārabheta ||*
> 
> अकाले वृक्ष-पतनादिषु तत् सन्त्यज्य शान्तिं कुर्यात्।
> *akāle vṛkṣa-patanādiṣu tat santyajya śāntiṃ kuryāt ||*
> 
> राजगृहे च उत्पन्ने राजानं वेश्मान्तरं प्राप्य, शान्तिं कृत्वा पुनरभिषेकं कृत्वा देव-ब्राह्मण-अनुज्ञया पुनः गृह-प्रवेशं कारयेत् ॥
> *rājagṛhe ca utpanne rājānaṃ veśmāntaraṃ prāpya, śāntiṃ kṛtvā punarabhiṣekaṃ kṛtvā deva-brāhmaṇa-anujñayā punaḥ gṛhapraveśaṃ kārayet ||*

#### English Translation
*   **Insects and Desecration:** One should remove swarms of flies (*makṣikādīni*), purify the area, and begin the pacification. If the temple is touched by untouchables/outcasts (*aspṛśya-sparśane*), having purified it, one should perform the pacification.

*   **Pillar Sprouting and Falling Trees:** If pillars or posts sprout/grow (*sthūṇādi-rohaṇādiṣu*), having repaired/renovated them, one should perform the pacification. If trees fall out of season (*akāle vṛkṣa-patanādiṣu*), having discarded/removed them (*tat santyajya*), one should perform the pacification.

*   **Portents in the Royal Palace:** If portents occur in the royal palace (*rājagṛhe ca utpanne*), one should move the king to another palace (*rājānaṃ veśmāntaraṃ prāpya*), perform the pacification, conduct the coronation/anointing ceremony again (*punar-abhiṣekaṃ kṛtvā*), and with the permission of the deity and the Brahmins (*deva-brāhmaṇa-anujñayā*), have him enter the palace again (*punaḥ gṛhapraveśaṃ kārayet*).

*   **Colophon:** (Thus ends the Seventy-fifth Chapter, titled *“The Rules of Pacification for Portents and Bad Omens”* (Utpāta-śānti-vidhiḥ), in the *Vimānārcanākalpa* of the *Vaikhānasa* school, compiled by Sage Marīci.)

#### Colophon

> (इति श्रीवैखानसे मरीचिप्रोक्ते विमानार्चनाकल्पे उत्पातशान्तिविधिर्नाम पञ्चसप्ततितमः पटलः ॥७५॥)

## Glossary of Key Terms

1.  **Utpāta / Adbhuta (उत्पात / अद्भुत):** A portent, anomaly, or unnatural event indicating divine displeasure or future calamity.
2.  **Divya-utpāta (दिव्योत्पात):** Celestial portents (planetary conflicts, eclipses, etc.).
3.  **Āntarikṣa-utpāta (आन्तरिक्षोत्पात):** Atmospheric portents (rainbows at night, meteors, double suns, etc.).
4.  **Bhauma-utpāta (भौमोत्पात):** Terrestrial portents, affecting animate beings (*cara*) or inanimate things (*sthira*).
5.  **Pratimā-rodana / Hasana (प्रतिमारोदन / हसन):** The weeping or laughing of a deity icon, considered a severe terrestrial portent.
6.  **Saudha-vivara (सौधविवर):** Cracks or holes appearing in brick/masonry structures of the temple.
7.  **Mṛt-sudhā-lepa (मृत्सुधालेप):** Plastering material made of clay and lime/mortar.
8.  **Paundarīka-agni (पौण्डरीकाग्नि):** The sacred fire in which pacification fire-offerings (*mahā-śānti*) are made.
9.  **Veśmāntara (वेश्मान्तर):** Moving to another residence or palace.
10. **Gṛha-praveśa (गृहप्रवेश):** The ritual of entering a newly purified house or palace.
