# Vimanarchana Kalpa of Sage Marīci (Vaikhānasa Āgama)
## Chapter 83: Praṇava-svarūpa-vidhiḥ (Rules on the Divine Form and Nature of the Pranava / Om)

This document contains the Sanskrit text, IAST transliteration, and English translation of the 83rd Chapter (*Tryaśītitemaḥ Paṭalaḥ*) of the **Vimānārcanākalpa** (attributed to Sage Marīci, representing the Vaikhānasa school of Vaishnavism).

This chapter presents the cosmic and phonetic analysis of *Praṇava* (Om). It explains:
1.  The Upanishadic declaration that "Om is Brahman", analyzing the three letters A, U, and M, and matching them with the Vedas (Rig, Yajur, Sama, Atharva), Gunas (Sattva, Rajas, Tamas), and primary deities (Viṣṇu, Brahmā, Rudra).
2.  The cosmic anatomy of the *Praṇava-puruṣa* (the personified sound Om), correlating the physical and subtle components of the universe (underworlds, earth, stars, oceans, mountains, Vedas, and daily sacrifices) with the limbs and organs of the divine sound.
3.  The absolute necessity of prefixing all Vedic mantras and silent recitations (*japa*) with Om to prevent their spiritual efficacy from being lost.

### Section 1: Phonetic and Cosmic Analysis of the Pranava

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **अथ त्र्यशीतितमः पटलः**
> **अथातः मन्त्राणां कल्पं व्याख्यास्यामः —**
> **प्रणवस्य ब्रह्मस्वरूपतादिकम्**

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **atha tryaśītitamaḥ paṭalaḥ**
> **athāto mantrāṇāṃ kalpaṃ vyākhyāsyāmaḥ —**
> **praṇavasya brahmasvarūpatādikam**

**Translation (English):**
*   **The Life of Mantras:** Now, we shall explain the ritual science of mantras (*athāto mantrāṇāṃ kalpaṃ vyākhyāsyāmaḥ*). The life-breath of all mantras is the Supreme Brahman in the form of Pranava (*mantrāṇāṃ prāṇaḥ praṇavākāraṃ brahmarūpam*). The Śruti declares: "Om is Brahman" (*‘om iti brahma’*). Therefore, the entire universe is not other than the Pranava; sages read the Supreme Lord (Bhagavan) as the Pranava.

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> मन्त्राणां प्राणः (or प्राणाः) प्रणवाकारं ब्रह्मरूपम्; ‘ओम् इति ब्रह्म’ (Taittiriya Aranyaka 10.64) इति श्रुतिः। तस्माद् जगत् कृत्स्नं प्रणवाद् अन्यं नास्ति; भगवन्तं प्रणवम् इति पठन्ति। प्रणवम् उच्चार्य — अकार-उकार-मकाराः इति; तेषां यथाक्रमेण ऋग्-यजुः-साम-अथर्व-मयाः, सत्त्व-रजस्-तमो-गुणाः, श्वेत-पीत-कृष्ण-निभाः, भूर्भुवःस्वः-स्थिताः, विष्णु-ब्रह्म-रुद्राः (or विष्णु-ब्रह्म-हराः) अधिदेवत्याः भवन्ति।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> mantrāṇāṃ prāṇaḥ (or prāṇāḥ) praṇavākāraṃ brahmarūpam; ‘om iti brahma’ iti śrutiḥ | tasmād jagat kṛtsnaṃ praṇavād anyaṃ nāsti; bhagavantaṃ praṇavam iti paṭhanti | praṇavam uccārya — akāra-ukāra-makārāḥ iti; teṣāṃ yathākrameṇa ṛg-yajuḥ-sāma-atharva-mayāḥ, sattva-rajas-tamo-guṇāḥ, śveta-pīta-kṛṣṇa-nibhāḥ, bhūrbhuvaḥsvaḥ-sthitāḥ, viṣṇu-brahma-rudrāḥ adhidevatyāḥ bhavanti ||

**Translation (English):**
*   **The Three Letters:** The Pranava is pronounced as the three syllables: A (*akāra*), U (*ukāra*), and M (*makāra*). In order, they correspond to:
    1.  **Vedas:** The Rig, Yajur, Sama, and Atharva Vedas (*ṛg-yajuḥ-sāma-atharva-mayāḥ*).
    2.  **Gunas:** The cosmic attributes of Sattva (purity), Rajas (activity), and Tamas (inertia) (*sattva-rajas-tamo-guṇāḥ*).
    3.  **Colors:** Bright white, golden yellow, and deep black (*śveta-pīta-kṛṣṇa-nibhāḥ*).
    4.  **Realms:** The physical, atmospheric, and celestial planes (*bhūrbhuvaḥsvaḥ-sthitāḥ*).
    5.  **Presiding Deities:** Viṣṇu, Brahmā, and Rudra (*viṣṇu-brahma-rudrāḥ adhidevatyāḥ*).

#### Verse / Paragraph 3
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> अकारो वलयाकारः, उकारः कुटिलाकारः, मकारो बिन्दुनादः, प्रणवस्य रूपस्य (or शिखायाः) अंशो भवति। अकार-उकार-संयोगाद् ओकारो भवति, बिन्दुनाद-संयुक्तम् ‘ओम्’ इति। प्रणवस्य ऋषि-च्छन्दः-अधिदेवताः पूर्ववत्। (गोप्तारं सर्वस्य / गोप्यतरं सर्वस्य) इति विज्ञायते ॥

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> akāro valayākāraḥ, ukāraḥ kuṭilākāraḥ, makāro bindunādaḥ, praṇavasya rūpasya (or śikhāyāḥ) aṃśo bhavati | akāra-ukāra-saṃyogād okāro bhavati, bindunāda-saṃyuktamp ‘om’ iti | praṇavasya ṛṣi-cchandas-adhidevatāḥ pūrvavat | (goptāraṃ sarvasya / gopyataraṃ sarvasya) iti vijñāyate ||

**Translation (English):**
*   **Phonetic Structure:** The letter A is ring-shaped (*valayākāraḥ*); the letter U is curved (*kuṭilākāraḥ*); the letter M is the point and nasal resonance (*bindunādaḥ*), which are the constituent parts of the Pranava's body. By joining A and U, the sound O is produced (*akāra-ukāra-saṃyogād okāro bhavati*), which, when integrated with the bindu-nada, constitutes the sound "Om". The seer, meter, and deity of the Pranava are as declared before. It is known as the ultimate protector of all (*goptāraṃ sarvasya*).

### Section 2: Cosmic Form of the Pranava-purusha and Ritual Use

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> अथातः प्रणवस्वरूपं वक्ष्ये —
> **प्रणवस्य जीवात्मत्वम्**
> *athātaḥ praṇavasvarūpaṃ vakṣye —*
> ***praṇavasya jīvātmatvam***
> 
> पीतवर्णः, सहस्रशीर्षः, सहस्राक्षः, सहस्रबाहुः, सहस्रोरुः, सहस्रपादः, ऊर्ध्वकेशः (or ऊर्ध्वलिङ्गः), रक्तास्य-पाणि-पादः, शुकपिच्छ-अम्बरधरो, विष्णुः जीवात्मा।
> *pītavarṇaḥ, sahasraśīrṣaḥ, sahasrāxaḥ, sahasrabāhuḥ, sahasroruḥ, sahasrapādaḥ, ūrdhvakeśaḥ (or ūrdhvalingaḥ), raktāsya-pāṇi-pādaḥ, śukapiccha-ambaradharo, viṣṇuḥ jīvātmā ||*
> 
> ब्रह्मा वदनं, ईशः कोपः, चित्तं सोमः, तलातलादि-सप्त-पातालाः पादाः, भुजङ्गाः अङ्गुलयः, अप्सरसो नखाः (or नद्यः), भूरादि-सप्तलोकाः कुक्षिः, मरुतो वसवश्च प्राणाः (or अङ्गानि), महत्-अहङ्कार-ब्रह्माण्डाः शीर्षाणि, अग्निष्टोमादि-यज्ञाः केशाः, व्योम गात्राणि (or तनुः), चन्द्र-सूर्यो चक्षुषी, दिशः कर्णौ, अश्विनौ नासिके, दन्ताः वायवः, जिह्वा सरस्वती, ओष्ठौ सन्ध्ये, नित्य-अग्निहोत्राणि हास्यं, सभ्यो वदनम् (or अग्निः वदनम्)।
> *brahmā vadanaṃ, īśaḥ kopaḥ, cittaṃ somaḥ, talātalādi-sapta-pātālāḥ pādāḥ, bhujaṅgāḥ aṅgulayaḥ, apsaraso naxāḥ (or nadyaḥ), bhūrādi-saptalokāḥ kukṣiḥ, maruto vasavaśca prāṇāḥ, mahat-ahaṅkāra-brahmāṇḍāḥ śīrṣāṇi, agniṣṭomādi-yajñāḥ keśāḥ, vyoma gātrāṇi, candra-sūryo caxuṣī, diśaḥ karṇou, aśvinou nāsike, dantāḥ vāyavaḥ, jihvā sarasvatī, oṣṭhou sandhye, nitya-agnihotrāṇi hāsyaṃ, sabhyo vadanam ||*
> 
> परिवाराः प्रजापतयः, दिशो बाहवः, रुद्राः सन्धि-हस्ताः, नक्षत्राणि अङ्गुलयः, तारकाः नखाः, वृषणौ मित्रावरुणौ, उपस्थः प्रजापतिः, मरुतः प्राणः (or रोमाणः), अस्थीनि पर्वताः, मेदो मांसम्, ओषधयः शोणितम् (or रोमाणि), प्राणाः नद्यः, मूत्रं समुद्रः, वपुः काञ्चनं, रेतोऽमृतम्, लोकालोकः त्वक्, ऋषयो रोमकूपाः, स्वेदो वर्षः, वसनं सर्वोदधयः, वेदेतिहासम् आभरणं, सृष्टि-स्थिति-संहाराः क्रीडा, कल्प-व्याकरण-शिक्षा-निरुक्तादि-चतुष्षष्टि-कलाः व्याख्यानम्।
> *parivārāḥ prajāpatayaḥ, diśo bāhavaḥ, rudrāḥ sandhi-hastāḥ, naxatrāṇi aṅgulayaḥ, tārakāḥ naxāḥ, vṛṣaṇou mitrāvaruṇou, upasthaḥ prajāpatiḥ, marutaḥ prāṇaḥ, asthīni parvatāḥ, medo māṃsam, oṣadhayaḥ śoṇitam, prāṇāḥ nadyaḥ, mūtraṃ samudraḥ, vapuḥ kāñcanaṃ, reto'mṛtam, lokālokaḥ tvak, ṛṣayo romakūpāḥ, svedo varṣaḥ, vasanaṃ sarvodadhayaḥ, vedetihāsam ābharaṇaṃ, sṛṣṭi-sthiti-saṃhārāḥ krīḍā, kalpa-vyākaraṇa-śikṣā-niruktādi-catuṣṣaṣṭi-kalāḥ vyākhyānam ||*
> 
> बहुना किम्? जगत् सर्वं प्रणव-दैवत्यम्; त्रिसन्धिषु यथाशक्ति जपम् आचरेत्। त्रिमात्रो द्विमात्रो वा सर्वकर्मणाम् अग्रतस्तदन्ते तथा करोति; प्रणव-पूर्वम् एव सर्वं जपम् आरभेत्। प्रणव-हीनं यदि स्यात् सर्वं नश्यति; तस्मात् प्रणवाद् अन्यो मन्त्रो नास्ति इति विज्ञायते ॥
> *bahunā kim? jagat sarvaṃ praṇava-daivatyam; trisandhiṣu yathāśakti japam ācaret | trimātro dvimātro vā sarvakarmaṇām agratastadante tathā karoti; praṇava-pūrvam eva sarvaṃ japam ārabhet | praṇava-hīnaṃ yadi syāt sarvaṃ naśyati; tasmāt praṇavād anyo mantro nāsti iti vijñāyate ||*

#### English Translation
*   **The Form of the Pranava-Puruṣa:** Now, I shall describe the cosmic form of the Pranava (*athātaḥ praṇavasvarūpaṃ vakṣye*). He is yellow-skinned (*pītavarṇaḥ*), thousand-headed, thousand-eyed, thousand-armed, thousand-thighed, and thousand-legged (*sahasraśīrṣaḥ sahasrāxaḥ sahasrabāhuḥ sahasroruḥ sahasrapādaḥ*). His hair rises upward (*ūrdhvakeśaḥ*), his face, hands, and feet are reddish (*raktāsya-pāṇi-pādaḥ*), he wears garments of the color of parrot feathers, and he is Viṣṇu who is the indwelling cosmic soul (*viṣṇuḥ jīvātmā*).

*   **The Cosmic Anatomy:** Equating the cosmos to the limbs of the Pranava-purusha:
    *   **Mouth and Mind:** Brahmā is his face, Rudra/Īśa is his anger, Chandra/Soma is his mind (*cittaṃ somaḥ*).
    *   **Lower Realms:** The seven underworld realms (Talatala, etc.) are his feet; serpents are his fingers; Apsaras are his fingernails (or rivers).
    *   **Torso and Head:** The seven upper worlds starting with Bhūḥ are his belly (*bhūrādi-saptalokāḥ kukṣiḥ*); the Maruts and Vasus are his vital breaths. The cosmic elements of Mahat (intellect), Ahaṅkāra (ego), and the physical Universe are his heads (*mahat-ahaṅkāra-brahmāṇḍāḥ śīrṣāṇi*).
    *   **Hair and Limbs:** Great sacrifices like Agniṣṭoma are his hair (*agniṣṭomādi-yajñāḥ keśāḥ*); space is his body; Moon and Sun are his eyes (*candra-sūryo caxuṣī*); the directions are his ears; the twin Aśvins are his nostrils; his teeth are the winds; Sarasvatī is his tongue; his lips are the morning and evening twilights (*oṣṭhou sandhye*).
    *   **Laughter and Speech:** The daily fire offerings are his smile; the Sabhya sacrificial fire is his mouth.
    *   **Retinue and Limbs:** The Prajāpatis are his retinue; the cardinal directions are his arms; the Rudras are his joints and hands; the asterisms are his fingers; the stars are his nails; Mitra and Varuṇa are his testicles; Prajāpatī is his generative organ; the winds (Maruts) are his hair; the mountains are his bones (*asthīni parvatāḥ*).
    *   **Tissue and Fluids:** Fat is his flesh; plants are his blood; his internal channels (pranas) are the rivers; his urine is the ocean (*mūtraṃ samudraḥ*); his body is gold; his semen is the nectar of immortality (*reto'mṛtam*).
    *   **Skin and Sweat:** The boundary mountain Lokāloka is his skin; the ancient sages are his pores; his sweat is the falling rain (*svedo varṣaḥ*); his garments are all the oceans (*vasanaṃ sarvodadhayaḥ*).
    *   **Activity and Arts:** The Vedas and Histories are his ornaments (*vedetihāsam ābharaṇam*); cosmic creation, preservation, and dissolution are his sport/play (*sṛṣṭi-sthiti-saṃhārāḥ krīḍā*); the sixty-four arts such as ritual, grammar, phonetics, and etymology are his speech/explanation.

*   **The Supreme Instruction:** What is the need of saying more? The entire universe is dominated by the Pranava (*jagat sarvaṃ praṇava-daivatyam*). During the three daily twilights, according to his capacity, one should perform the silent recitation (*japam ācaret*). The Pranava of three matras or two matras must be uttered at the beginning and end of all rituals (*sarvakarmaṇām agratastadante*). One should begin all japa only prefixed with the Pranava. If a ritual is devoid of the Pranava (*praṇava-hīnaṃ yadi syāt*), everything is completely destroyed (*sarvaṃ naśyati*). Therefore, it is known that there is no mantra other than the Pranava.

## Glossary of Key Terms

1.  **Praṇava (प्रणव):** The sacred syllable Om, representing the primordial sound, the source of all speech, and the essence of Brahman.
2.  **Jīvātman (जीवात्मा):** The individual soul or the indwelling consciousness, identified here on a cosmic scale with the Pranava-purusha.
3.  **Vēda-mayāḥ (वेदमयाः):** Composed of or embodying the Vedas, referring to the letters of Om representing the Rig, Yajur, Sama, and Atharva scriptures.
4.  **Bindu-nāda (बिन्दुनाद):** The point (bindu) and the nasalized resonance (nada) of the syllable Om, representing the transcendent fourth state of consciousness (Turiya).
5.  **Agniṣṭoma (अग्निष्टोम):** A major five-day Soma sacrifice, representing the highest order of Vedic fire offerings.
6.  **Lokāloka (लोकालोक):** The mythical mountain range that encircles the outer boundary of the visible universe, dividing the light world from the dark void.
7.  **Catuṣṣaṣṭi-kalā (चतुष्षष्टिकला):** The traditional sixty-four arts and sciences of ancient India, embodying all human knowledge and skills.
8.  **Trimātra (त्रिमात्र):** The three-measure pronunciation of Om (A-U-M), denoting the physical, subtle, and causal realms.
9.  **Dvimātra (द्विमात्र):** The two-measure quick pronunciation of Om, used for swift transitions during Agamic rituals.
10. **Aśvins (अश्विनौ):** The twin divine physicians of the gods, representing the nostrils and breath of the cosmic Puruṣa.
