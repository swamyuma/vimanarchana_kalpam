# Chapter 21: Rules for the Manifestations/Incarnations (Prādurbhāva-vidhiḥ)
### Section 1: Introduction & General Layout of Manifestations

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **अथैकविंशः पटलः -**
> अथ प्रादुर्भावाऽऽविर्भावानां वर्ण-वाहनादि व्याख्यास्यामः - मत्स्य-कूर्म-वराह-नारसिंह-वामन-राम-राम-राम-कृष्ण-कल्किन इति दशप्रादुर्भावान् प्रथमावरणे, द्वितीये वा, पृथक् पृथक् भित्तिपार्श्वे, प्रासादाभिमुखं चित्रं चित्रार्धं, आभासं वा, सङ्कल्प्य अभिमुखे तत्तत्कौतुकं च संन्यस्य अर्चयेत् ॥

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **athaikaviṃśaḥ paṭalaḥ -**
> atha prādurbhāvā'’virbhāvānāṃ varṇa-vāhanādi vyākhyāsyāmaḥ - matsya-kūrma-varāha-nārasiṃha-vāmana-rāma-rāma-rāma-kṛṣṇa-kalkina iti daśaprādurbhāvān prathamāvaraṇe, dvitīye vā, pṛthak pṛthak bhittipārśve, prāsādābhimukhaṃ citraṃ citrārdhaṃ, ābhāsaṃ vā, saṅkalpya abhimukhe tattatkautukaṃ ca saṃnyasya arcayet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Worship of the Manifestations (*Prādurbhāvāḥ*):**
    *   Now we shall explain the complexions, vehicles, and other iconographic rules of the manifestations (*prādurbhāvāḥ*) and appearances (*āvirbhāvāḥ*).
    *   One should worship the ten manifestations (*daśa-prādurbhāvāḥ*)—namely Matsya, Kūrma, Varāha, Narasiṃha, Vāmana, Rāma (Paraśurāma), Rāma (Rāghava/Ramachandra), Rāma (Balabhadra/Balarama), Kṛṣṇa, and Kalkin—either in the first enclosure or the second.
    *   They should be consecrated on separate temple walls, facing the main sanctum (*prāsāda-abhimukham*), and rendered as full-relief sculptures (*citram*), high/half-relief (*citrārdham*), or paintings (*ābhāsam*). In front of each, one should place the respective processional image (*kautuka-beram*) and perform the worship.

### Section 2: Matsya, Kūrma, Varāha, Narasiṃha, and Vāmana

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **मत्स्यस्य लक्षणम् -**
> पूर्वद्वारोत्तरे मत्स्यरूपं श्वेताभं, रक्तास्यं, रक्तनेत्रं, कृष्णपक्षयुतं सङ्कल्प्य, अभिमुखे कौतुकं चतुर्भुजं संन्यस्य "मत्स्यं प्रलयसंहारं स्रष्टारं वेदमयम्" इति अर्चयेत् ॥
> ***matsyasya lakṣaṇam -***
> *pūrvadvārottere matsyarūpaṃ śvetābhaṃ, raktāsyaṃ, raktanetram, kṛṣṇapakṣayutaṃ saṅkalpya, abhimukhe kautukaṃ caturbhujaṃ saṃnyasya "matsyaṃ pralayasaṃhāraṃ sraṣṭāraṃ vedamayam" iti arcayet.*
> 
> **कूर्मस्य लक्षणम् -**
> तद्दक्षिणे कूर्मरूपं श्वेताभं, पञ्चवर्णयुतं सङ्कल्प्य, अभिमुखे कौतुकं चतुर्भुजं संन्यस्य "कूर्मरूपं सुधाकारं महाशक्तिं महाबलम्" इति अर्चयेत् ॥
> ***kūrmasya lakṣaṇam -***
> *taddakṣiṇe kūrmarūpaṃ śvetābhaṃ, pañcavarṇayutaṃ saṅkalpya, abhimukhe kautukaṃ caturbhujaṃ saṃnyasya "kūrmarūpaṃ sudhākāraṃ mahāśaktiṃ mahābalam" iti arcayet.*
> 
> (मत्स्य-कूर्मयोः वाहन-केत्वादीन् आदिमूर्तेरिव समाचरेत्) ॥
> *(matsya-kūrmayoḥ vāhana-ketvādīn ādimurteriva samācaret).*
> 
> **वराहस्य लक्षणम् -**
> दक्षिणे वराहं पूर्ववत् सङ्कल्प्य, अभिमुखे कौतुकं चतुर्भुजं विष्णुरूपं समर्चयेत् ॥
> ***varāhasya lakṣaṇam -***
> *dakṣiṇe varāhaṃ pūrvavat saṅkalpya, abhimukhe kautukaṃ caturbhujaṃ viṣṇurūpaṃ samarcayet.*
> 
> **नृसिंहस्य लक्षणम् -**
> पश्चिमे नृसिंहः (प्राङ्मुखः) - सिंहासनोपरि द्वौ पादौ व्यत्यस्य, उत्कुटिकासीनः, बद्धयोगपट्टकः, चतुर्भुजः, शङ्ख-चक्र-धरः, जानूपरि प्रसारितहस्तः; भवेद् एष विशेषः। अन्यत् सर्वं पूर्ववत्। कौतुकं तद्रूपं चतुर्भुजं विष्णुरूपं वा सङ्कल्प्य अर्चयेत् ॥
> ***nṛsiṃhasya lakṣaṇam -***
> *paścime nṛsiṃhaḥ (prāṅmukhaḥ) - siṃhāsanopari dvau pādau vyatyasya, utkuṭikāsīnaḥ, baddhayogapaṭṭakaḥ, caturbhujaḥ, śaṅkha-cakra-dharaḥ, jānūpari prasāritahastaḥ; bhaved eṣa viśeṣaḥ. anyat sarvaṃ pūrvavat. kautukaṃ tadrūpaṃ caturbhujaṃ viṣṇurūpaṃ vā saṅkalpya arcayet.*
> 
> **वामनस्य लक्षणम् -**
> उत्तरे वामनं पूर्ववत् सङ्कल्प्य, कौतुकं चतुर्भुजमेव कृत्वा अर्चयेत् ॥
> ***vāmanasya lakṣaṇam -***
> *uttare vāmanaṃ pūrvavat saṅkalpya, kautukaṃ caturbhujameva kṛtvā arcayet.*

#### English Translation
*   **1. Matsya (The Fish Incarnation):**
    *   **Location:** North of the East Gate.
    *   **Iconography:** Consecrated in the form of a fish, white-complexioned, with a red mouth, red eyes, and dark fins (*kṛṣṇa-pakṣa-yutam*).
    *   **Processional Image & Worship:** In front of it, place a four-armed processional image (*kautuka-beram*). Mantra: *"matsyaṃ pralayasaṃhāraṃ sraṣṭāraṃ vedamayam"*.

*   **2. Kūrma (The Tortoise Incarnation):**
    *   **Location:** South of Matsya.
    *   **Iconography:** Consecrated in the form of a tortoise, white-complexioned, and possessing five colors.
    *   **Processional Image & Worship:** In front of it, place a four-armed processional image. Mantra: *"kūrmarūpaṃ sudhākāraṃ mahāśaktiṃ mahābalam"*.
    *   *(Note: The vehicles, banners, and other secondary rules for Matsya and Kurma should be executed in the same manner as for the primary deity).*

*   **3. Varāha (The Boar Incarnation):**
    *   **Location:** South of the main shrine.
    *   **Iconography:** Consecrated in the boar form as detailed in earlier chapters, with a four-armed Viṣṇu-form processional image in front.

*   **4. Narasiṃha (The Man-Lion Incarnation):**
    *   **Location:** West of the main shrine, facing East.
    *   **Iconography:** Seated on a lion-throne (*siṃhāsana*) with his two feet crossed on the seat (*utkuṭikāsīnaḥ*), bound by a yogic belt (*baddha-yogapaṭṭakaḥ*), four-armed, holding the conch and discus, with his hands extended over his knees (*jānūpari prasārita-hastaḥ*). This is his specific posture; all other attributes are like the primary deity.
    *   **Processional Image:** In front, place a processional image of identical form (or a four-armed Viṣṇu form).

*   **5. Vāmana (The Dwarf Incarnation):**
    *   **Location:** North of the main shrine.
    *   **Iconography:** Consecrated in the dwarf form as detailed previously, with a four-armed processional image placed in front.

### Section 3: Paraśurāma, Rāghava (Śrī Rama), Sītā, and Retinue

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **परशुरामस्य लक्षणम् -**
> आग्नेय्यां परशुरामः (पश्चिमाभिमुखः उत्तराभिमुखो वा) - रक्ताभः, श्वेताम्बरधरः, दक्षिणकरेण परशुधरः, उद्देश्य-वामकरः (pointing forward with left hand), जटामकुटयुतः, सोपवीतः, कमलापतिः (printed कमळेशः). अन्यत् सर्वं आदिमूर्तिरिव। मन्त्रः - "रामं ऋषिसुतं विष्णुं परशुपाणिम्" इति ॥
> ***paraśurāmasya lakṣaṇam -***
> *āgneyyāṃ paraśurāmaḥ (paścimābhimukhaḥ uttarābhimukho vā) - raktābhaḥ, śvetāmbaradharaḥ, dakṣiṇakareṇa paraśudharaḥ, uddeśya-vāmakaraḥ, jaṭāmakuṭayutaḥ, sopavītaḥ, kamalāpatiḥ. anyat sarvaṃ ādimurtiriva. mantraḥ - "rāmaṃ ṛṣisutaṃ viṣṇuṃ paraśupāṇim" iti.*
> 
> **कमलायाः (तद्देव्याः) लक्षणम् -**
> तद्दक्षिणे कमलां सीतावत् कारयेत् ॥
> ***kamalāyāḥ lakṣaṇam -***
> *taddakṣiṇe kamalāṃ sītāvat kārayet.*
> 
> **राघवस्य (श्रीरामस्य) लक्षणम् -**
> नैर्ऋत्यां राघवः (उत्तराभिमुखः प्राङ्मुखो वा) - श्यामाभः, रक्ताम्बरधरः, मकुटादिसर्वाभरणयुक्तः, श्रीवत्सवक्षाः, द्विभुजः, दक्षिणकरेण शरधरः, वामकरेण धनुर् आलम्ब्य त्रिभङ्गसंस्थितः (printed त्रिभङ्गवास्थितो), माघमासे पुनर्वसुजातः, सीतानाथः। अन्यत् सर्वं आदिमूर्तिरिव। मन्त्रः - "रामं दाशरथिं वीरं काकुत्स्थम्" इति ॥
> ***rāghavasya lakṣaṇam -***
> *nairṛtyāṃ rāghavaḥ (uttarābhimukhaḥ prāṅmukho vā) - śyāmābhaḥ, raktāmbaradharaḥ, makuṭādisarvābharaṇayutaḥ, śrīvatsavakṣāḥ, dvibhujaḥ, dakṣiṇakareṇa śaradharaḥ, vāmakareṇa dhanur ālambya tribhaṅgasaṃsthitaḥ, māghamāse punarvasujātaḥ, sītānāthaḥ. anyat sarvaṃ ādimurtiriva. mantraḥ - "rāmaṃ dāśarathiṃ vīraṃ kākutstham" iti.*
> 
> **सीतायाः लक्षणम् -**
> तद्दक्षिणे सीता - हेमाभा, शुक्लाम्बराधरा, धृतपद्मवामहस्ता, प्रसारितदक्षिणकरा, सुस्थितदक्षिणपादा, आकुञ्चितवामपादा, उद्वद्धकुन्तला (printed उद्धद्धकुन्तळा), करण्डमकुटयुक्ता वा, सी-आद्यक्षरबीजा। अन्यत् सर्वं श्रीवत्। मन्त्रः - "सीताम् अयोनिजां लक्ष्मीं वैदेहीम्" इति ॥
> ***sītāyāḥ lakṣaṇam -***
> *taddakṣiṇe sītā - hemābhā, śuklāmbaradharā, dhṛtapadmavāmahastā, prasāritadakṣiṇakarā, susthitadakṣiṇapādā, ākuñcitavāmapādā, udvaddhakuntalā, karaṇḍamakuṭayuktā vā, sī-ādyakṣarabījā. anyat sarvaṃ śrīvat. mantraḥ - "sītām ayonijāṃ lakṣmīṃ vaidehīm" iti.*
> 
> **सौमित्रेः (लक्ष्मणस्य) लक्षणम् -**
> वामे सौमित्रिः - कनकाभः, श्यामाम्बरधरः, द्विभुजः, रामवत् त्रिभङ्गसंस्थितः, चापधरः, उद्वद्धकुन्तलः, वैशाखमासे रोहिणीजातः, ल-आद्यक्षरबीजः (printed लक्ष्याद्यक्षरबीजो). अन्यत् सर्वं रामवत्। मन्त्रः - "रामानुजं सौमित्रिं लक्ष्मणं लक्ष्मीवर्धनम्" इति ॥
> ***saumitreḥ lakṣaṇam -***
> *vāme saumitriḥ - kanakābhaḥ, śyāmāmbaradharaḥ, dvibhujaḥ, rāmavat tribhaṅgasaṃsthitaḥ, cāpadharaḥ, udvaddhakuntalaḥ, vaiśākhamāse rohiṇījātaḥ, la-ādyakṣarabījaḥ. anyat sarvaṃ rāmavat. mantraḥ - "rāmānujaṃ saumitriṃ lakṣmaṇaṃ lakṣmīvardhanam" iti.*
> 
> **भरतस्य लक्षणम् -**
> दक्षिणभागे भरतः - श्यामाभः, रक्ताम्बरधरः, उद्वद्धकुन्तलः, शरचापौ खड्गखेटकौ वा दधानः, द्विभुजः, त्रिभङ्गस्थः, चैत्रमासे स्वात्युद्भवः, अमलेशापतिः (printed अमलेशो), भ-आद्यक्षरबीजः (printed भगाद्यक्षरबीजो); श्रीवत्स-कौस्तुभौ विना अन्यत् सर्वं रामवत्। मन्त्रः - "भरतं कैकेयं रामानुजं धर्मचारिणम्" इति ॥
> ***bharatasya lakṣaṇam -***
> *dakṣiṇabhāge bharataḥ - śyāmābhaḥ, raktāmbaradharaḥ, udvaddhakuntalaḥ, śaracāpau khaḍgakheṭakau vā dadhānaḥ, dvibhujaḥ, tribhaṅgasthaḥ, caitramāse svātyudbhavaḥ, amaleśāpatiḥ, bha-ādyakṣarabījaḥ; śrīvatsa-kaustubhau vinā anyat sarvaṃ rāmavat. mantraḥ - "bharataṃ kaikeyaṃ rāmānujaṃ dharmacāriṇam" iti.*
> 
> **शत्रुघ्नस्य लक्षणम् -**
> वामे शत्रुघ्नः - हेमाभः, रक्ताम्बरधरः, उद्वद्धकुन्तलः, ज्येष्ठमासे धनिष्ठाजातः, श-आद्यक्षरबीजः (printed नामाद्यक्षरबीजो), निर्मलेशापतिः (printed निर्मलेशाः). अन्यत् सर्वं लक्ष्मणवत्। मन्त्रः - "रामप्रियं शत्रुघ्नं विजयं भरतानुजम्" इति ॥
> ***śatrughnasya lakṣaṇam -***
> *vāme śatrughnaḥ - hemābhaḥ, raktāmbaradharaḥ, udvaddhakuntalaḥ, jyeṣṭhamāse dhaniṣṭhājātaḥ, śa-ādyakṣarabījaḥ, nirmaleśāpatiḥ. anyat sarvaṃ lakṣmaṇavat. mantraḥ - "rāmapriyaṃ śatrughnaṃ vijayaṃ bharatānujam" iti.*
> 
> **हनुमतः लक्षणम् -**
> दक्षिणे पुरतः हनुमान् - अञ्जनाभः, श्वेतवस्त्रधरः, कपिरूपः, सर्वाभरणभूषितः, द्विभुजः, दक्षिणहस्तेन आस्यं विधाय (covering mouth in respect), वामहस्तेन वस्त्रं विधाय (holding garment), अवनतगात्रस्थितः, वार्ताविज्ञापनपरः (reporting news), मृगवाहनः, दण्डध्वजः, महानादरवः, श्रवणमासे श्रवणजातः, ह-आद्यक्षरबीजः (printed कल्पाद्यक्षरबीजो). मन्त्रः - "कपिराजं हनुमन्तं शब्दराशिं महामतिम्" इति ॥
> ***hanumataḥ lakṣaṇam -***
> *dakṣiṇe purataḥ hanumān - añjanābhaḥ, śvetavastradharaḥ, kapirūpaḥ, sarvābharaṇabhūṣitaḥ, dvibhujaḥ, dakṣiṇahastena āsyaṃ vidhāya, vāmahastena vastraṃ vidhāya, avanatagātrasthitaḥ, vārtāvijñāpanaparaḥ, mṛgavāhanaḥ, daṇḍadhvajaḥ, mahānādaravaḥ, śravaṇamāse śravaṇajātaḥ, ha-ādyakṣarabījaḥ. mantraḥ - "kapirājaṃ hanumantaṃ śabdarāśiṃ mahāmatim" iti.*

#### English Translation
*   **6. Paraśurāma (Rama with the Axe):**
    *   **Location:** Southeast (Agneya), facing West or North.
    *   **Iconography:** Red-complexioned, wearing white garments, holding an axe in his right hand, left hand pointing forward, wearing a matted-hair crown (*jaṭā-makuṭa*), adorned with the sacred thread (*upavīta*), consort Kamalā. All other details are like the primary deity. Mantra: *"rāmaṃ ṛṣisutaṃ viṣṇuṃ paraśupāṇim"*.
    *   **Consort (Kamalā):** Positioned on his right, sculpted in the same manner as Sītā.

*   **7. Rāghava (Śrī Rama / Ramachandra):**
    *   **Location:** Southwest (Nairritya), facing North or East.
    *   **Iconography:** Dark-complexioned (*śyāmābhaḥ*), wearing red garments, adorned with all ornaments including a crown, Shrivatsa mark on chest, two-armed, holding an arrow in his right hand, and leaning on his bow with his left hand, standing in a triple-bent (*tribhaṅga*) posture; born in Magha under Punarvasu, lord of Sita. Mantra: *"rāmaṃ dāśarathiṃ vīraṃ kākutstham"*.

*   **8. Sītā:**
    *   **Location:** On the right of Śrī Rama.
    *   **Iconography:** Golden-complexioned, wearing white garments, holding a lotus in her left hand, right hand extended downward, standing firmly on her right leg with her left leg slightly bent, hair bound upward, adorned with a karanda-style crown (*karaṇḍa-makuṭa*), seed-syllable **Sī**. Mantra: *"sītām ayonijāṃ lakṣmīṃ vaidehīm"*.

*   **9. Saumitri (Lakshmana):**
    *   **Location:** On the left of Śrī Rama.
    *   **Iconography:** Golden-complexioned, wearing dark garments, two-armed, standing in the *tribhaṅga* posture like Rama, holding a bow, hair bound upward, born in Vaishakha under Rohini, seed-syllable **La**. Mantra: *"rāmānujaṃ saumitriṃ lakṣmaṇaṃ lakṣmīvardhanam"*.

*   **10. Bharata:**
    *   **Location:** On the southern side.
    *   **Iconography:** Dark-complexioned, wearing red garments, hair bound upward, holding a bow and arrow (or sword and shield), two-armed, standing in the *tribhaṅga* posture, born in Chaitra under Svati, consort Amaleśā, seed-syllable **Bha**. Adorned like Rama but without the Shrivatsa and Kaustubha marks. Mantra: *"bharataṃ kaikeyaṃ rāmānujaṃ dharmacāriṇam"*.

*   **11. Śatrughna:**
    *   **Location:** On the left of Bharata (or Rama's retinue).
    *   **Iconography:** Golden-complexioned, wearing red garments, hair bound upward, born in Jyeshtha under Dhanishtha, consort Nirmaleśā, seed-syllable **Śa**. Adorned like Lakshmana. Mantra: *"rāmapriyaṃ śatrughnaṃ vijayaṃ bharatānujam"*.

*   **12. Hanumān:**
    *   **Location:** In front, on the right side.

#### English Translation
*   **22. Gopīnātha (Gopala):**
    *   **Location:** On the right of Kṛṣṇa (or as a separate shrine).
    *   **Iconography:** Red-complexioned, wearing white garments, holding a flute, born in Magha under Shatabhisha, horse vehicle, swan sound, seed-syllable **Go**. Mantra: *"gopālaṃ sarvanāthaṃ kṛṣṇaṃ sarvādhyakṣam"*.

*   **23. Śrīdāmā:**
    *   **Location:** On the left of Gopinatha.
    *   **Iconography:** Dark-complexioned, wearing white garments, two-armed, hands joined in *prāñjali*, born in Bhadrapada under Purvashadha, seed-syllable **Śrī**, horse vehicle, flute sound, friend of Gopinatha (*gopīnātha-sakhaḥ*). Mantra: *"śrīdāmānaṃ puṇyarūpaṃ puṇyātmakaṃ devasakham"*.

*   **24. Sundara (Garuḍa):**
    *   **Location:** In front.
    *   **Iconography:** Resplendent as a portion of Garuḍa, seed-syllable **Vi**, all other attributes like Garuḍa. Mantra: *"sundaraṃ vihaṅgaṃ pataṅgaṃ tārkṣyam"*.

*   **25. Kalkin:**
    *   **Location:** Northwest (West and Northern part).
    *   **Iconography:** Horse-faced (*hayānanaḥ*), all other attributes like the primary deity.

*   **26. Processional Images (*Kautuka-bera*):**
    *   One should place and worship a four-armed processional image in front of each of these manifestation shrines.

*   **27. Alternative Guidelines (*Anukalpaḥ*):**
    *   Alternatively, within the temple walls, in a spacious and beautiful location, one should construct a shrine (*vimāna*), hall (*maṇḍapa*), or pavilion (*kūṭa*), and consecrate a primary image (*dhruva-bera*) in full relief, half relief, or painting, with a processional image (or in its absence, a pedestal) placed in front.
    *   Alternatively, one may construct a veranda (*ālinda*) on the side of the main temple, and place the images facing outward, supported by the temple walls.
    *   If one is unable to do this, some authorities state that one should place and worship the processional images of Śrī Rama on the right (South) and Kṛṣṇa on the left (North) within the main hall (*mukha-maṇḍapa*) or intermediate space (*antarāla*) inside the temple walls.

*   **Colophon:**
    *   Thus ends the Twenty-First Chapter (*Ekaviṃśaḥ Paṭalaḥ*) named **"Rules for the Manifestations/Incarnations"** in the Great Scripture, the Vimanarchana Kalpa, proclaimed by Marici.
