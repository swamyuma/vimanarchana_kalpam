# Chapter 22: Measurements, Materials, and Casting of Images (Kautukādi-lakṣaṇam / Madhūcchiṣṭa-vidhiḥ)
### Section 1: Materials for the Processional Image & General Layout

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **अथ द्वाविंशः पटलः -**
> **कौतुकद्रव्याणि -**
> ***atha dvāviṃśaḥ paṭalaḥ -***
> ***kautukadravyāṇi -***
> 
> अथ कौतुकादीनां लक्षणं वक्ष्ये - दारु-शिला-ताम्र-रजत-हेम-रत्नानि कौतुकद्रव्याणि ॥
> *atha kautukādīnāṃ lakṣaṇaṃ vakṣye - dāru-śilā-tāmra-rajata-hema-ratnāni kautukadravyāṇi.*
> 
> **तेषु गुणाधिक्यम् -**
> दारवात् शतगुणं शैलं, शैलात् ताम्रं सहस्रगुणं, ताम्राद् रजतम् अयुतगुणं, रजताद् हेम अर्बुदगुणं, रुक्मजं (gold) सर्वसाधनं, रत्नजम् अनन्तफलं भवति ॥
> ***teṣu guṇādhikyam -***
> *dāravāt śataguṇaṃ śailaṃ, śailāt tāmraṃ sahasraguṇaṃ, tāmrād rajatam ayutaguṇaṃ, rajatād hema arbudaguṇaṃ, rukmajaṃ sarvasādhanaṃ, ratnajam anantaphalaṃ bhavati.*
> 
> **फलविशेषकामनया द्रव्यविशेषः -**
> पुत्रार्थी दारवं, आयुष्कामः शैलं, समृद्धिकामः ताम्रजं, श्रीकामो रजतं, ऐहिकामुष्मिककामः सौवर्णं, सद्यःसायुज्यकामो रत्नजं कारयेत् ॥
> ***phalaviśeṣakāmanayā dravyaviśeṣaḥ -***
> *putrārthī dāravaṃ, āyuṣkāmaḥ śailaṃ, samṛddhikāmaḥ tāmrajaṃ, śrīkāmo rajataṃ, aihikāmuṣmikakāmaḥ sauvarṇaṃ, sadyaḥsāyujyakāmo ratnajaṃ kārayet.*
> 
> **रत्नजस्य रूपमात्रत्वम् -**
> रत्नजं रूपमात्रमेव (without specific details of limbs, etc., or small in size) ॥
> ***ratnajasya rūpamātratvam -***
> *ratnajaṃ rūpamātrameva.*
> 
> **उत्सव-बलिबेरयोः द्रव्यनिषेधः -**
> शिलया दारुणा च उत्सवबेरं बलिबेरं च न कारयेत् ॥
> ***utsava-baliberayoḥ dravyaniṣedhaḥ -***
> *śilayā dāruṇā ca utsavaberaṃ baliberaṃ ca na kārayet.*

#### English Translation
*   **Materials for the Processional Image (*Kautukadravyāṇi*):**
    *   Now I shall explain the characteristics of the processional images (*kautukam*) and others. The materials suitable for making the processional image are wood (*dāru*), stone (*śilā*), copper (*tāmra*), silver (*rajata*), gold (*hema/suvarṇa*), and gems (*ratna*).

*   **Hierarchy of Merits of Materials:**
    *   Stone is one hundred times more meritorious than wood; copper is one thousand times more meritorious than stone; silver is ten thousand times more meritorious than copper; gold is one hundred million (*arbuda*) times more meritorious than silver. An image made of gold achieves all desires, and one made of gems yields infinite fruit.

*   **Choice of Material Based on Specific Desires:**
    *   One who desires sons should make it of wood; one who desires longevity should make it of stone; one who desires prosperity should make it of copper; one who desires wealth (*śrī*) should make it of silver; one who desires both earthly pleasure and heavenly liberation should make it of gold; and one who desires immediate union with the Divine (*sāyujyam*) should make it of gems.

*   **Limitation on Gem Icons:**
    *   An icon made of gems is created merely in form (*rūpa-mātram*, without minor decorative divisions).

*   **Forbidden Materials for Festive and Offering Images:**
    *   One must never make the festive image (*utsava-bera*) or the offering image (*bali-bera*) of stone or wood.

#### English Translation
*   **Postures of Processional and Festive Images (*Sthiti-viśeṣaḥ*):**
    *   If the main image (*dhruva-bera*) is standing, the processional image (*kautuka*) must be standing; if the main is seated, the processional must be seated or standing; if the main is reclining (*śayana*), the processional should be seated or standing. Some authorities say that even if the main image is standing, the processional and festive (*utsava*) images may be seated.
    *   Images kept on vehicles (*yānaka*) and those used for bathing (*snapana*) must always be made in the standing posture.

*   **Iconographic Variations of Manifestations:**
    *   For the incarnations, the processional image can match their specific form, or it can be a standard four-armed Viṣṇu. However, for Matsya, Kurma, Varaha, and Vamana, the processional image must *never* be made in their zoomorphic/dwarf shapes; it must always be a standard four-armed Viṣṇu.

*   **Uniformity of Material for Retinue and Accessories:**
    *   One must make the consorts (*devyau*), the halo (*prabhā*), and the pedestal (*pīṭha*) of the exact same material of which the primary deity is made. Fails in this yield grave defects.

*   **Presence of Consorts and Fruits:**
    *   Even if the main image is without consorts, the processional image should be made with consorts to bestow all forms of prosperity. Others say that if the processional image is without consorts, the festive image must be accompanied by them, which is excellent.

*   **Proportions of Height (*Mānāni*):**
    *   Dividing the height of the main image into 3 parts, 1 part gives the *Uttama* (highest) height of the processional image. Dividing 2 parts of that height into 3, 1 part yields the *Madhyama* (middle) height. Dividing 1 part of that height into 3, 1 part yields the *Adhama* (lowest) height.
    *   Alternatively, dividing the main image height into 10 parts, 1 part is a *Tāla*. Dividing that *Tāla* into 12 parts gives an *Aṅgula*. Starting from 11 *aṅgulas* up to 63 *aṅgulas*, increasing by 2 *aṅgulas*, there are 27 possible heights for the processional image.
    *   Alternatively, using the *hasta-aṅgula* defined by the shrine measurements, starting from 7 *aṅgulas* up to 23 *aṅgulas*, increasing by 2 *aṅgulas*, there are 9 heights. Having selected the desired measurement from these, one should distribute a minor variation of 1, 2, 3, or 4 *yavas* (grain-widths) equally; this does not cause any defect. Therefore, one should make the processional, bathing, festive, and offering images of the appropriate size.

*   **Astrological Suitability Calculations (*Āyādi-ṣaḍ-vargaḥ*):**
    *   The suitability of the image's height is verified using the six astrological tests:
        1.  **Income (Āya):** Height multiplied by 3, divided by 8; the remainder represents income.
        2.  **Expenditure (Vyaya):** Height multiplied by 4, divided by 5; the remainder is expenditure.
        3.  **Asterism (Nakṣatra):** Height multiplied by 8, divided by 27; the remainder yields the nakshatra (starting from Ashvini). It should be compatible with the patron's birth star.
        4.  **Yoni:** Height multiplied by 3, divided by 8; the remainder represents the Yoni. The 8 Yonis are: 1. Dhvaja (East), 2. Dhuma (Southeast), 3. Simha (South), 4. Shvana (Southwest), 5. Vrisha (West), 6. Gardabha (Northwest), 7. Gaja (North), 8. Vayasa (Northeast). Odd yonis are auspicious, while even ones are inauspicious.
        5.  **Day of the Week (Vāra):** Height multiplied by 9, divided by 7; the remainder represents the day (Sunday, etc.).
        6.  **Portion (Aṃśaka):** Height multiplied by 4, divided by 9; the remainder represents the portion. The 9 portions are: 1. Thief (*taskara*), 2. Enjoyment (*bhukti*), 3. Power (*śakti*), 4. Wealth (*vitta*), 5. King (*avanīpāla*), 6. Eunuch (*klība*), 7. Fearlessness (*nirbhīti*), 8. Poor (*daridra*), 9. Servant (*preṣya*).
    *   The height is auspicious if it results in a favorable portion. If all six results are favorable, the image is highly auspicious. Therefore, one should select a height that maximizes positive attributes and minimizes negative ones.

### Section 3: Results of Measurements and Defect Remediation

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **आयादीनां फलम् -**
> बेरस्य उद्देशमानेन हीने तद्ग्राम-यजमानयोः आशु विनाशाय भवति; निर्देशमाने विहीने राज-राष्ट्र-विनाशाय भवति; बेरम् आयविहीनं व्याधिकं यजमानस्य अर्धहानिं, राज्ञः शत्रुवृद्धिं च कुर्यात्। आयाधिकं व्ययक्षीणं सर्वसम्पत्करं भवति।

> *berasya uddeśamānena hīne tadgrāma-yajamānayoḥ āśu vināśāya bhavati; nirdeśamāne vihīne rāja-rāṣṭra-vināśāya bhavati; beram āyavihīnaṃ vyādhikaṃ yajamānasya ardhahāniṃ, rājñaḥ śatruvṛddhiṃ ca kuryāt. āyādhikaṃ vyayakṣīṇaṃ sarvasampatkaraṃ bhavati.*

> 
> वारेषु - आदित्याङ्गारक-शनैश्चरान् (Sunday, Tuesday, Saturday) हित्वा अन्ये शुभा भवन्ति; अशुभाश्च योगबलयुक्ताश्चेन् मुख्या भवन्ति।
> *vāreṣu - ādityāṅgāraka-śanaiścarān hitvā anye śubhā bhavanti; aśubhāśca yogabalayuktāścen mukhyā bhavanti.*
> 
> अंशकेषु - तस्करं, षण्डं, दरिद्रं, प्रेष्यं च त्यक्त्वा अन्ये शुभावहा भवन्ति ॥
> *aṃśakeṣu - taskaraṃ, ṣaṇḍaṃ, daridraṃ, preṣyaṃ ca tyaktvā anye śubhāvahā bhavanti.*

> **मानाधिक-न्यूनयोः फलम् -**
> षण्मानसहितं मुख्यं (perfect with six measurements);
> *   **मानहीनं** धनधान्यनाशनं, **मानाधिकं** रोगवर्धनम्।

    *   Now I shall explain the lost-wax casting process for the metal image.
    *   This should begin in the bright fortnight under a favorable nakshatra of the patron. Sowing the sprouts (*aṅkurārpaṇam*) on the previous day, one should set up a decorated shed (*prapā*) facing the temple or at the patron's house. Smearing the center with cow-dung over a *gocarma* area (approx. 10x10 cubits) and decorating it with five-colored powders, one should set up the *aupāsana* fire-altar.
    *   Perform homa offerings with Vaishnava, Viṣṇu-sūkta, Puruṣa-sūkta, Śrī-Bhumi-sūkta, Pañcabhautika, and Vighna-svishtakrit mantras. Then, summon the sculptor. Smearing the workspace courtyard with cow-dung, set up a tripod (*tripadikā*) or stove (*cullī*), light the fire, place the wax (*madhūcchiṣṭam*) in a clean vessel, and melt it over the stove. The sculptor, facing East, should melt it over a steady fire, filter it through a new cloth, and collect the purified wax. The priest, facing East, should sprinkle the wax, touch it while chanting *"idaṃ viṣṇuḥ"*, shape the head of the deity first, followed by the consorts and others while chanting their respective mantras.
    *   Chant the *puṇyāham* (purificatory mantras), and the patron should distribute donations (*dakṣiṇā*) to the priest and others.
    *   Then, worship the sculptor. Using the purified wax, the sculptor makes a perfect wax model of the deity with all iconographic features (*salakṣaṇam*). Gradually apply layers of clay over it, let it dry, and bind the clay mold with iron bands. On another auspicious day, perform homas as before, heat the clay mold to melt and drain out the wax (*saṃsrāvya*). Heat the hollow mold again, and the sculptors should smoothly pour the molten metal (*santaptena lohena*) into the mold.
    *   In the morning, after bathing, the priest and the patron should ask the sculptors to gently break the clay mold (*mṛd-bhedanam*) and clean the metal image (*bimba-śuddhi*). If the cast image is incomplete, broken, or cracked, it should be treated as raw metal; perform homas again, melt it down, and recast the image.

> **प्रलम्बफलका-लक्षणम् -**
> *   अथ प्रलम्बफलकां वक्ष्ये - बेरस्य लम्बमानं पूर्वमेव परीक्षयेत्। अष्टषष्ट्यङ्गुलायतां (68 angulas long), चतुर्विंशत्यङ्गुलविस्तारां (24 angulas wide), द्व्यङ्गुलघनां (2 angulas thick) लघ्वीं श्लक्ष्णां फलकां गृहीत्वा, परितः त्र्यङ्गुलं नीत्वा मानसूत्रं विन्यस्य, प्राक्-पश्चिमभागस्थानि सूत्राणि प्रसारयेत्।
> *   मध्ये सूत्रं विन्यस्य, मध्यसूत्राद् एकाङ्गुलं पुटपर्यन्तसूत्रं, तद् एव दृगन्तरं स्यात्। तस्मात् त्र्यङ्गुलं नेत्रपर्यन्तं, तस्माद् द्व्यर्धाङ्गुलं मुखपर्यन्तं, तस्माद् द्व्यङ्गुलं कर्णपर्यन्तं, तस्मात् त्र्यङ्गुलं कक्षपर्यन्तं, तस्माद् दशाङ्गुलं बाहुपर्यन्तम्; एवं त्रयोदश सूत्राणि विन्यस्य परभुजस्य मणिबन्धावसानं मानसूत्रद्वयमेव, तदन्तरं नीत्वा अभ्यन्तरं भवेत्।
> *   पुरतः मध्यसूत्रयोगं (मध्यसूत्रं), तस्माद् द्व्यङ्गुलं पुटसूत्रं, तस्माद् यवाधिकं द्व्यङ्गुलं नेत्रसूत्रं, तद् एव वक्त्रबाह्यं, तस्मात् त्र्यङ्गुलम् अन्तर्भुजावधि, तद् एव कक्षपर्यन्तं, तदेव वा चतुरङ्गुलं बाहुपर्यन्तं, तद् एव पार्श्वयोः मध्ये पार्श्वमध्यसूत्रं स्यात्। तस्मात् द्व्यङ्गुलं कर्णावर्तं, तस्मात् त्र्यङ्गुलं शिरःपृष्ठावसानं, तद् एव परभुजावधि भवति।
> *   इत्येवं दक्षिणोत्तरगतानि सूत्राणि षट् च विन्यस्य, तत्तत्सूत्रसङ्गे सुषिरं कृत्वा, तन्तुना यवमात्राणि सूत्राणि सुषिरे संयोज्य, अग्रे प्रोतकुण्डलं लोहमयं मृण्मयं वा सम्बध्य सूत्राणि लम्बयेत्।
> *   मध्यसूत्रं शिखामणेरूर्ध्वं संस्पर्शमेव कृत्वा लम्बयेत्। स्थानकस्य मध्यसूत्रं मौलिशिखामणेः मध्ये, भ्रूयुगस्य मध्ये (printed मूर्ध्वोभ्रूसंगस्य), नासाग्रस्य च मध्ये, कण्ठस्य, हृदयस्य, कुक्षेः (printed कुशे), नाभेः, योनेः, पादयोश्च मध्ये प्रलम्बम्; तत्तत्सूत्रसंस्पर्शं नासाग्रं कुक्षिमध्यं च।
> *   सूत्राद् मौल्यग्रपर्यन्तं ६ अङ्गुलं, हनुमध्यं यवाधिक-४ अङ्गुलं, हिक्कान्तं ४ अङ्गुलं, हन्मध्यं २.५ अङ्गुलं, नाभिः ६ यवाः, योनिमूलम् १ अङ्गुलं, ऊरुमध्यं २ अङ्गुलं, जान्वन्तरं ३ अङ्गुलं, जङ्घान्तरं ६ अङ्गुलं, नलिकान्तरम् ८ अङ्गुलं, सूत्रात् पश्चार्धं ८.५ अङ्गुलं, अङ्गुष्ठाग्रं सूत्रात् परतो २.५ अङ्गुलम् - एवं मध्यसूत्रवशात् मानयेत्।
> *   बाहुपर्यन्तम् एव पार्श्वसूत्रं स्यात् - चूडामणि-मूर्ध-कर्णपाल्यन्तरे, कूर्परयोः पार्श्वे, जानु-जङ्घा-गुल्फानां मध्ये प्रलम्बयेत्।
> *   मौल्य-मूर्ध-कृकाटिका-ककुद्-अंस-स्फिक्-पार्ष्णीनां मध्ये पृष्ठमध्यसूत्रं प्रलम्बयेत्।
> *   गण्डपार्श्वे, वक्त्रबाह्ये, कर्णे, चूचुकाग्रमध्ये, श्रोणिपार्श्वे, ऊरु-जङ्घयोः मध्ये वक्त्रबाह्यसूत्रं प्रलम्बयेत्।
> *   एतानि षट् च पीठावलम्बानि, अन्यानि तत्तदङ्गसंस्पर्शानेव अवलम्बयेत्।
> *   आसनस्य (seated image) ऊर्ध्वकायस्योक्तं पूर्ववन् मध्यसूत्रादीनि षट्सूत्राणि आसनोपरि लम्बयेत्। मध्यसूत्रं जानोरुपरिष्टात् केशान्तं, तन्मध्यगं जानोः पार्श्वभ्यां बाहुभ्यां च मध्यगं सूत्रं प्रलम्बयेत्। सूत्राद् आमजान्वन्तरम् ऊर्ध्वकायार्धसमं दक्षिणजान्वन्तरं, तदेव चतुरङ्गुलं त्र्यङ्गुलं यवोनं वा अन्यत् सर्वं पूर्ववत् कारयेत् ॥

> **उपमानम् -**
> अथ उपमानं वक्ष्ये -
> *   परभुजस्य मणिबन्धान्तरम् ८ अङ्गुलम्।
> *   उष्णीष-शङ्खचक्रान्तरमुखं मध्योदरात् कूर्परान्तं ६ अङ्गुलम्।
> *   अभयहस्तस्य मध्यमाङ्गुलात् चूचुकान्तं १३ अङ्गुलम्।
> *   स्वागतहस्तस्य मध्यात् नाभ्यन्तरं १० अङ्गुलम्।
> *   उदरमध्यात् श्रोण्यन्तरं ९ अङ्गुलम्।
> *   वामहस्तस्य मध्यमाङ्गुलाग्राद् मेढ्रान्तरं ६ अङ्गुलम्।
> *   ऊरुमूलान्तरम् १ अङ्गुलम्, ऊरुमध्यन्तरं २ अङ्गुलम्।
> *   जान्वन्तरं ४ अङ्गुलम्, जङ्घयोर्मध्यं ५ अङ्गुलम्, नलिकान्तरं ६ अङ्गुलम्।
> *   कक्षयोर्अन्तरं ५ अङ्गुलम्, पार्ष्ण्यन्तरं (heels) ४ अङ्गुलम्।
> *   तलमध्यान्तरं ६ अङ्गुलम्, अङ्गुष्ठान्तरम् ८ अङ्गुलम्।
> *   शयनस्य (reclining image) - तिर्यग्गत-ब्रह्मसूत्रस्य दक्षिणतो मुखं ३ अङ्गुलम्, पादाङ्गुष्ठयोर्मध्यं २० अङ्गुलम्, पार्ष्ण्यन्तरं जान्वन्तरं च १४ अङ्गुलं स्यात् - एवम् उपमानं लक्षयेत्।
> *   अत्र अनुक्तं सर्वं शिल्पशास्त्रोक्तमार्गेणैव कारयेत् ॥

#### English Translation
*   **Measurement Units and Proportions (*Aṅgula-saṃjñā*):**
    *   I shall now explain the classification of measurements (*aṅgulas*) for shrines and icons. There are three types of *aṅgulas*: *Māna-aṅgula* (absolute measurement), *Mātrā-aṅgula* (proportional finger-breadth of the patron), and *Dehalabdha-aṅgula* (proportional unit derived from the icon's height).
    *   Definition of atoms and particles: When a ray of sunlight passes through a lattice window (*jāla-randhra*), the extremely small floating dust particles visible in it are called *aṇus* or *paramāṇus* (atoms).
    *   8 *paramāṇus* = 1 *rathareņu* (chariot-dust particle).
    *   8 *rathareṇus* = 1 *romāgra* (tip of a hair).
    *   8 *romāgras* = 1 *likṣā* (nit).
    *   8 *likṣās* = 1 *yūka* (louse).
    *   8 *yūkas* = 1 *yava* (barley-corn width).
    *   8 *yavas* = 1 *mātrā-aṅgula*.
    *   The standard *mātrā-aṅgula* is of three classes: *Uttama* (highest), *Madhyama* (middle, which is 1/8 less), and *Adhama* (lowest, which is 1/4 less).
    *   Alternatively, the width or length of the middle phalange of the middle finger of the right hand of a man of average build is defined as a *mātrā-aṅgula*.
    *   Dividing the total height of the icon into parts based on its *Tāla* proportion: 1 part is a *dehalabdha-aṅgula*, and 1/8 of that is a *yava*.

*   **Symbolic Names for Measurements from 1 to 27 Aṅgulas (*Eka-aṅgulādīnāṃ saṃjñāḥ*):**
    *   Each number of *aṅgulas* has technical synonyms based on cosmological or mythological concepts (e.g., 1 = Mātrā, 2 = Aśvinī/eyes, 3 = Agni/trident, 4 = Veda/Yuga, 5 = Indriya/elements, 8 = Vasus, 12 = Vitasti/Zodiac signs, 27 = Nakṣatra).

*   **The Six Kinds of Measurements (*Ṣaṇmānāni*):**
    *   All icons must be made using the six aspects of measurement:
        1.  **Māna (Height/Length):** Synonyms are *āyāma*, *āyata*, *dīrgha*, *māna*.
        2.  **Pramāṇa (Width):** Synonyms are *vistara*, *vistāra*, *tāra*, *vistṛti*, *visṛti*, *vyāsa*, *visāra*, *vipula*, *viṣkambha*, *viśāla*.
        3.  **Unmāna (Thickness/Depth):** Synonyms are *bahula*, *nīvra*, *ghana*, *ucchrāya*, *tuṅga*, *udaya*, *utsedha*, *ūrdhva*.
        4.  **Parimāṇa (Girth/Circumference):** Synonyms are *niṣkrama*, *niṣkṛti*, *nirgama*, *nahtam*, *pariṇāha*, *nāha*, *vṛtti*, *āvṛta*.
        5.  **Upamāna (Inter-limb distance):** Synonyms are *nīvra*, *vivara*, *antara*.
        6.  **Lambamāna (Plumb-line):** That which is measured by suspending plumb-lines.

*   **Tāla Proportions for Different Deities (*Tāla-vibhāgaḥ*):**
    *   **Uttama-daśa-tāla (124 units):** Used for the Supreme Lord (Viṣṇu).
    *   **Madhyama-daśa-tāla (120 units):** Used for Brahmā, Śiva, Śrī, Bhumi, Uma, and Sarasvati.
    *   **Adhama-daśa-tāla (116 units):** Used for Indra and other directional guardians, Sun, Moon, 12 Adityas, 11 Rudras, 8 Vasus, Ashvins, Bhrigu, Markandeya, Vishvaksena, Saisika, Durgā, Guha, and the Seven Sages.
    *   **Navārdha-tāla (114 units):** Used for Kubera, the Nine Planets, and other secondary deities.
    *   **Uttama-nava-tāla (108 units):** Used for Daityas, Rakshasas, snake-kings (*urageśas*), Siddhas, Gandharvas, and Charanas.
    *   **Nava-tāla (96 units):** Used for average humans, Nishacharas, and Asuras.
    *   **Aṣṭa-tāla (84 units):** Used for ordinary mortals.
    *   **Saptatala (72 units):** Used for Vetālas (vampires).
    *   **Shadatala (64 units):** Used for Pretas (ghosts).
    *   **Panchatala (54 units):** Used for hunchbacks (*kubjas*).
    *   **Caturtala (48 units):** Used for dwarfs (*vāmanas*).
    *   **Tritala (36 units):** Used for Bhūtas and Kinnaras.
    *   **Dvitala (24 units):** Used for Kushmandas.
    *   **Ekatala (12 units):** Used for Kabandhas (headless torsos).
    *   *(Note: Each of these categories is further divided into Uttama, Madhyama, and Adhama classes).*

*   **Linear Measurements (*Prādeśa-vibhāgaḥ*):**
    *   **Prādeśa:** The span between the tips of the thumb and index finger.
    *   **Tāla:** The span between the tips of the thumb and middle finger.
    *   **Vitasti:** The span between the tips of the thumb and ring finger.
    *   **Gokarṇa:** The span between the tips of the thumb and little finger.
    *   **Kiṣku (Cubit):** 24 *aṅgulas*.
    *   **Prājāpatya:** 25 *aṅgulas*.
    *   **Dhanurgraha:** 26 *aṅgulas*.
    *   **Dhanurmuṣṭi:** 27 *aṅgulas*.
    *   **Danda (Staff):** Four times the *dhanurmuṣṭi* (108 *aṅgulas*). The layout of villages and towns must be planned using this *daṇḍa*.

*   **Plumb-Line Board Guidelines (*Pralamba-phalakā-lakṣaṇam*):**
    *   To verify the vertical alignment of the icon, one should construct a plumb-board (*pralamba-phalakā*). It should be 68 *aṅgulas* long, 24 *aṅgulas* wide, and 2 *aṅgulas* thick, light and smooth.
    *   Draw a margin of 3 *aṅgulas* all around, mark the alignment grid, and suspend plumb-lines for the front and back.
    *   The positions of the 13 vertical reference lines (*sūtras*) from the central line (*madhya-sūtra*) are detailed: including the line for the bridge of the nose (*puṭa-sūtra*), eyes (*netra-sūtra*), mouth, ears, armpits, and arms.
    *   The six primary plumb-lines (*ṣaṭ-sūtrāṇi*) are: 1. Central line (*madhya-sūtra*), 2. Side lines (*pārśva-sūtra*), 3. Rear-center line (*pṛṣṭha-madhya-sūtra*), and 4. Face-outer lines (*vaktra-bāhya-sūtras*).
    *   These lines should be suspended with iron or clay weights (*protakuṇḍala*) attached to their ends to keep them taut. The vertical distances from the reference lines to the respective limbs (nose, chin, chest, navel, thighs, knees, ankles) are precisely measured. Similar rules apply to seated (*āsana*) and reclining (*śayana*) images.

*   **Secondary Measurements (*Upamānam*):**
    *   The horizontal or diagonal distances between specific limbs are detailed: including the distance between the two wrists (8 *aṅgulas*), between the center of the abdomen and the elbow (6 *aṅgulas*), between the middle finger of the *abhaya-mudra* hand and the nipple (13 *aṅgulas*), between the middle finger of the resting hand and the navel (10 *aṅgulas*), between the heels (4 *aṅgulas*), etc.
    *   For reclining images: the distance from the central axis to the face is 3 *aṅgulas*, the distance between the big toes is 20 *aṅgulas*, and the distance between the heels and knees is 14 *aṅgulas*.
    *   Any other measurements not detailed here should be executed according to the authoritative manuals of architecture (*śilpa-śāstra*).
