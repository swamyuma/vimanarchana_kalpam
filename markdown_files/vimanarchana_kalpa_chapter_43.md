#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **च वर्जयेत्।**
> **तण्डुल-संस्कारः -**
> ***...ca varjayet.***
> ***taṇḍula-saṃskāraḥ -***
> 
> शुद्धे देशे कुट्टितेषु (मुद्रितपाठे देशोकटेपु) वस्त्रेषु वा धान्यानि आस्तीर्य शोषयेत्। उलूखलमुसले (मुद्रितपाठे उलूखलमु-सली) प्रक्षाल्य, उलूखले हरिणीं (वा हरिं), मुसले विष्णुं च अभ्यर्च्य, उलूखले धान्यानि प्रप्रिक्षिप्य, धान्येषु सोमम् अभ्यर्च्य;
> *śuddhe deśe kuṭṭiteṣu vastreṣu vā dhānyāni āstīrya śoṣayet. ulūkhalamusale prakṣālya, ulūkhale hariṇīṃ, musale viṣṇuṃ ca abhyarcya, ulūkhale dhānyāni praxipya, dhānyeṣu somam abhyarcya;*
> 
> आचार्यः 'अतो देवाः' इत्यादिना किञ्चिद् अवघातं कृत्वा; ततः शुद्धाभिः स्त्रीभिः पुरुषैर्वा स्वेदरोमादीन् (मुद्रितपाठे स्वेेद-) परिहृत्य अवघातं कारयेत्;
> *ācāryaḥ 'ato devāḥ' ityādinā kiñcid avaghātaṃ kṛtvā; tataḥ śuddhābhiḥ strībhiḥ puruṣairvā svedaromādīn parihṛtya avaghātaṃ kārayet;*
> 
> ‘निरस्तम्’ इति शूर्पे प्रक्षिप्य, तुष-अश्म-कणादीन् संशोध्य, तण्डुलान् शुद्धान् 'आपो हि ष्ठा' इति प्रोक्ष्य, शुद्धे पात्रे 'देवस्य त्वा' इति गृह्णाति।
> *'nirastam' iti śūrpe praxipya, tuṣa-aśma-kaṇādīn saṃśodhya, taṇḍulān śuddhān 'āpo hi ṣṭhā' iti prokṣya, śuddhe pātre 'devasya tvā' iti gṛhṇāti.*
> 
> **हविः-परिमाणम् -**
> विष्णोः द्रोणं हविः उत्तमं, द्रोणार्धं मध्यमं, आढकम् अधमम्; देव्योः आढकं तदर्धं वा; परिवाराणां द्विप्रस्थैः प्रस्थाधिकैर्वा तण्डुलैः प्रोक्तम् ॥
> ***havih-parimāṇam -***
> *viṣṇoḥ droṇaṃ haviruttamaṃ, droṇārdhaṃ madhyamaṃ, āḍhakamadhamam; devyorāḍhakaṃ tadardhaṃ vā; parivārāṇāṃ dviprasthaiḥ prasthādhikairvā taṇḍulaiḥ proktam.*
> 
> **माषादि-लक्षणम् -**
> अष्टतिलं माषं, अष्टमाषं शाणं, विंशतिशाणं निष्कं; तिलाष्टकं शाणं, तदष्टकं माषम् इति भृगुः। दशनिष्कं पलं, अष्टनिष्कम् इति केचित्। चतुष्पलं कुडुबं, चतुष्कुडुबं प्रस्थं, चतुष्प्रस्थम् आढकं, चतुराढकं द्रोणं भवति।
> ***māṣādilakṣaṇam -***
> *aṣṭatilaṃ māṣaṃ, aṣṭamāṣaṃ śāṇaṃ, viṃśatiśāṇaṃ niṣkaṃ; tilāṣṭakaṃ śāṇaṃ, tadaṣṭakaṃ māṣam iti bhṛguḥ. daśaniṣkaṃ palaṃ, aṣṭniṣkam iti kecit. catuṣpalaṃ kuḍubaṃ, catuṣkuḍubaṃ prasthaṃ, catuṣprastham āḍhakaṃ, caturāḍhakaṃ droṇaṃ bhavati.*
> 
> **हविर्निर्वाप-विधिः -**
> लोहमयीं मृण्मयीं वा स्थालीं गृह्णीयात्। मृण्मयीपक्षे अतीते (एकवारप्रयोगानन्तरं) विसर्जयेत्; मासेऽतीते इति केचित्।
> ***havirnirvāpa-vidhiḥ -***
> *lohamayīṃ mṛṇmayīṃ vā sthālīṃ gṛhṇīyāt. mṛṇmayīpakṣe atīte visarjayet; māse'tīte iti kecit. 'prajāsthālīm' (vai.ma. 3) iti sthālīṃ prakṣālya, 'devasya tvā' iti taṇḍulān praxipya, śuddhābhiradbhiḥ 'āpa undantu' iti catuḥ prakṣālya; 'viṣṇave juṣṭam' iti cullyāṃ sthālīm āropya, indhanair nirvidhūmaiḥ samena agninā 'vācaspataye pavasva' (vai.ma.pra. 3) iti pacet.*
> 
> ‘प्रजास्थालीम्’ (वै०म० ३) इति स्थालीं प्रक्षाल्य, ‘देवस्य त्वा’ इति तण्डुलान् प्रक्षिप्य, शुद्धाभिः अद्भिः ‘आप उन्दन्तु’ (मुद्रितपाठे आप उन्दं त्विति) इति चतुः प्रक्षाल्य;
> ***upadaṃśāḥ -***
> *upadaṃśān — kadalī-cūta-panasa-nālikera-tintriṇī-kāravallītraya-vyāghra-śimbībheda-bṛhatībheda-siṃhī-vyāghranakhī-karkandhū-urvāruka-kumbhāṇḍa-kūṣmāṇḍa-kṛtabandhanā-alarka-kārkoṭakāni, sūraṇakanda-kṣudrakanda-mahākanda-vallīkanda-śṛṅgibera-utpalakaṃ, darpiṇḍa-śakuṭakandādīni ca; marīci-jīraka-sarṣapāṇi, mudgāḍhaka-kulattha-tilva-māṣa-masūrakādīni; śākeṣu jīvantī-ratharasī-raktabāṣpāṇi ca anyāni haviṣyāṇīti manvādyaiḥ uktāni yathālābham āharet.*
> 
> ‘विष्णवे जुष्टम्’ इति चुल्ल्यां (मुद्रितपाठे चुल्यां) स्थालीम् आरोप्य, इन्धनैः निर्विधूमैः (मुद्रितपाठे निर्विंधूमैः) समेनाग्निना ‘वाचस्पतये पवस्व’ (वै०म०प्र० ३) इति पचेत्।
> *kośātakīṃ paṭolīṃ alābuṃ ca viśeṣeṇa varjayet. kadalyādiṣu ekaṃ dvou trīn caturo'dhikān vā doṣavihīnān prakṣālya, yathāyogaṃ sugandharasasaṃyuktaṃ supacet.*
> 
> **उपदंशाः (Side Dishes & Condiments) -**
> उपदंशान् — कदली-चूत-पनस-नालिकेर-तिन्त्रिणी-कारवल्लीत्रय-व्याघ्र-शिम्बीभेद-बृहतीभेद-सिंही-व्याघ्रनखी-कर्कन्धू-उर्वारुक-कुम्भाण्ड-कूष्माण्ड-कृतबन्धना-अलर्क-कारकोटकानि, सूरणकन्द-क्षुद्रकन्द-महाकन्द-वल्लीकन्द-शृङ्गिबेर-उत्पलकं, दर्पिण्ड-शकुटकन्दादीनि च; मरीचि-जीरक-सर्षपाणि, मुद्ग-आढक-कुलत्थ-तिल्व-माष-मसूरकादीनि; शाकेषु जीवन्ती-रथरसी-रक्तबाष्पाणि च अन्यानि हविष्याणीति मन्वाद्यैः उक्तानि यथालाभम् आहरेत्।
> *āḍhakataṇḍulasya upadaṃśaṃ pratyekaṃ ṣaṭpalaṃ, pañcapalaṃ, catuṣpalaṃ vā; mudgasāraṃ prasthaṃ prasthārdhaṃ kuḍubaṃ vā, ghṛtaṃ kuḍubaṃ tadardhaṃ, gudaṃ ekapalaṃ, dadhiprasthaṃ pādahīnaṃ tadardhaṃ vā; kramukaphalāni ṣoḍaśa, dvādaśa, aṣṭa, ṣaṭ, catvāri, dve vā; tatriguṇāni dviguṇāni vā tāmbūlapatrāṇi; mātuliṅga-elā-lavaṅga-karpūrādi yathālābhaṃ bhavet; āḍhakataṇḍulād adhikeṣu evameva vardhayet.*
> 
> कोशातकीं पटोलीं अलाबुं (मुद्रितपाठे अलाबूं) च विशेषेण वर्जयेत्। कदल्यादिषु एकं द्वौ त्रीन् चतुरोऽधिकान् वा दोषविहीनान् प्रक्षाल्य, यथायोगं सुगन्धरससंयुक्तं सुपचेत्।
> ***prabhūta-mahāhaviṣo lakṣaṇam -***
> *dvidroṇādi ṣaddroṇāntaṃ prabhūtaṃ, śataprasthādi sahasraprasthāntaṃ mahāhavir ityācakṣate.*
> 
> आढकतण्डुलस्य उपदंशं प्रत्येकं षट्पलं, पञ्चपलं, चतुष्पलं वा; मुद्गसारं प्रस्थं प्रस्थार्धं कुडुबं वा, घृतं कुडुबं तदर्धं, गुडं (मुद्रितपाठे गुलम्) एकपलं, दधिप्रस्थं पादहीनं तदर्धं वा; क्रमुकफलानि षोडश, द्वादश, अष्ट, षट्, चत्वारि, द्वे वा; तत्रिगुणानि द्विगुणानि वा ताम्बूलपत्राणि; मातुलिङ्ग-एला-लवङ्ग-कर्पूरादि यथालाभं भवेत्; आढकतण्डुलाद् अधिकेषु एवमेव वर्धयेत्।
> ***ṣadvidha-havirlakṣaṇam -***
> *1. taṇḍulaiḥ kevalaiḥ pakvaṃ śuddhānnam;*
> *2. taṇḍulatulyaiḥ ardhairvā mudgasāraiḥ yuktaṃ maudgikam;*
> *3. taṇḍulatriguṇena payasā tadardhajalena taṇḍulārdhamudgasāraiḥ nālikera-phalasāraiśca pakvaṃ pāyasam;*
> *4. taṇḍulārdhatilacūrṇaiḥ yuktaṃ kṛsaram;*
> *5. taṇḍulādhikatoyena triguṇena payasā aṣṭabhāgaiḥ pañcabhāgairvā mudgasāraiḥ taṇḍulārdhena ghṛtena guḍena ca yuktaṃ gaulyam;*
> *6. taṇḍulārdhayavasāraiḥ pakvaṃ yāvakam — iti ṣadvidhaṃ bhavati.*
> 
> **प्रभूत-महाहविषो लक्षणम् -**
> द्विद्रोणादि षड्द्रोणान्तं प्रभूतं, शतप्रस्थादि सहस्रप्रस्थान्तं (मुद्रितपाठे सहस्त्र-) महाहविः इति आचक्षते।
> ***varjya-havis -***
> *droṇādhikem ekapātreṇa na pacet; māsātīte mṛdbhāṇḍe pacanaṃ na kuryāt. apakvaṃ, srāvitaṃ, gandhaduṣṭaṃ, nāsikāsyavāyūpahataṃ, āghrātaṃ, tuṣakeśādisaṃyuktaṃ, vivṛtaṃ, dvijātibhyo'nyajātidṛṣṭasvedaspṛṣṭaṃ, laṅghitaṃ, yāmātītaṃ, pātrāntargataṃ, śītaṃ, haviḥ upadaṃśaṃ ca varjanīyam. anyapātragataṃ punaḥ pākārhaṃ upadaṃśādi na varjanīyam.*
> 
> **षड्विध-हविर्लक्षणम् -**
> १. तण्डुलैः केवलैः पक्वं शुद्धान्नम्;
> २. तण्डुलतुल्यैः अर्धैर्वा मुद्गसारैः युक्तं मौद्गिकम्;
> ३. तण्डुलत्रिगुणेन पयसा तदर्धजलेन तण्डुलार्धमुद्गसारैः नालिकेरफलसारैश्च पक्वं पायसम् (मुद्रितपाठे मायसं);
> ४. तण्डुलार्धतिलचूर्णैः युक्तं कृसरम्;
> ५. तण्डुलाधिकतोयेन त्रिगुणेन पयसा अष्टभागैः पञ्चभागैः वा मुद्गसारैः तण्डुलार्धेन घृतेन गुडेन च युक्तं गौल्यम्;
> ६. तण्डुलार्धयवसारैः पक्वं यावकम् (मुद्रितपाठे पक्यवाकविति -> पक्वं यावकं) — इति षड्विधं भवति।
> ***havirānayana-vidhiḥ -***
> *sthālībāhyam adbhiḥ saṃśodhya, ūrdhvapuṇḍraṃ kṛtvā, vāruṇamantraiḥ jalasekaṃ kṛtvā, dakṣiṇottarapāṇibhyāṃ haviḥ praṇaveṇa uddhṛty, śaṅkhadhvanisamanvitam ānīya, ālayasya uttare pārśve viṣṇugāyatryā nidhāya, vidhivad nivedayet.*
> 
> **वर्ज्य-हविः -**
> द्रोणाधिकम् एकपात्रेण न पचेत्; मासातीते मृद्भाण्डे पचनं न कुर्यात्। अपक्वं, स्रावितं, गन्धदुष्टं, नासिकास्यवायूपहतं (मुद्रितपाठे नासिकाऽऽस्य-), आघ्रातं, तुषकेशादिसंयुक्तं, विवृतं, द्विजातिभ्यः अन्यजातिदृष्ट-स्वेदस्पृष्टं, लङ्घितं, यामातीतं, पात्रान्तरगतं (मुद्रितपाठे पात्राऽन्तरगतं), शीतं, हविः उपदंशं च वर्जनीयम्। अन्यपात्रगतं पुनः पाकार्हं (मुद्रितपाठे पाकाहं) उपदंशादि न वर्जनीयम् ॥
> ***haviḥṣu uttarottaraśraiṣṭhyaṃ niveditānnaviniyogaśca -***
> *śuddhānnāt maudgikaṃ mukhyaṃ, tasmāt kṛsaraṃ, tataḥ pāyasaṃ, tato yāvakaṃ, tasmāt gaulyam; śreṣṭhaṃ haviḥ śreṣṭhaphalaṃ syāt, tasminnadhike'pyadhikam; nityam evaṃ havirdāne phalam anantaṃ prāpnoti. vidhinaiva bhaktiyukto yena kāmena vā viṣṇordeveśasya yo havirdadyāt, tat sarvaṃ saṃpūrṇam asaṃśayaṃ labheta.*
> 
> **हविरानयन-विधिः -**
> स्थालीबाह्यम् अद्भिः संशोध्य, ऊर्ध्वपुण्ड्रं कृत्वा, वारुणमन्त्रैः जलसेकं कृत्वा, दक्षिणोत्तरपाणिभ्यां हविः प्रणवेन उद्धृत्य, शङ्खध्वनिसमन्वितम् आनीय, आलयस्य उत्तरे पार्श्वे विष्णुगायत्र्या निधाय, विधिवत् निवेदयेत् ॥
> *tat-tat-sthālīśoṣam ācāryāya dadyāt, arcakāya vā; devasya niveditamannādisarvaṃ mācāryāya dadyāt. tanna anyaiḥ grahītavyam; anyathā ced bhagavān kruddho bhavet, havirdevo na gṛhṇāti, sarvaṃ niṣphalaṃ syāt.*
> 
> **हविःषूत्तरोत्तरश्रैष्ठ्यं निवेदितान्नविनियोगश्च -**
> शुद्धान्नात् मौद्गिकं मुख्यं, तस्मात् कृसरं, ततः पायसं (मुद्रितपाठे पायं), ततो यावकं, तस्मात् गौल्यम्; श्रेष्ठं हविः श्रेष्ठफलं स्यात्, तस्मिन् अधिकेऽप्यधिकम्; नित्यम् एवं हविर्दाने फलं अनन्तं प्राप्नोति।
> *deveśasya niveditaṃ sarvaṃ śuddhaṃ hi; tat sarveṣāṃ suramunimanujānāṃ bhojyaṃ; yathā guroḥ ucchiṣṭaṃ putrabhṛtyānāṃ bhojyaṃ, tathā sarveṣāṃ guroḥ viṣṇor ucchiṣṭaṃ devānāṃ munīnāṃ ca nivedaniyam. tasmād vaiśvadevabalihaṇe ca yogyaṃ syāt; manuṣyeṣu dvijātīnāṃ bhojyam eva. tasmād ācāryādayo viṣṇoḥ niveditabhojanād eva sarvapāpavinirmuktāḥ pūtakāyāḥ sarvakarmasvadhikāriṇo bhaveyuḥ. śūdrādiṣu ca vaiṣṇavānāṃ bhoktavyāṃ, anyadevānāṃ niveditaṃ tadarcakānāṃ tadbhaktānāṃ ca bhojyaṃ; dvijair bhuktaṃ ceccāndrāyaṇaṃ prāyaścittaṃ bhaved iti āha marīciḥ.*
> 
> विधिनैव भक्तियुक्तो येन कामेन वा विष्णोः देवेशस्य यो हविर्दद्यात् (मुद्रितपाठे विष्णोर्देवेशस्ययोर्विर्द्यात्), तत्सर्वं संपूर्णम् असंशयं लभेत।
> 
> तत्तत्स्थालीशोषम् आचार्याय दद्यात्, अर्चकाय वा; देवस्य निवेदितान्नादिसर्वम् आचार्याय दद्यात्। तन्नान्यैः ग्रहीतव्यम्; अन्यथा चेद् भगवान् क्रुद्धो भवेत्, हविर्देवो न गृह्णाति (मुद्रितपाठे गुण्हाति), सर्वं निष्फलं स्यात्।
> 
> देवेशस्य निवेदितं सर्वं शुद्धं हि; तत् सर्वेषां सुरमुनिमनुजानां भोज्यं; यथा गुरोः उच्छिष्टं पुत्रभृत्यानां भोज्यं, तथा सर्वेषां गुरोः विष्णोः उच्छिष्टं देवानां मुनीनां च निवेदनीयम्। तस्मात् वैश्वदेवबलिहरणे (मुद्रितपाठे द्वैश्वदेवबलिरणे) च योग्यं स्यात्; मनुष्येषु द्विजातीनां भोज्यमेव।
> 
> तस्माद् आचार्यादयो विष्णोः निवेदितभोजनादेव सर्वपापविनिर्मुक्ताः पूतकायाः सर्वकर्मसु अधिकारिणो भवेयुः। शूद्रादिषु च वैष्णवानां भोक्तव्यं, अन्यदेवानां निवेदितं तदर्चकानां तद्भक्तानां च भोज्यं; द्विजैः भुक्तं चेच्चान्द्रायणं प्रायश्चित्तं भवेदिति आह मरीचिः ॥

#### English Translation
*   **Continuation and Drying Grains:** ...and should avoid [unclean objects]. One should dry grains by spreading them on clean, beaten (*kuṭṭita*) cloths.

*   **Sanctification of Processing Tools:** Having washed the mortar and pestle (*ulūkhala-musale*), worshipped Hariṇī (Goddess of the mortar) in the mortar and Viṣṇu in the pestle, placed the grains in the mortar, and worshipped Soma in the grains.

*   **The Pounding Process (*Avaghāta*):** The *ācārya* (teacher/priest) should perform the initial pounding (*avaghāta*) with the mantra "Ato devā," and then have the pounding continued by clean women or men who have avoided sweat, hair, and other bodily impurities.

*   **Purifying the Rice:** Throwing the grains into the winnowing basket with the mantra "Nirastam," cleaning them of chaff (*tuṣa*), stones, and broken particles, sprinkling the clean rice (*taṇḍula*) with the mantra "Āpo hi ष्ठा," he takes it into a clean vessel with "Devasya tvā."

*   **Oblation Quantities for Viṣṇu:** For Viṣṇu, a *droṇa* (a large ancient measure of volume) of oblation is the highest (*uttama*), half a *droṇa* is medium (*madhyama*), and an *āḍhaka* is the lowest (*adhama*).

*   **Oblation Quantities for Goddesses and Retinue:** For the two Goddesses (Śrī and Hariṇī/Bhūmi), an *āḍhaka* or half of that is prescribed; and for the retinue deities (*parivāra-devas*), two *prasthas* or more of rice is prescribed.

*   **Definitions of Capacity and Weight Measures:** The definition of capacity and weights: eight sesamum seeds (*tila*) make one *māṣa*; eight *māṣas* make one *śāṇa*; twenty *śāṇas* make one *niṣka*; according to Sage Bhṛgu, eight sesamum seeds make a *śāṇa*, and eight *śāṇas* make a *māṣa*.

*   **Higher Metric Conversions:** Ten *niṣkas* make one *pala*, though some say eight *niṣkas*; four *palas* make one *kuḍuba*; four *kuḍubas* make one *prastha*; four *prasthas* make one *āḍhaka*; and four *āḍhakas* make one *droṇa*.

*   **Vessel Materials and Lifespans:** One should use a cooking vessel (*sthālī*) made of metal or clay; in the case of a clay vessel, it must be discarded after the ritual period (or after a single use); some say it can be used for up to a month.

*   **Washing and Preparing the Rice:** Having washed the vessel with the mantra "Prajāsthālīm," thrown the rice into it with "Devasya tvā," washed it four times with pure water reciting "Āpa undantu."

*   **Cooking on the Fire:** Placing the vessel on the stove (*cullī*) with the mantra "Viṣṇave juṣṭam," one should cook it over an even, smokeless (*nirvidhūma*) fire fed by wood, reciting "Vācaspataye pavasva."

*   **Approved Condiments (*Upadaṃśa*):** For side dishes (*upadaṃśa*), one should obtain according to availability: plantain, mango, jackfruit, coconut, tamarind, three types of bitter gourd, wild beans, two types of eggplant, *siṃhī*, wild jasmine (*vyāghranakhī*), jujube (*karkandhū*), cucumber, gourd, ash gourd, ivy gourd, nightshade (*alarka*), spinous momordica (*kārkoṭaka*), elephant foot yam (*sūraṇakanda*), wild yam (*kṣudrakanda*), great yam (*mahākanda*), climbing yam (*vallīkanda*), ginger (*śṛṅgibera*), blue lotus, *darpiṇḍa*, water chestnut (*śakuṭakanda*), black pepper (*marīci*), cumin, mustard, mung bean, horse gram, sesamum, black gram, lentils, and among leafy vegetables, *jīvantī*, *ratharasī*, and red spinach (*raktabāṣpa*), which are declared as fit for oblation (*havisya*) by Manu and others.

*   **Forbidden Vegetables:** One should especially avoid sponge gourd (*kośātakī*), pointed gourd (*paṭolī*), and bottle gourd (*alābu*).

*   **Preparation of Yam and Plantains:** Having washed one, two, three, four, or more defect-free yams or plantains, one should cook them well, seasoned with appropriate fragrant juices.

*   **Proportion of Side Ingredients:** For an *āḍhaka* of rice, the side dishes should weigh six, five, or four *palas* each; mung bean (*mudga-sāra*) should be one *prastha*, half a *prastha*, or a *kuḍuba*; ghee should be a *kuḍuba* or half a *kuḍuba*; jaggery should be one *pala*; yogurt should be three-fourths of a *prastha* or half a *prastha*.

*   **Sacred Leaves and Fruits:** Areca nuts should be sixteen, twelve, eight, six, four, or two; betel leaves should be double or triple that quantity; citron, cardamom, cloves, camphor, etc., should be offered as available, and these quantities should be increased proportionally for amounts exceeding one *āḍhaka* of rice.

*   **Large Scale Oblations:** An oblation from two *droṇas* up to six *droṇas* is called *prabhūta-havis* (abundant oblation), and from one hundred *prasthas* up to one thousand *prasthas* is called *mahā-havis* (great oblation).

*   **Six Types of Cooked Oblations:** The six types of oblation are:
    1. cooked rice prepared with rice alone (*śuddhānna*);
    2. rice cooked with an equal or half quantity of mung beans (*maudgika*);
    3. sweet rice cooked with triple the amount of milk, half that amount of water, half the amount of mung beans, and coconut pulp (*pāyasam*);
    4. rice cooked with half the amount of sesame powder (*kṛsaram*);
    5. rice cooked with excess water, triple the amount of milk, five or eight parts of mung beans, half the amount of ghee, and jaggery (*gaulyam*);
    6. rice cooked with half the amount of barley (*yāvakam*).

*   **Prohibitions on Vessels and Cooking:** One should not cook more than a *droṇa* in a single vessel, and cooking should not be done in a clay pot that has been used for more than a month.

*   **Improper Oblations (*Varjya-havis*):** One must avoid oblation that is undercooked, over-drained of starch, spoiled in smell, contaminated by breath from the nose or mouth, smelt, mixed with chaff or hair, uncovered, seen by non-dvijas, touched by sweat, crossed over by animals, kept for more than three hours (*yāma*), transferred to another vessel, or cold.

*   **Exception for Recooking:** However, side dishes that need to be transferred to another vessel for recooking are not to be rejected.

*   **Carrying the Oblation Vessel:** Having purified the exterior of the cooking pot with water, marked it with the vertical sectarian mark (*ūrdhva-puṇḍra*), sprinkled it with water using the Varuṇa mantras, and lifted the oblation with both hands reciting the Pranava (Om), the priest should bring it accompanied by the blowing of conch shells.

*   **Placing and Presenting the Oblation:** He should place it on the northern side of the temple reciting the Viṣṇu-Gāyatrī, and offer it according to the rules.

*   **Hierarchy of Food Offerings:** Among the oblations, *maudgika* is superior to *śuddhānna*, *kṛsara* is superior to that, *pāyasa* is superior to that, *yāvaka* is superior to that, and *gaulya* is the most superior; a superior oblation yields superior fruits, and an increase in quantity yields an increase in merit.

*   **Fruit of Daily Food Offerings:** By offering oblations daily in this manner, one obtains infinite fruits; he who offers oblation to Viṣṇu, the Lord of Gods, with devotion and according to the rules, for whatever desire, obtains it completely without doubt.

*   **Distribution of Offered Food (*Naivedya-viniyogaḥ*):** The scrapings from the bottom of the cooking pot (*sthālī-śoṣa*) should be given to the *ācārya* or the *arcaka*; all the offered food and other items of the Lord should be given to the *ācārya*.

*   **Strict Prohibitions on Leftovers:** It should not be taken by others; otherwise, the Lord becomes angry, the deity does not accept the oblation, and everything becomes fruitless.

*   **Sacred Nature of Offered Food:** Everything offered to the Lord of Gods is indeed pure and fit to be eaten by all—gods, sages, and humans; just as the leftovers of the teacher are eaten by his sons and servants, so the leftovers of Viṣṇu, the Teacher of all, are fit to be offered to the gods and sages.

*   **Use in Vaiśvadeva:** Therefore, it is fit for use in the *Vaiśvadeva* offering, and among humans, it is fit to be eaten by the twice-born (*dvijātis*).

*   **Spiritual Purification of Priests:** Therefore, by eating the food offered to Viṣṇu, the *ācārya* and others become free from all sins, pure in body, and qualified for all rituals.
