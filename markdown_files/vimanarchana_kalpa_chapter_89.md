# Vimanarchana Kalpa of Sage Marīci (Vaikhānasa Āgama)
## Chapter 89: Bhūrādi-tattva-prakāśaḥ (The Exposition of Elements Beginning with Earth)

This document contains the Sanskrit text, IAST transliteration, and English translation of the 89th Chapter (*Ekōnanavatitamaḥ Paṭalaḥ*) of the **Vimānārcanākalpa** (attributed to Sage Marīci, representing the Vaikhānasa school of Vaishnavism).

This chapter presents the classification of the human body and mind based on the five gross elements (*mahābhūta*), the ten senses (*indriya*), and the four inner instruments of mind (*antaḥkaraṇa*). It maps body parts and actions to their elemental origins, lists the characteristics of the three cosmic qualities (*guṇa*: Sattva, Rajas, and Tamas) and their corresponding spatial realms, and outlines the four states of consciousness (*jāgrat*, *svapna*, *suṣupti*, and *turīya*), culminating in the description of the embodied soul as a twenty-five-fold entity (*pañcaviṃśaty-ātmakaḥ puruṣaḥ*).

---

### Section 1: The Senses, Mind, and the Five Elements

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **अथैकोननवतितमः पटलः**
> **अथातः पृथिव्यादि-महाभूतानां समवायं व्याख्यास्यामः ।**
> **शरीरे काठिन्याद्युपपादनम्, इन्द्रियोत्पत्तिः तद्विषयश्च**
> ***athaikōnanavatitamaḥ paṭalaḥ***
> ***athātaḥ pṛthivyādi-mahābhūtānāṃ samavāyaṃ vyākhyāsyāmaḥ |***
> ***śarīrē kāṭhinyādyupapādanam, indriyōtpattiḥ tadviṣayaśca***
> 
> शरीरे यत् कठिनं तत् पृथिव्याः, यो द्रवः सोऽम्भसः, यदुष्णं तत् तेजसः, यत् सञ्चरति सोऽनिलस्य, यत् सुषिरं तदाकाशस्य । तत्र श्रोत्रादीनि ज्ञानेन्द्रियाणि । श्रोत्रमाकाशे, वायौ त्वक्, तेजसश्चक्षुः, अद्भ्यो जिह्वा, पृथिव्या घ्राणम् । एवम् इन्द्रियाणां यथाक्रमेण शब्द-स्पर्श-रूप-रस-गन्धाः विषयाः । वाक्-पाणि-पाद-पायू-पस्थानि महाभूतेषूत्पन्नानि कर्मेन्द्रियाणि । तेषां क्रमेण वचनाऽऽदान-गमन-विसर्गाऽऽनन्दाश्च विषयाः ॥
> *śarīrē yat kaṭhinaṃ tat pṛthivyāḥ, yō dravaḥ sō'mbhasaḥ, yaduṣṇaṃ tat tējasaḥ, yat sañcarati sō'nilasya, yat suṣiraṃ tadākāśasya | tatra śrōtrādīni jñānēndriyāṇi | śrōtramākāśē, vāyau tvak, tējasaccaxuḥ, adbhyō jihvā, pṛthivyā ghrāṇam | ēvam indriyāṇāṃ yathākramēṇa śabda-sparśa-rūpa-rasa-gandhāḥ viṣayāḥ | vāk-pāṇi-pāda-pāyū-pasthāni mahābhūtēṣūtpannāni karmēndriyāṇi | tēṣāṃ kramēṇa vacanā'dāna-gamana-visargā'nandāśca viṣayāḥ ||*

#### English Translation
*   **Anatomical Composition:** Now we shall explain the combination of the five elements beginning with Earth (*athātaḥ pṛthivyādi-mahābhūtānāṃ samavāyaṃ vyākhyāsyāmaḥ*). In the body, whatever is solid belongs to earth (*śarīrē yat kaṭhinaṃ tat pṛthivyāḥ*); fluid belongs to water (*yō dravaḥ sō'mbhasaḥ*); heat belongs to fire (*yduṣṇaṃ tat tējasaḥ*); that which moves belongs to air (*yat sañcarati sō'nilasya*); and the empty cavities belong to space (*yat suṣiraṃ tadākāśasya*).

*   **The Five Cognitive Senses:** The ears, skin, eyes, tongue, and nose are the cognitive senses (*śrōtrādīni jñānēndriyāṇi*). The ear is in space (*śrōtramākāśē*), the skin is in air (*vāyau tvak*), the eye is in fire (*tējasaccaxuḥ*), the tongue is in water (*adbhyō jihvā*), and the nose is in earth (*pṛthivyā ghrāṇam*). Their respective objects, in order, are sound, touch, sight, taste, and smell (*ēvam indriyāṇāṃ yathākramēṇa śabda-sparśa-rūpa-rasa-gandhāḥ viṣayāḥ*).

*   **The Five Active Senses:** The vocal organ, hands, feet, excretory organ, and generative organ are the active senses produced from the elements (*vāk-pāṇi-pāda-pāyū-pasthāni mahābhūtēṣūtpannāni karmēndriyāṇi*). Their respective objects, in order, are speech, grasping, movement, excretion, and sexual delight (*tēṣāṃ kramēṇa vacanā'dāna-gamana-visargā'nandāśca viṣayāḥ*).

### Section 2: Inner Instruments and Elemental Allocations

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **बुद्ध्यादीनामुत्पत्ति-विषय-स्थानानि**
> चतूर्षु पृथिव्यादि-महाभूतेषु क्रमेणोत्पन्नानि मनो-बुद्ध्य-अहङ्कार-चित्तानीत्यन्तःकरणचतुष्टयम् । तेषां क्रमेण सङ्कल्प-विकल्पाऽध्यवसाय-ममाभिमानाऽऽत्मावबोध-अनुभूतार्थस्मरणानि च विषयाः । मनःस्थानं गलान्तरम्, बुद्धेः वदनम्, अहङ्कारस्य हृदयम्, चित्तस्य नाभिरिति ।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **buddhyādīnāmutpatti-viṣaya-sthānāni**
> catūrṣu pṛthivyādi-mahābhūtēṣu kramēṇōtpannāni manō-buddhy-ahaṅkāra-cittānītyantaḥkaraṇacatuṣṭayam | tēṣāṃ kramēṇa saṅkalpa-vikalpā'dhyavasāya-mamābhimānā'tmāvabōdha-anubhūtārthasmaraṇāni ca viṣayāḥ | manaḥsthānaṃ galāntaram, buddhēḥ vadanam, ahaṅkārasya hṛdayam, cittasya nābhiriti |

**Translation (English):**
*   **The Four Inner Instruments:** Produced in order from the four gross elements is the fourfold inner instrument: mind (*manas*), intellect (*buddhi*), ego (*ahaṅkāra*), and memory/consciousness (*citta*). Their respective functions are: resolve/doubt (*saṅkalpa-vikalpa*), decision (*adhyavasāya*), identification of self/possession (*mamābhimāna*), and recollection of experienced objects (*anubhūtārthasmaraṇa*). The mind is seated in the throat (*galāntaram*), the intellect in the face/mouth (*vadanam*), the ego in the heart (*hṛdayam*), and the consciousness in the navel (*nābhiḥ*).

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **अस्थि-त्वगादीनां पृथिव्याद्यंशत्वम्**
> अस्थि-चर्म-रोम-नाडी-मांसाश्च पृथिव्यंशाः । मूत्र-श्लेष्म-रक्त-शुक्र-मेदः-स्वेदाश्च अब्अंशाः । क्षुत्-तृष्णा-निद्रा-आलस्य-मोह-मैथुनानि तेजोंशाः । प्रसारण-आकुञ्चन-उन्मीलन-निमीलनादीनि वाय्वंशाः । काम-क्रोध-लोभ-मोह-भयानि आकाशांशाः ।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **asthi-tvagādīnāṃ pṛthivyādyaṃśatvam**
> asthi-carma-rōma-nāḍī-māṃsāśca pṛthivyaṃśāḥ | mūtra-ślēṣma-rakta-śukra-mēdaḥ-svēdāśca ab-aṃśāḥ | xut-tṛṣṇā-nidrā-ālasya-mōha-maithunāni tējōṃśāḥ | prasāraṇa-ākuñcana-unmīlana-nimīlanādīni vāyvaṃśāḥ | kāma-krōdha-lōbha-mōha-bhayāni ākāśāṃśāḥ |

**Translation (English):**
*   **Bodily Parts and Elements:** 
    *   Bones, skin, hair, nerves, and flesh are parts of Earth (*asthi-carma-rōma-nāḍī-māṃsāśca pṛthivyaṃśāḥ*).
    *   Urine, phlegm, blood, semen, fat, and sweat are parts of Water (*mūtra-ślēṣma-rakta-śukra-mēdaḥ-svēdāśca ab-aṃśāḥ*).
    *   Hunger, thirst, sleep, laziness, delusion, and copulation are parts of Fire (*xut-tṛṣṇā-nidrā-ālasya-mōha-maithunāni tējōṃśāḥ*).
    *   Extension, contraction, opening and closing of eyes are parts of Air (*prasāraṇa-ākuñcana-unmīlana-nimīlanādīni vāyvaṃśāḥ*).
    *   Desire, anger, greed, delusion, and fear are parts of Space (*kāma-krōdha-lōbha-mōha-bhayāni ākāśāṃśāḥ*).

#### Verse / Paragraph 3
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **शब्दादीनां पृथिव्यादि-गुणत्वम्**
> शब्द-स्पर्श-रूप-रस-गन्धाश्चैते पृथिवीगुणाः । तेषां गन्धविहीनाश्चत्वारः अब्गुणाः । गन्ध-रस-विहीनास्त्रयः अग्निगुणाः । शब्द-स्पर्शौ इति द्वौ वायुगुणौ । शब्द एक एव आकाशस्य गुणः ॥

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **śabdādīnāṃ pṛthivyādi-guṇatvam**
> śabda-sparśa-rūpa-rasa-gandhāścaitē pṛthivīguṇāḥ | tēṣāṃ gandhavihīnāścatvāraḥ abguṇāḥ | gandha-rasa-vihīnāstrayaḥ agniguṇāḥ | śabda-sparśau iti dvau vāyuguṇau | śabda ēka ēva ākāśasya guṇaḥ ||

**Translation (English):**
*   **Distribution of Sensible Qualities:** Sound, touch, sight, taste, and smell belong to Earth. Lacking smell, the remaining four (sound, touch, sight, taste) belong to Water. Lacking smell and taste, the remaining three (sound, touch, sight) belong to Fire. Sound and touch belong to Air. Sound alone is the quality of Space.

### Section 3: The Three Qualities and the Four States of Soul

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **सात्त्विकगुणाः तेषां कार्यं च**
> सात्त्विक-राजस-तामसा इति त्रयो गुणाः । अहिंसा, सत्यम्, अस्तेयम्, ब्रह्मचर्यम्, अक्रोधः, गुरुशुश्रूषा, शौचम्, सन्तोषः, आस्तिक्यम्, आर्जवम् इति सात्त्विकलक्षणानि । ‘अहं कर्ता, अहं वक्ता, अहं भोक्ता’ इत्यादयोऽभिमानगुणा राजसलक्षणानि । निद्राऽऽलस्य-मोह-मैथुन-स्तेय-कर्माणि तामसलक्षणानि ।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **sāttvikaguṇāḥ tēṣāṃ kāryaṃ ca**
> sāttvika-rājasa-tāmasā iti trayō guṇāḥ | ahiṃsā, satyam, astēyam, brahmacaryam, akrōdhaḥ, guruśuśrūṣā, śaucam, santōṣaḥ, āstikyam, ārjavam iti sāttvikalaxanāni | ‘ahaṃ kartā, ahaṃ vaktā, ahaṃ bhōktā’ ityadayō'bhimānaguṇā rājasalaxanāni | nidrā'lasya-mōha-maithuna-stēya-karmāṇi tāmasalaxanāni |

**Translation (English):**
*   **The Three Cosmic Qualities:** There are three qualities (*guṇāḥ*): Sattva (goodness), Rajas (passion), and Tamas (darkness).
    *   **Sattva:** Non-violence, truth, non-stealing, celibacy/continence, absence of anger, service to the guru, purity, contentment, faith in the divine, and simplicity are the characteristics of Sattva (*sāttvikalaxanāni*).
    *   **Rajas:** Ornaments of pride such as "I am the doer," "I am the speaker," and "I am the enjoyer" are the characteristics of Rajas (*rājasalaxanāni*).
    *   **Tamas:** Sleep, laziness, delusion, copulation, and theft are the characteristics of Tamas (*tāmasalaxanāni*).

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **सात्त्विकादीनां स्थानं तत्स्वरूपं च**
> ऊर्ध्वं सात्त्विकाः, मध्ये राजसाः, अधस्तात् तामसा इति । सम्यग्ज्ञानं सात्त्विकं, धर्मज्ञानं राजसं, तिमिरान्धं तामसमिति ।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **sāttvikādīnāṃ sthānaṃ tatsvarūpaṃ ca**
> ūrdhvaṃ sāttvikāḥ, madhyē rājasāḥ, adhastāt tāmasā iti | samyagjñānaṃ sāttvikaṃ, dharmajñānaṃ rājasaṃ, timirāndhaṃ tāmasamiti |

**Translation (English):**
*   **The Realms of Qualities:** Sattva belongs to the higher realms (*ūrdhvaṃ sāttvikāḥ*), Rajas to the middle (*madhyē rājasāḥ*), and Tamas to the lower realms (*adhastāt tāmasāḥ*). Right knowledge represents Sattva (*samyagjñānaṃ sāttvikaṃ*), ritual action/duty represents Rajas (*dharmajñānaṃ rājasaṃ*), and blinding ignorance represents Tamas (*timirāndhaṃ tāmasamiti*).

#### Verse / Paragraph 3
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **जाग्रदाद्यवस्थाभेदः**
> जाग्रत्-स्वप्न-सुषुप्ति-तुरीयम् इति चतुर्विधावस्थाः । ज्ञानेन्द्रिय-कर्मेन्द्रिय-अन्तःकरण-चतुष्टय-चतुर्दश-करण-युतं जाग्रत् । अन्तःकरण-चतुष्टय-मात्र-युक्तं स्वप्नम् । चित्तैक-करण-युता सुषुप्तिः । केवल-जीव-युक्तम् एव तुरीयम् इति ।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **jāgradādyavasthābhēdaḥ**
> jāgrat-svapna-suṣupti-turīyam iti caturvidhāvasthāḥ | jñānēndriya-karmēndriya-antaḥkaraṇa-catuṣṭaya-caturdaśa-karaṇa-yutaṃ jāgrat | antaḥkaraṇa-catuṣṭaya-mātra-yutaṃ svapnam | cittaika-karaṇa-yutā suṣuptiḥ | kēvala-jīva-yaktam ēva turīyam iti |

**Translation (English):**
*   **The Four States of Consciousness:** The four states of the soul are waking, dreaming, deep sleep, and the fourth transcendent state (*jāgrat-svapna-suṣupti-turīyam*).
    *   **Waking (Jāgrat):** Joined with fourteen instruments — the five cognitive senses, five active senses, and four inner instruments (*jñānēndriya-karmēndriya-antaḥkaraṇa-catuṣṭaya-caturdaśa-karaṇa-yutaṃ jāgrat*).
    *   **Dreaming (Svapna):** Joined only with the four inner instruments (*antaḥkaraṇa-catuṣṭaya-mātra-yutaṃ svapnam*).
    *   **Deep Sleep (Suṣupti):** Joined only with the single instrument of memory/consciousness (*cittaika-karaṇa-yutā suṣuptiḥ*).
    *   **Transcendent (Turīya):** Joined only with the pure individual soul (*kēvala-jīva-yaktam ēva turīyam*).

#### Verse / Paragraph 4
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> (उन्मीलित-जीव-मध्यस्थः) परमात्म-जीवात्मनोर्मध्ये जीवात्मा क्षेत्रज्ञ इति विज्ञायते । सोऽपि पञ्चमहाभूतानि, दशेन्द्रियाणि, पञ्च विषयाः, अन्तःकरणचतुष्टयम् इत्येतैः पञ्चविंशात्मकः पुरुषः । तस्मात् पञ्चविंशात्मको देह इति विज्ञायते ॥

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> (unmīlita-jīva-madhyasthaḥ) paramātma-jīvātmanōrmadhyē jīvātmā kṣētrajña iti vijñāyatē | sō'pi pañcamahābhūtāni, daśēndriyāni, pañca viṣayāḥ, antaḥkaraṇacatuṣṭayam ityētaiḥ pañcaviṃśaty-ātmakaḥ puruṣaḥ | tasmāt pañcaviṃśaty-ātmakō dēha iti vijñāyatē ||

**Translation (English):**
*   **The Twenty-Five-Fold Being:** Between the Supreme Soul and the individual soul, the individual soul residing in the body is known as the knower of the field, the Kshetrajna (*kṣētrajñaḥ*). Composed of the five gross elements, ten senses, five sense objects, and four inner instruments of mind (*pañcamahābhūtāni daśēndriyāni pañca viṣayāḥ antaḥkaraṇacatuṣṭayam*), he is the twenty-five-fold Puruṣa (*pañcaviṃśaty-ātmakaḥ puruṣaḥ*). Consequently, the physical body is also known to be composed of twenty-five elements (*pañcaviṃśaty-ātmakō dēhaḥ*).

### Glossary of Key Terms

1.  **Mahābhūta (महाभूत):** The five gross elements: earth (*pṛthivī*), water (*ap*), fire (*tējas*), air (*vāyu*), and space (*ākāśa*).
2.  **Jñānēndriya (ज्ञानेन्द्रिय):** The five cognitive senses: ears (*śrōtra*), skin (*tvak*), eyes (*caxu*), tongue (*jihvā*), and nose (*ghrāṇa*).
3.  **Karmēndriya (कर्मेन्द्रिय):** The five active senses: speech (*vāc*), hands (*pāṇi*), feet (*pāda*), excretory organ (*pāyu*), and generative organ (*upastha*).
4.  **Antaḥkaraṇa (अन्तःकरण):** The four inner instruments of mind: mind (*manas*), intellect (*buddhi*), ego (*ahaṅkāra*), and memory/consciousness (*citta*).
5.  **Jāgrat (जाग्रत्):** Waking consciousness, where all 14 organs of experience are active.
6.  **Svapna (स्वप्न):** Dreaming consciousness, where only the 4 internal mental faculties are active.
7.  **Suṣupti (सुषुप्ति):** Deep sleep, where only the consciousness (*citta*) remains active.
8.  **Turīya (तुरीय):** The fourth transcendent state of consciousness, representing pure spiritual liberation.
9.  **Kṣetrajña (क्षेत्रज्ञ):** The knower of the body, the individual soul (*jīvātman*).
10. **Pañcaviṃśaty-ātmaka (पञ्चविंशात्मक):** Composed of twenty-five elements (tattvas), representing the embodied soul in Samkhya/Agamic philosophy.
