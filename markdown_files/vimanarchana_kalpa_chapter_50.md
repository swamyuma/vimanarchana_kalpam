# Chapter 50: Specifications and Consecration of the Sudarśana Disc (Cakra-lakṣaṇa-vidhiḥ / Cakra-pratiṣṭhā-vidhiḥ)
### Section 1: Liturgical Rules for the Dimensions and Consecration of the Sudarśana Disc

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **अथ पञ्चाशत्तमः पटलः**
> **चक्रलक्षण-चक्रप्रतिष्ठा-विधिः -**
> ***atha pañcāśattamaḥ paṭalaḥ***
> ***cakralaxaṇa-cakrapratiṣṭhā-vidhiḥ -***
> 
> **(चक्रलक्षणविधिः)**
> अथोत्सवचक्रं सौवर्णं रजतं ताम्रम् आयसं दारवं वा कृत्वा; मानाङ्गुलेन, मात्राङ्गुलेन वा, ध्रुवबेरस्य, कौतुकस्य वा देहलब्धाङ्गुलेन वा; षोडशाङ्गुलं, द्वादशाङ्गुलं, अष्टाङ्गुलं वा समवृत्तं; तस्यापरपट्टविस्तारं त्र्यङ्गुलं, द्व्यङ्गुलम्, एकाङ्गुलं वा; तन्मध्यघनमेकाङ्गुलम्, आरकूटस्य विस्तारं त्र्यङ्गुलं, द्व्यङ्गुलं वा, पद्मं; सविशेषमराणाम् अरणिरेणुपत्रसमानि, चतुरश्राणि वा, चतुर्विंशत्यरमुत्तमं, विंशत्यरं मध्यमं, षोडशारमधमम् ।
> ***(cakralaxaṇavidhiḥ)***
> *athotsavacakraṃ souvarṇaṃ rajataṃ tāmram āyasaṃ dāravaṃ vā kṛtvā; mānāṅgulena, mātrāṅgulena vā, dhruvaberasya, kautukasya vā dehalabdhāṅgulena vā; ṣoḍaśāṅgulaṃ, dvādaśāṅgulaṃ, aṣṭāṅgulaṃ vā samavṛttaṃ; tasyāparapaṭṭavistāraṃ tryaṅgulaṃ, dvyaṅgulam, ekāṅgulaṃ vā; tanmadhyaghanamekāṅgulam, ārakūtasya vistāraṃ tryaṅgulaṃ, dvyaṅgulaṃ vā, padmaṃ; saviśeṣamarāṇām araṇireṇupatrasamāni, caturasrāṇi vā, caturviṃśatyaramuttamaṃ, viṃśatyaraṃ madhyamaṃ, ṣoḍaśāramadhamam.*
> 
> तन्मूर्ध्नि पार्श्वयोश्च ज्वालायाम् त्र्यङ्गुलं, प्रभाज्वाला-समज्वाला वा, तत्पट्टमध्ये वल्लीसमचतुर्लेखायुश्रीतम् (वल्लीसमचतुर्लेखाविभूषितम्), तस्याधस्तात् फलकाः कुम्भतापीश-युगाङ्ग-विस्तारोत्सेधाः षण्मात्रायताः, उभयोः पार्श्वयोः द्वौ सिंहौ चक्रमुद्वहन्तौ वा कारयेत् । दन्तद्रव्येण सहैव कुर्यात् । अथवा चन्दनेन वृक्षेण वा कुर्यात् ।
> *tanmūrdhni pārśvayośca jvālāyām tryaṅgulaṃ, prabhājvālā-samajvālā vā, tatpaṭṭamadhye vallīsamacaturlekhāyuśrītam (vallīsamacaturlekhāvibhūṣitam), tasyādhastāt phalakāḥ kumbhatāpīśa-yugāṅga-vistārotsedhāḥ ṣaṇmātrāyatāḥ, ubhayoḥ pārśvayoḥ dvou siṃhou cakramudvahantou vā kārayet. dantadravyeṇa sahaiva kuryāt. athavā candanena vṛxeṇa vā kuryāt.*
> 
> दण्डायामं चक्रायामात् त्रिगुणं, द्विगुणं, समं वा; षोडशाङ्गुलं, द्वादशाङ्गुलं, नवाङ्गुलं वा मूलनाहं; तदष्टांशोनमग्रनाहः; मूलादूर्ध्वं क्रमात् कृशदण्डादधस्तात् पीठं चतुरङ्गुलोन्नतम्, अष्टाङ्गुलोन्नत-विस्तार-आयामं, चतुरस्रं, वृत्तं वा, पद्माकारम् अष्टदलैरावृतं कृत्वा; सुदृढं योजयेत् । अत्रानुक्तं सर्वं शिल्पशास्त्रोक्तविधिना कारयेत् ।
> *daṇḍāyāmaṃ cakrāyāmāt triguṇaṃ, dviguṇaṃ, samaṃ vā; ṣoḍaśāṅgulaṃ, dvādaśāṅgulaṃ, navāṅgulaṃ vā mūlanāhaṃ; tadaṣṭāṃśonamagranāhaḥ; mūlādūrdhvaṃ kramāt kṛṣadaṇḍādadhasthāt pīṭhaṃ caturaṅgulonnatam, aṣṭāṅgulonnata-vistāra-āyāmaṃ, caturasraṃ, vṛttaṃ vā, padmākāram aṣṭadalairāvṛtaṃ kṛtvā; sudṛḍhaṃ yojayet. atrānuktaṃ sarvaṃ śilpaśāstroktavidhinā kārayet.*
> 
> अथवा नवतालेन चक्रं मानाङ्गुलेन समाङ्गुलं, नवाङ्गुलं, एकादशाङ्गुलं वा, द्विभुजं, प्राञ्जलीकृत्य स्थितं, किरीटाद्याभरणान्वितं, त्रिभागैकभागं पीठं, तस्योपरिभागे चक्रविस्तारं मानाङ्गुलेन द्वादशाङ्गुलम् उत्तमं; नवाङ्गुलं मध्यमं; षडङ्गुुलं कनिष्ठम् (मुद्रितपाठे मध्यमम्) । अन्यत्सर्वं पूर्ववत् । तस्य शिरसि चक्रं संयोज्य सुदृढं कुर्यात् ॥
> *athavā navatālena cakraṃ mānāṅgulena samāṅgulaṃ, navāṅgulaṃ, ekādaśāṅgulaṃ vā, dvibhujaṃ, prāñjalīkṛtya sthitaṃ, kirīṭādyābharaṇanvitaṃ, tribhāgaikabhāgaṃ pīṭhaṃ, tasyoparibhāge cakravistāraṃ mānāṅgulena dvādaśāṅgulam uttamaṃ; navāṅgulaṃ madhyamaṃ; ṣadaṅgulaṃ kaniṣṭham (mudritapāṭhe madhyamam). anyatsarvaṃ pūrvavat. tasya śirasi cakraṃ saṃyojya sudṛḍhaṃ kuryāt.*
> 
> **(चक्रप्रतिष्ठाविधिः)**
> अथ चक्रस्य प्रतिष्ठां वक्ष्ये - शुभे कर्तुरनुकूले नक्षत्रे कुर्यात् । तद्दिनात्पूर्वम् अङ्कुरानर्पयित्वा, तच्चक्रं संशोध्य, वास्तुहोमं हुत्वा, पर्यग्नि-पञ्चगव्याभ्यां संशोध्य, पुण्याहं कृत्वा, जले गव्ये कुशोदके च प्रत्येकमधिवासयेत् । प्रतिमा चेत् पूर्ववत् अक्षिमोचनं तन्मन्त्रेण कारयेत् ।
> ***(cakrapratiṣṭhāvidhiḥ)***
> *cakrapratiṣṭhāṃ vakṣye - śubhe karturanukūle naxatre kuryāt. taddinātpūrvam aṅkurānarpayitvā, taccakraṃ saṃśodhya, vāstuhomaṃ hutvā, paryagni-pañcagavyābhyāṃ saṃśodhya, puṇyāhaṃ kṛtvā, jale gavye kuśodake ca pratyekamadhivāsayet. pratimā cet pūrvavat aximocanaṃ tanmantreṇa kārayet.*
> 
> अधिवास्य, देवालयाभिमुखे यागशालां कृत्वा, तोरणाद्यैरलङ्कृत्य; तन्मध्ये शय्यावेदिं चक्रस्याऽध्यर्धविस्तारां तत्तृतीयां (तुरीयां) शोत्सेधां चतुरस्रां कृत्वा; तत्प्राच्यां मौपासनाग्निकुण्डं, तत्प्राच्यां स्नपनश्वभ्रं च कृत्वा; पूर्वेद्युरपराह्णे अधिवासगतं चक्रमादाय; शुद्धोदकैः संस्नाप्य; आलयमावेश्य; यज्ञालयात् पश्चिमे विष्टरे प्राङ्मुखं संस्थाप्य; आघारं हुत्वा; चतुराढकपूर्णं खण्डस्फुटितकालरहितं कुम्भं संगृहीत्वा; तन्तुना परिवेष्ट्य; अद्भिः प्रक्षाल्य अद्भिरापूर्य; वस्त्राभ्यां वेष्टयित्वा; पञ्चरत्नानि सुवर्णं वा पञ्चायुधाँश्च विक्षिप्य; चक्रस्याऽभिमुखे धान्यपीठे संन्यस्य; आचार्यश्चोत्तराभिमुखः तच्चक्रं ध्यायन्, कुम्भाम्भसि समावाह्य; तच्चक्रं कुम्भं च समादाय स्नपनश्वभ्रं प्रतिष्ठाप्य; सप्तभिः कलशैः तथैवाऽभिषिच्य; वस्त्रद्वयैरलङ्कृत्य; शय्यावेद्यां धान्योपरि पञ्चशयनानि पञ्चवस्त्राण्यास्तीर्य; कुम्भं चक्रं च समारोप्य; तदैव प्रतिसरं बद्ध्वा; तन्मन्त्रेण शाययितvā uttarācchādanaṃ kṛtvā; tathāiva houtrapraśaṃsya; cakrādipañcāyudhān mūrtimantraiḥ samāvāhya; āvāhanākrameṇa juṣṭākāra-svāhākārou kṛtvā; vaiṣṇavaṃ soudarśanaṃ cakragāyatrīṃ ca aṣṭottaraśatāvṛttyā huत्वा, रात्रिशेषं व्यपोह्य; प्रभाते स्नात्वा; आलयमाविश्य; चक्रमुत्थाप्य; अभ्यर्च्य, मुहूर्ते समनुप्राप्ते यजमान आचार्यादिभ्यो दक्षिणां दद्यात्, कुम्भं चक्रं च समादाय; आलयं प्रदक्षिणीकृत्य; अभ्यन्तरे मुखमण्डपे वा दक्षिणपार्श्वे कृते पीठे रत्नं सुवर्णं वा निक्षिप्य; तन्मन्त्रेण प्रतिष्ठाप्य; तद्बीजाक्षरं संन्यस्य; कुम्भस्थां शक्तिं कूर्चेनाऽऽदाय; तन्मन्त्रेण तन्मूर्तिमन्त्रैः आवाह्य; आसनाद्यैः अभ्यर्च्य, हविर्निवेदयेत् । एवं यः कुरुते भक्त्या रिपून् सर्वान् जित्वा; इष्टान् कामानवाप्य; विष्णोः लोके महीयते इति विज्ञायते ॥
> *adhivāsya, devālayābhimukhe yāgāśālāṃ kṛtvā, toraṇādyairalaṅkṛtya; tanmadhye śayyāvediṃ cakrasyā’dhyardhavistārāṃ tattṛtīyāṃ (turīyāṃ) śotsedhāṃ caturasrāṃ kṛtvā; tatprācyāṃ moupāsanāgnikuṇḍaṃ, tatprācyāṃ snapanasvabhraṃ ca kṛtvā; pūrvedyuraparāhṇe adhivāsagataṃ cakramādāya; śuddhodakaiḥ saṃsnāpya; ālayamāveśya; yajñālayāt paścime viṣṭare prāṅmukhaṃ saṃsthāpya; āghāraṃ hutvā; caturāḍhakapūrṇaṃ khaṇḍasphuṭitakālarahitaṃ kumbhaṃ saṃgṛhītvā; tantunā pariveṣṭya; adbhiḥ praṣālya adbhirāpūrya; vastrābhyāṃ veṣṭayitvā; pañcaratnāni suvarṇaṃ vā pañcāyudhāṃśca vixipya; cakrasyā’bhimukhe dhānyapīṭhe saṃnyasya; ācāryaścottarābhimukhaḥ taccakraṃ dhyāyan, kumbhāmbhasi samāvāhya; taccakraṃ kumbhaṃ ca samādāya snapanasvabhraṃ pratiṣṭhāpya; saptabhiḥ kalaśaiḥ tathāivā’bhiṣicya; vastradvayairalaṅkṛtya; śayyāvedyāṃ dhānyopari pañcaśayanāni pañcavastrāṇyāstīrya; kumbhaṃ cakraṃ ca samāropya; tadāiv pratisaraṃ baddhvā; tanmantreṇa śāyayitvā uttarācchādanaṃ kṛtvā; tathāiva houtrapraśaṃsya; cakrādipañcāyudhān mūrtimantraiḥ samāvāhya; āvāhanākrameṇa juṣṭākāra-svāhākārou kṛtvā; vaiṣṇavaṃ soudarśanaṃ cakragāyatrīṃ ca aṣṭottaraśatāvṛttyā hutvā, rātriśeṣaṃ vyapohya; prabhāte snātvā; ālayamāviśya; cakramutthāpya; abhyarcya, muhūrte samanuprāpte yajamāna ācāryādibhyo daxiṇāṃ dadyāt, kumbhaṃ cakraṃ ca samādāya; ālayaṃ pradacṣiṇīkṛtya; abhyantare muṣamaṇḍape vā daxiṇapārśve kṛte pīṭhe ratnaṃ suvarṇaṃ vā nixipya; tanmantreṇa pratiṣṭhāpya; tadbījāxaraṃ saṃnyasya; kumbhasthāṃ śaktiṃ kūrcenā’’dāya; tanmantreṇa tanmūrtimantraiḥ āvāhya; āsanādyaiḥ abhyarcya, havirnivedayet. evaṃ yaḥ kurute bhaktyā ripūn sarvān jitvā; iṣṭān kāmānavāpya; viṣṇoḥ loke mahīyate iti vijñāyate.*

#### English Translation
*   **Materials for the Processional Disc:** Now I shall describe the specifications for the processional disc (*utsava-cakra*): it should be made of gold, silver, copper, iron, or wood.

*   **Dimensions of the Disc:** Measured by standard finger units (*mānāṅgula*), patron's finger units (*mātrāṅgula*), or the units derived from the main deity's body (*dehalabdha-aṅgula*). It should be sixteen, twelve, or eight *aṅgulas* in diameter, perfectly circular. The width of its outer rim (*paṭṭa*) should be three, two, or one *aṅgula*, and its central thickness should be one *aṅgula*. The central lotus motif made of brass (*ārakūṭa*) should be three or two *aṅgulas* wide.

*   **Spokes and Flame Features:** The spokes (*aras*) should be shaped like the leaves of the *araṇi* or *reṇu* plants, or square, with twenty-four spokes being the highest (*uttama*), twenty spokes the middle (*madhyama*), and sixteen spokes the lowest (*adhama*) configuration. On top and on the sides, the flames (*jvālā*) should project three *aṅgulas*, shaped like the primary flame aura or matching flames. In the middle of the rim, it should be decorated with four creeping vine carvings (*vallī*). Below it, there should be plaques (*phalakās*) representing the water pot, fire, and other Agamic figures, measuring six parts in length. On both sides, there should be two lions carved as if carrying the disc. This can also be carved from ivory (*danta*), sandalwood (*candana*), or sacred wood.

*   **The Shaft and Pedestal:** The length of the carrying shaft (*daṇḍa*) should be three times, twice, or equal to the disc's diameter. The circumference at the base should be sixteen, twelve, or nine *aṅgulas*, and the top circumference should be one-eighth less than the base. From the base upwards, it should gradually taper. Below the thin shaft, there should be a pedestal four *aṅgulas* high, and eight *aṅgulas* in length, width, and height, shaped as a square, a circle, or an eight-petaled lotus. It must be joined very firmly. Anything not specified here should be made according to the rules of the *Śilpa-śāstra*.

*   **Anthropomorphic Disc Deity (Sudarśana Puruṣa):** Alternatively, one may carve the disc deity in the nine-span (*navatāla*) system, measured in standard finger units, nine or eleven *aṅgulas* high. He should have two arms, stand with folded hands (*prāñjali*), and be adorned with a crown and other ornaments. The pedestal should be one-third of the total height. On top of Him, the disc should be mounted, measuring twelve *aṅgulas* (for the highest), nine *aṅgulas* (for the middle), or six *aṅgulas* (for the lowest) class. All other features are as before. His head must be firmly joined to the disc.

*   **Preparations for Consecration (Cakra-pratiṣṭhā):** Now I shall describe the consecration of the disc (*cakra-pratiṣṭhā*): it should be done under a star favorable to the patron on an auspicious day. Having performed the seed-planting (*aṅkurārpaṇa*) before that day, purified the disc, performed the site fire-oblation (*vāstu-homa*), purified it with fire and *pañcagavya*, performed the purification ritual (*puṇyāha*), one should submerge it (*adhivāsa*) in water, cow products, and Darbha water separately. If it is an image (with eyes), one should perform the opening of the eyes (*akṣi-mocana*) with its specific mantra.

*   **Bathing and Altar Arrangement:** Having completed the submergence, built a sacrificial hall in front of the temple, decorated it with arches, made a bedding altar (*śayyā-vedi*) in the middle measuring one and a half times the disc's width and one-third (or one-fourth) of that in height, shaped as a square. On its east, make an Aupāsana fire-pit, and on its east, make a bathing pit (*snana-śvabhra*). In the afternoon of the previous day, take the submerged disc, bathe it with pure water, enter the temple, and place it facing east on a seat to the west of the sacrificial hall.

*   **Consecrating the Pot (*Kumbha*):** Having performed the introductory libation, take a vessel (*kumbha*) capable of holding four *āḍhakas* of water, free from cracks and chips, wrap it with cotton threads, wash it, fill it with water, wrap it with two cloths, throw in five gems or gold, and the five weapons of Viṣṇu. Place it on a grain pedestal facing the disc. The priest, facing north, meditating on the disc, should invoke its power into the water of the vessel.

*   **Bathing and laying to sleep:** Taking the disc and the vessel, placing them in the bathing pit, bathing them with seven water pots, decorating them with two cloths, spreading five beds and five cloths on the grain pedestal in the bedding altar, mounting the vessel and the disc on it, tying the wrist-band (*pratisara*) at that time, laying them to sleep with the respective mantra, and covering them with an upper cloth.

*   **Fire-Oblations and Consecration:** Worshipping the disc and the other five weapons with their respective form-mantras praised by the chanter, offering libations in order with the *vaṣaṭ* and *svāhā* calls, performing one hundred and eight oblations to Viṣṇu, Sudarśana, and the Cakra-gāyatrī, and spending the rest of the night.

*   **Morning Rituals and final establishment:** Waking up in the morning, bathing, entering the temple, raising the disc, worshipping it, and when the auspicious moment arrives, the patron should give sacrificial fees to the priest and others. Taking the vessel and the disc, circumambulating the temple, placing gold and gems on the pedestal prepared on the south side of the front porch (*mukha-maṇḍapa*), establishing the disc with its specific mantra, engraving its seed-letter (*bījākṣara*), taking the power from the vessel with a Darbha brush (*kūrca*), invoking it with its form-mantras, worshipping with *āsana* etc., he should offer food. He who performs this with devotion conquers all enemies, attains desired wishes, and resides in the world of Viṣṇu — so it is understood.
