# Vimanarchana Kalpa of Sage Marīci (Vaikhānasa Āgama)
## Chapter 94: Saṃsāra-hētu-tattaraṇōpāya-mōxa-svarūpa-vidhiḥ (The Cause of Transmigration, the Means of Crossing It, and the Nature of Liberation)

This document contains the Sanskrit text, IAST transliteration, and English translation of the 94th Chapter (*Caturnavatitamaḥ Paṭalaḥ*) of the **Vimānārcanākalpa** (attributed to Sage Marīci, representing the Vaikhānasa school of Vaishnavism).

This chapter outlines the core Vaikhānasa Agamic soteriology. It details the cause of worldly bondage (*saṃsāra*), describing how the embodied soul, deluded by the Lord's *māyā*, becomes bound by passions and repeatedly cycles through births and deaths. It then presents the path to deliverance: seeking refuge in Nārāyaṇa, whose grace removes *māyā* and grants right knowledge (*samyag-jñāna*). Finally, it defines liberation (*mōxa*) and explains the four distinct states of release (*caturvidha-mōxa*): *sālokya* (residing in the Lord's realm, Āmoda), *sāmīpya* (being near the Lord, Pramoda), *sārūpya* (having the Lord's form, Sammoda), and *sāyujya* (absolute union with the Lord, Vaikuṇṭha), culminating in eternal service (*nitya-kaiṅkarya*) and merging into the Supreme Light (*parañ-jyōtiḥ*).

### Section 1: The Cause of Bondage and the Means of Salvation

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **अथ चतुर्नवतितमः पटलः**
> **संसारहेतुः तत्तरणोपायश्च**
> ***atha caturnavatitamaḥ paṭalaḥ***
> ***saṃsārahētuḥ tattaraṇōpāyaśca***
> 
> अथ अयम् देहः जन्म लब्ध्वा (कृत्वा) मायामय-पाश-निबद्धो भगवन्मायया मोहितः काम-क्रोध-लोभ-मोह-मद-मात्सर्य-हिंसादीनि करोति ।
> योनिद्वारेण निष्क्रम्य पुनः पापीयसीं योनिमाश्रित्य पुनर्जायमानः स्वर्ग-नरक-पातकेषु कर्मसु प्रवर्तते । तस्माद् भगवन्मायया मोहितत्वाद् भगवन्तं समाश्रित्य भक्त्या नारायणमुपासीत ।
> *atha ayam dēhaḥ janma labdhvā (kṛtvā) māyāmaya-pāśa-nibaddhō bhagavanmāyayā mōhitaḥ kāma-krōdha-lōbha-mōha-mada-mātsarya-hiṃsādīni karōti |*
> *yōnidvārēṇa niṣkramya punaḥ pāpīyasīṃ yōnimāśritya punarjāyamānaḥ svarga-naraka-pātakēṣu karmasu pravartatē | tasmād bhagavanmāyayā mōhitatvād bhagavantaṃ samāśritya bhaktyā nārāyaṇamupāsīta ||*
> 
> **तत्तरणोपायः**
> तदुपासनात् सोऽपि भक्तवत्सलत्वाद् भक्तानुकम्पया स्वमायां मोचयति । ततः आत्मनः सम्यग्ज्ञानं प्रददाति (or प्रविशति) । पश्चाद् आश्रमधर्मयुक्तो भगवदाराधनं करोति । तदाराधनेन संसारार्णवनिमनो जीवात्मा परमात्मानं शरणं याति (or प्राप्नोति) । सोऽपि अपुनरावृत्तिकं दिव्यलोकं प्रयच्छति । पश्चात् कृतकृत्यो भवति ॥
> ***tattaraṇōpāyaḥ***
> *tadupāsanāt sō'pi bhaktavatsalatvād bhaktānukampayā svamāyāṃ mōcayati | tataḥ ātmanaḥ samyagjñānaṃ pradadāti (or praviśati) | paścād āśramadharmayuktō bhagavadārādhanaṃ karōti | tadārādhanēna saṃsārārṇavanimagnō jīvātmā paramātmānaṃ śaraṇaṃ yāti (or prāpnōti) | sō'pi apunarāvruttikaṃ divyalōkaṃ prayacchati | paścāt kṛtakṛtyō bhavati ||*

#### English Translation
*   **The Bound State of Man:** Now, this physical body, having taken birth (*atha ayam dēhaḥ janma labdhvā*), bound by the illusory nooses of worldly existence (*māyāmaya-pāśa-nibaddhō*), and deluded by the Maya of the Lord (*bhagavanmāyayā mōhitaḥ*), commits lust, anger, greed, delusion, pride, envy, and violence (*kāma-krōdha-lōbha-mōha-mada-mātsarya-hiṃsādīni karōti*).

*   **The Cycle of Transmigration:** Exiting through the birth canal, again taking refuge in a sinful womb, being born repeatedly (*yōnidvārēṇa niṣkramya punaḥ pāpīyasīṃ yōnimāśritya punarjāyamānaḥ*), the soul engages in actions that lead to heaven, hell, or sinful degradation (*svarga-naraka-pātakēṣu karmasu pravartatē*). Therefore, because of being deluded by the Lord's Maya (*tasmād bhagavanmāyayā mōhitatvād*), one must seek refuge in the Lord and worship Nārāyaṇa with devotion (*bhagavantaṃ samāśritya bhaktyā nārāyaṇamupāsīta*).

*   **The Path of Grace:** By that worship, the Lord, who is affectionate to his devotees (*sō'pi bhaktavatsalatvād*), out of compassion, dissolves His own Maya (*bhaktānukampayā svamāyāṃ mōcayati*). Then, He bestows right knowledge upon the soul (*tataḥ ātmanaḥ samyagjñānaṃ pradadāti*).

*   **Devotion and Deliverance:** Thereafter, performing the duties of one's stage of life, the devotee engages in the worship of the Lord (*paścād āśramadharmayuktō bhagavadārādhanaṃ karōti*). Through that worship, the individual soul submerged in the ocean of transmigration (*tadārādhanēna saṃsārārṇavanimagnō jīvātmā*) takes refuge in the Supreme Soul (*paramātmānaṃ śaraṇaṃ yāti*). The Lord then grants him the divine realm of non-return (*sō'pi apunarāvruttikaṃ divyalōkaṃ prayacchati*), and thereafter the soul becomes fully fulfilled (*paścāt kṛtakṛtyō bhavati*).

### Section 2: The Nature of Liberation and the Four States of Release

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **मोक्षस्वरूपं तद्भेदश्च**
> संसारबन्धन-वासना-विमुक्तिः मोक्षः । तदपि समाराधन-विशेष-चतुर्विध-पदावाप्तिः -- सालोक्यं, सामीप्यं, सारूप्यं, सायुज्यम् इति ।
> आमोदप्राप्तिः सालोक्यं, प्रमोदप्राप्तिः सामीप्यं, सम्मोदप्राप्तिः सारूप्यं, वैकुण्ठप्राप्तिः सायुज्यम् इति ।
> तच्च नित्यानन्दम् अमृतास्वादवत् (or अमृतरसपानवत्) सर्वदा तृप्तिकरं परमात्मनो नित्यनिषेवणं परञ्ज्योतिःप्रवेशनम् च । “तद्विष्णोः परमं पदं सदा पश्यन्ति सूरयः” इति श्रुतिः । भगवन्तं नान्यथा प्राप्नोतीति विज्ञायते ॥
> ***mōxasvarūpaṃ tadbhēdaśca***
> *saṃsārabandhana-vāsanā-vimuktiḥ mōxaḥ | tadapi samārādhana-viśēṣa-caturvidha-padāvāptiḥ -- sālokyaṃ, sāmīpyaṃ, sārūpyaṃ, sāyujyam iti |*
> *āmōdaprāptiḥ sālokyaṃ, pramōdaprāptiḥ sāmīpyaṃ, sammōdaprāptiḥ sārūpyaṃ, vaikuṇṭhaprāptiḥ sāyujyam iti |*
> *tacca nityānandam amṛtāsvādavat (or amṛtarasapānavat) sarvadā tṛptikaraṃ paramātmanō nityaniṣēvaṇaṃ parañjyōtiḥpravēśanaṃ ca | “tadviṣṇōḥ paramaṃ padaṃ sadā paśyantisūrayaḥ” iti śrutiḥ | bhagavantaṃ nānyathā prāpnōtīti vijñāyatē ||*

#### English Translation
*   **Definition of Moksha:** Liberation is absolute freedom from the bondage and latent impressions of transmigration (*saṃsārabandhana-vāsanā-vimuktiḥ mōxaḥ*).

*   **The Four States of Liberation:** That liberation also consists of the attainment of four states depending on the distinction of one's worship (*tadapi samārādhana-viśēṣa-caturvidha-padāvāptiḥ*):
    1.  **Sālokya:** Residing in the Lord's world, which is the attainment of the divine realm of Āmoda (*āmōdaprāptiḥ sālokyaṃ*).
    2.  **Sāmīpya:** Being in proximity to the Lord, which is the attainment of Pramoda (*pramōdaprāptiḥ sāmīpyaṃ*).
    3.  **Sārūpya:** Possessing the same form as the Lord, which is the attainment of Sammoda (*sammōdaprāptiḥ sārūpyaṃ*).
    4.  **Sāyujya:** Complete union/immersion into the Lord, which is the attainment of Vaikuṇṭha (*vaikuṇṭhaprāptiḥ sāyujyam*).

*   **The Nature of Eternal Bliss:** That state is eternal bliss (*tacca nityānandam*), like drinking the nectar of immortality (*amṛtarasapānavat*), ever-satisfying (*sarvadā tṛptikaraṃ*), and consists of the constant loving service of the Supreme Soul and merging into the Supreme Light (*paramātmanō nityaniṣēvaṇaṃ parañjyōtiḥpravēśanaṃ ca*).

#### Colophon
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> इति श्रीवैखानसे मरीचिप्रोक्ते विमानार्चनाकल्पे भगवत्सायुज्य-विधानं नाम चतुर्नवतितमः पटलः ॥९४॥

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> iti śrīvaikhānasē marīciprōktē vimānārcanākalpē bhagavatsāyujya-vidhānaṃ nāma caturnavatitamaḥ paṭalaḥ ||94||

**Translation (English):**
*   **The Vedic Conclusion:** The Vedic scripture declares: *"The wise always behold that supreme state of Viṣṇu"* (*“tadviṣṇōḥ paramaṃ padaṃ sadā paśyantisūrayaḥ”*). It is understood that one cannot attain the Lord by any other means (*bhagavantaṃ nānyathā prāpnōtīti vijñāyatē*).

### Glossary of Key Terms
*   **Māyā-pāśa (मायापाश):** The binding noose of cosmic illusion (*māyā*), which keeps the soul bound to the cycle of births and deaths.
*   **Nārāyaṇōpāsana (नारायणोपासना):** The contemplative worship and adoration of Nārāyaṇa, the supreme personal deity.
*   **Samyag-jñāna (सम्यग्ज्ञान):** Complete, right knowledge of reality, which arises by the grace of God and leads to liberation.
*   **Mōxa (मोक्ष):** Absolute liberation or release from transmigration (*saṃsāra*).
*   **Sālokya (सालोक्य):** The state of liberation where the soul resides in the same spiritual planet/realm as the Lord (associated with *Āmoda*).
*   **Sāmīpya (सामीप्य):** The state of liberation where the soul enjoys close proximity to the Lord (associated with *Pramoda*).
*   **Sārūpya (सारूप्य):** The state of liberation where the soul receives a spiritual form identical in appearance to the Lord (associated with *Sammoda*).
*   **Sāyujya (सायुज्य):** The highest state of liberation, representing complete union or absorption into the Lord (associated with *Vaikuṇṭha*).
*   **Nitya-niṣēvaṇa (नित्यनिषेवण):** Eternal, unceasing loving service (*kaiṅkarya*) rendered to the Lord in the liberated state.
*   **Parañ-jyōtiḥ (परञ्ज्योतिः):** The Supreme Light, the effulgence of Brahman into which the liberated soul merges.
