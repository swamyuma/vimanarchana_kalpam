# Chapter 65: Expiatory Rites for Consecration Errors (Pratiṣṭhā-prāyaścitta-vidhiḥ)
### Section 1: Liturgical Rules and Expiatory Rites for Mistakes, Deficiencies, and Reversals in Consecration Ceremonies

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **अथ पञ्चषष्टितमः पटलः**
> **(प्रतिष्ठा-प्रायश्चित्त-विधिः)**

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **atha pañcaṣaṣṭitamaḥ paṭalaḥ**
> **(pratiṣṭhā-prāyaścitta-vidhiḥ)**

**Translation (English):**
*   **Errors in Sprouting Ceremony:** If during the sprouting ceremony (*aṅkurārpaṇa*), the worship of deities like Brahmā and Soma or the presentation of food offerings (*havis*) is omitted, offer oblations to those specific deities and the *Vaiṣṇava* oblation, and then perform the worship and present the food offering again.

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> अथ प्रतिष्ठा-प्रायश्चित्तं वक्ष्ये — अङ्कुरार्पणकाले ब्रह्म-सोमादि-देवानाम् अर्चने, हविर्निवेदने च हीने, तत्-तद्-दैवतं वैष्णवं च हुत्वा पुनरभ्यर्च्य हविर्निवेदयेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> atha pratiṣṭhā-prāyaścittaṃ vakṣye — aṅkurārpaṇakāle brahma-somādi-devānām arcane, havirnivedane ca hīne, tat-tad-daivataṃ vaiṣṇavaṃ ca hutvā punarabhyarcya havirnivedayet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Complete Omission of Sprouting:** If the sprouting ceremony is omitted entirely, offer the *Vaiṣṇava*, *Viṣṇu-sūkta*, *Puruṣa-sūkta*, *Brāhma*, and *Saumya* oblations along with the *vyāhṛtis*, and perform the sprouting ceremony again.

#### Verse / Paragraph 3
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> अङ्कुरार्पणहीने वैष्णवं, विष्णुसूक्तं, पुरुषसूक्तं, ब्राह्मं, सौम्यं, व्याहतीश्च हुत्वा पुनरपि अङ्कुरार्पणं कुर्यात्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> aṅkurārpaṇahīne vaiṣṇavaṃ, viṣṇusūktaṃ, puruṣasūktaṃ, brāhmaṃ, soumyaṃ, vyāhṛtīśca hutvā punarapi aṅkurārpaṇaṃ kuryāt.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Consequences of Halting Rituals:** If after performing the sprouting ceremony, one does not execute the subsequent rites, it leads to the destruction of the king and the kingdom; therefore, execute them with great care.

#### Verse / Paragraph 4
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> अङ्कुरार्पणं कृत्वा पश्चात्कर्म नाचरेत् चेत्, राज-राष्ट्र-नाशाय भवति। तस्माद् यत्नतः कुर्यात्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> aṅkurārpaṇaṃ kṛtvā paścātkarma nācaret cet, rāja-rāṣṭra-vināśāya bhavati. tasmād yatnataḥ kuryāt.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Atonement for Halting Rituals:** To pacify that defect, perform the great peace ceremony (*mahā-śānti*), bow to the Lord saying "Forgive me", and perform the rites again, beginning with the sprouting ceremony.

#### Verse / Paragraph 5
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> तद्-दोषशमनाय महाशान्तिं हुत्वा 'क्षमस्व' इति देवं प्रणम्य पुनरपि अङ्कुरार्पणपूर्वं कर्म समाचरेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> tad-doṣaśamanāya mahāśāntiṃ hutvā 'kṣamasva' iti devaṃ praṇamya punarapi aṅkurārpaṇapūrvaṃ karma samāceret.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Missing Gems for Pedestal:** Before the eye-opening ceremony (*akṣi-mocana*), during the pedestal-assembly, if gems are unavailable, offer the *Vaiṣṇava*, *Viṣṇu-sūkta*, and *Puruṣa-sūkta* oblations, and deposit gold in their respective places.

#### Verse / Paragraph 6
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> अक्षिमोचनात् पूर्वं पीठसंघातकाले रत्नानाम् अलाभे वैष्णवं, विष्णुसूक्तं, पुरुषसूक्तं च हुत्वा तत्-तत्-स्थाने सुवर्णं निक्षिपेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> axi-mocanāt pūrvaṃ pīṭhasaṅghātakāle ratnānām alābhe vaiṣṇavaṃ, viṣṇusūktaṃ, puruṣasūktaṃ ca hutvā tat-tat-sthāne suvarṇaṃ nixipet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Missing Auspicious Sight Objects:** At the time of the eye-opening, if auspicious sight-objects such as cows are unavailable, offer the *Go-dāna-sūkta*, and the oblations to the presiding deities of those objects and the *Vaiṣṇava* oblation, and then present those objects according to the rules.

#### Verse / Paragraph 7
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> अक्षिमोचनकाले गवादि-दर्शनद्रव्यहीने गोदानसूक्तं तत्-तत्-द्रव्याधिदैवतं वैष्णवं च हुत्वा पश्चात् तद् द्रव्यं विधिवद् दर्शयेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> axi-mocanakāle gavādi-darśanadravyahīne godānasūktaṃ tat-tat-dravyādhidaivataṃ vaiṣṇavaṃ ca hutvā paścāt tad dravyaṃ vidhivat darśayet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Incorrect Timing for Eye-Opening:** If the eye-opening ceremony is omitted, or if it is performed during the rising of the Sun, Rahu (eclipses), or Saturn, or under blind or single-eyed asterisms (*nakṣatras*), it causes total ruin; offer the *Vaiṣṇava*, *Viṣṇu-sūkta*, and *Navagraha* (nine planets) oblations, and perform the eye-opening ceremony again.

#### Verse / Paragraph 8
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> अक्षिमोचने हीने, अर्क-राहु-सौराणाम् उदये, अन्धे च एकनेत्रे नक्षत्रे अक्षिमोचने कृते च सर्वनाशाय भवति। वैष्णवं, विष्णुसूक्तं, नवग्रहदैवतं च हुत्वा पुनरक्षिमोचनं कारयेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> axi-mocane hīne, arka-rāhu-sourāṇām udaye, andhe ca ekaneatre naxatre axi-mocane kṛte ca sarvanāśāya bhavati. vaiṣṇavaṃ, viṣṇusūktaṃ, navagrahadaivataṃ ca hutvā punaraxi-mocanaṃ kārayet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Errors in Reclining Rites:** If the reclining/immersion rites (*adhivāsana*) in *pañcagavya*, milk, or water are omitted, offer the *Vaiṣṇava*, *Ārṣa*, and *Viṣṇu-sūkta* oblations, and perform the water-reclining (*jalādhivāsa*) for three or eight days.

#### Verse / Paragraph 9
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> पञ्चगव्य-क्षीर-उदकेषु अधिवासने हीने वैष्णवम्, आर्षं, विष्णुसूक्तं च हुत्वा जलाधिवासं त्र्यहम् अष्टाहं वा कारयेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> pañcagavya-kṣīra-udakeṣu adhivāsane hīne vaiṣṇavam, ārṣaṃ, viṣṇusūktaṃ ca hutvā jalādhivāsaṃ tryaham aṣṭāhaṃ vā kārayet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Defects in the Sacrifice Hall:** If the sacrificial hall (*yajña-śālā*) or the main altar (*mahā-vedī*) is deficient or excessive in dimensions, constructed in an incorrect place, or lacks decorations, offer oblations to the presiding deities of those directions, and the *Vaiṣṇava*, *Viṣṇu-sūkta*, and *Śrī-Bhūmi-daivata* oblations.

#### Verse / Paragraph 10
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> यज्ञशालायां महावेद्यां च प्रमाणहीनायाम् अधिकायां च, अन्यदेशे कृतायां, अलङ्कारहीनायां च तत्-तत्-देशाधिपदैवत्ं, वैष्णवं, विष्णुसूक्तं, श्रीभूमिदैवत्यं च जुहुयात्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> yajñaśālāyāṃ mahāvedyāṃ ca pramāṇahīnāyām adhikāyāṃ ca, anyadeaśe kṛtāyāṃ, alaṅkārahīnāyāṃ ca tat-tat-deasādhipadaivataṃ, vaiṣṇavaṃ, viṣṇusūktaṃ, śrībhūmidaivatyāṃ ca juhuyāt.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Errors in Image Purification:** After the eye-opening of the stationary deity (*dhruva-bera*), if the icon-purification rites (*bera-śuddhi*) are omitted or performed contrarily, offer the *Vaiṣṇava*, *Viṣṇu-sūkta*, *Puruṣa-sūkta*, *Raudra*, *Ārṣa*, and *Vāruṇa* oblations, and perform the purification again.

#### Verse / Paragraph 11
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> ध्रुवबेरस्य अक्षिमोचनान्ते बेरशुद्धिक्रियायां हीनायां विपरीतायां वा वैष्णवं, विष्णुसूक्तं, पुरुषसूक्तं, रौद्रं, आर्षं, वारुणं च हुत्वा पुनराचरेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> dhruvaberasya axi-mocanānte beraśuddhikriyāyāṃ hīnāyāṃ viparītāyāṃ vā vaiṣṇavaṃ, viṣṇusūktaṃ, puruṣasūktaṃ, raudraṃ, ārṣaṃ, vāruṇaṃ ca hutvā punarācaret.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Defective Fire-Pits:** If the fire-pits (*agni-kuṇḍas*) are made with deficient or excessive dimensions, offer the *Brāhma*, *Saumya*, and *Āgneya* oblations.

#### Verse / Paragraph 12
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> अग्निकुण्डानि हीनाधिकप्रमाणेन कृतानि चेत्, ब्राह्मं, सौम्यं, आग्नेयं च जुहुयात्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> agnikuṇḍāni hīnādhikapramāṇeana kṛtāni cet, brāhmaṃ, soumyaṃ, āgneyaṃ ca juhuyāt.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Defects in Ritual Implements:** If the grass-borders (*paristaraṇa*), grass-brushes (*kūrca*), water-vessels (*praniḍhi*), ghee-pots, ladles (*sruk*, *sruva*), and other implements are deficient or excessive in dimensions, offer the *mindāhuti*, *Sāvitrī* oblation, and the *vyāhṛtis*.

#### Verse / Paragraph 13
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> परिस्तरण-कूर्च-प्रणिधि-आज्यपात्र-स्रुवादिषु प्रमाणहीनेषु अधिकेषु च, मिन्दाहुतिं, सावित्रीं च व्याहृत्यन्तं जुहुयात्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> paristaraṇa-kūrca-praṇidhi-ājyapātra-sruvādiṣu pramāṇahīneṣu adhikeṣu ca, mindāhutiṃ, sāvitrīṃ ca vyāhṛtyantaṃ juhuyāt.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Kindling Sacrificial Fires:** Having arranged the five fires in their prescribed places, after sprinkling and marking the ground, if churned fire (*mathita-agni*) is unavailable, bring fire from the house of the priest (*ācārya*) or a learned Brahmin (*śrotriya*), place it in the *Gārhapatya* pit, offer the introductory *āghāra* oblation, and then offer the *Vaiṣṇava*, *Bhūmi-daivata*, and *Āgneya* oblations.

#### Verse / Paragraph 14
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> यथोक्ते स्थाने पञ्चाग्नीन् परिकल्प्य, प्रोक्षणोल्लेखने कृते, मथिताग्नेरलाभे तु आचार्यागारतः श्रोत्रियागारतो वा अग्निम् आदर्तव्यं (अग्निम् आहृत्य) गार्हपत्याग्निकुण्डे निधाय, आघारं हुत्वा, वैष्णवं, भूमिदैवत्यं, आग्नेयं च जुहुयात्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> yathokte sthāne pañcāgnīn parikalpya, proxaṇollekhaṇe kṛte, mathitāgneralābhe tu agnim āhṛtya gārhapatyāgnikuṇḍe nidhāya, āghāraṃ hutvā, vaiṣṇavaṃ, bhūmidaivatyāṃ, āgneyaṃ ca juhuyāt.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Consecration of Five Fires:** Increase that fire and lead it to the *Anvāhārya* pit, then place it in the *Āhavanīya* pit, then lead it to the *Āvasathya* pit, and then to the *Sabhya* pit; thus perform the leading of the five fires. If this is done in reverse order or without the respective mantras, offer the *Vaiṣṇava*, *Āgneya*, *Brāhma*, and *Saumya* oblations, and lead the fires again. Do not cross the space between the fires before the *āghāra* oblation; if crossed out of ignorance, offer oblations to the presiding deities of the directions, and the *Vaiṣṇava* and *Āgneya* oblations.

#### Verse / Paragraph 15
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> तम् अग्निं वर्धयित्वा, अन्वाहार्ये प्रणीय, पश्चात् आहवनीये प्रणिधाय, पश्चात् आवसथ्ये प्रणीय, ततः सभ्ये प्रणीय, ततः पञ्चाग्नौ प्रणयनं कुर्यात्। विपरीते तत्-तद्-मन्त्रहीने च वैष्णवं, आग्नेयं, ब्राह्मं, सौम्यं च हुत्वा अग्निं प्रणयेत्। पश्चात् तत्-तद्-अग्निषु आधारात् पूर्वतः अन्तरा नैव गच्छेत्। अज्ञानाद् यदि गच्छेत्, तद्-दिकपालदैवत्यं वैष्णवम् आग्नेयं च जुहुयात्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> tam agniṃ vardhayitvā, anvāhārye praṇīya, paścāt āhavanīye praṇidhāya, paścāt āvasathye praṇīya, tataḥ sabhye praṇīya, tataḥ pañcāgnou praṇayanaṃ kuryāt. viparīte tat-tad-mantrahīne ca vaiṣṇavaṃ, āgneyaṃ, brāhmaṃ, soumyaṃ ca hutvā agniṃ praṇayet. paścāt tat-tad-agniṣu ādhārāt pūrvataḥ antarā naiva gacchet. ajñānād yadi gacchet, tad-dikpāladaivatyāṃ vaiṣṇavam āgneyaṃ ca juhuyāt.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Preparation of Oblation Rice:** Having cooked the sacrificial rice (*caru*) from one variety of rice in two *prasthas* (measures), and cows' butter (*gavya-navanīta*) in a common fire (*laukika-agni*), consecrate them with mantras in the respective sacred fires.

#### Verse / Paragraph 16
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> तण्डुलैः एकजातीयैः द्विप्रस्थैः चरुं, गव्यं नवनीतं च लौकिकाग्नौ पाचयित्वा, पुनः तत्-तद्-अग्निषु समन्त्रकं संस्कुर्यात्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> taṇḍulaiḥ ekajātīyaiah dviprasthaiah caruṃ, gavyaṃ navanītaṃ ca loukikāgnou pācayitvā, punaḥ tat-tad-agniṣu samantrakaṃ saṃskuryāt.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Impure Sacrificial Offerings:** If insects, worms, hair, or ants fall into the ghee-vessel or the sacrificial rice, remove them, discard the defiled portion, take fresh ingredients, and offer the *Prājāpatya*, *Āgneya*, and *Vaiṣṇava* oblations. If ghee is unavailable, remove the defect, purify the remaining portion with darbha grass, heat it up, and use it.

#### Verse / Paragraph 17
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> आज्यस्थाल्यां चरौ वा मक्षिका-कृमि-रोम-पिपीलिकादिषु पतितेषु तद् व्यपोह्य, अन्यद् द्रव्यम् आदाय, प्राजापत्यम्, आग्नेयं, वैष्णवं च जुहुयात्। आज्यालाभे तद् दोषं व्यपोह्य, दर्भैः उत्पूय, उद्दीप्य आहरेत्। आघारे कृते तस्मिन् अग्नौ गते 'अयं ते योनिः' इति मन्त्रेण तद्-भस्मनि समिधम् आरोप्य, 'उद्बुध्यस्व' इति मन्त्रेण लौकिकाग्नौ निधाय इन्धनानि विक्षिप्य, उज्ज्वाल्य, परिषिच्य, विच्छिन्नं, मिन्दाहुतीः, वैष्णवं, व्याहतीश्च यजेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> ājyasthālyāṃ carou vā maxikā-kṛmi-roma-pipīlikādiṣu patiteṣu tad vyapohya, anyad dravyām ādāya, prājāpatyam, āgneyaṃ, vaiṣṇavaṃ ca juhuyāt. ājyālābhe tad doṣaṃ vyapohya, darbhiah utpūya, uddīpya āharet. āghāre kṛte tasmin agnou gate 'ayaṃ te yoniḥ' iti mantreaṇa tad-bhasmani samidham āropya, 'udbudhyasva' iti mantreaṇa loukikāgnou nidhāya indhanāni vixipya, ujjvālya, pariṣicya, vicchinnaṃ, mindāhutīḥ, vaiṣṇavaṃ, vyāhṛtīśca yajet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Extinguished Sacrificial Fire:** If after the *āghāra* oblation is made, the fire dies out, place a fuel-stick (*samidh*) on the ashes reciting "Ayaṃ te yoniḥ", transfer it to a common fire reciting "Udbudhyasva", add fuel-wood, kindle it, sprinkle water around, and offer the *vicchinna*, *mindāhuti*, *Vaiṣṇava*, and *vyāhṛti* oblations.

#### Verse / Paragraph 18
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> परिस्तरणादि-सम्भाराणां दाहे, भेदे, छेदे, नाशे च पुनः तत्-तत् संयोज्य, महाव्याहृतीः, आग्नेयं, मिन्दाहुतीः, वैष्णवं च जुहुयात्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> paristaraṇādi-sambhārāṇāṃ dāhe, bhede, chede, nāṣe ca punaḥ tat-tat saṃyojya, mahāvyāhṛtīḥ, āgneyaṃ, mindāhutīḥ, vaiṣṇavaṃ ca juhuyāt.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Damage to Ritual Utensils:** If the grass-borders (*paristaraṇa*) and other implements are burned, broken, cut, or lost, replace them, and offer the *mahā-vyāhṛtis*, *Āgneya*, *mindāhuti*, and *Vaiṣṇava* oblations.

#### Verse / Paragraph 19
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> विशेषे अनुक्ते स्रुवेण होतव्यम्। हविर्विशेषे अनुक्ते च आज्येन, आज्यालाभे साज्येन चरुणा होमम् आचरेत्। उक्तद्रव्यस्य हीनं चेत्, कापिलेन घृतेन वैष्णवं विंशातिकृत्वो यजेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> viṣeṣe anukte sruveana hotavyam. havirviṣeṣe anukte ca ājyeana, ājyālābhe sājyeana caruṇā homam ācaret. uktadravyasya hīnaṃ cet, kāpilena ghṛteana vaiṣṇavaṃ viṃśatikṛtvo yajet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Unspecified Ritual Details:** If not specified, offer oblations with the *sruva* ladle. If the sacrificial offering (*havis*) is not specified, use ghee; if ghee is unavailable, use sacrificial rice (*caru*) mixed with ghee. If the specified materials are deficient, offer the *Vaiṣṇava* oblation twenty times using cow's ghee.

> sarveṣāṃ homānām anukte viparīte hīne ca pañcāgnou vaiṣṇavaṃ, viṣṇusūktaṃ, puruṣasūktaṃ ca hutvā yathoktaṃ ca juhuyāt.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Unspecified Sacrificial Failures:** For all other sacrifices, if an unspecified reversal or omission occurs, offer the *Vaiṣṇava*, *Viṣṇu-sūkta*, and *Puruṣa-sūkta* in the five fires, and then perform the rites as specified.

#### Verse / Paragraph 42
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> शयानं देवम् उद्धरेत् चेत्, श्रीभूमिदैवत्यं चतुष्कृत्वो हुत्वा तथैव शाययेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> śayānaṃ deavam uddharet cet, śrībhūmidaivatyāṃ catuṣkṛtvo hutvā tathāiva śāyayes.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Lifting the Reclining Deity:** If one must lift the reclining deity (*śayāna-deva*), offer the *Śrī-Bhūmi-daivata* oblation four times, and then place Him back in the same position.

#### Verse / Paragraph 43
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> अध्ययने हीने सारस्वतम् अष्टाधिकशतं जुहुयात्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> adhyayane hīne sārasvatam aṣṭādhikaśataṃ juhuyāt.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Omission of Chanting:** If the chanting of scripture is omitted, offer the *Sārasvata* oblation one hundred and eight times.

#### Verse / Paragraph 44
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> अस्य यज्ञस्य षोडश-ऋत्विजाम् अभावे यथालाभं सङ्गृह्य कुण्डाणां च लक्षणम्, आख्यां, आघारविशेषं तत्-तत्-लोकाधिदैवतं, जातवेदस्वरूपं, ब्रह्मासनस्य लक्षणं, पात्राणां लक्षणं, होमद्रव्यम्, ऋषि-च्छन्दो-अधिदैवतं, आघारप्रयोगं च सर्वं विधिना कारयेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> asya yajñasya ṣoḍaśa-ṛtvijām alābhe yathālābhaṃ saṅgṛhya kuṇḍāṇāṃ ca laxaṇam, ākhyāṃ, āghāraviṣeṣaṃ tat-tat-lokādhipadaivataṃ, jātavedasvarūpaṃ, brahmāsanasya laxaṇaṃ, pātrāṇāṃ laxaṇaṃ, homadravyam, ṛṣi-cchando-adhidaivataṃ, āghāraproyogaṃ ca sarvaṃ vidhinā kārayet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Absence of Sixteen Priests:** In this sacrifice, if sixteen priests (*ṛtvijs*) are unavailable, take whatever number is available, and perform the rites according to the rules, observing the characteristics, names, and special *āghāra* oblations of the fire-pits, the presiding deities of the directions, the form of *Jātavedas*, the seating of Brahmā (*brahmāsana*), the characteristics of the vessels, the offering-materials, the sages, meters, deities, and the application of the *āghāra* oblation.

#### Verse / Paragraph 45
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> पञ्चप्रतिष्ठायां त्रिविधोत्सवे च पुरश्चरणं कृत्वा, दीक्षितस्य आचार्यस्य अवभृथात् पूर्वम् आशौचं न स्यात्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> pañcapratiṣṭhāyāṃ trividhotsave ca puraṣcaraṇaṃ kṛtvā, dīṣitasya ācāryasya avabhṛthāt pūrvaṃ āśoucaṃ na syāt.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Preparatory Rituals and Pollution:** In the five kinds of installations (*pañca-pratiṣṭhā*) and the three kinds of festivals, perform the preparatory repetition of mantras (*puras-caraṇa*); for the initiated priest (*dīkṣita-ācārya*), no ritual pollution (*āśauca*) arises before the final bath (*avabhṛtha*).

#### Verse / Paragraph 46
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> आचार्य-स्थापकादीनां भर्त्सनं हननं शपनं वा कृत्वा, वैष्णवं, विष्णुसूक्तं, पुरुषसूक्तं, ब्राह्मं, सारस्वतं च हुत्वा तान् अनुज्ञाप्य कार्यम् आरभेत।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> ācārya-sthāpakādīnāṃ bhartsanaṃ hananaṃ śapanaṃ vā kṛtvā, vaiṣṇavaṃ, viṣṇusūktaṃ, puruṣasūktaṃ, brāhmaṃ, sārasvataṃ ca hutvā tān anujñāpya kāryam ārabhet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Insulting the Priests:** If one insults, strikes, or curses the priest (*ācārya*) or the assistants, offer the *Vaiṣṇava*, *Viṣṇu-sūkta*, *Puruṣa-sūkta*, *Brāhma*, and *Sārasvata* oblations, reconcile with them and obtain their permission (*anujñāpya*), and then begin the work.

#### Verse / Paragraph 47
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> आचार्य-दक्षिणा-कालातीते वैष्णवं, मुनिमन्त्रं, अष्टोत्तरशतं हुत्वा यथोक्तदक्षिणां दद्यात्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> ācārya-daxiṇā-kālātīte vaiṣṇavaṃ, munimantraṃ, aṣṭottaraśataṃ hutvā yathoktadaxiṇāṃ dadyāt.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Delay in Priestly Fees:** If the time for giving donations (*dakṣiṇā*) to the priest passes, offer the *Vaiṣṇava* and *Muni-mantra* oblations one hundred and eight times, and then give the specified donations.

#### Verse / Paragraph 48
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> अल्पदक्षिणा-यागो यजमानं हन्ति, सदक्षिणो यज्ञः फलम् अनन्तं ददाति। तस्माद् यज्ञस्य दक्षिणा जीवो भवेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> alpadaxiṇā-yāgo yajamānaṃ hanti, sadaxiṇo yajñaḥ phalam anantaṃ dadāti. tasmād yajñasya daxiṇā jīvo bhavet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Importance of Priestly Fees:** A sacrifice with insufficient donations (*alpa-dakṣiṇā*) ruins the patron, while a sacrifice with proper donations yields endless fruits; therefore, donation (*dakṣiṇā*) is the very life-force of a sacrifice.

#### Verse / Paragraph 49
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> कौतुकादीनाम् अलाभे सुवर्णं निक्षिप्य वैष्णवं, विष्णुसूक्तं, पुरुषसूक्तं, वायव्यं, दिग्दैवतं च जुहुयात्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> kautukādīnām alābhe suvarṇaṃ nixipya vaiṣṇavaṃ, viṣṇusūktaṃ, puruṣasūktaṃ, vāyavyaṃ, digdaivatyāṃ ca juhuyāt.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Missing Materials for Processional Icons:** If processional icons are unavailable, place gold, and offer the *Vaiṣṇava*, *Viṣṇu-sūkta*, *Puruṣa-sūkta*, *Vāyavya*, and *Digdevatā* oblations.

#### Verse / Paragraph 50
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> सभ्याग्नौ शेषहोमहीने वैष्णवम्, आग्नेयं, व्याहतीश्च दशकृत्वो जुहुयात्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> sabhyāgnou ṣeṣahomahīne vaiṣṇavam, āgneyaṃ, vyāhṛtīśca daśakṛtvo juhuyāt.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Omissions in Concluding Fire Rites:** If the concluding oblations (*śeṣa-homa*) in the *Sabhya* fire are omitted, offer the *Vaiṣṇava*, *Āgneya*, and the *vyāhṛtis* ten times.

#### Verse / Paragraph 51
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> नित्याग्निकुण्डे सभ्याग्निस्थापने हीने पूर्ववद् प्रायश्चित्तं हुत्वा, श्रोत्रियागारतः अग्निम् आहृत्य, निधाय आघारं हुत्वा नित्यहोमं समाचरेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> nityāgnikuṇḍe sabhyāgnisthāpane hīne pūrvavad prāyaścittaṃ hutvā, śrotriyāgārataḥ agnim āhṛtya, nidhāya āghāraṃ hutvā nityahomaṃ samācaret.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Delay in Daily Fire Setup:** If there is a delay in setting up the daily fire-pit (*nityāgni-kuṇḍa*) or the *Sabhya* fire, perform the expiations as before, bring fire from the house of a learned Brahmin, make the *āghāra* oblation, and perform the daily worship.

#### Verse / Paragraph 52
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> ध्रुवबेरात् कौतुकादिषु दीपाद् दीपम् इव आवाहयेत्, अज्ञानात् तत्-कुम्भ-जलेन तेषाम् आवाहने कृते महत्तरो दोषो भवति। ततः शीघ्रं शुद्धोदकेन 'इषे त्वोर्जे त्वा' इति मन्त्रेण स्नापयित्वा, देवम् अनुमान्य, वैष्णवं, विष्णुसूक्तं, पुरुषसूक्तं च हुत्वा पूर्ववद् ध्रुवबेराद् आवाहयेत् ॥

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> dhruvaberāt kautukādiṣu dīpād dīpam iva āvāhayet, ajñānāt tat-kumbha-jaleana teṣām āvāhane kṛte mahattaro doṣo bhavati. tataḥ śīghraṃ śuddhoadakeana 'iṣe tvoūrje tvā' iti mantreaṇa snāpayitvā, deavam anumānya, vaiṣṇavaṃ, viṣṇusūktaṃ, puruṣasūktaṃ ca hutvā pūrvavad dhruvaberād āvāhayet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Invocation from Main Icon:** One must invoke the deity from the stationary deity (*dhruva-bera*) to the processional icons (*kautuka-bera* etc.) just as one lights a lamp from another lamp; if due to ignorance they are invoked using the water of the consecration pot, a severe fault arises. If so, quickly bathe them with pure water reciting "Iṣe tvorje tvā", beg the forgiveness of the Lord, offer the *Vaiṣṇava*, *Viṣṇu-sūkta*, and *Puruṣa-sūkta* oblations, and invoke Him from the stationary deity as before.
