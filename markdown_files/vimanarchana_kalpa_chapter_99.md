# Vimanarchana Kalpa of Sage Marīci (Vaikhānasa Āgama)
## Chapter 99: Dhyāna-bhēda-vidhiḥ (Rules on the Classifications of Meditation)

This document contains the Sanskrit text, IAST transliteration, and English translation of the 99th Chapter (*Ēkōnaśatatamaḥ Paṭalaḥ*) of the **Vimānārcanākalpa** (attributed to Sage Marīci, representing the Vaikhānasa school of Vaishnavism).

This chapter expounds *dhyāna* (meditation), the seventh limb of the eightfold yoga. It defines *dhyāna* as the contemplation of the Supreme Soul (*paramātman*) by the individual soul (*jīvātman*), and divides it into two categories: unmanifest (*niṣkala*) and manifest (*sakala*). The unmanifest is further identified with attribute-free (*nirguṇa*) meditation, which contemplates the all-pervading, partless light of Nārāyaṇa residing in the heart-cave of all beings. The manifest (*sakala*) is identified with *saguṇa* (with attributes) meditation, which is divided into four techniques based on visualizing the elements (Earth/Mahendra, Water/Varuṇa) and cosmic disks (Fire/Sun/Moon) in the heart, where the deity is worshipped with attributes, ornaments, consorts, and retinue.

### Section 1: Definition of Meditation and Nirguna (Nishkala) Dhyana

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **अथ एकोनशततमः पटलः**
> **ध्यानभेदविद्यिः**
> ***atha ēkōnaśatatamaḥ paṭalaḥ***
> ***dhyānabhēdavidhiḥ***
> 
> अथातो ध्यानं वक्ष्ये -- परमात्मनो जीवात्मना चिन्तनं ध्यानं, निष्कलं सकलं चेति तत् द्विविधम् ।
> निष्कलं देवैरपि अनभिलक्ष्यं रूपं (स्वभावः) स्यात् । सकलं द्विविधं -- निर्गुणं सगुणं चेति । निर्गुणं निष्कलस्वभावः ।
> *athātō dhyānaṃ vaxyē -- paramātmanō jīvātmanā cintanaṃ dhyānaṃ, niṣkalaṃ sakalaṃ cēti tat dvividham |*
> *niṣkalaṃ dēvairapi anabhilaxyaṃ rūpaṃ (svabhāvaḥ) syāt | sakalaṃ dvividhaṃ -- nirguṇaṃ saguṇaṃ cēti | nirguṇaṃ niṣkalasvabhāvaḥ ||*
> 
> **निर्गुणध्यानम्**
> “परमात्मनोऽन्यत् न किञ्चिदस्तीति,” काष्ठे अग्निरिव सर्वव्यापी, आकाशोपमः, सर्वेषाम् आत्मगुहायां निहितः, अन्तर्बहिश्च संस्थितः, व्यक्ताव्यक्त-स्थूल-सूक्ष्मः, अमलोऽत्यच्छः अप्रमेयो निरवयवो निरुद्योगो नित्योऽचिन्त्यो निष्कलः, तमेव प्राणायाम-प्रत्याहार-धारणादि-आत्मसंस्कारं कृत्वा आत्मना पश्येत् ।
> हृत्कमले (हृन्मध्ये) व्यक्ताव्यक्तं नारायणं सर्वजगत्कारणम् अव्ययम् अव्यक्तम् एकरूपं परञ्ज्योतिः ज्वलति अवभासयति च । एवं निर्गुणध्यानम् । “नारायणपरं ज्योतिरात्मा नारायणः परः” इति श्रुतिः । तस्मात् प्रधान-परमोऽव्ययो विष्णुः सदा ध्येयः ।
> निष्कलमेव प्राणायाम-विकसित-हृदयकमलान्तराकाशे वैश्वानरशिखामध्ये परञ्ज्योतिर्ज्वारूपवत् स्वयमेव पुरुषः कृष्णपिङ्गलः ऊर्ध्वरेता विरूपाक्षः विश्वरूपः परमानन्दविग्रहो भवेत् । तं परमया भक्त्या पश्येत् । तत्र सन्निहितो भवेत् । इति निर्गुणध्यानं भवतीति विज्ञायते ॥
> ***nirguṇadhyānam***
> *“paramātmanō'nyat na kiñcidastīti,” kāṣṭhē agniriva sarvavyāpī, ākāśōpamaḥ, sarvēṣām ātmaguhāyāṃ nihitaḥ, antarbahiśca saṃsthitaḥ, vyaktāvyakta-sthūla-sūxmaḥ, amalō'tyacchaḥ apramēyō niravayavō nirudyōgō nityō'cintyō niṣkalaḥ, tamēva prāṇāyāma-pratyāhāra-dhāraṇādi-ātmasaṃskāraṃ kṛtvā ātmanā paśyēt |*
> *hṛtkamalē (hṛdmadhyē) vyaktāvyaktaṃ nārāyaṇaṃ sarvajagatkāraṇam avyayam avyaktam ēkarūpaṃ parañjyōtiḥ jvalati avabhāsayati ca | ēvaṃ nirguṇadhyānam | “nārāyaṇaparaṃ jyōtirātmā nārāyaṇaḥ paraḥ” iti śrutiḥ | tasmāt pradhāna-paramō'vyayō viṣṇuḥ sadā dhyēyaḥ |*
> *niṣkalamēva prāṇāyāma-vikasita-hṛdayakamalāntarākāśē vaiśvānaraśikhāmadhyē parañjyōtirjvalarūpavat svayamēva puruṣaḥ kṛṣṇapiṅgalaḥ ūrdhvarētā virūpāxaḥ viśvarūpaḥ paramānandavigrahō bhavēt | taṃ paramayā bhaktyā paśyēt | tatra sannihitō bhavēt | iti nirguṇadhyānaṃ bhavatīti vijñāyatē ||*

#### English Translation
*   **Definition of Meditation:** Now we shall explain meditation (*athātō dhyānaṃ vaxyē*). The contemplation of the Supreme Soul by the individual soul is meditation (*paramātmanō jīvātmanā cintanaṃ dhyānaṃ*); it is of two kinds: unmanifest (*niṣkala*) and manifest (*sakala*) (*niṣkalaṃ sakalaṃ cēti tat dvividham*).

*   **Nirguna (Attribute-Free) Meditation:** The unmanifest is a nature/form unperceivable even by the gods (*niṣkalaṃ dēvairapi anabhilaxyaṃ rūpaṃ syāt*). The manifest is of two kinds: attribute-free (*nirguṇa*) and with attributes (*saguṇa*) (*sakalaṃ dvividhaṃ -- nirguṇaṃ saguṇaṃ cēti*). The attribute-free represents the unmanifest state (*nirguṇaṃ niṣkalasvabhāvaḥ*).

*   **Meditation on the Inner Light:** Contemplating "there is nothing other than the Supreme Soul" (*paramātmanō'nyat na kiñcidastīti*), who is all-pervading like fire in wood (*kāṣṭhē agniriva sarvavyāpī*), like space (*ākāśōpamaḥ*), residing inside the heart-cave of all beings (*sarvēṣām ātmaguhāyāṃ nihitaḥ*), seated inside and outside (*antarbahiśca saṃsthitaḥ*), manifest and unmanifest, gross and subtle (*vyaktāvyakta-sthūla-sūxmaḥ*), pure, unblemished, immeasurable, limb-less, action-less, eternal, unthinkable, and part-less (*amalō'tyacchaḥ apramēyō niravayavō nirudyōgō nityō'cintyō niṣkalaḥ*); one should perceive Him through the self after purifying oneself through breath control, sensory withdrawal, and concentration (*tamēva prāṇāyāma-pratyāhāra-dhāraṇādi-ātmasaṃskāraṃ kṛtvā ātmanā paśyēt*).

*   **The Supreme Spirit:** In the middle of the heart, the manifest-unmanifest Nārāyaṇa (*hṛdmadhyē vyaktāvyaktaṃ nārāyaṇaṃ*), the cause of the entire universe, the imperishable, the unmanifest, the single-formed, shines and illuminates as the Supreme Light (*sarvajagatkāraṇam avyayam avyaktam ēkarūpaṃ parañjyōtiḥ jvalati avabhāsayati ca*). This is attribute-free meditation (*ēvaṃ nirguṇadhyānam*).

*   **Upanishadic Authority:** The Vedic scripture declares: *"Nārāyaṇa is the Supreme Light, the Soul, Nārāyaṇa is the Supreme"* (*“nārāyaṇaparaṃ jyōtirātmā nārāyaṇaḥ paraḥ”*). Therefore, the primal, supreme, imperishable Viṣṇu should always be meditated upon (*tasmāt pradhāna-paramō'vyayō viṣṇuḥ sadā dhyēyaḥ*).

*   **Perceiving the Lord's form:** Within the space of the heart-lotus blossomed through pranayama (*prāṇāyāma-vikasita-hṛdayakamalāntarākāśē*), in the middle of the Vaishvanara fire-flame (*vaiśvānaraśikhāmadhyē*), like the flame of the Supreme Light, He manifests as the Person who is dark and golden (*kṛṣṇapiṅgalaḥ*), with upward-directed energy (*ūrdhvarētā*), three-eyed (*virūpāxaḥ*), all-formed (*viśvarūpaḥ*), and of the form of supreme bliss (*paramānandavigrahō bhavēt*). One should behold Him with supreme devotion (*taṃ paramayā bhaktyā paśyēt*). He becomes present there. This is known as attribute-free meditation (*iti nirguṇadhyānaṃ bhavatīti vijñāyatē*).

### Section 2: Saguna (With-Attributes) Dhyana and its Four Forms

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **अथ सगुणध्यानं वक्ष्ये --**
> प्राणम् अपानेन संयोज्य, वह्निमण्डलं प्रविश्य तत्र पुरुषं दृष्ट्वा, पुनः पिङ्गलया आदित्यमण्डलम नुप्रविश्य, तत्रस्थं पुरुषं दृष्ट्वा, ततश्च इडया चन्द्रमण्डलम नुप्रविश्य, तत्र मण्डलपुरुषं दृष्ट्वा, ततो ज्वलिताग्निना कुण्डलिनीमुखं प्रज्वाल्य (or ज्वलनेन दग्ध्वा), सुषुम्नया चोर्ध्वं गत्वा, प्राणायामेन विकसित-हृदयकमलान्तराकाशे वैश्वानरशिखामध्ये चतुरश्रं हेमाभं बिन्दुनाद-सहितं लकारबीजान्वितं (or यकारबीजान्वितं) महेन्द्रमण्डलं, तन्मध्ये अर्धचन्द्राकारं श्वेतं बिन्दुनाद-सहितं वकारबीजान्वितं वारुणं मण्डलं ध्यात्वा, तन्मध्ये प्रणववेष्टितं सुवर्णाभम् आदिबीजं संस्मृत्य, प्रज्वलित-ज्योतिःस्वरूपम् एव भक्त्या सकलं सङ्कल्प्य, देवी-भूषण-आयुधैः सह परिषद्गणैः सह कल्याणगुणनिधिं पूर्ववद् ध्यायेत् । एतत् सगुणध्यानम् उत्तमम्, सर्वसिद्धिप्रदं सर्वत्र प्रयोक्तव्यम् ।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **atha saguṇadhyānaṃ vaxyē --**
> prāṇam apānēna saṃyōjya, vahnimaṇḍalaṃ praviśya tatra puruṣaṃ dṛṣṭvā, punaḥ piṅgalayā ādityamaṇḍalam anupraviśya, tatrasthaṃ puruṣaṃ dṛṣṭvā, tataśca iḍayā candramaṇḍalam anupraviśya, tatra maṇḍalapuruṣaṃ dṛṣṭvā, tatō jvalitāgninā kuṇḍalinīmuxaṃ prajvālya (or jvalanēna dagdhvā), suṣumnayā cōrdhvaṃ gatvā, prāṇāyāmēna vikasita-hṛdayakamalāntarākāśē vaiśvānaraśikhāmadhyē caturaśraṃ hēmābhaṃ bindunāda-sahitaṃ lakārabījānvitaṃ (or yakārabījānvitaṃ) mahēndramaṇḍalaṃ, tanmadhyē ardhacandrākāraṃ śvētaṃ bindunāda-sahitaṃ vakārabījānvitaṃ vāruṇaṃ maṇḍalaṃ dhyātvā, tanmadhyē praṇavavēṣṭitaṃ suvarṇābham ādibījaṃ saṃsmṛtya, prajvālita-jyōtiḥsvarūpam ēva bhaktyā sakalaṃ saṅkalpya, dēvī-bhūṣaṇa-āyudhaiḥ saha pariṣadgaṇaiḥ saha kalyāṇaguṇanidhiṃ pūrvavad dhyāyēt | ētat saguṇadhyānam uttamam, sarvasiddhipradaṃ sarvatra prayōktavyam ||

**Translation (English):**
*   **Manifest (Saguna) Meditation:** Now we shall explain manifest meditation (*atha saguṇadhyānaṃ vaxyē*).
    *   **Yogic Ascent:** Uniting prana with apana, entering the fire-mandala to behold the Puruṣa there; then entering the solar-mandala through Pingala to behold the Puruṣa there; and then entering the lunar-mandala through Ida to behold the Person of that sphere; one kindles the fire-mouth of Kundalini, ascends through the *suṣumnā*, and in the space of the heart-lotus opened through pranayama, within the Vaishvanara flame, visualizes:
    *   **The Mandalas:** The golden, square Mahendra (Earth) mandala associated with the seed-syllable *Lam* and *bindu-nāda* (*caturaśraṃ hēmābhaṃ ... lakārabījānvitaṃ mahēndramaṇḍalaṃ*); and in its center, the white crescent-shaped Varuṇa (Water) mandala associated with the seed-syllable *Vam* (*ardhacandrākāraṃ śvētaṃ ... vakārabījānvitaṃ vāruṇaṃ maṇḍalaṃ*).
    *   **The Supreme Visualization:** In its center, remembering the golden primal seed-syllable (*ādibījaṃ*) encircled by *Om* (*praṇavavēṣṭitaṃ*), visualizing the Supreme Spirit as a blazing light, one should meditate on the Lord as a treasure-house of auspicious qualities, adorned with consorts, ornaments, weapons, and retinue (*dēvī-bhūṣaṇa-āyudhaiḥ saha pariṣadgaṇaiḥ saha kalyāṇaguṇanidhiṃ pūrvavad dhyāyēt*). This manifest meditation is the highest, granting all accomplishments (*ētat saguṇadhyānam uttamam, sarvasiddhipradaṃ*).

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> *   **yajñamūrtidhyānam:** hṛtpadmāntarākāśē vaiśvānaraśikhāmadhyē agnimaṇḍalaṃ (arkamaṇḍalaṃ) pūrvavad dhyātvā, tanmadhyē parañjyōtirēva sakalaṃ saṅkalpya, dēvī-bhūṣaṇa-āyudhaiḥ pariṣadgaṇaiḥ sahitaṃ yajñamūrtiṃ pūrvavad dhyāyēt | agnihōtrādi-hōmaḥ tadapi saguṇadhyānam |

> *   **nārāyaṇadhyānam (sōmamaṇḍalē):** hṛdayakamalāntarākāśē vaiśvānaraśikhāmadhyē sōmamaṇḍalaṃ pūrvavad dhyātvā, tanmadhyē parañjyōtirēva sakalaṃ saṅkalpya, śuddhasphaṭikasaṅkāśaṃ nārāyaṇamūrtiṃ dhyāyēt | ētad saguṇadhyānaṃ sarvasiddhipradaṃ sarvatra prayōktavyam ||

**Translation (English):**
*   **The Specific Manifest Meditations:**
    1.  **Yajñamūrti (Fire/Agnimaṇḍala):** Meditating in the heart-space within the fire-flame on the fire-disc (*agnimaṇḍalaṃ*), envisioning the Supreme Light in a manifest form accompanied by consorts, ornaments, weapons, and retinue, one should meditate on Yajñamūrti (*yajñamūrtiṃ pūrvavad dhyāyēt*).     2.  **Viṣṇu (Sun/Arkamaṇḍala):** Meditating in the heart-space within the fire-flame on the solar-disc (*arkamaṇḍalaṃ*), envisioning the Supreme Light in a manifest form, one should meditate on Viṣṇu who is brilliant like the morning sun (*taruṇādityasaṅkāśaṃ viṣṇuṃ dhyāyēt*).
    3.  **Nārāyaṇa (Moon/Sōmamaṇḍala):** Meditating in the heart-space within the fire-flame on the lunar-disc (*sōmamaṇḍalaṃ*), envisioning the Supreme Light in a manifest form, one should meditate on Nārāyaṇa who resembles pure crystal (*śuddhasphaṭikasaṅkāśaṃ nārāyaṇamūrtiṃ dhyāyēt*).

**Transliteration (IAST):**

**Translation (English):**
*   **Conclusion:** Thus, manifest meditation is of four kinds (*ēvaṃ saguṇadhyānaṃ caturvidhaṃ bhavati*). Daily worshipping the Lord Nārāyaṇa through these limbs of yoga, one beholds everything in absorption (*samādhi*) — thus it is understood (*sarvaṃ samādhinā paśyatīti vijñāyatē*).

### Glossary of Key Terms
*   **Niṣkala (निष्कल):** Unmanifest, partless, or formless; the transcendent aspect of the Supreme Brahman.
*   **Sakala (सकल):** Manifest, with parts, or with form; the immanent aspect of the Supreme Brahman suitable for meditation and worship.
*   **Nirguṇa-dhyāna (निर्गुणध्यान):** Meditation on the formless, attribute-free aspect of Godhead, which is all-pervading and self-luminous.
*   **Saguṇa-dhyāna (सगुणध्यान):** Meditation on the manifest aspect of Godhead endowed with attributes, features, ornaments, and consorts.
*   **Mahēndra-maṇḍala (महेन्द्रमण्डल):** The energetic mandala of Earth/Indra, square in shape and golden in color, located in the lower torso.
*   **Vāruṇa-maṇḍala (वारुणमण्डल):** The energetic mandala of Water/Varuṇa, crescent-shaped and white in color, located above the earth mandala.
*   **Yajñamūrti (यज्ञमूर्ति):** The deity representing the Vedic sacrifice, worshipped in the fire-disc (*agnimaṇḍala*).
*   **Sōma-maṇḍala (सोममण्डल):** The lunar disc, visualized in the heart as the seat of Nārāyaṇa.
*   **Samādhi (समाधि):** Absolute absorption; the eighth and final limb of yoga where the meditator, the object of meditation, and the process of meditation merge into one.
