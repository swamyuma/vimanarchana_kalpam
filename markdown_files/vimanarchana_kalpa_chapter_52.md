# Chapter 52: Commencement of the Festival and Soil Gathering (Utsavārambha-vidhiḥ / Mṛt-saṅgrahaṇa-vidhiḥ)
### Section 1: Liturgical Rules for the Commencement of the Festival and the Soil-Gathering Ritual

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **अथ द्विपञ्चाशत्तमः पटलः**
> **उत्सवारम्भ-मृत्संग्रहण-विधिः -**
> ***atha dvipañcāśattamaḥ paṭalaḥ***
> ***utsavārambha-mṛtsaṅgrahaṇa-vidhiḥ -***
> 
> **(उत्सवारम्भविधिः)**
> अथ उत्सवारम्भः - यजमानः सर्वदोषशान्त्यर्थं विष्णोरुत्सवं कारयेत् । आलयाभिमुखेऽन्यस्मिन् शुभे देशे वा आस्थानमण्डपं कूटं वा पञ्चहस्तादिभेदेन प्राङ्मुखं दक्षिणामुखं आलयद्वारदिङ्मुखं वा परिकल्प्य, तत्र मुखे द्वात्रिंशत्स्तम्भादिशतस्तम्भान्तैः स्तम्भैर्युक्तं नृत्तमण्डपं कूटं प्रपां वा कृत्वा, प्रपायां मध्ये वर्षनिवारणं कूटं युक्त्या कारयेत् ।
> ***(utsavārambhavidhiḥ)***
> *atha utsavārambhaḥ - yajamānaḥ sarvadoṣaśāntyarthaṃ viṣṇorutsavaṃ kārayet. ālayābhimukhe’nyasmin śubhe deśe vā āsthānamaṇḍapaṃ kūṭaṃ vā pañcahastādibhedena prāṅmukhaṃ dakṣiṇāmukhaṃ ālayadvāradiṅmukhaṃ vā parikalpya, tatra mukhe dvātriṃśatstambhādiśatastambhāntaiḥ stambhairyutaṃ nṛttamaṇḍapaṃ kūṭaṃ prapāṃ vā kṛtvā, prapāyāṃ madhye varṣanivāraṇaṃ kūṭaṃ yuktyā kārayet.*
> 
> तत्सर्वं वितान-ध्वज-पताका-मुक्तादाम-स्तम्भवेष्टन-तरङ्ग-दर्भमालाद्यैः अलङ्कृत्य; द्वारेषु तोरण-पूर्णकुम्भ-दर्भमाला-क्रमुक-दीपाङ्कुरैः अलङ्कुर्यात् । शिबिका-रथ-रङ्ग-दोला-यन्त्र-मुखमण्डप-गज-तुरग-गरुड-सिंह-व्याल-हंस-भ्रमणादीनि यानानि शक्तितः कारयेत् । मालादीप-यन्त्रदीप-हस्तादिदीप-शूलदीपादीन्, भेरी-पटह-मर्दल-गोमुख-ताल-काहल-मुरज-शङ्ख-जर्झर-मञ्जुल-दुन्दुभि-शङ्खशृङ्ग-धारा-वीणा-वेणु-झल्लरीश्च शक्तितः कारयेत् ।
> *tatsarvaṃ vitāna-dhvaja-patākā-muktādāma-stambhaveṣṭana-taraṅga-darbhamālādyaiḥ alaṅkṛtya; dvāreṣu toraṇa-pūrṇakumbha-darbhamālā-kramuka-dīpāṅkuraiḥ alaṅkuryāt. śibikā-ratha-raṅga-dolā-yantra-muṣamaṇḍapa-gaja-turaga-garuḍa-siṃha-vyāla-hansa-bhramaṇādīni yānāni śaktitaḥ kārayet. mālādīpa-yantradīpa-hastādidīpa-śūladīpādīn, bherī-paṭaha-mardala-gomukha-tāla-kāhala-muraj-śaṅkha-jarjhara-mañjula-dundubhi-śaṅkhaśṛṅga-dhārā-vīṇā-veṇu-jhallarīśca śaktitaḥ kārayet.*
> 
> छत्राणि नानाविधानि पिञ्छान्यनेकविधानि व्यजनानि चामराणि विविधान् ध्वजान् पताकाँश्च वितान-तरङ्ग-स्तम्भवेष्टनानि आहृत्य; यथोक्तं समित्-पुष्प-दर्भ-होमद्रव्य-बलिद्रव्य-पुष्प-गन्ध-धूप-दीपार्घ्यद्रव्यं हविर्द्रव्यं नववस्त्रतन्तु-हेम-रत्न-धान्य-तण्डुल-घृत-दधि-क्षीराणि दीपार्थं घृतं तैलं वा आहरेत् । हविर्द्रोणं तण्डुलं, बहूपदंश-गुड-फलसहितं भक्तानां ब्राह्मणादीनां भोजनार्थानि यानि द्रव्याणि तानि सर्वाणि च संभरति । नर्तकान् गायकान् भक्तान् परिचारकाँश्च यत्नेनाऽऽह्वयेत् ।
> *chatrāṇi nānāvidhāni piñcānyanekavidhāni vyajanāni cāmarāni vividhān dhvajān patākāṃśca vitāna-taraṅga-stambhaveṣṭanāni āhṛtya; yathoktaṃ samit-puṣpa-darbha-homadravya-balidravya-puṣpa-gandha-dhūpa-dīpārghyadravyaṃ havisdravyaṃ navavastratantu-hema-ratna-dhānya-taṇḍula-ghṛta-dadhi-kṣīrāṇi dīpārthaṃ ghṛtaṃ tailaṃ vā āharet. havisdroṇaṃ taṇḍulaṃ, bahūpadaṃśa-guḍa-phalasahitaṃ bhaktānāṃ brāhmaṇādīnāṃ bhojanārthāni yāni dravyāni tāni sarvāṇi ca saṃbharati. nartakān gāyakān bhaktān paricārakāṃśca yatnenā’’hvayet.*
> 
> आचार्यो यजमानयुतः पूर्वेद्युरेव पूर्वाह्ने देवेशस्य द्विगुणार्चनार्थं कुम्भदेवाराधनार्थं होमार्थं बलिदानार्थम् आस्थानार्चनार्थं च पञ्च पदार्थान् पञ्च परिचारकाँश्च वरयेत् । तद्ग्रामवासिनः सर्वे अनन्यतत्पराश्च अलङ्कृताः हर्षयुता भवेयुः ।
> *ācāryo yajamānayutaḥ pūrvedyureva pūrvāhne deveśasya dviguṇārcanārthaṃ kumbhadevarādhanārthaṃ homārthaṃ balidānārtham āsthānārcanārthaṃ ca pañca padārthān pañca paricārakāṃśca varayet. tadgrāmavāsinaḥ sarve ananyatatparāśca alaṅkṛtāḥ harṣayutā bhaveyuḥ.*
> 
> तद्ग्रामवीथीश्च संशोध्य; गृहद्वाराणि दीपाङ्कुरोदकुम्भाद्यैरलङ्कृत्य; देवालयं मृत्सिक्तोपलेपनाद्यैः संशोध्य; पैष्ट्या भूमिमलङ्कृत्य; लाजाऽक्षत-कुसुमान्यवकीर्य; उत्सवं यद्दिने कर्तुमुद्योगः तद्दिनात्पूर्वम् नवमे सप्तमे पञ्चमे तृतीये वाऽह्नि अङ्कुरार्पणं कुर्यात् ।
> *tadgrāmavīthīśca saṃśodhya; gṛhadvārāṇi dīpāṅkurodakumbhādyairalaṅkṛtya; devālayaṃ mṛtsiktopalepanādyaiḥ saṃśodhya; paiṣṭyā bhūmimalaṅkṛtya; lājā’xata-kusumānyavakīrya; utsavaṃ yaddine kartumudyogaḥ taddinātpūrvam navame saptame pañcame tṛtīye vā’hni aṅkurārpaṇaṃ kuryāt.*
> 
> उत्सवादिदिने प्रभाते स्नात्वा आचार्यो यजमानयुतः प्रथमदिनोत्सवाद्यवभृथान्तं हविष्याशी अनन्यतत्परो नववस्त्रोत्तरीयाभरणयुतः पुरुषसूक्तपूर्वम् अरुण-नारायण-अष्टाक्षर-पारायणं कुर्यात् । तद्दिनेषु आशौचं न भवेत् ।
> *utsavādidine prabhāte snātvā ācāryo yajamānayutaḥ prathamadinotsavādyavabhṛthāntaṃ haviṣyāśī ananyatatparo navavastrottarīyābharaṇayutaḥ puruṣasūktapūrvam aruṇa-nārāyaṇa-aṣṭāxara-pārāyaṇaṃ kuryāt. taddineṣu āśaucaṃ na bhavet.*
> 
> ततो देवालयं प्रविश्य; देवं प्रणम्य; उत्सवबिम्बं देव्यौ च, तदलाभे स्नापनं, तदलाभे कौतुकं वा आहृत्य, मध्याह्नान्ते स्नपनोक्तविधानेन यथाशक्तिकलशैः स्नापयित्वा; अथवा शुद्धोदकैरभिषिच्य; हविर्निवेदयेत् । तदालयगतदेवेभ्यः सर्वेभ्यः परिवारदेवेभ्यश्चावभृथान्तं तावत् त्रिकालं द्विगुणं हविर्निवेदयेत् । परिवारदेवानां बिम्बाभावे तत्तत्पीठे तथैवाभ्यर्च्य; एककालं वा हविर्निवेदयेत् ।
> *tato devālayaṃ praviśya; devaṃ praṇamya; utsavabimbaṃ devyou ca, tadalābhe snapanaṃ, tadalābhe kautukaṃ vā āhṛtya, madhyāhnānte snapanoktavidhaneṣu yathāśaktikalaśaiḥ snāpayitvā; athavā śuddhodakairabhiṣicya; havisnivedayet. tadālayagatadevebhyaḥ sarvebhyaḥ parivāradevebhyaścāvabhṛthāntaṃ tāvat trikālaṃ dviguṇaṃ havisnivedayet. parivāradevānāṃ bimbābhāve tattatpīṭhe tathāivābhyarcya; ekakālaṃ vā havisnivedayet.*
> 
> **(मृत्संग्रहणविधिः)**
> सायाह्ने यजमानेन आचार्यः शिष्यैः सार्धं वस्त्रोत्तरीयाभरणैरलङ्कृतः, चक्र-वीश-शान्तान् समविंशतिविग्रहैः अभ्यर्च्य; अलङ्कृत्य; यानमारोप्य; शिरसा धारयन् तीर्थाङ्कुरार्थं मृत्संग्रहणार्थं भक्तानां ब्राह्मणानां शिरोभिः पालिकादीन् धारयन् वस्त्रमाल्यालङ्कृतं खनित्रं चाऽऽनीय; सर्ववाद्यसमायुक्तं सर्वालङ्कारसंयुक्तं ‘विष्णुमूरुं रक्षस्व’ इति अग्रतो गच्छेत् ।
> ***(mṛtsaṅgrahaṇavidhiḥ)***
> *sāyāhne yajamānena ācāryaḥ śiṣyaiḥ sārdhaṃ vastrottarīyābharaṇairalaṅkṛtaḥ, cakra-vīśa-śāntān samaviṃśativigrahaiḥ abhyarcya; alaṅkṛtya; yānamāropya; śirasā dhārayan tīrthāṅkurārthaṃ mṛtsaṅgrahaṇārthaṃ bhaktānāṃ brāhmaṇānāṃ śirobhiḥ pālikādīn dhārayan vastramālyālaṅkṛtaṃ khanitraṃ cā’’nīya; sarvavādyasamāyuktaṃ sarvalङ्kārasaṃyuktaṃ ‘viṣṇumūruṃ rakṣasva’ iti agrato gacchet.*
> 
> आलयाद् ग्रामाद्वा प्राचीम् उदीचीं मैशानीं वा दिशां गत्वा; संशोध्य, उपलिप्य; तस्मिन् भूलोकवद् वर्तुलम् उलिख्य; तन्मध्ये महीदेवीं ध्रुवबेरस्य बाहुसमां स्तनान्तां वा ऊर्ध्ववक्त्राम् ऐशानीन्यस्तमौलिकां नैर्ऋतीन्यस्तपादां दक्षिणमाकुञ्च्य, वामं प्रसार्य शयानां, द्विभुजां धृतोत्पलदक्षिणहस्ताम् ऊरुप्रसारितवामहस्तां यथालाभाभरणाऽम्बरान्वितां नवतालमानेन कल्पयित्वा; ‘आपो हि ष्ठा मयोभुवः’ इत्यादिना अभ्युक्ष्य, एकादशोपचारैः अभ्यर्च्य; तामुन्मन्य; ललाटे बाहोः स्तनयोर्वा मेदिनीति मन्त्रेण ‘त्वां खनामि’ इत्याचार्यः प्राङ्मुखः उदङ्मुखो वा खनित्वा; हेमादिपात्रे मृदं गृहीत्वा; वस्त्रैराच्छाद्य; तत्क्रमेण ग्रामं प्रदक्षिणीकृत्य; देवालयं प्रविश्य; अङ्कुरार्पणस्थाने मृदं संन्यस्य; विधिना अङ्कुरार्पणं कारयेत् ॥
> *ālayād grāmādvā prācīm udīcīṃ maiśānīṃ vā diśāṃ gatvā; saṃśodhya, upalipya; tasmin bhūlokavad vartulam uliṣya; tanmadhye mahīdevīṃ dhruvaberasya bāhusamāṃ stanāntāṃ vā ūrdhvavaktrām aiśānīnyastamoulikāṃ nairṛtīnyastapādāṃ daxiṇamākuñcya, vāmaṃ prasārya śayānāṃ, dvibhujāṃ dhṛtotpaladaxiṇahastām ūruprasāritavāmahastāṃ yathālābhābharaṇā’mbarānvitāṃ navatālamānena kalpayitvā; ‘āpo hi ṣṭhā mayobhuvaḥ’ ityādinā abhyuṣya, ekādaśopacāraiḥ abhyarcya; tāmunamnya; lalāṭe bāhoḥ stanayorvā medinīti mantreṇa ‘tvāṃ khanāmi’ ityācāryaḥ prāṅmukhaḥ udaṅmuṣo vā khanitvā; hemādipātre mṛdaṃ gṛhītvā; vastrairācchādya; tatkrameṇa grāmaṃ pradacṣiṇīkṛtya; devālayaṃ praviśya; aṅkurārpaṇastāne mṛdaṃ saṃnyasya; vidhinā aṅkurārpaṇaṃ kārayet.*

#### English Translation
*   **The Festival Pavilion Setup:** Now I shall describe the commencement of the festival (*utsavārambha*): the patron, for pacifying all defects, should perform the festival of Viṣṇu. Having prepared the assembly canopy (*āsthāna-maṇḍapa*) or dome facing east, south, or facing the temple door, measuring five or more cubits in size, in front of the temple or in another clean place. In front of it, make a dance pavilion (*nṛtta-maṇḍapa*), dome, or shed supported by thirty-two to one hundred pillars, and in the middle of the shed, build a rain-shelter dome strategically.

*   **Decoring the Pavilion:** Decorate all of it with canopies, flags, banners, pearl garlands, pillar wraps, waving cloths, and Darbha grass garlands. Decorate doors with arches, full water pots, Darbha garlands, areca nut sprouts, and lamps.

*   **Ritual Vehicles and Music:** Prepare vehicles (*yānas*) like palanquins (*śibikā*), chariots (*ratha*), swings (*dolā*), front porches, models of elephants, horses, Garudas, lions, mythical beasts (*vyāla*), swans, and rotating wheels according to capacity. Prepare row-lamps, mechanical lamps, hand-lamps, spear-lamps, drums (*bherī*, *paṭaha*, *mardaḷa*), cow-mouth horns (*gomukha*), cymbals (*tāla*), trumpets (*kāhala*), clay-drums (*muraj*), conches, brass-cymbals (*jarjhara*), flutes, and other instruments according to capacity.

*   **Supplies for the Feasts and Oblations:** Gather umbrellas of various kinds, peacock-feather fans, whisks, banners, flags, canopies, and pillar wraps. Gather sacrificial wood (*samidh*), flowers, Darbha grass, oblation materials, offering materials, perfumes, incense, lamps, *arghya* water, food materials, new garments, threads, gold, gems, paddy, rice, ghee, yogurt, milk, and ghee or oil for lamps. Gather one *droṇa* of rice for *havis*, along with many side-dishes, jaggery, and fruits, and all materials needed for feeding the devotees and Brahmins. Summon dancers, singers, devotees, and temple attendants with effort.

*   **Priestly Selections:** The *ācārya* along with the patron, in the morning of the previous day, should select five principal materials and five attendants for the double-worship (*dviguṇārcana*), pot-worship, fire-oblations, *bali* offerings, and assembly-hall worship. All the inhabitants of the village should be focused on this, dressed up, and filled with joy.

*   **Village Purification:** Cleaning the village streets, decorating house doors with lamps, sprouts, and water pots, purifying the temple by washing the floor and plastering with mud, decorating the ground with rice flour drawings, and scattering parched grains, unhusked rice, and flowers. If the festival is planned, perform the seed-planting (*aṅkurārpaṇa*) nine, seven, five, or three days prior.

*   **Chanting and Devotion:** On the morning of the festival day, having bathed, the *ācārya* along with the patron, eating only sacrificial food (*haviṣyāśī*) and remaining focused on the ritual until the concluding bath (*avabhṛtha*), dressed in new garments, upper cloths, and ornaments, should perform the recitation of the *Puruṣa-sūkta*, the *Aruṇa-mantras*, and the eight-syllable mantra. During those days, ritual impurity (*āśauca*) does not apply.

*   **Bathing the Processional Icons:** Entering the temple, bowing to the Lord, taking the festival icon along with the two Goddesses, or the bathing icon (*snāpana-bera*) or the *kautuka-bera* if unavailable, and at the end of the noon worship, bathing Him with pots according to capacity following the bathing rules, or bathing Him with pure water, and offering *havis*. To all the retinue deities of the temple, until the concluding bath, offer double the quantity of *havis* three times a day. If retinue deities lack icons, worship them on their respective pedestals and offer *havis* once a day.

*   **The Soil Gathering Procession (*Mṛt-saṅgrahana*):** In the evening, the *ācārya* accompanied by the patron and disciples, decorated with garments, upper cloths, and ornaments, having worshipped the Cakra, Viṣvaksena, and the peaceful icon with twenty forms, decorated them, mounted them on vehicles, or carrying them on their heads. The devotees and Brahmins, carrying clay cups (*pālikās*) for the holy sprouts on their heads, bringing a spade (*khanitra*) decorated with cloths and garlands, accompanied by all instruments and decorations, should walk in front reciting "Viṣṇumūruṃ rakṣasva".

*   **Invoking Goddess Earth:** Going to the East, North, or Northeast of the temple or village, cleaning the spot, plastering it, drawing a circle like the earth, and depicting Goddess Earth (*Mahī-devī*) inside, matching the main deity's arms or breasts in size. She should be depicted with her head facing upwards towards the Northeast and her feet towards the Southwest, her right leg bent and her left leg extended, sleeping, two-armed, holding a blue lotus in her right hand and her left hand extended along her thigh, adorned with ornaments and garments as available, following the nine-span (*navatāla*) system.

*   **Gathering the Holy Soil:** Sprinkling the image of the Earth Goddess reciting "Āpo hi ṣṭhā", worshipping her with eleven services, bowing to her, the priest, facing east or north, should dig the ground reciting the *Medinī* mantra on her forehead, arms, or breasts, saying "Tvāṃ khanāmi" (I dig thee). Gathering the soil in a gold or other metal vessel, covering it with cloths, circumambulating the village, entering the temple, depositing the soil in the seed-planting chamber, he should perform the seed-planting according to rules.
