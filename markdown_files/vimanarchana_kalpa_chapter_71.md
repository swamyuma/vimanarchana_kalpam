# Vimanarchana Kalpa of Sage Marīci (Vaikhānasa Āgama)
## Chapter 71: Punar-Bālālaya-vidhiḥ (Rules for Reconstruction/Re-establishment of the Temporary Shrine)

This document contains the Sanskrit text, IAST transliteration, and English translation of the 71st Chapter (*Ekasaptatitamaḥ Paṭalaḥ*) of the **Vimānārcanākalpa** (attributed to Sage Marīci, representing the Vaikhānasa school of Vaishnavism).

This chapter outlines the rules for building and using a temporary shrine (*bālālaya* or *taruṇālaya*) when the main fixed deity icon (*dhruva-bera*) or the temple structure (*vimāna*) is damaged, decayed, or desecrated. It includes detailed architectural specifications for the temporary shrine, the zoning layout for placing the deities, fire offering protocols, the ritual transfer of the divine presence (*śakti*) into a water vessel (*kumbha*), and the subsequent installation inside the temporary shrine.

---

### Section 1: Indications for Punar-Bālālaya (Re-establishment of Temporary Shrine)

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **अथ एकसप्ततितमः पटलः**
> **पुनर्बालालय-विधिः**

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **atha ekasaptatitamaḥ paṭalaḥ**
> **punar-bālālaya-vidhiḥ**

**Translation (English):**
*   **The Subject:** Now, I shall declare the rules for the re-establishment of the temporary shrine (*punar-bālālaya-vidhiḥ*).

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> अथ पुनर्बालालयविधिं वक्ष्ये —
> महाबेर-विमाने च पतिते, रणे छिन्ने, अङ्गे हीने, अशनिहते, वाताहते, क्षीणवर्णे, गर्भालय-स्थल-भ्रंशे, स्थान-गर्भ-पीठ-विनाशे, चण्डालादि-अस्पृश्य-प्रवेशे वा, अचिराद् एव बालालये देवं प्रतिष्ठापयेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> atha punar-bālālaya-vidhiṃ vakṣye —
> mahābera-vimāne ca patite, raṇe chinne, aṅge hīne, aśanihate, vātāhate, kṣīṇavarṇe, garbhālaya-sthala-bhraṃśe, sthāna-garbha-pīṭha-vināśe, caṇḍālādi-aspṛśya-praveśe vā, acirād eva bālālaye devaṃ pratiṣṭhāpayet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Indications for Bālālaya:** When the main fixed icon (*mahā-bera*) or the temple structure (*vimāna*) is fallen (*patita*), broken/severed in battle (*raṇe chinne*), damaged in its limbs (*aṅge hīne*), struck by lightning (*aśanihate*), damaged by severe wind/storm (*vātāhate*), faded in its color/paint (*kṣīṇavarṇe*), when the sanctum floor has subsided (*garbhālaya-sthala-bhraṃśe*), when the pedestal or foundation is destroyed (*sthāna-garbha-pīṭha-vināśe*), or when outcasts/untouchables enter the sanctum (*caṇḍālādi-aspṛśya-praveśe*), one should immediately install the deity in a temporary shrine (*bālālaya*).

#### Verse / Paragraph 3
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> बालालये अर्च्यमानेऽपि स्थान-सङ्कटे, चण्डालाद्यस्पृश्य-अधिष्ठिते, नद्यादिभिः स्थान-नाशने वा सति, अन्यत्र मनोरमे यथेष्टं पूर्ववत् कर्षणादि पुनः संस्कारं कृत्वा, प्रथमे प्रथमं द्वितीयं वा बालालयं कृत्वा तद् बिम्बम् आनीय प्रतिष्ठाप्य, पश्चात् मूलालयं कुर्यात्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> bālālaye arcyamāne'pi sthāna-saṅkaṭe, caṇḍālādya-aspṛśya-adhiṣṭhite, nadyādibhiḥ sthāna-nāśane vā sati, anyatra manorame yatheṣṭaṃ pūrvavat karṣaṇādi punaḥ saṃskāraṃ kṛtvā, prathame prathamaṃ dvitīyaṃ vā bālālayaṃ kṛtvā tad bimbam ānīya pratiṣṭhāpya, paścāt mūlālayaṃ kuryāt.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Re-establishing a Second Bālālaya:** Even while the daily worship is being performed in the temporary shrine (*bālālaya*), if there is a space constraint (*sthāna-saṅkaṭe*), if it is occupied/desecrated by outcasts or untouchables (*caṇḍālādya-aspṛśya-adhiṣṭhite*), or if the place is destroyed by rivers or floods (*nadyādibhiḥ sthāna-nāśane*), one should choose another beautiful, desired location, perform the preparatory rituals like ploughing (*karṣaṇādi*) and other purificatory rites (*punaḥ-saṃskāraṃ*) as before, construct a first or second temporary shrine (*bālālaya*), bring the icon there, install it, and subsequently rebuild/repair the main temple (*mūlālaya*).

### Section 2: Minor Damage to Structure or Icons

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **विमान-बाह्यस्थ-अङ्गादि-हीने तृणादिना भिन्ने च कर्तव्यम्**
> बाह्ये च अङ्गोपाङ्गहीने तृण-गुल्म-वल्मीक-माक्षिक-पादपाद्यैः मलिने सति, अन्य-बालालय-स्थानं विना, तद्विमान-अर्चिषं तदङ्गदेवश्च महाबेरे समारोप्य, नवीकृत्य विमानप्रतिष्ठोक्तविधिना विमानप्रतिष्ठां कारयेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **vimāna-bāhyastha-aṅgādi-hīne tṛṇādinā bhinne ca kartavyam**
> bāhye ca aṅgopāṅgahīne tṛṇa-gulma-valmīka-mākṣika-pādapādyaiḥ maline sati, anya-bālālaya-sthānaṃ vinā, tadvimāna-arciṣaṃ tadaṅgadevaśca mahābere samāropya, navīkṛtya vimānapratiṣṭhoktavidhinā vimānapratiṣṭhāṃ kārayet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Minor Structural Damage:** If only the outer minor limbs (*aṅgopāṅga*) of the temple are damaged, or if it is polluted by grass, shrubs, anthills, beehives, or trees growing on it, one can perform the repairs without establishing a separate temporary shrine (*bālālaya*). In this case, one should project/transfer the divine energy of the vimāna-deities (*vimāna-arciṣaṃ*) and its subsidiary deities (*tadaṅgadevān*) onto the main fixed icon (*mahā-bera*), carry out the renovation, and perform the temple consecration (*vimāna-pratiṣṭhā*) according to the rules of temple installation.

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **विमान-ध्रुवयोः विनाशे कर्तव्यम्**
> विमान-ध्रुवयोः विनाशे कौतुकादीन् बालागारे कालविज्ञापन-प्रार्थनामन्त्रं च विना प्रथमतरुणालयप्रतिष्ठोक्तविधिना प्रतिष्ठाप्य अर्चयेत्। तस्य काले अतीते न दोषाय भवति।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **vimāna-dhruvayoḥ vināśe kartavyam**
> vimāna-dhruvayoḥ vināśe kautukādīn bālāgāre kālavijñāpana-prārthanāmantraṃ ca vinā prathamataruṇālayapratiṣṭhoktavidhinā pratiṣṭhāpya arcayet. tasya kāle atīte na doṣāya bhavati.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Destruction of Temple and Main Icon:** If both the temple structure (*vimāna*) and the main fixed deity (*dhruva*) are destroyed, one should install the movable icons (*kautuka*, etc.) in the temporary shrine (*bālāgāra*) without the time-announcement prayer (*kāla-vijñāpana-prārthanā-mantra*), following the procedure for the first temporary shrine installation, and perform the worship. Even if a long time passes under these conditions, it does not lead to any fault/defect (*na doṣāya bhavati*).

#### Verse / Paragraph 3
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **संस्कारादि-हानौ कर्तव्यम्**
> यद्यत् संस्कारहानिः अङ्गहानिश्च भवति, बालागारं कृत्वा तत्तत् संस्कारं कृत्वा तस्य उपरि सन्धानं कारयेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **saṃskārādi-hānau kartavyam**
> yadyat saṃskārahāniḥ aṅgahāniśca bhavati, bālāgāraṃ kṛtvā tattat saṃskāraṃ kṛtvā tasya upari sandhānaṃ kārayet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Restoration of Rites & Icon Repairs:** Whatever loss of purificatory rites (*saṃskāra-hāni*) or damage to limbs (*aṅga-hāni*) occurs, one should build a temporary shrine, perform the respective purificatory rites, and carry out the repair/joining (*sandhāna*) of the icon there.

#### Verse / Paragraph 4
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **कौतुकादि-विनाशे कर्तव्यम्**
> कौतुकादीनां च विनाशे ध्रुवार्चायां तद्विमाने च जीर्णादि-दोष-संयुक्ते पुनः बालागारं सङ्कल्प्य यथालाभमानेन दारवं बिम्बम् आहृत्य, प्रथमतरुणालयप्रतिष्ठोक्तविधिना प्रतिष्ठाप्य, अभ्यर्च्य कौतुकादीन् सङ्कल्प्य प्रतिष्ठापयेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **kautukādi-vināṣe kartavyam**
> kautukādīnāṃ ca vināṣe dhruvārcāyāṃ tadvimāne ca jīrṇādi-doṣa-saṃyukte punaḥ bālāgāraṃ saṅkalpya yathālābhamānena dāravaṃ bimbam āhṛtya, prathamataruṇālayapratiṣṭhoktavidhinā pratiṣṭhāpya, abhyarcya kautukādīn saṅkalpya pratiṣṭhāpayet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Loss of Movable Icons:** If the movable icons (*kautuka*, etc.) are lost or destroyed, and the main fixed icon (*dhruvārcā*) and the temple structure (*vimāna*) are also affected by wear/decay (*jīrṇādi-doṣa-saṃyukte*), one should resolve to establish a temporary shrine, obtain a wooden icon (*dāravaṃ bimbam*) of available dimensions, install and worship it according to the first temporary shrine installation rules, and then consecrate the new movable icons.

### Section 3: Damage to Wooden Icons

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **दारौ खण्ड-स्फुटितादि-दोष-सम्भवे कर्तव्यम्**
> (कौतुकादीनाम् अलाभे) तस्मिन् दारवे खण्ड-स्फुटितादि-दोष-संयुक्ते, अन्यदारु-बिम्बम् आहृत्य वेराच्छक्तिम् आवाह्य, प्रतिष्ठाप्य तद् वैरं (बिम्बं) विधिना त्यक्त्वा मूलालये मूलबेरं कृत्वा प्रतिष्ठापयेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **dārau khaṇḍa-sphuṭitādi-doṣa-sambhave kartavyam**
> (kautukādīnām alābhe) tasmin dārave khaṇḍa-sphuṭitādi-doṣa-saṃyukte, anyadāru-bimbam āhṛtya verācchaktiṃ āvāhya, pratiṣṭhāpya tad vairaṃ (bimbaṃ) vidhinā tyaktvā mūlālaye mūlaberaṃ kṛtvā pratiṣṭhāpayet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Damage to Wooden Icon:** (If new movable icons cannot be obtained) and if that temporary wooden icon (*dārava*) itself suffers from breaks, cracks, or other defects, one should procure another wooden icon, invoke the divine power (*śakti*) from the old icon into the new one, install it, discard the old icon according to the rules, and subsequently fashion and consecrate the permanent main icon (*mūla-bera*) in the main temple (*mūlālaya*).

### Section 4: Specifications and Architecture of Bālālaya

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **बालागार-स्थानम्**
> बालागारस्थानं मूलागारस्य मध्यसूत्राद् दक्षिणे भागे प्रथमावरणे द्वितीये वा मालिकाभित्तिमध्ये अङ्गणे मण्डपादिषु मनोरमे यत्र परिहारोऽस्ति तत्र बालागारं प्रकल्पयेत्। द्वितीयावरणाद् ऊर्ध्वम् अनारम्भणीयम्।
> ***bālāgāra-sthānam***
> *bālāgārasthānaṃ mūlāgārasya madhyasūtrād dakṣiṇe bhāge prathamāvaraṇe dvitīye vā mālikābhittimadhye aṅgaṇe maṇḍapādiṣu manorame yatra parihāro'sti tatra bālāgāraṃ prakalpayet. dvitīyāvaraṇād ūrdhvam anārambhaṇīyam.*
> 
> एकहस्तादि-त्रिहस्त-अन्तं विस्तारं, विस्ताराद् अध्यर्धं पादोनद्विगुणं वा उत्सेधं, तदुत्सेधं पञ्चधा कृत्वा — एकांशम् अधिष्ठानं, द्व्यंशं भित्तिं, द्व्यंशं शिखरं, लुपोद्वहं च्छन्नं मृण्मयं दारुजं वा। मूलालयस्य आदितल-पाद-विष्कम्भ-समं तद् द्विगुणं त्रिगुणं चतुर्गुणं वा, तदवशिष्टं नालीगृहं तरुणालयम्। मण्डपयुक्तं चेद् भित्त्या सह नालीगृहं यथालाभ-भित्तिकं, मुखमण्डपसहितं रहितं वा, त्रिभागांश-इक्षितम् एव कारयेत्। समं तदर्धं वा मुखमण्डपं पूर्वापरायतं दक्षिणोत्तरायतं समं वा तरुणालयम् एवं कुर्यात्।
> *ekahastādi-trihasta-antaṃ vistāraṃ, vistārād adhyardhaṃ pādonadviguṇaṃ vā utsedhaṃ, tadutsedhaṃ pañcadhā kṛtvā — ekāṃṣam adhiṣṭhānaṃ, dvyaṃṣaṃ bhittiṃ, dvyaṃṣaṃ śikharaṃ, lupodvahaṃ channaṃ mṛṇmayaṃ dārujaṃ vā. mūlālayasya āditala-pāda-viṣkambha-samaṃ tad dviguṇaṃ triguṇaṃ caturguṇaṃ vā, tadavaśiṣṭaṃ nālīgṛhaṃ taruṇālayam. maṇḍapayuktaṃ ced bhittyā saha nālīgṛhaṃ yathālābha-bhittikaṃ, mukhamaṇḍapasahitaṃ rahitaṃ vā, tribhāgāṃṣa-īkṣitam eva kārayet. samaṃ tadardhaṃ vā mukhamaṇḍapaṃ pūrvāparāyataṃ dakṣiṇottarāyataṃ samaṃ vā taruṇālayaṃ evaṃ kuryāt.*

#### English Translation
*   **Location of the Bālāgāra:** The temporary shrine (*bālāgāra*) should be located to the right side of the main sanctum's central axis (*mūlāgārasya madhya-sūtrād dakṣiṇe bhāge*), either in the first enclosure (*prathamāvaraṇa*) or the second enclosure (*dvitīyāvaraṇa*), in the middle of the cloister wall (*mālikā-bhitti-madhye*), in the courtyard (*aṅgaṇe*), or in pavilions (*maṇḍapādiṣu*), in a beautiful place where there are no obstructions. It should not be constructed beyond the second enclosure.

*   **Dimensions and Parts:** The width should be from one cubit to three cubits (*eka-hasta* to *tri-hasta*). The height should be one-and-a-half times or one-and-three-quarters times the width. Dividing this height into five parts: one part for the base (*adhiṣṭhāna*), two parts for the walls (*bhitti*), and two parts for the tower/roof (*śikhara*), with rafters (*lupodvaha*) and a covered top, made of clay or wood.

*   **Structure of the Taruṇālaya:** The width of the temporary temple (*taruṇālaya*) or its central room (*nālī-gṛha*) should be equal to the width of the main temple's ground-floor pillar, or twice, thrice, or four times that width. If it is built with a pavilion (*maṇḍapa*), the central room along with the walls should have walls of available dimensions, and it may be with or without a porch (*mukha-maṇḍapa*), designed with three-part proportions. One should make the porch equal to or half of that width, oriented east-west (*pūrvāparāyata*), north-south (*dakṣiṇottarāyata*), or square.

### Section 5: Layout of the Temporary Sanctum and Pedestal

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **गर्भालये कौतुकादि-स्थापन-स्थानम्**
> गर्भालयं पञ्चविंशतिभागं कृत्वा, मध्ये चैकं ब्रह्मस्थानं, तत्परितः अष्टभागं दैविकं, तत्परितः षोडशभागं मानुषं भवति। कौतुकं ब्रह्मस्थाने प्रतिष्ठापयेत् — उत्तमम्; दैविके मध्यमं, मानुषे अधमम्। स्नपन-उत्सव-बलिबेराणि दैविक-मानुषयोः एव स्थापयेत्।
> ***garbhālaye kautukādi-sthāpana-sthānam***
> *garbhālayaṃ pañcaviṃśatibhāgaṃ kṛtvā, madhye caikaṃ brahmasthānaṃ, tatparitaḥ aṣṭabhāgaṃ daivikaṃ, tatparitaḥ ṣoḍaśabhāgaṃ mānuṣaṃ bhavati. kautukaṃ brahmasthāne pratiṣṭhāpayet — uttamam; daivike madhyamaṃ, mānuṣe adhamam. snapana-utsava-baliberāṇi daivika-mānuṣayor eva sthāpayet.*
> 
> **ध्रुवपीठ-विस्तारादि**
> प्राणपीठं यथालाभ-आयाम-विस्तार-उत्सेधं, त्रिमेखलम् एकमेखलं वा, संन्यासविहीनं कुर्यात्।
> ***dhruvāpīṭha-vistārādi***
> *prāṇapīṭhaṃ yathālābha-āyāma-vistāra-utsedhaṃ, trimekhalam ekamekhalaṃ vā, saṃnyāsavihīnaṃ kuryāt.*
> 
> आलयाभिमुखे बालागार-अग्रे वा, पूर्ववद् यागशालां कृत्वा, तोरणाद्यैः अलङ्कृत्य, सम्भारान् सम्यक् स्थाप्य, आचार्यान् ऋत्विजश्च वरयेत्। यागशालामध्ये...
> *ālayābhimukhe bālāgāra-agre vā, pūrvavad yāgaśālāṃ kṛtvā, toraṇādyair alaṅkṛtya, sambhārān samyak sthāpya, ācāryān ṛtvijaśca varayet. yāgaśālāmadhye...*

#### English Translation
*   **The Grid Layout of the Sanctum:** Having divided the sanctum sanctorum (*garbhālaya*) into twenty-five grid squares (*pañca-viṃśati-bhāga*), the single central square is the *Brahma-sthāna* (Brahmā zone), surrounding it are eight squares of *Daivika* (Divine zone), and surrounding that are sixteen squares of *Mānuṣa* (Human zone).

*   **Zoning of Icons:** One should install the *Kautuka* (worship icon) in the *Brahma-sthāna* (considered the best/uttama); or in the *Daivika* zone (medium/madhyama), or in the *Mānuṣa* zone (lowest/adhama). The *Snapana* (bath), *Utsava* (procession), and *Bali* (offering) icons should be installed only in the *Daivika* and *Mānuṣa* zones.

*   **Pedestal and Setup:** The life-pedestal (*prāṇa-pīṭha*) should be made with available length, width, and height, having one or three tiers (*tri-mekhalam eka-mekhalaṃ vā*), but without the special consecration rituals (*saṃnyāsa-vihīnam*).

*   **Sacrificial Hall Preparation:** Facing the temple or in front of the temporary shrine, having constructed a sacrificial hall (*yāga-śālā*) as before, decorated it with arches (*toraṇa*) and other ornaments, properly arranged the ritual materials, one should select the chief priest (*ācārya*) and assistant priests (*ṛtvij*). ### Section 6: Sacrificial Rites and Fire Offerings

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)

### Section 8: Laying the Deities to Rest in the Sacrifice Hall

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> तम् अनु स्थापकाः बिम्बान्य् आदाय, तरुणालये स्नानद्रोण्यां प्रतिष्ठाप्य, पूर्ववद् उदकादिभिः संस्नाप्य, बालागारस्य अग्रे शयने धान्योपरि पूर्ववत् पञ्चशयनानि वासांसि वा आस्तीर्य, प्रतिसरं बध्वा, तथैव शाययित्वा, उत्तराच्छादनं कृत्वा, प्रधानाग्निं परिषिच्य, पूर्ववद् हौत्रं प्रशंस्य, आवाहनादीनि कृत्वा, अग्निषु पूर्ववदुक्तहोमं हुत्वा, प्रधानाग्नौ स्रुवेणाऽऽज्यम् आदाय "त्रिः" वैष्णवं, विष्णुसूक्तं, पुरुषसूक्तं, एकाक्षरादिसूक्तं च हुत्वा, पश्चात् सर्वदेवत्यं, तदालयगतपरिषद्देवत्यं वा हुत्वा, शेषं व्यपोह्य, प्रभाते स्नात्वा, देवागारं प्रविश्य, देवं प्रणम्य, देवं प्रणवेनोत्थापयेत्। आचार्यादीन् सम्पूज्य दक्षिणां दद्यात्।

#### English Translation
*   **Preparation for Rest:** Following him, the assistant sculptors (*sthāpakāḥ*) should carry the deity icons, place them in the bathing trough (*snāna-droṇī*) of the temporary shrine, bathe them with water and other ingredients as before, and then lay them to rest in front of the temporary shrine on a bed of grains covered with five layers of bedding (*pañca-śayana*) or clothes.

*   **laying to Rest:** Having tied the protective thread (*pratisara*), laid them to rest, covered them with an upper cloth, sprinkled water around the primary fire pit, praised the hotar-priest's role as before, performed the invocations, and offered the prescribed oblations in the fires, the priest should take clarified butter with the sruva-ladle from the primary fire pit and offer it three times (*triḥ*) with the Vaiṣṇava, Viṣṇu-sūkta, Puruṣa-sūkta, and Ekākṣara-sūkta.

He should then worship the chief priest and others and distribute the ritual fees (*dakṣiṇā*).

### Section 9: Installation in the Bālālaya (Temporary Shrine)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> अग्निं प्रज्वाल्य स्विष्टकृदाहुतिं पूर्णाहुतिं च हुत्वा, अमिविसृज्य, आचार्यः शिरसा कुम्भं धारयन्, शकुनसूक्तं जपन् अग्रतो गच्छेत्। स्थापका हस्ताभ्यां देवम् आदाय, सर्ववाद्यसमायुक्तं सर्वालङ्कारसंयुतं तोयधारापुरस्सरं तरुणालयं प्रदक्षिणीकृत्य, अभ्यन्तरं प्रविश्य, स्थिरासने "प्र तद् विष्णुः" इति मन्त्रेण प्राणपीठे देवं प्रतिष्ठाप्य, दक्षिणे देवीं श्रियं वामे हरिणीं च तत्तन्मन्त्रेण संस्थाप्य, देवपादं स्पृशन् विष्णुसूक्तं जप्त्वा; पूर्ववद् अक्षरन्यासादीनि कृत्वा "विष्णुः" इति कुम्भस्थां शक्तिं कूर्चेनाऽऽदाय "आयातु भगवान्" इति कौतुकस्य मूर्ध्नि "विष्णुम् आवाहयामि" इति सम्श्राव्य आवाहयेत्।
> *agniṃ prajvālya sviṣṭakṛdāhutiṃ pūrṇāhutiṃ ca hutvā, amivisyjya, ācāryaḥ śirasā kumbhaṃ dhārayan, śakunasūktaṃ japan agrato gacchet. sthāpakā hastābhyāṃ devam ādāya, sarvavādyasamāyuktaṃ sarvālaṅkārasaṃyutaṃ toyadhārāpurassaraṃ taruṇālayaṃ pradakṣiṇīkṛtya, abhyantaraṃ praviśya, sthirāsane "pra tad viṣṇuḥ" iti maṇtreṇa prāṇapīṭhe devaṃ pratiṣṭhāpya, dakṣiṇe devīṃ śriyaṃ vāme hariṇīṃ ca tattanmantreṇa saṃsthāpya, devapādaṃ spṛśan viṣṇusūktaṃ japtvā; pūrvavad akṣaranyāsādīni kṛtvā "viṣṇuḥ" iti kumbhasthāṃ śaktiṃ kūrcenā'dāya "āyātu bhagavān" iti kautukasya mūrdhni "viṣṇum āvāhayāmi" iti saṃśrāvya āvāhayet.*
> 
> "दक्षिणे देवीं श्रियं, वामे हरिणीम्" इत्यावाह्य, देवं परितः अभ्यन्तरपरिषद्देवान् ब्रह्माणं शङ्करं पूजकमुनिं च आवाह्य, गर्भालये कौतुकस्य दक्षिणे वामे वा, मुखमण्डपस्य दक्षिणे वामे वा, स्नपन-उत्सव-बलिबेराणि संस्थाप्य कौतुकाद् आवाहयेत्।
> *"dakṣiṇe devīṃ śriyaṃ, vāme hariṇīm" ityāvāhya, devaṃ paritaḥ abhyantarapariṣaddevān brahmāṇaṃ śaṅkaraṃ pūjakamuniṃ cā'vāhya, garbhālaye kautukasya dakṣiṇe vāme vā, mukhamaṇḍapasya dakṣiṇe vāme vā, snapana-utsava-baliberāṇi saṃsthāpya kautukād āvāhayet.*

#### English Translation
*   **The Procession to Bālālaya:** Having rekindled the fire, offered the *sviṣṭakṛt* oblation and the final offering (*pūrṇāhuti*), and bid farewell to the sacred fire, the chief priest should carry the *kumbha* on his head and walk forward while chanting the auspicious *Śakuna-sūkta*. The sculptors should carry the deity icon with their hands.

*   **Entering the Shrine:** Accompanied by all musical instruments, decorated with all ornaments, and preceded by a continuous stream of poured water (*toya-dhārā-purassaram*), they should circumambulate the temporary shrine (*taruṇālayaṃ pradakṣiṇī-kṛtya*), enter the inner sanctum, place the deity on a stable seat (*sthira-āsane*) on the life-pedestal (*prāṇa-pīṭha*) while reciting the mantra *'pra tad viṣṇuḥ'*.

*   **Establishing the Goddesses:** He should establish Goddess Śrī on the right and Goddess Hariṇī (Bhū) on the left with their respective mantras, touch the Lord's feet while chanting the Viṣṇu-sūkta, perform the placement of letters (*akṣara-nyāsa*) as before, draw the divine power from the *kumbha* using a grass bundle (*kūrca*) reciting the syllable *'viṣṇuḥ'*, place it on the crown of the *Kautuka* icon's head reciting *'āyātu bhagavān'*, and proclaim *'viṣṇum āvāhayāmi'* (I invoke Viṣṇu) to complete the invocation.

*   **Establishing Surrounding Deities:** Having invoked Goddess Śrī on the right and Goddess Hariṇī on the left, and having invoked the internal court deities around the Lord—namely Brahmā, Śaṅkara (Śiva), and the worshipping sages (Mārkaṇḍeya and Bhṛgu)—he should place the *Snapana*, *Utsava*, and *Bali* icons either on the right or left of the *Kautuka* in the sanctum, or on the right or left of the porch (*mukha-maṇḍapa*), and invoke their power from the *Kautuka* icon.

**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **धात्रादीनां बालालयः**
> धात्रादि-विष्णुभूतान्तानां तदालयस्थपरिषद्देवानां सर्वेषाम्, आलयेषु ध्रुवकौतुकसंयुक्तेषु सत्सु ध्रुवबेराणि जीर्णादिदोषयुक्तानि चेत्, तरुणालयं परितोऽलिन्दं कृत्वा तत्र सर्वान् परिषत्कौतुकवन्तः तत्पदे प्रतिष्ठाप्य अर्चयेत्। निर्दोष-परिषद्देवानां तत्र तत्र अर्चनादीनि कारयेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **dhātrādīnāṃ bālālayaḥ**
> dhātrādi-viṣṇubhūtāntānāṃ tadālayasthapariṣaddevānāṃ sarveṣām, ālayeṣu dhruvakautukasaṃyukteṣu satsu dhruvaberāṇi jīrṇādidoṣayuktāni cet, taruṇālayaṃ parito'lindaṃ kṛtvā tatra sarvān pariṣatkautukavantaḥ tatpade pratiṣṭhāpya arcayet. nirdoṣa-pariṣaddevānāṃ tatra tatra arcanādīni kārayet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Subsidiary Shrines:** For all the subsidiary deities of the temple from Dhātrī to Viṣṇubhūta, if the main temple has fixed and movable icons in place but the main fixed icons have become decayed or damaged (*jīrṇādi-doṣa-yuktāni*), one should construct a veranda (*alinda*) around the temporary temple, install all the subsidiary movable icons in their respective positions there, and perform the worship. For those subsidiary deities whose icons are undamaged (*nirdoṣa*), their worship and other rites should be performed in their original locations.

#### Verse / Paragraph 4
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **बालालयेऽपि उत्सवादि कर्तव्यम्**
> स्थापकाद्यैः सह पुण्याहं कृत्वा आसनाद्यैः अभ्यर्च्य हविः निवेद्य, उत्सवं स्नपनं च कारयेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **bālālaye'pi utsavādi kartavyam**
> sthāpakādyaiḥ saha puṇyāhaṃ kṛtvā āsanādyair abhyarcya haviḥ nivedya, utsavaṃ snapanaṃ ca kārayet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Festivals in Bālālaya:** (Festivals and other rites should also be performed in the temporary temple:) Having performed the purificatory ceremony (*puṇyāha*) along with the sculptors, worshipped the deity with seat-mantras (*āsanādyaiḥ*), and offered food (*haviḥ*), one should conduct the festival (*utsava*) and consecrate bath (*snapana*).

#### Colophon
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> (इति श्रीवैखानसे मरीचिप्रोक्ते विमानार्चनाकल्पे पुनर्बालालयविधिर्नाम एकसप्ततितमः पटलः ॥७१॥)

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> (iti śrī-vaikhānase marīciprokte vimānārcanākalpe punarbālālaya-vidhir-nāma ekasaptatitamaḥ paṭalaḥ ||71||)

**Translation (English):**
*   **Colophon:** (Thus ends the Seventy-first Chapter, titled *“The Rules for Re-establishment of the Temporary Shrine”* (Punar-bālālaya-vidhi), in the *Vimānārcanākalpa* of the *Vaikhānasa* school, compiled by Sage Marīci.)

## Glossary of Key Terms

1.  **Punar-Bālālaya (पुनर्बालालय):** The process of re-establishing a temporary/interim shrine when the main temple or the existing temporary temple suffers damage or desecration.
2.  **Mahā-bera / Mūla-bera (महाबेर / मूलबेर):** The primary, immovable deity icon fixed in the main sanctum (*garbhagṛha*).
3.  **Kautuka-bera (कौतुकबेर):** The primary movable metal icon of the temple used for daily archana/worship in the inner sanctum.
4.  **Taruṇālaya / Bālāgāra (तरुणालय / बालागार):** Alternative terms for the temporary temple constructed to house the deities while repairs are made to the main temple.
5.  **Adhiṣṭhāna, Bhitti, Śikhara (अधिष्ठान, भित्ति, शिखर):** The three primary structural components of the temporary shrine—the base, walls, and tower/roof.
6.  **Brahma-sthāna, Daivika, Mānuṣa (ब्रह्मस्थान, दैविक, मानुष):** The three zones of the sanctum sanctorum based on a 25-square grid layout. Brahma-sthāna is the center (9 squares), surrounded by Daivika (8 squares) and Mānuṣa (8 squares).
7.  **Prāṇa-pīṭha (प्राणपीठ):** The pedestal on which the deity icon is installed, providing the focal point for the deity's presence.
8.  **Kumbha (कुम्भ):** The sacred water vessel used to temporarily store the divine energy (*śakti*) of the deities during the transition from the main temple to the temporary shrine.
9.  **Pratisara (प्रतिसर):** The protective thread tied around the wrist of the deity or the kumbha during consecration and transitional rituals.
10. **Śakuna-sūkta (शकुनसूक्त):** The Vedic hymn chanted for invoking auspiciousness and good omens during processions and installations.
