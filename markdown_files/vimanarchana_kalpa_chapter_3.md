# Vimanarchana Kalpa of Sage Marīci (Vaikhānasa Āgama)
## Chapter 3: Padadevatā-Balividhiḥ (The Sowing of Seeds, Ploughing, and Offerings to Grid Deities)

This document contains the Sanskrit text, IAST transliteration, and English translation of the 3rd Chapter (*Tṛtīyaḥ Paṭalaḥ*) of the **Vimānārcanākalpa** (attributed to Sage Marīci, representing the Vaikhānasa school of Vaishnavism).

This chapter discusses the classifications of settlements (village, city, camp), grid alignments for temples, calculation formulas (*āyādi*) for dimensions, guidelines for residential layouts, the ritual of first ploughing (*karṣaṇa*), sowing of seeds (*bījāvāpa*), and detailed offerings (*bali*) to the 45 deities occupying the 81-square (*ekāśīti-pada*) mandala.

---

### Section 1: Classification of Settlements (Vāstubhedaḥ)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **अथ तृतीयः पटलः**
> **ग्रामादिवास्तुभेदः**
> ***atha tṛtīyaḥ paṭalaḥ***
> ***grāmādivāstubhedaḥ***
> 
> अथ ग्रामादिवास्तुभेदः — ग्रामाग्रहार-नगर-पत्तन-खर्वाट-कुटिक-सेनामुख-राजधानी-शिबिरा इति भेदा भवन्ति।
> *atha grāmādivāstubhedaḥ — grāmāgrahāra-nagara-pattana-kharvāṭa-kuṭika-senāmukha-rājadhānī-śibirā iti bhedā bhavanti.*
> 
> **ग्रामादीनां स्वरूपम्**
> विप्राणां सभृत्यानां निवासो ग्रामः। विप्रमुख्यानाम् अग्रहारः। अनेकजनसम्बाधम् अनेकशिल्पि-क्रयकविक्रयकाधिष्ठितं सर्वदेवतासंयुक्तं नगरम्। द्वीपान्तरगतद्रव्य-क्रयकविक्रयकाधिष्ठितं पत्तनम्। उभयसंमिश्रं खर्वाटम्। सपरिवारकैकग्रामणीकं कुटिकम्। सर्वजातिसमाकीर्णं नृपवेश्मसमायुक्तं सेनामुखम्। चतुरङ्गसमाकीर्णा नृपवद् भृत्यजुष्टा राजधानी। नृपसेना-चमूनाथ-निवेशनं शिबिरम् इति।
> ***grāmādīnāṃ svarūpam***
> *viprāṇāṃ sabhṛtyānāṃ nivāso grāmaḥ. vipramukhyānām agrahāraḥ. anekajanasambādham anekaśilpi-krayakavikrayakādhiṣṭhitaṃ sarvadevatāsaṃyuktaṃ nagaram. dvīpāntaragatadravya-krayakavikrayakādhiṣṭhitaṃ pattanam. ubhayasaṃmiśraṃ kharvāṭam. saparivārakaikagrāmaṇīkaṃ kuṭikam. sarvajātisamākīrṇaṃ nṛpaveśmasamāyuktaṃ senāmukham. caturaṅgasamākīrṇā nṛpavad bhṛtyajuṣṭā rājadhānī. nṛpasenā-camūnātha-niveśanaṃ śibiram iti.*

#### English Translation
*   **Classification of Settlements:** 
    *   Now, the classifications of settlement sites (*vāstu-bheda*) are: Village (*grāma*), Agrahara, City (*nagara*), Port/Market-town (*pattana*), Belt-town (*kharvāṭa*), Hamlet (*kuṭika*), Military outpost (*senāmukha*), Capital city (*rājadhānī*), and Military camp (*śibira*).

*   **Definitions of Settlements:**
    *   **Village (*grāma*):** The residence of Brahmanas along with their servants/dependents.
    *   **Agrahara:** The exclusive residence of prominent Brahmanas.
    *   **City (*nagara*):** Densely populated, occupied by various artisans, buyers, and sellers, and containing temples of all deities.
    *   **Port-town (*pattana*):** A trading hub where commodities from other islands/lands are bought and sold.
    *   **Belt-town (*kharvāṭa*):** A settlement combining features of both village and market-town.
    *   **Hamlet (*kuṭika*):** A small settlement governed by a single leader with his family.
    *   **Military Outpost (*senāmukha*):** Inhabited by all castes and containing a royal palace/fort.
    *   **Capital (*rājadhānī*):** Inhabited by the fourfold army (elephants, chariots, cavalry, infantry) and served by royal attendants.
    *   **Military Camp (*śibira*):** Temporary settlement housing the king's army and commanders.

### Section 2: Street Layouts in Villages & Agraharas (Vinyāsabhedaḥ)

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **ग्रामाग्रहारयोः प्रभेदः**
> तत्र ग्रामविन्यासं प्रागग्रम् उत्तराग्रं वा — एकवीधिकं दण्डकम्, प्राङ्मुखं द्विवीधिकं करीदण्डकम्, नीला-अनिला-अनलपुच्छकं, दक्षिणादि-चतुर्वीधिकं स्वस्तिकं, सदेवं सतृतीय-द्विवीधिकं नन्द्यावर्त्तं, सदीर्घक्षुद्रवीधिकं पद्मवीधिकम्, प्रागग्र-अनलवीधिकं चतुष्पथञ्च, षट्-सम-अष्ट-नव-अधिकदश-अन्यतमक्षुद्रवीधिकं प्रकीर्णकम्, एक-द्वि-त्रि-चतुः-पञ्चाधिक-विंशद्वीधिकम् उत्कीर्णकम् इति ग्रामाग्रहारयोः विन्यासभेदः।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **grāmāgrahārayoḥ prabhedaḥ**
> tatra grāmavinyāsaṃ prāgagram uttarāgraṃ vā — ekavīdhikaṃ daṇḍakam, prāṅmukhaṃ dvivīdhikaṃ karīdaṇḍakam, nīlā-anilā-analapucchakaṃ, dakṣiṇādi-caturvīdhikaṃ svastikaṃ, sadeveṃ satṛtīya-dvivīdhikaṃ nandyāvarttaṃ, sadīrghakṣudravīdhikaṃ padmavīdhikam, prāgagra-analavīdhikaṃ catuṣpathañca, ṣaṭ-sama-aṣṭa-nava-adhikadaśa-anyatamakṣudravīdhikaṃ prakīrṇakam, eka-dvi-tri-catuḥ-pañcādhika-viṃśadvīdhikam utkīrṇakam iti grāmāgrahārayoḥ vinyāsabhedaḥ.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Layout Variations for Villages & Agraharas:** 
    *   The street layouts for villages or Agraharas (aligned towards the East or North) are:
        *   **Daṇḍaka:** A layout containing a single main street.
        *   **Karīdaṇḍaka:** Facing East with two streets.
        *   **Nīlā-Anilā-Analapucchaka:** Layouts with specific wind/fire tail alignments.
        *   **Svastika:** Four streets starting from the South.
        *   **Nandyāvarta:** Streets arranged in a swirled pattern with three levels.
        *   **Padmavīdhika:** Combining long and short streets resembling a lotus.
        *   **Catuṣpatha:** Crossing streets with east-facing alignments.
        *   **Prakīrṇaka:** Scattered layouts with 6, 8, 9, or 10 small streets.
        *   **Utkīrṇaka:** Detailed layout containing 21 to 25 streets.

### Section 3: Shrine Placement Rules on the Grid

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **दण्डके स्वस्तिकादिषु च देवपदादिनिरूपणम्**
> तत्र दण्डके पश्चिमे प्राङ्मुखम्, उत्तरे दक्षिणामुखं वा, स्वस्तिकादिषु (पश्चिमे) पैशाचाभ्यन्तरे बाह्ये वा, ब्रह्मसूत्राद् उत्तरे, श्रीवत्सादिषु ब्रह्मपद-वायव्ये पञ्चमूर्तिविधानेन देवेशं विष्णुं संस्थापयेत्। दिशासु विदिशासु च, यथा दक्षिणदृष्ट्या ग्रामं पश्येत्, तथा ग्रामाभ्यन्तरे ग्रामाभिमुखं च संस्थापयेत्। तद्बहिर्दृष्टिः शुभदा, हरपृष्ठश्च शुभदः; सर्वत्र वाराह-नारसिंह-वामन-राम-कृष्णान् ग्रामप्रेक्षकानेव संस्थापयेत्। ग्रामादिषु षट्पञ्चाशद्-द्विशतपदेषु पैशाचं परिहृत्यैव देवेशं संस्थापयेत्। अन्येषु पश्चिमे पैशाचबाह्ये विष्णोरालयं कुर्यात्; अभ्यन्तरे तान्त्रिकेण विधिना अर्चयेत् न वैदिकेन। प्रतिलोम-अन्तराल-व्रात्यावासेषु नैवं विष्णुप्रतिष्ठां कारयेत्। श्रीवत्सादीनां वितथ-भृङ्गराज-भल्लाट-अदिति-महेन्द्रेषु निर्गमं कारयेत्। अविहितपदनिर्गमः, अन्यवास्तुसम्प्रेक्षणं, ग्रामयजमानयोः आशु विनाशाय भवति।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **daṇḍake svastikādiṣu ca devapadādinirūpaṇam**
> tatra daṇḍake paścime prāṅmukham, uttare dakṣiṇamukhaṃ vā, svastikādiṣu (paścime) paiśācābhyantare bāhye vā, brahmasūtrād uttare, śrīvatsādiṣu brahmapada-vāyavye pañcamūrtividhānena deveśaṃ viṣṇuṃ saṃsthāpayet. diśāsu vidikṣāsu ca, yathā dakṣiṇadṛṣṭyā grāmaṃ paśyet, tathā grāmābhyantare grāmābhimukhaṃ ca saṃsthāpayet. tadbahirdṛṣṭiḥ śubhadā, harapṛṣṭhaś-ca śubhadaḥ; sarvatra vārāha-nārasiṃha-vāmana-rāma-kṛṣṇān grāmaprekṣakāneva saṃsthāpayet. grāmādiṣu ṣaṭpañcāśad-dviśatapadeṣu paiśācaṃ parihṛtyaiva deveśaṃ saṃsthāpayet. anyeṣu paścime paiśācabāhye viṣṇorālayaṃ kuryāt; abhyantare tāntrikeṇa vidhinā arcayet na vaidikenā. pratiloma-antarāla-vrātyāvāseṣu naivaṃ viṣṇupratiṣṭhāṃ kārayet. śrīvatsādīnāṃ vitatha-bhṛṅgarāja-bhallāṭa-aditi-mahendreṣu nirgamaṃ kārayet. avihitapadanirgamaḥ, anyavāstusamprekṣaṇaṃ, grāmayajamānayor-āśu vināśāya bhavati.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Rules for Placements on the Grid:** 
    *   In a *Daṇḍaka* village layout, the temple of Lord Viṣṇu should be placed:
        *   In the West facing East, or in the North facing South.
    *   In a *Svastika* or other layout, it should be placed in the West, either inside or outside the outermost *Paiśāca* zone, North of the central axis, or in the North-West of the *Brahma-pada* under *Śrīvatsa* alignment, establishing the five forms (*pañcamūrti*).
    *   It should face the village. Shrines looking outwards (*bahir-dṛṣṭi*) or built behind Śiva's temple (*hara-pṛṣṭha*) are auspicious.
    *   Images of Varaha, Narasimha, Vamana, Rama, and Kṛṣṇa must face the village (*grāma-prekṣa*).
    *   In layouts of 256 squares, avoid building the temple in the outermost *Paiśāca* zone. In other cases, if built in the outer West *Paiśāca* zone, worship must be performed by Tantric rites and not Vedic rites.
    *   Never install Viṣṇu icons in settlements of mixed-caste, outcaste, or uninitiated populations (*vrātya*).
    *   The gateways should exit through specific zones (Vitatha, Bhringaraja, Bhallata, Aditi, or Mahendra). Exiting through unsanctioned zones or having the temple look directly onto another property causes the rapid destruction of the village and the patron.

### Section 4: Mathematical Calculation Formulas (Āyādi-nirūpaṇam)

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **आयादिनिरूपणम्**
> व्यासायामसमूहे अष्टगुणिते द्वादशभक्तावशिष्टम् आयं, त्रिगुणिते चतुर्दशभक्तावशिष्टं व्ययं, अष्टभक्तावशिष्टं योनिः, अष्टगुणिते सप्तविंशतिहृते फलं यच्छिष्टं ऋक्षं, नाहे त्रिंशद्धृते अवशिष्टं तिथिः, सप्तभक्तावशिष्टं वारं (व्ययाधिक-कोणयोनौ नवभिर्गुणिते सप्तभिर्हृतावशिष्टं वारं) चतुर्भिर्गुणिते नवभिर्हृतम् अंशकम्, राजयजमानयोः विपत्-प्रत्यरि-अन्त्य-वैनाशिक-शशाङ्काष्टम-अमर्त्य-असुर-ऋक्ष-योग-तिथिवारयोगदोषं क्रूरवारं च परिहृत्य रिक्ता-मध्यान्तरिक्षं कुर्यात्। क्रूरवार-रिक्तादयोऽपि सिद्धाऽमृतवारयोगाः शुभाः; कर्तृ-वास्तु-नक्षत्रयोः ऐक्ये मैत्रे च विपदादयः शुभा भवन्ति।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **āyādinirūpaṇam**
> vyāsāyāmasamūhe aṣṭaguṇite dvādaśabhaktāvaśiṣtam āyaṃ, triguṇite caturdaśabhaktāvaśiṣṭaṃ vyayaṃ, aṣṭabhaktāvaśiṣṭaṃ yoniḥ, aṣṭaguṇite saptaviṃśatihṛte phalaṃ yacchiṣṭaṃ ṛkṣaṃ, nāhe triṃśaddhṛte avaśiṣṭaṃ tithiḥ, saptabhaktāvaśiṣṭaṃ vāraṃ (vyayādhika-koṇayonau navabhirguṇite saptabhirhṛtāvaśiṣṭaṃ vāraṃ) caturbhirguṇite navabhirhṛtam aṃśakam, rājayajamānayoḥ vipat-pratyari-antya-vaināśika-śaśāṅkāṣṭama-amartya-asura-ṛkṣa-yoga-tithivārayogadoṣaṃ krūravāraṃ ca parihṛtya riktā-madhyāntarikṣaṃ kuryāt. krūravāra-riktādayo'pi siddhā'mṛtavārayogāḥ śubhāḥ; kartṛ-vāstu-nakṣatrayoḥ aikye maitre ca vipadādayaḥ śubhā bhavanti.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Formulas for Architectural Calculations (*Āyādi*):** 
    *   Take the sum of the width and length, and apply the following calculations:
        *   **Income (*Āya*):** Multiply by 8 and divide by 12; the remainder is the *Āya*.
        *   **Expenditure (*Vyaya*):** Multiply by 3 and divide by 14; the remainder is the *Vyaya*.
        *   **Source orientation (*Yoni*):** Divide by 8; the remainder is the *Yoni*.
        *   **Asterism (*Ṛkṣa*):** Multiply by 8 and divide by 27; the remainder is the Nakshatra/star.
        *   **Lunar Day (*Tithi*):** Take the perimeter (*nāha*), divide by 30; the remainder is the *Tithi*.
        *   **Week Day (*Vāra*):** Divide by 7; the remainder is the *Vāra* (or multiply the diagonal remainder by 9 and divide by 7).
        *   **Class/Fraction (*Aṃśaka*):** Multiply by 4 and divide by 9; the remainder is the *Aṃśaka*.
    *   Avoid configurations where the income is less than expenditure, or those resulting in inauspicious astrological associations (*vipat*, *pratyari*, *vaināśika*, etc.) for the king or patron.
    *   Even otherwise harsh days/tithis are acceptable if they form *Siddha* or *Amṛta* yogas, or if they are astrologically compatible (*maitra*) with the patron's star.

### Section 5: Residential Zoning and Tradesmen Placements (Arcakādisthānam)

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **गृहश्रेणीनिर्माणदेशः सहेतुकं पूजाविहीनग्रामे वासनिषेधः**
> वास्तुपुरुषमर्मसूत्रपातान् परिहृत्य, देवालय-प्राकारसंश्लिष्टां गृहश्रेणीं कुर्यात्। विष्णुपूजाविहीने विप्रो न जात्वपि वसेत्, तत्र तामसभावात्। तत्र ग्रामाभ्यन्तरे देवं संस्थाप्य, महतीं पूजां वैदिकेन विधिना एव कारयेत्, सौम्यत्वात् भुक्तिमुक्तिफलप्रदत्वाच्च तद्विधेः।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **gṛhaśreṇīnirmāṇadeśaḥ sahetukaṃ pūjāvihīnagrāme vāsaniṣedhaḥ**
> vāstupuruṣamarmasūtrapātān parihṛtya, devālaya-prākārasaṃśliṣṭāṃ gṛhaśreṇīṃ kuryāt. viṣṇupūjāvihīne vipro na jātvapi vaset, tatra tāmasabhāvāt. tatra grāmābhyantare devaṃ saṃsthāpya, mahatīṃ pūjāṃ vaidikenā vidhinā eva kārayet, saumyatvāt bhuktimukhipradatvācca tadvidheḥ.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Rules for Housing Rows & Purity:** 
    *   Avoiding the vital energy lines (*marma-sūtra*) of the *Vāstu Puruṣa*, rows of houses should be built aligned with the temple walls.
    *   A Brahmana must never reside in a village devoid of Viṣṇu worship, due to the presence of dark/ignorant (*tāmasa*) energies there. One should establish the deity in the center of the village and perform elaborate worship strictly according to Vedic rites, which is gentle and bestows both enjoyment and liberation.

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **अर्चकादीनां स्थानम्**
> तदर्चकानां तत्पार्श्वे स्थानं, नाभ्यैशान्यां सभास्थानम्, आग्नेय्यां गोष्ठागारं, नैर्ऋत्याम् आपणं, पैशाचबाह्ये प्राच्यां कुलाल-ऊर्ध्वनापित-अम्बष्ठानाम्, याम्ये तन्तुवाय-चक्रिणाम्, वारुणे क्रयविक्रयकारिणां वणिजाम्, सौम्ये द्विजभृत्यानां वादित्रजीविनां च, आग्नेय्यां क्रोशमात्रे तक्षकादीनां, नैर्ऋते गव्यूतिमात्रे चण्डालपक्कणम्, वायव्ये मृगव्याध-शाकुनिक-अधोनापितानाम्, पैशाचाद् बाह्यतः प्राकारपरिधेः ऐशान्यां नदीतीरे वा दूरे श्मशानं ग्रामायामसमं, वारुण्यां तटाकं त्रिदण्डसहस्रम् अर्धं तदर्धं वा, सेतुबन्धं दृढतरम् अत्युन्नतम् अनवच्छेद्यं, तत्र निर्झर-उपकुल्या-महाकुल्यादि-जलयन्त्राणि च सम्यक् प्रकल्पयेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **arcakādīnāṃ sthānam**

#### English Translation
*   **The Ritual of First Ploughing (*Karṣaṇa*):** 
    *   During the twelve months starting from *Mārgaśīrṣa* (Nov-Dec), under a favorable month, in the bright fortnight, and under the patron's star, one should determine the boundaries of the temple, halls, and enclosures (ranging from 3 to 59 cubits).
    *   Having cleared weeds, shrubs, and creepers, and leveled the ground, prepare the yoke and plough. The yoke should be of bamboo (12 spans long), the shaft of Khadira wood or Asana (5 spans), the plough-head of Sirisha wood (4 spans), and the whip proportioned appropriately.
    *   Using a threefold Munja grass rope, take two bulls (entirely white, red, or black—not mixed), healthy, with gilded horns and silver hooves.
    *   Accompanied by music, the Acharya, patron, and Vaishnavas enter the site, erect a shed in the West, invoke Vishvaksena facing East, the Sudarśana Disc in the South, and the Lord in the North on a grain altar.
    *   Offer bean-rice to Garuḍa and pure cooked rice to the others. Feed the bulls grass. 
    *   The Acharya, honored with garments and dakshina, yokes the bulls reciting *"rudraṃ manyum..."* and *"tryambakam..."* (Rigveda 7.59.12) and ploughs three furrows from West to East reciting Vaishnava mantras.

*   **Sowing of Seeds (*Bījāvāpa*):**
    *   Having decorated the ploughman, plough the whole area repeatedly and sow the grains: Shashtika rice (best), Priyangu (medium), or beans and mustard (least).
    *   Worship Soma, touch the seeds reciting the Viṣṇu Sūkta and *"somaṃ rājānam..."*, and sow from the South-West onwards. Sprinkle water using Varuṇa mantras.
    *   After the crops grow, worship Vishvaksena in the West, offer cooked food, bring cows, and present the harvested crops to the herd reciting the *Godāna* hymn (*"ā godānāt..."*).

> garbhālayapradeśe prāgantai-ruttarāntaiḥ pañcabhiḥ sūtraiḥ ṣoḍaśabhāgaṃ kṛtvā, tatsūtrasandhau īśānādi nāgaṃ bhūtaṃ yakṣaṃ durgāṃ ghoṭamukhīṃ dhātrīṃ vapuṣaṃ rākṣasaṃ jayaṃ kṛṣṇaṃ muruṇḍaṃ śivaṃ prāṇaṃ kapiṃ śakraṃ puruhūtaṃ vidyāṃ yaśasaṃ bhadrāṃ vedaśrutaṃ tāpasaṃ sindhuṣāṃ vidyām amitaṃ pāñcabhautikaṃ ca, eteṣām ekapātre droṇārdhaiḥ taṇḍulaiḥ caruṃ paktvā, tattan-nāmnā pūrvavad baliṃ dattvā, puṇyāhaṃ vācayet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **The Sixteen-Square Grid in the Sanctum Sanctorum:**
    *   In the sanctum area (*garbhālaya*), draw 5 lines in each direction to make a grid of 16 squares.
    *   At the intersections of the lines, worship the deities: Ishana, Naga, Bhuta, Yaksha, Durgā, Ghotamukhi, Dhatri, Vapus, Rakshasa, Jaya, Kṛṣṇa, Murunda, Śiva, Prana, Kapi, Shakra, Puruhuta, Vidya, Yashas, Bhadra, Vedashruta, Tapasa, Sindhuṣa, Vidya, Amita, and Pañcabhautika.
    *   Cook *caru* in a single pot using half a *droṇa* of rice, offer the *bali* in their names, and perform the *Puṇyāha* (purification) ritual.

#### Verse / Paragraph 3
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **अशक्तविषये बालालयं विनापि देवप्रतिष्ठाविधिः**
> अशक्तश्चेद् एवं शुद्धिं कृत्वा, तरुणालयम् (बालाल्यं) विना विमानं ध्रुवबेरं कौतुकाद्यं च कृत्वा, प्रतिष्ठां कारयेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **aśaktaviṣaye bālālayaṃ vināpi devapratiṣṭhāvidhiḥ**
> aśaktaśced evaṃ śuddhiṃ kṛtvā, taruṇālayaṃ vinā vimānaṃ dhruvaberaṃ kautukādyaṃ ca kṛtvā, pratiṣṭhāṃ kārayet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Ritual in Case of Inability:**
    *   If the patron is unable to build a temporary shrine (*bālālaya*/*taruṇālaya*), he may proceed with purification and directly consecrate the main temple, the fixed stone icon (*dhruva-bera*), and the worship icon (*kautuka*).
