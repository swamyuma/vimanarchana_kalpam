# Vimanarchana Kalpa of Sage Marīci (Vaikhānasa Āgama)
## Chapter 19: Sthānakādi-Dhruvabera-Vidhāna-Paṭalaḥ (Rules for Standing, Seated, and Reclining Immovable Icons)

This document contains the Sanskrit text, IAST transliteration, and English translation of the 19th Chapter (*Ekonaviṃśaḥ Paṭalaḥ*) of the **Vimānārcanākalpa** (attributed to Sage Marīci, representing the Vaikhānasa school of Vaishnavism).

This chapter discusses the rules for establishing the immovable icon (*dhruva-bera*), classifying them into standing (*sthānaka*), seated (*āsana*), and reclining (*śayana*) postures. Each posture is further divided into four categories: *yoga* (meditative), *bhoga* (experiential/majestic), *vīra* (heroic), and *ābhicārika* (protective/destructive). It details the architectural styles of the *vimāna* corresponding to each posture, the directions of the doors and the reclining head (*śiras*), the pedestal (*pīṭha*) and throne (*siṃhāsana*) designs, and the specific iconographic attributes, colors, and proportions of the Lord and His accompanying retinue of deities and sages (such as Brahmā, Śiva, Śrī Devī, Bhū Devī, Bhrigu, Markandeya, Garuḍa, and the personified weapons).

---

### Section 1: Classification and Vimāna Designs for Immovable Icons (Dhruvabera-trividhatva-vimāna-vidhiḥ)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **अथ एकोनविंशः पटलः**
> अथ ध्रुवबेर-विधिं वक्ष्ये - तत् त्रिविधं स्थानकम् आसनं शयनं च इति। पुनः तद् एकैकं चतुर्विधं स्यात् - योगं वीरं भोगम् आभिचारिकम् इति। योगार्थं योगमार्गं, भोगार्थं भोगं, वीर्यार्थं वीरमार्गं, शत्रुजयार्थम् आभिचारिकं कारयेत्। ग्रामाद् बाह्ये विविक्ते देशे, नदीतीरे, तत्-सङ्गमे, पर्वते, वने, वनपार्श्वे वा योगस्थानकं; तद्-बाह्ये वा वीरस्थानकं; वन-गिरि-जल-दुर्गे, राष्ट्रान्ते, शत्रु-दिङ्मुखे च आभिचारिकं भवति।
> ***atha ekonaviṃśaḥ paṭalaḥ***
> *atha dhruvabera-vidhiṃ vakṣye - tat trividhaṃ sthānakam āsanaṃ śayanaṃ ca iti. punaḥ tad ekaikaṃ caturvidhaṃ syāt - yogaṃ vīraṃ bhogām ābhicārikam iti. yogārthaṃ yogamārgaṃ, bhogārthaṃ bhogaṃ, vīryārthaṃ vīramārgaṃ, śatrujayārtham ābhicārikaṃ kārayet. grāmād bāhye vivikte deśe, nadītīre, tat-saṅgame, parvate, vane, vanapārśve vā yogasthānakaṃ; tad-bāhye vā vīrasthānakaṃ; vana-giri-jala-durge, rāṣṭrānte, śatru-diṅmukhe ca ābhicārikaṃ bhavati.*
> 
> विमानं - नलिनकं, विमलाकृति, सर्वतोभद्रं, नन्द्यावर्तं, श्रीप्रतिष्ठितं, स्वस्तिभद्रं, पद्माकारं, वृत्तगेहम्, उद्योगपद्मं, योगकान्तम् इति आदि समचतुरश्रम्, आयताश्रं, वृत्तं, वृत्तायतं **स्थानकस्य** (विमानं भवति)।
> *vimānaṃ - nalinakaṃ, vimalākṛti, sarvatobhadraṃ, nandyāvartaṃ, śrīpratiṣṭhitaṃ, svastibhadraṃ, padmākāraṃ, vṛttageham, udyogapadmaṃ, yogakāntam iti ādi samacaturaśram, āyatāśraṃ, vṛttaṃ, vṛttāyataṃ **sthānakasya** (vimānaṃ bhavati).*
> 
> अष्टाङ्गं, सर्वतोभद्रं, महापद्मं, वृत्तपद्मं, चतुर्मुखं, दिशास्वस्तिकं, महापद्मं, चतुष्पुटं, पर्वताकारं, खण्डहर्म्यं, उद्योगपद्मं, समचतुरश्रं, समवृत्तं वा **आसनस्य** (विमानं भवति)।
> *aṣṭāṅgaṃ, sarvatobhadraṃ, mahāpadmaṃ, vṛttapadmaṃ, caturmukhaṃ, diśāsvastikaṃ, mahāpadmaṃ, catuṣpuṭaṃ, parvatākāraṃ, khaṇdaharmyaṃ, udyogapadmaṃ, samacaturaśraṃ, samavṛttaṃ vā **āsanasya** (vimānaṃ bhavati).*
> 
> स्वस्तिकं, वृत्तभद्रं, वृत्तगेहम्, अवेदिकं, प्रेक्षागृहं, महाराजच्छन्दं, श्रीप्रतिष्ठितं, शयनयोगं, मातृखण्डं, गणिकाविशालम् एवम् आदीनि आयताश्राणि वर्तुलायतानि वा **शयनस्य** (विमानं भवति)।
> *svastikaṃ, vṛttabhadraṃ, vṛttageham, avedikaṃ, prekṣāgṛhaṃ, mahārājacchandaṃ, śrīpratiṣṭhitaṃ, śayanayogaṃ, mātṛkhaṇdaṃ, gaṇikāviśālam evam ādīni āyatāśrāṇi vartulāyatāni vā **śayanasya** (vimānaṃ bhavati).*
> 
> स्थानके विमाने स्थानक-बेरम्, आसने च आसनं, शयने शयनं, सर्वत्र स्थानकम् आसनं यानकं वा, शयने शयनं कर्तव्यम्; अन्ययोः शयनं नैव कल्पयेत्। शयनागार-द्वार-विस्तारं पादयोः अन्तरं तद्-धामोदयाष्टांशोनं द्वारोत्सेधम् एवम् आसनागारे स्थानके वा कारयेत् इत्येके।
> *sthānake vimāne sthānaka-beram, āsane ca āsanaṃ, śayane śayanaṃ, sarvatra sthānakam āsanaṃ yānakaṃ vā, śayane śayanaṃ kartavyam; anyayoḥ śayanaṃ naiva kalpayet. śayanāgāra-dvāra-vistāraṃ pādayoḥ antaraṃ tad-dhāmodeyāṣṭāṃśonaṃ dvārotsedham evam āsanāgāre sthānake vā kārayet ityeke.*
> 
> यानके सर्वविमानं ध्रुवबेरेषु इष्टं मानं विनिश्चित्य, सलक्षणं बेरं कृत्वा, गर्भगृहे कृते पदे दैविके स्थानकं यानकं च, किंचिन् मानुषम् आश्रित्य दैविके च आसनं, किंचिद् दैविकम् आश्रित्य मानुषांशे शयनं संस्थापयेत्। प्राग्द्वारं सर्व-सम्पत्करं
> *yānake sarvavimānaṃ dhruvabereṣu iṣṭaṃ mānaṃ viniścitya, salakṣaṇaṃ beraṃ kṛtvā, garbhagṛhe kṛte pade daivike sthānakaṃ yānakaṃ ca, kiñcin mānuṣam āśritya daivike ca āsanaṃ, kiñcid daivikam āśritya mānuṣāṃśe śayanaṃ saṃsthāpayet. prāgdvāraṃ sarva-sampatkaraṃ*

#### English Translation
*   **Rules for establishing the Immovable Icon (*Dhruva-bera-vidhiḥ*):**
    *   Now, I shall describe the rules for establishing the immovable icon (*dhruva-bera*). It is of three main types: standing (*sthānaka*), seated (*āsana*), and reclining (*śayana*).
    *   Furthermore, each of these three postures is divided into four classes: meditative/ascetic (*yoga*), heroic (*vīra*), experiential/majestic (*bhoga*), and protective/destructive (*ābhicārika*).
    *   Construct the temple according to the intended path: the *yoga* path for spiritual union, *bhoga* for experiencing wordly prosperity, *vīra* (or *vīrya*) for gaining strength and valor, and *ābhicārika* for victory over enemies.
    *   **Approved Locations for the Temples:**
        *   Establish the *yoga* temple outside the village limits in isolated places, on river banks, at the confluence of rivers (*saṅgame*), on mountains, in forests, or at the edges of forests.
        *   Establish the *vīra* temple outside the village limits.
        *   Establish the *ābhicārika* temple in forests, mountain caves, water fortresses, at the borders of the kingdom, or facing the direction of the enemy.
    *   **Architectural Designs of Vimānas for Standing Icons:**
        *   For standing icons (*sthānaka*), the appropriate vimana styles are Nalinaka, Vimalākṛti, Sarvatobhadra, Nandyāvarta, Śrīpratiṣṭhita, Swastibhadra, Padmākāra, Vṛttageha (circular), Udyogapadma, Yogakānta, etc., which can be square, rectangular, circular, or elliptical.
    *   **Vimānas for Seated Icons:**
        *   For seated icons (*āsana*), the appropriate vimanas are Aṣṭāṅga, Sarvatobhadra, Mahāpadma, Vṛttapadma, Caturmukha, Diśāsvastika, Mahāpadma, Catuṣpuṭa, Parvatākāra, Khaṇḍaharmya, Udyogapadma, and can be square or circular.
    *   **Vimānas for Reclining Icons:**
        *   For reclining icons (*śayana*), the appropriate vimanas are Swastika, Vṛttabhadra, Vṛttageha, Avedika, Prekṣāgṛha, Mahārājacchanda, Śrīpratiṣṭhita, Śayanayoga, Mātṛkhaṇḍa, Gaṇikāviśāla, etc., which are rectangular or oval/elliptical.
    *   **General Assembly and Installation Rules:**
        *   Establish a standing icon inside a standing-type vimana, a seated icon in a seated-type, and a reclining icon in a reclining-type. A standing, seated, or moving icon (*yānaka*) may be placed anywhere, but the reclining icon must only be placed in a reclining-type shrine; never place a reclining icon in the other two types of shrines.
        *   In a reclining shrine, make the door height one-eighth less than the total height of the shrine (*dhāmodaya*), and make the door width equal to the distance between the feet of the icon. Some say the same rule applies to the standing and seated shrines.
    *   **Installation in the Garbhagṛha Grid:**
        *   Determine the desired dimensions of the immovable icon according to the vimana's size. Divide the garbhagriha floor into grids.
        *   Install the standing icon and the moving icon (*yānaka*) in the *Daivika* zone.
        *   Install the seated icon in the *Daivika* zone, but extending slightly into the *Mānuṣa* zone (by placing its pedestal slightly forward).
        *   Install the reclining icon in the *Mānuṣa* zone, but extending slightly into the *Daivika* zone.
        *   A temple with an eastern door (*prāgdvāram*) brings all forms of prosperity.

### Section 2: Door Openings, Cardinal Directions, and Orientations (Dvāra-śiro-grahaṇa-vidhiḥ)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> पश्चिमद्वारं कीर्तिदं, दक्षिणद्वारं जयदं, उत्तरद्वारं ज्ञानदम्।
> *paścimadvāraṃ kīrtidaṃ, dakṣiṇadvāraṃ jayadaṃ, uttaradvāraṃ jñānadam.*
> 
> **शिरो-दिङ्-निर्णयः -**
> (शयने) प्राक्शीर्षकं शान्तिकं, पश्चिमशीर्षं पौष्टिकं, दक्षिणशीर्षं जयदं, उत्तरशीर्षम् आभिचारिकं (भवति)।
> ***śiro-diṅ-niṇayaḥ -***
> *(śayane) prākśīrṣakaṃ śāntikaṃ, paścimaśīrṣaṃ pauṣṭikaṃ, dakṣiṇaśīrṣaṃ jayadaṃ, uttaraśīrṣam ābhicārikaṃ (bhavati).*
> 
> ग्रामात् दक्षिणे शयानं (शयनबेरं) उत्तरद्वारं चेत् प्राक्शीर्षं; पश्चिमे प्राग्द्वारं चेत् दक्षिणशीर्षम्; उत्तरे दक्षिणद्वारं चेत् पश्चिमशीर्षं; प्राच्यां पश्चिमद्वारं चेत् दक्षिणशीर्षम् एव कारयेत्।
> *grāmād dakṣiṇe śayānaṃ (śayanaberaṃ) uttaradvāraṃ cet prākśīrṣaṃ; paścime prāgdvāraṃ cet dakṣiṇaśīrṣam; uttare dakṣiṇadvāraṃ cet paścimaśīrṣaṃ; prācyāṃ paścimadvāraṃ cet dakṣiṇaśīrṣam eva kārayet.*
> 
> स्थानक-आसन-शयनानि ग्रामप्रेक्षाणि एव कारयेत्।
> *sthānaka-āsana-śayanāni grāmaprekṣāṇi eva kārayet.*

#### English Translation
*   **Cardinal Directions and Orientations (*Dvāra-śiro-grahaṇa-vidhiḥ*):**
    *   A west-facing temple door brings fame (*kīrtidam*), a south-facing door brings victory (*jayadam*), and an north-facing door brings knowledge (*jñānadam*).
    *   **Direction of the Head (*Śiras*) for Reclining Icons:**
        *   Reclining with the head to the east (*prākśīrṣakam*) brings peace (*śāntikam*).
        *   Reclining with the head to the west (*paścimaśīrṣam*) brings nourishment and growth (*pauṣṭikam*).
        *   Reclining with the head to the south (*dakṣiṇaśīrṣam*) brings victory (*jayadam*).
        *   Reclining with the head to the north (*uttaraśīrṣam*) is for black-magic/destructive purposes (*ābhicārikam*).
    *   **Sanctum Orientation Combinations:**
        *   If the reclining deity is located south of the village and faces north (*uttaradvāram*), orient the head to the east.
        *   If located in the west and facing east (*prāgdvāram*), orient the head to the south.
        *   If located in the north and facing south (*dakṣiṇadvāram*), orient the head to the west.
        *   If located in the east and facing west (*paścimadvāram*), orient the head to the south.
        *   Always align the standing, seated, and reclining deity icons so that they look towards the village (*grāma-prekṣāṇi* - facing the village).

### Section 3: Standing Deity Icon Classifications (Sthānaka-tri-vidhatvam)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **योगस्थानक-त्रैविध्यम् -**
> देवेशं श्यामवर्णं चतुर्भुजं शङ्ख-चक्र-धरम् अभय-दक्षिण-हस्तं वरद-कट्यवलम्बित-वामहस्तं;
> ***yogasthānaka-trividhyam -***
> *deveśaṃ śyāmavarṇaṃ caturbhujaṃ śaṅkha-cakra-dharam abhaya-dakṣiṇa-hastaṃ varada-katyavalambita-vāmahastam;*
> 
> दक्षिणे एकजानुना समासीनं भृगुं, तथा मार्कण्डेयं, तथैव भृगु-मार्कण्डेयौ वा;
> *dakṣiṇe ekajānunā samāsīnaṃ bhṛguṃ, tathā mārkaṇḍeyaṃ, tathāiva bhṛgu-mārkaṇḍeyau vā;*
> 
> दक्षिणे भित्ति-पार्श्वे हेमाभं चतुर्भुजम् अक्षमाला-कमण्डलु-धर-अभय-कट्यवलम्बित-हस्तं स्थितं ब्रह्माणम् उत्तराभिमुखं; उत्तरे भित्तिमूले पार्श्वे श्वेताभं चतुर्भुजं मृग-परशु-धरम् अभय-कट्यवलम्बित-हस्तं शङ्करं दक्षिणाभिमुखं स्थितम् एव कारयेत्।
> *dakṣiṇe bhitti-pārśve hemābhaṃ caturbhujam akṣamālā-kamaṇḍalu-dhara-abhaya-katyavalambita-hastaṃ sthitaṃ brahmāṇam uttarābhimukhaṃ; uttare bhittimūle pārśve śvetābhaṃ caturbhujaṃ mṛga-paraśu-dharam abhaya-katyavalambita-hastaṃ śaṅkaraṃ dakṣiṇābhimukhaṃ sthitam eva kārayet.*
> 
> तद् **उत्तमं** योगस्थानकम्। ब्रह्म-शङ्कराभ्यां हीनं **मध्यमं**; पूजक-मुनिभ्यां हीनम् **अधमम्**॥
> *tad **uttamaṃ** yogasthānakam. brahma-śaṅkarābhyāṃ hīnaṃ **madhyamaṃ**; pūjakamunibhyāṃ hīnam **adhamam**.*
> 
> **भोगस्थानक-त्रैविध्यम् -**
> देवं चतुर्भुजं श्यामाभं शङ्ख-चक्र-धरम् अभय-वरद-दक्षिण-हस्तं कट्यवलम्बित-सिंहकर्ण-वामहस्तं;
> ***bhogasthānaka-trividhyam -***
> *devaṃ caturbhujaṃ śyāmābhaṃ śaṅkha-cakra-dharam abhaya-varada-dakṣiṇahastaṃ katyavalambita-siṃhakarṇa-vāmahastam;*
> 
> तथा देव्योः उभयोः - दक्षिणे श्रियं देवीं रुक्माभां सुस्थिर-दक्षिण-पादां कुञ्चित-वाम-पादां धृत-कमल-दक्षिण-हस्तां प्रसारित-वाम-करां देवं किंचित् समीक्ष्य स्थितां;
> *tathā devyor ubhayoḥ - dakṣiṇe śriyaṃ devīṃ rukmābhāṃ susthira-dakṣiṇa-pādāṃ kuñcita-vāma-pādāṃ dhṛta-kamala-dakṣiṇa-hastāṃ prasārita-vāma-karāṃ devaṃ kiñcit samīkṣya sthitāṃ;*
> 
> वामे हरिणीं (भूदेवीं) श्यामाभां प्रसारित-दक्षिण-हस्तां धृत-उत्पल-वाम-हस्तां संस्थित-वाम-पादां किंचित् कुञ्चित-दक्षिण-पादां देवं किंचित् समीक्ष्य स्थितां कारयेत्। (अथवा) देव्योः हस्त-पादौ विपरीतौ वा कारयेत्।
> *vāme hariṇīṃ (bhūdevīṃ) śyāmābhāṃ prasārita-dakṣiṇa-hastāṃ dhṛta-utpala-vāma-hastāṃ saṃsthita-vāma-pādāṃ kiñcit kuñcita-dakṣiṇa-pādāṃ devaṃ kiñcit samīkṣya sthitāṃ kārayet. (athavā) devyor hastapādau viparītau vā kārayet.*
> 
> कुड्य-कौतुकयोः मध्ये - दक्षिणे भृगुं दक्षिणेन जानुना आसीनं वामेना उत्कटिकम् आसीनं स-पद्म-वाम-हस्तं दक्षिण-ऊरु-निहित-दक्षिण-करं; वामे मार्कण्डेयं (पुण्यं) वामेन जानुना आसीनं दक्षिणेन उत्कटिकम् आसीनं स-पद्म-दक्षिण-हस्तं वाम-ऊरु-विन्यस्त-वाम-करं कारयेत्। एवम् आसयित्वा हस्तौ सम्पुटीकृतौ वा कारयेत्।
> *kuḍya-kautukayor madhye - dakṣiṇe bhṛguṃ dakṣiṇena jānunā āsīnaṃ vāmena utkaṭikam āsīnaṃ sa-padma-vāma-hastaṃ dakṣiṇa-ūru-nihita-dakṣiṇa-karam; vāme mārkaṇḍeyaṃ (puṇyaṃ) vāmena jānunā āsīnaṃ dakṣiṇena utkaṭikam āsīnaṃ sa-padma-dakṣiṇa-hastaṃ vāma-ūru-vinyasta-vāma-karaṃ kārayet. evam āsayitvā hastau sampuṭīkṛtau vā kārayet.*
> 
> भित्ति-ऊर्ध्व-भागे - मायां संह्लादिनीं च, व्यजनीभ्यां, तुम्बुरु-नारदाभ्यां, किन्नर-मिथुनाभ्यां, यक्ष-विद्याधराभ्यां, सनक-सनत्कुमाराभ्यां, सूर्य-चन्द्राभ्यां च युक्तम् **उत्तमं** भोगस्थानकम्।
> *bhitti-ūrdhva-bhāge - māyāṃ saṃhlādinīṃ ca, vyajanībhyāṃ, tumburu-nāradābhyāṃ, kinnara-mithunābyāṃ, yakṣa-vidyādharābyāṃ, sanaka-sanatkumārābyāṃ, sūrya-candrābyāṃ ca yuktam **uttamaṃ** bhogasthānakam.*
> 
> तुम्बुरु-नारदाभ्यां यक्ष-विद्याधराभ्यां च हीनं **मध्यमं**; सनक-सनत्कुमाराभ्याम् आदित्य-चन्द्राभ्यां पूजक-मुनिभ्यां च हीनम् **अधमम्**॥
> *tumburu-nāradābhyāṃ yakṣa-vidyādharābyāṃ ca hīnaṃ **madhyamaṃ**; sanaka-sanatkumārābyām āditya-candrābyāṃ pūjaka-munibhyāṃ ca hīnam **adhamam**.*
> 
> **वीरस्थानक-त्रैविध्यम् -**
> देवं श्यामाभं चतुर्भुजं शङ्ख-चक्र-धरं कारयेत्।
> ***vīrasthānaka-trividhyam -***
> *devaṃ śyāmāhaṃ caturbhujaṃ śaṅkha-cakra-dharaṃ kārayet.*
> 
> दक्षिण-वामयोः - ब्रह्म-ईशानाभ्यां, भृगु-मार्कण्डेयाभ्यां, किष्किन्ध-सुन्दराभ्यां, व्यजकाभ्यां, सनक-सनत्कुमाराभ्यां, सूर्य-चन्द्राभ्यां च युक्तम् **उत्तमं** वीरस्थानकम्।
> *dakṣiṇa-vāmayoḥ - brahma-īśānābhyāṃ, bhṛgu-mārkaṇḍeyābyāṃ, kiṣkindha-sundarābyāṃ, vyajakābyāṃ, sanaka-sanatkumārābyāṃ, sūrya-candrābyāṃ ca yuktam **uttamaṃ** vīrasthānakam.*
> 
> किष्किन्ध-सुन्दराभ्यां सनक-सनत्कुमाराभ्यां च हीनं **मध्यमं**; आदित्य-चन्द्राभ्यां पूजक-मुनिभ्यां च हीनम् **अधमम्**॥
> *kiṣkindha-sundarābyāṃ sanaka-sanatkumārābyāṃ ca hīnaṃ **madhyamaṃ**; āditya-candrābyāṃ pūjaka-munibhyāṃ ca hīnam **adhamam**.*
> 
> **आभिचारिक-स्थानकम् -**
> एवं द्विभुजं चतुर्भुजं वा धूम्रवर्णं श्यामाम्बरधरं शुष्काङ्गं तमोगुण-अन्वितं मूर्ध्वनेत्रं, ब्रह्मादि-देवैः विवर्जितं पैशाच-पदस्थम्, आर्द्रादि-अनुक्त-नक्षत्रे शर्वर्यां चरराशौ स्थापितं विमानं च लक्षणहीनं कारयेत्। एतद् आभिचारिक-स्थानकं स्यात्॥
> ***ābhicārika-sthānakam -***
> *evaṃ dvibhujaṃ caturbhujaṃ vā dhūmravarṇaṃ śyāmāmbaradharaṃ śuṣkāṅgaṃ tamoguṇa-anvitaṃ mūrdhvaneṭraṃ, brahmādi-devaiḥ vivarjitaṃ paiśāca-padastham, ārdrādi-anukta-nakṣatre śarvaryāṃ cararāśou stāphitaṃ vimānaṃ ca lakṣaṇahīnaṃ kārayet. etad ābhicārika-sthānakaṃ syāt.*

#### English Translation
*   **Standing Deity Icon Classifications (*Sthānaka-tri-vidhatvam*):**
    *   **1. Meditative Standing Icon (*Yogasthānaka*):**
        *   **Deity:** The Lord of Devas is dark-complexioned (*śyāmavarṇa*), four-armed, holding the conch and discus, with His right hand in the *abhaya* pose and His left hand in the *varada-katyavalambita* posture (resting on the hip in boon-giving pose).
        *   **Attendant Sages:** On His right side, Bhrigu and Markandeya kneel on one knee (*eka-jānunā samāsīnaṃ*).
        *   **Surrounding Deities:**
            *   On the right wall-side, place the golden-hued, four-armed Brahmā, holding a rosary (*akṣamālā*) and water-pot (*kamaṇḍalu*), with one hand in *abhaya* and the other in *katyavalambita*, standing and facing north.
            *   On the left wall-side, place the white-complexioned, four-armed Śiva (Shankara), holding a deer (*mṛga*) and axe (*paraśu*), with one hand in *abhaya* and the other in *katyavalambita*, standing and facing south.
        *   **Three Classes:** The assembly with all these features is **Uttama** (Superior). If Brahmā and Śiva are absent, it is **Madhyama** (Middling). If the worshipping sages (Bhrigu and Markandeya) are also absent, it is **Adhama** (Inferior).
    *   **2. Experiential/Majestic Standing Icon (*Bhogasthānaka*):**
        *   **Deity:** The Lord is four-armed, dark-hued, holding the conch and discus, with His right hand in *abhaya* or *varada* pose, and His left hand in the *katyavalambita-siṃhakarṇa* pose (resting on the hip).
        *   **Goddesses:**
            *   On the right is Śrī Devī, golden-hued (*rukmābhā*), standing with her right foot firmly placed and her left leg slightly bent, holding a lotus in her right hand, and her left hand hanging loose, looking slightly at the Lord.
            *   On the left is Hariṇī (Bhū Devī), dark-complexioned, standing with her left foot firmly placed and her right leg slightly bent, holding a blue water-lily (*utpala*) in her left hand, and her right hand hanging loose, looking slightly at the Lord. Alternatively, the postures of the hands and feet of the two Goddesses may be reversed.
        *   **Attendant Sages:** Between the wall and the icon, place Bhrigu on the right, kneeling on his right knee, with his left leg in *utkaṭika* posture, carrying a lotus in his left hand and his right hand resting on his right thigh. On the left, place Markandeya, kneeling on his left knee, with his right leg in *utkaṭika*, carrying a lotus in his right hand and his left hand resting on his left thigh. Alternatively, their hands may be held in the supplication gesture (*samputīkṛtau*).
        *   **Celestial Retinue:** On the upper wall, place Māyā and Saṃhlādinī (the two female energies), two fly-whisk bearers (*vyajanībhyām*), Tumburu and Nārada, a pair of Kinnaras, Yaksas and Vidyadharas, Sanaka and Sanatkumāra, and the Sun and Moon.
        *   **Three Classes:** The assembly with all these retinue figures is **Uttama**. Without Tumburu, Narada, Yaksas, and Vidyadharas, it is **Madhyama**. Without Sanaka, Sanatkumara, Sun, Moon, and the worshipping sages, it is **Adhama**.
    *   **3. Heroic Standing Icon (*Vīrasthānaka*):**
        *   **Deity:** The Lord is dark-hued, four-armed, holding the conch and discus.
        *   **Retinue:** On the right and left sides, place Brahmā and Śiva, Bhrigu and Markandeya, Kishkindha and Sundara (the gatekeepers/attendants), fly-whisk bearers, Sanaka and Sanatkumāra, and the Sun and Moon. This is the **Uttama** class of Virasthanaka.
        *   **Three Classes:** Without Kishkindha, Sundara, Sanaka, and Sanatkumara, it is **Madhyama**. Without the Sun, Moon, and the worshipping sages, it is **Adhama**.
    *   **4. Destructive/Protective Standing Icon (*Ābhicārikasthānaka*):**
        *   **Deity:** The Lord is two-armed or four-armed, smoky-hued (*dhūmravarṇa*), clad in dark garments, with emaciated limbs, possessing a wrathful disposition (*tamoguṇa-anvita*), and an upward-directed third eye (*mūrdhvaneṭram*).
        *   **Details:** The icon is carved without any attendant deities (no Goddesses, no sages) and is installed in the *Paiśāca* zone of the temple. The temple itself must be devoid of standard architectural embellishments (*lakṣaṇahīna*). The installation ceremony is performed on the dark eighth moon phase (*kṛṣṇāṣṭamī*), under inauspicious stars, at night, during a movable zodiac sign (*cararāśi*). This is the *Ābhicārika* standing style.

### Section 4: Pedestal Proportions for Standing Icons (Sthānaka-pīṭha-pramāṇam)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **पीठ-परिमाणम् -**
> स्थानकस्य पीठोच्चं - बेरोदयं द्वारोदयं वा अष्टभागं षड्भागं वा कृत्वा, एकांशं भुवङ्ग-समम्, अध्यर्धं द्विगुणं वा; समवृत्तं पद्माकारं देवपादं परितः चतुरङ्गुलं षडङ्गुलम् अष्टाङ्गुलं वा देहलब्ध-अङ्गुलेन नीत्वा, तदूर्ध्वं षोडश-दलैः युक्तं कारयेत्।
> ***pīṭha-parimāṇam -***
> *sthānakasya pīṭhoccaṃ - berodayaṃ dvārodayaṃ vā aṣṭabhāgaṃ ṣaḍbhāgaṃ vā kṛtvā, ekāṃśaṃ bhuvaṅga-samam, adhyardhaṃ dviguṇaṃ vā; samavṛttaṃ padmākāraṃ devapādaṃ paritaḥ caturaṅgulaṃ ṣaḍaṅgulam aṣṭāṅgulaṃ vā dehalabdha-aṅgulena nītvā, tadūrdhvaṃ ṣoḍaśa-dalaiḥ yuktaṃ kārayet.*
> 
> तस्मात् एकाङ्गुल-हीनं देव्योः पीठं कारयेत्।
> *tasmāt ekāṅgula-hīnaṃ devyoḥ pīṭhaṃ kārayet.*
> 
> अथवा कौतुकस्य अर्चापीठस्थ-पश्चिमभागं देव-मानुष-पैशाच-पदान्तं पीठोत्सेध-मानेन समस्थलं कृत्वा, तदूर्ध्वं देवीभ्यां सह देवं यथोक्तदेशे संस्थाप्य, तत्स्थले कौतुकादीन् संस्थापयेत्॥
> *athavā kautukasya arcāpīṭhastha-paścimabhāgaṃ deva-mānuṣa-paiśāca-padāntaṃ pīṭhotsedha-manena samasthalaṃ kṛtvā, tadūrdhvaṃ devībyāṃ saha devaṃ yathoktadeśe saṃsthāpya, tatsthale kautukādīn saṃsthāpayet.*

#### English Translation
*   **Pedestal Proportions for Standing Icons (*Sthānaka-pīṭha-pramāṇam*):**
    *   Determine the height of the standing deity's pedestal (*pīṭhoccam*) relative to either the height of the icon (*berodaya*) or the door height (*dvārodaya*): divide it into six or eight parts; one part represents the height of the plinth.
    *   Make the total pedestal height 1.5 or 2 times this plinth height.
    *   Make the pedestal circular (*samavṛttam*) or lotus-shaped (*padmākāram*), extending around the Lord's feet by four, six, or eight digits based on the icon's proportional units (*dehalabdha-aṅgula*). Adorn the upper portion with sixteen carved lotus petals (*ṣoḍaśa-dalaiḥ*).
    *   Make the pedestals of the two consort Goddesses one digit lower than the Lord's pedestal.
    *   Alternatively, level the western part of the pedestal of the worship-icon (*kautuka-bera*) extending through the *Deva*, *Mānuṣa*, and *Paiśāca* zones. Place the main deity together with the two Goddesses on this common platform in the prescribed location, and establish the smaller worship-icons (*kautuka*, *utsava*, *snapana*, *bali*) around them.

> दक्षिणे ब्रह्माणं मनुं च, वामे शङ्करं भृगुं च, रतिं वाणीं च, चामरधारिण्यौ च कारयेत्। सनक-सनत्कुमाराभ्यां तुम्बुरु-नारदाभ्यां च सहितम् **उत्तमं** वीरासनम्।
> *dakṣiṇe brahmāṇam manuṃ ca, vāme śaṅkaraṃ bhṛguṃ ca, ratiṃ vāṇīṃ ca, cāmara-dhāriṇyau ca kārayet. sanaka-sanatkumārabhyāṃ tumburu-nāradābhyāṃ ca sahitam **uttamaṃ** vīrāsanam.*
> 
> तुम्बुरु-नारदाभ्यां सनक-सनत्कुमाराभ्यां व्यजनीभ्यां च हीनं **मध्यमं**; ब्रह्म-शङ्कराभ्यां सूर्य-चन्द्राभ्यां पूजक-स्थाने देवीभ्यां च हीनम् **अधमम्**॥
> *tumburu-nāradābhyāṃ sanaka-sanatkumārabhyāṃ vyajanībhyāṃ ca hīnaṃ **madhyamaṃ**; brahma-śaṅkarābyāṃ sūrya-candrābyāṃ pūjaka-sthāne devībyāṃ ca hīnam **adhamam**.*
> 
> **आभिचारिकासन-त्रैविध्यम् -**
> देवं वेदिकासने समासीनं चतुर्भुजं द्विभुजं वा नीलाभं श्यामवस्त्रधरं तमोगुण-अन्वितं मूर्ध्वनेत्रं, देव्यादीन् विना लक्षणहीने विमाने शत्रुदिङ्मुखे, कृष्णाष्टम्याम् अग्न्यादि-अनुक्त-नक्षत्रे शर्वर्यां चरराशौ गर्भाळये पैशाच-पदे स्थापयेत्। एतद् आभिचारिकासनं भवति॥
> ***ābhicārikāsana-trividhyam -***
> *devaṃ vedikāsane samāsīnaṃ caturbhujaṃ dvibhujaṃ vā nīlābhaṃ śyāmavastradharaṃ tamoguṇa-anvītam mūrdhvaneṭraṃ, devyādīn vinā lakṣaṇahīne vimāne śatrudiṅmukhe, kṛṣṇāṣṭamyām agnyādi-anukta-nakṣatre śarvaryāṃ cararāśou garbhālaye paiśāca-pade sthāpayet. etad ābhicārikāsanaṃ bhavati.*

#### English Translation
*   **Seated Deity Icon Classifications (*Āsana-tri-vidhatvam*):**
    *   **1. Meditative Seated Icon (*Yogāsana*):**
        *   **Deity:** The Lord is white-complexioned (*śvetābha*), four-armed, wearing yellow garments, with a matted-hair crown (*jaṭāmukuṭa*), seated on a white lotus pedestal. His right leg is placed upward (resting on the left thigh) and His left leg is resting below. His left hand is placed on His lap, and His upper hands are carved without the conch and discus. He wears a white upper garment (*śveta-vastra-uttarīya*), a sacred thread, earrings, armlets, necklaces, and has half-closed eyes.
        *   **Retinue:** On the right wall-side is Brahmā, on the left Shankara (Śiva). On the upper wall are the Sun and Moon, and Sanaka and Sanatkumāra. At the feet (in front of the pedestal) are Bhrigu and Markandeya. This is **Uttama** Yogāsana.
        *   **Classes:** Without Sun, Moon, Sanaka, and Sanatkumara, it is **Madhyama**. Without the two worshipping sages (Bhrigu and Markandeya), it is **Adhama**.
    *   **2. Experiential/Majestic Seated Icon (*Bhogāsana*):**
        *   **Deity:** The Lord is seated on a throne (*siṃhāsana*), dark-bodied, four-armed, holding the conch and discus, with His right hand in *abhaya* or *varada* pose and His left hand in the *siṃhakarṇa* pose resting on His lap or hip.         *   **Goddesses:**
            *   On the right is Śrī Devī, seated on the throne, with her left leg folded and her right leg extended downward.
            *   On the left is Bhū Devī, seated on the throne, with her right leg folded and her left leg extended downward. Their inner hands hold a lotus and a blue lily respectively, while their outer hands rest on the throne in *siṃhakarṇa* pose (or vice versa).
        *   **Measurements:** The distance between the Lord and the Goddesses is 1 span (*tāla*) and 2 barley-corns (*yava*), which may be increased or decreased by 1/4.
        *   **Retinue:** On the right and left sides are Brahmā and Śiva, and Bhrigu and Markandeya kneeling on one knee. All other details are same as the standing *Bhoga* class. On the upper wall are Maya and Samhladini, fly-whisk bearers, Tumburu and Narada, Kinnara couples, Yaksas, Vidyadharas, Sanaka, Sanatkumara, Sun, and Moon. This is **Uttama** Bhogāsana.
        *   **Classes:** Without Tumburu, Narada, Kinnaras, Yaksas, and Vidyadharas, it is **Madhyama**. Without Sanaka, Sanatkumara, Sun, Moon, and the worshipping sages, it is **Adhama**.
    *   **3. Heroic Seated Icon (*Vīrāsana*):**
        *   **Deity:** On a seat placed on the throne (*siṃhāsanopari sthitāsane*), the Lord is seated with His left leg folded and His right leg slightly extended, four-armed, holding the conch and discus, with His right hand in *abhaya* pose and His left hand in *siṃhakarṇa* pose. He is coral-red-complexioned (*pravālābha*) and clad in dark garments.
        *   **Goddesses:** At the worshipping position, Śrī Devī on the right and Bhū Devī on the left are seated, kneeling on one knee.
        *   **Retinue:** On the right are Brahmā and Manu; on the left are Śiva (Shankara) and Bhrigu. Place Rati and Vāṇī (Saraswati) carrying fly-whisks. Together with Sanaka, Sanatkumara, Tumburu, and Narada, this is **Uttama** Vīrāsana.
        *   **Classes:** Without Tumburu, Narada, Sanaka, Sanatkumara, and fly-whisk bearers, it is **Madhyama**. Without Brahmā, Śiva, Sun, Moon, worshipping sages, and Goddesses, it is **Adhama**.
    *   **4. Destructive/Protective Seated Icon (*Ābhicārikāsana*):**
        *   **Deity:** The Lord is seated on a simple pedestal (*vedikāsane*), four-armed or two-armed, blue-complexioned, clad in dark garments, with a wrathful disposition, and a third eye.
        *   **Details:** Carved without Goddesses or attendant deities, in a plain, featureless temple facing the enemy's direction, installed in the *Paiśāca* zone on a dark eighth moon phase, under inauspicious stars, at night, during a movable zodiac sign. This is the *Ābhicārika* seated style.

        *   Divide this height into 6 parts: 1 part for the base ledge (*kampa*), 3 parts for the downward-facing lotus petals (*adhodalam*), and 2 parts for the upward-facing lotus petals (*ūrdhvadalam*). It should be carved with a hundred petals facing up and down.

            *   1 part: petal profile (*dala*)
            *   3 parts: torus moulding (*kumuda*)
            *   1 part: water-lily moulding (*ambuja*)
            *   1 part: ledge (*kampa*)
            *   4 parts: neck (*kaṇṭha*) adorned with lion carvings
            *   1 part: upper ledge (*kampa*)
            *   1 part: upper lotus profile (*padma*)
            *   2 parts: large top fillet (*mahāpaṭṭikā*).
        *   This assembly constitutes the *Padmasiṃhāsana*.
    *   **4. Auspicious Seat (*Bhadrāsanam*):**

            *   1 part: ledge (*kampa*)
            *   4 parts: neck (*kaṇṭha*) with lion carvings
            *   1 part: upper ledge (*kampa*)
            *   2 parts: top fillet (*mahāpaṭṭikā*).
        *   This assembly is *Bhadrāsana*. Carve two supporting lions on both sides.
        *   **Plumb-line Alignment:** Drop straight vertical plumb-lines (*sūtram*) touching the knees, the armpits, the sides of the knees, and the mid-points of the arms of the seated deity. Any detail not mentioned here must be executed as per the *Śilpaśāstra*.

### Section 7: Reclining Deity Icon Classifications and Serpent-Bed Proportions (Śayana-tri-vidhatva-anantaśayana-lakṣaṇam)

> *śiraḥ-pārśve - śriyaṃ devīṃ devasya bāhu-saṃspṛśāṃ sa-padma-dakṣiṇa-hastāṃ kaṭi-nyasta-anya-hastāṃ; pādapārśve - mahīṃ devīṃ devasya vāmāṅghri-saṃspṛśāṃ dhṛta-utpala-dakṣiṇa-hastāṃ kaṭi-nyasta-vāma-hastāṃ.*
> 
> कौतुकात् - दक्षिणे मार्कण्डेयं वामे भृगुं; तद्दक्षिणे भित्ति-पार्श्वे च आसीनं ब्रह्माणं वामे शङ्करं च, दक्षिणे वक्रतुण्डं, वामे विन्ध्यवासिनीं; पादपार्श्वे - मधुकैटभौ उग्रवेग-समन्वितौ समुद्र-तरङ्ग-आच्छन्न-जानू अनन्त-विष-ज्वालाभिः दह्यमानौ च कारयेत्।
> *kautukāt - dakṣiṇe mārkaṇḍeyaṃ vāme bhṛguṃ; taddakṣiṇe bhitti-pārśve ca āsīnaṃ brahmāṇaṃ vāme śaṅkaraṃ ca, dakṣiṇe vakratuṇḍaṃ, vāme vindhyavāsinīṃ; pādapārśve - madhukaiṭabhou ugravega-samanvitou samudra-taraṅga-ācchanna-jānū ananta-viṣa-jvālābhiḥ dahyamānou ca kārayet.*
> 
> नाभिदेशे - पद्मे समासीनं ब्रह्माणं; तद्दक्षिणे - पञ्चायुधान् गरुडम्; उपरिष्टात् दक्षिणे - भास्करं, तद्दक्षिणे - निशाकरं, तथा बालरूपिणौ तुम्बुरु-नारदौ, तद्दक्षिणे - अश्विनौ, अष्टलोकपालान्, तथा अप्सरसः च कारयेत्। एतद् **उत्तमं** भोगशयनम्।
> *nābhideśe - padme samāsīnaṃ brahmāṇaṃ; taddakṣiṇe - pañcāyudhān garudam; upariṣṭāt dakṣiṇe - bhāskaraṃ, taddakṣiṇe - niśākaraṃ, tathā bālarūpiṇou tumburu-nāradou, taddakṣiṇe - aśvinou, aṣṭalokapālān, tathā apsarasaḥ ca kārayet. etad **uttamaṃ** bhogaśayanam.*
> 
> तुम्बुरु-नारदाभ्याम् अश्विनोः लोकपालैः च विना **मध्यमं**; पूजक-मुनिभ्याम् अप्सरोभिश्च विना **अधमम्**॥
> *tumburu-nāradābhyām aśvinoḥ lokapālaiḥ ca vinā **madhyamaṃ**; pūjaka-munibhyām apsarobhiś ca vinā **adhamam**.*
> 
> **वीरशयन-त्रैविध्यम् -**
> देवं श्यामाभं चतुर्भुजं शङ्ख-चक्र-धरं सव्यहस्तं शिरोपधानं कृत्वा वामहस्तं प्रसार्य शयानं;
> ***vīrasayana-trividhyam -***
> *devaṃ śyāmābhaṃ caturbhujaṃ śaṅkha-cakra-dharaṃ savyahastaṃ śiropadhānaṃ kṛtvā vāmahastaṃ prasārya śayānaṃ;*
> 
> पादपार्श्वे - श्री-भूमिभ्यां पादमर्दिनीभ्यां, मधुकैटभाभ्यां च संयुक्तं;
> *pādapārśve - śrī-bhūmibhyāṃ pādamardinībhyāṃ, madhukaiṭabhābhyāṃ ca saṃyuktaṃ;*
> 
> कौतुकात् दक्षिणे मार्कण्डेयं वामे भृगुं; तद्दक्षिणे भित्ति-पार्श्वे च नाभि-पद्मे समासीनं ब्रह्माणं, पञ्चायुधान् गरुडं, चन्द्रादित्यौ, सप्तर्षीन्, द्वादशादित्यान्, एकादशरुद्रान्, जयादि-अप्सरसः, तुम्बुरु-नारदौ, सनक-सनत्कुमारौ, ब्रह्मेशौ च कारयेत्। एतद् **उत्तमं** वीरशयनम्।
> *kautukāt dakṣiṇe mārkaṇḍeyaṃ vāme bhṛguṃ; taddakṣiṇe bhitti-pārśve ca nābhi-padme samāsīnaṃ brahmāṇaṃ, pañcāyudhān garudaṃ, candrādityou, saptarṣīn, dvādaśādityān, ekādaśarudrān, jayādy-apsarasaḥ, tumburu-nāradou, sanaka-sanatkumārou, brahmeśou ca kārayet. etad **uttamaṃ** vīraśayanam.*
> 
> रुद्र-आदित्य-अप्सरोभिः च विना **मध्यमं**; किन्नर-मिथुनाभ्यां सनक-सनत्कुमाराभ्यां पूजक-मुनिभ्यां च हीनम् **अधमम्**॥
> *rudra-āditya-apsarobhiḥ ca vinā **madhyamaṃ**; kinnara-mithunābhyāṃ sanaka-sanatkumārabhyāṃ pūjaka-munibhyāṃ ca hīnam **adhamam**.*
> 
> **आभिचारिक-शयनम् -**
> शेषं (अनन्त-तल्पं) लक्षणहीनं द्विफणं द्विवलयम् अनुन्नत-शिरः-पार्श्वं;
> ***ābhicārika-śayanam -***
> *śeṣaṃ (ananta-talpaṃ) lakṣaṇahīnaṃ dviphaṇaṃ dvivalayam anunnata-śiraḥ-pārśvaṃ;*
> 
> देवं नीलाभं द्विभुजं चतुर्भुजं वा समशयनं महानिद्रा-समायुक्तं शुष्कवक्त्रं शुष्काङ्गं श्यामवस्त्रधरं सर्वदेवैः विहीनं कारयेत्।
> *devaṃ nīlābhaṃ dvibhujaṃ caturbhujaṃ vā samaśayanaṃ mahānidrā-samāyuktaṃ śuṣkavaktraṃ śuṣkāṅgaṃ śyāmavastradharaṃ sarvadevaiḥ vihīnaṃ kārayet.*
> 
> एतद् **उत्तमम्** आभिचारिक-शयनं। एकफणम् एकवलयं **मध्यमं**; सर्पदेहं विना समस्थल-शयनम् **अधमम्**॥
> *etad **uttamam** ābhicārika-śayanam. ekaphaṇam ekavalayaṃ **madhyamaṃ**; sarpadehaṃ vinā samasthala-śayanam **adhamam**.*
> 
> **अनन्तशयन-लक्षणम् -**
> अनन्तस्य उत्सेधं - बेरायामं द्वारोत्सेधं वा त्रिधा कृत्वा, एकांशं मध्यमं, तद्-अष्टांश-अधिकम् उत्तमं, तद्-अष्टांशोनम् अधमं;
> ***anantaśayana-lakṣaṇam -***
> *anantasya utsedhaṃ - berāyāmaṃ dvārotsedhaṃ vā tridhā kṛtvā, ekāṃśaṃ madhyamaṃ, tad-aṣṭāṃśa-adhikam uttamaṃ, tad-aṣṭāṃśonam adhamam;*
> 
> (अथवा अङ्गुल-मानेन) षट्त्रिंशदङ्गुलम् उत्तमं, त्रिंशदङ्गुलं मध्यमं, चतुर्विंशत्यङ्गुलम् अधमं - शिलालये शिलाभिः, मृण्मये इष्टकाभिः कुर्यात्।
> *(athavā aṅgula-mānena) ṣat-triṃśadaṅgulam uttamaṃ, triṃśadaṅgulaṃ madhyamaṃ, caturviṃśatyaṅgulam adhamaṃ - śilālaye śilābhiḥ, mṛṇmaye iṣṭakābhiḥ kuryāt.*
> 
> तस्य उत्सेधम् अष्टधा कृत्वा - द्विभागं शिरोभागोच्चं, तन्-मध्यानतं त्र्यङ्गुलम् एवं **योगशयनस्य** कारयेत्।
> *tasya utsedham aṣṭadhā kṛtvā - dvibhāgaṃ śirobhāgoccaṃ, tan-madhyānataṃ tryaṅgulam evaṃ **yogaśayanasya** kārayet.*
> 
> उत्सेधं षड्भागं कृत्वा - द्विभागं शिरोभागोच्चं, तन्-मध्यानतं पञ्चाङ्गुलम् एवं **भोगशयनस्य** कारयेत्।
> *utsedhaṃ ṣadbhāgaṃ kṛtvā - dvibhāgaṃ śirobhāgoccaṃ, tan-madhyānataṃ pañcāṅgulam evaṃ **bhogaśayanasya** kārayet.*
> 
> उत्सेधं सप्तधा कृत्वा - द्विभागं शिरोभागोच्चं, तन्-मध्यानतं चतुरङ्गुलम् एवं **वीरशयनस्य** कारयेत्।
> *utsedhaṃ saptadhā kṛtvā - dvibhāgaṃ śirobhāgoccaṃ, tan-madhyānataṃ caturaṅgulam evaṃ **vīraśayanasya** kārayet.*
> 
> त्रिपञ्चसप्त-वलयैः तल्पीकृतं स्यात्। फणान् सप्त पञ्च नव च हेमाभान् समाहितान् अधस्तात् श्वेतवर्णं मकुटोपरि संछाद्य स्थितान् व्याल-सिं-मुखान् भृङ्गमध्य-शिखामणिं च कुर्यात्।
> *tri-pañca-sapta-valayaiḥ talpīkṛtaṃ syāt. phaṇān sapta pañca nava ca hemābhān samāhitān adhastāt śvetavarṇaṃ makuṭopari sañchādya sthitān vyāla-siṃha-mukhān bhṛṅgamadhya-śikhāmaṇiṃ ca kuryāt.*
> 
> तेषाम् उत्सेधं शयनोत्सेधम् अनन्तस्य व्यासः देवम् उभयोः पार्श्वयोः पादे च यथोचितं स्यात् एवं शयनं परिकल्पयेत्॥
> *teṣām utsedhaṃ śayanotsedham anantasya vyāsaḥ devam ubhoyoḥ pārśvayoḥ pāde ca yathocitaṃ syāt evaṃ śayanaṃ parikalpayet.*

#### English Translation
*   **Reclining Deity Icon Classifications (*Śayana-tri-vidhatvam*):**
    *   **1. Meditative Reclining Icon (*Yogaśayana*):**
        *   **Deity:** The Lord is two-armed, reclining halfway (*ardhādhaḥ-śayānaṃ* - slightly elevated), yellow-dark-complexioned. His right hand rests on the pillow (*upadhāne*), raising the top of His crown slightly and extending fully. His left hand is slightly bent at the elbow, raised up, and either rests on His hip or is extended along His thigh. His left leg is slightly folded and His right leg is extended. He is adorned with all ornaments, with eyes slightly open, worshipped at His feet by Bhrigu and Markandeya.
        *   **Retinue:** Near His feet are Madhu and Kaiṭabha. Emerging from His navel-lotus is Brahmā. Around are the five personified weapons (*pañcāyudhān*), Garuḍa, Viṣvaksena, and the seven sages (*saptarṣīn*) standing on the upper wall with folded hands in supplication. On the right wall-side is Brahmā and on the left Śiva, both seated. This is **Uttama** Yogaśayana.
        *   **Classes:** Without the seven sages and Visvaksena, it is **Madhyama**. Without the two worshipping sages and Madhu-Kaitabha, it is **Adhama**.
    *   **2. Experiential/Majestic Reclining Icon (*Bhogaśayana*):**
        *   **Deity:** The Lord is handsome, well-nourished, four-armed or two-armed, reclining halfway on the serpent-bed, with His body gracefully curved like a bow. His right hand rests on the pillow, supporting His crown. His left hand is either slightly extended or rests on His left thigh. His right leg is extended and His left leg is folded.
        *   **Measurements:** The distance between the feet is 20 digits, the distance between the heels is 1 part, the distance between the knees is 14 digits, and the face is turned 3 digits to the right from the central line (*brahmasūtram*).
        *   **Goddesses:**
            *   Near His head is Śrī Devī, touching the Lord's shoulder, holding a lotus in her right hand, and her other hand resting on her hip.
            *   Near His feet is Bhū Devī, touching the Lord's left foot, holding a blue lily in her right hand, and her left hand resting on her hip.
        *   **Retinue:** At the worshipping position are Markandeya on the right and Bhrigu on the left. On the right wall-side is Brahmā, on the left Śiva. On the right side is Gaṇeśa (*vakratuṇḍam*), on the left Durgā (*vindhyavāsinīṃ*). Near the feet are Madhu and Kaitabha, depicted with fierce speed, with their knees covered by sea waves, appearing to be consumed by the poison-flames of the serpent Ananta.
        *   **Upper Register:** Emerging from His navel-lotus is Brahmā. On His right are the five weapons and Garuḍa. Above on the right is the Sun, on the left the Moon. Also place Tumburu and Narada as young boys, the Ashwins, the eight world protectors (*aṣṭalokapālān*), and Apsaras. This is **Uttama** Bhogaśayana.
        *   **Classes:** Without Tumburu, Narada, Ashwins, and the world-protectors, it is **Madhyama**. Without the worshipping sages and Apsaras, it is **Adhama**.
    *   **3. Heroic Reclining Icon (*Vīraśayana*):**
        *   **Deity:** The Lord is dark-hued, four-armed, holding the conch and discus, using His right arm as a pillow and His left arm extended fully.
        *   **Goddesses:** At His feet are Śrī Devī and Bhū Devī, massaging His feet (*pādamardinībhyāṃ*).
        *   **Retinue:** Together with Madhu and Kaitabha, Markandeya on the right and Bhrigu on the left; on the right wall-side is Brahmā seated on His navel-lotus, the five weapons, Garuḍa, Sun and Moon, the seven sages, the twelve Adityas, the eleven Rudras, Apsaras including Jaya, Tumburu and Narada, Sanaka and Sanatkumara, and Brahmā and Śiva. This is **Uttama** Vīraśayana.
        *   **Classes:** Without the Rudras, Adityas, and Apsaras, it is **Madhyama**. Without the Kinnaras, Sanaka, Sanatkumara, and worshipping sages, it is **Adhama**.
    *   **4. Destructive/Protective Reclining Icon (*Ābhicārika-śayanam*):**
        *   **Deity:** The serpent-bed is devoid of auspicious signs, having only two hoods (*dviphaṇam*) and two coils (*dvivalayam*), with its head and sides un-elevated. The Lord is blue-complexioned, two-armed or four-armed, reclining flat, in deep sleep (*mahānidrā*), with dry mouth and emaciated limbs, clad in dark garments, and completely devoid of any accompanying deities.
        *   **Classes:** This is **Uttama** Abhicarikasayana. If it has one hood and one coil, it is **Madhyama**. If it is without the serpent-body, reclining on a flat ground, it is **Adhama**.
    *   **Serpent-Bed Proportions (*Anantaśayana-lakṣaṇam*):**
        *   Make the height of the serpent-bed (*anantasya utsedham*) relative to either the icon's length or the door height: divide it into 3 parts; 1 part represents the **Madhyama** height. One-eighth more is **Uttama**, and one-eighth less is **Adhama**.
        *   Alternatively, in digits: 36 digits is **Uttama**, 30 digits is **Madhyama**, and 24 digits is **Adhama**. For stone temples (*śilālaye*), make it of stone; for clay temples (*mṛṇmaye*), make it of bricks.
        *   **Inclination of the Head-rest:**
            *   For *Yogaśayana*, divide the total height of the bed into 8 parts: raise the head-rest by 2 parts, and slope the middle portion downward by 3 digits.
            *   For *Bhogaśayana*, divide the height into 6 parts: raise the head-rest by 2 parts, and slope the middle portion by 5 digits.
            *   For *Vīraśayana*, divide the height into 7 parts: raise the head-rest by 2 parts, and slope the middle portion by 4 digits.
        *   Construct the serpent-bed with three, five, or seven coils (*tri-pañca-sapta-valayaiḥ*). Carve the hoods as five, seven, or nine in number, golden-hued, white on the underside, sheltering the Lord's crown. The mouths should be depicted as dragon-like (*vyāla*) or lion-like (*siṃhamukhān*), with a crest-jewel (*śikhāmaṇi*) in the middle. The height, width, and placement of the serpent hoods should be aligned proportionally with the Lord's body.

### Section 8: Placement and Proportions of Brahmā and Attendant Deities (Brahmādi-sthana-pramāṇa-vidhiḥ)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **ब्रह्मादीनां स्थानप्रमाण-वर्णादि-निर्णयः -**
> (देवस्य) नाभिसूत्रात् दक्षिणे चोपरिष्टात् हस्तमात्रे, भित्तिम् आश्रित्य नाभ्यम्बुजं स्यात्। तद्विस्तारम् अष्टादशाङ्गुलं, तदष्टांशोनम् ऊर्ध्वदल-विशालम्, अधोदलनतं चतुरङ्गुलं, ऊर्ध्वदळनतं त्रिमात्रं, कर्णिकायुतम्, ऊर्ध्वाधः शतपत्रैः संयुक्तम्। नालं मध्य-अर्धाङ्गुल-परिणाहं; नालस्य दक्षिणे तद्दक्षिणे च बाल-अबाल-पर्णयुतं; बालपर्णस्य विशालं तत्पद्मोर्ध्वदल-विशाल-समम्; इतरपरपर्ण-विशालम् अधोदल-विस्तृत-समम्; तयोः नालम् एकाङ्गुल-परिणाहं कुर्यात्।
> ***brahmādīnāṃ sthānapramāṇa-varṇādi-niṇayaḥ -***
> *(devasya) nābhisūtrāt dakṣiṇe copariṣṭāt hastamātre, bhittim āśritya nābhyambujaṃ syāt. tadvistāram aṣṭādaśa-aṅgulaṃ, tad-aṣṭāṃśonam ūrdhvadala-viśālam, adhodalanataṃ caturaṅgulaṃ, ūrdhvadalanataṃ trimātraṃ, karṇikāyutam, ūrdhvādhaḥ śatapatraiḥ saṃyuktam. nālaṃ madhya-ardha-aṅgula-pariṇāham; nālasya dakṣiṇe taddakṣiṇe ca bāla-abāla-parṇayutaṃ; bālaparṇasya viśālaṃ tatpadmordhvadala-viśāla-samam; itaraparaparṇa-viśālam adhodala-vistṛta-samam; tayoḥ nālam ekāṅgula-pariṇāhaṃ kuryāt.*
> 
> तत्-पद्म-कर्णिकामध्ये - ब्रह्माणं चतुर्मुखं जटामुकुटधरं हिरण्यवर्णं पुष्पाम्बरधरं सर्वाभरण-भूषितम् उत्तरीयोपवीताभ्यां युक्तं ब्राह्मासने समासीनम्, अक्षमाला-कमण्डलु-धरं, दक्षिणम् आलोड्यकरं वामं कटिविन्यस्तं, चतुर्हस्ताभ्यां प्राञ्जलीकृत्य वा समासीनम्, अथवा स्वाङ्के वाम-अवाम-करौ प्रसृतौ कारयेत्॥
> *tat-padma-karṇika-madhye - brahmāṇaṃ caturmukhaṃ jaṭāmukuṭadharaṃ hiraṇyavarṇaṃ puṣpāmbaradharaṃ sarvābharaṇa-bhūṣitam uttarīyopavītābyāṃ yuktaṃ brāhmāsane samāsīnam, akṣamālā-kamaṇḍalu-dharaṃ, dakṣiṇam āloḍyakaraṃ vāmaṃ kaṭvinyastaṃ, caturbhujābyāṃ prāñjalīkṛtya vā samāsīnam, athavā svāṅke vāma-avāma-karou prasṛtou kārayet.*
> 
> ध्रुवबेरायामस्य दशांश-एकांशं तालं, तस्य आदित्य-एकांशम् (द्वादशभागम्) अङ्गुलं, तेन ब्रह्मादि-देवान् कारयेत्।
> *dhruvaberāyāmasya daśāṃśa-ekāṃśaṃ tālaṃ, tasya āditya-ekāṃsyam (dvādaśabhāgam) aṅgulaṃ, tena brahmādi-devān kārayet.*
> 
> *   **ब्रह्माणं** - त्रितालेन;

#### English Translation
*   **Proportions of Brahmā and Attendant Deities (*Brahmādi-sthāna-pramāṇa-vidhiḥ*):**
    *   **Lotus of Brahmā:** One cubit (*hasta*) above and to the right of the Lord's navel-line (*nābhisūtram*), place the navel-lotus (*nābhyambujam*) against the back wall. Its diameter should be 18 digits. Its upper petals are one-eighth less in width, and the downward petals are bent by 4 digits. The upper petals are bent by 3 units and fitted with a central pericarp (*karṇikā*), adorned with lotus petals facing up and down. The lotus stalk (*nālam*) has a thickness of half a digit at the middle.
    *   To the right and further right of the stalk, place young and mature lotus leaves. The size of the young leaf is equal to the upper lotus petals, and the mature leaf is equal to the downward petals. The leaf-stalks should have a thickness of one digit.
    *   **Brahmā's Form:** In the center of the pericarp of this lotus, place Brahmā: four-faced, wearing a matted-hair crown (*jaṭāmukuṭa*), golden-hued, wearing flowered garments, adorned with all ornaments, wearing an upper garment and sacred thread, seated on a Brahma-style seat (*brāhmāsane*). His hands carry a rosary and a water-pot, with the right hand held in *āloḍya* pose (holding the rosary or blessing) and the left resting on His hip. Alternatively, He may be depicted with four hands, with two hands folded in supplication, or with His left and right hands resting open on His lap.
    *   **Attendant Deity Proportions (*Tāla-lakṣaṇam*):**
        *   One-tenth of the height of the main immovable icon is defined as the *tāla* unit for the attendant deities, and one-twelfth of that *tāla* represents one digit (*aṅgulam*). Carve the attendant deities according to this scale:
            *   **Brahmā:** 3-tala scale (*tritālena*).
            *   **Garuḍa:** 2-tala scale increased by 6 units (*ṣaṇmātrādhika-dvitālena*). He is two-armed, wearing a basket-crown (*karaṇdamakuṭa*), with His left hand in flying pose (*uḍḍīya-kara*) and His right hand held in striking pose, golden-complexioned.
            *   **Five Weapons:** Represented in personified forms (*āyudha-puruṣa*), on the same 2-tala scale, with their respective emblems carved on their heads:
                *   *Cakra (Discus):* Male form, red-complexioned.
                *   *Śaṅkha (Conch):* Lotus-like dwarf-like (*bhūtākāram*) circular form, white-complexioned.
                *   *Śārṅga (Bow):* Neuter form, yellow-complexioned.
                *   *Gadā (Mace):* Female form, red-complexioned.
                *   *Khaḍga (Sword):* Male form, white-complexioned.
            *   **Madhu and Kaiṭabha:** 12-tala scale (*dviṣat-tālena*).
            *   **Sun and Moon:** White and red-complexioned respectively, 3-tala scale.
            *   **Sanaka and Sanatkumāra:** Depicted as children of the color of vakula fruit (brownish-yellow), on a 2-tala scale.
            *   **Tumburu and Nārada:** 3-tala scale, yellow-red-complexioned.
            *   **Kinnara Couples:** Yellow-complexioned, 2.5-tala scale (*dvyardhatālena*).
            *   **Vighna (Gaṇeśa):** Red-complexioned, 6-tala scale (*ṣaṭtālena*).
            *   **Durgā:** Dark-complexioned, 6-tala scale.
            *   **Sages (Ṛṣis):** 3.5-tala scale (*tryardhatālena*).
            *   **Apsaras, the Thirty-Three Gods, and the Guardians (Lokapālas):** 4-tala scale (*catustālena*).
        *   Make the length of the Lord's arms up to the breast line. Make the heights of the Goddesses, the worshipping sages, and Brahmā and Śiva on the sides, one part less than the main deity's corresponding proportions. Any detail not described here must be executed as per the rules of the *Śilpaśāstra*.
        *   Thus concludes the Nineteenth Chapter of the Vimanarchanakalpa of Sage Marīci, titled the "Rules for Standing and Other Immovable Icons".
