# Chapter 47: Pure Bathing Ritual and First-Fruits Offering (Śuddha-snapana-vidhiḥ / Āgrayaṇa-vidhiḥ)
### Section 1: Liturgical Rules for the Pure Bathing and First-Fruits Offering Rituals

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **अथ सप्तचत्वारिंशः पटलः**
> **शुद्धस्नपन-आग्रयण-विधिः -**
> ***atha saptacatvāriṃśaḥ paṭalaḥ***
> ***śuddhasnapana-āgrayaṇa-vidhiḥ -***
> 
> **(शुद्धस्नपनविधिः)**
> अथ शुद्धस्नपनविधिं वक्ष्ये - नित्यार्चनायां स्नपने चोत्सवे अन्यर्क्षेषु, पुण्यर्क्षेषु च, शुद्धस्नपनं समाचरेत् । स्नपनद्रव्याणि संभरति । देवागारं प्रविश्य, देवस्य पादयोः पुष्पाञ्जलिं दत्त्वा, ‘परं रहः’ (वै०म०प्र० ८) इति हस्ताभ्यां देवमादाय अलंकृते मण्डपपीठे ‘प्रतद्विष्णुः’ इति संस्थाप्य, पात्रे परिच्छदादिपरिवृतं राजवदुपचारं कारयित्वा, ‘त्रीणि पदा’ इति, ‘इन्द्रोऽभिमन्ते’ ति देवस्य पादौ प्रक्षाल्य, ‘विष्णुमूरुं रक्षस्व’ इति धौतेन विमृज्य, ‘विष्णवे नम’ इति पुष्पाञ्जलिं दत्त्वा, ‘शं नो देवीः’ इत्याचमनं ददाति ।
> ***(śuddhasnapanavidhiḥ)***
> *atha śuddhasnapanavidhiṃ vakṣye - nityārcanāyāṃ snapane cotsave anyarṣeṣu, puṇyarṣeṣu ca, śuddhasnapanaṃ samācaret. snapanadravyāṇi saṃbharati. devāgāraṃ praviśya, devasya pādayoḥ puṣpāñjaliṃ dattvā, ‘paraṃ rahaḥ’ (vai.ma.pra. 8) iti hastābhyāṃ devamādāya alaṅkṛte maṇḍapapīṭhe ‘pratadviṣṇuḥ’ iti saṃsthāpya, pātre paricchadādiparivṛtaṃ rājavadupacāraṃ kārayitvā, ‘trīṇi padā’ iti, ‘indro’bhimante’ ti devasya pādau praṣālya, ‘viṣṇumūruṃ rakṣasva’ iti dhautena vimṛjya, ‘viṣṇave nama’ iti puṣpāñjaliṃ dattvā, ‘śaṃ no devīḥ’ ityācamanaṃ dadāti.*
> 
> अष्टाक्षरेण मुखं पाणी च धौतेन विमृज्य, ‘घृतात्परी’ ति मुखवासं प्रदाय, विविधैः स्तोत्रैः नृत्तगेयवाद्यैश्च विनोदं कारयेत् । मुख्यगौणं च ज्ञात्वा, देवेशं प्रणम्य, वस्त्राभरण-गन्धमाल्यादीनि विमोच्य, ‘वसोः पवित्र’ मित्यनेन वाससाऽऽच्छाद्य, मुखवासं निवेद्य, तत्काले वैष्णवैर्मन्त्रैः जयशब्दैश्च स्तुत्वा, देवेशं प्रणम्य, कार्यं विज्ञापयेत् ।
> *aṣṭākṣareṇa mukhaṃ pāṇī ca dhautena vimṛjya, ‘ghṛtātparī’ ti mukhavāsaṃ pradāya, vividhaiḥ stotraiḥ nṛttageyavādyaiśca vinodaṃ kārayet. mukhyagauṇaṃ ca jñātvā, deveśaṃ praṇamya, vastrābharaṇa-gandhamālyādīni vimocya, ‘vasoḥ pavitra’ mityanena vāsasā’’cchādya, mukhavāsaṃ nivedya, tatkāle vaiṣṇavairmantraiḥ jayaśabdaiśca stutvā, deveśaṃ praṇamya, kāryaṃ vijñāpayet.*
> 
> पूजकः स्वयमेव श्रावयेत् । तद्वाक्यं विष्णोर्वाक्यमिति स्मृत्वा, सर्वं समाचरेत् । पुनःपुनः मुखवासं निवेद्य, विष्णुगायत्र्या धौतेन वस्त्रेण देवस्य स्कन्धं समाच्छाद्य, अभावे नववस्त्रेण, ‘अतो देवाः’ इत्यलकान् विकीर्य, ‘इदं विष्णुः’ इति अलकशोधनं कृत्वा, तैले सोममभ्यर्च्य, ‘त्रीणि पदा’ इति तैलं शिरसि संस्राव्य, ‘सोमं राजानम्’ इति मर्दयित्वा, ‘विष्णोः कर्माणि’ इत्यावर्त्याऽऽवर्त्य करोति । अलकान् संबध्य, ‘tadविष्णोः परमं पदम्’ इति परिमलैः पुष्पमाल्यैरलंकृत्य, करौ प्रक्षाल्य, स्कन्धाद्वस्त्रं विमोच्य, ‘तद्विप्रासः’ इत्यङ्गे अभ्यञ्जनं कृत्वा, तच्छेषं भक्तेभ्यः प्रद्यात् ।
> *pūjakaḥ svayameva śrāvayet. tadvākyaṃ viṣṇorvākyamiti smṛtvā, sarvaṃ samācaret. punaḥpunaḥ mukhavāsaṃ nivedya, viṣṇugāyatryā dhautena vastreṇa devasya skandhaṃ samācchādya, bhāve navavastreṇa, ‘ato devāḥ’ ityālakān vikīrya, ‘idaṃ viṣṇuḥ’ iti alakāśodhanaṃ kṛtvā, taile somamabhyarcya, ‘trīṇi padā’ iti tailaṃ śirasi saṃsrāvya, ‘somaṃ rājānam’ iti mardayitvā, ‘viṣṇoḥ karmāṇi’ ityāvartyā’’vartya karoti. alakān saṃbadhya, ‘tadviṣṇoḥ paramaṃ padam’ iti parimalaiḥ puṣpamālyairalaṅkṛtya, karau praṣālya, skandhādvastraṃ vimocya, ‘tadviprāsaḥ’ ityāṅge abhyañjanaṃ kṛtvā, taccheṣaṃ bhaktebhyaḥ pradyāt.*
> 
> ‘विष्णोर्नुकम्’ इति शालिपिष्टेन देवेशं परिमृज्य, ‘तदस्य प्रियम्’ इति वस्त्रेणार्द्रेणाविमृज्य, शकुनसूक्तेन देवमादाय, ‘प्रतद्विष्णुः स्तवते’ इति स्नानपीठे देवं संस्थाप्य, पूर्ववत् पादौ प्रक्षाल्य, सौवर्णैः साधनैश्चूतपत्र-सिराभिर्वा ‘परो मात्रया विचक्रमे’ इति दन्तधावनं तालुशोधनं च भावयति । पूर्ववदाचमनं दद्यात् ।
> *‘viṣṇornukam’ iti śālipīṣṭena deveśaṃ parimṛjya, ‘tadasya priyam’ iti vastreṇārdreṇāvimṛjya, śakunasūktena devamādāya, ‘pratadviṣṇuḥ stavate’ iti snānapīṭhe devaṃ saṃsthāpya, pūrvavat pādau praṣālya, souvarṇaiḥ sādhanaiścūtapatra-sirābhirvā ‘paro mātrayā vicakrame’ iti dantadhāvanaṃ tāluśodhanaṃ ca bhāvayati. pūrvavadācamanaṃ dadyāt.*
> 
> एकादश्याम्, परवाणि, नवम्याम्, अष्टम्यां च वर्जयेत् । तासु कुर्याच्चेत्, राजशत्रुपालनं ग्रामयजमानयोरभयप्रदं भवति ।
> *ekādaśyām, parvaṇi, navamyām, aṣṭamyāṃ ca varjayet. tāsu kuryāccet, rājaśatrupālanaṃ grāmayajamānayorbhayapradaṃ bhavati.*
> 
> दूर्वा-अक्शत-पुष्पाणि देवस्य मूर्धादिपादपर्यन्तं, ‘त्रिदेव’ इति सन्धौ सन्धौ संयोज्य, पादयोः प्रक्षिपेत् । वस्त्रादीनि विमोच्य, ‘परिलिखित’ इति आम्लेन संशोध्य, सूक्ष्मसिकताभिः ‘विष्णवे नम’ इति यथा बिम्बशुद्धिस्तथा शोधयित्वा, ‘वारीश्चतस्रः’ इति शुद्धोदकैरभिषिच्य, पूर्ववद्धौतेन वस्त्रेणाऽऽच्छाद्य, पाद्याचमनं दत्त्वा, कर्पूरादिपरिमलैर्युक्तेन हरिद्रापिण्डेन ‘सिनीवाली’ ति देवस्य सर्वाङ्गमालिप्य, पूर्ववच्छुद्धोदकैरभिषेचयेत् ।
> *dūrvā-akṣata-puṣpāṇi devasya mūrdhādīpādaparyantaṃ, ‘trideva’ iti sandhou sandhou saṃyojya, pādayoḥ praṣipet. vastrādīni vimocya, ‘parilikhita’ iti āmlena saṃśodhya, sūṣmasikatābhiḥ ‘viṣṇave nama’ iti yathā bimbaśuddhistasthā śodhayitvā, ‘vārīṣcatasraḥ’ iti śuddhodakairabhiṣicya, pūrvvaddhautena vastreṇā’’cchādya, pādyācamanaṃ dattvā, karpūrādiparimalairyuktena haridrāpiṇḍena ‘sinīvālī’ ti devasya sarvāṅgamālipya, pūrvvacchuddhodakairabhiṣecayet.*
> 
> पुनः संशोध्य, आचमनं दत्त्वा, वस्त्रमाल्याद्यैर्विभूष्य, ‘नमो वरुणः शुद्धः’ इति क्षीरेण उष्णोदकैर्वा अभिषिच्य, ‘भूरानिलय’ इति गन्धतोयैः पुनः संस्नाप्य पूर्ववद्वस्त्रमाल्यादिभिरलंकृत्य, ‘अग्निं दूतम्’ इति उपवीतं, ‘सोमस्य तनूरसि’ इत्युत्तरीयं च दद्यात् ।
> *punaḥ saṃśodhya, ācamanaṃ dattvā, vastramālyādyairvibhūṣya, ‘namo varuṇaḥ śuddhaḥ’ iti kṣīreṇa uṣṇodakaiṛvā abhiṣicya, ‘bhūrānilaya’ iti gandhatoyaiḥ punaḥ saṃsnāpy pūrvadvastramālyādibhiralaṅkṛtya, ‘agniṃ dūtam’ iti upavītaṃ, ‘somasya tanūrasi’ ityuttarīyaṃ ca dadyāt.*
> 
> ‘इदं ब्रह्म पुनीमहे’ इति देवस्य हस्तयोः पवित्रं संयोज्य, ‘तद्विप्रासः’ इति चन्दनेन सर्वाङ्गमालिप्य, पाद्यार्घ्याभ्यर्च्य, देवेशं प्रणम्य, सहस्रधारामभ्यर्च्य, पूर्ववत्सहस्रधारयाभिषेचयेत् । अलाभे षोडशादि चतुरन्तं यथालाभं कुम्भोदकैः ‘वसोः पवित्रम्’ इति, पुरुषसूक्तेन वा अभिषेचयेत् । तत्तत्काले चतुर्दिक्षु वेदानध्यापयेत् । स्तोत्रैः जयशब्दैः नृत्तगेयवाद्यैश्च घोषयत् ।
> *‘idaṃ brahma punīmahe’ iti devasya hastayoḥ pavitraṃ saṃyojya, ‘tadviprāsaḥ’ iti candanena sarvāṅgamālipya, pādyārghyābhyarcya, deveśaṃ praṇamya, sahasradhārāmabhyarcya, pūrvavatsahasradhārayāabhiṣecayet. alābhe ṣoḍaśādi caturantaṃ yathālābhaṃ kumbhodakaiḥ ‘vasoḥ pavitram’ iti, puruṣasūktena vā abhiṣecayet. tattatkāle caturdikṣu vedānadhyāpayet. stotraiḥ jayaśabdaiḥ nṛttageyavādyaiśca ghoṣayat.*
> 
> पुनः शुद्धोदकैरभिषिच्य, वस्त्रमाल्यादीनि विमोच्य, अङ्गं प्रति ‘मित्रः सुपर्णः’ इति प्लोतेन विमृज्य, धौतवस्त्रेणाऽच्छाद्य, पाद्याचमनं दत्त्वा, पुष्पाञ्जलिं दत्त्वा, शकुनसूक्तेन देवमुद्धृत्य, पूर्ववन्मण्डपे पीठे संन्यस्य, वस्त्राभरण-गन्धमाल्याद्यैरलंकृत्य, पाद्याद्यर्घ्यान्तमभ्यर्च्य, दूर्वा-अक्षत-गन्धपुष्पाण्ये कपात्रे संगृह्य देवस्य पुरतः संस्थाप्य, सोममभ्यर्च्य, हस्ताभ्यां गृहीत्वा, विष्णुगायत्र्या मकुटोपरि त्रिःप्रदक्षिणामावर्त्य, पुरतः निक्षिपेत् । पश्चात् रक्षादीपविधानेन रक्षादीपं कारयेत् । तत्काले ‘देवस्य त्वा’ इत्यादर्शं दर्शयित्वा, अष्टाक्षरेण देवस्य छत्र-चामर-परिच्छदादीनि योजयित्वा विनोदमपि कारयेत् ।
> *punaḥ śuddhodakairabhiṣicya, vastramālyādīni vimocya, aṅgaṃ prati ‘mitraḥ suparṇaḥ’ iti plotena vimṛjya, dhautavastreṇā’’cchādya, pādyācamanaṃ dattvā, puṣpāñjaliṃ dattvā, śakunasūktena devamuddhṛty, pūrvavanmaṇḍape pīṭhe saṃnyasya, vastrābharaṇa-gandhamālyādyairalaṅkṛtya, pādyādyarghyāntamabhyarcya, dūrvā-akṣata-gandhapuṣpāṇye kapātre saṃgṛhya devasya purataḥ saṃsthāpya, somamabhyarcya, hastābhyāṃ gṛhītvā, viṣṇugāyatryā makuṭopari triḥpradakṣiṇāmāvartya, purataḥ praṣipet. paścāt rakṣādīpavidhānena rakṣādīpaṃ kārayet. tatkāle ‘devasya tvā’ ityādarśaṃ darśayitvā, aṣṭākṣareṇa devasya chatra-cāmara-paricchadādīni yojayitvā vinodamapi kārayet.*
> 
> शकुनसूक्तेन देवमुद्धृत्य, haविर्निवेदनस्थाने ‘भूरसि’ इत्यादिना प्रतिष्ठाप्य आसनाद्यैरुपचारैरभ्यर्च्य, पानीयाचमन-मुखवासं दत्त्वा, देवेशमुद्धृत्य, पूर्ववन्मण्डपे पीठे संन्यस्य, वस्त्रमाल्यादीनि विमोच्य, अन्यैर्वस्त्रमाल्याद्यैरलंकृत्य, अपूपादीनि भक्ष्याणि भोज्यानि विविधानि पक्वान्यपक्वानि च विष्णुगायत्र्या अष्टाक्षरेण वा निवेदयेत् । पूर्ववत् पाद्याचमन-मुखवासौ दत्त्वा, यानमारोप्य, आलयं प्रदक्षिणीकृत्य, देवागारं प्रविश्य, पूर्वस्थाने न्यसेत् । अर्चनं चेज्जीवस्थाने प्रतिष्ठाप्यार्चयेत् ॥
> *śakunasūktena devamuddhṛty, havirnivedanasthāne ‘bhūrasi’ ityādinā pratiṣṭhāpya āsanādyairupacārairabhyarcya, pānīyācamana-mukhavāsaṃ dattvā, deveśamuddhṛty, pūrvavanmaṇḍape pīṭhe saṃnyasya, vastramālyādīni vimocya, anyairvastramālyādyairalaṅkṛtya, apūpādīni bhaxyāṇi bhojyāni vividhāni pakvānyapakvāni ca viṣṇugāyatryā aṣṭākṣareṇa vā nivedayet. pūrvavat pādyācamana-mukhavāsou dattvā, yānāmāropya, ālayaṃ pradakṣiṇīkṛtya, devāgāraṃ praviśya, pūrvasthāne nyaset. arcanaṃ cejjīvasthāne pratiṣṭhāpyārcayet.*
> 
> **(आग्रयणविधिः)**
> अथ देवेशस्याग्रयणविधिं वक्ष्ये - शुभे मुहूर्ते कर्तुरनुकूलनक्षत्रे शाल्यादीन् व्रीहीन् प्रियङ्गवादि धान्यानि वा आहृत्य आलयमलङ्कृत्य, शुचौ देशे सर्वोपदंशान् पयोदधिघृतगुडान् दीपार्थं तैलं च, कदली-चूत-पनस-नालिकेर-पूगफलानि मरीचादि-कर्पूर-हरिद्रा-कदलीशाकान् विततताम्बूल-कदलीपत्रानि, नवानि वस्त्राणि, विविधानि पुष्पाणि च संगृह्य; आचार्यो यजमानश्च वैष्णवैः सह; सर्ववाद्यसमायुक्तं सर्वालङ्कारसंयुक्तं, अमितं चक्रं वा पुरस्कृत्य, ग्रामप्रदक्षिणीकृत्य, आलयं प्रविश्य, अभिमुखे शाल्यादीन् संन्यस्याऽभ्यर्च्य, देवमानम्य, धान्यसंग्रहणं कृत्वा, haविश्याकोक्तविधिना धान्यानि संशोध्य, अवघातं कृत्वा, संशोध्य, तण्डुलान् द्वित्रिश्चतुर्वाऽद्भिः संक्षाल्य, नालिकेर-गुड-मरीच-जीरकैः मिश्रीकृत्य निधाय, पश्चाद्व्रीहीनपि बहूनि सर्वोपदंशांश्च पाचयित्वा, देवेशं संस्नाप्य, नववस्तप्रुष्पाद्यैरलङ्कृत्य, आस्थाने अर्चास्थाने वा संस्थाप्य, अष्टोपचारैः अभ्यर्च्य, पात्राणि संशोध्य, अधिदेवमाराध्य, पुनः प्रक्षाल्य, अभिपाच्य तण्डुलान् प्रक्षिप्य, ‘देवस्य त्वा’ इति घृतेनाभिघार्य, ‘अतो देवाः’ इति अभिमृश्य ‘आप्यायस्व’ इति निवेदयेत् ।
> ***(āgrayaṇavidhiḥ)***
> *atha deveśasyāgrayaṇavidhiṃ vakṣye - śubhe muhūrte karturanukūlanaxatre śālyādīn vrīhīn priyaṅgvādi dhānyāni vā āhṛtya ālayamalṅkṛtya, śucou deśe sarvopadaṃśān payodadhighṛtaguḍān dīpārthaṃ tailaṃ ca, kadalī-cūta-panas-nālikera-pūgaphalāni marīcādi-karpūra-haridrā-kadalīśākān vitatatāmbūla-kadalīpatrāṇi, navāni vastrāṇi, vividhāni puṣpāṇi ca saṃgṛhya; ācāryo yajamānaśca vaiṣṇavaiḥ saha; sarvavādyasamāyuktaṃ sarvalङ्kārasaṃyuktaṃ, amitaṃ cakraṃ vā puraskṛty, grāmapradakṣiṇīkṛty, ālayaṃ praviśya, abhimukhe śālyādīn saṃnyasyā’’bhyarcya, devamānamya, dhānyasaṃgrahaṇaṃ kṛtvā, haviṣyākoktavidhinā dhānyāni saṃśodhya, avaghātaṃ kṛtvā, saṃśodhya, taṇḍulān dvitriścaturvā’’dbhiḥ saṃṣālya, nālikera-guḍ-marīca-jīrakaiḥ miśrīkṛtya nidhāya, paścādvrīhīnapi bahūni sarvopadaṃśāṃśca pācayitvā, deveśaṃ saṃsnāpy, navavastrapuṣpādyairalaṅkṛtya, āsthāne arcāsthāne vā saṃsthāpya, aṣṭopacāraiḥ abhyarcya, pātrāṇi saṃśodhya, adhidevamārādhya, punaḥ praṣālya, abhipācya taṇḍulān praṣipya, ‘devasya tvā’ iti ghṛtenābhighārya, ‘ato devāḥ’ iti abhimṛśya ‘āpyāyasva’ iti nivedayet.*
> 
> आचमनमुखवासं च दत्त्वा, यथाविधि प्रभूतं haविर्निवेदनं च कृत्वा, नित्याग्निकुण्डे चुल्ल्यां वा वैष्णवं, श्रीभूमिदेवत्यं, तदालयगतपरिषद्देवानां मूर्तिमांत्रांश्च हुत्वा, परिषद्देवेभ्यो निवेद्य शक्तश्चेद्बलिदानं कारयित्वा, देवस्य निवेदिततण्डुलान् भक्ताः ब्राह्मणादयः प्राश्नीयुः, एवं आग्रयणं विष्णोः कारयेत् । सोऽपि धनधान्यसमृद्धिमाप्नोति इति विज्ञायते ॥
> *ācamanamukhavāsaṃ ca dattvā, yathāvidhi prabhūtaṃ havirnivedanaṃ ca kṛtvā, nityāgnikuṇḍe cullyāṃ vā vaiṣṇavaṃ, śrībhūmidevatyāṃ, tadālayagatapariṣaddevānāṃ mūrtimāntrāṃśca hutvā, pariṣaddevebhyo nivedya śaktaścedbalidānaṃ kārayitvā, devasya niveditataṇḍulān bhaktāḥ brāhmaṇādayaḥ prāśnīyuḥ, evaṃ āgrayaṇaṃ viṣṇoḥ kārayet. so’pi dhanadhānyasamṛddhimāpnoti iti vijñāyate.*

#### English Translation
*   **Auspicious Timing for Pure Bathing:** Now I shall describe the procedure for the Pure Bathing Ritual (*śuddha-snapana*): during daily worship, special bathing, festivals, other astronomical stars, and auspicious stars, one should perform the *śuddha-snapana*.

*   **Transferring the Deity to the Pavilion:** Having gathered the bathing ingredients, entered the temple sanctum, offered a handful of flowers (*puṣpāñjali*) to the feet of the Lord, the priest should take the deity in both hands reciting "Paraṃ rahaḥ" (VM 8.2), establish Him on the decorated pedestal in the pavilion reciting "Pratad viṣṇuḥ", and surround Him with vessels and royal services.

*   **Foot-washing and Purification:** Washing the feet of the Lord reciting "Trīṇi padā" and "Indro'bhimante", drying Him with a clean cloth reciting "Viṣṇumūruṃ rakṣasva", offering *puṣpāñjali* with "Viṣṇave namaḥ", he should give sipping water (*ācamana*) reciting "Śaṃ no devīḥ".

*   **Devotional Pleasures and Preparation:** Having wiped the face and hands with a clean cloth reciting the eight-syllable mantra (*aṣṭākṣara*), offered mouth-fresheners reciting "Ghṛtātparī", he should entertain Him with various hymns, dance, vocal and instrumental music. Knowing the primary and secondary services, bowing to the Lord, removing garments, ornaments, sandalwood, and garlands, covering Him with a cloth reciting "Vasoḥ pavitram", offering mouth-fresheners, praising Him with Vaiṣṇava mantras and shouts of victory, bowing to the Lord, he should submit his prayer.

*   **Oil Massage and Hair Grooming:** The priest should recite the prayer himself, and remembering that those words are the words of Viṣṇu, perform all tasks. Repeatedly offering mouth-fresheners, covering the shoulders of the Lord with a washed cloth with the Viṣṇu-gāyatrī, or with a new cloth if unavailable, spreading His locks (*alaka*) reciting "Ato devāḥ", parting the hair reciting "Idaṃ viṣṇuḥ", consecrating the oil with the moon deity (*Soma*), pouring the oil on His head reciting "Trīṇi padā", massaging reciting "Somaṃ rājānam", he should repeat it reciting "Viṣṇoḥ karmāṇi".

*   **Scenting and Scrubbing:** Tying the locks, decorating with perfumes and flower garlands reciting "Tad viṣṇoḥ paramaṃ padam", washing his hands, removing the cloth from the shoulders, applying ointment (*abhyañjana*) on His limbs reciting "Tad viprāsaḥ", he should distribute the remains to the devotees. Scrubbing the Lord with rice flour (*śāli-piṣṭa*) reciting "Viṣṇornukam", drying Him with a wet cloth reciting "Tad asya priyam", taking the deity reciting the *Śakuna-sūkta*, establishing Him on the bathing seat reciting "Pratad viṣṇuḥ stavate", washing His feet as before, he should perform tooth-brushing (*danta-dhāvana*) and palate cleaning (*tālu-śodhana*) using golden tools or the veins of mango leaves, reciting "Paro mātrayā vicakrame". He should give *ācamana* as before.

*   **Prohibited Days:** One should avoid this on the eleventh lunar day (*ekādaśī*), astronomical junctions (*parvan*), the ninth day (*navamī*), and the eighth day (*aṣṭamī*). If performed on those days, it protects the enemies of the king and causes fear to the village and the patron.

*   **Applying Turmeric Paste:** Placing dūrvā grass, unhusked rice, and flowers from the head of the Lord to His feet, connecting them joint by joint reciting "Trideva", he should drop them at His feet. Removing the garments, cleaning with sour liquids reciting "Parilikhita", scrubbing with fine sand reciting "Viṣṇave namaḥ" until the icon is thoroughly cleansed, bathing with pure water reciting "Vārīṣcatasraḥ", covering Him with a washed cloth, giving foot-washing and *ācamana*, applying turmeric paste (*haridrā-piṇḍa*) mixed with camphor and other perfumes on all His limbs reciting "Sinīvālī", he should bathe Him with pure water as before.

*   **Sacred Vestments and Anointing:** Having cleaned again, given *ācamana*, decorated with garments and garlands, bathed Him with milk or warm water reciting "Namo varuṇaḥ śuddhāḥ", bathed Him again with scented water reciting "Bhūrānilaya", decorated Him as before with garments and garlands, he should offer the sacred thread (*upavīta*) reciting "Agniṃ dūtam", and the upper garment (*uttarīya*) reciting "Somasya tanūrasi".

*   **The Thousand-Stream Bath (*Sahasradhārā*):** Placing the sacred ring (*pavitra*) on both hands of the Lord reciting "Idaṃ brahma punīmahe", applying sandalwood paste on all limbs reciting "Tad viprāsaḥ", worshipping with *pādya* and *arghya*, bowing to the Lord, worshipping the thousand-stream shower plate (*sahasradhārā*), he should bathe Him with the *sahasradhārā* as before. If unavailable, he should bathe Him with water from four to sixteen vessels as available, reciting "Vasoḥ pavitram" or the *Puruṣa-sūkta*. At that time, they should have the Vedas taught in the four directions, accompanied by hymns, shouts of victory, dance, and music.

*   **Drying and Redressing:** Bathing again with pure water, removing garments and garlands, wiping each limb with a towel (*plotena*) reciting "Mitraḥ suparṇaḥ", dressing in clean garments, giving *pādya* and *ācamana*, offering a handful of flowers, lifting the deity reciting the *Śakuna-sūkta*, placing Him on the pedestal in the pavilion as before, decorating Him with garments, ornaments, perfumes, and garlands, worshipping from *pādya* to *arghya*, gathering dūrvā grass, unhusked rice, sandalwood, and flowers in a single vessel, placing it in front of the Lord, worshipping Soma, holding it in both hands, circumambulating His crown three times with the Viṣṇu-gāyatrī, he should drop it in front.

*   **Protective Rites:** Afterwards, he should perform the protective lamp (*rakṣā-dīpa*) according to rules. At that time, showing the mirror (*ādarśa*) reciting "Devasya tvā", he should offer the umbrella, fly-whisk, and other royal insignia reciting the eight-syllable mantra, and entertain Him.

*   **Feast and Procession:** Lifting the deity reciting the *Śakuna-sūkta*, establishing Him in the food offering area reciting "Bhūrasi", worshipping with *āsana* and other services, giving drinking water, *ācamana*, and mouth-fresheners, lifting the Lord, establishing Him on the pedestal in the pavilion as before, removing garments and garlands, decorating Him with other garments and garlands, he should offer cakes (*apūpa*) and various cooked and uncooked foods to be chewed and eaten, reciting the Viṣṇu-gāyatrī or the eight-syllable mantra. Having given *pādya*, *ācamana*, and mouth-fresheners as before, mounted Him on a vehicle, circumambulated the temple, entered the sanctum, he should place Him in His original spot. If it is a worship in the life-icon (*jīva-bera*), he should establish Him there and worship.

*   **First-Fruits Offering (Āgrayaṇa):** Now I shall describe the first-fruits offering procedure (*āgrayaṇa*): on an auspicious moment and under a star favorable to the patron, having gathered new paddy, grains starting with shali, and millet (*priyaṅgu*), decorated the temple, gathered all side-dishes (*upadaṃśa*), milk, yogurt, ghee, jaggery, oil for lamps, plantains, mangoes, jackfruits, coconuts, areca nuts, black pepper, camphor, turmeric, plantain vegetables, spread betel leaves and plantain leaves, new garments, and various flowers in a clean place; the *ācārya* and the patron, accompanied by Vaiṣṇavas, all instruments, all decorations, and putting forward the boundless disc (*Cakra*), should circumambulate the village.

*   **Threshing and Cooking:** Entering the temple, placing the shali and other grains in front of the deity, worshipping Him, bowing to the Lord, taking the grains, purifying them according to the rules of *haviṣyāka*, performing threshing/dehusking (*avaghāta*), purifying, washing the rice two, three, or four times with water, mixing it with coconut, jaggery, black pepper, and cumin (*jīraka*), setting it aside, and then cooking many grains and all side-dishes, bathing the Lord, decorating Him with new garments and flowers, establishing Him in the assembly hall or worship place, worshipping with eight services, purifying the vessels, worshipping the presiding deity, washing again, throwing in the cooked rice, pouring ghee reciting "Devasya tvā", touching it reciting "Ato devāḥ", he should offer it reciting "Āpyāyasva".

*   **Feast and Blessings:** Having given *ācamana* and mouth-fresheners, offered abundant food (*havis*) according to rules, performed oblation in the daily fire-pit or stove to Viṣṇu, Śrī, Bhūmi, and the retinue deities of the temple with their respective form-mantras, offered to the retinue deities, and if capable, distributed the *bali*, the devotees, Brahmins, and others should eat the offered rice. Thus one should perform the *āgrayaṇa* for Viṣṇu. He also attains prosperity of wealth and grains — so it is understood.
