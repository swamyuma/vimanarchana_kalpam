# Vimanarchana Kalpa of Sage Marīci (Vaikhānasa Āgama)
## Chapter 95: Caturvidha-bhagavat-samāśrayaṇa-vidhiḥ (The Fourfold Seeking of Refuge in the Lord and the Five Forms)

This document contains the Sanskrit text, IAST transliteration, and English translation of the 95th Chapter (*Pañcanavatitamaḥ Paṭalaḥ*) of the **Vimānārcanākalpa** (attributed to Sage Marīci, representing the Vaikhānasa school of Vaishnavism).

This chapter expounds the fourfold path of seeking refuge in the Lord (*bhagavat-samāśrayaṇa*): *japa* (recitation of mantras), *huta* (sacrificial offerings in the fire), *arcana* (image worship), and *dhyāna* (meditative union). It establishes *arcana* (worship of images) as the most effective path for all human goals. It then details the Vaikhānasa doctrine of the fivefold manifestation of Brahman (*pañca-mūrti*): Viṣṇu, Puruṣa, Satya, Acyuta, and Aniruddha. It maps these five forms to the five elements, the five sacred fires (*pañcāgni*), and the five vital winds, explaining their corresponding cosmic qualities (Dharma, Jñāna, Aiśvarya, and Vīrya/Vairāgya) and calling for their unified meditation through the eightfold yoga.

---

### Section 1: The Fourfold Path of Seeking Refuge

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **अथ पञ्चनवतितमः पटलः**
> **चतुर्विध-भगवत्-समाश्रयणम्**
> ***atha pañcanavatitamaḥ paṭalaḥ***
> ***caturvidha-bhagavat-samāśrayaṇam***
> 
> अथ भगवत्समाश्रयणं चतुर्विधं भवति (समाराधनम् इत्यामनन्ति) -- जप-हुत-अर्चना-ध्यानम् इति ।
> सावित्रीपूर्वां वैष्णवीमृचम्, अष्टाक्षरं वा, भगवन्तं ध्यात्वा अभ्यसेत् स जपः । अग्निहोत्रादिकर्मणि यत् हूयते तद् धुतं होमः । गृहे देवायतने वा वैदिकेन मार्गेण प्रतिमादिषु पूजयेत् तद् अर्चनम् । निष्कल-सकल-विभागं च ज्ञात्वा अष्टाङ्गयोगमार्गेण परमात्मानं जीवेन आत्मना चिन्तयेत् तद् ध्यानम् इति ॥
> *atha bhagavatsamāśrayaṇaṃ caturvidhaṃ bhavati (samārādhanam ityāmananti) -- japa-huta-arcanā-dhyānam iti |*
> *sāvitrīpūrvāṃ vaiṣṇavīmṛcam, aṣṭāxaraṃ vā, bhagavantaṃ dhyātvā abhyasēt sa japaḥ | agnihōtrādikarmaṇi yat hūyatē tad dhutaṃ hōmaḥ | gṛhē dēvāyatanē vā vaidikēna mārgēṇa pratimādiṣu pūjayēt tad arcanam | niṣkala-sakala-vibhāgaṃ ca jñātvā aṣṭāṅgayōgamārgēṇa paramātmānaṃ jīvēna ātmanā cintayēt tad dhyānam iti ||*

#### English Translation
*   **The Fourfold Refuge:** Seeking refuge in the Lord is fourfold — which they also term as the four methods of worship (*atha bhagavatsamāśrayaṇaṃ caturvidhaṃ bhavati*) — namely: *japa* (recitation), *huta* (sacrificial offering), *arcana* (ritual worship), and *dhyāna* (meditation) (*japa-huta-arcanā-dhyānam iti*).

*   **Japa:** Meditating on the Lord and repeating the Vedic Gayatri (*Savitri*) preceded by a Vaishnava Rgvedic verse, or the eight-syllable mantra (*Om Namo Narayanaya*) is known as *japa* (*sāvitrīpūrvāṃ vaiṣṇavīmṛcam, aṣṭāxaraṃ vā, bhagavantaṃ dhyātvā abhyasēt sa japaḥ*).

*   **Huta:** That which is offered in the sacred fire during rituals like the Agnihotra is *huta* or homa (*agnihōtrādikarmaṇi yat hūyatē tad dhutaṃ hōmaḥ*).

*   **Arcana:** Worshipping the Lord on images in one's home or in a temple according to the Vedic procedures is *arcana* (*gṛhē dēvāyatanē vā vaidikēna mārgēṇa pratimādiṣu pūjayēt tad arcanam*).

*   **Dhyāna:** Understanding the division between the unmanifest (*niṣkala*) and the manifest (*sakala*), and contemplating the Supreme Soul through the individual self by means of the path of the eightfold yoga is *dhyāna* (*niṣkala-sakala-vibhāgaṃ ca jñātvā aṣṭāṅgayōgamārgēṇa paramātmānaṃ jīvēna ātmanā cintayēt tad dhyānam iti*).

### Section 2: Preeminence of Worship and the Fivefold Manifestation

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **अर्चनस्योत्तमत्वं, पञ्चमूर्ति-भेद-निरूपणं च**
> तेषु अर्चनं सर्वार्थसाधनं स्यात् । तत्र परमात्मैव पञ्चधा भवति । “स वा एष पुरुषः पञ्चधा पञ्चात्मा” इति श्रुतिः ।
> तस्माद् आकाशादि-महाभूतानां क्रमेण परमात्मनि भेद एव । सभ्य-आहवनीय-अन्वाहार्य-गार्हपत्य-आवसथ्याः इति पञ्चाग्निवत् “पञ्चाग्नीन् व्यक्रमत” इति श्रुतिः । प्राणादि-पञ्चवायु-भेदैरिव पञ्चमूर्ति-भेदैः भिद्यत इति । “पूयमानः परमः स्वगुण-प्रसन्नः सर्वान् इमान् धारयिष्यसि” इति श्रुतिः ।
> तस्मात् “विष्णु-पुरुष-सत्य-अच्युत-अनिरुद्धाः” इति (पञ्च मूर्तयः) ।
> मूर्तीनां पञ्चनाम् आदिमूर्तिर्विष्णुः, तदुद्भूताश्च चतस्रो मूर्तयः । “तद्विष्णोः परमात्मानुदाया चतुर्गुणाय” इति श्रुतिः । तस्मात् ब्रह्म चतुष्पाद् भवति । पादात्, द्विपादात्, त्रिपादात्, केवलात् इति क्रमेण धर्म-ज्ञान-ऐश्वर्य-वैराग्यैः (वीर्यैः) इति विष्णुगुणैः चतुर्भिः चतस्रो मूर्तयो भवन्ति ।
> विष्णुः, महाविष्णुः, सदाविष्णुः, व्यापी, नारायण इति भवन्ति । विष्णोरंशः पुरुषः, महाविष्णोरंशः सत्यः, सदाविष्णोरंशः अच्युतः, व्यापिनोऽंशः अनिरुद्धः इति धर्मादि-ब्रह्मगुणैः चतुर्धा भिद्यते ।
> पुरुषः पुरुषात्मकः परमपुरुषः धर्ममय इति । सत्यः सत्यात्मकः ज्ञानमयः सर्वतेजोमय इति । अच्युतोऽपरिमितः ऐश्वर्यमयः श्रीपतिरिति । अनिरुद्धः महान् वीर्यमयः (वैराग्यमयः) सर्वसंहार इति । एषां रूपं पुरा वर्णादिकथने विधिना चोक्तम् । एवं पञ्चमूर्तिनामभिः अभेदं गुरूपदेशेन अष्टाङ्गयोगमार्गेण ध्यात्वा तद् अर्चयेद् इत्याह मरीचिः ॥
> ***arcanasyōttamatvaṃ, pañcamūrti-bhēda-nirūpaṇaṃ ca***
> *tēṣu arcanaṃ sarvārthasādhanaṃ syāt | tatra paramātmaiva pañcadhā bhavati | “sa vā eṣa puruṣaḥ pañcadhā pañcātmā” iti śrutiḥ |*
> *tasmād ākāśādi-mahābhūtānāṃ kramēṇa paramātmani bhēda ēva | sabhya-āhavanīya-anvāhārya-gārhapatya-āvasathyāḥ iti pañcāgnivat “pañcāgnīn vyakramata” iti śrutiḥ | prāṇādi-pañcavāyu-bhēdairiva pañcamūrti-bhēdair bhidyata iti | “pūyamānaḥ paramaḥ svaguṇa-prasannaḥ sarvān imān dhārayiṣyasi” iti śrutiḥ |*
> *tasmāt “viṣṇu-puruṣa-satya-acyuta-aniruddhāḥ” iti (pañca mūrtayaḥ) |*
> *mūrtīnāṃ pañcanām ādimūrtirviṣṇuḥ, tadudbhūtāśca catasrō mūrtayaḥ | “tadviṣṇōḥ paramātmānudāyā caturguṇāya” iti śrutiḥ | tasmāt brahma catuṣpād bhavati | pādāt, dvipādāt, tripādāt, kēvalāt iti kramēṇa dharma-jñāna-aiśvarya-vairāgyaiḥ (vīryaiḥ) iti viṣṇuguṇaiḥ caturbhiḥ catasrō mūrtayō bhavanti |*
> *viṣṇuḥ, mahāviṣṇuḥ, sadāviṣṇuḥ, vyāpī, nārāyaṇa iti bhavanti | viṣṇōraṃśaḥ puruṣaḥ, mahāviṣṇōraṃśaḥ satyaḥ, sadāviṣṇōraṃśaḥ acyutaḥ, vyāpinō'ṃśaḥ aniruddhaḥ iti dharmādi-brahmaguṇaiḥ caturdhā bhidyatē |*
> *puruṣaḥ puruṣātmakaḥ paramapuruṣaḥ dharmamaya iti | satyaḥ satyātmakaḥ jñānamayaḥ sarvatējōmaya iti | acyutō'parimitaḥ aiśvaryamayaḥ śrīpatiriti | aniruddhaḥ mahān vīryamayaḥ sarvasaṃhāra iti | ēṣāṃ rūpaṃ purā varṇādikathanē vidhinā cōktam | ēvaṃ pañcamūrtināmabhiḥ abhēdaṃ gurūpadēśēna aṣṭāṅgayōgamārgēṇa dhyātvā tad arcayēd ityāha marīciḥ ||*

#### English Translation
*   **The Preeminence of Worship:** Among the four paths, *arcana* (worship of images) is the best means to accomplish all human goals (*tēṣu arcanaṃ sarvārthasādhanaṃ syāt*). In this context, the Supreme Soul manifests as fivefold (*tatra paramātmaiva pañcadhā bhavati*). The Vedic scripture declares: *"That Person indeed is fivefold, of fivefold nature"* (*“sa vā eṣa puruṣaḥ pañcadhā pañcātmā”*).

*   **Analogies of Fivefold Division:**
    *   **Elements:** Just as the one space becomes divided in the elements beginning with space (*tasmād ākāśādi-mahābhūtānāṃ kramēṇa paramātmani bhēda ēva*).
    *   **Sacred Fires:** Like the five sacred fires — Sabhya, Āhavanīya, Anvāhāryapacana, Gārhapatya, and Āvasathya (*sabhya-āhavanīya-anvāhārya-gārhapatya-āvasathyāḥ iti pañcāgnivat*), referencing the scripture: *"He stepped over the five fires"* (*“pañcāgnīn vyakramata”*).
    *   **Winds:** Just as the single life-breath is divided into the five vital winds beginning with prana, so the Lord is divided into the five forms (*prāṇādi-pañcavāyu-bhēdairiva pañcamūrti-bhēdair bhidyatē*), referencing the scripture: *"Being purified, Supreme Lord, pleased with your own qualities, you shall support all these"* (*“pūyamānaḥ paramaḥ svaguṇa-prasannaḥ sarvān imān dhārayiṣyasi”*).

*   **The Five Deities:** Therefore, the five forms are Viṣṇu, Puruṣa, Satya, Acyuta, and Aniruddha (*tasmāt viṣṇu-puruṣu-satya-acyuta-aniruddhāḥ*).

*   **Origin of the Four Forms:** Of these five, Viṣṇu is the primal form (*ādimūrtirviṣṇuḥ*), and the other four forms arise from Him (*tadudbhūtāśca catasrō mūrtayaḥ*). Hence, Brahman is four-footed (*tasmāt brahma catuṣpād bhavati*). Corresponding to one foot, two feet, three feet, and the absolute (*pādāt, dvipādāt, tripāt, kēvalāt*), the four forms manifest respectively through the four qualities of Viṣṇu, namely: virtue (*dharma*), knowledge (*jñāna*), sovereignty (*aiśvarya*), and dispassion/potency (*vairāgya/vīrya*) (*dharma-jñāna-aiśvarya-vairāgyaiḥ iti viṣṇuguṇaiḥ caturbhiḥ catasrō mūrtayō bhavanti*).

*   **Correlation of Manifestations:**
    *   The five cosmic divisions are Viṣṇu, Mahāviṣṇu, Sadāviṣṇu, Vyāpī, and Nārāyaṇa (*viṣṇuḥ, mahāviṣṇuḥ, sadāviṣṇuḥ, vyāpī, nārāyaṇa*).
    *   Puruṣa is a portion of Viṣṇu (*viṣṇōraṃśaḥ puruṣaḥ*), Satya is a portion of Mahāviṣṇu (*mahāviṣṇōraṃśaḥ satyaḥ*), Acyuta is a portion of Sadāviṣṇu (*sadāviṣṇōraṃśaḥ acyutaḥ*), and Aniruddha is a portion of Vyāpī (*vyāpinō'ṃśaḥ aniruddhaḥ*). Thus the Lord is divided fourfold by the qualities of Brahman beginning with Dharma (*dharmādi-brahmaguṇaiḥ caturdhā bhidyatē*).

*   **Natures of the Four Forms:**
    *   **Puruṣa:** He is of the nature of the cosmic person, the supreme person, and is composed of righteousness/virtue (*puruṣaḥ puruṣātmakaḥ paramapuruṣaḥ dharmamaya*).
    *   **Satya:** He is of the nature of truth, composed of knowledge and all-pervading light (*satyaḥ satyātmakaḥ jñānamayaḥ sarvatējōmaya*).
    *   **Acyuta:** He is the limitless, composed of sovereignty, and is the husband of Śrī (*acyutō'parimitaḥ aiśvaryamayaḥ śrīpatir*).
    *   **Aniruddha:** He is the great, composed of potency, and is the destroyer of all at cosmic dissolution (*aniruddhaḥ mahān vīryamayaḥ sarvasaṃhāra*).

#### Colophon
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> इति श्रीवैखानसे मरीचिप्रोक्ते विमानार्चनाकल्पे ज्ञानस्वरूपनिरूपणं नाम पञ्चनवतितमः पटलः ॥९५॥

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> iti śrīvaikhānasē marīciprōktē vimānārcanākalpē jñānasvarūpanirūpaṇaṃ nāma pañcanavatitamaḥ paṭalaḥ ||95||

**Translation (English):**
*   **Method of Meditation:** Their iconography has been declared previously in the rules for describing their colors and features (*ēṣāṃ rūpaṃ purā varṇādikathanē vidhinā cōktam*). Having understood the non-difference of these five forms under their respective names through the instruction of a guru (*ēvaṃ pañcamūrtināmabhiḥ abhēdaṃ gurūpadēśēna*), and meditating on them through the path of the eightfold yoga, one should worship Him — thus declared Sage Marīci (*aṣṭāṅgayōgamārgēṇa dhyātvā tad arcayēd ityāha marīciḥ*).

### Glossary of Key Terms
*   **Bhagavat-samāśrayaṇa (भगवत्समाश्रयण):** Seeking refuge or shelter in the Supreme Lord.
*   **Japa (जप):** Recitation of mantras (such as the Gayatri or the Ashtakshara) while contemplating the Lord.
*   **Huta (हुत):** Fire-sacrifices (*homa*), where offerings are poured into the consecrated fire.
*   **Arcana (अर्चन):** Ritual worship of the deity in an image (*pratimā*) in home or temple shrines.
*   **Dhyāna (ध्यान):** Silent meditation, where the individual soul (*jīva*) contemplates its unity and relationship with the Supreme Soul (*paramātman*).
*   **Pañca-mūrti (पञ्चमूर्ति):** The five primary forms of Godhead in Vaikhānasa theology: Viṣṇu, Puruṣa, Satya, Acyuta, and Aniruddha.
*   **Pañcāgni (पञ्चाग्नि):** The five sacred fires of Vedic tradition: Sabhya, Āhavanīya, Anvāhāryapacana (Dakṣiṇāgni), Gārhapatya, and Āvasathya.
*   **Brahma-caturpād (ब्रह्मचतुष्पाद):** The four-footed aspect of Brahman, representing the four emanations (Puruṣa, Satya, Acyuta, Aniruddha) that arise from the primal Viṣṇu.
*   **Dharma-Jñāna-Aiśvarya-Vīrya (धर्म-ज्ञान-ऐश्वर्य-वीर्य):** The four cosmic attributes corresponding to the four emanations.
