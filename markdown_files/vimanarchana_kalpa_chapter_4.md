# Vimanarchana Kalpa of Sage Marīci (Vaikhānasa Āgama)
## Chapter 4: Bālālaya-Pratiṣṭhā-Vidhiḥ (Consecration of the Temporary Temple)

This document contains the Sanskrit text, IAST transliteration, and English translation of the 4th Chapter (*Caturthaḥ Paṭalaḥ*) of the **Vimānārcanākalpa** (attributed to Sage Marīci, representing the Vaikhānasa school of Vaishnavism).

This chapter outlines the guidelines for establishing a temporary temple (*taruṇālaya* or *bālālaya*) when building or renovating the main temple (*mūlālaya*). It details the site selection, construction dimensions and materials (prohibiting stone and brick), the preparation and measurement of the wooden temporary icon (*bālabera*), the setup of the sacrificial hall (*yāga-śālā*), the consecration of the water pot (*kumbha*), the ritual bathing, the transfer of divine power (*śakti*) from the pot to the icon, and the daily worship protocol.

### Section 1: Site Selection and Construction of the Temporary Temple

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **अथ चतुर्थः पटलः**
> **तरुणालयदेशानिरूपणम्**
> ***atha caturthaḥ paṭalaḥ***
> ***taruṇālayadeśanirūpaṇam***
> 
> यत्र मूलालयं कुर्यात्, तस्माद् ऐशान्ये चेन्द्रसूत्राद् उत्तरे, सौम्ये, वायव्ये, त्रिहस्तादि-त्रिंशद्धस्तान्तं तरुणालयं, द्वितीयावरणे, तृतीये वा, समारभेत।
> *yatra mūlālayaṃ kuryāt, tasmād aiśānye cendra-sūtrād uttare, saumye, vāyavye, trihastādi-triṃśaddhastāntaṃ taruṇālayaṃ, dvitīyāvaraṇe, tṛtīye vā, samārabhet.*
> 
> **तरुणालयनिर्माणविधिः**
> भित्तिमूलविशालं मात्राङ्गुलेन द्वितालं त्रितालं चतुस्तालं वा, तदवशेषं नालीगृहविशालं, समं त्रिपादं अर्धं वा, मुखमण्टपं यथा मूलालयद्वारं तथा तरुणालये, मृण्मयलेपोपरिष्ठात् तृणाच्छन्नं लोष्टाच्छन्नं वा कृत्वा, अत्रानुक्तं सर्वं द्वितीयतरुणालयस्योक्तविधिना कारयेत्; नैव शिला-इष्टका-दिभिः। तन्मध्ये द्विवेदिसहितं अर्चापीठं च कृत्वा, पश्चात् बालबेरं कारयेत्।
> ***taruṇālayanirmāṇavidhiḥ***
> *bhittimūlavisālaṃ mātrāṅguleṇa dvitālaṃ tritālaṃ catustālaṃ vā, tadavaśeṣaṃ nālīgṛhavisālaṃ, samaṃ tripādaṃ ardhaṃ vā, mukhamantapaṃ yathā mūlālayadvāraṃ tathā taruṇālaye, mṛṇmayalepopariṣṭhāt tṛṇācchannaṃ loṣṭācchannaṃ vā kṛtvā, atrānuktaṃ sarvaṃ dvitīyataruṇālayasyoktavidhinā kārayet; naiva śilā-iṣṭakā-dibhiḥ. tanmadhye dvivedisahitaṃ arcāpīṭhaṃ ca kṛtvā, paścāt bālaberaṃ kārayet.*

#### English Translation
*   **Site Selection for the Temporary Temple (*Taruṇālaya-deśa-nirūpaṇam*):** 
    *   Where the main temple (*mūlālaya*) is to be built, one should commence the construction of the temporary temple (*taruṇālaya* or *bālālaya*) in the North-East (*aiśānya*) thereof, or to the North of the East-West axis (*indra-sūtra*), or in the North (*saumya*), or North-West (*vāyavya*). 
    *   It should measure from three to thirty cubits (*tri-hasta-ādi triṃśad-hasta-antam*), and should be located in the second (*dvitīya*) or third (*tṛtīya*) enclosure (*āvaraṇa*).

*   **Construction Method of the Temporary Temple (*Taruṇālaya-nirmāṇa-vidhiḥ*):**
    *   The thickness of the walls at the base should be measured in standard units (*mātrāṅgula*) of two, three, or four spans (*tāla*). The remaining space should equal the width of the sanctum (*nālī-gṛha*), or be three-quarters, or half of it.
    *   The front porch (*mukha-maṇṭapa*) and the doorway of the temporary temple should be designed just like those of the main temple.
    *   It should be plastered with clay and thatched with grass (*tṛṇācchanna*) or covered with terracotta tiles (*loṣṭācchanna*).
    *   Everything else not specified here should be done according to the rules for the second temporary temple. It should never be constructed with stone (*śilā*) or brick (*iṣṭakā*).
    *   In its center, having constructed a worship pedestal (*arcā-pīṭha*) along with two altars (*vedi*), one should thereafter make the temporary deity icon (*bālabera*).

### Section 2: Materials for the Balabera and Sacrificial Hall Setup

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **बालबेरद्रव्य-बेराधिवासादिनिरूपणपूर्वं यागशालाविधिः**
> खदिर-असन-चम्पक-अर्जुन-पनस-वकुल-मधूक-अश्वत्थ-बिल्व-शमीनाम् अन्यतमं विधिना आहृत्य, शोषयित्वा त्वचं व्यपोह्य, मानाङ्गुलेन सप्ताङ्गुलं नवाङ्गुलं एकादशाङ्गुलं त्रयोदशाङ्गुलं वा, देवीभ्यां सहितं रहितं वा देवं सलक्षणं स्थितम् आसीनं वा कृत्वा, स्थापनदिवसात् पूर्वं विधिना अङ्कुरानर्पयित्वा, अक्ष्युन्मोचनं कृत्वा, अधिवास्य, बालालयाभिमुखे दक्षिणे वा यागशालां कृत्वा, तोरणाद्यैः अलङ्कृत्य तन्मध्ये शय्यावेदिं द्विहस्तायतविस्तारां तत्तुरीयोत्सेधां, तत्र प्राच्यां सभ्याग्निकुण्डं, तत्र प्राच्याम् ऐशान्यां वा स्नानवेदिं च कुर्यात्; पञ्चाग्नीन् कल्पयेत् इति आहुः एके।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **bālaberadravya-berādhivāsādinirūpaṇapūrvaṃ yāgaśālāvidhiḥ**
> khadira-asana-campaka-arjuna-panasa-vakula-madhūka-aśvattha-bilva-śamīnām anyatamaṃ vidhinā āhṛtya, śoṣayitvā tvacaṃ vyapohya, mānāṅguleṇa saptāṅgulaṃ navāṅgulaṃ ekādaśāṅgulaṃ trayodaśāṅgulaṃ vā, devībhyāṃ sahitaṃ rahitaṃ vā devaṃ salakṣaṇaṃ sthitam āsīnaṃ vā kṛtvā, sthāpanadivasāt pūrvaṃ vidhinā ankurān-arpayitvā, akṣyunmocanaṃ kṛtvā, adhivāsya, bālālāyābhimukhe dakṣiṇe vā yāgaśālāṃ kṛtvā, toraṇādyaiḥ alaṅkṛtya tanmadhye śayyāvedīṃ dvihastāyatavistārāṃ tatturīyotsedhāṃ, tatra prācyāṃ sabhyāgnikuṇḍaṃ, tatra prācyām aiśānyāṃ vā snānavedīṃ ca kuryāt; pañcāgnīn kalpayet iti āhuḥ eke.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Sacrificial Hall for Consecrating the Temporary Icon (*Yāgaśālā-vidhiḥ*):**
    *   The temporary icon (*bālabera*) should be carved from one of the following wood types: Khadira, Asana, Champaka, Arjuna, Panasa, Vakula, Madhuka, Asvattha, Bilva, or Shami.
    *   The wood should be collected in accordance with the rules, dried, and its bark removed.
    *   The icon should be measured in standard digits (*mānāṅgula*) as 7, 9, 11, or 13 angulas. It may be made with or without the two Goddesses (Sridevi and Bhudevi).
    *   The deity icon should possess all proper iconographic marks, and be in a standing or sitting posture.
    *   Prior to the day of installation, having sown the sprouts (*ankura*), performed the opening of the eyes (*akṣy-unmocana*), and performed the preliminary consecration (*adhivāsa*), one should erect a sacrificial hall (*yāga-śālā*) facing the Balalaya or to its South.
    *   The hall should be decorated with archways (*toraṇa*). In its center, one should construct a bed-altar (*śayyā-vedī*) measuring two cubits in length and width, and one-fourth of that in height.
    *   There, in the East, one should make a Sabhya fire pit (*sabhya-agnikuṇḍa*), and in the East or North-East, a bathing altar (*snāna-vedī*). Some say one should arrange five fires (*pañcāgni*).

### Section 3: Consecration of the Water Pot and preliminary Rites

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **बालालयप्रतिष्ठाविधिः**
> बालालयस्योत्तरे वैखानससूत्रोक्तविधिना वास्तुहोमं हुत्वा, पर्यग्निपञ्चगव्याभ्यां शोधयित्वा, पुण्याहं वाचयेत्। सभ्याग्निकुण्डे वा आघारं हुत्वा, आलयाभिमुखे देवं संस्थाप्य, अभिमुखे कुम्भं द्रोणद्वयपूर्णं खण्ड-स्फुटित-काल-रहितं त्रिगुणेन एकेन वा सूत्रेण वैष्णवमन्त्रमुच्चार्य प्रदक्षिणं वेष्टयित्वा, "शुची वो हव्या" इति प्रक्षाल्य, "धारासु समः" इति अपामृजनं कृत्वा, कुम्भजलेन "नमः सुशेवाः" इति आपूर्य, "इदम् अपः शिवाः" इति अपोऽभिमन्त्र्य, चन्दन-उशीर-आदिभिः वासयित्वा, वस्त्रयुग्मेन आवेष्ट्य, सौवर्णादि-मङ्गल-पञ्चायुध-चिह्नानि नवरत्न-कुश-कूर्चांश्च पृथक् पृथक् विष्णुगायत्र्या क्षित्वा, धान्यपीठे कुम्भं सपिधानं संन्यस्य...

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **bālālayapratiṣṭhāvidhiḥ**
> bālālayasyottare vaikhānasasūtroktavidhinā vāstuhomaṃ hutvā, paryagnipañcagavyābhyāṃ śodhayitvā, puṇyāhaṃ vācayet. sabhyāgnikuṇḍe vā āghāraṃ hutvā, ālayābhimukhe devaṃ saṃsthāpya, abhimukhe kumbhaṃ droṇadvayapūrṇaṃ khaṇḍa-sphuṭita-kāla-rahitaṃ triguṇena ekena vā sūtreṇa vaiṣṇavamantram-uccārya pradakṣiṇaṃ veṣṭayitvā, "śucī vo havyā" iti prakṣālya, "dhārāsu samaḥ" iti apāmṛjanaṃ kṛtvā, kumbhajalena "namaḥ suśevāḥ" iti āpūrya, "idam apaḥ śivāḥ" iti apo'bhimantrya, candana-uśīra-ādibhiḥ vāsayitvā, vastrayugmena āveṣṭya, sauvarṇādi-maṅgala-pañcāyudha-cihnāni navaratna-kuśa-kūrcāṃś-ca pṛthak pṛthak viṣṇugāyatriyā kṣitvā, dhānyapīṭhe kumbhaṃ sapidhānaṃ saṃnyasya...

**Translation (English):**
*   **Preparatory Rites for the Consecration:** 
    *   To the North of the Balalaya, having performed the Vastu-homa sacrifice according to the Vaikhānasa Sutra, purified the area with fire and Panchagavya, and performed the Punyaha ritual...
    *   ...and having offered the initial ghee oblations (*āghāra*) in the Sabhya fire pit, the priest should place the deity icon facing the temple.
    *   In front of the deity, place a pot (*kumbha*) filled with water equal to two *drona* measures. The pot must be free from chips, cracks, or discoloration. It should be wrapped with single or threefold threads.
    *   Reciting the Vaishnava mantras, wind the thread clockwise. Wash the pot reciting *"śucī vo havyā..."*, wipe/purify it with water reciting *"dhārāsu samaḥ..."*, and fill it with water reciting *"namaḥ suśevāḥ..."*.
    *   Bless the water reciting *"idam apaḥ śivāḥ..."*, perfume it with sandalwood and Usheera, wrap the pot with a pair of garments, and drop into it gold, symbols of the five weapons, the nine gems, and Kusa grass/kurcas while reciting the Viṣṇu Gayatri.
    *   Place the pot with its lid on a pedestal of grains (*dhānya-pīṭha*).

M.) iti devaṃ śayane śāyayet, uttarācchādanaṃ kṛtvā, agniṃ samujjavalya vaiṣṇavaṃ hutvā, hautraṃ praśaṃsya, āvāhana-juṣṭākāra-svāhākārān kṛtvā, vaiṣṇavaṃ viṣṇusūktaṃ śrīsūktaṃ mahīsūktaṃ sarvadaivatyaṃ ca hutvā, agnim ānamya, rātriśeṣaṃ vyapohya...

**Translation (English):**
*   **The Sacrificial Bathing and Placement on the Bed-Altar:**
    *   The Acharya, facing North and seated in the Brahmā posture, should meditate according to the consecration rules, invoke the deity into the pot, and thereafter recite the *Śakuna* hymn.
    *   Taking both the pot and the icon, place them in the bathing vessel (*snāna-śvabhra*), bathe them with 14 pots of water, decorate them with garments and upper cloths, and worship with the eight services (*aṣṭopacāra*).
    *   Spread five beds or cloths on the bed-altar on top of grains. Place the deity and the pot there, perform the Punyaha, and tie a protective thread of gold or string (*pratisara*) reciting *"svastidā viśaspatiḥ..."* (Rigveda 10.66.10).
    *   Lay the Lord down on the bed reciting *"yad vaiṣṇavam..."*, and cover Him with an upper cloth.
    *   Kindling the fire, offer Vaishnava oblations, praise the Hotri recitations, perform the invocation, and offer oblations with the Viṣṇu Sūkta, Śrī Sūkta, Mahi Sūkta, and Sarva-daivatya hymns. Bow to the fire and spend the rest of the night.

### Section 4: Installation in the Sanctum and Daily Worship Protocol

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> (...प्रविश्य,) शुभे चरराशौ "प्र तद् विष्णुः" (ऋ० सं० १-१५४-२) इति देवं संस्थाप्य, विष्णुसूक्तं पुरुषसूक्तं जप्त्वा, देवपादौ स्पृशन् "भूर्भुवस्सुवः" इति बिम्बस्य मूर्ध्नि नाभौ पादयोश्च क्रमेण विन्यस्य, यकारं पादयोरन्तरे पीठे न्यस्य, अकारं हृदये न्यस्य, तं प्रणवैः वेष्टयित्वा, कुम्भस्थां शक्तिं "इदं विष्णुः" (ऋ० सं० १-२२-१७) इति मन्त्रेण कूर्चेन आदाय, "आयातु भगवान्" (वै० म०) इति बिम्बस्य मूर्ध्नि "विष्णुम् आवाहयामि" इति संस्राव्य, आवाहयेत्। "दक्षिणे श्रियं, वामे हरिणीम्" इति आवाह्य, पीठान्ते प्रागादि-द्वारादि-आवरणत्रय-देवान् आवाह्य, आसनाद्युपचारैः अभ्यर्च्य, पुण्याहं वाचयित्वा, दक्षिणे ब्रह्माणम्, उत्तरे मार्कण्डेयं, वामे शङ्करं, पद्मापितरं, द्वारेषु धात्रादि-द्वारदेवान् द्वारपालान् विष्वक्सेन-गरुड-श्रीभूत-आदि-महाभूत-अन्तान् परिषद्देवान् नवभेदेषु एकमार्गेण आवाहयेत्। पायसं शुद्धान्नं प्रभूतं वा निवेदयेत्; अन्यथा आवाहन-विसर्जनं च हित्वा नित्यार्चनाविधानेन नित्यं सम्यगर्चयेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> (...praviśya,) śubhe cararāśau "pra tad viṣṇuḥ" (Ṛgveda Saṃhitā 1.154.2) iti devaṃ saṃsthāpya, viṣṇusūktaṃ puruṣasūktaṃ japtvā, devapādau spṛśan "bhūrbhuvassuvaḥ" iti bimbasya mūrdhni nābhau pādayoś-ca krameṇa vinyasya, yakāraṃ pādayorantare pīṭhe nyasya, akāraṃ hṛdaye nyasya, taṃ praṇavaiḥ veṣṭayitvā, kumbhasthāṃ śaktiṃ "idaṃ viṣṇuḥ" (Ṛgveda Saṃhitā 1.22.17) iti mantreṇa kūrcena ādāya, "āyātu bhagavān" (Vai. M.) iti bimbasya mūrdhni "viṣṇum āvāhayāmi" iti saṃsrāvya, āvāhayet. "dakṣiṇe śriyaṃ, vāme hariṇīm" iti āvāhya, pīṭhānte prāgādi-dvārādi-āvaraṇatraya-devān āvāhya, āsanādyupacāraiḥ abhyarcya, puṇyāhaṃ vācayitvā, dakṣiṇe brahmāṇam, uttare mārkaṇḍeyaṃ, vāme śaṅkaraṃ, padmāpitaraṃ, dvāreṣu dhātrādi-dvāradevān dvārapālān viṣvaksena-garuḍa-śrībhūta-ādi-mahābhūta-antān pariṣaddevān navabhedeṣu ekamārgeṇa āvāhayet. pāyasaṃ śuddhānnaṃ prabhūtaṃ vā nivedayet; anyatra āvāhana-visarjanaṃ ca hitvā nityārcanavidhānena nityaṃ samyag-arcayet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Establishment in the Inner Sanctum:**
    *   Having entered the inner sanctum under an auspicious moveable zodiac sign, they should establish the deity reciting *"pra tad viṣṇuḥ..."* (Rigveda 1.154.2).
    *   Mutter the Viṣṇu Sūkta and Puruṣa Sūkta, touch the feet of the Lord, and mentally project the *Vyāhṛti* syllables (*bhūr bhuvaḥ suvaḥ*) onto the head, navel, and feet of the icon.
    *   Project the letter *Ya* on the pedestal between the feet, and the letter *A* in the heart. Envelop the icon with *Pranava* (*Om*).
    *   Draw the divine power from the pot onto the *Kūrca* reciting *"idaṁ viṣṇuḥ..."* (Rigveda 1.22.17), and transfer/transfuse it onto the head of the icon reciting *"āyātu bhagavān..."*.
    *   Invoke Lord Viṣṇu saying *"viṣṇum āvahāyami"*, and invoke Goddess Śrī on His right and Goddess Harini (Bhudevi) on His left.
    *   Invoke the door-deities, door-keepers, Vishvaksena, Garuḍa, and the surrounding deities (*pariṣad-devān*) in one path in the nine divisions.
    *   Offer sweet milk rice (*pāyasa*) or abundant pure food.
    *   Omitting the elaborate invocation (*āvāhana*) and dismissal (*visarjana*) on subsequent days, worship Him daily according to the rules of daily worship (*nityārcana-vidhāna*).
