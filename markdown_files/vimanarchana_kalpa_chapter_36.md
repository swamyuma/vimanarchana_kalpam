# Chapter 36: Consecration — Iconography and Placement of the Nine Images (Nava-mūrti-lakṣaṇa-vidhiḥ)
### Section 1: Iconography and Attendant Deities of the Nine Images

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **नव-मूर्तीनां मानं रूपं च -**
> अथ नवमूर्तीनां ध्रुवबेराणि मानं च वक्ष्ये — धाम-गर्भ-गृह-द्वार-स्तम्भ-यजमानमान-अङ्गुलेषु इष्टमानेन ध्रुवबेराणि कल्पयेत्। देव्यौ बाहु-कर्ण-सीमा-मानेन, मुन्यादि-परिवारांश्च तत्-समेन, त्रिपादेन, अर्धेन वा कारयेत् ॥
> ***nava-mūrtīnāṃ mānaṃ rūpaṃ ca -***
> *atha navamūrtīnāṃ dhruvaberāṇi mānaṃ ca vakṣye — dhāma-garbha-gṛha-dvāra-stambha-yajamānamāna-aṅguleṣu iṣṭamānena dhruvaberāṇi kalpayet. devyou bāhu-karṇa-sīmā-mānena, munyādi-parivārāṃśca tat-samena, tripādena, ardhena vā kārayet.*
> 
> **मध्यम-तले (Second storey):**
> *   **आदिमूर्तिः (Seated):** मध्यम-तले विष्णुम् आदिमूर्तिं सिंहासने समासीनं, श्री-भूमिभ्यां, भृगु-मार्कण्डेयाभ्यां (भृगु-पुण्याभ्यां), ब्रह्म-शङ्कराभ्यां च युक्तं; उपरि-भित्ति-ऊर्ध्वभागे दक्षिण-वामयोः वेदमानौ, चामरधारिणीं, किष्किन्धसुन्दरीं च कामिनी-व्याजिनीभ्याम् अर्क-चन्द्राभ्यां युक्तं; प्रथम-द्वारस्य दक्षिणे तुहिनं, वामे बलिदं, द्वितीय-द्वारे तापस-सिद्धीदौ च कारयेत्।
> *   **नर-नारायणौ (Gallery South):** तस्य अलिन्द्रे दक्षिणे दक्षिणामुखौ नर-नारायणौ, स्थितौ, प्रजापति-शङ्कराभ्याम् अर्चक-मुनिभ्यां कामिनी-व्याजिनीभ्याम् अर्क-चन्द्राभ्यां युक्तौ; द्वारस्य दक्षिण-वामयोः चक्रचूलि-शङ्खचूलिनौ च कारयेत्।
> *   **नृसिंहः (Gallery West):** तस्य अलिन्द्रे पश्चिमे नृसिंहं योगासने समासीनं, कक्षयोः आबद्धं (योगपट्ट-बद्धं), ब्रह्म-शङ्कराभ्याम् अर्चक-मुनिभ्याम् अर्क-चन्द्राभ्यां युक्तं; द्वारस्य दक्षिण-वामयोः चण्ड-प्रचण्डाभ्यां सहैव कुर्यात्।
> *   **वाराहः (Gallery North):** तस्य अलिन्द्रे च उत्तरे उत्तरामुखं (उत्तराभिमुखं) वाराहं पूर्ववत्; देवस्य दक्षिणे चन्द्रं, वामे हलेशं, पूजक-मुनी, ब्रह्म-शङ्करौ, अपर-भित्त्युपरि ऊर्ध्वभागे सनक-सनत्कुमारौ, चन्द्रादित्यौ, द्वार-दक्षिण-वामयोः दुर्गां विघ्नेशं च कुर्यात्।
> ***madhyama-tale (Second Storey):***
> **   **Ādimūrtiḥ:** seated on a throne, flanked by Śrī and Bhūmi, sages Bhṛgu and Mārkaṇḍeya, and Brahmā and Śiva. Above the wall are the fly-whisk bearers, Kiṣkindhasundarī, and figures representing the sun and moon. The first gateway has Tuhiṇa on the right and Balida on the left; the second has Tāpasa and Siddhīda.*
> **   **Nara-Nārāyaṇa:** In the southern gallery, facing south, seated, accompanied by Prajāpati and Śaṅkara, worshipping sages, female attendants with fans, and sun and moon. The doorway has Cakracūlin and Śaṅkhacūlin.*
> **   **Nṛsiṃha:** In the western gallery, seated in yoga-posture, bound at the knees with a yoga-strap, accompanied by Brahmā, Śiva, worshipping sages, and sun and moon. The doorway has Caṇḍa and Pracaṇḍa.*
> **   **Varāha:** In the northern gallery, facing north, with Moon on the right and Haladhara (Balarāma) on the left, worshipping sages, Brahmā and Śiva, and Sanaka-Sanatkumāra, sun and moon on the upper wall. The doors have Durgā and Gaṇeśa.*
> 
> **अधस्तले प्राच्यां (Ground storey - East):**
> *   **पुरुषः (Seated):** अधस्तले प्राच्यां पुरुषं भोगासन-मार्गेण आसीनं, पूजक-मुनिभ्याँ ब्रह्म-शङ्कराभ्यां, अपर-भित्ति-ऊर्ध्वभागे श्वेतच्छत्रेण, वामयोः चामरधारिणीभ्यां, माया-संहादिनीभ्यां, सनक-सनत्कुमाराभ्यां, चन्द्र-सूर्याभ्यां च युक्तं कारयेत्।
> *   **द्वारपालाः:** प्रथमद्वारे तुहिन-बलिन्दौ, द्वितीयद्वारे शङ्ख-पद्म-निधी, द्वार-सोपान-स्थल-भित्तौ प्राङ्मुखीं श्रियं (लक्ष्मीं) वारणाभिषेक-युक्तां कारयेत्।
> ***Ground Storey (Adhastala):***
> **   **Puruṣa (East):** Seated in *bhogāsana*, accompanied by worshipping sages, Brahmā and Śiva, with a white umbrella on the back wall, fly-whisk bearers, Māyā and Saṃhādinī, Sanaka and Sanatkumāra, and sun and moon. The first gate has Tuhiṇa and Balinda; the second has Śaṅkhanidhi and Padmanidhi. On the steps is Gaja-Lakṣmī facing east.*
> **   **Satya (South):** Seated facing south, accompanied by Goddesses, sages, Brahmā and Śiva, with umbrella, fly-whisk bearers, Jayā and Bhadrā, sun and moon, and Śukra and Bṛhaspati. The first gate has the Aśvins; the second has Cakracūlin and Śaṅkhacūlin. Sarasvatī (*Vāgdevī*) is on the southern steps wall.*
> **   **Achyuta (West):** Seated facing west, accompanied by Goddesses, sages, Brahmā and Śiva, with umbrella, fly-whisk bearers, Jayā and Bhadrā, and sun and moon. The first gate has Guha and Jāmbavat; the second has Śaṅkhanidhi and Padmanidhi. Kāmadeva (*Manmatha*) is on the steps wall.*
> **   **Aniruddha (North):** Seated facing north, with or without Goddesses, accompanied by sages, Brahmā and Śiva, with umbrella, fly-whisk bearers, Jayā and Puṣṭikā, and sun and moon. The first gate has Vidyut and Apsaras; the second has Cakracūlin and Śaṅkhacūlin. Sage Mudgala is on the steps wall.*
> 
> **दक्षिणे (Ground storey - South):**
> *   **सत्यः (Seated):** दक्षिणे सत्यमूर्तिं दक्षिणामुखं, देवीभ्यां, पूजक-मुनिभ्याँ ब्रह्म-शङ्कराभ्यां, अपर-भित्ति-ऊर्ध्वभागे छत्रेण चामरधारिणीभ्यां जया-भद्राभ्यां सूर्य-चन्द्राभ्यां शुक्र-बृहस्पतिभ्यां युक्तं; प्रथमद्वारे नासत्य-दस्रौ, द्वितीयद्वारे चक्र-शङ्ख-चूलिनौ, दक्षिणे सोपान-स्थल-भित्तौ दक्षिणामुखं वाग्देवीं (सरस्वतीं) च कारयेत्।
> ***Third Storey (Tritala):***
> **   **Yoga-śayana (Reclining):** Reclining with His head to the south, shaded by the seven or five hoods of Ananta. Goddess Bhūmi and Sage Mārkaṇḍeya are at His feet; His right hand rests on Mārkaṇḍeya's head, and His left hand is in the *kaṭaka* posture. Brahmā is seated on the navel-lotus, accompanied by the five weapons, and Garuḍa. Near His feet are Madhu and Kaiṭabha; on the right is Karki (Kaustubha) and Sanaka; on the left is Sarasvatī and Sanatkumāra, with the Vedas above, and Brahmā and Śiva. The first gate has Gaṅgā and Yamunā; the second has Surasundarī.*
> **   **Kautuka-bera:** In the center, a four-armed *kautuka-bera* holding conch and discus, seated on Ananta's lap and shaded by His seven or five hoods.*
> 
> **पश्चिमे (Ground storey - West):**
> *   **अच्युतः (Seated):** पश्चिमे पश्चिमाभिमुखम् अच्युतं, देवीभ्यां, पूजक-मुनिभ्याँ ब्रह्म-शङ्कराभ्यां, अपर-भित्ति-ऊर्ध्वभागे छत्रेण चामरधारिणीभ्यां जया-भद्राभ्यां सूर्य-चन्द्राभ्यां युक्तं; प्रथमद्वारे गुह-जाम्बवन्तौ, द्वितीयद्वारे शङ्ख-पद्म-निधी, सोपान-स्थल-भित्तौ पश्चिमामुखं मन्मथं कारयेत्।
> 
> **उत्तरे (Ground storey - North):**
> *   **अनिरुद्धः (Seated):** उत्तरे उत्तराभिमुखम् अनिरुद्धं, देवीभ्यां सहितं रहितं वा, पूजक-मुनिभ्याँ ब्रह्म-शङ्कराभ्यां, अपर-भित्ति-ऊर्ध्वभागे छत्रेण चामरधारिणीभ्यां जया-पुष्टिकाभ्यां सूर्य-चन्द्राभ्यां युक्तं; प्रथमद्वारे विद्युत्-अप्सरसौ, द्वितीयद्वारे चक्र-शङ्ख-चूलिनौ, सोपान-स्थल-भित्तौ च उत्तराभिमुखं मुद्गलं मुनिं च कारयेत्।
> 
> **तृतीयतले (Third storey):**
> *   **योगशयनम् (Reclining):** तृतीयतले योगशयनं, दक्षिण-शीर्षं, अनन्तस्य सप्तभिः पञ्चभिः वा फणैः छन्न-मौलिं, द्विभुजं शयानं; पूजक-स्थाने मही-मार्कण्डेयौ, देवस्य दक्षिणहस्तं मार्कण्डेय-शिरसि न्यस्तं, वामं समुद्धत-कटकं, पादं दक्षिणं प्रसारितम् ईषत्-कुञ्चितं, नाभ्यम्बुजे समासीनं ब्रह्माणं, पञ्चायुधान्, गरुडं च उद्देश्य-करतल-प्रहार-युक्तं, पाद-पार्श्वे मधु-कैटभौ च; दक्षिण-पार्श्वे कर्किणं (कौस्तुभं) सनकं, वामे भारतीं सनत्कुमारं, ऊर्ध्वं वेदान्; दक्षिण-ोत्तरयोः ब्रह्म-शङ्करौ; प्रथम-द्वारे गङ्गा-यमुने, द्वितीय-द्वारे सुरसुन्दरीं च कारयेत्।
> 
> **ब्रह्मस्थाने कौतुकम् -**
> ब्रह्मस्थाने कौतुकं चतुर्भुजं शङ्ख-चक्र-धरं अनन्तोत्सङ्गे आसीनं, फणैः सप्तभिः पञ्चभिः वा छन्न-मौलिं कारयेत्।
> 
> आदिमूर्तीनां सर्वासाम् मूर्तीनां तत्-तत्-कौतुक-बिम्बानां पूर्वोक्त-विधिना तत्-तत्-ध्रुवबेर-अनुरूपं, विष्णुं चतुर्भुजं वा कारयेत्। इन्द्रादि-लोकपालानाम् अन्येषां परिवाराणां नवभेदेषु एकमार्गेण नव-षट्-पञ्च-मूर्तीः विमाने कारयेत् ॥

#### English Translation
*   **Iconographic Standards:** I shall describe the measurements and forms of the nine images. The fixed images (*dhruva-bera*) should be fashioned using the standard cubit/finger measurements of the temple sanctum, doorway, pillar, or the patron's hand. The Goddesses are made according to the arm-to-ear proportion, and the subsidiary sages and entourage deities are made in equal, three-quarter, or half height.

*   **Worship on the Second Storey (*Bhūmi-saṃsthā-lakṣaṇam*):**
    *   The primary *Ādimūrti* is seated on a throne, flanked by Śrī and Bhūmi, sages Bhṛgu and Mārkaṇḍeya, and Brahmā and Śiva. The back walls feature fly-whisk bearers, Kiṣkindhasundarī, and figures representing the sun and moon. Gateways are guarded by Tuhiṇa-Balida and Tāpasa-Siddhīda.
    *   Nara-Nārāyaṇa sit in the south gallery, facing south, flanked by Prajāpati and Śaṅkara, worshipping sages, and attendants. Gateway is guarded by Cakracūlin and Śaṅkhacūlin.
    *   Nṛsiṃha sits in the west gallery in yoga-posture, bound at the knees with a yoga-strap, accompanied by Brahmā, Śiva, sages, and sun/moon. Gateway is guarded by Caṇḍa and Pracaṇḍa.
    *   Varāha sits in the north gallery facing north, flanked by the Moon and Haladhara (Balarāma), sages, Brahmā and Śiva, and Sanaka-Sanatkumāra. Gateway is guarded by Durgā and Gaṇeśa.

*   **Worship on the Ground Storey (*Bhūmi-bhāga-lakṣaṇam*):**
    *   **Puruṣa (East):** Seated in *bhogāsana*, accompanied by sages, Brahmā, Śiva, a white umbrella, fly-whisk bearers, Māyā and Saṃhādinī, Sanaka and Sanatkumāra, and sun and moon. The first gate has Tuhiṇa and Balinda; the second has Śaṅkhanidhi and Padmanidhi. On the steps is Gaja-Lakṣmī.
    *   **Satya (South):** Seated facing south, accompanied by Goddesses, sages, Brahmā, Śiva, umbrella, fly-whisk bearers, Jayā and Bhadrā, sun and moon, and Śukra and Bṛhaspati. The first gate has the Aśvins; the second has Cakracūlin and Śaṅkhacūlin. Sarasvatī (*Vāgdevī*) is on the southern steps wall.
    *   **Achyuta (West):** Seated facing west, accompanied by Goddesses, sages, Brahmā, Śiva, umbrella, fly-whisk bearers, Jayā and Bhadrā, and sun and moon. The first gate has Guha and Jāmbavat; the second has Śaṅkhanidhi and Padmanidhi. Kāmadeva (*Manmatha*) is on the steps wall.
    *   **Aniruddha (North):** Seated facing north, with or without Goddesses, accompanied by sages, Brahmā, Śiva, umbrella, fly-whisk bearers, Jayā and Puṣṭikā, and sun and moon. The first gate has Vidyut and Apsaras; the second has Cakracūlin and Śaṅkhacūlin. Sage Mudgala is on the steps wall.

*   **Worship on the Third Storey (*Tritala-lakṣaṇam*):**
    *   **Yoga-śayana:** Reclining with His head to the south, shaded by the seven or five hoods of Ananta. Goddess Bhūmi and Sage Mārkaṇḍeya are at His feet; His right hand rests on Mārkaṇḍeya's head, and His left hand is in the *kaṭaka* posture. Brahmā is seated on the navel-lotus, accompanied by the five weapons, and Garuḍa. Near His feet are Madhu and Kaiṭabha; on the right is Karki (Kaustubha) and Sanaka; on the left is Sarasvatī and Sanatkumāra, with the Vedas above, and Brahmā and Śiva. The first gate has Gaṅgā and Yamunā; the second has Surasundarī.
    *   **The Kautuka Image:** In the central position, the four-armed *kautuka-bera* holds the conch and discus, seated on the lap of Ananta and shaded by His seven or five hoods. The other movable images must represent the four-armed form of Viṣṇu matching the respective fixed images.
