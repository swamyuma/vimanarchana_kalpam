# Chapter 37: Consecration — The Consecration Rituals for the Multi-Image layouts (Nava-ṣaṭ-pañca-mūrti-pratiṣṭhā-vidhiḥ)
### Section 1: Consecration Rites and Layouts of the Sacrificial Halls

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **नव-षट्-पञ्च-मूर्ति-प्रतिष्ठा-विधिः -**
> अथ नव-षट्-पञ्च-मूर्तीनां प्रतिष्ठा-विशेषं वक्ष्ये — पूर्वोक्तेषु नक्षत्रेषु एकस्मिन् यजमान-अनुकूले नक्षत्रे प्रतिष्ठां कुर्यात्। तद्-दिनात् पूर्वं यथोक्तम् अङ्कुरान् अर्पयित्वा, नवमूर्तीः समुद्दिश्य प्रत्येकम् आचार्यं स्थापकान् ऋत्विजश्च पूर्वोक्त-लक्षण-सम्पन्नान् उक्त-दोष-विवर्जितान् वरयेत् ॥
> ***nava-ṣaṭ-pañca-mūrti-pratiṣṭhā-vidhiḥ -***
> *atha nava-ṣaṭ-pañca-mūrtīnāṃ pratiṣṭhā-viśeṣaṃ vakṣye — pūrvokteṣu nakṣatreṣu ekasmin yajamana-anukūle nakṣatre pratiṣṭhāṃ kuryāt. tad-dināt pūrvaṃ yathoktam aṅkurān arpayitvā, navamūrtīḥ samuddicya pratyekam ācāryaṃ sthāpakān ṛtvijaśca pūrvokta-lakṣaṇa-sampannān ukta-doṣa-vivarjitān varayet.*
> 
> **आदिमूर्तेः यागशाला-लक्षणम् -**
> तथैव सम्भारान् सम्भृत्य, आलय-अभिमुखे विष्णोः आदिमूर्त्तेः यागशालां पञ्च-हस्त-आयत-विस्तारां षट्त्रिंशत्-स्तम्भ-संयुक्तां प्रपां कृत्वा; तन्मध्ये वर्ष-निवारण-कूटं कृत्वा, तस्मिन् पञ्चविंशति-भागं कृत्वा, तन्मध्ये पदे शय्या-वेदिं तद्-भित्ति-सम-आयाम-विस्तारां तद्-तुरीयांश-उत्सेधां दर्पण-उदर-वत् स्थलं सङ्कल्प्य;
> ***ādimūrteḥ yāgāśālā-lakṣaṇam -***
> *tathaiva sambhārān sambhṛtya, ālaya-abhimukhe viṣṇoḥ ādimūtteḥ yāgāśālāṃ pañca-hast-āyata-vistārāṃ ṣaṭtriṃśat-stambha-saṃyuktāṃ prapāṃ kṛtvā; tanmadhye varṣa-nivāraṇa-kūṭaṃ kṛtvā, tasmin pañcavaiṃśati-bhāgaṃ kṛtvā, tanmadhye pade śayyā-vediṃ tad-bhitti-sama-āyāma-vistārāṃ tad-turīyāṃśa-utsedhāṃ darpaṇa-udara-vat sthalaṃ saṅkalpya;*
> 
> परितश्च अष्टसु पदेषु उपपदेषु अष्टाङ्गुल-उत्सेधानि स्थलानि कृत्वा; शय्यावेद्याः पूर्वे सभ्यम् आहवनीयं च, याम्ये अन्वाहार्यम्, पश्चिमे गार्हपत्यं, सौम्ये चावसथ्यम्, आग्नेय्यां पौण्डरीकं, नैर्ऋत्यां विष्टरं, वायव्यां सम्भारवेदिम्, ऐशान्यां स्नानश्वभ्रं तद्-बाह्ये षोडशभाग-एकभाग-अङ्गुल-उत्सेधं स्थलं कृत्वा;
> *paritaśca aṣṭasu padeṣu upapadeṣu aṣṭāṅgula-utsedhāni sthalāni kṛtvā; śayyāvedyāḥ pūrve sabhyam āhavanīyaṃ ca, yāmye anvāhāryam, paścime gārhapatyaṃ, saumye cāvasathyama, āgneyyāṃ pouṇḍarīkaṃ, nairṛtyāṃ viṣṭaraṃ, vāyavyāṃ sambhāravedim, aiśānyāṃ snānaśvabhraṃ tad-bāhye ṣoḍaśabhāga-ekabhāga-aṅgula-utsedhaṃ sthalaṃ kṛtvā;*
> 
> इन्द्रादि-दिग्देवानां तत्-तत्-दिक्षु औपासन-अग्निकुण्डानि कुर्यात्।
> *indrādi-digdevānāṃ tat-tat-dikṣu oupāsana-agnikuṇḍāni kuryāt.*
> 
> **मध्य-अन्तराल-अग्निकुण्डानि -**
> इन्द्र-अग्न्योः मध्ये गरुडस्य; यम-अग्न्योः (यम-पावकयोः) मध्ये ब्रह्मणः; यम-निरृत्योः (यम-नीयोः) मध्ये भृगोः; निरृति-वरुणयोः (नील-वरुणयोः) मध्ये श्रीदेव्याः; वरुण-वाय्वोः (वरुण-उदानयोः) मध्ये महीदेव्याः; वायु-सोमयोः मध्ये मार्कण्डेयस्य; सोम-ईशानयोः मध्ये शङ्करस्य; इन्द्र-ईशानयोः मध्ये विष्वक्सेनस्य च; अन्येषां परिवाराणां तत्-तत्-दिक्षु यागशालां परितश्च औपासन-अग्निकुण्डानि कुर्यात्।
> ***madhya-antarāla-agnikuṇḍāni -***
> *indra-agnoyoḥ madhye garuḍasya; yama-agnoyoḥ (yama-pāvakayoḥ) madhye brahmaṇaḥ; yama-nirṛtyoḥ (yama-nīyoḥ) madhye bhṛgoḥ; nirṛti-varuṇayoḥ (nīla-varuṇayoḥ) madhye śrīdevyāḥ; varuṇa-vāyvoḥ (varuṇa-udānayoḥ) madhye mahīdevyāḥ; vāyu-somayoḥ madhye mārkaṇḍeyasya; soma-īśānayoḥ madhye śaṅkarasya; indra-īśānayoḥ madhye viṣvaksenasya ca; anyeṣāṃ parivārāṇāṃ tat-tat-dikṣu yāgāśālāṃ paritaśca oupāsana-agnikuṇḍāni kuryāt.*
> 
> तेषां बेरालयं यदि स्यात्, तत्-तत्-अभिमुखे च औपासन-अग्निकुण्डं कुम्भ-पूजनं च पृथग् एव कुर्यात्। बिम्बाभावेऽपि इन्द्रादि-ईशान-पर्यन्तानां द्वारेषु द्वारपालानं न्यक्षादीनाम् अभिमुखे श्रीभूतस्य अनपायिनं च होमं कुर्याद् इति केचित्।
> *teṣāṃ berālayaṃ yadi syāt, tat-tat-abhimukhe ca oupāsana-agnikuṇḍaṃ kumbha-pūjanaṃ ca pṛthag eva kuryāt. bimbābhāve'pi indrādi-īśāna-paryantānāṃ dvāreṣu dvārapālānaṃ nyakṣādīnām abhimukhe śrībhūtasya anapāyinaṃ ca homaṃ kuryād iti kecit.*
> 
> **पुरुषादीनां यागशाला-लक्षणम् -**
> प्राच्यां पुरुषमूर्त्तेः यागशालां षोडश-स्तम्भ-संयुक्तां तन्मध्ये शय्या-वेदिं परितः चतुरग्निकुण्डानि च परिकल्प्य;
> ***puruṣādīnāṃ yāgāśālā-lakṣaṇam -***
> *prācyāṃ puruṣamūtteḥ yāgāśālāṃ ṣoḍaśa-stambha-saṃyuktāṃ tanmadhye śayyā-vediṃ paritaḥ caturagnikuṇḍāni ca parikalpya;*
> 
> आलयाद् दक्षिणे तद्-द्वार-अभिमुखे सत्यमूर्त्तेः यागशालां तन्मध्ये शय्या-वेदिं परितः चतुरग्निकुण्डानि परिकल्प्य;
> *ālayād dakṣiṇe tad-dvāra-abhimukhe satyamūtteḥ yāgāśālāṃ tanmadhye śayyā-vediṃ paritaḥ caturagnikuṇḍāni parikalpya;*
> 
> तद्-दक्षिणे नरनारायणयोः यागशालां तन्मध्ये शय्या-वेदिं तन्मध्ये चतुरस्रं सङ्कल्प्य;
> *tad-dakṣiṇe naranārāyaṇayoḥ yāgāśālāṃ tanmadhye śayyā-vediṃ tanmadhye caturasraṃ saṅkalpya;*
> 
> पश्चिमे अच्युतमूर्त्तेः यागशालां तन्मध्ये शय्या-वेदिं परितः चतुरस्र-अग्निकुण्डान्;
> *paścime acyutamūtteḥ yāgāśālāṃ tanmadhye śayyā-vediṃ paritaḥ caturasra-agnikuṇḍān;*
> 
> तत्-पश्चिमे नृसिंहस्य यागशालां तन्मध्ये शय्या-वेदिं परितः चतुरस्र-अग्निकुण्डान् परिकल्प्य;
> *tat-paścime nṛsiṃhasya yāgāśālāṃ tanmadhye śayyā-vediṃ paritaḥ caturasra-agnikuṇḍān parikalpya;*
> 
> उत्तरे अनिरुद्धमूर्त्तेः यागशालां तन्मध्ये शय्या-वेदिं परितः चतुरस्र-अग्निकुण्डान् परिकल्प्य;
> *uttare aniruddhamūtteḥ yāgāśālāṃ tanmadhye śayyā-vediṃ paritaḥ caturasra-agnikuṇḍān parikalpya;*
> 
> तस्य उत्तरे वाराहस्य यागशालां तन्मध्ये शय्या-वेदिं परितः चतुरस्र-अग्निकुण्डान् परिकल्प्य; अन्यत् सर्वं पूर्ववत् कुर्यात्।
> *tasya uttare vārāhasya yāgāśālāṃ tanmadhye śayyā-vediṃ paritaḥ caturasra-agnikuṇḍān parikalpya; anyat sarvaṃ pūrvavat kuryāt.*
> 
> आलयस्य उत्तरे श्रामणकाग्निकुण्डं कृत्वा, सूत्रोक्त-विधिना वास्तु-होमं हुत्वा, सर्वत्र पर्यग्नि-पञ्चगव्याभ्यां संशोध्य, प्रोक्षणैः प्रोक्ष्य, अधिवासनगतान् देवान् आदाय अभिषिच्य, वस्त्र-उत्तरीयाद्यैः विभूष्य, अष्ट-उपचारैः अभ्यर्च्य, आलयम् आवेश्य, तत्-तत्-यागशालायां पश्चिमे विष्टरे प्राङ्मुखान् देवान् संस्थाप्य, सर्वेषु अग्निकुण्डेषु आघारं हुत्वा, सर्वेषां आचार्याः पूर्ववत् प्रत्येकं कुम्भ-पूजनं कुर्युः। यजमानः आचार्यान् वस्त्राद्यैः अर्चनं कुर्यात् ॥
> *ālayasya uttare śrāmaṇakagnikuṇḍaṃ kṛtvā, sūtrokta-vidhinā vāstu-homaṃ hutvā, sarvatra paryagni-pañcagavyābhyāṃ saṃśodhya, prokṣaṇaiḥ prokṣya, adhivāsanagatān devān ādāya abhiṣicya, vastra-uttarīyādyaiḥ vibhūṣya, aṣṭa-upacāraiḥ abhyarcya, ālayam āveśya, tat-tat-yāgāśālāyāṃ paścime viṣṭare prāṅmukhān devān saṃsthāpya, sarveṣu agnikuṇḍeṣu āghāraṃ hutvā, sarveṣāṃ ācāryāḥ pūrvavat pratyekaṃ kumbha-pūjanaṃ kuryuḥ. yajamānaḥ ācāryān vastrādyaiḥ arcanaṃ kuryāt.*

#### English Translation
*   **Auspicious Timing and Priestly Selection:** I shall describe the specific consecration rites for the nine, six, and five forms of Viṣṇu. The priest should perform the consecration under an auspicious asterism (*nakṣatra*) that is favorable to the patron (*yajamāna*). Prior to that day, having sown the seeds (*aṅkurārpaṇa*), he should choose one principal teacher (*Ācārya*), assistants (*Sthāpakas*), and priests (*Ṛtvijs*) for each of the nine deities, ensuring they are endowed with scriptural qualities and free from blemishes.

*   **The Main Altar for the Primary Deity (Ādimūrti):**
    *   Having gathered the materials, facing the temple, the builder sets up a sacrificial hall (*yāgāśālā*) for the primary *Ādimūrti* measuring five cubits in length and width, supported by thirty-six pillars.
    *   In the center, he constructs a weather-proof canopy (*kūṭa*), divides it into twenty-five compartments, and sets up a couch-altar (*śayyā-vedi*) in the middle, sized to match the walls, with its height being one-quarter of the image, level and smooth like a mirror.
    *   He raises eight platforms, eight inches high, in the surrounding intermediate compartments.

*   **Layout of the Fire-Pits:**
    *   To the east of the couch-altar, he places the *Sabhya* and *Āhavanīya* fires; to the south (*Yāmya*), the *Anvāhārya* fire; to the west, the *Gārhapatya* fire; to the north, the *Āvasathya* fire. To the southeast, he places the *Pauṇḍarīka* fire; to the southwest, the *Viṣṭara* (grass seat); to the northwest, the material altar (*sambhāra-vedi*); and to the northeast, the bathing pit (*snāna-śvabhra*).
    *   Along the outer perimeter, he raises a platform one-sixteenth of an inch high. He sets up domestic fire-pits (*aupāsana-agni*) for the directional guardians like Indra in their respective directions.

*   **Intermediate Deities Fire-Pits:**
    *   Between East and Southeast (Indra and Agni): For Garuḍa.
    *   Between South and Southeast (Yama and Agni): For Brahmā.
    *   Between South and Southwest (Yama and Nirṛti): For Bhṛgu.
    *   Between Southwest and West (Nirṛti and Varuṇa): For Goddess Śrī.
    *   Between West and Northwest (Varuṇa and Vāyu): For Goddess Bhūmi (*Mahī*).
    *   Between Northwest and North (Vāyu and Soma): For Mārkaṇḍeya.
    *   Between North and Northeast (Soma and Īśāna): For Śiva (*Śaṅkara*).
    *   Between Northeast and East (Īśāna and Indra): For Viṣvaksena.
    *   If separate shrines exist for these deities, he should set up individual domestic fire-pits and conduct pot-worship directly facing them. Even if images are absent, some say he should offer fire oblations facing the doorways for the door-guardians and other gate deities.

*   **Individual Sacrificial Halls for the Other Eight Images:**
    *   **Puruṣa (East):** A sacrificial hall with sixteen pillars, a couch-altar in the center, and four surrounding fire-pits.
    *   **Satya (South):** Facing the southern door, a hall with a couch-altar and four fire-pits.
    *   **Nara-Nārāyaṇa (South-South):** A hall with a couch-altar and square compartments.
    *   **Achyuta (West):** A hall with a couch-altar and square fire-pits.
    *   **Nṛsiṃha (West-West):** A hall with a couch-altar and square fire-pits.
    *   **Aniruddha (North):** A hall with a couch-altar and square fire-pits.
    *   **Varāha (North-North):** A hall with a couch-altar and square fire-pits.

*   **Purification of the Halls:**
    *   To the north of the temple, having prepared the *Śrāmaṇaka* fire-pit and offered the site-homa (*vāstu-homa*), he purifies all areas with fire and the five products of the cow (*pañca-gavya*), and sprinkles them with water.
    *   He brings the deities from their temporary placement (*adhivāsana*), bathes them, adorns them with garments, upper cloths, and ornaments, and worships them with eight services (*aṣṭopacāra*).
    *   Entering the temple, he establishes the deities on seats facing east in the west of their respective sacrificial halls. Offering ghee in all the fire-pits, the teachers perform the pot-worship (*kumbha-pūjā*) for each. The patron honors the teachers with garments and gems.

### Section 2: Preparations, couch Placement, and Chief Fires

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **कुम्भ-संन्यासादि-शयन-अधिवासन-विधिः -**
> कुम्भान् आदाय तन्तुना परिवेष्ट्य, प्रक्षाल्य, नादेयैः वारिभिः पूरयित्वा, पृथक् पृथक् वस्त्र-युग्मेन आवेष्ट्य, प्रत्येकं नवरत्नानि सौवर्णानि अष्टमङ्गल-पञ्चायुध-वर्णचिह्नानि च विन्यस्य; तत्-तत्-अगारे, बालागारे वा देवाभिमुखे धान्यराश्युपरि कुम्भान् संन्यस्य, पूर्ववद् ध्यात्वा आवाह्य; पश्चात् स्थापकान् आहूय, आदिमूर्त्यादीन् यथाक्रमेण आचार्याः तत्-तत्-समादाय पुरतो गच्छेयुः।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **kumbha-saṃnyāsādi-śayana-adhivāsana-vidhiḥ -**
> kumbhān ādāya tantunā pariveṣṭya, prakṣālya, nādeyaiḥ vāribhiḥ pūrayitvā, pṛthak pṛthak vastra-yugmena āveṣṭya, pratyekaṃ navaratnāni souvarṇāni aṣṭamaṅgala-pañcāyudha-varṇacihnāni ca vinyasya; tat-tat-agāre, bālāgāre vā devābhimukhe dhānyarāśyupari kumbhān saṃnyasya, pūrvavad dhyātvā āvāhya; paścāt sthāpakān āhūya, ādimūrtyādīn yathākrameṇa ācāryāḥ tat-tat-samādāya purato gaccheyuḥ.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Preparation of the Ritual Pots:** Taking the pots, the priests wrap them with thread, wash them, and fill them with river water. They wrap each pot in a pair of cloths and place inside them the nine gems, golden replicas of the eight auspicious signs, the five weapons, and color signs.

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> तत्-तत्-यागशालाम् आसाद्य, स्नानश्वभ्रे प्रतिष्ठाप्य, सप्तभिः कलशैः संस्नाप्य, वस्त्र-उत्तरीय-आभरणैः अलङ्कृत्य, अभ्यर्च्य; पुण्याहान्ते प्रतिसरं बद्ध्वा, तत्-तत्-वेद्यां धान्योपरि पञ्च-शयनानि वासांसि वा आस्तीर्य, तथैव शाययित्वा, उत्तराच्छादनं कृत्वा; होतृन् सर्वान् वस्त्र-उत्तरीयाद्यैः अलङ्कृत्य, तत्-तत्-प्रधानहोमे हौत्रं प्रशंस्य, तथैव आवाहनादीनि कुर्यात्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**

#### English Translation
*   **Secondary Homa Rites:** Having performed the thousand-fold oblation (*sahasrāhuti*), he offers the five *Vāruṇa* offerings, the *Kūṣmāṇḍa* homa (using *"yad devāḥ"*), the *सर्वदेवत्य* mantras starting with *"brahma jajñānaṃ"*, the *Vaiṣṇava* offerings, the *vinda-homa*, and the *vicchinna-homa* once.

*   **The Main Altar Oblations:** In the *Āhavanīya*, he offers the *Puruṣa-sūkta*; in the *Anvāhārya*, the *Viṣṇu-sūkta*; in the *Gārhapatya*, the *Vaiṣṇava* homa; and in the *Āvasathya*, the *Ekākṣara* sūkta three times.

*   **Special Offerings:**
    *   With the *Viṣṇu-sūkta*, he casts offerings mixed with milk, curd, ghee, parched grain, and raw rice in the five fires using the *upajuhū* ladle.
    *   Taking ghee in the *sruk*, he offers the *Vaiṣṇava* homa once and then offers it in the *Pauṇḍarīka* fire.
    *   He offers a hundred lotuses or Bilva leaves smeared with ghee while reciting the *Viṣṇu-Gāyatrī*.
    *   Meditating on the Lord, he offers oblations with the *Puruṣa-sūkta* and the *Pāramātmika* hymns starting with *"viṣṇuḥ sarveṣām"*.
    *   For the assembly deities (*pariṣad-devatā*), he offers their respective mantras twelve or eight times.

*   **Oblation Details for the Other Eight Images:**
    *   **Yoga-śayana (3rd Storey):** The priest conducts the fire offerings in the five fires, the *hotṛ* hymns, and the invocation of the deity forms according to the couch-placement rules.
    *   **Puruṣa (East):** In his sacrificial hall: *Puruṣa-sūkta* in the *Āhavanīya*, *Viṣṇu-sūkta* in the *Anvāhārya*, *Vaiṣṇava* homa (*Śrī* deity) in the *Gārhapatya*, the worshippers' mantra, and *Ekākṣara* sūkta (*Bhūmi* deity) and the *Raudra* homa in the *Āvasathya*.
    *   **Satya (South):** In his hall: *Viṣṇu-sūkta* in the *Anvāhārya*, *Vaiṣṇava* homa (*Śrī* deity) and *Brāhma* homa in the *Gārhapatya*, *Ekākṣara* sūkta (Goddess Bhūmi and Mārkaṇḍeya's mantra) in the *Āvasathya*, and *Puruṣa-sūkta* (*Bhūmi* deity) and *Raudra* homa in the *Āhavanīya*.
    *   **Achyuta (West):** In his hall: *Vaiṣṇava* homa in the *Gārhapatya*, *Ekākṣara* sūkta (*Śrī* deity) and *Brāhma* homa in the *Āvasathya*, *Puruṣa-sūkta* (sages' mantra) in the *Āhavanīya*, and *Viṣṇu-sūkta* (*Bhūmi* deity) and *Raudra* homa in the *Anvāhārya*.
    *   **Aniruddha (North):** In his hall: *Ekākṣara* sūkta in the *Āvasathya*, *Puruṣa-sūkta* (*Śrī* deity) and *Brāhma* homa in the *Āhavanīya*, *Viṣṇu-sūkta* (sages' mantra) in the *Anvāhārya*, and *Vaiṣṇava* homa (*Bhūmi* deity) and *Vaiṣṇava* homa in the *Gārhapatya*.
    *   **Nara-Nārāyaṇa (2nd Storey South):** In their hall: *Viṣṇu-sūkta* and *Viṣṇu-Gāyatrī* in the *Anvāhārya*, *Vaiṣṇava* homa (*Āditya* deity) in the *Gārhapatya*, *Ekākṣara* sūkta in the *Āvasathya*, and *Puruṣa-sūkta* and *Prājāpatya* homa in the *Āhavanīya*.
    *   **Nṛsiṃha (2nd Storey West):** In his hall: *Vaiṣṇava* homa in the *Gārhapatya*, *"yo vā nṛsiṃhaḥ"* once, *Ekākṣara* sūkta and *Brāhma* homa in the *Āvasathya*, and *Puruṣa-sūkta* in the *Āhavanīya*, and *Viṣṇu-sūkta* and *Raudra* homa in the *Anvāhārya*.
    *   **Varāha (2nd Storey North):** In his hall: *Ekākṣara* sūkta and *"yat svayaṃ sṛṣṭam"* once (*Bhūmi* deity) in the *Āvasathya*, *Puruṣa-sūkta* and *Aindra* homa in the *Āhavanīya*, *Viṣṇu-sūkta* in the *Anvāhārya*, and *Vaiṣṇava* homa and *Vāruṇa* homa in the *Gārhapatya*.

*   **Alternative General Method:** He chants all the mantras sixteen or twelve times. Some say that for the four forms starting with Puruṣa, and the four starting with Nara-Nārāyaṇa, the priest should offer the homa specifically into their designated chief fires.

> ***āvāhanaṃ pūjā ca -***

> *ṣaṇmūrti-pratiṣṭhā cet, viṣṇu-adi-pañcamūrtīnāṃ śayanaṃ ca yathokta-vidhinā pratiṣṭhāpayet.*

#### English Translation
*   **The Final Offerings (*Antahoma*):** Excluding the *Sabhya* and *Pauṇḍarīka* fires of the primary image and the designated chief fires of the other images, the priest offers the final oblation (*antahoma*) in all the other fires. They keep vigil for the remainder of the night.

*   **Morning Awakening:**
    *   In the morning, the *Ācārya*, the *Sthāpakas*, and others bathe, enter the temple, and circumambulate it.
    *   They return to the sacrificial halls, awaken all the deities as before, and worship them.
    *   They perform the gem-placement (*ratna-nyāsa*) on the pedestals of the movable images (*kautuka-pīṭha*) in all the inner sanctums.
    *   Excluding the *Sabhya* fire, they offer the final oblation (*antahoma*) in all other fires.
    *   The patron offers the prescribed fees (*dakṣiṇā*) along with water in front of the deity to the *Ācāryas* and others.
    *   Strewing grass around the *Sabhya* fire, he offers the final oblation and establishes that fire for daily temple worship (*nitya-homa*).

*   **The Grand Procession (*Pratiṣṭhā-praveśaḥ*):**
    *   When the auspicious moment arrives, the *Ācāryas* enter the sacrificial halls, offer a handful of flowers saying *"bhūḥ prapadye"*, bow to the respective deities, and recite the *Śakuna-sūkta*.
    *   Accompanied by all decorations, the sounding of all musical instruments, and preceded by a stream of water, the *Ācāryas*, carrying the pots on their heads, walk in front. The *Sthāpakas*, carrying the movable images, follow.
    *   They circumambulate the temple clockwise, carrying first the primary *Ādimūrti*, and then Puruṣa, Satya, Achyuta, Aniruddha, the reclining deity, Nara-Nārāyaṇa, Nṛsiṃha, and Varāha in order.
    *   Entering their respective sanctums, they place the pots to the right side of the fixed images (*dhruva-bera*). They establish the movable images (*kautuka-bera*) while chanting *"pra tad viṣṇuḥ"*, and place the two Goddesses on both sides as before.

*   **Worship and Invocation:** Reciting the *Ātma-sūkta*, touching the feet of the fixed image, and chanting the sūktas, they perform the placement of syllables (*akṣara-nyāsa*). Concentrating their minds and meditating on the Lord, they invoke Him as before. Having performed the *puṇyāha-vācana*, they worship Him according to the rules of daily worship, offer sweet rice (*pāyasa*) and other food oblations, offer the fire oblation, distribute the offerings (*bali*), and perform the lifting of the bali. This constitutes the special process; all else is identical to the standard rules. If establishing six images, he should consecrate the reclining posture of the five images starting with Viṣṇu according to the rules.

### Section 5: Simplified Layouts for Unable Patrons (Aśakta-viṣaya-kalpaḥ)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **अशक्त-विषयक-कल्पः (Simplified layouts) -**
> एवं सर्वासां मूर्तीनां पृथक् कर्तुम् अशक्तश्चेत्, प्राच्याम् एकां यागशालां पूर्ववत् कृत्वा; मध्ये पदे विष्णोः प्रागादि-ईशानान्तम् अष्टसु पदेषु यथाक्रमेण संवेशनं (शयन-वेदि-स्थापनं) — यथाक्रमेण अनन्तशयन-पुरुष-सत्य-नरनारायण-अच्युत-नृसिंह-अनिरुद्ध-वाराहाणाम् शयनवेदिं, तत्-तत्-प्राच्यां तत्-तत्-प्रधानाग्निं च कृत्वा एकाग्निविधानेन प्रतिष्ठापयेद् इति केचित् ॥
> ***aśakta-viṣayaka-kalpaḥ -***

> अशक्तानाम् एकतल-प्रासादे नव-षट्-पञ्च-मूर्तीनां प्रतिष्ठा-विशेषं वक्ष्ये — पूर्ववद् आचार्यम् एकं, स्थापकादींश्च वरयित्वा, यथोक्तम् अङ्कुरान् अर्पयित्वा, सम्भारान् सम्भृत्य, तथैव अक्षिमोचन-अधिवासौ च कृत्वा;
> ***ekatale navamūrtīnāṃ pratiṣṭhā-viśeṣaḥ -***
> *aśaktānām ekatala-prāsāde nava-ṣaṭ-pañca-mūrtīnāṃ pratiṣṭhā-viśeṣaṃ vakṣye — pūrvavad ācāryam ekaṃ, sthāpakādīṃśca varayitvā, yathoktam aṅkurān arpayitvā, sambhārān sambhṛtya, tathaiva akṣimocana-adhivāsou ca kṛtvā;*

> आलय-अभिमुखे, दक्षिणे वा पूर्ववद् यागशालां महावेदिं च कृत्वा, परितः अग्निकुण्डानि पौण्डरीकाग्निकुण्डं च कृत्वा, वास्तु-शुद्धिं च कृत्वा, सर्वेषु अग्निषु आघारं हुत्वा, पूर्ववत् कुम्भं संस्थाप्य; आलये बालागारे वा देवाभिमुखे धान्य-उपरि कुम्भं संन्यस्य;

> आचार्यः उत्तराभिमुखः आसीत्वा, पूर्ववद् ध्यायन् कुम्भे सर्वान् देवान् समावाह्य, कुम्भं सङ्गृह्य अग्रतो गच्छेत्। स्थापकाः शकुन-सूक्तं समुच्चार्य, पूर्वं विष्णुम् आदिमूर्तिं, पश्चात् पुरुषादीन्, स्नपन-उत्सव-बलि-बेराणि च आदाय अनुगच्छेयुः।
> *ācāryaḥ uttarābhimukhaḥ āsītvā, pūrvavad dhyāyan kumbhe sarvān devān samāvāhya, kumbhaṃ saṅgṛhya agrato gacchet. sthāpakāḥ śakuna-sūktaṃ samuccārya, pūrvaṃ viṣṇum ādimūrtiṃ, paścāt puruṣādīn, snapana-utsava-bali-berāṇi ca ādāya anugaccheyuḥ.*
> 
> स्नानवेद्यां मध्ये विष्णुं, दक्षिणे देवीं श्रियं, पुरुष-सत्य-नरनारायण-नारसिंहांश्च; उत्तरे महीं, अच्युत-अनिरुद्ध-वाराहांश्च शयानं च संस्थाप्य, एषां दक्षिण-पार्श्वे कुम्भं संन्यस्य, स्नपन-उत्सव-बलि-बेराणि च तत्-क्रमेण संस्थाप्य, पूर्ववत् प्रत्येकं कलशैः संस्नाप्य, वस्त्र-उत्तरीयाद्यैः विभूष्य अभ्यर्च्य;
> *snānavedyāṃ madhye viṣṇuṃ, dakṣiṇe devīṃ śriyaṃ, puruṣa-satya-naranārāyaṇa-nārasiṃhāṃśca; uttare mahīṃ, acyuta-aniruddha-vārāhāṃśca śayānaṃ ca saṃsthāpya, eṣāṃ dakṣiṇa-pārśve kumbhaṃ saṃnyasya, snapana-utsava-bali-berāṇi ca tat-krameṇa saṃsthāpya, pūrvavat pratyekaṃ kalaśaiḥ saṃsnāpya, vastra-uttarīyādyaiḥ vibhūṣya abhyarcya;*

> *yajñāśālaye nairṛte devān saṃsthāpya, pūrvavat pratisaraṃ baddhvā, dhruvabere'pi pratisaram ābaddhya; puruṣādīnāṃ dhruvaberaṃ yadi syāt, ābhāsaṃ vinā pratisaraṃ bandhayet.*
> 
> पूर्ववत् पृथक् पृथक् शयनानि आस्तीर्य, मध्ये विष्णुम् आदिमूर्तिं देवीभ्यां सह; दक्षिणे पुरुष-सत्य-नरनारायण-नृसिंहांश्च शयानं च तत्-तत्-देवीभ्यां सह समारोप्य; तेषां दक्षिणे स्नपनं, उत्तरे च उत्सव-बलि-बेरं च समारोप्य, पूर्ववद् आच्छादयेत्।
> *pūrvavat pṛthak pṛthak śayanāni āstīrya, madhye viṣṇum ādimūrtiṃ devībhyāṃ saha; dakṣiṇe puruṣ-satya-naranārāyaṇa-nṛsiṃhāṃśca śayānaṃ ca tat-tat-devībhyāṃ saha samāropya; teṣāṃ dakṣiṇe snapanaṃ, uttare ca utsava-bali-beraṃ ca samāropya, pūrvavad ācchādayet.*
> 
> यतो द्वारं ततस्तेषां मौलिं कृत्वा, उत्तराच्छादनं कृत्वा, सभ्याग्नौ पूर्ववद् धौत्रं प्रशंस्य, आवाहनादीनि कृत्वा, सर्वान् अभ्यर्च्य, चतुर्दिक्षु वेदान् अध्यापयेत्।
> *yato dvāraṃ tatesteṣāṃ mouliṃ kṛtvā, uttarācchādanaṃ kṛtvā, sabhyāgnou pūrvavad dhoutraṃ praśaṃsya, āvāhanādīni kṛtvā, sarvān abhyarcya, caturdikṣu vedān adhyāpayet.*
> 
> सभ्याग्नौ पूर्वोक्त-होमं हुत्वा, आहवनीयादिषु यथोक्तं होमं जुहुयुः; तथैव प्रायश्चित्त-होमं हुत्वा, सर्व-परिवाराणां तत्-तत्-देवत्यं तत्-तत्-होमे सभ्ये वा तथैव हुत्वा, नृत्त-गेय-वाद्यैः रात्रि-शेषं नयेत्।
> *sabhyāgnou pūrvokta-homaṃ hutvā, āhavanīyādiṣu yathoktaṃ homaṃ juhuyuḥ; tathaiva prāyaścitta-homaṃ hutvā, sarva-parivārāṇāṃ tat-tat-devatyaṃ tat-tat-home sabhye vā tathaiva hutvā, nṛtta-geya-vādyaiky rātri-śeṣaṃ nayet.*
> 
> **प्रातः-काल-कृत्यम् -**
> अथाचार्यः प्रभाते स्नात्वा, आलयम् आविश्य, देवान् उत्थाप्य, पूर्ववद् विष्णु-आदीनां सर्वेषां कौतुकादीनां पीठेषु रत्नादीनि संन्यसेयुः; यजमानः आचार्यादिभ्यो दक्षिणां दद्यात्। सभ्याग्निं परिस्तीर्य अन्तहोमं हुत्वा, तद्-अग्निं नित्य-होमाय विन्यसेत्।
> ***prātaḥ-kāla-kṛtyam -***
> *athācāryaḥ prabhāte snātvā, ālayam āviśya, devān utthāpya, pūrvavad viṣṇu-ādīnāṃ sarveṣāṃ kautukādīnāṃ pīṭheṣu ratnādīni saṃnyaseyuḥ; yajamānaḥ ācāryādibhyo dakṣiṇāṃ dadyāt. sabhyāgniṃ paristīrya antahomaṃ hutvā, tad-agniṃ nitya-homāya vinyaset.*
> 
> मुहूर्ते प्राप्ते सर्व-अलङ्कार-संयुक्तं, तोयधारा-पुरस्सरं शकुन-सूक्तं जप्त्वा, देवं ध्यायन् आचार्यः शिरसा कुम्भं धारयन् अग्रतो गच्छेत्। स्थापकाः विष्णु-आदि-मूर्तीः पूर्ववद् आदाय अनुगच्छेयुः।
> *muhūrte prāpte sarva-alaṅkāra-saṃyuktaṃ, toyadhāra-purassaraṃ śakuna-sūktaṃ japtvā, devaṃ dhyāyan ācāryaḥ śirasā kumbhaṃ dhārayan agrato gacchet. sthāpakāḥ viṣṇu-ādi-mūrtīḥ pūrvavad ādāya anugaccheyuḥ.*
> 
> आलय-प्रदक्षिणं कृत्वा, अभ्यन्तरं प्रविश्य, ध्रुवबेरस्य दक्षिणे कुम्भं संन्यस्य;
> *   ब्रह्मस्थाने: विष्णु-आदि-मूर्तिं (Ādimūrti)।
> *   तद्-द्वार-दक्षिणे अलिन्द्रे: प्राङ्मुखं पुरुषम्।
> *   दक्षिणे: दक्षिणामुखं सत्यम्।
> *   तत्-पश्चिमे: नरनारायणौ दक्षिणाभिमुखौ।
> *   पश्चिमे: पश्चिमाभिमुखम् अच्युतम्।
> *   तद्-उत्तरे: तथैव नृसिंहम्।
> *   उत्तरे: उत्तराभिमुखम् अनिरुद्धम्।
> *   तत्-पश्चिमे: तथैव वाराहम्।
> *   पूर्वद्वार-उत्तरे अलिन्द्रे: शयानं प्राङ्मुखम् —
> पूर्ववत् स्थापयेयुः। तस्मिन् मुखमण्टपे अभ्यन्तरे वा दक्षिणे स्नपनं, उत्तरे च उत्सव-बलि-बेरं च स्थापयेत्।
> *ālaya-pradakṣiṇaṃ kṛtvā, abhyantaraṃ praviśya, dhruvaberasya dakṣiṇe kumbhaṃ saṃnyasya;*
> **   brahmasthāne: viṣṇu-adi-mūrtiṃ.*
> **   tad-dvāra-dakṣiṇe alindre: prāṅmukhaṃ puruṣam.*
> **   dakṣiṇe: dakṣiṇāmukhaṃ satyam.*
> **   tat-paścime: naranārāyaṇou dakṣiṇābhimukhou.*
> **   paścime: paścimābhimukham acyutam.*
> **   tad-uttare: tathaiva nṛsiṃham.*
> **   uttare: uttarābhimukham aniruddham.*
> **   tat-paścime: tathaiva vārāham.*
> **   pūrvadvāra-uttare alindre: śayānaṃ prāṅmukham —*
> *pūrvavat sthāpayeyuḥ. tasmin mukhamanṭape abhyantare vā dakṣiṇe snapanaṃ, uttare ca utsava-bali-beraṃ ca sthāpayet.*
> 
> **अन्तिम-आवाहनम् -**
> आचार्यश्च आत्म-सूक्तं जप्त्वा, ध्रुवस्य पादं स्पृशन् सूक्तानि जप्त्वा, दक्षिणे च उत्तराभिमुखः तिष्ठन्, ध्रुवबेरे अक्षराणि विन्यस्य; समाहितः कुम्भस्थां शक्तिम् आदाय, 'आयातु भगवान्' इति ध्रुवबेरस्य मूर्ध्नि 'विष्णुम् आवाहयामि' इति समावाहयेत्। तथैव पुरुषादीनां ध्रुवबेरेषु तत्-तत्-मूर्त्तिभिः समावाहयेत्। तथैव पुरुषादीनां ध्रुवबेरं हीनं चेत्, आदिमूर्तेः ध्रुवबेरस्य मूर्ध्नि सर्वां मूर्तिम् आवाहयेत्।
> ***antima-āvāhanam -***
> *ācāryaśca ātma-sūktaṃ japtvā, dhruvasya pādaṃ spṛśan sūktāni japtvā, dakṣiṇe ca uttarābhimukhaḥ tiṣṭhan, dhruvabere akṣarāṇi vinyasya; samāhitaḥ kumbhasthāṃ śaktim ādāya, 'āyātu bhagavān' iti dhruvaberasya mūrdhni 'viṣṇum āvāhayāmi' iti samāvāhayet. tathaiva puruṣādīnāṃ dhruvabereṣu tat-tat-mūrttibhiḥ samāvāhayet. tathaiva puruṣādīnāṃ dhruvaberaṃ hīnaṃ cet, ādimūrteḥ dhruvaberasya mūrdhni sarvāṃ mūrtim āvāhayet.*
> 
> ध्रुवस्य पादं परितः पुरुषादि-आवरणत्रय-देवान् समावाह्य, दक्षिणे श्रियं, वामे हरिणीं (भूमिं) समावाहयेत्।
> *dhruvasya pādaṃ paritaḥ puruṣādi-āvaraṇatraya-devān samāvāhya, dakṣiṇe śriyaṃ, vāme hariṇīṃ (bhūmiṃ) samāvāhayet.*
> 
> विष्णु-आदीनां कौतुकानां, स्नपन-उत्सव-बलि-बेराणां बीजमन्त्रन्यासम् अक्षरन्यासं च कृत्वा; पूर्ववत् सौवर्णादि-पात्रं शङ्खं वा पूतवारिणा आपूर्य, देवं ध्यायन्, ध्रुवबेरात् प्रणिधि-जले दीपात् दीपम् इव आवाह्य; विष्णु-आदीनां कौतुकेषु यथाक्रमेण आवाहयेत्। तदैव स्नपन-उत्सव-बलि-बेरेषु च आवाहयेत्। एष विशेषः, अन्यत् सर्वं समानम् ॥
> *viṣṇu-ādīnāṃ kautukānāṃ, snapana-utsava-bali-berāṇāṃ bījamantranyāsam akṣaranyāsaṃ ca kṛtvā; pūrvavat souvarṇādi-pātraṃ śaṅkhaṃ vā pūtavāriṇā āpūrya, devaṃ dhyāyan, dhruvaberaāt praṇidhi-jale dīpāt dīpam iv āvāhya; viṣṇu-yādīnāṃ kautukeṣu yathākrameṇa āvāhayet. tadāiva snapana-utsava-bali-bereṣu ca āvāhayet. eṣa viśeṣaḥ, anyat sarvaṃ samānam.*

#### English Translation
*   **The Single Sacrificial Hall Alternative:** If unable to set up separate sacrificial halls for all the images, some say that the priest should construct a single sacrificial hall on the east. In the center, in the eight directions surrounding the central grid square of Viṣṇu, he sets up eight couch-altars in order:
    1. Reclining form on Ananta (*Ananta-śayana*)
    2. Puruṣa
    3. Satya
    4. Nara-Nārāyaṇa
    5. Achyuta
    6. Nṛsiṃha
    7. Aniruddha
    8. Varāha
    - He establishes their respective chief fires in their east, and performs the consecration using the single-fire method (*ekāgni-vidhāna*).

*   **Alternative Consecration in a Single-Storeyed Temple:**
    *   For those unable to build multi-storeyed temples, I shall describe the simplified consecration in a single-storeyed temple. Having chosen one *Ācārya* and *Sthāpakas*, sown seeds, gathered the materials, and performed eye-opening and couch-placement:
    *   They set up a single sacrificial hall facing the temple or on the south, construct the main altar (*mahā-vedi*), the surrounding fire-pits, and the *Pauṇḍarīka* fire-pit.
    *   Having purified the site, they offer the initial ghee oblation in all the fires and establish the pot.
    *   In the temple or the temporary shrine (*bālālaya*), they place the pot on a heap of grain facing the deity. Seated facing north and meditating, the *Ācārya* invokes all the deities into the pot, lifts it, and walks in front.

*   **Bathing in the Assembly Hall:**
    *   Chanting the *Śakuna-sūkta*, the *Sthāpakas* carry the primary *Ādimūrti*, followed by Puruṣa and others, and the bathing, festival, and offering images.
    *   On the bathing altar (*snāna-vedi*), they place Viṣṇu in the center, flanked by Goddess Śrī and the four forms (Puruṣa, Satya, Nara-Nārāyaṇa, Nṛsiṃha) on the right; and Goddess Bhūmi (*Mahī*), the three forms (Achyuta, Aniruddha, Varāha), and the reclining deity on the left.
    *   They place the pot to their right, place the bathing, festival, and offering images in order, bathe them with individual pots, adorn them with garments and upper cloths, and worship them.

*   **Simplified Couch Placement:**
    *   They place the deities in the southwest of the sacrificial hall and tie the protective thread (*pratisara*) on the images, including the fixed images.
    *   They prepare individual couches. On the central couch, they place *Ādimūrti* along with the two Goddesses. On the right, they place Puruṣa, Satya, Nara-Nārāyaṇa, and Nṛsiṃha along with the reclining deity and their respective Goddesses. They place the bathing and festival/offering images to their right and cover them.
    *   Arranging the images with their heads facing the doorway, they cover them, praise the *hotṛ* hymns in the *Sabhya* fire, perform the invocation, and cause the chanting of the Vedas in the four directions.

*   **Oblations and Vigil:** They offer the main oblations in the *Sabhya* fire, the prescribed oblations in the *Āhavanīya* and other fires, perform the penance homas, and offer the specific deity-mantras in their respective fires or in the *Sabhya* fire, keeping vigil through the night with dance, song, and music.

*   **Morning Rituals:** In the morning, having bathed, they enter the temple, awaken the deities, and place the gems on the pedestals. The patron offers fees to the priests. They strew grass around the *Sabhya* fire, offer the final oblation, and establish it for daily offerings.

*   **Final Installation and Positioning:**
    *   When the auspicious moment arrives, carrying the pot on his head, the *Ācārya* walks in front, followed by the *Sthāpakas* carrying the images.
    *   Circumambulating the temple and entering the inner sanctum, they place the pot to the right of the fixed image.
    *   They position the deities as follows:
        *   **Center (Brahma-sthāna):** The seated *Ādimūrti* (Viṣṇu).
        *   **Eastern gallery (Alistala/Alindra) to the right of the door:** Puruṣa, facing east.
        *   **South:** Satya, facing south.
        *   **South-South:** Nara-Nārāyaṇa, facing south.
        *   **West:** Achyuta, facing west.
        *   **West-West:** Nṛsiṃha, facing west.
        *   **North:** Aniruddha, facing north.
        *   **North-North:** Varāha, facing north.
        *   **Northeast gallery of the eastern door:** Reclining deity (*Yoga-śayana*), facing east.
    *   They place the bathing, festival, and offering images in the front porch (*mukha-maṇṭapa*) or to the right of the inner sanctum.

*   **Closing Invocation:** Reciting the *Ātma-sūkta*, touching the feet of the fixed image, and writing the syllables on the fixed image, the *Ācārya* stands facing north on the right side. Taking the energy from the pot, saying *"May the Lord come!"*, he invokes Him upon the head of the fixed image. He invokes the respective deity forms into the other fixed images. If the other fixed images are damaged or absent, he invokes all the forms on the head of the primary *Ādimūrti*. Flanking Him, he invokes Śrī on the right and Harinī on the left. Translating the energy from the fixed image into the vessel water like one lamp lighting another, he invokes the energy into the movable and subsidiary images in order.
