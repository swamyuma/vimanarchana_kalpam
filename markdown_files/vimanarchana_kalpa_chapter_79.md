# Vimanarchana Kalpa of Sage Marīci (Vaikhānasa Āgama)
## Chapter 79: Mārgaśīrṣa-dvādaśī-samārādhana-vidhiḥ (Rules for the Annual Expiation and Consecration on the Margashirsha Twelfth Day)

This document contains the Sanskrit text, IAST transliteration, and English translation of the 79th Chapter (*Ekonāśītitemaḥ Paṭalaḥ*) of the **Vimānārcanākalpa** (attributed to Sage Marīci, representing the Vaikhānasa school of Vaishnavism).

This chapter outlines the annual expiatory ritual (*newnātirikta-prāyaścitta*) performed to rectify any deficiencies or excesses in the daily temple worship accumulated throughout the year. The ritual is performed specifically on the twelfth day of the bright fortnight of the month of Mārgaśīrṣa, involving special fire offerings, bathing the deity with fourteen water pots, laying the deity on a bed of grass, reciting the four Vedas, presenting a golden sacred thread, and chanting prayers of apology.

---

### Section 1: Preparations and Initial Fire Offerings

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **अथ एकोनाशीतितमः पटलः**
> **अथ वर्षे वर्षे नित्यार्चनायां न्यूनातिरिक्त-प्रायश्चित्तम्**
> **तस्य कालः प्रकारश्च**
> ***atha ekonāśītitamaḥ paṭalaḥ***
> ***atha varṣe varṣe nityārcanāyāṃ nyūnātirikta-prāyaścittam***
> ***tasya kālaḥ prakāraśca***
> 
> मार्गशीर्षमासे शुक्लद्वादश्यां तद्दिनात् पूर्वे अङ्कुरान् अर्पयित्वा, आलयात् अभिमुखे दक्षिणे वा पौण्डरीकाग्निं साधयित्वा, पूर्ववत् नित्यार्चना-अवसाने, तस्मिन् वैष्णवं, विष्णुसूक्तं, पुरुषसूक्तं च, विष्णुगायत्र्या अष्टोत्तरशतं (108) बिल्वपत्रं वा घृताक्तम् अग्नौ जुहुयात् ॥
> *mārgaśīrṣamāse śukladvādaśyāṃ taddināt pūrve aṅkurān arpayitvā, ālayāt abhimukhe daxiṇe vā pauṇḍarīkāgniṃ sādhayitvā, pūrvavat nityārcanā-avasāne, tasmin vaiṣṇavaṃ, viṣṇusūktaṃ, puruṣasūktaṃ ca, viṣṇugāyatriyā aṣṭottaraśataṃ (108) bilvapatraṃ vā ghṛtāktam agnau juhuyāt ||*

#### English Translation
*   **The Subject:** Now, I shall declare the annual expiation for deficiencies and excesses in the daily worship (*atha varṣe varṣe nityārcanāyāṃ nyūnātirikta-prāyaścittam*).

*   **Timing and Sprouts:** In the month of Mārgaśīrṣa (November-December), on the twelfth day of the bright fortnight (*mārgaśīrṣamāse śukladvādaśyāṃ*), having offered the germinated sprouts (*aṅkurān arpayitvā*) on the preceding day (*taddināt pūrve*).

*   **Setting up the Fire:** Having prepared the *Pauṇḍarīka* fire (*pauṇḍarīkāgniṃ sādhayitvā*) in front of the temple or to the south (*ālayāt abhimukhe daxiṇe vā*), at the conclusion of the daily worship as before (*pūrvavat nityārcanā-avasāne*).

*   **Offerings:** One should offer into that fire (*tasmin agnau juhuyāt*) oblations with the Vaiṣṇava, Viṣṇu-sūkta, and Puruṣa-sūkta hymns, and one hundred and eight bilva leaves smeared with ghee (*aṣṭottaraśataṃ bilvapatraṃ vā ghṛtāktam*) while reciting the Viṣṇu-gāyatrī (*viṣṇugāyatriyā*).

### Section 2: Consecration of the Bed, Bathing, and Vedic Recitations

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> देवेशं ध्यायन् पारमात्मिकम् ओङ्कारादीन् च हुत्वा, अभ्यन्तरं प्रविश्य, देवं प्रणम्य आनीय, गर्भगृह-अन्तरस्थं देवेशं चतुर्दशकलशैः विधिना अभिषिच्य, नववस्त्र-उत्तरीयाद्यैः अलङ्कृत्य, शय्यावेद्याम् उपरि पूर्ववद् दर्भशय्यादीन् आस्तीर्य, तथैव प्रतिसरं बद्ध्वा, देवं शाययित्वा, प्रागादि-चतुर्दिक्षु चतुर्वेद-अध्ययनं कृत्वा, पश्चाद् अग्निं प्रज्वाल्य, पूर्ववद् घृतप्रकाशादि कृत्वा, आवाहनं जुष्टाकारं स्वाहाकारं च कृत्वा, वैष्णवं, श्री-भूमि-देवत्यं, ब्राह्मं, रौद्रं, मुनिभ्याम् (Bhrigu and Marīci) मन्त्रं, धात्रादि-सर्वदेवत्यं च हुत्वा, विष्णुसूक्तेन पयो-दधि-घृत-लाजैः युक्तम् उपोदकं (or उपले) हुत्वा, अग्निम् आनम्य, नृत्त-गेय-वाद्यैश्च रात्रिदोषं व्यपोह्य (or यापयेत्) ॥
> *deveśaṃ dhyāyan pāramātmikam oṅkārādīn ca hutvā, abhyantaraṃ praviśya, devaṃ praṇamya ānīya, garbhagṛha-antarasthaṃ deveśaṃ caturdaśakalaśaiḥ vidhinā abhiṣicya, navavastra-uttarīyādyaiḥ alaṅkṛtya, śayyāvedyām upari pūrvavad darbhaśayyādīn āstīrya, tathaiva pratisaraṃ baddhvā, devaṃ śāyayitvā, prāgādi-caturdikṣu caturveda-adhyayanaṃ kṛtvā, paścād agniṃ prajvālya, pūrvavad ghṛtaprakāśādi kṛtvā, āvāhanaṃ juṣṭākāraṃ svāhākāraṃ ca kṛtvā, vaiṣṇavaṃ, śrī-bhūmi-devatyaṃ, brāhmaṃ, raudraṃ, munibhyām (bhṛgu-marīcyoḥ) mantraṃ, dhātrādi-sarvadevatyaṃ ca hutvā, viṣṇusūktena payo-dadhi-ghṛta-lājaiḥ yuktam upodakaṃ (or upale) hutvā, agnim ānamya, nṛtta-geya-vādyaiśca rātridoṣaṃ vyapohya (or yāpayet) ||*

#### English Translation
*   **Bathing the Deity:** Meditating on the Lord of Gods (*deveśaṃ dhyāyan*), offering oblations with the Pāramātmika and Oṅkāra mantras (*pāramātmikam oṅkārādīn ca hutvā*), entering the temple (*abhyantaraṃ praviśya*), bowing to the deity, and bringing him out (*devaṃ praṇamya ānīya*), one should bathe the Lord of Gods residing in the inner sanctum (*garbhagṛha-antarasthaṃ deveśaṃ*) with fourteen water pots according to the rules (*caturdaśakalaśaiḥ vidhinā abhiṣicya*).

*   **Laying the Deity to Rest:** Having decorated him with new garments and upper cloths (*navavastra-uttarīyādyaiḥ alaṅkṛtya*), spread a bed of darbha grass on top of the bed-altar as before (*śayyāvedyām upari pūrvavad darbhaśayyādīn āstīrya*), tied the protective cord (*pratisaraṃ baddhvā*), and laid the deity to rest (*devaṃ śāyayitvā*).

*   **Vedic Recitations and Concluding Fires:** Having arranged the recitation of the four Vedas in the four directions starting with the east (*prāgādi-caturdikṣu caturveda-adhyayanaṃ kṛtvā*), afterwards lit the fire (*paścād agniṃ prajvālya*), performed the ghee lighting ceremony as before (*pūrvavad ghṛtaprakāśādi kṛtvā*), and executed the invocation (*āvāhanaṃ*), *Juṣṭākāra*, and *Svāhākāra* offerings. Having offered oblations for the Vaishnava, Śrī-Bhūmi, Brahmā, Rudra, the Sage-pair (Bhrigu and Marīci) (*munibhyām*), and Dhātā along with all retinue deities (*dhātrādi-sarvadevatyaṃ ca hutvā*). Under the recitation of the Viṣṇu-sūkta, offering the mixture of milk, curd, ghee, and puffed rice (*payo-dadhi-ghṛta-lājaiḥ yuktam*), bowing to the fire (*agnim ānamya*), one should keep vigil to dispel the defects of the night with dance, song, and music (*nṛtta-geya-vādyaiśca rātridoṣaṃ vyapohya*).

### Section 3: Concluding Worship, Yajñopavīta, and Apology Verses

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> अभ्यर्च्य, विष्णुसूक्तेन पुष्पाञ्जलिं दत्त्वा, देवेशाय निवेदयेत्। सौवर्णं यज्ञोपवीतम् आचार्यः उत्तराभिमुखो भूत्वा ‘इदं विष्णुः’ इति मन्त्रेण दद्यात्। देवं प्रार्थयेत् —
> *abhyarcya, viṣṇusūktena puṣpāñjaliṃ dattvā, deveśāya nivedayet | sauvarṇaṃ yajñopavītam ācāryaḥ uttarābhimukho bhūtvā ‘idaṃ viṣṇuḥ’ iti mantreṇa dadyāt | devaṃ prārthayet —*
> 
> ‘न शक्नोम्य् एव विधिवत् कर्तुं ब्रह्मादीनाम् अपि पूजनम्।
> न्यूनातिरिक्त-व्याघात-दोष-हीनं जगत्पते’ ॥ इति स्तुत्वा —
> *‘na śaknomy eva vidhivat kartuṃ brahmādīnām api pūjanam |*
> *nyūnātirikta-vyāghāta-doṣa-hīnaṃ jagatpate’ || iti stutvā —*
> 
> ‘अज्ञानानाम् अशक्तानाम् अस्माकम् अकृतात्मनाम्।
> तस्मान् नित्यार्चनायां हि यद् द्रव्य-मोह-लोभतः’ (or यत् कृतं द्रव्य-लोभतः) ॥
> ‘पूजया तु अनया विष्णो प्रशान्तं सर्वम् अस्तु नः’ ॥
> *‘ajñānānām aśaktānām asmākam akṛtātmanām |*
> *tasmān nityārcanāyāṃ hi yad dravya-moha-lobhataḥ’ (or yat kṛtaṃ dravya-lobhataḥ) ||*
> *‘pūjayā tu anayā viṣṇo praśāntaṃ sarvam astu naḥ’ ||*
> 
> इति नत्वा, दण्डवत्-प्रणामं कृत्वा, यथाभाग-उपदंश-युक्तं पायस-कृसर-गौल्यं (or लाजं), यावकं शुद्धान्नं च निवेद्य, पानीय-आचमनीयं दत्त्वा, कर्पूर-मुखवासं ताम्बूलं च दत्त्वा, देवालयं प्रदक्षिणीकृत्य, अर्चास्थाने संस्थाप्य, अभ्यर्च्य, वैष्णवान् सम्पूज्य, ब्राह्मण-भोजनं कृत्वा, आचार्यादिभ्यो दक्षिणां दद्यात्।
> *iti natvā, daṇḍavat-praṇāmaṃ kṛtvā, yathābhāga-upadaṃśa-yuktaṃ pāyasa-kṛsara-gaulyaṃ, yāvakaṃ śuddhānnaṃ ca nivedya, pānīya-ācamanīyaṃ dattvā, karpūra-mukhavāsaṃ tāmbūlaṃ ca dattvā, devālayaṃ pradaxiṇīkṛtya, arcāsthāne saṃsthāpya, abhyarcya, vaiṣṇavān sampūjya, brāhmaṇa-bhojanaṃ kṛtvā, ācāryādibhyo daxiṇāṃ dadyāt ||*
> 
> एवं संवत्सरं प्रति समाचरेत्। ते संवत्सर-नित्यार्चना-फलं लभेरन् इति विज्ञायते ॥
> *evaṃ saṃvatsaraṃ prati samāceret | te saṃvatsara-nityārcanā-phalaṃ labheran iti vijñāyate ||*

#### English Translation
*   **Golden Sacred Thread Offering:** Having worshipped, and offered a handful of flowers while reciting the Viṣṇu-sūkta (*viṣṇusūktena puṣpāñjaliṃ dattvā*), he presents it to the Lord of Gods. The priest, facing north (*ācāryaḥ uttarābhimukho bhūtvā*), should offer a golden sacred thread (*sauvarṇaṃ yajñopavītam*) reciting the mantra "idaṃ viṣṇuḥ" (*‘idaṃ viṣṇuḥ’ iti mantreṇa dadyāt*). He then prays to the Lord:

*   **The Apology Hymns:**
    > "I am indeed not capable (*na śaknomy eva*) of performing worship in the perfect manner as even Brahmā and other gods do (*brahmādīnām api pūjanam*). O Lord of the Universe (*jagatpate*)! May this worship be free from the defects of deficiency, excess, or obstruction (*nyūnātirikta-vyāghāta-doṣa-hīnam*)."
    >
    > "For us who are ignorant (*ajñānānām*), incapable (*aśaktānām*), and of unrefined/unprepared minds (*akṛtātmanām*), whatever was done during the daily worship out of confusion or greed for materials (*yad dravya-moha-lobhataḥ*), O Viṣṇu (*viṣṇo*)! By this worship indeed (*pūjayā tu anayā*), may all of ours be pacified and made complete (*praśāntaṃ sarvam astu naḥ*)."

*   **Concluding Rites:** Having bowed thus and made a full prostration (*daṇḍavat-praṇāmaṃ kṛtvā*), he offers sweet payasa, krisara, barley gruel, and pure rice accompanied by proper side-dishes according to shares (*yathaibhāga-upadaṃśa-युक्तं pāyasa-kṛsara-gaulyaṃ yāvakaṃ śuddhānnaṃ ca nivedya*). Presenting drinking and sipping water, betel leaves, and camphor mouth-freshener, he circumambulates the temple (*devālayaṃ pradaxiṇīkṛtya*), replaces the icon in the place of worship, worships, honors the Vaishnavas (*vaiṣṇavān sampūjya*), feeds the Brahmins (*brāhmaṇa-bhojanaṃ kṛtvā*), and distributes dakshina to the priest and others (*ācāryādibhyo daxiṇāṃ dadyāt*).

*   **Annual Necessity:** Thus one should perform this ritual every year (*evaṃ saṃvatsaraṃ prati samāceret*). By doing so, they will obtain the full fruits of the year's daily worship (*te saṃvatsara-nityārcanā-phalaṃ labheran*), so it is declared (*iti vijñāyate*).

#### Colophon
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> इति श्रीवैखानसे मरीचिप्रोक्ते विमानार्चनाकल्पे मार्गशीर्ष-द्वादशी-समाराधनं नाम एकोनाशीतितमः पटलः ॥७९॥

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> iti śrīvaikhānase marīciprokte vimānārcanākalpe mārgaśīrṣa-dvādaśī-samārādhanaṃ nāma ekonāśītitamaḥ paṭalaḥ ||79||

## Glossary of Key Terms

1.  **Nyūnātirikta-prāyaścitta (न्यूनातिरिक्तप्रायश्चित्त):** The expiatory ritual performed to clear all omissions (deficiencies) and commissions (excesses) made during temple rituals over the course of a year.
2.  **Mārgaśīrṣa-dvādaśī (मार्गशीर्षद्वादशी):** The twelfth lunar day of the bright fortnight of the month of Margashirsha, considered highly auspicious for Vaishnava purification rituals.
3.  **Sauvarṇa-yajñopavīta (सौवर्णयज्ञोपवीत):** A sacred thread made of gold, offered to the deity at the conclusion of annual expiations to denote supreme purity.
4.  **Payo-dadhi-ghṛta-lāja (पयोदधिघृतलाज):** A mixture of milk, curd, clarified butter (ghee), and puffed rice used as a sacred offering in Agamic fire rituals.
5.  **Darbha-śayyā (दर्भशय्या):** A ritual bed made of sacred darbha grass upon which the deity is laid to rest overnight during consecrations.
6.  **Yāvaka (यावक):** A food offering prepared from barley, considered highly purifying in Vedic and Agamic traditions.
7.  **Kr̥sara (कृसर):** A ritual dish made of rice cooked with green gram (mung-bean) or sesame seeds and spices.
8.  **Gaulya (गौल्य):** Sweet foods prepared using jaggery (guda) as a key ingredient, offered during concluding temple ceremonies.
9.  **Brahmajapa (ब्रह्मजप):** The silent recitation of Vedic hymns, particularly the Gayatri and other primary suktas, performed by priests to preserve spiritual purity.
10. **Aṣṭottara-śata-bilvapatra (अष्टोत्तरशतबिल्वपत्र):** The offering of exactly 108 leaves of the sacred wood-apple (bilva) tree, smeared with ghee, into the fire.
