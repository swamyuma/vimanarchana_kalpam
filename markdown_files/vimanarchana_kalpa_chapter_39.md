# Chapter 39: Consecration of the Temple/Vimāna (Vimāna-pratiṣṭhā-vidhiḥ)
### Section 1: Consecration Rituals and Architecture of the Vimāna

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **विमान-प्रतिष्ठा-विधिः -**
> अथ विमानप्रतिष्ठाविधिं वक्ष्ये — विमानं विधिवत् कृत्वा, देवान् एतान् प्रकल्पयेत्।
> *   मुखमण्टप-द्वार-दक्षिणे: तापसम्; उत्तरे: सिद्धिदम्।
> *   विमानस्य दक्षिणे: सत्यं; पश्चिमे: अच्युतं; उत्तरे: अनिरुद्धम्।
> *   मुखमण्टप-दक्षिणे: विनायकं; उत्तरे: विन्ध्यवासिनीं च।
> *   मूलतले (भूतले): स्थानकम् आसनं वा।
> *   उपरि वा द्वितीयतले प्रागादिषु: पुरुषादीन्।
> *   अथवा — पूर्वे: चक्रं, दक्षिणे: नृसिंहं, पश्चिमे: अच्युतम्, उत्तरे: वाराहं च।
> *   गलस्थाने (कण्ठभागे) पूर्वादिषु: पुरुषादीन्, दक्षिणे: दक्षिणामूर्तिं, उत्तरे: पितामहं (ब्रह्माणं), पश्चिमे: नृसिंहं, पूर्वे: वराहं च कारयेत्।
> *   कोणेषु: द्विजेन्द्रं (गरुडं), मृगेन्द्रं (सिंहं) वा कारयेत्।
> *   अस्मिन् विमाने च आदितले मध्ये, तृतीये वा, यथेष्टदेशे: नृसिंहं दक्षिणामुखं पश्चिमाभिमुखं वा कारयेत्। एतद् 'सर्वशत्रुविनाशः सर्वार्थसाधनम्' इति विशेषोक्तम्।
> ***vimāna-pratiṣṭhā-vidhiḥ -***
> *atha vimānapratiṣṭhāvidhiṃ vakṣye — vimānaṃ vidhivat kṛtvā, devān etān prakalpayet.*
> **   mukhamanṭapa-dvāra-dakṣiṇe: tāpasam; uttare: siddhidam.*
> **   vimānasya dakṣiṇe: satyaṃ; paścime: acyutaṃ; uttare: aniruddham.*
> **   mukhamanṭapa-dakṣiṇe: vināyakaṃ; uttare: vindhyavāsinīṃ ca.*
> **   mūlatale (bhūtale): sthānakam āsanaṃ vā.*
> **   upari vā dvitīyatale prāgādiṣu: puruṣādīn.*
> **   athavā — pūrve: cakraṃ, dakṣiṇe: nṛsiṃhaṃ, paścime: acyutaṃ, uttare: vārāhaṃ ca.*
> **   galasthāne (kaṇṭhabhāge) pūrvādiṣu: puruṣādīn, dakṣiṇe: dakṣiṇāmūrtiṃ, uttare: pitāmahaṃ (brahmāṇaṃ), paścime: nṛsiṃhaṃ, pūrve: varāhaṃ ca kārayet.*
> **   koṇeṣu: dvijendraṃ (garuḍaṃ), mṛgendraṃ (siṃhaṃ) vā kārayet.*
> **   asmin vimāne ca āditale madhye, tṛtīye vā, yatheṣṭadeśe: nṛsiṃhaṃ dakṣiṇāmukhaṃ paścimābhimukhaṃ vā kārayet. etad 'sarvaśatruvināśaḥ sarvārthasādhanam' iti viśeṣoktam.*
> 
> **बेर-परिमाणं द्रव्यं च -**
> तेषाम् उद्देशमानं मानाङ्गुलम् एकादशाङ्गुलं समारभ्य, सप्तविंशति-मात्रान्तं, द्व्यङ्गुलं वर्धयेत्, नवधा भवति; एतेषु इष्टमानेन शैल-मृण्मय-ऐष्टक-सौध-आलेख्यं वा यथार्हं कारयेत्। शैलं बेरं वर्णयुक्तं यदीच्छेत् — प्रथमं पूर्वोक्त-सशर्कराकल्के, द्विगुणं द्विगुणार्धं त्रिगुणं वालुकां मिश्रीकृत्य, निर्यासाम्बुना सह तस्य उपरिष्टाद् आवेष्टयेद् इति विशेषः। शेषं समानम्।
> ***bera-parimāṇaṃ dravyaṃ ca -***
> *teṣām uddeśamānaṃ mānāṅgulam ekādaśāṅgulaṃ samārabhya, saptaviṃśati-mātrāntaṃ, dvyaṅgulaṃ vardhayet, navadhā bhavati; eteṣu iṣṭamānena śaila-mṛṇmaya-aiṣṭaka-saudha-ālekhyaṃ vā yathārhaṃ kārayet. śailaṃ beraṃ varṇayuktaṃ yadīcchet — prathamaṃ pūrvokta-saśarkarākalke, dviguṇaṃ dviguṇārdhaṃ triguṇaṃ vālukāṃ miśrīkṛtya, niryāsāmbunā saha tasya upariṣṭād āveṣṭayed iti viśeṣaḥ. śeṣaṃ samānam.*
> 
> **अक्षिमोचन-विधिः -**
> अस्मिन् विमानाङ्गदेवानां, विमानपाल-द्वारपालानां, श्रीभूम्यादेः सर्वं परिषदं च प्रतिमाकरणे, तत्र आयव्ययाद्यं नेष्टम्। स्वप्रधाने बेरे विमाने च प्रतिष्ठादिवसात् पूर्वम् अङ्कुरान् अर्पयित्वा, आलय-अभिमुखे दक्षिणे पश्चिमे उत्तरे वा, विमानं परितो वा प्रपां कृत्वा, तोरणाद्यैः अलङ्कृत्य; प्रतिष्ठादिवसात् पूर्वं तृतीये द्वितीये वा अहनि, विमानदेवानाम् अक्षिमोचनम् अमन्त्रकं समन्त्रकं च कुर्यात्।
> ***akṣimocana-vidhiḥ -***
> *asmin vimānāṅgadevānāṃ, vimānapāla-dvārapālānāṃ, śrībhūmyādeḥ sarvaṃ pariṣadaṃ ca pratimākaraṇe, tatra āyavyayādyaṃ neṣṭam. svapradhāne bere vimāne ca pratiṣṭhādivasāt pūrvam aṅkurān arpayitvā, ālaya-abhimukhe dakṣiṇe paścime uttare vā, vimānaṃ parito vā prapāṃ kṛtvā, toraṇādyaiḥ alaṅkṛtya; pratiṣṭhādivasāt pūrvā tṛtīye dvitīye vā ahani, vimānadevānām akṣimocanam amantrakaṃ samantrakaṃ ca kuryāt.*
> 
> अक्षिमोचनार्थं तस्य आलयस्य उत्तरे कुण्डम् औपासनं कृत्वा, वास्तुहोमं हुत्वा, पर्यग्नि-पञ्चगव्याभ्याम् आलयं संशोध्य, पुण्याहं वाचयित्वा; आलय-अभिमुखे च औपासनाग्निकुण्डं कृत्वा, आघारान्ते वैष्णवं विष्णुसूक्तं, पुरुषसूक्तं, मही-भूत-देवत्यं, 'दद्भ्यः स्वाहा' इत्यादि अङ्गहोमं स्तूप्यादि-उपान-पर्यन्त-अङ्गानाम् अभिजुहुयात्।
> *akṣimocanārthaṃ tasya ālayasya uttare kuṇḍam aupāsanaṃ kṛtvā, vāstuhomaṃ hutvā, paryagni-pañcagavyābhyām ālayaṃ saṃśodhya, puṇyāhaṃ vācayitvā; ālaya-abhimukhe ca aupāsanāgnikuṇḍaṃ kṛtvā, āghārānte vaiṣṇavaṃ viṣṇusūktaṃ, puruṣusūktaṃ, mahī-bhūta-devatyaṃ, 'dadbhyaḥ svāhā' ityādi aṅgahomaṃ stūpyādi-upāna-paryanta-aṅgānām abhijuhuyāt.*
> 
> **विमान-त्रिषष्ट्यङ्ग-होमाः (Oblations to the 63 Parts of the Temple) -**
> 'स्तूपिमुकुलाय, पद्माय, फलकायै, पद्माय, कण्ठाय, क्षेपणाय, वेत्राय, क्षेपणाय, कण्ठाय, क्षेपणाय, पङ्कजाय, कुम्भाय, पङ्कजाय, क्षेपणाय, पद्माय, फलकायै, अवलग्नाय, कुम्भाय, शक्तिध्वजाय, पद्माय, उभाय, नासिकायै, फलकायै, दण्डाय, मृणालिकायै, मुष्टिबन्धाय, वाजनाय, उत्तराय, कण्ठाय, ऊहप्रत्यूहाय, वेदिकायै, भूमिकल्पाय, वाजनाय, प्रतये, अन्तरिताय, लिङ्गाय, शक्तिध्वजाय, नासिकायै, कर्णपालिकायै, कपोताय, वाजनाय, पङ्कजाय, वाजनाय, उत्तराय, बोधिकायै, वीरकाण्डाय, फलकायै, वेत्राय, उत्सन्धिकाय, वृषाय, कुम्भाय, वृष-आस्याय, पद्माय, भीरवे, स्कन्धाय, पद्ममालायै, स्तम्भान्तराय, वेदिकायै, महापट्टिकायै, कण्ठाय, कुमुदाय, जगत्यै, उपानाय, (इति ६३ अङ्गानि) स्वाहा' इति व्याहृत्यन्तं हुत्वा;
> ***vimāna-triṣaṣṭyaṅga-homāḥ -***
> *'stūpimukulāya, padmāya, phalakāyai, padmāya, kaṇṭhāya, kṣepaṇāya, vetrāya, kṣepaṇāya, kaṇṭhāya, kṣepaṇāya, paṅkajāya, kumbhāya, paṅkajāya, kṣepaṇāya, padmāya, phalakāyai, avalagnāya, kumbhāya, śaktidhvajāya, padmāya, ubhāya, nāsikāyai, phalakāyai, daṇḍāya, mṛṇālikāyai, muṣṭibandhāya, vājanāya, uttarāya, kaṇṭhāya, ūhapratyūhāya, vedikāyai, bhūmikalpāya, vājanāya, prataye, antaritāya, liṅgāya, śaktidhvajāya, nāsikāyai, karṇapālikāyai, kapotāya, vājanāya, paṅkajāya, vājanāya, uttarāya, bodhikāyai, vīrakāṇḍāya, phalakāyai, vetrāya, utsandhikāya, vṛṣāya, kumbhāya, vṛṣa-āsyāyai, padmāya, bhīrave, skandhāya, padmamālāyaai, stambhāntarāya, vedikāyai, mahāpaṭṭikāyai, kaṇṭhāya, kumudāya, jagatyai, upānāya, (iti 63 aṅgāni) svāhā' iti vyāhṛtyantaṃ hutvā;*

> विमानं परितः प्रागादि-चतुर्दिक्षु सवत्सां गां, घृतं, मधु, क्षीरम्, अष्टधान्यराशींश्च सङ्कल्प्य, प्रच्छन्नपटं कृत्वा, देवानां तत्-तत्-मन्त्रेण अक्षिमोचनं कृत्वा; तत् प्रच्छन्नपटं विसृज्य, गवादिदर्शनं द्रव्याणि दर्शयित्वा; अग्निं विसृज्य, तत्काले तद्-विमान-शुद्ध्यर्थं कुम्भं सूत्रेण वस्त्राभ्यां च वेष्टयित्वा, पूतवारिणा आपूर्य, सुवर्णम् अक्षतान् कूर्चं च विक्षिप्य, 'इदमापः शिवाः' इत्यभिमन्त्र्य, विष्णुसूक्तेन 'आपो हि ष्ठा' पवमानसूक्तैः च विमानं समभ्युक्ष्य, पुण्याहं कारयेत्।
> *vimānaṃ parito prāgādi-caturdikṣu savatsāṃ gāṃ, ghṛtaṃ, madhu, kṣīram, aṣṭadhānyarāśīṃśca saṅkalpya, pracchannapaṭaṃ kṛtvā, devānāṃ tat-tat-mantreṇa akṣimocanaṃ kṛtvā; tat pracchannapaṭaṃ visṛjya, gavādidarśanaṃ dravyāṇi darśayitvā; agniṃ visṛjya, tatkāle tad-vimāna-śuddhyarthaṃ kumbhaṃ sūtreṇa vastrābhyāṃ ca veṣṭayitvā, pūtavāriṇā āpūry, suvarṇam akṣatān kūrcaṃ ca vikṣipya, 'idamāpaḥ śivāḥ' ityabhimantry, viṣṇusūktena 'āpo hi ṣṭhā' pavamānasūktaiḥ ca vimānaṃ samabhyukṣya, puṇyāhaṃ kārayet.*
> 
> **यज्ञशाला-प्रवेशनं कुम्भावाहनं च -**
> प्रतिष्ठा-उक्त-दिनात् पूर्वे अपराह्णे प्रागादि यज्ञशालां संशोध्य — प्राच्यां मुखमण्टप-पूर्वे सभ्यम् आहवनीयं च, विमानस्य दक्षिणभागे अन्वाहार्यं, पश्चिमे गार्हपत्यम्, उत्तरे च आवसथ्यं च सङ्कल्प्य; तद्-आलय-उपरिस्थ-देवानां प्रत्येकम् औपासनाग्निकुण्डं तत्-तत्-अभिमुखे विमानं परितः कल्पयेत्, एतद् उत्तमम्। सभ्यादि पञ्चाग्नीन् परिकल्पयेत्; एतन्मध्यमं सभ्याग्निम् एकमेव कल्पयेद् इति अधमम् आचक्षते।
> ***yajñaśālā-praveśanaṃ kumbhāvāhanaṃ ca -***
> *pratiṣṭhā-ukta-dināt pūrve aparāhṇe prāgādi yajñaśālāṃ saṃśodhya — prācyāṃ mukhamanṭapa-pūrve sabhyam āhavanīyaṃ ca, vimānasya dakṣiṇabhāge anvāhāryaṃ, paścime gārhapatyam, uttare ca āvasathyaṃ ca saṅkalpya; tad-ālaya-uparistha-devānāṃ pratyekam aupāsanāgnikuṇḍaṃ tat-tat-abhimukhe vimānaṃ parito kalpayet, etad uttamam. sabhyādi pañcāgnīn parikalpayet; etanmadhyamaṃ sabhyāgnim ekameva kalpayed iti adhamam ācakṣate.*
> 
> सर्वेषु आघारं हुत्वा, द्रोणद्वयपूर्णं कुम्भं संगृह्य, त्रिगुणेन एकेन वा सूत्रेण वेष्टयित्वा, 'शुची वो हव्य' (ऋ० ५-४-२४) इति प्रक्षाल्य, 'धारासु' इति अपाम् उत्पवनं कृत्वा, अद्भिरापूर्य, 'इदमापः शिवाः' इति अभिमन्त्र्य, वस्त्रयुग्मेन आवेष्ट्य; पञ्चरत्नानि सुवर्णं वा, अष्टमङ्गल-पञ्चायुध-वर्णचिह्नानि पृथक् पृथक् विष्णुसूक्तेन प्रक्षिप्य; अभ्यन्तरं प्रविश्य, ध्रुवबेरस्य अभिमुखे धान्यराशौ कुम्भं संन्यस्य, आचार्यः उत्तराभिमुखो भूत्वा ब्राह्मम् आसनम् आस्थाय, प्राणायामं कृत्वा, समाहितो देवं ध्यायन् ध्रुवबेरात् कुम्भेऽम्भसि तद्विमान-शक्तिं सम्यग् आवाहयेत्। यस्माद् देवेशो विष्णुः सर्वेषां प्राणभूतः सर्वावासश्च, तस्माद् विमानस्य ध्रुवाद् आवाहनं, तस्मिन् उद्धरासनं च कारयेत्।
> *sarveṣu āghāraṃ hutvā, droṇadvayapūrṇaṃ kumbhaṃ saṃgṛhya, triguṇena ekena vā sūtreṇa veṣṭayitvā, 'śucī vo havya' (ṛṣi 5-4-24) iti prakṣālya, 'dhārāsu' iti apām utpavanaṃ kṛtvā, adbhirāpūry, 'idamāpaḥ śivāḥ' iti abhimantry, vastrayugmena āveṣṭya; pañcaratnāni suvarṇaṃ vā, aṣṭamaṅgala-pañcāyudha-varṇacihnāni pṛthak pṛthak viṣṇusūktena prakṣipya; abhyantaraṃ praviśya, dhruvaberasya abhimukhe dhānyarāśou kumbhaṃ saṃnyasya, ācāryaḥ uttarābhimukho bhūtvā brāhmam āsanam āsthāya, prāṇāyāmaṃ kṛtvā, samāhito devaṃ dhyāyan dhruvaberāt kumbhe'mbhasi tadvimāna-śaktiṃ samyag āvāhayet. yasmād deveśo viṣṇuḥ sarveṣāṃ prāṇabhūtaḥ sarvāvāsaśca, tasmād vimānasya dhruvād āvāhanaṃ, tasmin uddharāsanaṃ ca kārayet.*
> 
> **कलश-स्थापनम् अभिषेकश्च -**
> पश्चात् शकुनसूक्तं समुच्चार्य, कुम्भम् आनीय, तस्य आलयस्य अभिमुखे सभ्याग्निकुण्डस्य पश्चिमे धान्यवेद्यां वस्त्रेणास्तीर्य, तस्यां कुम्भं संन्यस्य; आलयं परितः चतुर्विंशति कलशान् न्यासार्थं धान्यैः पङ्क्तिं कृत्वा; तस्याम् ऐशानादि पञ्चगव्य-घृत-मधु-दधि-क्षीर-गन्धोदक-अक्षतोदक-फलोदक-कुशोदक-रत्नोदक-जप्योदक-सर्वौषध्युदकानि प्रत्येकम् उपस्नान-संयुक्तानि यथाक्रमेण विन्यस्य, अधिदेवान् समाराध्य; यथोक्तमन्त्रैः विमानस्य पादमूले प्रदक्षिणावशेन अभिषिच्य, "विमानं विष्णुमयं देवावासं वैकुण्ठोद्भवम्" इति चतुर्भिः मन्त्रैः घण्टान्तम् अभ्यर्च्य, हेमादिपात्रे तण्डुलोपरि न्यस्त-प्रतिसरं त्रिवृता स्वस्तिसूक्तेन अभिमृश्य, "स्वस्तिदा विशस्पतिः" इति तस्य पादमूले बध्नीयात्।
> ***kalaśa-sthāpanam abhiṣekaśca -***
> *paścāt śakunasūktaṃ samuccārya, kumbham ānīya, tasya ālayasya abhimukhe sabhyāgnikuṇḍasya paścime dhānyavedyāṃ vastreṇāstīrya, tasyāṃ kumbhaṃ saṃnyasya; ālayaṃ parito caturviṃśati kalaśān nyāsārthaṃ dhānyaiḥ paṅktiṃ kṛtvā; tasyām aiśānādi pañcagavya-ghṛta-madhu-dadhi-kṣīra-gandhodaka-akṣatodaka-phalodaka-kuśodaka-ratnodaka-japyodaka-sarvouṣadhyudakāni pratyekam upasnāna-saṃyuktāni yathākratena vinyasya, adhidevān samārādhya; yathoktamantraiḥ vimānasya pādamūle pradakṣiṇāvaśena abhiṣicya, "vimānaṃ viṣṇumayaṃ devāvāsaṃ vaikuṇṭhodbhavam" iti caturbhiḥ mantraiḥ ghanṭāntam abhyarcya, hemādipātre taṇḍulopari nyasta-pratisaraṃ trivṛtā svastisūktena abhimṛśya, "svastidā viśaspatiḥ" iti tasya pādamūle badhnīyāt.*
> 
> **शयनाधिवासः होमश्च -**
> विमानस्य शयनार्थं तत्-परितः शय्यावेदिं कृत्वा, हस्तमात्रविस्तारां तालोत्सेधां द्विभागोत्सेधां भागोत्सेधां वा व्रीहिभिः कृत्वा; नवैः वस्त्रैः स्तूप्यादि-विमानान्तम् अधिष्ठान-अन्तं, कपोतान्तं वा निश्छिद्रं परिवेष्ट्य, "यद् वैष्णवम्" इति शयनं सङ्कल्प्य; चतुर्दिक्षु चतुर्वेद-अध्ययनं कारयित्वा; सभ्याग्निं समुज्वल्य, हौत्रं प्रशंस्य, दक्षिण-प्रणिधौ "विमानं विष्णुमयं देवावासं वैकुण्ठोद्भवम्" इति, उत्तर-प्रणिधौ आलयाङ्गदेवान् न्यक्षादीन् इन्द्रादि-लोकपालान् च आवाह्य, आवाहनक्रमेण जुष्टाकार-स्वाहाकारं च कृत्वा;
> ***śayanādhivāsaḥ homaśca -***
> *vimānasya śayanārthaṃ tat-parito śayyāvediṃ kṛtvā, hastamātravistārāṃ tālotsedhāṃ dvibhāgotsedhāṃ bhāgotsedhāṃ vā vrīhibhiḥ kṛtvā; navaiḥ vastraiḥ stūpyādi-vimānāntam adhiṣṭhāna-antaṃ, kapotāntaṃ vā niśchidraṃ pariveṣṭya, "yad vaiṣṇavam" iti śayanaṃ saṅkalpya; caturdikṣu caturveda-adhyayanaṃ kārayitvā; sabhyāgniṃ samujvalya, hautraṃ praśaṃsya, dakṣiṇa-praṇidhou "vimānaṃ viṣṇumayaṃ devāvāsaṃ vaikuṇṭhodbhavam" iti, uttara-praṇidhou ālayāṅgadevān nyakṣādīn indrādi-lokapālān ca āvāhya, āvāhanakrameṇa juṣṭākāra-svāhākāraṃ ca kṛtvā;*
> 
> पश्चात् वैष्णव-होमं (विष्णुसूक्तं पुरुषसूक्तं तदालयगतदेवानां मन्त्रांश्च जुहुयात्), महेन्द्रदेवत्यादि तत्-तत्-अङ्ग-मन्त्रैः सह न्यक्षादि-देवत्यमन्त्रांश्च आवसथ्ये, च एकाक्षरादि सूक्तं क्षत्रदेवत्यम् आलयोपरिस्थ-देवानां अग्निकुण्डेषु तत्-तत्-देवत्यमन्त्रान् दश-पर्यायतो जुहोति।
> *paścāt vaiṣṇava-homaṃ (viṣṇusūktaṃ puruṣasūktaṃ tadālayagatadevānāṃ mantrāṃśca juhuyāt), mahendradevatyādi tat-tat-aṅga-mantraiḥ saha nyakṣādi-devatyamantrāṃśca āvasathye, ca ekākṣarādi sūktaṃ kṣatradevatyam ālayoparistha-devānāṃ agnikuṇḍeṣu tat-tat-devatyamantrān daśa-paryāyato juhoti.*
> 
> **प्रातः-काले प्रतिष्ठा -**
> नृत्तगेयवाद्यैः रात्रिशेषं नयेत्। आचार्यादयः प्रभाते स्नात्वा आलयम् आविश्य देवं प्रणमेयुः; सुमुहूर्ते प्राप्ते, यजमानः आचार्यादीन् वस्त्र-उत्तरीयाद्यैः अलङ्कृत्य दक्षिणादानं दद्यात्। पश्चात् सर्वान् अग्नीन् विसृजेयुः।
> ***prātaḥ-kāle pratiṣṭhā -***
> *nṛttageyavādyaiḥ rātriśeṣaṃ nayet. ācāryādayaḥ prabhāte snātvā ālayam āviśya devaṃ praṇameyuḥ; sumuhūrte prāpte, yajamānaḥ ācāryādīn vastra-uttarīyādyaiḥ alaṅkṛtya dakṣiṇādānaṃ dadyāt. paścāt sarvān agnīn visṛjeyuḥ.*
> 
> आचार्यः शिरसा कुम्भं धारयन्, सर्ववाद्यसमायुक्तं सर्वालङ्कारसंयुक्तम् आलयं प्रदक्षिणीकृत्य, कुम्भं विमाने समारोप्य, तस्य मूर्ध्नि "आयातु भगवान्" इति मन्त्रान्ते "विमानं विष्णुमयं देवावासं वैकुण्ठोद्भवम्" इति चतुर्भिः मन्त्रैः सम्यग् आवाह्य, पश्चात् प्रागादि-तद्विमानगतान् देवांश्च तत्-तत्-मन्त्रेण आवाह्य, पश्चात् तद्विमानगतान् देवान् तत्-तत्-मन्त्रेण आवाहयेत्। एवं विमानप्रतिष्ठायां महाप्रतिष्ठाफलं लभते। तस्माद् देवेशेन सार्धं, पृथग् एव वा कारयेद् इति आह मरीचिः ॥
> *ācāryaḥ śirasā kumbhaṃ dhārayan, sarvavādyasamāyuktaṃ sarvālaṅkārasamyaktam ālayaṃ pradakṣiṇīkṛtya, kumbhaṃ vimāne samāropya, tasya mūrdhni "āyātu bhagavān" iti mantrānte "vimānaṃ viṣṇumayaṃ devāvāsaṃ vaikuṇṭhodbhavam" iti caturbhiḥ mantraiḥ samyag āvāhya, paścāt prāgādi-tadvimānagatān devāṃśca tat-tat-mantreṇa āvāhya, paścāt tadvimānagatān devān tat-tat-mantreṇa āvāhayeet. evaṃ vimānapratiṣṭhāyāṃ mahāpratiṣṭhāphalaṃ labhate. tasmād deveśena sārdhaṃ, pṛthag eva vā kārayed iti āha marīciḥ ॥*

#### English Translation
*   **Consecration of the Vimāna (Temple Spire):** I shall now declare the consecration rules for the *Vimāna* (temple spire/sanctum structure). Having constructed the *Vimāna* in the prescribed manner, one should establish the following deities:
    *   *Porch Entrance:* Tāpasa (the ascetic) on the right of the front porch (*mukha-maṇṭapa*) door, and Siddhideva (the bestower of success) on the left (north).
    *   *Vimāna Walls:* Satya on the south of the Vimāna, Achyuta on the west, and Aniruddha on the north.
    *   *Porch Flanks:* Vināyaka (Gaṇeśa) on the south of the front porch, and Vindhyavāsinī (Durgā) on the north.
    *   *Ground Level:* A standing (*sthānaka*) or a seated (*āsana*) form of the Lord on the ground floor (*mūla-tala*).
    *   *Second Level:* The forms of Puruṣa, etc., in the east and other directions on the second floor. Alternatively, the Discus (*Cakra*) in the east, Narasiṃha in the south, Achyuta in the west, and Varāha in the north.
    *   *Neck Level (Gala-sthāna):* Puruṣa, etc., in the east and other directions, Dakṣiṇāmūrti in the south, Pitāmaha (Brahmā) in the north, Narasiṃha in the west, and Varāha in the east.
    *   *Corners:* Dvijendra (Garuḍa) or Mṛgendra (Lion) in the corners.
    *   *Special Form:* In this Vimāna, on the first floor in the center, or on the third floor, in any desired place: Narasiṃha facing south or facing west. This is specifically declared to destroy all enemies and accomplish all goals.

*   **Measurements and Materials:**
    *   Their dimensions should start from eleven *mānāṅgula* up to twenty-seven, increasing by two fingers at a time, resulting in nine different size variations (*navadhā*). With one of these desired measurements, the icons should be made of stone (*śaila*), clay (*mṛṇmaya*), brick (*aiṣṭaka*), mortar (*saudha*), or painted (*ālekhya*), as appropriate.
    *   If a stone icon (*śaila-bera*) is desired to be colored, one should first mix sand in double, half-double, or triple proportions with the sugar-cement (*sarkara-kalka*) described earlier, and wrap it over the icon with resin-water (*niryāsa-ambu*). The rest of the procedure is the same.

*   **Preparation and Timing:**
    *   For the auxiliary deities of the Vimāna, the Vimāna-guardians, gatekeepers, and the entourage of Śrī, Bhūmi, etc., in making their icons, calculations of income and expenditure (*āya-vyaya*) are not required.
    *   Having sown seeds (*aṅkurārpaṇa*) before the day of consecration for the main deity and the Vimāna, and having built a canopy (*prapā*) facing the temple on the south, west, or north, or around the Vimāna, and having decorated it with archways (*toraṇa*), etc., one should perform the eye-opening (*akṣi-mocana*) of the Vimāna deities on the third or second day prior to the consecration day, either with or without mantras.

*   **The Eye-Opening and Fire Rituals:**
    *   For the eye-opening, one should set up an *aupāsana* fire-pit on the north of the temple, offer the *vāstu-homa*, purify the temple with fire circumambulation (*paryagni*) and *pañcagavya*, perform the *puṇyāha* (declaration of auspiciousness), and set up an *aupāsana* fire-pit facing the temple.
    *   After the introductory offerings (*āghāra*), one should offer the *Vaiṣṇava* homa, *Viṣṇu-sūkta*, *Puruṣa-sūkta*, *Mahī-bhūta-devatya* homa, and the limb-homas (*aṅga-homa*) from the finial (*stūpī*) down to the base plinth (*upāna*) of the Vimāna, saying *"dādbhyaḥ svāhā"* (greetings to the teeth/mouth of the temple) and so forth.

*   **Oblations to the 63 Parts of the Vimāna:**
    *   Oblations are offered to the 63 parts of the Vimāna structure, including the finial cupola (*stūpi-mukulā*), the lotus ornament (*padma*), the abacus (*phalakā*), the neck (*kaṇṭha*), the projection (*kṣepaṇa*), the band (*vetra*), the water-pot ornament (*kumbha*), the side projections, the brackets, the lion/elephant motifs, the rafters, the cornices, the plinths, and the base (*upāna*), concluding with the word *svāhā*.
    *   Having offered these, one should place cows with calves, ghee, honey, milk, and eight types of grains in the four directions around the Vimāna.
    *   Having set up a screening curtain (*pracchanna-paṭa*), the priest performs the eye-opening of the deities with their respective mantras, removes the curtain, and shows them auspicious sights like cows.
    *   He dismisses the fire, and to purify the Vimāna at that time, he wraps a pot (*kumbha*) with threads and cloths, fills it with pure water, places gold, unbroken rice, and a grass-bundle (*kūrca*) inside, consecrates it with the mantra *"idam āpaḥ śivāḥ"*, and sprinkles the Vimāna with the *Viṣṇu-sūkta* and the *pavamāna* verses, followed by the *puṇyāha* ritual.

*   **Preparing the Sacrificial Pavilion (Yāgaśālā):**
    *   In the afternoon before the consecration day, after purifying the sacrificial hall starting from the east, the priest establishes the *Sabhya* and *Āhavanīya* fires in the east, in front of the porch, the *Anvāhārya* fire on the south of the Vimāna, the *Gārhapatya* on the west, and the *Āvasathya* on the north. He should set up an individual *aupāsana* fire-pit facing each deity situated on the temple structure, around the Vimāna. This is considered the best method.
    *   One should set up the five fires starting with the *Sabhya*, or at least the *Sabhya* fire alone (which is considered the lower option).

*   **Invoking the Vimāna Divinity:**
    *   Having offered the introductory libations (*āghāra*) in all fires, the priest takes a principal pot (*kumbha*) filled with two *droṇas* of water, wraps it with a triple or single thread, washes it with *"śucī vo havya"*, purifies the water with *"dhārāsu"*, fills it with water, touches it with *"idam āpaḥ śivāḥ"*, wraps it in a pair of cloths, places five gems or gold, the eight auspicious symbols (*aṣṭa-maṅgala*), and the symbols of the five weapons and castes, and drops them in with the *Viṣṇu-sūkta*.
    *   Entering the sanctum, he places the pot on a heap of grains facing the fixed icon (*dhruva-bera*). The priest, facing north, sits in the *brahmāsana* posture, performs breath control (*prāṇāyāma*), and with a concentrated mind meditating on the Lord, invokes the divinity of the Vimāna from the fixed icon into the water of the pot.
    *   Because Lord Viṣṇu is the life-force and abode of all, the invocation is done from the fixed icon, and the priest should also establish the *uddharāsana* (raising seat) on it.

*   **Bathing and Tying the Protective Thread:**
    *   Chanting the *Śakuna-sūkta*, he brings the pot, spreads a cloth on a bed of grains to the west of the *Sabhya fire-pit* facing the temple, and places the pot there.
    *   Around the temple, he places twenty-four pots or half of that on heaps of grains in a row. He places the waters of the five cow-products (*pañcagavya*), ghee, honey, curd, milk, scented water, rice water, fruit water, kuśa water, gem water, prayer-consecrated water, and all-herb water, each with its secondary bath-vessel (*upasnāna*), in order starting from the northeast.
    *   Having worshipped the presiding deities, he bathes (*abhiṣecayet*) the base of the Vimāna (*pāda-mūla*) in a clockwise direction with the prescribed mantras, worships the Vimāna with the four mantras starting with *"vimānaṃ viṣṇumayaṃ devāvāsaṃ vaikuṇṭhodbhavam"*, touches the protective thread (*pratisara*) placed over rice in a golden vessel with the *Svasti-sūkta*, and ties it at the base of the Vimāna reciting *"svastidā viśaspatiḥ"*.

*   **The Couch-Placement (Śayana-vidhi) and Fire Sacrifices:**
    *   For the reclining of the Vimāna, he sets up a couch-altar (*śayyā-vedi*) around it, measuring one cubit in width and one or two spans in height, made of paddy. He wraps it seamlessly from the finial down to the base plinth or up to the cornice with nine new cloths, and projects the reclining state reciting *"yad vaiṣṇavam"*.
    *   Having arranged for the recitation of the four Vedas in the four directions, he kindles the *Sabhya* fire, praises the *hautra* duties, invokes the Vimāna with *"vimānaṃ viṣṇumayaṃ"* in the southern water-vessel (*praṇidhi*), and the Vimāna-deities like Nyakṣa and the guardians of the directions starting with Indra in the northern vessel.
    *   Having offered the *juṣṭākāra* and *svāhākāra* oblations in order, he should offer the *Vaiṣṇava* oblations (using the *Viṣṇu-sūkta*, *Puruṣa-sūkta*, and the mantras of the temple deities), the *Indra-devatyā* homa with the respective limb-mantras, and the *Nyakṣa-devatyā* mantras in the *Āvasathya* fire.
    *   He offers the *Ekākṣara-sūkta* for the royal deities, and in the fire-pits of the deities on the temple structure, he offers their respective deity-mantras ten times.

*   **Morning Rituals and Final Consecration:**
    *   The priest spends the rest of the night with dance, song, and music. In the morning, having bathed, the priests enter the temple, awaken the Lord and the Goddesses with the *Praṇava* (Om), worship them, and place five gems or gold in the pedestals.
    *   The patron gives sacrificial fees (*dakṣiṇā*) to the teachers and assistants. Having dismissed the fires, at the auspicious hour, the teacher carries the pot of Śrī Devi and Bhumi Devi on his head, walking in front, and the assistants follow carrying the icons.
    *   Accompanied by all musical instruments, they circumambulate the temple, enter the sanctum, and place the two pots on a heap of grains facing the fixed deity.
    *   They place the two Goddesses on the pedestals to the right and left of the *Kautuka* icon with their respective mantras. The teacher, facing north, performs the letter-placement (*akṣara-nyāsa*) on the fixed icon, invokes the energy first, and then performs the letter-placement on the *Kautuka* icon.
    *   Meditating on the two Goddesses, he invokes their presence from the fixed icon to the movable ones, like transferring light from one lamp to another (*dīpād dīpam iva āvāhayet*). On the fixed icon containing the Goddesses, he performs the letter-placement, invokes them, and then does the same for the two Goddesses.
    *   Offering them a seat with *"astv āsanam"*, declaring the day auspicious, and worshipping the Lord along with the two Goddesses according to the daily worship rules, he offers food oblations and performs five prostrations. If the Goddesses are consecrated in separate shrines, he should perform the same ritual for them separately.
