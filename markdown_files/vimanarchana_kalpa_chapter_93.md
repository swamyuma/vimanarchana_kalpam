# Vimanarchana Kalpa of Sage Marīci (Vaikhānasa Āgama)
## Chapter 93: Jīvōdbhava-vidhiḥ (Exposition of Vital Winds, the Soul, and its Paths of Transmigration)

This document contains the Sanskrit text, IAST transliteration, and English translation of the 93rd Chapter (*Trinavatitamaḥ Paṭalaḥ*) of the **Vimānārcanākalpa** (attributed to Sage Marīci, representing the Vaikhānasa school of Vaishnavism).

This chapter is a comprehensive Agamic exposition on the nature of the vital winds (*vāyu*), the embodied soul (*jīva*), and the post-mortem destinations based on karma. It begins by describing the ten vital winds (Prāṇa, Apāna, Vyāna, Udāna, Samāna, Nāga, Kūrma, Kṛkara, Devadatta, and Dhanañjaya), their colors, bodily locations, and physiological functions. It then defines the nature of the individual soul (*jīvātman*) and explains how it acts and moves after death through the processes of cosmic dissolution (*pañcabhautika-pralaya*). Finally, it describes the divergent post-mortem paths of the soul: the hellish realms (*naraka*) for the doers of bad deeds, the Path of the Ancestors (*pitṛyāṇa* / *dhūmādi-mārga*) for performes of ritual actions seeking temporary heaven, and the Path of the Gods (*devayāna* / *arcirādi-mārga*) for those who practice devotion and surrender to the Supreme Brahman, leading to final liberation in the realm of Viṣṇu.

### Section 1: The Ten Vital Winds and their Functions

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **अथ त्रिनवतितमः पटलः**
> **नाडीषु प्राणादिवायुसञ्चारः, तेषां मुख्यता च**

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **atha trinavatitamaḥ paṭalaḥ**
> **nāḍīṣu prāṇādivāyusañcāraḥ, tēṣāṃ mukhyatā ca**

**Translation (English):**
*   **The Ten Winds:** Within these channels, ten vital winds circulate (*atha ētāsu nāḍīṣu ... daśa vāyavaḥ sañcaranti*): Prāṇa, Apāna, Vyāna, Udāna, Samāna, Nāga, Kūrma, Kṛkara, Devadatta, and Dhanañjaya.

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> अथ एतासु नाडीषु प्राण-अपान-व्यान-उदान-समानाः नाग-कूर्म-कृकर-देवदत्त-धनञ्जयाः इति दश वायवः सञ्चरन्ति । तेषु प्राणादयः पञ्च वायवो मुख्याः । तेषु प्राण-अपानौ मुख्यौ; एतयोः प्राण एव मुख्यः ।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> atha ētāsu nāḍīṣu prāṇa-apāna-vyāna-udāna-samānāḥ nāga-kūrma-kṛkara-dēvadatta-dhanañjayāḥ iti daśa vāyavaḥ sañcaranti | tēṣu prāṇādayaḥ pañca vāyavō mukhyāḥ | tēṣu prāṇa-apānau mukhyau; ētayōḥ prāṇa ēva mukhyaḥ ||

**Translation (English):**
*   **Hierarchy of Winds:** Among them, the first five are principal (*teṣu prāṇādayaḥ pañca vāyavō mukhyāḥ*). Among those, Prāṇa and Apāna are the chief ones (*teṣu prāṇa-apānau mukhyau*); and between these two, Prāṇa is the supreme (*ētayōḥ prāṇa ēva mukhyaḥ*).

#### Verse / Paragraph 3
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **प्राणादीनां स्थानं स्वरूपं च**
> प्राणस्थानम् -- आस्य-नासिकाद्वय-मध्यं, हृन्मध्यं, नाभि-मध्यं, पादाङ्गुष्ठौ इति पञ्च । स च प्राणः राजावर्तमणिप्रभः निःश्वासोच्छ्वासकरः इत्ययं समाश्रित्य मुखनासिके करोति ।
> अपानः इन्द्रगोपकसमप्रभः गुदं (नाभिं च) समाश्रित्य विण्मूत्रविसर्जनं करोति । समानः सन्धिगतः फेनविशदवर्णः नाभिं समाश्रित्य हानाऽऽदान-चेष्टादीनि करोति । उदानः किञ्जल्कसदृशः कण्ठम् आश्रित्य अन्न-पानादि-पोषणक्रियां करोति । व्यानः व्योमाभः क्षीरे सर्पिरिव सर्वं व्याप्य कर-चरण-चलनादिकं करोति । नागः श्वेतः कण्ठं समाश्रित्य उद्गारादीन् करोति । कूर्मः कृष्णः (रक्तः) नेत्रे समाश्रित्य निमीलन-उन्मेषणादि करोति । कृकरः कृष्णः उदरं समाश्रित्य क्षुत्-पिपासादीनि करोति । देवदत्तः पीतवर्णः सर्वगः जृम्भणादि (तन्द्रीं च) करोति । धनञ्जयः श्यामाभः सर्वगः शोथादिकर्म करोति ।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **prāṇādīnāṃ sthānaṃ svarūpaṃ ca**
> prāṇasthānam -- āsya-nāsikadvaya-madhyaṃ, hṛdmadhyaṃ, nābhimadhyaṃ, pādāṅguṣṭhau iti pañca | sa ca prāṇaḥ rājāvartamaṇiprabhaḥ niḥśvāsōcchvāsakaraḥ ityayaṃ samāśritya muxanāsikē karōti |
> apānaḥ indragōpakasamaprabhaḥ gudaṃ (nābhiṃ ca) samāśritya viṇmūtravisarjanaṃ karōti | samānaḥ sandhigataḥ phēnaviśadavarṇaḥ nābhiṃ samāśritya hānā'dāna-cēṣṭādīni karōti | udānaḥ kiñjalkasadṛśaḥ kaṇṭham āśritya anna-pānādi-pōṣaṇakriyāṃ karōti | vyānaḥ vyōmābhaḥ kṣīrē sarpiriva sarvaṃ vyāpya kara-caraṇa-calanādikaṃ karōti | nāgaḥ śvētaḥ kaṇṭhaṃ samāśritya udgārādīn karōti | kūrmaḥ kṛṣṇaḥ nētrē samāśritya nimīlana-unmēṣaṇādi karōti | kṛkaraḥ kṛṣṇaḥ udaraṃ samāśritya xut-pipāsādīni karōti | dēvadattaḥ pītavarṇaḥ sarvagaḥ jṛmbhaṇādi (tandrīṃ ca) karōti | dhanañjayaḥ śyāmābhaḥ sarvagaḥ śōthādikarma karōti ||

**Translation (English):**
*   **Locations and Functions of the Ten Winds:**
    *   **Prāṇa:** Located in five places: the mouth and nostrils, the heart center, the navel center, and the two big toes (*prāṇasthānam -- āsya-nāsikadvaya-madhyaṃ, hṛdmadhyaṃ, nābhimadhyaṃ, pādāṅguṣṭhau*). Shined like the lapis lazuli gem (*rājāvartamaṇiprabhaḥ*), it governs exhalation and inhalation through the mouth and nose (*niḥśvāsōcchvāsakaraḥ*).
    *   **Apāna:** Resembling the bright red cochineal insect (*indragōpakasamaprabhaḥ*), it resides in the anus/lower abdomen and governs the excretion of feces and urine (*gudaṃ samāśritya viṇmūtravisarjanaṃ karōti*).
    *   **Samāna:** Present in all joints (*sandhigataḥ*), having a frothy white color (*phēnaviśadavarṇaḥ*), it resides in the navel and governs the assimilation and movement of food/nutrients (*nābhiṃ samāśritya hānā'dāna-cēṣṭādīni karōti*).
    *   **Udāna:** Resembling the filament of a flower (*kiñjalkasadṛśaḥ*), it resides in the throat and governs the swallowing of food, drinking, and the nourishment of the body (*kaṇṭham āśritya anna-pānādi-pōṣaṇakriyāṃ karōti*).
    *   **Vyāna:** Possessing the color of the sky (*vyōmābhaḥ*), pervading the entire body like ghee in milk (*kṣīrē sarpiriva sarvaṃ vyāpya*), it governs the bending, stretching, and movement of hands and feet (*kara-caraṇa-calanādikaṃ karōti*).
    *   **Nāga:** White in color (*śvētaḥ*), it resides in the throat and causes belching and hiccups (*kaṇṭhaṃ samāśritya udgārādīn karōti*).
    *   **Kūrma:** Dark in color (*kṛṣṇaḥ*), it resides in the eyes and governs the opening and closing of eyelids (*nētrē samāśritya nimīlana-unmēṣaṇādi karōti*).
    *   **Kṛkara:** Dark in color (*kṛṣṇaḥ*), it resides in the stomach and generates hunger and thirst (*udaraṃ samāśritya xut-pipāsādīni karōti*).
    *   **Devadatta:** Yellow in color (*pītavarṇaḥ*), all-pervading (*sarvagaḥ*), it causes yawning and combats lethargy (*jṛmbhaṇādi karōti*).
    *   **Dhanañjaya:** Of dark/bluish hue (*śyāmābhaḥ*), all-pervading, it remains in the body even after death and causes swelling of the corpse (*śōthādikarma karōti*).

### Section 2: The Embodied Soul and its Post-Mortem Descent

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **जीवात्मस्वरूपं तस्य कर्म तस्य जननक्रमश्च**
> जीवात्माऽञ्जनाभः नित्यशुद्धः (बुधः बोधनिर्विकारः नित्याणुप्रमाणः) सर्वगत इत्येवम्भूतं देहं प्रविश्य शुभाशुभकर्माणि करोति । तत्कर्म द्विधा भवति -- ऐहिकम् आमुष्मिकं चेति । तत्रैहिकं भोजनाऽऽच्छादन-स्थान-गमन-आसन-शयनादिकम् । आमुष्मिकं तु अहिंसा-दान-धर्म-परोपकार-भगवदाराधनादि-पुण्य-पापानि सर्वाणि भवन्ति । दैवयोगाद् देहावसाने स्वकर्मानुरूपं परलोकं प्राप्य तत्तत्कर्मफलं भुक्त्वा, क्षीणे फले निवृत्तः सन् आकाशं विशति ।
> आकाशद् वायुर्भूत्वा, वायुभूतोऽग्निं विशति, अग्निभूतो धूमं भूत्वा, धूमो भूत्वा अभ्रं विशति, अभ्रभूत्वा अपो विशति, अब्भूत्वा भूमौ वर्षति, भूम्या ओषधि-वनस्पतीन् विशति, ओषधीभ्योऽन्नं, अन्नाच्छुक्ल-शोणितौ भवतः । ततः स्वविषयां योनिं विशति (प्रविशति) । प्रतिदिवस-वृद्ध्या परिणतो जायते च । एवं क्षेत्र-क्षेत्रज्ञयोश्च परस्परं सम्बन्धात् अनन्तसंसार-स्थावर-जङ्गम-नर-पशु-पक्षि-जरायुज-अण्डज-स्वेदज-उद्भिज्ज-प्रभेदा बहवो भवन्ति इति विज्ञायते ॥
> ***jīvātmasvarūpaṃ tasya karma tasya jananakramaśca***
> *jīvātmā'ñjanābhaḥ nityaśuddhaḥ (budhaḥ bōdhanirvikāraḥ nityāṇupramāṇaḥ) sarvagata ityēvambhūtaṃ dēhaṃ praviśya śubhāśubhakarmāṇi karōti | tatkarma dvidhā bhavati -- aihikam āmuṣmikaṃ cēti | tatraihikaṃ bhōjanā'cchādana-sthāna-gamana-āsana-śayanādikam | āmuṣmikaṃ tu ahiṃsā-dāna-dharma-parōpakāra-bhagavadārādhanādi-puṇya-pāpāni sarvāṇi bhavanti | daivayōgād dēhāvasānē svakarmānurūpaṃ paralōkaṃ prāpya tattatkarmaphalaṃ bhuktvā, kṣīṇē phalē nivṛttaḥ san ākāśaṃ viśati |*
> *ākāśād vāyurbhūtvā, vāyubhūtō'gniṃ viśati, agnibhūtō dhūmaṃ bhūtvā, dhūmō bhūtvā abhraṃ viśati, abhrabhūtvā apō viśati, abbhūtvā bhūmau varṣati, bhūmyā oṣadhi-vanaspatīn viśati, oṣadhībhyō'nnaṃ, annācchukla-śōṇitau bhavataḥ | tataḥ svaviṣayāṃ yōniṃ viśati (praviśati) | pratidivasa-vṛddhyā pariṇatō jāyatē ca | ēvaṃ kṣētra-kṣētrajñayōśca parasparaṃ sambandhāt anantasaṃsāra-sthāvara-jaṅgama-nara-paśu-pakṣi-jarāyuja-aṇdaja-svēdaja-udbhijja-prabhēdā bahavō bhavanti iti vijñāyatē ||*

#### English Translation
*   **The Nature of the Soul:** The individual soul (*jīvātmā*) has the brilliance of collyrium (*añjanābhaḥ*), is eternally pure, wise, changeless consciousness, and atomic in size (*nityaśuddhaḥ budhaḥ bōdhanirvikāraḥ nityāṇupramāṇaḥ*). It enters the physical body (*dēhaṃ praviśya*) and performs auspicious and inauspicious actions (*śubhāśubhakarmāṇi karōti*).

*   **Twofold Actions:** Karma is of two kinds: worldly/temporal (*aihika*) and otherworldly/spiritual (*āmuṣmika*). Worldly actions include eating, clothing, dwelling, walking, sitting, and sleeping. Spiritual actions include non-violence, charity, righteousness, helping others, and worship of the Lord, which generate merit and demerit.

*   **The Path of Descent:** When the body dies by destiny (*dēhāvasānē svakarmānurūpaṃ paralōkaṃ prāpya*), the soul reaches the other world, experiences the fruits of its actions, and when the fruits are exhausted, it returns to the sky (*ākāśaṃ viśati*).

*   **Ascent through the Elements:** From the sky, it becomes wind; as wind, it enters fire; as fire, it becomes smoke; as smoke, it enters clouds; as clouds, it becomes rain; as rain, it falls on the earth; from the earth, it enters herbs and plants; from plants, it becomes food; from food, semen and blood are generated. Then it enters the womb of the appropriate mother (*tataḥ svaviṣayāṃ yōniṃ viśati*).

*   **Embodiment:** Developing day by day, it is born. Thus, due to the mutual connection between the field (body) and the knower of the field (soul) (*ēvaṃ kṣētra-kṣētrajñayōśca parasparaṃ sambandhāt*), infinite variations of beings are born in the cycle of transmigration: stationary (plants), moving, humans, animals, birds, viviparous, oviparous, sweat-born, and sprout-born (*sthāvara-jaṅgama-nara-paśu-pakṣi-jarāyuja-aṇdaja-svēdaja-udbhijja-prabhēdāḥ*).

### Section 3: The Process of Individual Dissolution (Death)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **पञ्चभौतिक-प्रलयः (मरणक्रमः)**
> अथातः पञ्चभौतिकप्रलयं व्याख्यास्यामः --
> यदा पुराकृतकर्मक्षयात् देहो विनाशमुपयाति, तदा प्रथमं गन्धात्मनाऽवस्थितं भूमिगुणं तत्कारणभूता एव आपः कवलीकुर्वन्ति । एवमद्भिः कवलीकृते तस्मिन् शारीरकाठिन्यं नश्यति । तदा शारीरं कुलालचक्रारूढ-भ्रमणक्रियामनुभवदिव व्याकुलमवतिष्ठते । एवं स्वकारणभूतास्वप्सु तत्कार्यभूतगुणे प्रविशति, तदा स्वशरीरम् आपादचूडं बाह्यवन्मन्यते । ततः कीलालाख्यं रुधिरं शुष्यति ।
> तस्यामवस्थायां रुधिरात्मनाऽवस्थितं जलं च स्वकारणे तेजसि विलीयते । क्लेदश्च स्वाशयात् भ्रश्यति, जलाधारत्वात् श्लेष्मा च स्वाशयान्मुक्तः शिथिलबन्धो भवति । तदा विमुक्तबन्धे श्लेष्माख्ये दोषे पित्ताख्यदोषेण सहाग्नौ पतिते, स चाग्निस्तं दग्ध्वा पुनर्दाह्यपदार्थाभावादुपरमति । तदाऽग्निः स्वकारणभूतं प्राणादिवायुमुपैति ।
> तदा वायुरात्मवान् भवति । एवमग्निना संयोगादरूढो वायुः सर्वस्रोतोमुखैः तिर्यग्-ऊर्ध्वम्-अधश्च आपादतलमस्तकं प्रविश्य शरीरम् इतस्ततश्चम्पयन् वेदनादिभिः मनो व्याकुलं करोति । तस्मान्मनसो व्याकुलीभावात् नष्टस्मृतिः मोहमावहति ।
> तदा आत्मीयव्यापारान्मुक्तः पञ्चवायुः स्वयोनिं विसृज्य उच्छ्वासमभिनिष्क्रामयति । एवमभिनिष्क्रामित उच्छ्वासो ब्रह्मनाडीभूत-सुषुम्नारन्ध्रं भित्त्वा शरीरस्थितम् ऊष्माणं च सङ्गृह्य, यथा चर्मभस्त्रायाः वातो निर्गच्छति मुखं न प्रविशति तद्वत् इदं मन्दं मृत्युरिति वहति । तस्मिन् काले वेगेनोत्थाय मुख्यप्राणभूतो वायुः कण्ठकूबर-सप्तद्वार-सन्धौ घुरघुरशब्दं कुर्वन् जीवात्मानम् एवं प्रकारेण विकृष्य, यथा जलूका पादान्तरम् अलभमाना न पूर्वपादं मुञ्चति, तथा जीवसहितो वायुरपि स्थानात् स्थानान्तरं विन्दन् उत्क्रामति । एवं गच्छन्तं जीवात्मानं सूक्ष्मशरीरिणं सुकृतदुष्कृते सह प्रधावेताम् । एवं पञ्चभौतिक-प्रलयक्रम इति विज्ञायते ॥
> ***pañcabhautika-pralayaḥ (maraṇakramaḥ)***
> *athātaḥ pañcabhautikapralayaṃ vyākhyāsyāmaḥ --*
> *yadā purākṛtakarmaxayāt dēhō vināśamupayāti, tadā prathamaṃ gandhātmanā'vasthitaṃ bhūmiguṇaṃ tatkāraṇabhūtā ēva āpaḥ kavalīkurvanti | ēvamadbhiḥ kavalīkṛtē tasmin śārīrakāṭhinyaṃ naśyati | tadā śārīraṃ kulālacakrārūḍha-bhramaṇakriyāmanubhavadiva vyākulamavatiṣṭhatē | ēvaṃ svakāraṇabhūtāsvapsu tatkāryabhūtaguṇē praviśati, tadā svaśarīram āpādacūḍaṃ bāhyavanmanyatē | tataḥ kīlālāxyaṃ rudhiraṃ śuṣyati |*
> *tasyāmavasthāyāṃ rudhirātmanā'vasthitaṃ jalaṃ ca svakāraṇē tējasi vilīyatē | klēdaśca svāśayāt bhraśyati, jalādhāratvāt ślēṣmā ca svāśayānmuktaḥ śithilabandhō bhavati | tadā vimuktabandhē ślēṣmāxyē dōṣē pittāxyadōṣēṇa sahāgnau patitē, sa cāgnistaṃ dagdhvā punardāhyapadārthābhāvāduparamati | tadā'gniḥ svakāraṇabhūtaṃ prāṇādivāyumupaiti |*
> *tadā vāyurātmavān bhavati | ēvamagninā saṃyōgādarūḍhō vāyusarvasrōtōmuxaiḥ tiryag-ūrdhvam-adhaśca āpādatalamastakaṃ praviśya śarīram itastatascampayan vēdanādibhiḥ manō vyākulaṃ karōti | tasmānmanasō vyākulībhāvāt naṣṭasmṛtiḥ mōhamāvahati |*
> *tadā ātmīyavyāpārānmuxaḥ pañcavāyuḥ svayōniṃ visṛjya ucchvāsamabhiniṣkrāmayati | ēvamabhiniṣkrāmitōcchvāsō brahmanāḍībhūta-suṣumnārandhraṃ bhittvā śarīrasthitam ūṣmāṇaṃ ca saṅgṛhya, yathā carmabhastrāyāḥ vātō nirgacchati muxaṃ na praviśati tadvat idaṃ mandaṃ mṛtyuriti vahati | tasmin kālē vēgēnōtthāya muxyaprāṇabhūtō vāyuḥ kaṇṭhakūbara-saptadvāra-sandhau ghurghuraśabdaṃ kurvan jīvātmānam ēvaṃ prakārēṇa vikṛṣya, yathā jalūkā pādāntaram alabhamānā na pūrvapādaṃ muñcati, tathā jīvasahitō vāyurapi sthānāt sthānāntaraṃ vindan utkrāmati | ēvaṃ gacchantaṃ jīvātmānaṃ sūxmaśarīriṇaṃ sukṛtaduṣkṛtē saha pradhāvētām | ēvaṃ pañcabhautika-pralayakrama iti vijñāyatē ||*

#### English Translation
*   **The Individual Dissolution:** Now we shall explain the dissolution of the five elements at death (*athātaḥ pañcabhautikapralayaṃ vyākhyāsyāmaḥ*). When the body perishes due to the exhaustion of past karma (*yadā purākṛtakarmaxayāt dēhō vināśamupayāti*), first, the quality of earth, smell, is swallowed up by water, which is its cause (*prathamaṃ gandhātmanā'vasthitaṃ bhūmiguṇaṃ tatkāraṇabhūtā ēva āpaḥ kavalīkurvanti*).

*   **Loss of Solidity:** When earth is swallowed by water, bodily solidity vanishes (*śārīrakāṭhinyaṃ naśyati*). The body feels dizzy as if spinning on a potter's wheel (*kulālacakrārūḍha-bhramaṇakriyāmanubhavadiva*). As the elements merge, the body is perceived as something external from head to toe (*svaśarīram āpādacūḍaṃ bāhyavanmanyatē*). Then the blood (*kīlālāxyaṃ rudhiraṃ*) dries up.

*   **Water Merges into Fire:** Next, water (blood/fluid) dissolves into its cause, fire (*rudhirātmanā'vasthitaṃ jalaṃ ca svakāraṇē tējasi vilīyatē*). Body moisture is lost, and phlegm (*ślēṣmā*) becomes loose and detached from its seat.

*   **Fire Merges into Wind:** When phlegm and bile (*pitta*) fall into the digestive/internal fire, the fire burns them. Lacking any further fuel, the fire subsides and merges into its cause, the vital wind (*tada'gniḥ svakāraṇabhūtaṃ prāṇādivāyumupaiti*).

*   **Wind Agitates the Body:** Agitated by the heat of fire, the vital wind spreads through all sensory channels (*sarvasrōtōmuxaiḥ*), moving sideways, upwards, and downwards from head to toe (*tiryag-ūrdhvam-adhaśca āpādatalamastakaṃ praviśya*), causing intense pain (*vēdanādibhiḥ*) and delusion (*mōham*), leading to loss of consciousness/memory (*naṣṭasmṛtiḥ*).

*   **The Departure of Breath:** The five vital winds abandon their regular functions and exit as the final exhalation (*ucchvāsam abhiniṣkrāmayati*). This breath breaks through the *suṣumnā* channel, gathers the bodily heat, and exits like air leaving a leather bellows, never to return (*yathā carmabhastrāyāḥ vātō nirgacchati muxaṃ na praviśati tadvat*); this is death.

*   **The Leech Metaphor for the Soul:** The primary breath (*prāṇa*), making a rattling sound (*ghurghuraśabdaṃ*) in the throat and the seven gateways, pulls the individual soul. Just as a leech (*jalūkā*) does not release its previous foothold until it secures a new one (*yathā jalūkā pādāntaram alabhamānā na pūrvapādaṃ muñcati*), so the vital breath carrying the soul does not leave the body until it finds its next destination.

*   **Fruit of Deeds:** As the subtle-bodied soul departs (*gacchantaṃ jīvātmānaṃ sūxmaśarīriṇaṃ*), its merits and demerits run along with it (*sukṛtaduṣkṛtē saha pradhāvētām*).

### Glossary of Key Terms
*   **Vāyu (वायु):** The vital winds or energies governing all physiological and cognitive functions of the body.
*   **Indragōpaka (इन्द्रगोपक):** A bright red velvet insect (cochineal), used as a classical comparison for the red color of *apāna-vāyu*.
*   **Kṣētra-Kṣētrajña (क्षेत्र-क्षेत्रज्ञ):** The field (body) and the knower of the field (the individual soul).
*   **Pañcabhautika-pralaya (पञ्चभौतिक-प्रलय):** The dissolution of the five elements at the micro-cosmic level during the death of an individual.
*   **Jalūkā (जलूका):** A leech. The "leech metaphor" is a classical Upanishadic illustration of the transition of the soul from one body to the next.
*   **Naraka (नरक):** Hellish realms of torment where the unrighteous undergo purification and punishment.
*   **Pitṛyāṇa (पितृयाण):** The path of the ancestors (smoke-path), leading to temporary heavenly realms and followed by rebirth.
*   **Devayāna (देवयाण):** The path of the gods (light-path), leading to absolute liberation in Vaikuntha without rebirth.
*   **Virajā (विरजा):** The boundary river between the material universe (*brahmāṇḍa*) and the spiritual realm (*vaikuṇṭha*), where the departing soul sheds its subtle body.
