# Chapter 28: Consecration — The Rite of the Three Placements (Adhivāsa-traya-vidhiḥ)
### Section 1: Introduction and Purpose of the Placement Rites (Adhivāsanodyogaḥ)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **अथाष्टाविंशः पटलः -**
> **अधिवास-त्रय-विधिः**
> ***athāṣṭāviṃśaḥ paṭalaḥ -***
> ***adhivāsa-traya-vidhiḥ***
> 
> अथातः भगवतोऽधिवासनम् — अधिवासं कृत्त्वैव (कृत्वा एव) स्थापनं करोति। अनधिवासितं बेरं यत्र स्थापितं चेत्, तद् ग्राम-यजमान-स्थापकादीनाम् आशु-विनाशाय भवति।
> *athātaḥ bhagavato'dhivāsanam — adhivāsaṃ kṛttvaiva (kṛtvā eva) sthāpanaṃ karoti. anadhivāsitaṃ beraṃ yatra sthāpitaṃ cet, tad grāma-yajamāna-sthāpakādīnām āśu-vināśāya bhavati.*
> 
> अतः देवालयस्य उत्तरे, पुरस्ताद् वा, प्रपां, कूटं वा कृत्वा, तन्-मध्ये तद्-बिम्ब-अर्ध-अधिकां याम-विस्तारां बिम्ब-दघ्न-निम्नां जल-द्रोणीं भूम्याम् अवटं वा कृत्वा, संशोध्य, वितान-ध्वज-दर्भ-माला-तरङ्ग-स्तम्भ-वेष्टनाद्यैः अलंकृत्य, परितः प्रावरणं कृत्वा।
> *ataḥ devālayasya uttare, purastād vā, prapāṃ, kūṭaṃ vā kṛtvā, tan-madhye tad-bimba-ardha-adhikāṃ yāma-vistārāṃ bimba-daghna-nimnāṃ jala-droṇīṃ bhūmyām avaṭaṃ vā kṛtvā, saṃśodhya, vitāna-dhvaja-darbha-mālā-taraṅga-stambha-veṣṭanādyaiḥ alaṅkṛtya, paritaḥ prāvaraṇaṃ kṛtvā.*

#### English Translation
*   Now, the preliminary placement/residence ritual (*adhivāsana*) of the Lord is explained.

*   **Necessity:** The installation (*sthāpana*) of the image must only be performed after conducting the placement rites (*adhivāsana*).

*   **Consequence of Neglect:** If an un-consecrated (un-placed) image (*anadhivāsita-bera*) is installed, it leads to the swift and complete ruin of the village, the temple patron (*yajamāna*), and the installing priests (*sthāpaka*) alike.

*   **Preparation of the Pavilion:** Therefore, to the north or in front of the temple, one should construct a temporary shed (*prapā*) or a pavilion (*kūṭa*).

*   **The Trough or Pit:** In the middle of this structure, one should place a water-trough (*jala-droṇī*) or dig a pit (*avaṭa*) in the ground. Its length and width must be greater than half the image's height, and its depth must correspond to the height of the image (*bimba-daghna*).

*   **Decoration:** After purifying this trough/pit, it must be adorned with canopies (*vitāna*), banners (*dhvaja*), garlands of darbha-grass (*darbha-mālā*), decorative festoons (*taraṅga*), and pillar-wraps (*stambha-veṣṭana*), and fully enclosed on all sides.

### Section 2: First Placement — Consecration in the Five Cow Products (Pañcagavyādhivāsaḥ)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> पञ्चगव्यानि यथा-योगं संयोज्य, 'वसोः पवित्रम्' (वै॰ म॰ प्र॰ ८) इति पञ्चगव्यैः आपूर्य, अधिदेवम् ईशानम् अभ्यर्च्य, नव वस्त्राणि आस्तीर्य, तद्-प्राच्याम् औपासन-अग्नि-कुण्डं कृत्वा, आघारं हुत्वा;
> *pañcagavyāni yathā-yogaṃ saṃyojya, 'vasoḥ pavitram' (vai. ma. pra. 8) iti pañcagavyaiḥ āpūrya, adhidevam īśānam abhyarcya, nava vastrāṇi āstīrya, tad-prācyām aupāsana-agni-kuṇḍaṃ kṛtvā, āghāraṃ hutvā;*
> 
> तस्य उत्तरे देवेशं सप्तभिः कलशैः संस्नाप्य, पुण्याहं वाचयित्वा, नव-वस्त्र-उत्तरीयाद्यैः अलंकृत्य अभ्यर्च्य, 'विष्णोर्नुकम्' (ऋ॰ सं॰ १-१५४-१) आदीन् जपन्, तिष्ठन् तस्मिन् प्राक्-शिरः शाययित्वा;
> *tasya uttare deveśaṃ saptabhiḥ kalaśaiḥ saṃsnāpya, puṇyāhaṃ vācayitvā, nava-vastra-uttarīyādyaiḥ alaṅkṛtya abhyarcya, 'viṣṇornukam' (ṛ. sa. 1-154-1) ādīn japan, tiṣṭhan tasmin prāk-śiraḥ śāyayitvā;*
> 
> अग्निं परिस्तीर्य, 'विष्णवे, विश्वेशाय, जनार्दनाय, सर्व-भूतात्मने, अव्यक्ताय, अचलाय, देवेभ्यः, भूतेभ्यः, नागेभ्यः, लोक-पालेभ्यः, विमान-पालेभ्यः स्वाहा' इति व्याहृत्यन्तं हुत्वा; वैष्णवं, ब्राह्मं, रौद्रं, प्राजापत्यं, वारुणं, वैघ्नं, गारुडं, वैष्वक्सेनं, दिग्दैवत्यं च हुत्वा, अग्निं विसृज्य, तद्-रात्रौ तद्-आलय-गत-देवेभ्यः सर्वेभ्यः मौद्गिकं बलिं निर्वाप्य।
> *agniṃ paristīrya, 'viṣṇave, viśveśāya, janārdanāya, sarva-bhūtātmane, avyaktāya, acalāya, devebhyaḥ, bhūtebhyaḥ, nāgebhyaḥ, loka-pālebhyah, vimāna-pālebhyah svāhā' iti vyāhṛtyantaṃ hutvā; vaiṣṇavaṃ, brāhmaṃ, raudraṃ, prājāpatyaṃ, vāruṇaṃ, vaighnaṃ, gāruḍaṃ, vaiṣvakṣenaṃ, digdaivatyaṃ ca hutvā, agniṃ visṛjya, tad-rātrau tad-ālaya-gata-devebhyaḥ sarvebhyaḥ maudgikaṃ baliṃ nirvāpya.*

#### English Translation
*   **Preparation of the Fluid:** The priest mixes the five cow products (*pañcagavya*) in their proper proportions, and fills the trough with them while chanting the Vedic mantra beginning with *"vasoḥ pavitram"* (Vaikhānasa Mantra Praśna 8).

*   **Worship & Setup:** He worships Lord Īśāna as the presiding deity (*adhideva*) of the fluid, spreads nine new cloths, prepares the *aupāsana* fire-pit to the east of the trough, and performs the *āghāra* (introductory) oblation.

*   **Bathing the Deity:** To the north of the fire-pit, having bathed the Lord with seven pots of water (*saptabhiḥ kalaśaiḥ*), and performed the *puṇyāha* (purificatory benediction), he adorns the image with new garments and upper-cloths, and worships it.

*   **Laying Down:** While standing and chanting the *Vaiṣṇava* hymns starting with *"viṣṇor nukaṃ vīryāṇi"* (Ṛg Veda 1.154.1), he lays the image down in the trough with its head pointing east (*prāk-śiraḥ*).

*   **Oblations:** Having spread the firewood around the altar (*paristīrya*), he offers oblations with the formulas: *"To Viṣṇu, to the Lord of the Universe (Viśveśa), to Janārdana, to the Soul of all beings (Sarvabhūtātman), to the Unmanifest (Avyakta), to the Unshakeable (Acala), to the gods (Devas), to the elemental spirits (Bhūtas), to the serpents (Nāgas), to the world-guardians (Lokapālas), and to the temple-guardians (Vimānapālas) — svāhā"*, concluding with the sacred utterances (*vyāhṛtis*).

*   **Deity Homams:** He then performs the specific fire offerings: *Vaiṣṇava*, *Brāhma*, *Raudra*, *Prājāpatya*, *Vāruṇa*, *Vaighna* (to Gaṇeśa), *Gāruḍa*, *Vaiṣvaksena* (to Viṣvaksena), and the offerings to the directional deities (*digdaivatya*).

*   **Dismissal & Food Offering:** The fire is then dismissed. That night, the priest must distribute food offerings made of cooked green gram (*maudgika-bali*) to all the deities residing within the temple.

### Section 3: Second Placement — Consecration in Milk (Kṣīrādhivāsaḥ)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> प्रभाते स्नात्वा आलयम् आविश्य, अधिवास-गत-देवम् उद्धृत्य, सप्तभिः कलशैः संस्नाप्य, वस्त्र-उत्तरीयाद्यैः अलंकृत्य अभ्यर्च्य;
> *prabhāte snātvā ālayaṃ āviśya, adhivāsa-gata-devam uddhṛtya, saptabhiḥ kalaśaiḥ saṃsnāpya, vastra-uttarīyādyaiḥ alaṅkṛtya abhyarcya;*
> 
> द्रोणीम् अवटं वा संशोध्य, नव वस्त्राणि आस्तीर्य, 'शन्नो देवीः' (अथ॰ १-१-१) इति गव्येन क्षीरेण आपूर्य, अधिदेवम् अथर्व-वेदम् अभ्यर्च्य, पुण्य-पुष्पाणि निक्षिप्य, 'क्षीरं क्षीरोदधिः' इति स्मृत्वा, तस्मिन् देवं पूर्व-वत् शाययेत्;
> *droṇīm avaṭaṃ vā saṃśodhya, nava vastrāṇi āstīrya, 'śanno devīḥ' (ath. 1-1-1) iti gavyena kṣīreṇa āpūrya, adhidevam atharva-vedam abhyarcya, puṇya-puṣpāṇi nikṣipya, 'kṣīraṃ kṣīrodadhiḥ' iti smṛtvā, tasmin devaṃ pūrva-vat śāyayet;*
> 
> पूर्व-वत् होमं हुत्वा, तद्-रात्रौ पूर्व-वत् बलिं निर्वाप्य; प्रभाते स्नात्वा, 'विष्णोर्नुकम्' आदीन् जप्त्वा (निजप्त्वा), बिम्बम् उद्धृत्य, सप्तभिः कलशैः संस्नाप्य, नव-वस्त्र-उत्तरीयाद्यैः अलंकृत्य अभ्यर्च्य ।
> *pūrva-vat homaṃ hutvā, tad-rātrau pūrva-vat baliṃ nirvāpya; prabhāte snātvā, 'viṣṇornukam' ādīn japtvā, bimbam uddhṛtya, saptabhiḥ kalaśaiḥ saṃsnāpya, nava-vastra-uttarīyādyaiḥ alaṅkṛtya abhyarcya.*

#### English Translation
*   **Morning Transition:** In the morning, after bathing, the priest enters the temple, raises the deity from the *pañcagavya* fluid, bathes Him with seven pots of water, adorns Him with garments and upper-cloths, and worships Him.

*   **Preparing the Milk Bath:** He thoroughly cleanses the trough or pit, spreads new cloths, and fills it with cow's milk while chanting the Vedic mantra *"śan no devīr abhiṣṭaye"* (Atharva Veda 1.1.1).

*   **Milk Consecration:** He worships the Atharva Veda as the presiding deity (*adhideva*) of the milk, sprinkles auspicious flowers, and meditating on the formula *"This milk is the Milk Ocean (kṣīraṃ kṣīrodadhiḥ)"*, lays the deity down in the milk as before.

*   **Homa and Bali:** Having performed the fire offerings (*homa*) as before, and offered the nightly food offerings (*bali*) as before, he bathes in the morning, recites the *"viṣṇor nuka"* and other hymns, raises the image, bathes it with seven pots of water, adorns it with new garments and upper-cloths, and worships it.

### Section 4: Third Placement — Consecration in Water (Jalādhivāsaḥ)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> पुण्यं, देवावास' मिति चतुर्भीर्मन्त्रैर्ध्यान्तमभ्यर्च्य, नववस्त्राण्यास्तीर्य, देवं नववस्त्रोत्तरीयाद्यैरलंकृत्याऽभ्यर्च्य, वैष्णवैर्मन्त्रैः प्राक् शिरःशाययित्वा, अग्निंपरिस्तीर्य, धातादि पञ्चवारुणं मूलहोममाग्नेयं च हुत्वा, ततःपरिषेकं कृत्वा ।
> *नदीं, हृदं, शुद्ध-तटाकं वा आश्रित्य, तद्-जले प्रपां कूटं वा कृत्वा, वितान-ध्वज-दर्भ-माला-धूप-दीपाद्यैः अलंकृत्य, परितः प्रावरणं कृत्वा, तत्-तीरे अग्नि-शालां कृत्वा;*
> 
> *अग्निं निधाय, परिस्तीर्य, परिषिच्य, वैष्णवं हुत्वा; पश्चात् स-घृतं पायसम् आदाय, 'योगेशाय, शेष-पर्यङ्क-शायिने, शब्द-ब्रह्मणे, समुद्रेभ्यः, पर्वतेभ्यः, देवेभ्यः, ऋषिभ्यः, पितृभ्यः, नागेभ्यः, भूतेभ्यः स्वाहा' इति हुत्वा;*
> 
> *तद्-तीर्थम् 'इदम् आपः शिवाः' (वै॰ म॰ प्र॰ ८) इति अभिमृश्य, अधिदेवं वरुणम्, 'पुण्यतीर्थं', 'शिवं', 'पुण्यं', 'देवावासम्' इति चतुर्भिः मन्त्रैः ध्यात्वा अभ्यर्च्य, नव वस्त्राणि आस्तीर्य, देवं नव-वस्त्र-उत्तरीयाद्यैः अलंकृत्य अभ्यर्च्य, वैष्णवैः मन्त्रैः प्राक्-शिरः शाययित्वा;*
> 
> *अग्निं परिस्तीर्य, धातादि-पञ्च-वारुणं, मूल-होमं, आग्नेयं च हुत्वा, ततः परिषेकं कृत्वा।*

### Section 5: Night Rites, Procession, and Return of the Deity (Prasāda-saṃsthāpanam)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> तद्-रात्रौ प्रभूतं बलिम् आहत्य, मुद्ग-निष्पाव-तिल-तिल्व-लाज-माष-अपूप-संयुक्तं कटाहे प्रक्षिप्य, पूर्व-वत् तद्-आलये ग्रामे च प्रदक्षिण-क्रमेण दिग्-देवताभ्यः बलिं निर्वाप्य, रात्रि-शेषं व्यपोह्य;
> *tad-rātrau prabhūtaṃ balim āhatya, mudga-niṣpāva-tila-tilva-lāja-māṣa-apūpa-saṃyuktaṃ kaṭāhe prakṣipya, pūrva-vat tad-ālaye grāme ca pradakṣiṇa-krameṇa dig-devatābhyaḥ baliṃ nirvāpya, rātri-śeṣaṃ vyapohya;*
> 
> प्रभाते स्नात्वा, देवम् उत्थाप्य, शुद्ध-उदकैः संस्नाप्य, गन्ध-उदकैः अभिषिच्य, अग्निं विसृज्य, नव-वस्त्र-उत्तरीयाद्यैः अलंकृत्य अभ्यर्च्य;
> *prabhāte snātvā, devam utthāpya, śuddha-udakaiḥ saṃsnāpya, gandha-udakaiḥ abhiṣicya, agniṃ visṛjya, nava-vastra-uttarīyādyaiḥ alaṅkṛtya abhyarcya;*
> 
> यानम् आरोप्य, ग्रामम् आलयं वा प्रदक्षिणं कारयित्वा (कारयित्वि), आलयम् आविश्य, आलयाभिमुखे (आलयभिमुखे), यज्ञालयस्य पश्चिमे वा, देवं संस्थापयेत् (संस्थाप्येत)।
> *yānam āropya, grāmam ālayaṃ vā pradakṣiṇaṃ kārayitvā, ālayaṃ āviśya, ālayābhimukhe, yajñālayasya paścime vā, devaṃ saṃsthāpayet.*
> 
> एवं त्रयाणाम् अधिवासानां प्रत्येकम् अहोरात्रं दिवा चैकं (एकं) वा अधिवासयेत्।
> *evaṃ trayāṇām adhivāsānāṃ pratyekam ahorātraṃ divā caikaṃ (ekaṃ) vā adhivāsayet.*

#### English Translation
*   **Abundant Bali offering:** That night, the priest prepares a grand offering (*prabhūta-bali*) in a large pan, mixing cooked green gram (*mudga*), flat beans (*niṣpāva*), sesame (*tila*), wild seeds (*tilva*), puffed rice (*lāja*), black gram (*māṣa*), and flour-cakes (*apūpa*).

*   **Distribution:** He distributes this offering to the directional deities around the temple and the village in a clockwise (*pradakṣiṇa*) direction as before.

*   **Morning Ceremonies:** When the remainder of the night has passed, having bathed in the morning, he raises the deity from the water, bathes Him with pure water, and sprinkles Him with perfumed water (*gandhodaka*). The fire is dismissed.

*   **Procession:** Having adorned the deity with new garments and upper-cloths and worshipped Him, he places the image on a vehicle (*yāna*), takes Him in procession around the village or the temple precincts, and enters the temple.

*   **Temporary Placement:** Facing the temple, or to the west of the sacrificial hall (*yajñālaya*), he installs the deity.

*   **Duration:** Each of these three placements (*adhivāsana*) should ideally last for a full day and night (*ahorātra*), or at least for one day.

### Section 6: Abbreviated and Alternative Placement Methods (Saṅkṣepādhivāsaḥ)

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> एवम् असम्भवे — पूर्वेद्युः (पूर्वे अहनि चेत्) पूर्व-वत् पञ्चगव्य-अधिवासं, क्षीर-अधिवासं, जल-अधिवासं च पृथक् एव सङ्कल्प्य, देवं वस्त्र-उत्तरीयाद्यैः अलंकृत्य, अभिषिच्य, प्रत्येकं याम-मात्रम् अधिवासयेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> evam asamhave — pūrvedyuḥ (pūrve ahani cet) pūrva-vat pañcagavya-adhivāsaṃ, kṣīra-adhivāsaṃ, jala-adhivāsaṃ ca pṛthak eva saṅkalpya, devaṃ vastra-uttarīyādyaiḥ alaṅkṛtya, abhiṣicya, pratyekaṃ yāma-mātram adhivāsayet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Abbreviated Method (Previous Day):** If the full duration is not possible, and the ritual is performed on the day prior to consecration: having made separate resolutions (*saṅkalpa*) for the three placements — in the five cow products, milk, and water respectively — and having adorned the deity with garments and upper-cloths, the priest bathes Him and keeps Him in each placement for a duration of one *yāma* (three hours) each.

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> सद्यः चेत् पृथक्-पृथक् मुहूर्तं यथा-लाभ-कालं वा अधिवासयेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> sadyaḥ cet pṛthak-pṛthak muhūrtaṃ yathā-lābha-kālaṃ vā adhivāsayet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Same-Day Method (Sadyaḥ):** If performed on the same day as the consecration, one should keep the deity in each placement for one *muhūrta* (48 minutes) or whatever time is available.

#### Verse / Paragraph 3
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> अलाभे (अलामे) त्रीन् कुम्भान् द्रव्यैः जलैः आपूर्य, पृथक् पृथक् सङ्कल्प्य, अभिषेचयेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> alābhe trīn kumbhān dravyair jalair āpūrya, pṛthak pṛthak saṅkalpya, abhiṣecayet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Emergency Pot-Bathing Method:** If the trough-placements are not possible at all: having filled three pots (*kumbhas*) with the respective substances (the five cow products, milk, and water), and having made separate resolutions (*saṅkalpa*), the priest should bathe the deity.

#### Verse / Paragraph 4
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> एक-होमे सर्वं तन्त्रयित्वा, सकृद् (एकदा) बलिं निर्वाप्य कारयेत् (करयेत्)।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> eka-home sarvaṃ tantrayitvā, sakṛd (ekadā) baliṃ nirvāpya kārayet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Consolidated Fire-Offerings:** In that case, combining all the fire-offerings (*homa*) into a single fire and performing a single food offering (*bali*), he should carry out the rites.

#### Verse / Paragraph 5
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> होमं बलिं च विना सर्वत्र जल-अधिवासनम् एव कारयेत् इति विज्ञायते।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> homaṃ baliṃ ca vinā sarvatra jala-adhivāsanam eva kārayet iti vijñāyate.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Essential Minimum:** It is understood from scriptural tradition that in all cases where the full fire offerings and food offerings are omitted, at least the placement in water (*jalādhivāsa*) alone must be performed.

#### Colophon
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **इति श्रीवैखानसे मरीचिप्रोक्ते विमानार्चनाकल्पे अधिवास-त्रय-विधिः नाम अष्टाविंशः पटलः ॥ २८ ॥**

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **iti śrīvaikhānase marīciprokte vimānārcanākalpe adhivāsa-traya-vidhiḥ nāma aṣṭāviṃśaḥ paṭalaḥ || 28 ||**
