# Chapter 30: Consecration — Characteristics of the Fire-Pits, Setup of Secondary Shrines, and Prāṇāyāma Rites (Agnikuṇḍa-vidhiḥ)
### Section 1: Theological Origin of the Five Sacred Fires (Pañcāgni-sṛṣṭiḥ)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **अथ त्रिंशः पटलः -**
> **अग्निकुण्ड-विधिः**
> ***atha triṃśaḥ paṭalaḥ -***
> ***agnikuṇḍa-vidhiḥ***
> 
> अथ अग्निकुण्ड-लक्षणं व्याख्यास्यामः — ब्रह्मा अग्निं पञ्चधा सृष्ट्वा पञ्च-लोकेषु व्यभजत्।
> *atha agnikuṇḍa-lakṣaṇaṃ vyākhyāsyāmaḥ — brahmā agniṃ pañcadhā sṛṣṭvā pañca-lokeṣu vyabhajat.*
> 
> आहवनीयं स्वर्ग-लोके, अन्वाहार्यम् अन्तरिक्ष-लोके, अस्मिन् लोके (भूलोके) गार्हपत्यम्, आवसथ्यं महर्-लोके, सभ्यं जनो-लोके च।
> *āhavanīyaṃ svarga-loke, anvāhāryam antarikṣa-loke, asmin loke (bhūloke) gārhapatyam, āvasathyaṃ mahar-loke, sabhyaṃ jano-loke ca.*
> 
> इति चतुरश्रः वै स्वर्ग-लोकः — तद्वद् आहवनीयम्; अन्तरिक्ष-लोकः अर्ध-चन्द्राकृतिः — तद्वद् अन्वाहार्यम्; भूमि-मण्डल-आकृतिः (वृत्ताकारः) — तद्वद् गार्हपत्यम्; त्र्यश्रः वै महर्-लोकः — तद्वद् आवसथ्यम्; चतुरश्रः वै जनो-लोकः — तद्वद् सभ्यम्; पुण्डरीक-वत् तपो-लोकः — तद्वत् पौण्डरीकम् (circular/lotus-shaped) कुर्यात्।
> *iti caturaśraḥ vai svarga-lokaḥ — tadvad āhavanīyam; antarikṣa-lokaḥ ardha-candrākṛtiḥ — tadvad anvāhāryam; bhūmi-maṇḍala-ākṛtiḥ (vṛttākāraḥ) — tadvad gārhapatyam; tryaśraḥ vai mahar-lokaḥ — tadvad āvasathyam; caturaśraḥ vai jano-lokaḥ — tadvad sabhyam; puṇḍarīka-vat tapo-lokaḥ — tadvat pauṇḍarīkam kuryāt.*

#### English Translation
*   Now we shall explain the characteristics of the fire-pits (*agnikuṇḍa-lakṣaṇa*).

*   **The Five Cosmic Fires:** Brahmā, having created fire in five aspects (*pañcadhā*), distributed them across the five worlds:
    *   **Āhavanīya:** Assigned to the heaven-world (*Svargaloka*).
    *   **Anvāhārya (Dakṣiṇāgni):** Assigned to the atmosphere-world (*Antarikṣaloka*).
    *   **Gārhapatya:** Assigned to this earthly world (*Bhūloka*).
    *   **Āvasathya:** Assigned to the great world (*Maharloka*).
    *   **Sabhya:** Assigned to the people-world (*Janaloka*).

*   **Pit Geometries and Cosmic Worlds:**
    *   Since the heaven-world is square, the **Āhavanīya** pit must be **square** (*caturaśra*).
    *   Since the atmosphere-world is shaped like a half-moon, the **Anvāhārya** pit must be **semi-circular** (*ardha-candrākṛti*).
    *   Since the earth is circular, the **Gārhapatya** pit must be **circular** (*vṛttākāra/bhūmi-maṇḍalākṛti*).
    *   Since the *Maharloka* is triangular, the **Āvasathya** pit must be **triangular** (*tryaśra*).
    *   Since the *Janaloka* is square, the **Sabhya** pit must be **square** (*caturaśra*).
    *   The **Pauṇḍarīka** pit corresponds to the *Tapoloka* and must be shaped like a **lotus** (*puṇḍarīkavat*).

### Section 2: Layout, Dimensions, and Steps of the Fire-Pits (Kuṇḍa-vibhāgaḥ)

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> यज्ञ-शालां नवधा विभज्य, मध्ये ब्राह्मे शय्या-वेदिम्, ऐन्द्रे पदे सभ्यम् आहवनीयं च;

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> yajña-śālāṃ navadhā vibhajya, madhye brāhme śayyā-vedim, aindre pade sabhyam āhavanīyaṃ ca;

**Translation (English):**
*   **Dividing the Hall:** Dividing the sacrificial hall into a nine-square grid:
    *   **Center (Brāhma):** The bed-platform (*śayyā-vedi*).
    *   **East (Aindra):** The *Sabhya* and *Āhavanīya* fires.

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> आहवनीयं च औपासन-अग्नि-कुण्ड-वत् कुर्यात्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> āhavanīyaṃ ca aupāsana-agni-kuṇḍa-vat kuryāt.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Āhavanīya Fire-Pit:** Constructed like the *aupāsana* fire-pit (square, with two steps).

#### Verse / Paragraph 3
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> याम्ये (दक्षिणे) अन्वाहार्यम् अर्ध-चन्द्राकृतिं — भागम् ऊर्ध्व-वेदि-विस्तारं, दक्षिण-उत्तरं त्रिंशद्-अङ्गुलं, प्राक्-पश्चिमं षष्ट्य्-अङ्गुलं, द्वि-वेदि-सहितम्, एकैकं भाग-विस्तार-उत्सेधं, मध्ये निम्नं त्र्य्-अङ्गुलं (त्रिकोलकं - three angulas/spans);

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> yāmye (dakṣiṇe) anvāhāryam ardha-candrākṛtiṃ — bhāgam ūrdhva-vedi-vistāraṃ, dakṣiṇa-uttaraṃ triṃśad-aṅgulaṃ, prāk-paścimaṃ ṣaṣṭy-aṅgulaṃ, dvi-vedi-sahitam, ekaikaṃ bhāga-vistāra-utsedhaṃ, madhye nimnaṃ try-aṅgulam;

**Translation (English):**
*   **Anvāhārya (Dakṣiṇāgni) Pit:** 
    *   **Shape:** Semi-circular (*ardha-candrākṛti*), located to the south (*yāmye*).
    *   **Dimensions:** 30 angulas from south to north, 60 angulas from east to west.
    *   **Steps:** Equipped with two steps (*dvi-vedi*), each step having a width and height of one portion (*bhāga*).
    *   **Depth:** 3 angulas deep at the center.

#### Verse / Paragraph 4
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> तत्-पश्चिमे गार्हपत्यं पूर्ण-चन्द्राकृतिं (वृत्ताकारं) — ऊर्ध्व-वेदि-विस्तारं सप्त-अधिक-त्रिंशद्-अङ्गुलं, द्वि-वेदि-सहितम्, एकैक-भाग-विस्तार-उत्सेधं, मध्ये निम्नं षड्-अङ्गुलम्;

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> tat-paścime gārhapatyaṃ pūrṇa-candrākṛtiṃ (vṛttākāraṃ) — ūrdhva-vedi-vistāraṃ sapta-adhika-triṃśad-aṅgulaṃ, dvi-vedi-sahitam, ekaika-bhāga-vistāra-utsedhaṃ, madhye nimnaṃ ṣaḍ-aṅgulam;

**Translation (English):**
*   **Gārhapatya Pit:**
    *   **Shape:** Circular (*pūrṇa-candrākṛti*), located to the west of the center.
    *   **Dimensions:** 37 angulas in diameter at the upper step.
    *   **Steps:** Two steps, with one-portion width/height.
    *   **Depth:** 6 angulas deep at the center.

#### Verse / Paragraph 5
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> उत्तरे च आवसथ्यं त्रिकोणं — षड्-यव-अधिक-अष्ट-चत्वारिंशद्-अङ्गुलं तद्-एक-भुज-युत-ऊर्ध्व-वेदिकं, द्वि-वेदि-सहितम्, एकैकं भाग-विस्तार-उत्सेधं, मध्ये निम्नं त्र्य्-अङ्गुलं प्राग्-अग्रं कारयति।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> uttare ca āvasathyaṃ trikoṇaṃ — ṣaḍ-yava-adhika-aṣṭa-catvāriṃśad-aṅgulaṃ tad-eka-bhuja-yuta-ūrdhva-vedikaṃ, dvi-vedi-sahitam, ekaikaṃ bhāga-vistāra-utsedhaṃ, madhye nimnaṃ try-aṅgulaṃ prāg-agraṃ kārayati.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Āvasathya Pit:**
    *   **Shape:** Triangular (*trikoṇa*), located to the north.
    *   **Dimensions:** 48 angulas and 6 barley-grains (*yava*) along one side of the upper step.
    *   **Steps:** Two steps, with one-portion width/height.
    *   **Depth:** 3 angulas deep, pointing east (*prāg-agra*).

#### Verse / Paragraph 6
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> शय्या-वेद्याः प्राच्याम् आहवनीयस्य पश्चिमे सभ्य-अग्नि-कुण्डं — त्रि-वेदि-सहितम्, एकैक-भाग-विस्तार-उत्सेधम्; ऊर्ध्व-वेदि-आयामं द्वात्रिंशद्-अङ्गुलं, मध्य-वेदि-विस्तारं पञ्च-अङ्गुलं, द्विचत्वारिंशद्-अङ्गुलम् अधो-वेदि-आयामं, पञ्चदश-अङ्गुलं (अधोवेदि-विस्तारं); मध्ये निम्नम् अष्टाङ्गुुलं, दशाङ्गुुलं द्वादशाङ्गुुलं वा कारयेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> śayyā-vedyāḥ prācyām āhavanīyasya paścime sabhya-agni-kuṇḍaṃ — tri-vedi-sahitam, ekaika-bhāga-vistāra-utsedham; ūrdhva-vedi-āyāmaṃ dvātriṃśad-aṅgulaṃ, madhya-vedi-vistāraṃ pañca-aṅgulaṃ, dvicatvāriṃśad-aṅgulam adho-vedi-āyāmaṃ, pañcadaśa-aṅgulaṃ (adhovedi-vistāraṃ); madhye nimnam aṣṭāṅgulaṃ, daśāṅgulaṃ dvādaśāṅgulaṃ vā kārayet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Sabhya Pit:**
    *   **Shape:** Square, located east of the bed-platform and west of the *Āhavanīya*.
    *   **Steps:** Three steps (*tri-vedi*), with one-portion width/height.
    *   **Dimensions:** Upper step is 32 angulas long; middle step is 5 angulas wide; lower step is 42 angulas long and 15 angulas wide.
    *   **Depth:** 8, 10, or 12 angulas deep at the center.

#### Verse / Paragraph 7
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> आग्नेय्यां पौण्डरीकं — त्रि-वेदि-सहितं सुवृत्तं, ऊर्ध्व-वेदि-विस्तार-आयामं सप्त-अधिक-त्रिंशद्-अङ्गुलं, एकैक-भाग-विस्तार-उत्सेधम्, अधो-वेदि-विस्तारं षडङ्गुलं, प्राग्-आदि-षोडश-दलैः युतं, मध्ये निम्नम् अष्टाङ्गुुलं दशाङ्गुुलं वा करोति ॥

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> āgneyyāṃ pauṇḍarīkaṃ — tri-vedi-sahitaṃ suvṛttaṃ, ūrdhva-vedi-vistāra-āyāmaṃ sapta-adhika-triṃśad-aṅgulaṃ, ekaika-bhāga-vistāra-utsedham, adho-vedi-vistāraṃ ṣaḍaṅgulaṃ, prāg-ādi-ṣoḍaśa-dalaiḥ yutaṃ, madhye nimnam aṣṭāṅgulaṃ daśāṅgulaṃ vā karoti.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Pauṇḍarīka Pit:**
    *   **Shape:** Circular (*suvṛtta*), located to the south-east (*āgneyyāṃ*).
    *   **Steps & Design:** Three steps. The upper step is 37 angulas in diameter, the lower step is 6 angulas wide. Adorned with sixteen petals (*ṣoḍaśa-dala*) starting from the east.
    *   **Depth:** 8 or 10 angulas deep.

### Section 3: Rites for Bathing, Ornamentation, and Materials (Snān-ārtha-śvabhrādi-karaṇam)

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **स्नानाद्यर्थं श्वभ्रादि-करणम् -**
> ऐशान्यां (उत्तर-पूर्वे) आहवनीय-वत् द्वि-वेदि-सहितं, तद्-वेदि-पीठात् प्रति-दिक् चतुर-अङ्गुल-अधिक-विस्तारं स्नानाय श्वभ्रं (water pit) च, उत्तरे वारि-मार्गं (drain) कारयेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **snānādyarthaṃ śvabhrādi-karaṇam -**
> aiśānyāṃ (uttara-pūrve) āhavanīya-vat dvi-vedi-sahitaṃ, tad-vedi-pīṭhāt prati-dik catura-aṅgula-adhika-vistāraṃ snānāya śvabhraṃ ca, uttare vāri-mārgaṃ kārayet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Bathing Pit (Śvabhra):** In the north-east corner (*aiśānyāṃ*), one should construct a pit for ritual bathing (*snāna-śvabhra*), shaped like the *Āhavanīya* pit (square, with two steps), extending four angulas wider on all sides than its base altar. A drainage channel (*vāri-mārga*) must be directed to the north.

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> नैर्ऋत-पदे (दक्षिण-पश्चिमे) देवस्य अलङ्कार-अर्थं द्वि-हस्त-आयत-विस्तारां, ताल-उत्तुङ्गां (तालोन्नतां) चतुरश्रां वेदिं कृत्वा;

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> nairṛta-pade (dakṣiṇa-paścime) devasya alaṅkāra-arthaṃ dvi-hasta-āyata-vistārāṃ, tāla-uttuṅgāṃ caturaśrāṃ vediṃ kṛtvā;

**Translation (English):**
*   **Ornamentation Altar:** In the south-west corner (*nairṛta-pade*), one should construct a square altar (*vedi*) for the decoration (*alaṅkāra*) of the deity, measuring two cubits in length and width, and one span (*tāla*) in height.

#### Verse / Paragraph 3
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> वायव्ये (उत्तर-पश्चिमे) यागोपकरण-द्रव्य-विन्यास-अर्थं चतुः-हस्त-आयत-विस्तारां, भाग-उत्तुङ्गां (भागोन्नतां) वेदिं कुर्यात् ॥

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> vāyavye (uttara-paścime) yāgopakaraṇa-dravya-vinyāsa-arthaṃ catuḥ-hasta-āyata-vistārāṃ, bhāga-uttuṅgāṃ vediṃ kuryāt.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Material Altar:** In the north-west corner (*vāyavye*), one should construct an altar measuring four cubits in length and width, and one portion (*bhāga*) in height, for arranging the sacrificial implements and materials (*yāgopakaraṇa-dravya-vinyāsa*).

### Section 4: Rites for the Subsidiary Deities (Parivāra-devānāṃ kuṇḍa-karaṇam)

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **परिवार-देवानामपि पृथग्-आलये आहवनीय-कुण्ड-करणम् -**
> सर्वेषां परिवाराणां तत्-तद्-आलयाभिमुखे दक्षिणे वा आहवनीय-अग्निकुण्डं, कुम्भ-पूजनं, हौत्र-शंसनं, मूर्ति-आवाहन-आदिकं च चरेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **parivāra-devānām api pṛthag-ālaye āhavanīya-kuṇḍa-karaṇam -**
> sarveṣāṃ parivārāṇāṃ tat-tad-ālayābhimukhe dakṣiṇe vā āhavanīya-agnikuṇḍaṃ, kumbha-pūjanaṃ, hautra-śaṃsanaṃ, mūrti-āvāhana-ādikaṃ ca caret.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Subsidiary Fire-Pits:** For all the subsidiary deities (*parivāra-devas*), facing their respective shrines or to their south, one should prepare an *Āhavanīya* fire-pit, perform pot-worship (*kumbha-pūjana*), recite the *hotṛ* hymns, and execute the invocation of the deity (*mūrti-āvāhana*).

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> अथवा देवेशस्य मूर्ति-आवाहन-काले परिवार-देवानाम् अध्वर्यवः च तत्-तत्-मन्त्रान् जप्त्वा, तत्-तद्-होमे दक्षिण-प्रणिधौ आवाहन-आदि कृत्वा, तत्-तन्-मन्त्रं वैष्णवं च तत्-तत्-क्रमेण षोडशधा दश-पर्यायतो वा जुहुयुः;

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> athavā deveśasya mūrti-āvāhana-kāle parivāra-devānām adhvaryavaḥ ca tat-tat-mantrān japtvā, tat-tad-home dakṣiṇa-praṇidhau āvāhana-ādi kṛtvā, tat-tan-mantraṃ vaiṣṇavaṃ ca tat-tat-krameṇa ṣoḍaśadhā daśa-paryāyato vā juhuyuḥ;

**Translation (English):**
*   **Alternative Invocation:** Alternatively, during the invocation of the main Lord, the *adhvaryu* priests of the subsidiary deities, having chanted their respective mantras, and having invoked them into the southern vessel (*dakṣiṇa-praṇidhi*) during the respective fire offerings, should offer oblations with their own mantras and the *Vaiṣṇava* mantras, sixteen or ten times in order.

#### Verse / Paragraph 3
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> परिवाराणाम् आलये प्रतिमा-हीनं चेत्, सभ्य-अग्निकुण्डे तत्-तद्-दैवतं स-वैष्णवं च हुत्वा, देवेशस्य आवाहनान्ते तं कुम्भं नीत्वा, तत्-तत्-स्थाने पीठे तत्-तद्-देवं ध्यात्वा आवाहयेत् ॥

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> parivārāṇām ālaye pratimā-hīnaṃ cet, sabhya-agnikuṇḍe tat-tad-daivataṃ sa-vaiṣṇavaṃ ca hutvā, deveśasya āvāhanānte taṃ kumbhaṃ nītvā, tat-tat-sthāne pīṭhe tat-tad-devaṃ dhyātvā āvāhayet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Aniconic Shrines:** In the shrines of subsidiary deities where no physical image (*pratimā-hīna*) is present: having offered oblations to the respective deity along with *Vaiṣṇava* mantras in the *Sabhya* fire-pit, and at the conclusion of the main Lord's invocation, the priest carries the consecrated pot (*kumbha*), meditates on the deity on their respective pedestal, and invokes them.

### Section 5: Layout of the Fire-Pits for the Guardian Deities (Parivārāgni-kuṇḍādikam)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **परिवार-अग्निकुण्डादिकम् -**
> अथवा देवेशस्य अस्य यज्ञस्य पञ्च-अग्नीन्, पौण्डरीक-अग्निं च कृत्वा, यागशालां परितः च एक-भक्ति-युतां (a single boundary wall) प्रपां कृत्वा;
> ***parivāra-agnikuṇḍādikam -***
> *athavā deveśasya asya yajñasya pañca-agnīn, pauṇḍarīka-agniṃ ca kṛtvā, yāgaśālāṃ paritaḥ ca eka-bhakti-yutāṃ prapāṃ kṛtvā;*
>

### Section 7: The Priest's Breathing Exercises (Prāṇāyāma-vidhiḥ)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **प्राणायाम-विधिः -**
> स्थापकः स-व्याहृतिकां गायत्रीं स-शिरस्कां दश-प्रणव-सहितां त्रिः पठेयुः (पठेत्) — आयत-प्राणाः;
> ***prāṇāyāma-vidhiḥ -***
> *sthāpakaḥ sa-vyāhṛtikāṃ gāyatrīṃ sa-śiraskāṃ daśa-praṇava-sahitāṃ triḥ paṭhet — āyata-prāṇāḥ;*
> 
> सहस्र-शतं (११०० वारं) वा संयुक्त-रेचक-पूरक-कुम्भक-प्रकारैः रुद्ध-गतैः (रुद्ध-प्राणैः), एक-द्वि-त्रि-विधं मन्द-मध्यम-उत्तमैः — क्लान्ति-सुषुप्ति-आदि-निर्वेद-क्षुधा-कम्पनाद् अन्यत्र — यथा-विधि श्रद्धया अनालस्यं यथा-बलं प्राणायामं धारयेत् ॥
> *sahasra-śataṃ vā saṃyukta-recaka-pūraka-kumbhaka-prakārair ruddha-gataih, eka-dvi-tri-vidhaṃ manda-madhyama-uttamaiḥ — klānti-suṣupti-ādi-nirveda-kṣudhā-kampanād anyatra — yathā-vidhi śraddhayā anālasyaṃ yathā-balaṃ prāṇāyāmaṃ dhārayet.*

#### English Translation
*   **The Purification Mantra:** The installing priest (*sthāpaka*), holding his breath (*āyata-prāṇaḥ*), should chant three times the Gāyatrī mantra accompanied by the *vyāhṛtis*, the head-mantra (*śiras*), and ten *praṇavas* (Om).

*   **Breathing Technique:** Alternatively, he may perform up to 1,100 rounds using the combined methods of exhalation (*recaka*), inhalation (*pūraka*), and retention (*kumbhaka*) with restricted breath.

*   **Gradation of Breath-Control:** The exercise is practiced in three grades — mild (*manda*), moderate (*madhyama*), and intense (*uttama*).

#### Colophon
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **इति श्रीवैखानसे मरीचिप्रोक्ते विमानार्चनाकल्पे अग्निकुण्ड-विधिः नाम त्रिंशः पटलः ॥ ३० ॥**

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **iti śrīvaikhānase marīciprokte vimānārcanākalpe agnikuṇḍa-vidhiḥ nāma triṃśaḥ paṭalaḥ || 30 ||**

**Translation (English):**
*   **Precautions & Attitude:** Except in conditions of extreme exhaustion (*klānti*), drowsiness (*suṣupti*), depression (*nirveda*), hunger (*kṣudhā*), or trembling (*kampana*), he should steadily practice breath-control (*prāṇāyāma*) according to the rules, with faith, alertness, and to the best of his physical capacity.
