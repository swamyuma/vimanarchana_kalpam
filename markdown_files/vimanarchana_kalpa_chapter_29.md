# Chapter 29: Consecration — Gathering Sacrificial Implements, Utensils, and the Friction-Ignition of the Sacred Fire (Yāgopakaraṇa-dravya-sañcaya-vidhiḥ)
### Section 1: The Fruit of Sacrificial Worship and Construction of the Sacrificial Hall (Devayajana-phalam, Yāgaśālā-nirmāṇaṃ ca)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **अथ एकोनत्रिंशः पटलः -**
> **यागोपकरण-द्रव्य-सञ्चय-विधिः**
> ***atha ekonatriṃśaḥ paṭalaḥ -***
> ***yāgopakaraṇa-dravya-sañcaya-vidhiḥ***
> 
> अथ देवयजनं व्याख्यास्यामः — 'अग्निहोत्रेण लभेत स्वर्गम्' इति आहुः; 'सङ्गत-देव-पूजानां यज्ञाः' इति सूरयो वदन्ति; यज्ञेषु एतेषु देवयागो (विष्णुयागो) विशिष्यते। यजमानस्य अपि भुवि शाश्वतं कीर्तिः तिष्ठति; तस्माद् राज्ञः राष्ट्रस्य यजमान-वंशस्य च अभिवृद्धिं करोति ॥
> *atha devayajanaṃ vyākhyāsyāmaḥ — 'agnihotreṇa labheta svargam' iti āhuḥ; 'saṅgata-deva-pūjānāṃ yajñāḥ' iti sūrayo vadanti; yajñeṣu eteṣu devayāgo (viṣṇuyāgo) viśiṣtyate. yajamānasyāpi bhuvi śāśvataṃ kīrtiḥ tiṣṭhati; tasmād rājñaḥ rāṣṭrasya yajamāna-vaṃśasya ca abhivṛddhiṃ karoti.*
> 
> **यागशाला-निर्माणम् -**
> तस्य अग्निशरणस्य (यज्ञ-शालायाः) विधानं (आधानं) वक्ष्ये — उक्तेषु मास-तिथि-वार-नक्षत्र-लग्नेषु, यद्-दिने कर्तुम् उद्योगः, तद्-दिनात् पूर्वस्मिन् दिने अङ्कुरार्पणं कुर्यात्। आलयात् पुरतः दक्षिणे वा यागशालां षोडश-स्तम्भ-संयुक्तां त्रि-पङ्क्तिं चतुरश्रां कुर्यात् ॥
> ***yāgaśālā-nirmāṇam -***
> *tasya agniśaraṇasya (yajña-śālāyāḥ) vidhānaṃ (ādhānaṃ) vakṣye — ukteṣu māsa-tithi-vāra-nakṣatra-lagneṣu, yad-dine kartum udyogaḥ, tad-dināt pūrvasmin dine aṅkurārpaṇaṃ kuryāt. ālayāt purataḥ dakṣiṇe vā yāgaśālāṃ ṣoḍaśa-stambha-saṃyuktāṃ tri-paṅktiṃ caturaśrāṃ kuryāt.*

#### English Translation
*   Now we shall explain the worship of the gods (*devayajana*).

*   **Fruit of Devayajana:** They say: *"By the daily fire-sacrifice (agnihotra), one attains heaven."* The wise (*sūri*) say: *"Sacrifice is the assembly of all worship of the gods."* Among all these sacrifices, the worship of the Lord (*devayāga/viṣṇuyāga*) is the most excellent. It ensures that the fame of the patron (*yajamāna*) remains eternal on earth; and it brings about the prosperity of the king, the nation, and the lineage of the patron.

*   **Setting up the Pavilion:** We shall describe the arrangement of that sacrificial fire-hall (*agni-śālā/agniśaraṇa*). Under the auspicious month, tithi, weekday, nakshatra, and lagna described before, on the day before the proposed ritual day, one should perform the sprouting rite (*aṅkurārpaṇa*).

*   **Location & Form:** In front of or to the south of the temple, one should construct a square sacrificial hall (*yāga-śālā*) equipped with sixteen pillars, organized in three rows/aisles (*tri-paṅkti*).

### Section 2: The Bed-Platform and Canopy Shed (Śayyā-vediḥ, Prapādiś ca)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **शय्या-वेदिः प्रपा च -**
> तद्-विमान-निर्देश-हस्तेन त्रि-हस्त-आदि-पञ्च-हस्तान्तं पाद-हस्त-विवृद्ध्या नवधा भित्ति-मानं; तस्य स्थले तालं द्वि-भागं, तद्-अर्धं, पाद-उत्तुङ्गं वा; भित्ति-मान-समं, पादाधिकं, अर्धाधिकं, पादोन-द्विगुणं, द्विगुणं वा शय्या-वेदि-विस्तारं; भित्ति-मान-समं, पादोनम् अर्धं, बिम्ब-अर्ध-अधिकं, त्रिपाद-अधिक-द्विगुणं, अर्ध-अधिक-द्विगुणं, त्रिगुणं वा; तत्-चतुर्थ-अंशम् उत्सेधं; तद्-ऊर्ध्वे कूटं वर्ष-निवारं कृत्वा;
> ***śayyā-vediḥ prapā ca -***
> *tad-vimāna-nirdeśa-hastena tri-hasta-ādi-pañca-hastāntaṃ pāda-hasta-vivṛddhyā navadhā bhitti-mānaṃ; tasya sthale tālaṃ dvi-bhāgaṃ, tad-ardhaṃ, pāda-uttuṅgaṃ vā; bhitti-māna-samaṃ, pādādhikaṃ, ardhādhikaṃ, pādona-dviguṇaṃ, dviguṇaṃ vā śayyā-vedi-vistāraṃ; bhitti-māna-samaṃ, pādōnam ardhaṃ, bimba-ardha-adhikaṃ, tripāda-adhika-dviguṇaṃ, ardha-adhika-dviguṇaṃ, triguṇaṃ vā; tat-caturtha-aṃśam utsedhaṃ; tad-ūrdhve kūṭaṃ varṣa-nivāraṃ kṛtvā;*
> 
> तत्-परितः प्रपां चतुः-द्वार-समायुक्तां चतुः-तोरण-भूषितां कृत्वा, तत् सर्वं वितान-ध्वज-दर्भ-माला-तरङ्ग-स-स्तम्भ-वेष्टन-मुक्ता-दामाद्यैः अलंकृत्य, दर्भ-माला-रज्जुं पर्वणि पर्वणि द्वि-ताल-लम्ब-दर्भ-द्वय-युक्तां;
> *tat-paritaḥ prapāṃ catuḥ-dvāra-samāyuktāṃ catuḥ-toraṇa-bhūṣitāṃ kṛtvā, tat sarvaṃ vitāna-dhvaja-darbha-mālā-taraṅga-sa-stambha-veṣṭana-muktā-dāmādyaiḥ alaṅkṛtya, darbha-mālā-rajjuṃ parvaṇi parvaṇi dvi-tāla-lamba-darbha-dvaya-yuktāṃ;*
> 
> मध्ये अरत्नि-मात्र-त्रिशूल-युतानि तोरणानि कारयित्वा — 'अग्निमीळे' (ऋ॰ सं॰ १-१-१) इति प्राग्-द्वारे अश्वत्थम्; 'इषे त्वोर्जे त्वा' (य॰ वे॰) इति दक्षिणे औदुम्बरं; 'अग्न आयाहि' (सा॰ वे॰) इति पश्चिमे प्लक्षं; 'शन्नो देवीः' (अथ॰ १-१-१) इति उत्तरे वटं च एव द्वारेषु संस्थाप्य; तत्-द्वार-पार्श्वे कदली-क्रमुक-उदकुम्भ-दीप-अङ्कुराद्यैः अलंकृत्य, तन्-मध्ये शय्या-वेदिं परितः च अग्नि-कुण्डानि कारयेत् ॥
> *madhye aratni-mātra-triśūla-yutāni toraṇāni kārayitvā — 'agnimīḷe' (ṛ. sa. 1-1-1) iti prāg-dvāre aśvattham; 'iṣe tvorje tvā' (ya. ve.) iti dakṣiṇe audumbaraṃ; 'agna āyāhi' (sā. ve.) iti paścime plakṣaṃ; 'śanno devīḥ' (ath. 1-1-1) iti uttare vaṭaṃ ca eva dvāreṣu saṃsthāpya; tat-dvāra-pārśve kadalī-kramuka-udakumbha-dīpa-aṅkurādyaiḥ alaṅkṛtya, tan-madhye śayyā-vediṃ paritaḥ ca agni-kuṇḍāni kārayet.*

#### English Translation
*   **Measuring Standards:** The bed-platform (*śayyā-vedi*) is measured using the same cubit (*hasta*) defined for the temple building (*vimāna-hasta*).

*   **Dimensions:** It ranges from three cubits to five cubits, increasing by quarter-cubits (*pāda-hasta*), providing nine optional sizes. On its designated site, its height should be two spans (*tālas*), one span, half a span, or a quarter-span, equal to the foundation-wall height, or higher by a quarter, a half, three-quarters, or double, or equal to the foundation-wall.

*   **Width & Height:** Its width should be equal to the foundation-wall, or less by a quarter, or half, or greater by half the image's height, or three-quarters, or double, or double and a half, or triple, and its height should be one-fourth of its width.

*   **Pavilion & Enclosure:** Above it, one should construct a pavilion (*kūṭa*) to keep out the rain. Around it, one should erect a canopy-shed (*prapā*) equipped with four doors and adorned with four arched gateways (*toraṇas*).

*   **Decorations:** One should decorate all of it with canopies, banners, darbha-grass garlands, festoons, pillar-coverings, and pearl-strings. The darbha-grass garlands should have two-span-long double darbha blades hanging at every joint. In the middle of the gateways, one should place arches equipped with a one-cubit (*aratni*) trident (*triśūla*).

*   **Sacred Portal Trees:** At the doors, one should place branches of the sacred trees as follows:
    *   **East door:** Aśvattha (sacred fig), chanting *"agnimīḷe"* (Ṛg Veda 1.1.1).
    *   **South door:** Udumbara (cluster fig), chanting *"iṣe tvorje tvā"*.
    *   **West door:** Plakṣa (white fig), chanting *"agna āyāhi"*.
    *   **North door:** Vaṭa (banyan), chanting *"śan no devīḥ"*.

*   **Portal Offerings:** At the sides of the doors, one should decorate with banana trees, areca-nut trees, water-pots, lamps, and sprouts. In the middle, one should place the bed-platform (*śayyā-vedi*), and construct the fire-pits (*agni-kuṇḍas*) around it.

### Section 3: Measurements of the Churning Woods (Araṇy-ādi-mānam)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **अरणि-आदि-मानम् -**
> अरणि-अर्थम् अश्वत्थ-शमी-जातम् (शमी-वृक्षे उत्पन्नम् अश्वत्थं) अन्यं वा सङ्गृह्य, द्वि-ताल-आयामां षोडश-अङ्गुलां (षड्-अङ्गुलां) वा विस्तृतां भाग-उत्सेधाम् अरणिं (अधर-अरणिं), तद्-समां च ऊर्ध्व-फलकां (उत्तर-अरणिं);
> ***araṇi-ādi-mānam -***
> *araṇi-artham aśvattha-śamī-jātam (śamī-vṛkṣe utpannam aśvatthaṃ) anyaṃ vā saṅgṛhya, dvi-tāla-āyāmāṃ ṣoḍaśa-aṅgulāṃ (ṣaḍ-aṅgulāṃ) vā vistṛtāṃ bhāga-utsedhām araṇiṃ (adhara-araṇiṃ), tad-samāṃ ca ūrdhva-phalakāṃ (uttara-araṇiṃ);*
> 
> द्वि-ताल-आयामां प्रादेश-परिणाहं मूल-अग्रयोः एक-अङ्गुल-मात्र-शिखा-युक्तं मन्थ-दण्डं च कृत्वा, मौर्व्या त्रिवृतां रज्जुं मन्थनाय समाहरेत्;
> *dvi-tāla-āyāmāṃ prādeśa-pariṇāhaṃ mūla-agrayoḥ eka-aṅgula-mātra-śikhā-yuktaṃ mantha-daṇḍaṃ ca kṛtvā, maurvyā trivṛtāṃ rajjuṃ manthanāya samāharet;*
> 
> खदिर-अश्वत्थ-बिल्वाद्यैः यज्ञ-वृक्षैः यज्ञ-पात्राणि कुर्यात् ।
> *khadira-aśvattha-bilvādyaiḥ yajña-vṛkṣaiḥ yajña-pātrāṇi kuryāt.*

#### English Translation
*   **Wood Selection:** For the purpose of friction-ignition (*araṇi*), one should gather wood from an Aśvattha tree growing on a Śamī tree, or any other suitable sacrificial wood.

*   **The Lower Board:** One should prepare the lower churning-board (*adharāraṇi*) of two spans (*tālas*) in length, sixteen (or six) angulas in width, and one portion (*bhāga*) in thickness.

*   **The Upper Board:** The upper board (*uttarāraṇi/ūrdhva-phalakā*) should be of equal size.

*   **Churning Rod & Cord:** The churning rod (*mantha-daṇḍa*) should be two spans long, one span (*prādeśa*) in circumference, and equipped with a pin (*śikhā*) of one angula at both the top and bottom. A three-fold rope made of *mūrvā* grass (*maurvī-rajju*) should be obtained for churning.

*   **Vessels:** The sacrificial vessels (*yajña-pātras*) should be made from the wood of sacrificial trees like Khadira (*acacia*), Aśvattha, Bilva (*wood apple*), etc.

### Section 4: Forms of the Sacrificial Ladles (Sruv-ādi-svarūpam)

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **स्रुव-स्वरूपम् -**
> स्रुवस्य आयामं द्वि-प्रादेशं, मूलं प्रादेश-परिणाहं, तद्-अर्धम् अग्र-नाहं, मूल-अग्रयोः एक-अङ्गुल-मात्र-शिखा-युक्तं, बिलाद् अर्वाक् आनुपूर्व्येण सङ्क्षिप्तं (संक्षिप्रम्), अग्रे प्रादेश-परिमण्डलं, गोलकं बिल-विस्तारं, माष-आच्छादन-गर्त-द्वय-युतम् एवम् स्रुवं कारयेत् ।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **sruva-svarūpam -**
> sruvasya āyāmaṃ dvi-prādeśaṃ, mūlaṃ prādeśa-pariṇāhaṃ, tad-ardham agra-nāhaṃ, mūla-agrayoḥ eka-aṅgula-mātra-śikhā-yuktaṃ, bilād arvāk ānupūrvyeṇa saṅkṣiptaṃ, agre prādeśa-parimaṇḍalaṃ, golakaṃ bila-vistāraṃ, māṣa-ācchādana-garta-dvaya-yutam evaṃ sruvaṃ kārayet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **The Sruva Ladle:** The *sruva* (small wooden ladle) should be made as follows:
    *   **Length:** Two spans (*prādeśas*).
    *   **Circumference:** The base circumference should be one span, and the front circumference should be half of that.
    *   **Pin:** The base and tip should have a pin (*śikhā*) of one angula.
    *   **Form:** It should taper (*saṅkṣipta*) gradually from the bowl (*bila*) backwards. The front portion should be a span in circumference, the bowl-cavity (*golaka*) should be equal to the width of the bowl, and it should have two depressions (grooves) of a black-gram width (*māṣa-ācchādana-garta*) for overflow.

### Section 5: The Ghee-Pouring Spoon and Other Ladles (Agnihotra-havaṇī-juhū-upajuhū-darvī-mānam)

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **अग्निहोत्रहवणी-जुहू-उपजुहू-दर्वी-मानम् -**
> **अग्निहोत्र-हवणी:** षड्विंशति-अङ्गुलायामा; अधस्तात् षोडश-अङ्गुल-दण्डः द्वादश-अङ्गुल-परिणाहः; तद्-ऊर्ध्वं पञ्च-अङ्गुलायामं वृत्तं मध्ये-निम्न-भागं, परितः भित्तिः एकाङ्गुला; तस्माद् अग्र-अन्तं घृत-धारा-युतम् एक-अङ्गुल-विस्तारं त्र्यश्रम् (triangular); अग्राद् अधस्तात् पञ्च-अङ्गुलम् आनुपूर्व्येण सङ्क्षिप्तं; घृत-धारा-पार्श्वयोः एक-अङ्गुलं, भित्तिः अर्धाङ्गुला, उपरि छत्र-आकारं घन-भागम् एवं कारयेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **agnihotrahavaṇī-juhū-upajuhū-darvī-mānam -**
> **agnihotra-havaṇī:** ṣaḍviṃśati-aṅgulāyāmā; adhastāt ṣoḍaśa-aṅgula-daṇḍaḥ dvādaśa-aṅgula-pariṇāhaḥ; tad-ūrdhvaṃ pañca-aṅgulāyāmaṃ vṛttaṃ madhye-nimna-bhāgaṃ, paritaḥ bhittiḥ ekāṅgulā; tasmād agra-antaṃ ghṛta-dhārā-yutam eka-aṅgula-vistāraṃ tryaśram; agrād adhastāt pañca-aṅgulam ānupūrvyeṇa saṅkṣiptaṃ; ghṛta-dhārā-pārśvayoḥ eka-aṅgulaṃ, bhittiḥ ardhāṅgulā, upari chatra-ākāraṃ ghana-bhāgam evaṃ kārayet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Agnihotra-havaṇī (Spoon):** It should be twenty-six angulas long. The lower handle should be sixteen angulas long, with a circumference of twelve angulas. Above it, the bowl should be five angulas long, circular, and hollow in the middle, with a rim-wall of one angula. From the bowl to the tip, there should be a triangular spout (*tryaśra*) of one angula in width for pouring the ghee. Below the tip, it should taper (*saṅkṣipta*) gradually for five angulas. The sides of the ghee-spout should be one angula, the rim-wall should be half an angula, and the top should be shaped like an umbrella (*chatra-ākāra*) with a solid section.

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **जुहूः:** जुहू-प्रमाणं द्वि-ताल-आयामां (द्वि-प्रादेशं), द्वादश-अङ्गुल-परिणाहं; मूलं पद्म-मुकुलाकारम्, अग्रे च अष्टाङ्गुलायामं, चतुरङ्गल-विस्तारं, द्वादश-अङ्गुलोन्नतं (षड्-अङ्गुलोन्नतं) वा; मध्ये निम्नम् अर्ध-अङ्गुलं, भित्तिः एकाङ्गुला, घृत-धारा-युतम्, ऊर्ध्व-मुकुलाकारम्, अधस्ताद् दण्ड-आकारं कारयेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **juhūḥ:** juhū-pramāṇaṃ dvi-tāla-āyāmāṃ, dvādaśa-aṅgula-pariṇāhaṃ; mūlaṃ padma-mukulākāram, agre ca aṣṭāṅgulāyāmaṃ, caturaṅgula-vistāraṃ, dvādaśa-aṅgulonnataṃ (ṣaḍ-aṅgulonnataṃ) vā; madhye nimnam ardha-aṅgulaṃ, bhittiḥ ekāṅgulā, ghṛta-dhārā-yutam, ūrdhva-mukulākāram, adhastād daṇḍa-ākāraṃ kārayet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Juhū (Sacrificial Ladle):** The *juhū* ladle should be two spans (*tālas*) long, with a base circumference of twelve angulas, shaped like a lotus bud (*padma-mukulākāra*). The front should be eight angulas long, four angulas wide, and twelve (or six) angulas high. The middle cavity should be half an angula deep, with a rim-wall of one angula, equipped with a channel for the ghee-stream, shaped like an open bud at the top, and handle-shaped below.

#### Verse / Paragraph 3
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **उपजुहूः:** उपजुहू-प्रमाणं द्वि-ताल-आयामां, द्वादश-अङ्गुल-परिणाहं; अग्रे अष्टाङ्गुलायामां, समवृत्तां, भित्तिः अर्धाङ्गुला, घृत-धारा-युतम् एवं कारयेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**

### Section 7: The Friction-Ignition of the Sacred Fire (Agni-manthanam)

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **अग्नि-मन्थन-विधिः -**
> अथ अग्निमन्थनार्थं यज्ञ-अगारे च आग्नेय्यां गोमयेन उपलिप्य, पञ्च-वर्णैः अलंकृत्य, मण्डलं कृत्वा, दर्भान् आस्तीर्य, तत्र अरणिं प्राङ्-मुखं सन्यस्य;

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **agni-manthana-vidhiḥ -**
> atha agnimanthanārthaṃ yajña-agāre ca āgneyyāṃ gomayena upalipya, pañca-varṇair alaṅkṛtya, maṇḍalaṃ kṛtvā, darbhān āstīrya, tatra araṇiṃ prāṅ-mukhaṃ sanyasya;

**Translation (English):**
*   **Ignition Site Preparation:** For friction-ignition (*agni-manthana*), having plastered the south-eastern corner (*āgneyī*) of the sacrificial hall with cow-dung, decorated it with five-coloured powders, drawn a mandala, and spread darbha-grass, the priest lays the lower churning board (*araṇi*) facing east.

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> अरण्यां महीं, मन्थे विष्णुम्, उत्तर-पट्टिकायाम् अग्निम् अभ्यर्च्य;

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> araṇyāṃ mahīṃ, manthe viṣṇum, uttara-paṭṭikāyām agnim abhyarcya;

**Translation (English):**
*   **Invocation in Implements:** He worships the Earth-goddess (*Mahī*) in the lower board, Lord Viṣṇu in the churning rod (*mantha*), and Agni in the upper block (*uttara-paṭṭikā*).

#### Verse / Paragraph 3
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> आचार्यः प्राङ्-मुखः 'अग्न आयाहि' (सा॰ वे॰) इति अरणिं सन्यस्य, 'आयुर्दाः' (वै॰ म॰ प्र॰ ८) इति मन्थ-दण्डं संस्थाप्य, 'अग्निं दूतं' (ऋ॰ सं॰ १-१२-१) इति मन्थे रज्जुं समावेष्ट्य, 'अग्निर्मूर्धा' (ऋ॰ सं॰ ८-४४-१६) इति उत्तर-पट्टिकां संयोज्य, 'जातवेदो भुवनस्य' (वै॰ म॰ प्र॰ ८) इति अग्निं मथित्वा;

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> ācāryaḥ prāṅ-mukhaḥ 'agna āyāhi' (sā. ve.) iti araṇiṃ sanyasya, 'āyurdāḥ' (vai. ma. pra. 8) iti mantha-daṇḍaṃ saṃsthāpya, 'agniṃ dūtaṃ' (ṛ. sa. 1-12-1) iti manthe rajjuṃ samāveṣṭya, 'agnirmūrdhā' (ṛ. sa. 8-44-16) iti uttara-paṭṭikāṃ saṃyojya, 'jātavedo bhuvanasya' (vai. ma. pra. 8) iti agniṃ mathitvā;

**Translation (English):**
*   **The Churning Rites:** The priest, facing east, places the board chanting *"agna āyāhi"*. He installs the churning rod chanting *"āyurdāḥ"*. He wraps the cord around the rod chanting *"agniṃ dūtaṃ"*. He joins the upper block chanting *"agnir mūrdhā"*. Churning the wood while chanting *"jātavedo bhuvanasya"*, he generates the fire.

#### Verse / Paragraph 4
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> उत्पन्नम् अग्निम् 'आयुर्दाः' इति प्रणम्य, 'उपावरोह' (वै॰ म॰ प्र॰ ८) इति गो-करीषैः समुज्वाल्य, 'घृतप्रतीकः' (वै॰ म॰ प्र॰ ८) इति पात्रिकायाम् आदाय, 'अग्निरिन्द्रः' इति अभिवादनं कृत्वा, अप्रमादं निधाय;

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> utpannam agnim 'āyurdāḥ' iti praṇamya, 'upāvaroha' (vai. ma. pra. 8) iti go-karīṣaiḥ samujjvālya, 'ghṛtapratīkaḥ' (vai. ma. pra. 8) iti pātrikāyām ādāya, 'agnirindraḥ' iti abhivādanaṃ kṛtvā, apramādaṃ nidhāya;

**Translation (English):**
*   **Kindling the Flame:** He bows to the newly generated fire chanting *"āyurdāḥ"*, and ignites it using dry cow-dung cakes (*gokarīṣa*) chanting *"upāvaroha"*. Taking it in a small vessel chanting *"ghṛtapratīkaḥ"*, he salutes it chanting *"agnir indraḥ"*, and places it carefully.

#### Verse / Paragraph 5
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> स्थापन-दिवसात् पूर्व-रात्रे आलयाद् उत्तरे च औपासन-अग्नि-कुण्डं च कृत्वा, आघारं हुत्वा, सूत्र-उक्त-विधिना वास्तु-होमं हुत्वा, आघार-पर्यग्निकरणं कृत्वा, पञ्चगव्यैः 'अणोरणीयान्' (तै॰ आ॰ १०-१०) इति अभ्युक्ष्य (इत्यभ्युक्ष्य) पुण्याहं वाचयेत् ।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> sthāpana-divasāt pūrva-rātre ālayād uttare ca aupāsana-agni-kuṇḍaṃ ca kṛtvā, āghāraṃ hutvā, sūtra-ukta-vidhinā vāstu-homaṃ hutvā, āghāra-paryagnikaraṇaṃ kṛtvā, pañcagavyaiḥ 'aṇoraṇīyān' (tai. ā. 10-10) ity abhyukṣya puṇyāhaṃ vācayet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Sacrificial Hall Consecration:** On the night before the installation day, having prepared the *aupāsana* fire-pit to the north of the temple, he performs the *āghāra* oblation, conducts the site-consecration fire (*vāstu-homa*) according to the rules of the manual, performs the *āghāra-paryagnikaraṇa*, sprinkles it with the five cow products chanting *"aṇor aṇīyān"*, and recites the *puṇyāha* (purificatory benediction).
