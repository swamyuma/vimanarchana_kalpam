# Vimanarchana Kalpa of Sage Marīci (Vaikhānasa Āgama)
## Chapter 85: Tattvajñānopadeśa-Niṣkala-vidhiḥ (Rules for the Instruction of Truth and the Unmanifest Form of Brahman)

This document contains the Sanskrit text, IAST transliteration, and English translation of the 85th Chapter (*Pañcāśītitemaḥ Paṭalaḥ*) of the **Vimānārcanākalpa** (attributed to Sage Marīci, representing the Vaikhānasa school of Vaishnavism).

This chapter introduces the science of metaphysical truth (*tattvajñāna*), responding to the request of the assembly of sages to Sage Marīci. It defines the word *tattva* (the essential nature of the Supreme Lord Nārāyaṇa) and describes the *niṣkala* (unmanifest, unattributed, and transcendent) aspect of the Supreme Brahman, drawing heavily on Vedic Upanishadic statements (such as *ākaśāśarīraṃ brahma*, *aṇor aṇīyān*, etc.) to depict His omnipresence and realization within the heart.

---

### Section 1: Introduction to Metaphysical Truth (Tattvajñāna)

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **अथ पञ्चाशीतितमः पटलः**
> **अथ तत्त्वज्ञानोपदेश-विधिः**
> **तत्त्वोपदेशोपयुक्तोपोद्घातः**

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **atha pañcāśītitamaḥ paṭalaḥ**
> **atha tattvajñānopadeśa-vidhiḥ**
> **tattvopadeśopayuktopodghātaḥ**

**Translation (English):**
*   **The Sages' Query:** Then, the assembly of sages bowed to Sage Marīci and requested (*munayaḥ praṇamya ayācanta*): "Of the four paths consisting of conduct, ritual action, knowledge, and yoga (*carita-kriyā-jñāna-yogeṣu caturṣu*), the path of conduct and action was previously declared in detail by Lord Vikhanas in the Sutra (*bhagavatā vikhanasā sūtre vistareṇa praṇītam*). That was declared in brief by other sages, but the practical path of action has been detailed by you through various ritual applications (*kriyāmārgastvayā praṇītaḥ*). O Lord! We now desire to hear the yoga of metaphysical knowledge of truth (*tattvajñānayogaṃ ca śrotum icchāmaḥ*)."

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> अथातो मरीचिं मुनयः प्रणम्य — ‘चरित-क्रिया-ज्ञान-योगेषु चतुर्षु, पूर्वम् एषु चरितं (or क्रिया-मार्गः) भगवता विखनसा सूत्रे विस्तरेण प्रणीतम्; तद् अन्यैः सङ्क्षेपेणोक्तं, तदुपन्यासो बहुप्रयोग-क्रमेण क्रियामार्गस्त्वया प्रणीतः। भगवन्! तत्त्वज्ञानयोगं च श्रोतुद् इच्छामः’ इति अयाचन्त। तान् समीक्ष्य, ‘श्रूयताम्’ इति मरीचिरुवाच —

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> athāto marīciṃ munayaḥ praṇamya — ‘carita-kriyā-jñāna-yogeṣu caturṣu, pūrvam eṣu caritaṃ (or kriyā-mārgaḥ) bhagavatā vikhanasā sūtre vistareṇa praṇītam; tad anyaiḥ saṅxapeṇoktaṃ, tadupanyāso bahuprayoga-krameṇa kriyāmārgastvayā praṇītaḥ | bhagavan! tattvajñānayogaṃ ca śrotum icchāmaḥ’ iti ayācanta | tān samīxya, ‘śrūyatām’ iti marīciruvāca —

**Translation (English):**
*   **Marīci's Reply:** Looking at them, Marīci replied, "Listen!" (*tān samīxya ‘śrūyatām’ iti marīciruvāca*). I shall declare the rules for instruction of the science of truth (*tattvajñānopadeśa-vidhiṃ vaxye*).

#### Verse / Paragraph 3
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **तत्त्वज्ञानोपदेश-विधिं वक्ष्ये —**
> **तत्त्वपद-निर्वचनम्, ब्रह्मणः ज्ञेयत्वं च**
> **तस्य सकलत्व-निष्कलत्वे**

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **tattvajñānopadeśa-vidhiṃ vaxye —**
> **tattvapada-nirvacanam, brahmaṇaḥ jñeyatvaṃ ca**
> **tasya sakalatva-niṣkalatve**

**Translation (English):**
*   **Definition of Tattva:** The state of "That" (*tasya bhāvaḥ*) is called Truth (*tattva*); it refers to the essential nature of the Supreme Brahman, the Supreme Soul Nārāyaṇa (*parabrahmaṇaḥ paramātmano nārāyaṇasya bhāvaḥ*). The Śruti declares: "Nārāyaṇa is the Supreme Brahman, Nārāyaṇa is the Supreme Truth" (*‘nārāyaṇaḥ paraṃ brahma tattvaṃ nārāyaṇaḥ paraḥ’*).

#### Verse / Paragraph 4
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> तस्य भावः तत्त्वम् इति, तस्य परब्रह्मणः परमात्मनो नारायणस्य भावः। ‘नारायणः परं ब्रह्म तत्त्वं नारायणः परः’ इति श्रुतिः। तदवगमनं ज्ञानं, तद्विदः ब्रह्मविदः। तस्मात् परमात्मा ज्ञेयः, जीवात्मा ज्ञाता, श्रुतयो ज्ञानम् इति ब्रह्मवादिनो वदन्ति। तद् ब्रह्मणो निष्कलः सकलश्च स्वभावः ॥

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> tasya bhāvaḥ tattvam iti, tasya parabrahmaṇaḥ paramātmano nārāyaṇasya bhāvaḥ | ‘nārāyaṇaḥ paraṃ brahma tattvaṃ nārāyaṇaḥ paraḥ’ iti śrutiḥ | tadavagamanaṃ jñānaṃ, tadvidaḥ brahmavidaḥ | tasmāt paramātmā jñeyaḥ, jīvātmā jñātā, śrutayo jñānam iti brahmavādino vadanti | tad brahmaṇo niṣkalaḥ sakalaśca svabhāvaḥ ||

**Translation (English):**
*   **Knower, Known, and Knowledge:** The understanding of that Truth is knowledge (*jñānam*), and those who realize it are the knowers of Brahman (*brahmavidaḥ*). Therefore, the Supreme Soul is the object of knowledge (*paramātmā jñeyaḥ*), the individual soul is the knower (*jīvātmā jñātā*), and the Vedic Śrutis are the knowledge (*śrutayo jñānam*) — so declare the expounders of Brahman. That Brahman possesses both an unmanifest (*niṣkala*) and a manifest (*sakala*) nature.

### Section 2: The Unmanifest Form of Brahman (Niṣkala-Svarūpa)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **निष्कल-स्वरूपम्**
> निष्कलः — परमात्मनः अन्यत् न किञ्चिद् अस्ति इति; क्षीरे सर्पिः, तिले तैलं, पुष्पे गन्धः, फले रसः, काष्ठे अग्निः इव, अन्तर्बहिश्च तत् सर्वं व्याप्य, आकाशोपमः; ‘अन्तर्बहिश्च तत् सर्वं व्याप्य नारायणः स्थितः’ (Mahanarayana Upanishad 13.5) इति। ‘आकाशशरीरं ब्रह्म’ (Taittiriya Upanishad 1.6) इति। अशरीरः शरीरेषु व्याप्य तिष्ठति; विश्व-व्यापकत्वाद् विष्णुः, ध्येयः, अत्यच्छः, अमलः, नित्यः, अचिन्त्यः, अप्रमेयः, निर्गुणः, निश्चलः, निरवयवः, निरञ्जनः, निर्विकल्पः, निर्द्वन्द्वः, अनिर्वचनीयः, अतीन्द्रियः, सुसूक्ष्मत्वाद् अनिर्देश्यः, सदसद् इति ब्रह्मवादिनो वदन्ति।
> ***niṣkala-svarūpam***
> *niṣkalaḥ — paramātmanaḥ anyat na kiñcid asti iti; kṣīre sarpiḥ, tile tailaṃ, puṣpe gandhaḥ, phale rasaḥ, kāṣṭhe agniḥ iva, antarbahisca tat sarvaṃ vyāpya, ākāśopamaḥ; ‘antarbahisca tat sarvaṃ vyāpya nārāyaṇaḥ sthitaḥ’ iti | ‘ākāśaśarīraṃ brahma’ iti | aśarīraḥ śarīreṣu vyāpya tiṣṭhati; viśva-vyāpakatvād viṣṇuḥ, dhyeyaḥ, atyacchaḥ, amalaḥ, nityaḥ, acintyaḥ, aprameyaḥ, nirguṇaḥ, niścalaḥ, niravayavaḥ, nirañjanaḥ, nirvikalpaḥ, nirdvandvaḥ, anirvacanīyaḥ, atīndriyaḥ, susūxmatvād anirdeśyaḥ, sadasad iti brahmavādino vadanti ||*
> 
> ततः परं धाम, परं ज्योतिः, सर्वेश्वरः, सर्वशक्तिमयः, सर्वदेवमयः, सर्वधर्ममयः, सर्वाधारः, सनातनः, ज्ञातृ-ज्ञेय-ज्ञान-हीनः, ज्ञानघनः, जाग्रत्-स्वप्न-सुषुप्ति-तुरीय-स्थानगतः (or स्थानगो), बहिःप्रज्ञः, प्रज्ञावस्थः, स्थूल-सूक्ष्म-विभागेन वैश्वानर-तैजस-स्वरूपेण भुञ्जानः, सर्वेषाम् आत्मा गुहायां निहितः; ‘अणोर् अणीयान् महतो महीयान् आत्मा गुहायां निहितोऽस्य जन्तोः’ (Katha Upanishad 1.2.20) इति श्रुतिः। तस्माद् हृदय-कमल-अन्तराकाशे उपलब्धव्यं (or उपलब्ध-सत्त्वं) विभुं परमात्मा तिष्ठति। ‘तस्याः शिखाया मध्ये परमात्मा व्यवस्थितः स शिवः’ (Mahanarayana Upanishad 13.11-12) इति श्रुतिः। एवं निष्कलम् इति विज्ञायते ॥
> *tataḥ paraṃ dhāma, paraṃ jyotiḥ, sarveśvaraḥ, sarvaśaktimayaḥ, sarvadevamayaḥ, sarvadharmamayaḥ, sarvādhāraḥ, sanātanaḥ, jñātṛ-jñeya-jñāna-hīnaḥ, jñānaghanaḥ, jāgrat-svapna-suṣupti-turīya-sthānagataḥ (or sthānago), bahiḥprajñaḥ, prajñāvasthaḥ, sthūla-sūxma-vibhāgena vaiśvānara-taijasa-svarūpeṇa bhuñjānaḥ, sarveṣām ātmā guhāyāṃ nihitaḥ; ‘aṇor aṇīyān mahato mahīyān ātmā guhāyāṃ nihito'sya jantoḥ’ iti śrutiḥ | tasmād hṛdaya-kamala-antarākāśe upalabdhavyaṃ (or upalabdha-sattvaṃ) vibhuṃ paramātmā tiṣṭhati | ‘tasyāḥ śikhāyā madhye paramātmā vyavasthitaḥ sa śivaḥ’ iti śrutiḥ | evaṃ niṣkalam iti vijñāyate ||*

#### English Translation
*   **The Omnipresent Metaphor:** The unmanifest aspect (*niṣkalaḥ*) means there is nothing other than the Supreme Soul (*paramātmanaḥ anyat na kiñcid asti*). Just as ghee exists invisibly in milk (*kṣīre sarpiḥ*), oil in sesame seeds (*tile tailaṃ*), fragrance in flowers (*puṣpe gandhaḥ*), sweetness in fruits (*phale rasaḥ*), and fire inside wood (*kāṣṭhe agniḥ*), so does He stand permeating all inside and outside, resembling space (*ākāśopamaḥ*). The Upanishad states: "Nārāyaṇa stands permeating all that is inside and outside in the universe." The Taittiriya states: "Brahman has space as His body." Being bodiless, He stands permeating all bodies.

*   **The Attributes of the Unattributed:** Because He is all-pervading, He is called Viṣṇu (*viṣṇuḥ*). He is the target of meditation (*dhyeyaḥ*), extremely transparent (*atyacchaḥ*), taintless (*amalaḥ*), eternal (*nityaḥ*), unthinkable (*acintyaḥ*), immeasurable (*aprameyaḥ*), without attributes (*nirguṇaḥ*), unmoving (*niścalaḥ*), without parts (*niravayavaḥ*), without blemish (*nirañjanaḥ*), without doubt (*nirvikalpaḥ*), free from dualities (*nirdvandvaḥ*), indescribable (*anirvacanīyaḥ*), beyond the senses (*atīndriyaḥ*), and indefinable due to extreme subtlety (*susūxmatvād anirdeśyaḥ*). Sages call Him "being and non-being" (*sadasad*).

*   **The Cosmic Consciousness:** He is the supreme abode, the supreme light (*paraṃ jyotiḥ*), the Lord of all, embodying all power, all gods, and all righteousness (*sarvadharmamayaḥ*), the support of all (*sarvādhāraḥ*), eternal, free from the triad of knower, known, and knowledge (*jñātṛ-jñeya-jñāna-hīnaḥ*), a mass of wisdom (*jñānaghanaḥ*). He is present in the waking, dreaming, deep sleep, and the fourth transcendent states (*jāgrat-svapna-suṣupti-turīya-sthānagataḥ*). He has consciousness directed outward, is in the state of pure wisdom, and enjoys as the gross and subtle divisions through the *Vaiśvānara* (waking) and *Taijasa* (dreaming) aspects.

*   **Realization in the Heart:** He is the soul of all residing in the heart-cave (*sarveṣām ātmā guhāyāṃ nihitaḥ*). The Śruti declares: "Subtler than the subtle, greater than the great, the soul resides in the heart-cave of this creature" (*‘aṇor aṇīyān mahato mahīyān ātmā guhāyāṃ nihito'sya jantoḥ’*). Therefore, the all-pervading Supreme Soul resides, to be realized within the space of the heart-lotus (*hṛdaya-kamala-antarākāśe upalabdhavyaṃ*). The Upanishad states: "In the center of that flame, the Supreme Soul is established, He is Śiva (Auspicious)." Thus is known the unmanifest state.

## Glossary of Key Terms

1.  **Niṣkala (निष्कल):** The unmanifest, formless, and attributeless aspect of Brahman ( Nirguna Brahman), which is transcendent and omnipresent.
2.  **Tattvajñāna (तत्त्वज्ञान):** The metaphysical knowledge of truth, specifically the realization of the identity and nature of the Supreme Soul Nārāyaṇa.
3.  **Hṛdaya-kamala (हृदयकमल):** The lotus of the heart, representing the primary spiritual center where the unmanifest Lord is realized.
4.  **Vibhū (विभु):** The all-pervading, omnipresent, and mighty nature of the Supreme Soul.
5.  **Jñāna-ghana (ज्ञानघन):** A solid mass of pure consciousness or wisdom, free from mental modifications.
6.  **Turīya (तुरीय):** The fourth state of consciousness, transcending waking (*jāgrat*), dreaming (*svapna*), and deep sleep (*suṣupti*), representing pure spiritual realization.
7.  **Vaiśvānara (वैश्वानर):** The cosmic soul in the waking state, experiencing the external, gross material world.
8.  **Taijasa (तैजस):** The cosmic soul in the dreaming state, experiencing internal, subtle mental impressions.
9.  **Anirvacanīya (अनिर्वचनीय):** That which is indescribable, indefinable, and beyond intellectual categories of existence or non-existence.
10. **Nirañjana (निरञ्जन):** Pure, spotless, and entirely free from the blemishes of material nature or karma.
