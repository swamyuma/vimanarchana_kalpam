# Vimanarchana Kalpa of Sage Marīci (Vaikhānasa Āgama)
## Chapter 2: Bhūparīkṣā-Ālayadeśavidhiḥ (Examination of the Ground and Selection of the Site)

This document contains the Sanskrit text, IAST transliteration, and English translation of the 2nd Chapter (*Dvitīyaḥ Paṭalaḥ*) of the **Vimānārcanākalpa** (attributed to Sage Marīci, representing the Vaikhānasa school of Vaishnavism).

This chapter outlines the qualifications required for the performing Acharya, the astrological timings for testing the ground, the purification rituals and observing of omens prior to construction, the visual, olfactory, tactile, and gustatory parameters for testing the soil, the types of land to be rejected, the soil density test, and the mandala layout mapping of the main temple of Lord Viṣṇu and the ancillary shrines of Śiva, Gaṇeśa, Durgā, Kārtikeya, and the various Avatars in different zones of a village or city.

---

### Section 1: Selection of the Qualified Acharya (Viśiṣṭācāryavaraṇam)

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **विशिष्टाचार्यवरणम्**
> अथ आलय-भूपरीक्षादि-समारम्भकालात् पूर्वस्मिन् यजमानः — आचार्यं सर्वकार्योपदेशकं वैखानससूत्रोक्तविधिना निषेकादिसंस्कारैः संस्कृतं विप्रं वेदविदं नित्यस्वाध्याययुतं गृहस्थं पत्न्यपत्ययुतं दयादिगुणैर्युक्तं शुभलक्षणैर्युक्तं भूपरीक्षादि-सर्वक्रियामार्गज्ञं ज्ञानयोगविदं जितेन्द्रियं नित्यध्यानपरं निश्चलं नित्यार्चनपरं वैष्णवं भक्तिमन्तम् एकं वरयित्वा अभिवन्द्य सम्पूज्य याचयेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **viśiṣṭācāryavaraṇam**
> atha ālaya-bhūparīkṣādi-samārambhakālāt pūrvasmin yajamānaḥ — ācāryaṃ sarvakāryopadeśakaṃ vaikhānasasūtroktavidhinā niṣekādisaṃskāraiḥ saṃskṛtaṃ vipraṃ vedavidaṃ nityasvādhyāyayutaṃ gṛhasthaṃ patnyapatyayutaṃ dayādiguṇairyuktaṃ śubhalakṣaṇairyuktaṃ bhūparīkṣādi-sarvakriyāmārgajñaṃ jñānayogavidaṃ jitendriyaṃ nityadhyānaparaṃ niścalaṃ nityārcanaparaṃ vaiṣṇavaṃ bhaktimantam ekaṃ varayitvā abhivandya sampūjya yācayet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Selection of the Acharya:** 
    *   Prior to commencing the temple construction, testing the ground, etc., the patron (*yajamāna*) should select an Acharya who will instruct all activities. He should be:
        *   A Brahmana who is purified by the Vaikhānasa Smarta sacraments beginning with Nisheka (conception).
        *   Knowledgeable in the Vedas, regularly performing daily Veda study (*svādhyāya*), a householder (*gṛhastha*) living with his wife and children.
        *   Endowed with virtues like compassion, possessing auspicious physical marks, and expert in the path of all rituals like testing the ground.
        *   Knower of the path of knowledge and yoga (*jñāna-yoga-vid*), having conquered his senses, steady in constant meditation, unwavering, devoted to daily worship (*nityārcana*), a dedicated Vaishnava.
    *   Having honored, bowed to, and worshipped him, the patron should request his services.

### Section 2: Method & Time for Ground Testing (Bhūparīkṣā-vidhiḥ)

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **भूपरीक्षाविधिः तत्कालश्च**
> आचार्यः यजमानेन भूमिं सम्यक् परीक्षयेत्। यथेष्टमासे शुक्लपक्षे कृष्णपक्षे त्रिधाद्यके वा, कर्तुरनुकूलर्क्षे भूपरीक्षां कारयेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **bhūparīkṣāvidhiḥ, tatkālaś-ca**
> ācāryaḥ yajamānena bhūmiṃ samyak parīkṣayet. yatheṣṭamāse śuklapakṣe kṛṣṇapakṣe tridhādyake vā, karturanukūlarkṣe bhūparīkṣāṃ kārayet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Method and Time for Testing the Ground:** 
    *   The Acharya, together with the patron, should examine the ground thoroughly.
    *   This ground testing should be performed in a favorable month, during either the bright fortnight or the first three parts (first 10 days) of the dark fortnight, under an auspicious constellation favorable to the patron.

### Section 3: Ritual for Observing Omens (Nimitta-darśana-vidhiḥ)

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **अङ्कुरार्पणपूर्वं निमित्तदर्शनविधिः**
> तद्दिनात् पूर्वे अङ्कुरानर्पयित्वा, हर्म्यागारादि-शिल्पिशास्त्रज्ञं लक्षणसम्पन्नं शुद्धम् अरोगिणं कुशलं शिल्पिनम् आहूय, वैष्णवैः सार्धं "प्रीयतां भगवान्" इति देवेशं प्रणम्य, देवं ध्यायन् "विष्णुर्मा रक्षतु" इति आत्मरक्षां कृत्वा, सर्वालङ्कारसंयुक्तां दीप-अम्बुज-करकां कन्यां पुरस्कृत्य, तद्देशं प्रविश्य, प्रदक्षिणीकृत्य, शकुनान्युपलक्षयेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **ankurārpaṇapūrvaṃ nimittadarśanavidhiḥ**
> taddināt pūrve ankurān-arpayitvā, harmyāgārādi-śilpiśāstrajñaṃ lakṣaṇasampannaṃ śuddham aroginaṃ kuśalaṃ śilpinam-āhūya, vaiṣṇavaiḥ sārdhaṃ "prīyatāṃ bhagavān" iti deveśaṃ praṇamya, devaṃ dhyāyan "viṣṇurmā rakṣatu" iti ātmarakṣāṃ kṛtvā, sarvālaṅkārasaṃyuktāṃ dīpa-ambuja-karakāṃ kanyāṃ puraskṛtya, tad-deśaṃ praviśya, pradakṣiṇīkṛtya, śakunāny-upalakṣayet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Observing Omens Preceded by Sowing Seeds:** 
    *   On the day prior, having sown the sprouts (*ankura*), the priest should summon a skilled architect/sculptor (*śilpin*) who is healthy (free from disease), pure, knowledgeable in the architectural scriptures (*śilpi-śāstra*), and endowed with proper qualifications.
    *   Together with other Vaishnavas, saying *"May the Lord be pleased"*, he should bow to the Lord of Devas, meditate on the Lord, and perform self-protection reciting the mantra *"May Viṣṇu protect me"* (*viṣṇur mā rakṣatu*).
    *   Preceded by a young maiden who is adorned with all ornaments and holding a lamp, a lotus, and a water-pot, he should enter the site, circumambulate it, and observe the omens.

### Section 4: Testing & Gathering the Soil (Mṛt-saṅgrahah)

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **भूपरीक्षापूर्वं मृत्संग्रहः**
> चतुरश्राम् आयताश्रां वा, प्रागानताम् उत्तरानतां समां वा भूमिं, शब्द-स्पर्श-रूप-रस-गन्धैः सम्परीक्षयेत्। वीणा-वेणु-दुन्दुभि-गज-तुरङ्ग-वेद-अब्धि-ध्वनि-युतां, सुखस्पर्शां, श्वेत-पीत-रक्त-कृष्ण-वर्णाभां, मधुर-अम्ल-तिक्त-कटु-कषाय-लवण-रसां सुगन्धसंयुक्तां, प्रदक्षिणोदवर्तीं, क्षीरवृक्ष-तुलसी-कुश-दर्भ-विश्वामित्र-विष्णुक्रान्त-स्थलारविन्द-दूर्वा-युतां भूमिं, सुमुहूर्ते गृहीत्वा, आचार्यः प्राङ्मुखः उदङ्मुखो वा भूत्वा, यस्मिन् देशे "जीवन्तीः" इति जलं प्रक्षिप्य, मेदिन्यादिमन्त्रैः हस्ताभ्यां भूमिम् अभिमृशेत्, "इदं विष्णुः" (ऋ० स० १-२२-१७) इति आहरेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **bhūparīkṣāpūrvaṃ mṛtsaṅgrahaḥ**
> caturaśrām āyatāśrāṃ vā, prāgānatām uttarānatāṃ samāṃ vā bhūmiṃ, śabda-sparśa-rūpa-rasa-gandhaiḥ samparīkṣayet. vīṇā-veṇu-dundubhi-gaja-taraṅga-veda-abdhi-dhvani-yutāṃ, sukhasparśāṃ, śveta-pīta-rakta-kṛṣṇa-varṇābhāṃ, madhura-amla-tikta-kaṭu-kaṣāya-lavaṇa-rasāṃ sugandhasaṃyuktāṃ, pradakṣiṇodavartīṃ, kṣīravṛkṣa-tulasī-kuśa-darbha-viśvāmitra-viṣṇukrānta-sthālāravinda-dūrvā-yutāṃ bhūmiṃ, sumuhūrte gṛhītvā, ācāryaḥ prāṅmukhaḥ udaṅmukho vā bhūtvā, yasmin deśe "jīvantīḥ" iti jalaṃ prakṣipya, medinyādimantraiḥ hastābhyāṃ bhūmim abhimṛśet, "idaṃ viṣṇuḥ" (Ṛgveda Saṃhitā 1.22.17) iti āharet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Gathering Soil before Ground Testing:** 
    *   He should select a plot of land that is square or rectangular, sloping towards the East or North, or level.
    *   He should test it by its sound, touch, sight, taste, and smell.
    *   It should have pleasant sounds (resembling veena, flute, drum, elephant, horse, Veda chanting, or the ocean), be pleasant to touch, colored white, yellow, red, or black, taste sweet, sour, bitter, pungent, astringent, or salty, have a pleasant fragrance, and slope in a clockwise/north-east direction.
    *   It should grow trees with milky sap, Tulasi, Kusa grass, Darbha grass, Visvamitra grass, Vishnukranta, land-lotuses, and Durva grass.
    *   Having selected the land at an auspicious hour, the Acharya should face East or North, sprinkle water saying *"jīvantīḥ..."* (purifying waters), touch the ground with both hands reciting the *Medini* mantra, and gather the soil reciting *"idaṃ viṣṇuḥ..."* (Rigveda Samhita 1.22.17).

### Section 5: Lands to Reject and the Soil Density Test

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **त्याज्यभूमिकथनपूर्वं निमित्तदर्शनप्रकारः**
> नपुंसकवृक्षाढ्यां मूष-कपाल-अस्थि-पाषाण-शर्करा-आकीर्णां, वल्मीक-पतङ्ग-कूप-भस्म-अङ्गार-तुष-केशान्विताम्, अन्त्यजादिभूयिष्ठां भूमिं त्यजेत्। पूर्वोक्ते देशे हस्तायत-विस्तार-अवगाढं खनित्वा, तदवटे तां मृदं प्रक्षिपेत्; अधिके महतीं वृद्धिं, हीने हानिं, समे समं, विद्यात्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **tyājyabhūmikathanapūrvaṃ nimittadarśanaprakāraḥ**
> napuṃsakavṛkṣāḍhyāṃ mūṣa-kapāla-asthi-pāṣāṇa-śarkarā-ākīrṇāṃ, valmīka-pataṅga-kūpa-bhasma-aṅgāra-tuṣa-keśānvitām, antyajādibhiyṣṭhāṃ bhūmiṃ tyajet. pūrvokte deśe hastāyata-vistāra-avagādhaṃ khanitvā, tad-avaṭe tāṃ mṛdaṃ prakṣipet; adhike mahatīṃ vṛddhiṃ, hīne hāniṃ, same samaṃ, vidyāt.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Omens and Rejected Land:** 
    *   The priest should reject land that is filled with barren/sterile trees, strewn with rat-holes, potsherds, bones, stones, and gravel, or containing anthills, insects, wells, ashes, embers, husks, and hair, or mostly inhabited by outcastes.

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **निमित्तान्तरदर्शनपूर्वं कार्यारम्भविधिः**
> अथ अन्यैः निमित्तैः संलक्ष्य कार्यम् आरभेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **nimittāntaradarśanapūrvaṃ kāryārambhavidhiḥ**
> atha anyaiḥ nimittaiḥ saṃlakṣya kāryam-ārabhet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **The Soil Density/Volume Test:**
    *   Having dug a pit measuring one cubit in length, width, and depth in the selected area, he should throw the dug-out soil back into it.
        *   If the soil overflows the pit (*adhika*), it indicates great prosperity (*vṛddhi*).
        *   If it falls short (*hīna*), it indicates loss (*hāni*).
        *   If it is exactly level (*sama*), it indicates average/equal results (*sama*).
    *   Then, observing other omens (*nimitta*), he should commence the work.

### Section 6: Layout of the Town & Shrine Placements (Mandala-vidhiḥ)

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **ग्रामादौ चतुष्षष्ट्यादिभागकरणपूर्वं विष्णुवर्चनविधिः**
> ग्रामादिषु एवं विधाय, चतुष्षष्टिपदम् एकाशीतिपदं षट्पञ्चाशदुत्तर-द्विशतपदं वा कृत्वा, तन्मध्यं ब्रह्मभागं चतुर्धा कृत्वा, तद् वायव्यांशे पश्चिमे पूर्वे वा, मध्यसूत्राद् उत्तरे दक्षिणे वा, तत्सूत्रं देवस्य दक्षिणपादपार्श्वं प्रासादप्राकाराणां भित्तिं वा समाश्रितं, एवं सङ्कल्प्य, देवेशं विष्णुं नव-षट्-पञ्च-मूर्ति-विधानेन प्रतिष्ठाप्य अर्चयेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **grāmādau catuṣṣaṣṭyādibhāgakaraṇapūrvaṃ viṣṇuvarcanavidhiḥ**
> grāmādiṣu evaṃ vidhāya, catuṣṣaṣṭipadaṃ ekāśītipadaṃ ṣaṭpañcāśaduttara-dviśatapadaṃ vā kṛtvā, tanmadhyaṃ brahmabhāgaṃ caturdhā kṛtvā, tad vāyavyāṃśe paścime pūrve vā, madhyasūtrād uttare dakṣiṇe vā, tatsūtraṃ devasya dakṣiṇapādapārśvaṃ prāsādaprākārāṇāṃ bhittiṃ vā samāśritaṃ, evaṃ saṅkalpya, deveśaṃ viṣṇuṃ nava-ṣaṭ-pañca-mūrti-vidhānena pratiṣṭhāpya arcayet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Worship Layout on the Grid:** 
    *   In the village laid out in this manner, having made a grid of 64, 81, or 256 squares (*pada*), the central part (*Brahma-bhāga*) should be divided into four.
    *   In its North-Western, Western, or Eastern part, or North/South of the central axis—aligned with the right foot of the deity or the wall of the temple sanctum or enclosures—the priest should consecrate and worship Lord Viṣṇu according to the rule of 9, 6, or 5 forms (*nava-ṣaṭ-pañca-mūrti-vidhāna*).

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **ऐशान्यादिषु शङ्करादिभवनम्**
> ऐशान्यां शङ्करभवनं प्राङ्मुखं, वारुणे देवेशं प्राङ्मुखम् आसीनं स्थितं वा, आदित्यांशे भास्करालयं प्रत्यङ्मुखं, याम्ये विन्ध्यवासिनीं प्राङ्मुखीं, भूतेशे विघ्नेशं, सुग्रीवे षण्मुखं, अदितौ मातृकोष्ठं पराङ्मुखं, जयन्ते दौवारिके वा कृष्णस्यालयं, पुष्पदन्ते शोषणे वा राघवस्यालयं, महेन्द्रे महामत्स्यं, सत्यके कूर्मस्यालयं, नैर्ऋत्यां वाराहं, जवने वा अन्तरिक्षे वा नारसिंहं, रोहिणे नागे वा वामनं त्रिविक्रमं च, गन्धर्वे बलभद्रं, मुख्ये परशुपाणिं, सूरिदेवे कल्कितम्, सोमे शयानं, ग्रहक्षते दैविकं वासुदेवं, वितथे मानुषं वासुदेवं, उष्णशालादिमूर्तिं भृङ्गराजे, ग्रामाद् बाह्ये वा पञ्चवीराणां स्थानम्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **aiśānyādiṣu śaṅkarādibhavanam**
> aiśānyāṃ śaṅkarabhavanaṃ prāṅmukhaṃ, vāruṇe deveśaṃ prāṅmukham āsīnaṃ sthitaṃ vā, ādityāṃśe bhāskarālayaṃ pratyaṅmukhaṃ, yāmye vindhyavāsinīṃ prāṅmukhīṃ, bhūteśe vighneśaṃ, sugrīve ṣaṇmukhaṃ, aditau mātṛkoṣṭhaṃ parāṅmukhaṃ, jayante dauvārike vā kṛṣṇasyālayaṃ, puṣpadante śoṣaṇe vā rāghavasyālayaṃ, mahendre mahāmatsyaṃ, satyake kūrmasyālayaṃ, nairṛtyāṃ vārāhaṃ, javane vā antarikṣe vā nārasiṃhaṃ, rohiṇe nāge vā vāmanaṃ trivikramaṃ ca, gandharve balabhadraṃ, mukhye paraśupāṇiṃ, sūrideve kalkitam, some śayānaṃ, grahakṣate daivikaṃ vāsudevaṃ, vitathe mānuṣaṃ vāsudevaṃ, uṣṇaśālādīmūrtiṃ bhṛṅgarāje, grāmād bāhye vā pañcavīrāṇāṃ sthānam.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Temples of Śiva and other Deities in the Cardinal Directions:** 
    *   **Śiva (Sankara):** North-East, facing East.
    *   **Viṣṇu:** West, facing East, sitting or standing.
    *   **Sūrya (Bhāskara):** Āditya zone, facing West.
    *   **Durgā (Vindhyavasini):** South, facing East.
    *   **Gaṇeśa (Vighnesa):** Bhutesa zone.
    *   **Kārtikeya (Shanmukha):** Sugriva zone.
    *   **Seven Mothers (Matrikas):** Aditi zone, facing away.
    *   **Kṛṣṇa:** Jayanta or Dauvarika zone.
    *   **Rama (Raghava):** Pushpadanta or Shoshana zone.
    *   **Matsya:** Mahendra zone.
    *   **Kurma:** Satyaka zone.
    *   **Varaha:** South-West (*Nairṛtya*).
    *   **Narasimha:** Javana or Antariksha zone.
    *   **Vamana & Trivikrama:** Rohina or Naga zone.
    *   **Balarama (Balabhadra):** Gandharva zone.
    *   **Parashurama (Parashupani):** Mukhya zone.
    *   **Kalki:** Surideva zone.
    *   **Reclining Viṣṇu (Sayana):** Soma zone.
    *   **Divine Vāsudeva:** Grahakshata zone.
    *   **Human Vāsudeva:** Vitatha zone.
    *   **Ushnashala Deity:** Bhringaraja zone.
    *   **Panchaveeras (Five Heroes):** Outside the village.

### Section 7: Orientation of Deity Icons and Temple Doors

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **ग्रामादौ तत्प्रेक्षमूर्तिस्थापनविधिः**
> ग्राममध्ये बाह्ये दिक्षु विदिक्षु वा सर्वत्र स्थितम् आसीनं शयानं यानकं वा, ध्रुवकौतुकं, ध्रुवार्चां वा, ग्रामप्रेक्षमेव कारयेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **grāmādau tatprekṣāmūrtisthāpanavidhiḥ**
> grāmamadhye bāhye dikṣu vidikṣu vā sarvatra sthitam āsīnaṃ śayānaṃ yānakaṃ vā, dhruvakautukaṃ, dhru vārcāṃ vā, grāmaprekṣam-eva kārayet.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Worship Facing the Village:** 
    *   In the center or outside of the village, in the cardinal or intermediate directions, all deity icons—standing, sitting, reclining, or riding—should be made facing the village (*grāma-prekṣa*).

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **ग्रामाद् बाह्ये विप्रावासस्थले नदीतीरादौ वा**
> ग्रामाद् बाह्येऽपि यत्र विप्रैः वस्तव्यं तत्रैव मध्ये दिशासु विदिशासु च देवं, देवीभ्यां सहितं वा, संस्थापयेत्। वन-नदी-अद्रि-समुद्र-पार्श्वे अन्यत्र तटाक-आराम-समीपे विविक्तदेशे वा।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **grāmād bāhye viprāvāsasthale nadītīrādau vā**
> grāmād bāhye'pi yatra vipraiḥ vastavyaṃ tatraiva madhye diśāsu vidikṣāsu ca devaṃ, devībhyāṃ sahitaṃ vā, saṃsthāpayet. vana-nadī-adri-samudra-pārśve anyatra taṭāka-ārāma-samīpe viviktadeśe vā.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Temples in Solitary Places or Outskirts:**
    *   Even outside the village, where Brahmanas reside, the Lord should be established in the center or in the directions, either alone or with Sridevi and Bhudevi, near forests, rivers, mountains, oceans, ponds, gardens, or in a solitary place.

#### Verse / Paragraph 3
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **आलयद्वारस्योत्तमोत्तमत्वादिकम्**
> प्राग्द्वारम् उत्तमोत्तमं, पश्चिमद्वारम् उत्तमं, दक्षिणद्वारं मध्यमम्, उत्तरद्वारम् अधमम्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **ālayadvārasyottamottamatvādikam**
> prāgdvāram uttamottamaṃ, paścimadvāram uttamaṃ, dakṣiṇadvāraṃ madhyamam, uttardvāram adhamam.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Directions of the Temple Doors:** 
    *   East-facing door (*prāg-dvāra*): Best of the best (*uttamottama*).
    *   West-facing door (*paścima-dvāra*): Excellent/good (*uttama*).
    *   South-facing door (*dakṣiṇa-dvāra*): Medium/average (*madhyama*).
    *   North-facing door (*uttara-dvāra*): Lowest/least preferred (*adhama*).

#### Verse / Paragraph 4
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **एकानेकमूर्तिविधानम्**
> सहस्रविप्राधिक-ग्रामादौ मध्ये विष्णुम् अनेकमूर्तिविधानेन अर्चयेत्, अशक्तश्चेद् एकमूर्तिविधानेन वा। तन्न्यूने पञ्चविंशतिद्वयाद् अर्वागपि पञ्चमूर्तिं प्राङ्मुखं, प्रत्यङ्मुखम् अनेकमूर्तिं वा, अन्येषां नगर्यादीनां पश्चिमे च एकमूर्तिविधिना विष्णुं प्रतिष्ठाप्यार्चयेत् इति आह मरीचिः।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **ekānekamūrtividhānam**
> sahasraviprādhika-grāmādau madhye viṣṇum anekamūrtividhānena arcayet, aśaktaśced ekamūrtividhānena vā. tannyūne pañcaviṃśatidvayād arvāgapi pañcamūrtiṃ prāṅmukhaṃ, pratyaṅmukham anekamūrtiṃ vā, anyeṣāṃ nagaryādīnāṃ paścime ca ekamūrtividhinā viṣṇuṃ pratiṣṭhāpyārcayet iti āha marīciḥ.

**Translation (English):**
*   **Single or Multi-icon Worship:** 
    *   In a village with more than a thousand Brahmanas, one should worship Viṣṇu in the center according to the multi-deity method (*aneka-mūrti*); if unable, according to the single-deity method (*eka-mūrti*).
    *   In smaller settlements, down to fifty households, one should consecrate and worship the five forms of Viṣṇu (*pañcamūrti*) facing East, or multi-forms facing West.
    *   In the West of other cities (*nagaryādīnām*), Viṣṇu should be established according to the single-form method. Thus spoke Sage Marīci.
