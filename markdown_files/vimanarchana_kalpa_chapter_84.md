# Vimanarchana Kalpa of Sage Marīci (Vaikhānasa Āgama)
## Chapter 84: Sāvitrī-mantra-kalpa-vidhiḥ (Rules on the Savitri / Gayatri Mantra)

This document contains the Sanskrit text, IAST transliteration, and English translation of the 84th Chapter (*Caturaśītitemaḥ Paṭalaḥ*) of the **Vimānārcanākalpa** (attributed to Sage Marīci, representing the Vaikhānasa school of Vaishnavism).

This chapter presents the comprehensive ritual science (*kalpa*) of the *Sāvitrī* (commonly known as the *Gāyatrī*) mantra. It details:
1.  The symbolic structure of the Gayatri as a cosmic goddess whose limbs and features represent the Vedas, Upanishads, fields of grammar, directions, and cosmic elements.
2.  The three-fold meditation (*dhyāna*) of the Sandhya goddesses across the three junctions of the day: Savitri (morning, swan-riding, Brahma-ruled), Gayatri (noon, bull-riding, Rudra-ruled), and Sarasvati (evening, Garuḍa-riding, Viṣṇu-ruled).
3.  The placement of the twenty-four syllables of the mantra on twenty-four specific spots on the human body (*varṇa-nyāsa*).
4.  The specific color, presiding deity, sin-cleansing power, and spiritual fruit (such as attaining specific worlds) for each of the twenty-four syllables.

### Section 1: The Cosmic Form of the Savitri / Gayatri Goddess

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **अथ चतुरशीतितमः पटलः**
> **गायत्र्याः अक्षरेषु न्यासः, सावित्रीति नाम-निमित्तं च**
> **ऋष्यादिकं, पादेषु अक्षर-सङ्ख्या च**
> ***atha caturaśītitemaḥ paṭalaḥ***
> ***gāyatriyāḥ axareṣu nyāsaḥ, sāvitrīti nāma-nimittaṃ ca***
> ***ṛṣyādikaṃ, pādeṣu axara-saṅkhyā ca***
> 
> अथ सावित्रीकल्पं वक्ष्ये —
> ‘चतुर्विंशत्यक्षरा गायत्री’ इति श्रुतिः; सा गायत्री सवितृ-देवत्या, तस्मात् सावित्री इति उच्यते। तस्याः ऋषि-च्छन्दः-अधिदैवतं पुरस्ताद् उक्तम्। अष्टाक्षरा त्रिपदा, चतुष्पदा च (द्वात्रिंशदक्षरा)। जप्ये त्रिपदा, अर्चने चतुष्पदा।
> *atha sāvitrīkalpaṃ vakṣye —*
> *‘caturviṃśatyaxarā gāyatrī’ iti śrutiḥ; sā gāyatrī savitṛ-devatyā, tasmāt sāvitrī iti ucyate | tasyāḥ ṛṣi-cchandas-adhidevataṃ purastād uktam | aṣṭāxarā tripadā, catuṣpadā ca (dvātriṃśadaxarā) | japye tripadā, arcane catuṣpadā ||*
> 
> **तस्याः स्वरूपम्**
> अग्निवर्णा, षट्कुक्षिः, पञ्चशीर्षा, शुकमुखा (or श्वेतमुखा), कमलेक्षणा; ऋग्वेदः प्रथमः पादः, यजुर्वेदो द्वितीयः पादः, सामवेदः तृतीयः पादः, पृथिवी चरणम्, ऊरू पर्वताः, अम्बरम् अस्थि; पूर्वः प्रथमः कुक्षिः, दक्षिणो द्वितीयः, पश्चिम्स्तृतीयः, उत्तरः चतुर्थः, पञ्चमम् ऊर्ध्वम्, अधरः षष्ठः इति; पुराणानि आननम्, जगती दिव्य-रूपम्, उदर-अन्तरम् आकाशः, छन्दः स्तनौ, धर्मः स्वरः (or घ्राणं), जिह्वा सरस्वती (or वाणी), न्यायो बाहुः, गीः (or गिरयः) कराः, ग्रीवा, प्रथमं शिरः शब्द-शास्त्रं, द्वितीयं शिक्षा, तृतीयं कल्पं, चतुर्थं निरुक्तं, पञ्चमं ज्योतिषम्; अनलो मुखं, वदनम् इन्दु-मण्डलं, वसनं वायुः, अलकाः भूरूपाः (or भूर्लोकाः), सहस्रकिरणो मौलिः, ताराः कुसुमानि, नक्षत्रमाला हारः, ग्रीवा रत्न-विभूषिता, ब्राह्मं मूर्धा, शिखा सोमः, विष्णुः आत्मा, वेदान्ताः विमलं मनः, वेदाः प्राणाः, मीमांसा चित्तम् इति एवम् ज्ञात्वा त्रिसन्धिषु त्रिविधं ध्यानम् आचरेत् ॥
> ***tasyāḥ svarūpam***
> *agnivarṇā, ṣaṭkukṣiḥ, pañcaśīrṣā, śukamukhā (or śvetamukhā), kamalexaṇā; ṛgvedaḥ prathamaḥ pādaḥ, yajurvedo dvitīyaḥ pādaḥ, sāmavedaḥ tṛtīyaḥ pādaḥ, pṛthivī caraṇam, ūrū parvatāḥ, ambaram asthi; pūrvaḥ prathamaḥ kukṣiḥ, daxiṇo dvitīyaḥ, paścimastṛtīyaḥ, uttaraḥ caturthaḥ, pañcamam ūrdhvam, adharaḥ ṣaṣṭhaḥ iti; purāṇāni ānanam, jagatī divya-rūpam, udara-antaram ākāśaḥ, chandaḥ stanou, dharmaḥ svaraḥ (or ghrāṇaṃ), jihvā sarasvatī, nyāyo bāhuḥ, gīḥ (or girayaḥ) karāḥ, grīvā, prathamaṃ śiraḥ śabda-śāstraṃ, dvitīyaṃ śikṣā, tṛtīyaṃ kalpaṃ, caturthaṃ niruktaṃ, pañcamaṃ jyotiṣam; analo mukhaṃ, vadanam indu-maṇḍalaṃ, vasanaṃ vāyuḥ, alakāḥ bhūrūpāḥ, sahasrakiraṇo mouliḥ, tārāḥ kusumāni, naxatramālā hāraḥ, grīvā ratna-vibhūṣitā, brāhmaṃ mūrdhā, śikhā somaḥ, viṣṇuḥ ātmā, vedāntāḥ vimalaṃ manaḥ, vedāḥ prāṇāḥ, mīmāṃsā cittam iti evam jñātvā trisandhiṣu trividhaṃ dhyānam ācaret ||*

#### English Translation
*   **The Name:** Now, I shall explain the ritual science of Savitri (*atha sāvitrīkalpaṃ vakṣye*). The Śruti declares: "The Gayatri has twenty-four syllables" (*‘caturviṃśatyaxarā gāyatrī’*). Because that Gayatri has Savitr as her deity (*savitṛ-devatyā*), she is called Savitri (*sāvitrī*). Her seer, meter, and deity have been declared earlier. She has eight syllables per quarter, and consists of three quarters (24 syllables) or four quarters (32 syllables). In silent chanting she has three quarters (*japye tripadā*); in ritual worship she has four quarters (*arcane catuṣpadā*).

*   **The Anatomy of the Goddess:**
    *   **Heads and Limbs:** She is of the color of fire (*agnivarṇā*), six-bellied (*ṣaṭkukṣiḥ*), five-headed (*pañcaśīrṣā*), white/parrot-faced (*śukamukhā*), and lotus-eyed (*kamalexaṇā*).
    *   **Vedic Quarters:** The Rigveda is her first quarter; the Yajurveda is her second; the Samaveda is her third; the Earth is her feet; the mountains are her thighs; space is her bone.
    *   **The Bellies:** The east is her first belly, the south is the second, the west is the third, the north is the fourth, the upward direction is the fifth, and the downward direction is the sixth.
    *   **Face and Organs:** The Puranas are her face; the Jagati meter is her divine form; the inner stomach is space; the meters are her breasts; Dharma is her nostrils/voice; Sarasvati is her tongue; Justice is her arms; sounds/words are her hands; the neck is her connection.
    *   **The Six Vedangas:** The science of grammar (shabda-shastra) is her first head; phonetics (Shiksha) is her second; ritual (Kalpa) is her third; etymology (Nirukta) is her fourth; astronomy (Jyotisha) is her fifth.
    *   **Cosmic Ornaments:** Fire is her mouth; her face is the moon's orb; her garment is wind; her curls of hair are the Earth's forms; the thousand-rayed Sun is her crown; the stars are flowers; the constellations are her necklace; her neck is gem-studded; the realm of Brahmā is her head; the moon (Soma) is her hair-tuft.
    *   **Philosophical Mind:** Viṣṇu is her soul (*viṣṇuḥ ātmā*); the Upanishads (Vedanta) are her pure mind; the Vedas are her breaths; Mimamsa is her intellect. Knowing this, one should perform three-fold meditation during the three twilights (*trisandhiṣu trividhaṃ dhyānam ācaret*).

### Section 2: The Three-fold Meditation on the Twilight Goddesses

#### Verse / Paragraph 1
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> **त्रिविधं ध्यानम्**
> पूर्वासन्ध्यां कौमारीं रक्तवर्णां हंसवाहिनीं अक्षसूत्र-यज्ञोपवीत-कमण्डलु-धारिणीं ब्रह्म-देवत्यां सावित्रीं नाम, समिदाधान-हस्तां (or समिद्-अग्नि-धारिणीं), यथाक्रमेण भूमिं, गार्हपत्य-अग्निम्, उपनयनं च (or कात्यायनम् इति एवम्) ध्यायेत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> **trividhaṃ dhyānam**
> pūrvāsandhyāṃ koumārīṃ raktavarṇāṃ haṃsavāhinīṃ axasūtra-yajñopavīta-kamaṇḍalu-dhāriṇīṃ brahma-devatyāṃ sāvitrīṃ nāma, samidādhāna-hastāṃ, yathākrameṇa bhūmiṃ, gārhapatya-agnim, upanayanaṃ ca (or kātyāyanam iti evam) dhyāyet ||

**Translation (English):**
*   **Morning Twilight (Prātah-sandhyā):** One should meditate on the morning twilight goddess as a young maiden (*koumārīm*), red-colored (*raktavarṇāṃ*), riding a swan (*haṃsavāhinīṃ*), holding a rosary and a water pot, wearing the sacred thread, representing the deity Brahmā, named Savitrī, holding firewood for sacrifices (*samidādhāna-hastāṃ*). One meditates on her in connection with the Earth, the Gārhapatya fire, and the Upanayana initiation.

#### Verse / Paragraph 2
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> मध्याह्न-सन्ध्यायां यौवनां शङ्ख-चक्र-अर्धचन्द्र-शूल-धारिणीं श्वेतवर्णां वृषभवाहिनीं रुद्र-देवत्यां गायत्रीं नाम, गायत्री-हृदयम्, अन्यानि पूर्ववत्।

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> madhyāhna-sandhyāyāṃ yauvanāṃ śaṅkha-cakra-ardhacandra-śūla-dhāriṇīṃ śvetavarṇāṃ vṛṣabhavāhinīṃ rudra-devatyāṃ gāyatrīṃ nāma, gāyatrī-hṛdayam, anyāni pūrvavat ||

**Translation (English):**
*   **Noon Twilight (Madhyāhna-sandhyā):** One should meditate on the midday twilight goddess as a youthful woman (*yauvanāṃ*), holding a conch, discus, crescent moon, and trident (*śaṅkha-cakra-ardhacandra-śūla-dhāriṇīṃ*), white in color (*śvetavarṇāṃ*), riding a bull (*vṛṣabhavāhinīṃ*), representing the deity Rudra, named Gāyatrī, reciting the Gayatri-hridaya, and other things as before.

#### Verse / Paragraph 3
**Sanskrit (Devanagari):**
> सायंसन्ध्यां सुलक्षणं श्यामवर्णां सर्वाभरण-भूषितां शङ्ख-चक्र-धारिणीं गरुडवाहिनीं विष्णु-देवत्यां सरस्वतीं नाम, सरस्वती-हृदयम्, अन्यानि पूर्ववत्। एवं ध्यात्वा जपम् आरभेत् ॥

**Transliteration (IAST):**
> sāyaṃsandhyāṃ sulaxaṇaṃ śyāmavarṇāṃ sarvābharaṇa-bhūṣitāṃ śaṅkha-cakra-dhāriṇīṃ garuḍavāhinīṃ viṣṇu-devatyāṃ sarasvatīṃ nāma, sarasvatī-hṛdayam, anyāni pūrvavat | evaṃ dhyātvā japam ārabhet ||

**Translation (English):**
*   **Evening Twilight (Sāyaṃ-sandhyā):** One should meditate on the evening twilight goddess as beautiful, dark-colored (*śyāmavarṇāṃ*), decorated with all ornaments (*sarvābharaṇa-bhūṣitāṃ*), holding a conch and discus (*śaṅkha-cakra-dhāriṇīṃ*), riding Garuḍa (*garuḍavāhinīṃ*), representing the deity Viṣṇu, named Sarasvatī, reciting the Sarasvati-hridaya, and other things as before. Having meditated thus, he should begin the silent recitation (*evaṃ dhyātvā japam ārabhet*).

### Section 3: The Syllable Placements on the Body (Varṇa-nyāsa)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **अक्षरन्यासः न्यास-फलं च**
> पादादि-मूर्धान्तं क्रमेण चतुर्विंशति-स्थानेषु अक्षराणि संन्यसेत् —
> 1. गुल्फे (or पादयोः) तकारं,
> 2. अङ्गुलिषु सकारं,
> 3. जङ्घयोः विकारं,
> 4. जान्वोः तुकारं,
> 5. ऊर्वोः वकारं,
> 6. गुदे रकारं,
> 7. वृषणे णिकारं,
> 8. कटिप्रदेशे यकारं,
> 9. नाभौ भकारं,
> 10. जठरे गोकारं,
> 11. स्तनयोः दकारं,
> 12. हृदये वकारं,
> 13. कण्ठे स्यकारं (or यकारं),
> 14. वदने धीकारं,
> 15. तालौ मकारं,
> 16. नासाग्रे हिकारं,
> 17. चक्षुषोः धिकारं,
> 18. भ्रूमध्ये योकारं,
> 19. ललाटे योकारं विन्यस्य;
> 20. पूर्वतः पूर्वाभिमुखं नःकारं,
> 21. दक्षिणतो दक्षिणाभिमुखं प्रकारं,
> 22. पश्चिमतः पश्चिमाभिमुखं चोकारं,
> 23. उत्तरतः उत्तराभिमुखं दकारं,
> 24. मूर्ध्नि चतुर्मुखं स्वयं व्यवस्थितं यत्कारं (or यात्कारम्);
> एवं मन्त्रस्याक्षराणि शरीरे विन्यस्य ज्ञात्वा, सर्वपाप-विनिर्मुक्तो भवति इति विज्ञायते ॥
> ***axaranyāsaḥ nyāsa-phalaṃ ca***
> *pādādi-mūrdhāntaṃ krameṇa caturviṃśati-sthāneṣu axarāṇi saṃnyaset —*
> *1. gulpha-padayoḥ takāraṃ,*
> *2. aṅguliṣu sakāraṃ,*
> *3. jaṅghayoḥ vikāraṃ,*
> *4. jānvoḥ tukāraṃ,*
> *5. ūrvoḥ vakāraṃ,*
> *6. gude rakāraṃ,*
> *7. vṛṣaṇe ṇikāraṃ,*
> *8. kaṭipradeśe yakāraṃ,*
> *9. nābhou bhakāraṃ,*
> *10. jaṭhare gokāraṃ,*
> *11. stanayoḥ dakāraṃ,*
> *12. hṛdaye vakāraṃ,*
> *13. kaṇṭhe syakāraṃ,*
> *14. vadane dhīkāraṃ,*
> *15. tālou makāraṃ,*
> *16. nāsāgre hikāraṃ,*
> *17. caxuṣoḥ dhikāraṃ,*
> *18. bhrūmadhye yokāraṃ,*
> *19. lalāṭe yokāraṃ vinyasya;*
> *20. pūrvataḥ pūrvābhimukhaṃ naḥkāraṃ,*
> *21. daxiṇato daxiṇābhimukhaṃ prakāraṃ,*
> *22. paścimataḥ paścimābhimukhaṃ cokāraṃ,*
> *23. uttarataḥ uttarābhimukhaṃ dakāraṃ,*
> *24. mūrdhni caturmukhaṃ svayaṃ vyavasthitaṃ yatkāram ||*
> *evaṃ mantrasyāxarāṇi śarīre vinyasya jñātvā, sarvapāpa-vinirmukto bhavati iti vijñāyate ||*

#### English Translation
*   **The Placement of Syllables:** One should place the twenty-four syllables of the Gayatri mantra in order from the feet to the head at twenty-four spots on the body:
    1.  **Ta (त):** On the ankles/feet.
    2.  **Sa (स):** On the fingers/toes.
    3.  **Vi (वि):** On the shanks.
    4.  **Tu (तु):** On the knees.
    5.  **Va (व):** On the thighs.
    6.  **Re (रे):** On the anus.
    7.  **Ṇya (णि):** On the testicles.
    8.  **Yaṃ (य):** On the hips.
    9.  **Bha (भ):** On the navel.
    10. **Rgo (गो):** On the stomach.
    11. **De (द):** On the breasts.
    12. **Va (व):** On the heart.
    13. **Sya (स्य):** On the throat.
    14. **Dhī (धी):** On the face.
    15. **Ma (म):** On the palate.
    16. **Hi (हि):** On the tip of the nose.
    17. **Dhi (धि):** On the eyes.
    18. **Yo (यो):** On the space between the eyebrows.
    19. **Yo (यो):** On the forehead.
    20. **Naḥ (नः):** Facing east, representing the eastern quarter.
    21. **Pra (प्र):** Facing south, representing the southern quarter.
    22. **Co (चो):** Facing west, representing the western quarter.
    23. **Da (द):** Facing north, representing the northern quarter.
    24. **Yāt (य):** On the head, representing the self-established four-faced form.

*   **The Fruit:** Having thus placed and known these syllables on one's body, he is freed from all sins (*sarvapāpa-vinirmukto bhavati*).

### Section 4: Syllable Meditations, Deities, and Concluding Fruits

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **अक्षराणां ध्यानं देवताश्च**
> अथातः अक्षराणां ध्यानं सर्वेषां वर्णानां त्रिसन्धिषु पूर्ववत् — ब्रह्म-विष्णु-रुद्र-रूपम्।
> ***axarāṇāṃ dhyānaṃ devatāśca***
> *athātaḥ axarāṇāṃ dhyānaṃ sarveṣāṃ varṇānāṃ trisandhiṣu pūrvavat — brahma-viṣṇu-rudra-rūpam ||*
> 
> **प्रत्येकम् अक्षर-देवता तस्य फलं च**
> सर्व-देवता-फलं च पृथक् एव वक्ष्यते —
> 1.  **तत्कारं (tat):** पीताभं ब्रह्म-देवत्यं महापातक-नाशनम्।
> 2.  **सकारं (sa):** श्यामाभं प्राजापत्यम् उपपातक-नाशनम्।
> 3.  **विकारं (vi):** पिङ्गलाभं सौम्यं महापातक-नाशनम्।
> 4.  **तुकारं (tu):** नीलाभं ईश्वर-देवत्यं दुष्ट-ग्रह-उपद्रव-नाशनम्।
> 5.  **वकारं (va):** वह्निसवर्णं सौम्यं भ्रूणहत्या-पाप-मोचनम्।
> 6.  **रेकारं (re):** ज्वालारूपं बार्हस्पत्यं अगम्यागमन-पाप-नाशनम्।
> 7.  **णिकारं (ṇi):** विद्युन्निभं रौद्रम् अभक्ष्य-भक्षण-पाप-नाशनम्।
> 8.  **यकारं (ya):** हेमाभं वैष्णवं ब्रह्महत्यादि-सर्वपाप-नाशनम्।
> 9.  **भकारं (bha):** कृष्णाभं याम्य-देवत्यं पुरुषहत्यादि-पाप-नाशनम्।

#### English Translation
*   **The Trinity Meditation:** Now, the meditation on the letters of the mantra (*athātaḥ axarāṇāṃ dhyānam*): for all the syllables, during the three junctions of the day as before, they represent the forms of Brahmā, Viṣṇu, and Rudra.

*   **Syllables and Fruits:** The deity and fruit of each syllable are declared separately:
    1.  **Tat:** Yellow, Brahma-ruled, destroys great sins (*mahāpātaka-nāśanam*).
    2.  **Sa:** Dark-colored, Prajāpati-ruled, destroys minor sins (*upapātaka-nāśanam*).
    3.  **Vi:** Reddish-brown, Soma-ruled, destroys great sins.
    4.  **Tu:** Blue-colored, Rudra-ruled, destroys evil planetary afflictions (*duṣṭa-graha-upadrava-nāśanam*).
    5.  **Va:** Fire-colored, Soma-ruled, releases from the sin of abortion/destroying an embryo (*bhrūṇahatyā-pāpa-mocanam*).
    6.  **Re:** Flame-like, Brihaspati-ruled, destroys the sin of illicit sexual unions.
    7.  **Ni:** Lightning-like, Rudra-ruled, destroys the sin of eating forbidden foods.
    8.  **Ya:** Gold-colored, Viṣṇu-ruled, destroys all sins starting with killing a Brahmin (*brahmahatyādi-sarvapāpa-nāśanam*).
    9.  **Bha:** Blackish-colored, Yama-ruled, destroys the sin of homicide.
    10. **Go:** Red-colored, Savitar-ruled, releases from the sin of killing a cow.
    11. **De:** Dark-colored, Tvashtar-ruled, destroys the sin of killing a woman.
    12. **Va:** White, Pushan-ruled, destroys the sin of killing a preceptor/guru.
    13. **Sya:** Gold-colored, Rudra-ruled, destroys the sin of deceit/forgery.
    14. **Dhī:** Lotus-like, Vayu-ruled, destroys the defect of eating impure foods.
    15. **Ma:** Ruby-like, Vamadeva-ruled, destroys sins committed in prior lifetimes.
    16. **Hi:** White, Mitra-Varuṇa-ruled, destroys all sins.
    17. **Yo (first):** Tawny, Vishvedevas-ruled, destroys the sin of violence against living beings.
    18. **Yo (second):** Viṣṇu-ruled, destroys evil sins.
    19. **Naḥ:** Sun-colored, Rudra-ruled, grants the world of Rudra (*rudralokapradam*).
    20. **Pra:** Blue-black, Soma-ruled, grants the world of Isvara/Siva.
    21. **Co:** Yellow, Brahma-ruled, grants the world of Brahmā.
    22. **Da:** Pure crystal-like, Viṣṇu-ruled, grants the world of Viṣṇu.
    23. **Yāt:** Gold-colored, four-faced (Brahma-like), grants all achievements, connected with twilight.

*   **The Chanting Regimen:** mentally chanting the Savitri (Gayatri) preceded by Om and the Vyahritis (*‘om bhūrbhuvahsvaḥ tat savitur...’*) one hundred times during each of the three twilights (*śataṃ trisandhyaṃ manasā japet*).

*   **Final Merits:** From this study/chanting of Savitri, there arises long life, wealth (*śrīḥ*), spiritual/Vedic luster (*brahmavarcasaṃ*), abundance of offspring, and abundance of wealth and grain. It brings all prosperity, and yields both worldly and other-worldly fruits (*aihika-āmuṣmika-phalapradaṃ*). They enjoy their desired objects in this world for a long time, and afterwards obtain ultimate liberation (*paścāt muktiṃ samālabhante*), so declares Sage Marīci.

## Glossary of Key Terms

1.  **Sāvitrī-kalpa (सावित्रीकल्प):** The ritual, spiritual, and physical science of the Gayatri/Savitri mantra, detailing its geometry, meditations, placements, and fruits.
2.  **Caturviṃśaty-axara (चतुर्विंशत्यक्षर):** The twenty-four syllables composing the classical Gayatri mantra, corresponding to twenty-four cosmic principles.
3.  **Varṇa-nyāsa (वर्णन्यास):** The ritual practice of placing/projecting the syllables of a mantra onto specific physical parts of the body to divinize the practitioner's form.
4.  **Tri-sandhyā (त्रिसन्ध्या):** The three junctions of the day (dawn, noon, and dusk) when spiritual practices and meditations are prescribed.
5.  **Koumārī (कौमारी):** The maiden form of the twilight goddess representing the creative power of Brahmā at dawn.
6.  **Yauvanā (यौवना):** The youthful form of the twilight goddess representing the destructive/transformative power of Rudra at noon.
7.  **Sarasvatī (सरस्वती):** The evening form of the twilight goddess representing the preserving power of Viṣṇu at dusk.
8.  **Mahā-pātaka (महापातक):** The class of five mortal/heinous sins in Hindu law (e.g. killing a Brahmin, drinking liquor) which are cleansed by Gayatri japa.
9.  **Upa-pātaka (उपपातक):** The class of minor sins or moral lapses, which are also cleansed by specific syllables of the mantra.
10. **Aihika-āmuṣmika (ऐहिक-आमुष्मिक):** Pertaining to this world (earthly prosperity) and the other world (liberation/moksha).
