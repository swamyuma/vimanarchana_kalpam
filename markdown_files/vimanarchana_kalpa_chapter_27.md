# Chapter 27: Consecration — Auspicious Months, Priest Qualifications, Requisite Rites, Thread-Release, and Image-Purification (Pratiṣṭhā-māsādikam)
### Section 1: Auspicious Months and Stellar Conditions for Consecration (Pratiṣṭhā-māsādikam)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **अथ सप्तविंशः पटलः -**
> **प्रतिष्ठा-मासादिकम्**
> ***atha saptaviṃśaḥ paṭalaḥ -***
> ***pratiṣṭhā-māsādikam***
> 
> अथातः भगवतः नारायणस्य प्रतिष्ठा-विधिं व्याख्यास्यामः — उदगयने फाल्गुन, चैत्र, वैशाख, तैष्य (पौष), ज्येष्ठ-पूत्तमम् (उत्तमम्); दक्षिणायने श्रावण-आश्वयुज-कार्तिकेषु मध्यमम्; प्रौष्ठपद-आषाढयोः अधमम्; मार्गशीर्ष-माघयोः विवर्जयेत् ।
> *athātaḥ bhagavataḥ nārāyaṇasya pratiṣṭhā-vidhiṃ vyākhyāsyāmaḥ — udagayane phālguna, caitra, vaiśākha, taiṣya (pauṣa), jyeṣṭha-pūttamam (uttamam); dakṣiṇāyane śrāvaṇa-āśvayuja-kārtikeṣu madhyamam; prauṣṭhapada-āṣāḍhayoḥ adhamam; mārgaśīrṣa-māghayoḥ vivarjayet.*
> 
> उत्तरात्रय-रोहिणी-श्रवण-पुष्य-पुनर्वसु-स्वाती-हस्त-चित्ता-अनुराधा-अश्विनीषु, शुक्ल-पक्षे पक्ष-छिद्रां विवर्ज्य, कृष्ण-पक्षे प्रथमा-द्वितीया-पञ्चमीषु, सोम-गुरु-सौम्य-काव्य-वारेषु, करणेषु विशिष्टेषु ध्रुवे पुश्चरेषु वा, विष्टिं (भद्रा) मेष-विधान्त्यक्त्वा (मेष-विधौ अन्त्यं अक्त्वा), चर-राशिं विहाय, स्थिर-राशौ, उभय-राशौ वा, श्रेष्ठं विष्णोः प्रतिष्ठां कुर्यात् ।
> *uttarātraya-rohiṇī-śravaṇa-puṣya-punarvasu-svātī-hasta-citrā-anurādhā-aśvinīṣu, śukla-pakṣe pakṣa-chidrāṃ vivarjya, kṛṣṇa-pakṣe prathamā-dvitīyā-pañcamīṣu, soma-guru-saumya-kāvya-vāreṣu, karaṇeṣu viśiṣṭeṣu dhruve puścareṣu vā, viṣṭiṃ (bhadrā) meṣa-vidhāntyaktvā, cara-rāśiṃ vihāya, sthira-rāśau, ubhaya-rāśau vā, śreṣṭhaṃ viṣṇoḥ pratiṣṭhāṃ kuryāt.*
> 
> आर्द्रा-आश्लेष-मूल-नक्षत्रेषु, कृष्ण-अष्टम्याम्, उल्का-पाते, अशनि-पाते, दिग्दाहे, उपसुलोहित-उपल-वर्षे, व्यतीपाते, मृक-पा-अतिमारुते, राज-मरणे, चन्द्र-सूर्य-उपराग-युक्तेषु ऋक्षेषु रात्रौ च परिवर्जयेत् ।
> *ārdrā-āśleṣa-mūla-nakṣatreṣu, kṛṣṇa-aṣṭamyām, ulkā-pāte, aśani-pāte, digdāhe, upasulohi­ta-upala-varṣe, vyatīpāte, mṛka-pā-atimārute, rāja-maraṇe, candra-sūrya-uparāga-yukteṣu ṛkṣeṣu rātrau ca parivarjayet.*

#### English Translation
*   Now we shall explain the rite of consecration (*pratiṣṭhā-vidhi*) of Lord Nārāyaṇa.

*   **Best months (Uttama):** During the northward course of the sun (*udagayana*) — Phālguna, Caitra, Vaiśākha, Pauṣa (Taiṣya), and Jyeṣṭha are the best.

*   **Middling months (Madhyama):** During the southward course (*dakṣiṇāyana*) — Śrāvaṇa, Āśvayuja (Āśvina), and Kārtika are middling.

*   **Inferior months (Adhama):** Prauṣṭhapada (Bhādrapada) and Āṣāḍha are inferior.

*   **Months to avoid entirely:** Mārgaśīrṣa and Māgha.

*   **Auspicious stars (Nakṣatras):** The three Uttara stars (Uttara-Phālgunī, Uttara-Āṣāḍhā, Uttara-Bhādrapadā), Rohiṇī, Śravaṇa, Puṣya, Punarvasu, Svātī, Hasta, Citrā, Anurādhā, and Aśvinī.

*   **Lunar day (Tithi):** In the bright fortnight, avoiding *pakṣa-chidrā* (the juncture days); in the dark fortnight, the 1st, 2nd, and 5th are acceptable.

*   **Weekdays:** Monday (Soma), Thursday (Guru), Wednesday (Saumya/Budha), and Friday (Kāvya/Śukra).

*   **Karaṇas:** Only the auspicious, fixed (*dhruva*), or moveable (*puścara*) ones; the *viṣṭi* (Bhadrā) karaṇa must be avoided.

*   **Zodiac sign:** Moveable (*cara*) signs should be avoided; fixed (*sthira*) or dual (*ubhaya*) signs are appropriate.

*   **Absolute prohibitions:** The stars Ārdrā, Āśleṣā, and Mūla; the 8th tithi of the dark fortnight; meteor-falls; lightning-strikes; atmospheric fire (*digdāha*); hail of blood-red stones; *vyatīpāta* yoga; violent storms; the death of a king; solar or lunar eclipses — and nighttime — are all to be avoided.

### Section 4: Thread-Release and Its Rite (Akṣayunmocanam)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **अक्ष-युन्-मोचनम् -**
> अथातः नव-बिम्बानाम् अक्ष-युन्-मोचनम् । प्रतिष्ठा-दिवसात् पूर्वस्मिन् नवमे, सप्तमे, पञ्चमे, त्र्यहे (तृतीये), द्वितीये वा अग्नि अमन्त्रकम् अक्ष-युन्-मोचनं शिल्पिना कारयित्वा पञ्चान्त-समन्त्रकं च कुर्यात्; अर्क-राहु-सौरीणाम् उदयम् अन्तक्त्वा (हित्वा) द्वि-लोचने नक्षत्रे अक्ष-युन्-मोचनं कारयेत् ।
> ***akṣa-yun-mocanam -***
> *athātaḥ nava-bimbānām akṣa-yun-mocanam. pratiṣṭhā-divasāt pūrvasmin navame, saptame, pañcame, tryahe (tṛtīye), dvitīye vā agni amantrakam akṣa-yun-mocanaṃ śilpinā kārayitvā pañcānta-samantrakaṃ ca kuryāt; arka-rāhu-saurīṇām udayam antaktvā (hitvā) dvi-locane nakṣatre akṣa-yun-mocanaṃ kārayet.*

#### English Translation
*   Now, the thread-release (*akṣa-yun-mocana*) of the new images (*nava-bimba*) is described.

*   **Timing:** On the 9th, 7th, 5th, 3rd, or 2nd day before the day of consecration.

*   **Procedure:** The sculptor (*śilpin*) should first perform the thread-release without mantras (*amantrakam*); then the five-fold thread-release with mantras (*pañcānta-samantrakam*) should be done.

*   **Astronomical prohibition:** Avoiding the rising of the Sun (Arka), Rāhu, and Saturn (Sauri), the thread-release should be performed under a two-eyed (*dvi-locana*) asterism.

### Section 6: The Officiant's Worship with Vessels, Ornaments, and Offerings (Ācārya-sragvalya-alaṅkāra)
