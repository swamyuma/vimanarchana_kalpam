# Vimanarchana Kalpa of Sage Marīci (Vaikhānasa Āgama)
## Chapter 16: Śilā-Bera-Dhruvārcā-Bera-Sthāpana-Vidhiḥ (Rules for the Consecration and Installation of Stone and Immovable Deity Icons)

This document contains the Sanskrit text, IAST transliteration, and English translation of the 16th Chapter (*Ṣoḍaśaḥ Paṭalaḥ*) of the **Vimānārcanākalpa** (attributed to Sage Marīci, representing the Vaikhānasa school of Vaishnavism).

This chapter discusses the rules for selecting auspicious times for consecration, the preliminary water-immersion (*jalādhivāsa*) of the icon, the construction of the sacrificial pavilion (*yāgaśālā*) and the couch (*śayyā-vedī*), the couching ceremony (*śayanādhivāsa*), the consecration of the main immovable icon (*dhruva-bera*), the transfer of divine energy from the water-jar (*kumbha-āvāhana*), and the detailed preparation of the *Aṣṭabandha* (eight-ingredient) bonding cement.

---

### Section 1: Auspicious Times and Preliminary Water Immersion (Pratiṣṭhā-kāla-jalādhivāsa-vidhiḥ)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> अथ शिला-बेर-स्थापन-विधिं वक्ष्ये -
> ***atha ṣoḍaśaḥ paṭalaḥ***
> *atha śilā-bera-sthāpana-vidhiṃ vakṣye -*
> 
> उत्तरायणे मार्गशीर्ष-माघौ विवर्ज्य, शुक्लपक्षे, कृष्णे (कृष्णपक्षे) अन्त्य-त्रिभागं हित्वा; श्रवण-रोहिणी-श्रविष्ठा-हस्त-अनुराधा-रेवती-त्र्युत्तरासु, च अन्यस्मिन् सुप्रशस्ते कर्तुः अनुकूले नक्षत्रे, स्थिरे शुभे राशौ प्रतिष्ठां कुर्यात्।
> *uttarāyaṇe mārgaśīrṣa-māghau vivarjya, śuklapakṣe, kṛṣṇe (kṛṣṇapakṣe) antya-tribhāgaṃ hitvā; śravaṇa-rohiṇī-śraviṣṭhā-hasta-anurādhā-revatī-tryuttarāsu, ca anyasmin supraśaste kartuḥ anukūle nakṣatre, sthire śubhe rāśau pratiṣṭhāṃ kuryāt.*
> 
> तद्-दिनात् पूर्वम् अङ्कुरान् अर्पयित्वा, सम्भारान् आहृत्य, तद्-बेर-शुद्ध्यर्थम् आलय-अभिमुखे उत्तरे वा, बिम्ब-अर्ध-अधिक-मानेन अवटं बिम्ब-दध्नं (बिम्ब-प्रमाण-गम्भीरं) कृत्वा; तस्मिन् जले, गव्ये, कुशोदके च, प्रत्येकं दिनत्रयम्, एकाहं, यथा-सम्भव-कालं वा, अधिवासं कारयेत्। बिम्बं महत् चेत्, कुम्भ-जलैः "आपो हि ष्ठा", "हिरण्यवर्णाः", "पवमानः" इति मन्त्रैः अभिषेचयेत्।
> *tad-dināt pūrvam aṅkurān arpayitvā, sambhārān āhṛtya, tad-bera-śuddhyartham ālaya-abhimukhe uttare vā, bimba-ardha-adhika-mānena avaṭaṃ bimba-dadhnaṃ kṛtvā; tasmin jale, gavye, kuśodake ca, pratyekaṃ dinatrayam, ekāhaṃ, yathā-sambhava-kālaṃ vā, adhivāsaṃ kārayet. bimbaṃ mahat cet, kumbha-jalaiḥ "āpo hi ṣṭhā", "hiraṇyavarṇāḥ", "pavamānaḥ" iti mantraiḥ abhiṣecayet.*

#### English Translation
*   **Rules for Consecrating Stone Icons (*Śilā-bera-sthāpana-vidhiḥ*):**
    *   Now, I shall describe the procedure for installing the stone deity icon (*śilā-bera*).
    *   **Auspicious Times (*Pratiṣṭhā-kālaḥ*):** Perform the installation ceremony during the Northern solstice (*uttarāyaṇe*), excluding the months of Mārgaśīrṣa (November-December) and Māgha (January-February). It should be performed during the bright fortnight (*śuklapakṣe*), or during the dark fortnight (*kṛṣṇapakṣe*) excluding its final third part (the last five days). The chosen asterisms should be Śravaṇa, Rohiṇī, Śraviṣṭhā (Dhaniṣṭhā), Hasta, Anurādhā, Revatī, or the three Uttaras (Uttarāṣāḍhā, Uttaraphalgunī, Uttarabhādrapadā), or any other highly auspicious nakṣatra favorable to the patron (*kartuḥ anukūle*), under a stable (*sthira*) and benefic sign of the zodiac (*śubhe rāśau*).
    *   **Water Immersion Ritual (*Jalādhivāsaḥ*):**
        *   Prior to the consecration day, offer the fresh sprouts (*aṅkura-arpaṇam*), gather all necessary ritual materials (*sambhārān*), and for the purification of the icon (*bera-śuddhi*), dig a pit (*avaṭam*) in front of the temple or to its North. The pit's dimensions should exceed half the size of the icon, and its depth should be equal to the height of the icon (*bimba-dadhnam*).
        *   Immerse the icon in this pit filled successively with water (*jale*), cow products (*gavye* - milk, curd, ghee), and kuśa-grass water (*kuśodake*), for three days, one day, or whatever duration is practically feasible, to establish its preliminary residence (*adhivāsa*).
        *   If the icon is very large (*mahat*), instead of complete immersion, bathe it with water from consecrated jars (*kumbha-jalaiḥ*) while chanting the sacred Vedic hymns: "Āpo hi ṣṭhā" (O Waters, indeed you are), "Hiraṇyavarṇāḥ" (Golden-colored waters), and the "Pavamāna-sūktas" (Purification hymns).

### Section 2: Construction of the Ritual Pavilion and Couching of the Lord (Yāgaśālā-śayana-adhivāsa-vidhiḥ)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> आलय-अभिमुखे दक्षिणे वा यागशालां षोडश-स्तम्भ-संयुक्तां प्रपां (पण्डालं) वा कृत्वा, कूटं वा कृत्वा, तोरण-आद्यैः अलङ्कृत्य; तन्मध्ये शय्यावेदिं चतुरश्रां तद्-भित्ति-सम-विस्तारं तद्-तुरीयांश-उत्सेधाम्, अथवा बिम्ब-अर्ध-अधिक-आयामां यथा-लाभ-उत्सेधां वेदिं कृत्वा;
> *ālaya-abhimukhe dakṣiṇe vā yāgaśālāṃ ṣoḍaśa-stambha-saṃyuktāṃ prapāṃ vā kṛtvā, kūṭaṃ vā kṛtvā, toraṇa-ādyaiḥ alaṅkṛtya; tanmadhye śayyāvediṃ caturaśrāṃ tad-bhitti-sama-vistāraṃ tad-turīyāṃśa-utsedhām, athavā bimba-ardha-adhika-āyāmāṃ yathā-lābha-utsedhāṃ vediṃ kṛtvā;*
> 
> तत्र प्राच्यां सभ्याग्नि-कुण्डं, तस्य उत्तरे स्नानवेदिं च कृत्वा; आलयाद् उत्तरे वास्तुहोमं हुत्वा, आलयं यागशालां च पर्यग्नि-पञ्चगव्याभ्यां शोधयित्वा; सभ्याग्नौ आधारं हुत्वा, स्नानवेद्यां देवं संस्थाप्य;
> *tatra prācyāṃ sabhyāgni-kuṇḍaṃ, tasya uttare snānavediṃ ca kṛtvā; ālayād uttare vāstuhomaṃ hutvā, ālayaṃ yāgasthānaṃ ca paryagni-pañcagavyābhyāṃ śodhayitvā; sabhyāgnau ādhāraṃ hutvā, snānavēdyāṃ devaṃ saṃsthāpya;*
> 
> तस्य अभिमुखे व्रीहिभिः दण्डवत्-पङ्क्तिं कृत्वा, पञ्चगव्यादि-द्वादश-द्रव्य-सम्पूर्णान् कलशान् सोपस्नान् (वस्त्र-सहितान्) विन्यस्य, विधिना संस्नाप्य, वस्त्र-आद्यैः अलङ्कृत्य, प्रतिसरम् (कङ्कणं) आबध्य;
> *tasya abhimukhe vrīhibhiḥ daṇḍavat-paṅktiṃ kṛtvā, pañcagavyādi-dvādaśa-dravya-sampūrṇān kalaśān sopasnān vinyasya, vidhinā saṃsnāpya, vastra-ādyaiḥ alaṅkṛtya, pratisaram ābadhya;*
> 
> शय्यावेद्यां धान्योपरि पञ्चशयनानि पञ्चवस्त्राणि वा आस्तीर्य, देवीभ्यां (श्री-भूमिभ्यां) सह देवेशं शाययेत्। मुनि-आदीनां स्थापनं यदि, प्रत्येकं स्नापन-शयन-आदीनि कारयेत्।
> *śayyāvedyāṃ dhānyopari pañcaśayanāni pañcavastrāṇi vā āstīrya, devībhyāṃ saha deveśaṃ śāyayet. muni-ādīnaṃ sthāpanaṃ yadi, pratyekaṃ snāpana-śayana-ādīni kārayet.*

#### English Translation
*   **The Ritual Pavilion and Deity Preparation (*Yāgaśālā-śayana-adhivāsa-vidhiḥ*):**
    *   **Ritual Pavilion (*Yāgaśālā*):** In front of the temple or to its South, construct a sacrificial pavilion (*yāgaśālā*) supported by sixteen pillars, or build a temporary canopy (*prapā*) or a hall (*kūṭa*), and decorate it beautifully with arches (*toraṇa*), banners, and garlands.
    *   **The Couch Platform (*Śayyā-vedī*):** In the center of the pavilion, construct a square couch-platform (*śayyā-vedī*). Its width should be equal to the thickness of the pavilion walls, and its height should be one-fourth of that width. Alternatively, its length can exceed the height of the icon by more than a half, with its height adjusted as per availability.
    *   **Sacrificial Fire-Pit and Bathing Altar:** To the East of this platform, dig the sacrificial fire-pit (*sabhyāgni-kuṇḍam*), and to its North, construct a bathing altar (*snāna-vedi*).
    *   **Purification of the Temple:** To the North of the temple building, perform the *vāstu-homa* (earth-consecration fire offering). Purify the temple building and the sacrificial pavilion using fire circumambulation (*paryagni*) and by sprinkling *pañcagavya*. Make initial offerings (*ādhāram*) into the *sabhyāgni* fire.
    *   **Bathing the Deity:** Place the deity icon on the bathing altar. In front of the icon, arrange grains of unhusked rice (*vrīhibhiḥ*) in a straight line resembling a staff (*daṇḍavat-paṅktim*). On this arrangement, place jars (*kalaśān*) filled with twelve consecrated substances—beginning with the five products of the cow (*pañcagavya*)—wrapped with towels/sacred cloths (*sopasnān*). Bathe the deity according to scriptural rules (*vidhinā saṃsnāpya*), adorn it with garments and ornaments, and tie the protective wrist-thread (*pratisaram*).
    *   **The Couch Ceremony (*Śayanādhivāsaḥ*):** Spread five mattresses (*pañca-śayanāni*) or five cloths (*pañca-vastrāṇi*) over a bed of grains on the couch platform. Lay the Lord (*deveśam*) down to rest on this couch, flanked by His two consorts (*devībhyām* - Śrīdevī and Bhūdevī). If retinue deities or sages (*muni-ādīnaṃ*) are also being installed, perform separate bathing and couching ceremonies for each of them.

### Section 3: Fire Offerings, Night Vigil, and Placement in the Sanctum (Homa-garbhagṛha-praveśa-vidhiḥ)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> यतो द्वारं ततो मौलिं कृत्वा, उत्तराच्छादनं कुर्यात्।
> *yato dvāraṃ tato mauliṃ kṛtvā, uttarācchādanaṃ kuryāt.*
> 
> बेरे महति, जलाधिवासं हित्वा; यागस्थान-मध्ये शय्यावेदिं विना, तत्-कालात् पूर्वे बेरम् आनीय; शुद्धोदकैः पवमानाद्यैः संस्नाप्य, अभ्यर्च्य, पश्चाद् वास्तुशुद्धिं कृत्वा, प्रधानहोमं कलश-स्नापनं च कारयेत्।
> *bere mahati, jalādhivāsaṃ hitvā; yāgasthāna-madhye śayyāvediṃ vinā, tat-kālāt pūrve beram ānīya; śuddhōdakaiḥ pavamānādyaiḥ saṃsnāpya, abhyarcya, paścād vāstuśuddhiṃ kṛtvā, pradhānahomaṃ kalaśa-snāpanaṃ ca kārayet.*
> 
> अभिमुखे धान्य-वेद्युपरि शयनानि आस्तीर्य, प्रतिसरं बद्ध्वा, देवेशं "शयने शेते" इति स्मृत्वा, उत्तराच्छादनैः बिम्बं समाच्छाद्य, अधिवास्य; अग्निं परिस्तीर्य, हौत्रं प्रशंस्य, आवाहनादि-जुष्टाकार-स्वाहाकारान् कृत्वा; पुरुषसूक्तं, वैष्णवम्, एकाक्षरादि-सूक्तं, महीसूक्तं, ब्राह्मं, मैन्द्रं, सर्वदैवत्यं च हुत्वा, रात्रिशेषं व्यपोह्य;
> *abhimukhe dhānya-vedyupari śayanāni āstīrya, pratisaraṃ baddhvā, deveśam "śayane śete" iti smṛtvā, uttarācchādanaiḥ bimbaṃ samācchādya, adhivāsya; agniṃ paristīrya, hautraṃ praśaṃsya, āvāhanādi-juṣṭākāra-svāhākārān kṛtvā; puruṣasūktaṃ, vaiṣṇavam, ekākṣarādi-sūktaṃ, mahīsūktaṃ, brāhmaṃ, maindraṃ, sarvadaivatyaṃ ca hutvā, rātriśeṣaṃ vyapohya;*
> 
> प्रभाते स्नात्वा, आलयम् आवेश्य, शयनस्थं देवम् आदाय, अष्टोपचारैः अभ्यर्च्य; पदविभागं कृत्वा, तद्-विहित-पदे रत्नादीनि विन्यस्य; पश्चाद् अग्निं विसृज्य, बेरं गर्भालायं नीत्वा, "प्र तद् विष्णुः" इति मन्त्रेण बेरं संस्थाप्य; विष्णुसूक्तं, पुरुषसूक्तं, वैष्णवं, ध्रुवसूक्तं च जप्त्वा, बेरस्य पादं स्पृशेत्। देव्यादीन् तत्तन्-मन्त्रम् उच्चार्य तत्तत्-स्थाने प्रतिष्ठापयेत्।
> *prabhāte snātvā, ālayam āveśya, śayanasthaṃ devam ādāya, aṣṭopacāraiḥ abhyarcya; padavibhāgaṃ kṛtvā, tad-vihita-pade ratnādīni vinyasya; paścād agniṃ visṛjya, beraṃ garbhālayaṃ nītvā, "pra tad viṣṇuḥ" iti mantreṇa beraṃ saṃsthāpya; viṣṇusūktaṃ, puruṣasūktaṃ, vaiṣṇavaṃ, dhruvasūktaṃ ca japtvā, berasya pādaṃ spṛśet. devyādīn tattan-mantram uccārya tattat-sthāne pratiṣṭhāpayet.*

#### English Translation
*   **Offerings, Vigil, and Sanctum Placement (*Homa-garbhagṛha-praveśa-vidhiḥ*):**
    *   **Positioning on the Couch:** Place the deity's head (*mauliḥ*) in the direction of the doorway, and cover the upper portion of the icon with a sacred covering (*uttarācchādanam*).
    *   **Procedure for Large Icons:** If the icon is exceptionally large (*mahati*), omit the water-immersion ritual (*jalādhivāsa*). Do not construct a couch-platform in the middle of the sacrificial pavilion; instead, bring the icon to the pavilion just prior to the consecration time, bathe it with pure water and *pavamāna* hymns, worship it, perform the *vāstu-śuddhi* (space purification), and execute the primary fire sacrifice (*pradhāna-homa*) and jar-consecration bath (*kalaśa-snāpanam*).
    *   **Consecration Rites and Vigil:**
        *   In front of the deity, spread mattresses on an altar of grains, tie the protective wrist-band, meditate on the Lord with the phrase "He rests upon His couch" (*śayane śete*), cover the icon with upper cloths, and perform the night vigil (*adhivāsa*).
        *   Spread the sacrificial fire, praise the Hotṛ priest's duties (*hautram*), perform the invocation (*āvāhana*), and offer oblations with the *juṣṭākāra* and *svāhākāra* sounds.
        *   Offer oblations while chanting the *Puruṣa-sūkta*, *Vaiṣṇava-sūktas*, the single-syllable *Ekākṣara-sūkta*, *Mahī-sūkta*, *Brāhma-sūkta*, *Maindra-sūkta*, and the hymn for all deities (*sarvadaivatya-homa*), keeping vigil through the remainder of the night (*rātri-śeṣam vyapohya*).
    *   **Placement in the Sanctum:** In the morning, bathe, enter the temple, worship the resting Lord with the eight forms of hospitality (*aṣṭopacāraiḥ*), divide the sanctum floor into grids, and place the gems and precious items (*ratnādīni*) in the designated square. Dismiss the sacrificial fires, carry the deity icon into the inner sanctum (*garbhālayam*), and establish the icon in its place while chanting the mantra: "Pra tad viṣṇuḥ stavate..." (Thus is Viṣṇu praised). Recite the *Viṣṇu-sūkta*, *Puruṣa-sūkta*, *Vaiṣṇava-sūkta*, and *Dhruva-sūkta*, and touch the feet of the icon (*berasya pādaṃ spṛśet*). Consecrate and install the consort Goddesses and other subsidiary deities in their respective stations by chanting their specific mantras.

### Section 4: Prohibitions, Materials, and Visual Alignment of the Icon (Bera-lakṣaṇa-niyama-vidhiḥ)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> शिल्पिना बेरं संस्थाप्य, पश्चान् मन्त्रेण स्थापनं कुर्यात् इति एके (इत्येके)।
> *śilpinā beraṃ saṃsthāpya, paścān mantreṇa sthāpanaṃ kuryāt iti eke.*
> 
> बेरे महति अल्पे वा, अधिष्ठान-अवसाने (तल-पूरणावसाने) बेरं संस्थाप्य, पश्चाद् विमानं (गर्भालय-शिखरं) कारयेत्।
> *bere mahati alpe vā, adhiṣṭhāna-avasāne beraṃ saṃsthāpya, paścād vimānaṃ kārayet.*
> 
> शिला-बेरं, ताम्रजं वा, अथ ऐष्टकं (इष्टकामयं), शैलं (शिलमयम्), मृण्मयं, दारवं (काष्ठमयम्) वा संस्थापयेत्। मृण्मये, तृणाच्छन्ने (पर्णशालामये) च, एक-बेरम् एव संस्थापयेत्।
> *śilā-beraṃ, tāmrajaṃ vā, atha aiṣṭakaṃ, śailaṃ, mṛṇmayaṃ, dāravaṃ vā saṃsthāpayet. mṛṇmaye, tṛṇācchanne ca, eka-beram eva saṃsthāpayet.*
> 
> ऊर्ध्व-दृष्टिम्, अधो-दृष्टिं, तिर्यग्-ईक्षणं च युक्तं बेरं न एव कारयेत् (नैव बेरम्)। सम-दृष्टिं (बेरं) संस्थाप्य, पश्चाच् शिल्पिना घटी-शर्करा-आदीनि (घटी-शर्करा-लेपन-आदिकं) कारयेत्।
> *ūrdhva-dṛṣṭim, adho-dṛṣṭiṃ, tiryag-īkṣaṇaṃ ca yuktaṃ beraṃ na eva kārayet. sama-dṛṣṭiṃ saṃsthāpya, paścāc śilpinā ghaṭī-śarkarā-ādīni kārayet.*

#### English Translation
*   **Rules for Positioning, Materials, and Eye Alignment (*Bera-lakṣaṇa-niyama-vidhiḥ*):**
    *   **Positioning Order:** Some authorities hold that the architect should physically position the icon first, and then the priest should perform the spiritual installation using mantras. Whether the icon is large or small, install it upon the completion of the basement moldings (*adhiṣṭhāna*), and construct the superstructure (*vimāna*) of the sanctum only after the icon is established.
    *   **Materials for the Icon:** The main icon may be made of stone (*śilā*), copper (*tāmra*), brick and plaster (*aiṣṭaka*), stone masonry (*śaila*), clay (*mṛṇmaya*), or wood (*dārava*). In a temple where the main deity is made of clay (*mṛṇmaye*), or in a simple thatched-roof shrine (*tṛṇācchanne*), install only a single icon (*eka-beram*) without retinue deities.
    *   **Gaze of the Deity (*Dṛṣṭi-lakṣaṇam*):** Do not carve an icon with an upward gaze (*ūrdhva-dṛṣṭi*), a downward gaze (*adho-dṛṣṭi*), or a sideways/slanting squint (*tiryag-īkṣaṇam*). Establish the icon with a straight, balanced, and serene gaze (*sama-dṛṣṭi*). Only after the icon is securely positioned should the architect apply the *ghaṭī-śarkarā* bonding paste (lime-gravel plaster) and other finishing touches.

### Section 5: Detailed Consecration and Concluding Rituals for the Main Icon (Dhruvārcā-sthāpana-vidhiḥ)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> अथ ध्रुवार्चा-स्थापन-विधिं वक्ष्ये -
> *atha dhruvārcā-sthāpana-vidhiṃ vakṣye -*
> 
> बेरं शिल्पिभिः निरपेक्षम् एव कारयित्वा, यथोक्तम् अङ्कुरादीन् अर्पयित्वा; कौतुकस्य उक्त-विधिना अक्षि-मोचनं कृत्वा, पञ्चगव्य-अधिवासं कृत्वा; रात्रौ आलयस्य उत्तरे वास्तुहोमं हुत्वा, पर्यग्नि-पञ्चगव्याभ्यां आलयं यागस्थानं च संशोध्य, पुण्याहं वाचयित्वा;
> *beraṃ śilpibhiḥ nirapekṣam eva kārayitvā, yathoktam aṅkurādīn arpayitvā; kautukasya ukta-vidhinā akṣi-mocanaṃ kṛtvā, pañcagavya-adhivāsaṃ kṛtvā; rātrau ālayasya uttare vāstuhomaṃ hutvā, paryagni-pañcagavyābhyām ālayaṃ yāgasthānaṃ ca saṃśodhya, puṇyāhaṃ vācayitvā;*
> 
> यागस्थानस्य मध्ये शय्यावेदिं, परितः च अग्निकुण्डानि कृत्वा; अधिवास-गत-देवम् आसाद्य, अभिषिच्य, वस्त्र-उत्तरीय-आद्यैः अलङ्कृत्य, अभ्यर्च्य, आलयम् आवेश्य;
> *yāgasthānasa madhye śayyāvediṃ, paritaś ca agnikuṇḍāni kṛtvā; adhivāsa-gata-devam āsādya, abhiṣicya, vastra-uttarīya-ādyaiḥ alaṅkṛtya, abhyarcya, ālayam āveśya;*
> 
> यज्ञालये स्नान-श्वभ्रस्य पश्चिमे धान्य-राशौ संस्थाप्य; सर्व-होमेषु आधारं हुत्वा, देवस्य अभिमुखे यथोक्तेन विधिना कुम्भ-पूजां कृत्वा, तस्मिन् देवम् आवाह्य, सम-कलशैः कुम्भं समभ्युक्ष्य; पश्चाद् बेरं संस्नाप्य, वस्त्र-उत्तरीय-आद्यैः अलङ्कृत्य, अभ्यर्च्य;
> *yajñālaye snāna-śvabhrasya paścime dhānya-rāśau saṃsthāpya; sarva-homeṣu ādhāraṃ hutvā, devasya abhimukhe yathoktena vidhinā kumbha-pūjāṃ kṛtvā, tasmin devam āvāhya, sama-kalaśaiḥ kumbhaṃ samabhyukṣya; paścād beraṃ saṃsnāpya, vastra-uttarīya-ādyaiḥ alaṅkṛtya, abhyarcya;*
> 
> पुण्याह-अन्ते प्रतिसरं बद्ध्वा; शय्यावेद्यां धान्योपरि अण्डजादीनि (कम्बल-आदीनि) पञ्चशयनानि पञ्चवस्त्राणि वा आस्तीर्य, कुम्भं बिम्बं च आरोप्य, विधिना शाययेत्।
> *puṇyāha-ante pratisaraṃ baddhvā; śayyāvedyāṃ dhānyopari aṇṇḍajādīni pañcaśayanāni pañcavastrāṇi vā āstīrya, kumbhaṃ bimbaṃ ca āropya, vidhinā śāyayet.*

> *yadi syād autsavaṃ baliberaṃ ca, devena saha akṣi-mocanaṃ kṛtvā, pṛthag eva adhivāsanaṃ snapanaṃ ca kṛtvā, pratisaraṃ baddhvā, tad-vedyāṃ pratyekaṃ śayanāni āstīrya, tad-devaṃ śāyayet.*
> 
> सभ्याग्नौ होत्रं प्रशंस्याऽऽवाहनादीनि कृत्वा, चतुर्दिक्षु चतुर्वेद-अध्ययनं कारयित्वा, सर्व-होमेषु यथोक्तं जुहुयात्।
> *sabhyāgnau hautraṃ praśaṃsya āvāhanādīni kṛtvā, caturdikṣu caturveda-adhyayanaṃ kārayitvā, sarva-homeṣu yathoktaṃ juhuyāt.*

#### English Translation
*   **Consecration of the Main Immovable Icon (*Dhruvārcā-sthāpana-vidhiḥ*):**
    *   **Preparation:** Carve the icon through the sculptors without any deficiencies, and perform the sprout offering (*aṅkura-arpaṇam*). Perform the eye-opening ceremony (*akṣi-mocanam*) for the festival icon (*kautuka*) according to scriptural rules, and carry out the five-day immersion in cow products (*pañcagavyādhivāsa*). At night, perform the *vāstu-homa* to the North of the temple, purify both the temple building and the sacrificial pavilion using fire circumambulation and *pañcagavya*, and perform the purificatory declaration (*puṇyāha-vācanam*).
    *   **Ritual Arena Setup:** In the center of the sacrificial arena, construct the couch platform (*śayyā-vedī*), and surround it with the designated fire pits (*agnikuṇḍāni*). Bring the deity icon from its temporary residence, bathe it, adorn it with garments and upper cloths, worship it, and enter the temple.
    *   **Bathing and Kumbha Consecration:** Place the deity on a heap of grains to the West of the bathing drain/pit (*snāna-śvabhram*) in the sacrificial hall. Perform the preliminary offerings (*ādhāra-homa*) in all fire pits. In front of the deity, worship the primary water-jar (*kumbha-pūjā*), invoke the Lord's presence into it, and sprinkle the jar with water from the secondary vessels (*sama-kalaśaiḥ*). Then, bathe the deity icon, adorn it with garments and upper cloths, and offer worship.
    *   **The Couch Vigil for Main and Retinue Icons:**
        *   At the conclusion of the purification rite, tie the protective wrist-thread (*pratisara*). Spread five layers of bedding—beginning with soft woolen blankets (*aṇḍajādīni*)—or five cloths over grains on the couch platform. Lay both the consecrated water-jar (*kumbha*) and the deity icon (*bimba*) onto this couch according to the rules.
        *   If the festival icon (*utsava-bera*) and the offering icon (*bali-bera*) are also being installed, perform the eye-opening ceremony for them alongside the main deity. Conduct their purification immersion and bathing separately, tie their protective wrist-bands, spread separate bedding for each on the altar, and lay them to rest.
    *   **Vigil Offerings:** Praise the priest's duties (*hautram*) in the *sabhyāgni fire, perform the invocation rituals, arrange for the recitation of the four Vedas in the four cardinal directions, and offer the prescribed oblations (*juhuyāt*) in all fire pits.

### Section 6: Concluding Consecration, Aṣṭabandha Binding, and Power Infusion (Aṣṭabandha-bandhana-śaktyāvāhana-vidhiḥ)

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> रात्रिशेषं व्यपोह्य; प्रभाते स्नात्वा, आलयम् आविश्य, देवं प्रणम्य, उत्थाप्य, आराध्य; गर्भालाय-पदविभागं कृत्वा, यथोक्ते पदे पीठे रत्नादीनि न्यस्य; यजमानः आचार्यादिभ्यो दक्षिणां दत्त्वा, पूर्णाहूतीः जुहुयात्।
> *rātriśeṣaṃ vyapohya; prabhāte snātvā, ālayam āviśya, devaṃ praṇamya, utthāpya, ārādhya; garbhālaya-padavibhāgaṃ kṛtvā, yathokte pade pīṭhe ratnādīni nyasya; yajamānaḥ ācāryādibhyo dakṣiṇāṃ dattvā, pūrṇāhūtīḥ juhuyāt.*
> 
> पश्चात् (शुभ)मुहूर्ते प्राप्ते, देवेशं प्रणम्य, अनुमान्य (अनुज्ञां प्राप्य); कुम्भं विना देवं गर्भालायम् आनीय, रत्नोपरि विष्णुसूक्तं पुरुषसूक्तं ध्रुवसूक्तं वैष्णवं च जप्त्वा; "प्र तद् विष्णुः" इति देवं संस्थाप्य, पश्चाद् अष्टबन्धं प्रयोजयेत्।
> *paścāt muhūrte prāpte, deveśaṃ praṇamya, anumānya; kumbhaṃ vinā devaṃ garbhālayam ānīya, ratnopari viṣṇusūktaṃ puruṣasūktaṃ dhruvasūktaṃ vaiṣṇavaṃ ca japtvā; "pra tad viṣṇuḥ" iti devaṃ saṃsthāpya, paścād aṣṭabandhaṃ prayojayet.*
> 
> **अष्टबन्ध-प्रकारः -**
> लाक्षा, गुडः, मधूच्छिष्टं, गुग्गुलुः च इति एतान् परस्परं समभागं कृत्वा; एषां द्विगुणं सज्जरसं, गैरिकं, घनचूर्णम् (शङ्खचूर्णादिकं) अर्धं, सर्वेषाम् अर्धं तैलं च संयोज्य; लोहपात्रे निक्षिप्य, लोहदर्व्या समेनाग्निना (मन्दतापयुक्त-वह्निना) सुपक्वं पचेत्; तद् अदग्धं (अदग्धदशायाम् एव) मानीय, तत्-पीठ-रन्ध्रे प्रक्षिपेत्; तद् दृढं दृषत्-समं (पाषाणवद् कठिनं) भवति।
> ***aṣṭabandha-prakāraḥ -***
> *lākṣā, guḍaḥ, madhūcchiṣṭaṃ, gugguluḥ ca iti etān parasparaṃ samabhāgaṃ kṛtvā; eṣāṃ dviguṇaṃ sajjarasaṃ, gairikaṃ, ghanacūrṇam ardhaṃ, sarveṣām ardhaṃ tailaṃ ca saṃyojya; lohapātre nikṣipya, lohadarvyā samenāgninā supakvaṃ pacet; tad adagdham ānīya, tat-pīṭha-randhre prakṣipet; tad dṛḍhaṃ dṛṣat-samaṃ bhavati.*
> 
> तस्य ध्रुवबेर-उक्त-शुद्धिं कृत्वा; पश्चाद् आचार्यः शय्यावेदिम् आसाद्य, कुम्भं संगृह्य शिरसा धारयन् अग्रतो गच्छेत्; स्थापकाः औत्सवं बलिबेरं च आदाय अनुगच्छेयुः; आलयं प्रदक्षिणीकृत्य अभ्यन्तरं प्रविश्य, देव-अभिमुखे धान्योपरि कुम्भं सन्यस्य, मुखमण्डपे औत्सवं बलिबेरं च प्रतिष्ठापयेत्।
> *tasya dhruvabera-ukta-śuddhiṃ kṛtvā; paścād ācāryaḥ śayyāvedim āsādya, kumbhaṃ saṃgṛhya śirasā dhārayan agrato gacchet; sthāpakāḥ autsavaṃ baliberaṃ ca ādāya anugaccheyuḥ; ālayaṃ pradakṣiṇīkṛtya abhyantaraṃ praviśya, deva-abhimukhe dhānyopari kumbhaṃ sanyasya, mukhamaṇḍape autsavaṃ baliberaṃ ca pratiṣṭhāpayet.*
> 
> आचार्यः च आत्मसूक्तं जप्त्वा, मन्त्रन्यासम् अक्षरन्यासं च कृत्वा; समाहितः उत्तराभिमुखो भूत्वा देवं ध्यायन्, "इदं विष्णुः" इति कुम्भस्थां शक्तिम् आदाय, "आयातु भगवान्" इति बिम्बस्य मूर्ध्नि "विष्णुम् आवाहयामी" इति सङ्ख्याप्य (इति उच्चार्य) आवाहयेत्।
> *ācāryaḥ ca ātmasūktaṃ japtvā, mantranyāsam akṣaranyāsaṃ ca kṛtvā; samāhitaḥ uttarābhimukho bhūtvā devaṃ dhyāyan, "idaṃ viṣṇuḥ" iti kumbhasthāṃ śaktim ādāya, "āyātu bhagavān" iti bimbasya mūrdhni "viṣṇum āvāhayāmi" iti saṅkhyāpya āvāhayet.*
> 
> बेरं परितः पुरुषादि-आवरण-त्रय-देवान्, मित्रादि-आवरण-त्रय-देवान् च आवाह्य, "अस्त्वेतदासनम्" इति आसनं दत्त्वा; पश्चाद् औत्सवे बलिबेरे च तस्मात् (ध्रुवबेरात्) दीपात् दीपम् इव आवाहयेत्। ध्रुवार्चा-बेरम् आवाहन-विसर्गाभ्यां विना आसनाद्यैः अर्चयेत्। एष विशेषः, अन्यत् सर्वं समानम् इति विज्ञायते॥
> *beraṃ paritaḥ puruṣādi-āvaraṇa-traya-devān, mitrādi-āvaraṇa-traya-devān ca āvāhya, "astvetadāsanam" iti āsanaṃ dattvā; paścād autsave balibere ca tasmāt dīpāt dīpam iva āvāhayet. dhruvārcā-beram āvāhana-visargābhyāṃ vinā āsanādyaiḥ arcayet. eṣa viśeṣaḥ, anyat sarvaṃ samānam iti vijñāyate.*

#### English Translation
*   **The Final Installation, Aṣṭabandha Paste, and Divine Infusion (*Aṣṭabandha-bandhana-śaktyāvāhana-vidhiḥ*):**
    *   **Morning Rituals:** At daybreak, after completing the night vigil, bathe and enter the temple. Bow down to the sleeping Lord, awaken Him, offer worship, and lay out the spatial grid divisions (*padavibhāga*) of the inner sanctum. Place the gems (*ratnādīni*) in the designated squares of the pedestal (*pīṭhe*). The patron (*yajamānaḥ*) must then distribute sacrificial fees (*dakṣiṇā*) to the preceptor (*ācārya*) and priests, and offer the final oblation (*pūrṇāhūti*) into the fire.
    *   **Final Installation of the Icon:** At the arrival of the auspicious astrologically determined hour (*muhūrte*), bow to the Lord and seek His leave. Bring the icon (excluding the *kumbha* water jar) into the inner sanctum. Over the gem-deposit, recite the *Viṣṇu-sūkta*, *Puruṣa-sūkta*, *Dhruva-sūkta*, and *Vaiṣṇava-sūkta*. Establish the deity firmly in place chanting "Pra tad viṣṇuḥ...". Following this, apply the *Aṣṭabandha* clay cement.
    *   **Preparation of the Aṣṭabandha (Eight-ingredient bonding cement):**
        *   Take equal parts of lacquer (*lākṣā*), jaggery (*guḍa*), beeswax (*madhūcchiṣṭa*), and bdellium resin (*guggulu*).
        *   Add twice the total quantity of sal-tree resin (*sajjarasa*), red ochre (*gairika*), and half the quantity of fine shell lime (*ghanacūrṇam*).
        *   Mix in oil (*tailam*) equal to half the volume of all other ingredients.
        *   Place the mixture in an iron pot (*lohapātre*) and cook it thoroughly with an iron ladle (*lohadarvyā*) over moderate, even heat (*samenāgninā*).
        *   Without letting it burn or char (*adagdham*), pour the hot paste into the gaps/crevices of the pedestal (*pīṭha-randhre*). Once cooled, it binds tightly, becoming hard as stone (*dṛṣat-samam*).
    *   **Infusion of Divine Power (*Śaktyāvāhanam*):**
        *   Perform the prescribed purifications (*śuddhi*) of the main immovable icon. The preceptor (*ācārya*) then goes to the couch platform, lifts the main consecrated water-jar (*kumbha*), and carries it on his head, walking at the front of the procession. The assisting priests follow behind, carrying the festival icon (*autsava-bera*) and the offering icon (*bali-bera*).
        *   Circumambulate the temple, enter the inner sanctum, and place the main *kumbha* on a bed of grains directly facing the deity. Place the festival and offering icons in the hall (*mukha-maṇḍapa*).
        *   The preceptor chants the *Ātma-sūkta*, performs the placement of syllables (*mantra-nyāsa* and *akṣara-nyāsa*), and, focusing his mind while facing North, meditates on the Supreme Lord. Reciting the mantra "Idaṃ viṣṇur...", he extracts the divine energy stored in the *kumbha* water jar, and transfers it to the head of the main icon, saying: "Come, O Lord" (*āyātu bhagavān*) and "I invoke Viṣṇu" (*viṣṇum āvāhayāmi*).
        *   Invoke the guardian deities of the three surrounding enclosures (*āvaraṇa-devān*)—such as Puruṣa and others, and Mitra and others—worship them, and offer seats saying: "Let this seat be accepted" (*astvetadāsanam*).
        *   Subsequently, transfer a spark of the divine energy from the main immovable icon to the festival and offering icons, just as one lights a lamp from another lamp (*dīpāt dīpam iva āvāhayet*).
        *   Perform daily worship (*arcanam*) for the main immovable icon (*dhruva-bera*) using seats and other offerings, without repeating the invocation (*āvāhana*) and dismissal (*visarga*) rites. Understand that this is the specific rule of the immovable icon, while all other worship procedures remain common to both types.
