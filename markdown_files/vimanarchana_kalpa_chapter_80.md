# Vimanarchana Kalpa of Sage Marīci (Vaikhānasa Āgama)
## Chapter 80: Agnikuṇḍādi-homopakaraṇa-devatā-vidhiḥ (Deities of Fire Pits and Sacrificial Implements)

This document contains the Sanskrit text, IAST transliteration, and English translation of the 80th Chapter (*Aśītitemaḥ Paṭalaḥ*) of the **Vimānārcanākalpa** (attributed to Sage Marīci, representing the Vaikhānasa school of Vaishnavism).

This chapter details the esoteric associations of the fire pits (*kuṇḍa*), altars (*sthaṇḍila*), and various sacrificial utensils (spoons, pots, firewood, grass bundles) with their presiding deities. It also provides the detailed iconographical description for meditating on Agni within the fire pit, and defines the rules for offering oblations into the seven specific tongues (*jihvā*) of the fire, warning against the spiritual and physical hazards of making offerings in non-blazing parts of the fire.

---

### Section 1: Presiding Deities of the Fire Pit and Implements

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **अथ अशीतितमः पटलः**
> **अथ अग्निकुण्डादि-होमोपकरणानाम् अधिदैवं व्याख्यास्यामः**
> **स्रुक्-स्रुवादीनां देवताः**
> ***atha aśītitamaḥ paṭalaḥ***
> ***atha agnikuṇḍādi-homopakaraṇānām adhidevaṃ vyākhyāsyāmaḥ***
> ***sruk-sruvādīnāṃ devatāḥ***
> 
> सिकता-मृदोः भूमि-दैवत्यं, स्थण्डिलस्य सोमः, कुण्डानाम् ऊर्ध्ववेदिः ब्रह्मा, मध्यवेदिः प्राजापत्या, सभ्य-पौण्डरीकयोः अधोवेदिः दलयुता सौम्या, दलानां वसु-दैवतम्, खननं पितृ-दैवत्यं, मेखलाः स्कन्द-दैवत्याः, प्रागग्रम् उदगग्रं च उल्लेखनं (or लेखनं) दैवत्यम्।
> *sikatā-mṛdoḥ bhūmi-daivatyaṃ, sthaṇḍilasya somaḥ, kuṇḍānām ūrdhvavediḥ brahmā, madhyavediḥ prājāpatyā, sabhya-pauṇḍarīkayoḥ adhovediḥ dalayutā saumyā, dalānāṃ vasu-daivatam, khananaṃ pitṛ-daivatyaṃ, mekhalāḥ skanda-daivatyāḥ, prāgagram udagagram ca ullekhanaṃ (or lekhanaṃ) daivatyam ||*
> 
> कूर्चानाम् अग्रं ब्रह्मा, ग्रन्थिः विष्णुः, मूलं शम्भु-दैवत्यम्; परिस्तरण-दर्भाणां विष्णुः; दक्षिण-परिधौ ब्रह्मा, उत्तर-परिधौ सोमः, पश्चिम-परिधौ गन्धर्वः, दक्षिण-परिधौ इन्द्रः, उत्तर-परिधौ मित्रावरुणौ वा; आज्य-अवनत-अध-ऊर्ध्व-समिधोः प्रजापतिः ईशः, वर्षिष्ठ-समिधः जुह्वादेश्च अग्निः, स्रुवस्य सोमः; आज्यस्थाली-चरुस्थाल्योः च पृथिवी, चरुः सर्वदेवत्यश्च; आज्यस्य अग्निः, इन्धनस्य सर्वदेवताः, अन्येषां होमद्रव्याणां सविता; श्री-दैवत्यं बैल्वं, पालाशम् आग्नेयं, नैयग्रोधं याम्यं, सौम्यम् औदुम्बरं, सौरम् आश्वत्थं, शाम्यं (or शमी-समिधः) वायव्यं, खादिरं राहवम् (or राहुः) इति अधिदेवताः भवन्ति। दैवत-हीने अज्ञाते अग्निः इति विज्ञायते ॥
> *kūrcānām agraṃ brahmā, granthiḥ viṣṇuḥ, mūlaṃ śambhu-daivatyam; paristaraṇa-darbhāṇāṃ viṣṇuḥ; daxiṇa-paridhou brahmā, uttara-paridhou somaḥ, paścima-paridhou gandharvaḥ, daxiṇa-paridhou indraḥ, uttara-paridhou mitrāvaruṇou vā; ājya-avanata-adha-ūrdhva-samidhoḥ prajāpatiḥ īśaḥ, varṣiṣṭha-samidhaḥ juhvādaiśca agniḥ, sruvasya somaḥ; ājyasthālī-carusthālyoḥ ca pṛthivī, caruḥ sarvadevatyaśca; ājyasya agniḥ, indhanasya sarvadevatāḥ, anyeṣāṃ homadravyāṇāṃ savitā; śrī-daivatyaṃ bailvaṃ, pālāśam āgneyaṃ, naiyagrodhaṃ yāmyaṃ, saumyam audumbaraṃ, sauram āśvatthaṃ, śāmyaṃ (or śamī-samidhaḥ) vāyavyaṃ, khādiraṃ rāhavam (or rāhuḥ) iti adhidevatāḥ bhavanti | daivata-hīne ajñāte agniḥ iti vijñāyate ||*

#### English Translation
*   **Implements Deities:** Now, we shall explain the presiding deities of the fire pits and other fire offering utensils (*atha agnikuṇḍādi-homopakaraṇānām adhidevaṃ vyākhyāsyāmaḥ*).

*   **Altar Elements:** For the sand and earth, the deity is Bhūmi (*sikatā-mṛdoḥ bhūmi-daivatyaṃ*); for the altar (*sthaṇḍilasya*), Soma; for the fire pits, the upper tier is Brahmā (*kuṇḍānām ūrdhvavediḥ brahmā*), and the middle tier is Prajāpatī. For the Sabhya and Pauṇḍarīka fire pits, the lower tier, which is adorned with petals, is Soma-ruled (*sabhya-pauṇḍarīkayoḥ adhovediḥ dalayutā saumyā*); the petals themselves are ruled by the Vasus (*dalānāṃ vasu-daivatam*).

*   **Actions and Enclosures:** Digging the pit is ruled by the Pitṛs (ancestral spirits); the three girdles/tiers of the pit are ruled by Skanda (*mekhalāḥ skanda-daivatyāḥ*); and drawing the three lines pointing east and north is ruled by the Devas (*prāgagram udagagram ca ullekhanaṃ daivatyam*).

*   **Grass and Enclosing Sticks:** For the grass bundles (*kūrcānām*), the tip is Brahmā (*agraṃ brahmā*), the knot is Viṣṇu (*granthiḥ viṣṇuḥ*), and the root is Śambhu/Śiva (*mūlaṃ śambhu-daivatyam*). For the grass spread around the pit (*paristaraṇa-darbhāṇām*), the deity is Viṣṇu. For the enclosing sticks (*paridhi*): the southern is Brahmā, the northern is Soma, the western is Gandharva, the southern (outer) is Indra, and the northern (outer) is Mitrāvaruṇa.

*   **Wood and Offering Spoons:** For the lower and upper firewood sticks bent for clarified butter (*ājya-samidhoḥ*), the deities are Prajāpatī and Īśa (Śiva); for the largest firewood stick (*varṣiṣṭha-samidhaḥ*) and the Juhū spoon, Agni; for the Sruva spoon, Soma. For the clarified butter pot and the charu pot, the deity is Pṛthivī (Earth); the *charu* rice offering itself is dedicated to all deities (*caruḥ sarvadevatyaśca*). For the clarified butter, Agni; for the firewood, all deities; for other offering materials, Savitṛ.

*   **Firewood Tree Species:** Wood from the Bilva tree is ruled by Śrī (*śrī-daivatyaṃ bailvaṃ*); Palāśa wood is ruled by Agni (*pālāśam āgneyaṃ*); Nyagrodha (Banyan) is ruled by Yama; Udumbara is ruled by Soma; Aśvattha (Peepal) is ruled by Sūrya; Śamī is ruled by Vāyu; Khādira is ruled by Rāhu.

*   **Default Deity:** If a deity is not specified or remains unknown for any implement (*daivata-hīne ajñāte*), it is understood to be Agni (*agniḥ iti vijñāyate*).

### Section 2: Meditation on the Form of Agni in the Fire Pit

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **कुण्डे अग्नि-मूर्ति-ध्यानम्**
> अथ अग्निकुण्डस्य मध्ये पश्चिमाभिमुखं ब्राह्मे आसने आसीनं, सर्वदैवतैः परितः, रक्तवर्णं, त्रिपादं, द्विशीर्षं, चतुःशृङ्गं, द्विनासिकम्, आस्यद्वयं, षडक्षं, मुकुटादि-आभरण-अन्वितं, सप्तहस्तं, दक्षिणे (हस्तचतुष्टये) अक्षमाला-खड्ग-शक्ति-युतं, वामे (हस्तत्रये) तोमर-व्यजन-खेटक-पात्र-युतं (or स्रुक्-स्रुव-युतं), अजबाहनं, दक्षिण-वामयोः स्वाहा-स्वधाभ्यां संयुक्तं, इध्महोमे स्थितम्, अन्नहोमे आसीनम्, आज्यहोमे शयितम् इति ध्यायेत् ॥
> ***kuṇḍe agni-mūrti-dhyānam***
> *atha agnikuṇḍasya madhye paścimābhimukhaṃ brāhme āsane āsīnaṃ, sarvadaivataiḥ paritaḥ, raktavarṇaṃ, tripādaṃ, dviśīrṣaṃ, catuḥśṛṅgaṃ, dvināsikam, āsyadvayaṃ, ṣadaxaṃ, mukuṭādi-ābharaṇa-anvitaṃ, saptahastaṃ, daxiṇe (hastacatuṣṭaye) axamālā-khaḍga-śakti-yutaṃ, vāme (hastatraye) tomara-vyajana-kheṭaka-pātra-yutaṃ (or sruk-sruva-yutaṃ), ajabāhanaṃ, daxiṇa-vāmayoḥ svāhā-svadhābhyāṃ saṃyuktaṃ, idhmahome sthitam, annahome āsīnam, ājyahome śayitam iti dhyāyet ||*

#### English Translation
*   **The Posture:** Now, in the center of the fire pit (*agnikuṇḍasya madhye*), facing west, one should meditate on Agni seated on a seat of Brahmā (*brāhme āsane āsīnaṃ*).

*   **Physical Form:** He is surrounded by all deities (*sarvadaivataiḥ paritaḥ*), red in color (*raktavarṇaṃ*), three-legged (*tripādaṃ*), two-headed (*dviśīrṣaṃ*), four-horned (*catuḥśṛṅgaṃ*), two-nosed (*dvināsikam*), two-mouthed (*āsyadvayaṃ*), six-eyed (*ṣadaxaṃ*), adorned with crowns and ornaments (*mukuṭādi-ābharaṇa-anvitaṃ*), and seven-handed (*saptahastaṃ*).

*   **Weapons and Mount:** In his right hands (*daxiṇe*), he holds a rosary, a sword, and a spear (*axamālā-khaḍga-śakti-yutaṃ*); in his left hands (*vāme*), he holds a javelin, a fan, a shield, and a ritual vessel (*tomara-vyajana-kheṭaka-pātra-yutaṃ*). He rides a goat as his vehicle (*ajabāhanam*).

*   **Consorts and Postures:** He is accompanied by his consorts Svāhā and Svadhā on his right and left (*daxiṇa-vāmayoḥ svāhā-svadhābhyāṃ saṃyuktaṃ*). He should be visualized as standing during firewood offerings (*idhmahome sthitam*), seated during food offerings (*annahome āsīnam*), and reclining/lying down during clarified butter offerings (*ājyahome śayitam*).

### Section 3: The Seven Tongues of Agni and Offerings

#### Sanskrit (Devanagari) & Transliteration (IAST)
> **सप्तजिह्वानां नामानि वर्णाः, तत्र होमद्रव्याणि**
> ‘हिरण्या कनका रक्ता कृष्णा च एव वसुप्रभा अतिरक्ता बहुरूपा’ इति सप्त जिह्वाः।
> तासु —
> 1. ऐशान्ये हेमाभायां हिरण्यायां समिद्-होमं,
> 2. पूर्वस्यां हेमाभायां (or पीताभायां) कनकायां आज्य-होमं,
> 3. आग्नेय्यां रक्ताभायां रक्तायां यव-होमं,
> 4. नैर्ऋत्यां नीलाभायां कृष्णायां लाज-होमं,
> 5. वारुण्यां पीताभायां वसुप्रभायां सक्तु-होमं,
> 6. वायव्ये श्वेताभायां अतिरक्तायां तिल-होमं,
> 7. दक्षिण-उत्तरयोः मध्ये बहुवर्णनिभायां बहुरूपायाम् इध्म-चरु-होमं,
> सप्तजिह्वासु च क्रमेण सप्तसमिद्-होमम्। अन्यानि होमद्रव्याणि सर्वाणि मध्यमायां जुहुयात्।
> ***saptajihvānāṃ nāmāni varṇāḥ, tatra homadravyāṇi***
> *‘hiraṇyā kanakā raktā kṛṣṇā ca eva vasuprabhā atiraktā bahurūpā’ iti sapta jihvāḥ |*
> *tāsu —*
> *1. aiśānye hemābhāyāṃ hiraṇyāyāṃ samid-homaṃ,*
> *2. pūrvasyāṃ hemābhāyāṃ (or pītābhāyāṃ) kanakāyāṃ ājya-homaṃ,*
> *3. āgneyyāṃ raktābhāyāṃ raktāyāṃ yava-homaṃ,*
> *4. nairṛtyāṃ nīlābhāyāṃ kṛṣṇāyāṃ lāja-homaṃ,*
> *5. vāruṇyāṃ pītābhāyāṃ vasuprabhāyāṃ saktu-homaṃ,*
> *6. vāyavye śvetābhāyāṃ atiraktāyāṃ tila-homaṃ,*
> *7. daxiṇa-uttarayoḥ madhye bahuvarṇanibhāyāṃ bahurūpāyām idhma-caru-homaṃ,*
> *saptajihvāsu ca krameṇa saptasamid-homam | anyāni homadravyāṇi sarvāṇi madhyamāyāṃ juhuyāt ||*
> 
> **अग्नेः श्रोत्रादि-स्थानं विज्ञाय, ज्वलदग्नौ होमस्य कर्तव्यता**
> यत्र काष्ठं तत् श्रोत्रं, धूमो नासिका, अल्पज्वलितं नेत्रं, भस्म शीर्षम् इति ज्ञात्वा; श्रोत्रे (हुते) व्याधि-पीडितं, चक्षुषि अन्धत्वं, नासिकायां महारोगं, मस्तके (or भस्मनि हुते) सर्वनाशः; तस्माद् दीप्त-ज्वलने जुहुयाद् इति आह मरीचिः ॥
> ***agneḥ śrotrādi-sthānaṃ vijñāya, jvaladagnou homasya kartavyatā***
> *yatra kāṣṭhaṃ tat śrotraṃ, dhūmo nāsikā, alpajvalitaṃ netraṃ, bhasma śīrṣam iti jñātvā; śrotre (hute) vyādhi-pīḍitaṃ, caxuṣi andhatvaṃ, nāsikāyāṃ mahārogaṃ, mastake (or bhasmani hute) sarvanāśaḥ; tasmād dīpta-jvalane juhuyād iti āha marīciḥ ||*

#### English Translation
*   **The Seven Tongues (Jihvā):** The seven tongues of the fire are: Hiranyā (golden/bright), Kanakā (yellow/gold), Raktā (red), Kṛṣṇā (black/blue), Vasuprabhā (radiant), Atiraktā (deep red), and Bahurūpā (multi-colored) (*‘hiraṇyā kanakā raktā kṛṣṇā ca eva vasuprabhā atiraktā bahurūpā’ iti sapta jihvāḥ*).

*   **Tongue and Offering Mapping:** In these tongues, one should offer the following materials:
    1.  **Hiranyā (NE, gold-colored):** Offering of firewood (*samid-homa*).
    2.  **Kanakā (East, golden/yellow-colored):** Offering of clarified butter (*ājya-homa*).
    3.  **Raktā (SE, red-colored):** Offering of barley (*yava-homa*).
    4.  **Kṛṣṇā (SW, blue-colored):** Offering of puffed rice (*lāja-homa*).
    5.  **Vasuprabhā (West, bright-colored):** Offering of barley meal (*saktu-homa*).
    6.  **Atiraktā (NW, red/white-colored):** Offering of sesame seeds (*tila-homa*).
    7.  **Bahurūpā (Center, multi-colored):** Offering of firewood and *charu* rice (*idhma-caru-homa*).
    *   In the seven tongues, in order, one should offer the seven kinds of firewood. All other general offering materials should be offered in the central tongue (*anyāni homadravyāṇi sarvāṇi madhyamāyāṃ juhuyāt*).

*   **The Spiritual Anatomy of Fire:** One must know the locations of the ears, etc., of Agni (*agneḥ śrotrādi-sthānaṃ vijñāya*): where there is unburnt wood, that is the ear (*yatra kāṣṭhaṃ tat śrotram*); where there is smoke, that is the nose (*dhūmo nāsikā*); where there is weak/smoldering flame, that is the eye (*alpajvalitaṃ netram*); where there is cold ash, that is the head (*bhasma śīrṣam*).

*   **Dangers of Misplaced Offerings:** Knowing this, if one makes offerings:
    *   In the **ear** (unburnt wood): causes diseases (*vyādhi-pīḍitam*).
    *   In the **eye** (weak flame): causes blindness (*andhatvam*).
    *   In the **nose** (smoke): causes great sickness (*mahārogam*).
    *   In the **head** (cold ash): causes total ruin (*sarvanāśaḥ*).
    *   *Conclusion:* Therefore, one must always make offerings only in a brightly blazing, clean flame (*tasmād dīpta-jvalane juhuyāt*), so declares Sage Marīci.

## Glossary of Key Terms

1.  **Sruksruvāḥ (स्रुक्स्रुव):** The pair of wooden ladles (Sruk and Sruva) used for pouring clarified butter into the sacred fire.
2.  **Sthaṇḍila (स्थण्डिल):** A raised, consecrated square altar made of clean sand and earth, used as the site for fire offerings.
3.  **Agnimūrti (अग्निमूर्ति):** The personified iconographic form of the Fire God (Agni), visualized as having multiple heads, limbs, and specific weapons.
4.  **Saptajihvā (सप्तजिह्वा):** The seven tongues or flames of Agni, each linked to a cardial direction, color, and specific type of offering.
5.  **Paridhi (परिधि):** Consecrated wooden sticks placed on three sides of the fire pit to form a protective boundary for the fire.
6.  **Idhma (इध्म):** Firewood bundles consisting of specific sacred wood types, tied together and offered during the homa.
7.  **Saktu (सक्तु):** Flour made from parched barley or grain, offered into the fire during specific Agamic rituals.
8.  **Lāja (लाज):** Puffed rice grains offered into the fire, symbolizing fertility and expansion.
9.  **Dīpta-jvalana (दीप्तज्वलन):** The brightly burning, smokeless, clean flame of the sacrificial fire, which is the only valid zone for offerings.
10. **Aṣṭākṣara (अष्टाक्षर):** The eight-syllable sacred mantra of Viṣṇu, used during purifications and offerings.
